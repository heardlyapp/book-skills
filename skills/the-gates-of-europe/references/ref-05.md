# Reference: The 2022 Invasion

## Overview
On February 24, 2022, Russia launched a full-scale invasion of Ukraine. Putin expected Kyiv to fall in days. Instead, the invasion triggered the largest war in Europe since 1945 and transformed Ukraine into a nation united as never before.

## Case: The Pre-War Buildup
Throughout 2021, Russia massed troops on Ukraine's borders. Putin published a long essay claiming that Ukraine was not a real country — that it was an artificial creation of the Soviet Union, a "Nazi" state, and a threat to Russia's security. The US warned of an imminent invasion. Many European leaders did not believe it. When the tanks crossed the border, the world was shocked. But Plokhy's history shows that the invasion was the culmination of centuries of Russian imperial thinking.

## Case: The Battle of Kyiv
Russian forces attempted to seize Kyiv in the first days of the war. They reached the suburbs but were stopped by Ukrainian resistance. The will to fight was the decisive factor. Russia expected Ukrainian soldiers to surrender; instead, they fought. The battle of Kyiv was a catastrophic failure for Russia and a turning point in the war.

## Case: The Global Impact
The 2022 invasion had consequences far beyond Ukraine. Energy prices spiked. Global food supplies were threatened (Ukraine is a major grain exporter). NATO strengthened and expanded (Finland and Sweden joined). The war redefined European security. Plokhy's book, published before the invasion, was prescient about the historical forces that made it inevitable.
