# Reference: Soviet Ukraine

## Overview
The 20th century was catastrophic for Ukraine. It became a battlefield for two world wars, an experiment for Stalinist social engineering, and a victim of the worst famine in European history.

## Case: The Holodomor
The Holodomor (Ukrainian for "death by hunger") was a man-made famine that killed 3-5 million Ukrainians in 1932-33. Stalin's forced collectivization of agriculture created massive resistance from Ukrainian peasants. Stalin responded by seizing all grain, sealing the Ukrainian borders, and preventing peasants from leaving their villages. People starved in full view of authorities who had plenty of food. The Holodomor is recognized by many countries as a genocide against the Ukrainian people.

## Case: World War II in Ukraine
Ukraine was one of the main battlegrounds of WWII. The Nazi invasion in 1941 was initially welcomed by some Ukrainians as liberation from Stalin. The welcome quickly turned to horror as the Nazis treated Ukrainians as subhumans, sending 2 million to Germany as forced laborers and murdering 1.5 million Ukrainian Jews in the Holocaust. The Nazi occupation was followed by Soviet reoccupation, which was followed by more repression. Ukraine lost an estimated 6-8 million people in WWII.

## Case: Chernobyl
The Chernobyl nuclear disaster (April 26, 1986) happened in northern Ukraine. The explosion released radioactive material across Europe. The disaster was a direct result of Soviet secrecy and mismanagement. For Ukrainians, Chernobyl became a symbol of Soviet incompetence and the regime's disregard for human life. It accelerated the nationalist movement that led to independence.
