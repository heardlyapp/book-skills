# Reference: Ancient and Medieval Ukraine

## Overview
Ukraine's history begins with the ancient steppe civilizations that inhabited the vast grasslands between the Carpathians and the Black Sea. The Scythians, Sarmatians, Greeks, and later the Kyivan Rus all left their mark on the land that would become Ukraine.

## Case: The Scythians
The Scythians were nomadic warriors who dominated the Ukrainian steppe from the 7th to 3rd centuries BCE. They were legendary archers and horsemen. Their burial mounds (kurgans) dot the Ukrainian landscape. The Greeks who founded Black Sea colonies called them the first people of the steppe. Herodotus wrote extensively about them. The Scythian gold found in Ukrainian burial mounds is among the most beautiful archaeological treasures in the world.

## Case: Kyivan Rus
Kyivan Rus was the first East Slavic state, established in the 9th century with Kyiv as its capital. Under Prince Volodymyr the Great (980-1015), Rus adopted Orthodox Christianity from Byzantium in 988 — a decision that would shape the region's identity for a millennium. The Kyivan Rus period is claimed as a national origin by both Ukraine and Russia. This shared claim is one of the most contested issues in the history of both nations.

## Case: The Cossack Hetmanate
After the Mongol invasion destroyed Kyivan Rus, Ukraine was divided between the Grand Duchy of Lithuania and the Kingdom of Poland. The Cossacks emerged in the 15th-16th centuries as semi-autonomous military communities along the Dnipro River. The Cossack Hetmanate (1648-1764) was an independent state under Hetman Bohdan Khmelnytsky. It was eventually absorbed by the Russian Empire. The Cossacks remain a powerful symbol of Ukrainian independence and military tradition.
