# Reference: Independent Ukraine

## Overview
Ukraine declared independence on August 24, 1991, and a referendum confirmed it with 92% support. The next three decades were a turbulent journey of building a nation from the ruins of empire.

## Case: The Orange Revolution (2004)
The 2004 presidential election was rigged in favor of Viktor Yanukovych, a Russia-backed candidate. Massive protests (the Orange Revolution) forced a new election, which Viktor Yushchenko won. The revolution was peaceful and demonstrated Ukrainians' commitment to democracy and European integration. But Yushchenko's government failed to deliver on its promises of reform, and the country became disillusioned.

## Case: The Euromaidan (2013-2014)
When Yanukovych won the presidency in 2010, he pursued a balancing act between Europe and Russia. In November 2013, he suddenly rejected an EU association agreement in favor of closer ties with Russia. Protests began in Kyiv's Maidan (Independence Square). The protests grew for three months, even after violent crackdowns. In February 2014, over 100 protesters were killed. Yanukovych fled to Russia. The Revolution of Dignity was a defining moment for Ukrainian national identity.

## Case: Crimea and Donbas (2014)
In the immediate aftermath of Yanukovych's fall, Russia annexed Crimea. Ukraine's military was in no position to resist. Then Russia-backed separatists seized parts of the Donbas. Ukraine lost control of Crimea and parts of two eastern provinces. The Minsk Agreements (2014-2015) stopped the worst fighting but did not resolve the conflict. The war in Donbas killed 14,000 people before 2022.
