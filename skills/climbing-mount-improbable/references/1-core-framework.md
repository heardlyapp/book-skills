# Core Framework: Mount Improbable and Cumulative Selection

> Source: Climbing Mount Improbable, Introduction and Chapters 1-2

## The Mountain Metaphor

Mount Improbable represents the challenge of evolving complex structures. The sheer cliff is "irreducible complexity." The gentle slope is cumulative selection — small steps over deep time.

> **Case: The Eye** (Chapter 1): Creationists argue the eye is irreducibly complex. Dawkins shows a plausible step-by-step path: light-sensitive patch → cup eye → pinhole eye → lens eye. Each intermediate stage is useful. The eye evolved independently 40+ times across different lineages.

> **Case: Camouflage and Mimicry** (Chapter 1): Leaf-mimicking insects, ant-mimicking beetles, and the leafy sea dragon — these examples of perfect camouflage show cumulative selection in action. Partial resemblance provides partial protection.

> **Key takeaway**: What looks like a sheer cliff from below is a gentle slope when you trace the actual evolutionary path. Every step along the way is advantageous for survival.

## Designoid vs. Design

Things that look designed but aren't: Dawkins calls them "designoid."

> **Case: The Biomorphs** (Chapter 1): Dawkins' Blind Watchmaker computer program starts with a simple shape. Users select variants. In a few generations, complex, lifelike forms emerge — no designer required.

> **Key takeaway**: Cumulative selection creates the illusion of design. The process is not random — it's cumulative, building on each previous success.
