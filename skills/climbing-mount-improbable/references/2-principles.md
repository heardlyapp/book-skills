# Principles: Spider Silk — Evolution's Engineering Marvel

> Source: Climbing Mount Improbable, Chapter 2

## The Multi-Tool of Spider Silk

Spiders evolved different silks for different purposes: draglines (strong and stiff), capture spirals (elastic and sticky), wrapping silk, egg cases, and mating threads. Each has unique mechanical properties optimized over millions of years of evolution.

> **Case: The Orb Web** (Chapter 2): An orb web uses at least four different silks from different glands. The frame is strong and stiff. The spokes are non-sticky. The spiral is elastic and sticky. The hub is soft. Each evolved for its specific function through cumulative selection.

> **Case: The Mating Thread** (Chapter 2): Male spiders attach a special thread to the female's web. They pluck it like a jazz musician to suppress her feeding instinct and lure her for mating. This specialized behavior and silk type evolved to solve sexual cannibalism.

> **Key takeaway**: Spider silk is a family of specialized proteins, each optimized for a different task.

## The Evolution of Web Architecture

> **Case: The Bolas Spider** (Chapter 2): Some spiders abandoned webs entirely. The bolas spider uses a single thread with a sticky blob, luring moths by mimicking pheromones. It evolved from orb-web ancestors. Evolution strips away structures when simpler strategies work better.

> **Key takeaway**: Evolution doesn't always add complexity. It can remove it when a simpler strategy is better. The evolutionary slope goes both up and down.
