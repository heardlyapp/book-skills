# 4 — Philanthropy and Giving Back

> Source: "Beyoncé: A Little Golden Book Biography" by Lavaille Lavette

## BeyGOOD Foundation

Created in 2013 after Beyoncé "wanted to share her success with others." The mission: help people who don't have enough to eat and provide homes for people without shelter. "To inspire people to be kind and charitable, and to be good to themselves, others, their community, and the world."

## Giving Back as a Principle

Beyoncé's philanthropy is not an afterthought — it's a core value she learned from watching her parents give to others. The BeyGOOD Foundation has supported food banks, homeless shelters, disaster relief, and scholarship programs.

> **Case: Sharing Success** (BeyGOOD Foundation, 2013): Beyoncé launched the foundation at the peak of her solo success. She didn't wait until she had "enough" to give back. She started giving while still climbing. The foundation's name reflects her philosophy: BE GOOD.
> **Key takeaway**: You don't need to be at the top to start giving. Generosity is a practice, not a destination. Give while you climb, not just when you arrive.

> **Case: Why She Started BeyGOOD** (BeyGOOD Foundation): Beyoncé started giving back because she "wanted to share her success with others." The anti-pattern she avoided: waiting until you're fully successful to start helping. Many people think "I'll give back when I've made it." Beyoncé gave back while she was still climbing.
> **Key takeaway**: Generosity is not a reward for success — it's a practice that makes success meaningful. Start giving now, no matter how small.
