# 1 — The Beyoncé Story

> Source: "Beyoncé: A Little Golden Book Biography" by Lavaille Lavette

## Childhood in Houston

Beyoncé Giselle Knowles-Carter was born on September 4, 1981, in Houston, Texas. She grew up with her younger sister Solange, their mom Celestine (Ms. Tina) who owned a hair salon, and their dad Mathew who was a salesman.

> **Case: Sweeping Floors** (Early Life): Young Beyoncé helped her mom at the salon by sweeping the floor. She watched her mom turn ideas into reality. This is where she learned her work ethic.
> **Key takeaway**: You don't need to start big. Start where you are. Every sweep of the floor is preparation for something greater.

> **Case: The Dance Teacher** (Elementary School): During dance class, her teacher hummed a song and Beyoncé sang along, hitting every note. The teacher encouraged her to sing as well as dance. This was her first discovery of her vocal talent.
> **Key takeaway**: Sometimes others see your gift before you do. Pay attention to encouragement.

## The Path to Fame

She auditioned for Girls Tyme at age 9, met Kelly Rowland (her lifelong best friend), and they performed on Star Search. Although they didn't win, it fueled their dreams. Her father left his job to manage the group. They got a record deal that fell through, then became Destiny's Child with Columbia Records. Their first single: "No, No, No."

## Solo Stardom

After Destiny's Child, Beyoncé released Dangerously in Love (2003), married Jay-Z, won 28 Grammys (most-awarded woman ever), and created the BeyGOOD Foundation.
