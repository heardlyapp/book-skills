# 1 — The Beyoncé Story Framework

> Source: "Beyoncé: A Little Golden Book Biography" by Lavaille Lavette

## The Story Arc: Sweeping Floors to Global Icon

### Stage 1: Houston Roots (1981-1990)

Born September 4, 1981 in Houston, Texas. Youngest of two daughters (sister Solange). Mom Celestine (Ms. Tina) owned a hair salon. Dad Mathew was a salesman.

> **Case: Sweeping Floors at the Salon** (Early Life): Young Beyoncé helped her mom at the salon by sweeping the floor. She saw how hard her mom worked and watched her turn ideas into reality. This is where she learned her work ethic — not from a lecture, but from watching her mother build something from nothing.
> **Key takeaway**: The most powerful lessons children learn are not what you tell them, but what they see you do every day.

### Stage 2: Discovery — Dance and Voice (Elementary School)

During dance class, her teacher hummed a song. Beyoncé started singing along, hitting every note perfectly. Her teacher encouraged her to sing, not just dance. This was her first discovery of her vocal talent.

### Stage 3: Girls Tyme → Destiny's Child

At age 9, she auditioned for Girls Tyme. Met Kelly Rowland — they became best friends. Competed on Star Search. Didn't win, but it fueled their dreams.

> **Case: The Setback That Almost Ended Everything** (Early Career): At age 14, Girls Tyme got a record deal. Then the label dropped them without releasing a single album. Devastating. But "the girls didn't let the setback stop them." They changed their name to Destiny's Child, signed with Columbia Records, and released "No, No, No" as their first single.
> **Key takeaway**: The difference between those who make it and those who don't is not avoiding failure — it's refusing to stay down after failure.
