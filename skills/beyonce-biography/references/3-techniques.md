# 3 — Solo Career and Achievements

> Source: "Beyoncé: A Little Golden Book Biography" by Lavaille Lavette

## Solo Breakthrough

After Destiny's Child split, Beyoncé released her first solo album *Dangerously in Love* (2003). The lead single "Crazy in Love" featuring Jay-Z won a Grammy for Best R&B Song.

## Major Achievements

- **28 Grammy Awards** — Most-awarded woman in Grammy history (as of 2021)
- **Multi-platinum albums**: *B'Day*, *I Am... Sasha Fierce*, *Beyoncé*, *Lemonade*
- **Film career**: *Austin Powers in Goldmember*, *Dreamgirls*, *The Lion King* (voice)
- **BeyGOOD Foundation**: Launched 2013 to fight hunger and homelessness
- **Family**: Married Jay-Z, three children (Blue Ivy, twins Rumi and Sir)

> **Case: Most-Awarded Woman** (2021): Beyoncé became the most-awarded woman in Grammy Awards history with 28 wins. This was decades after a little girl in Houston started sweeping floors at her mom's salon.
> **Key takeaway**: Great achievements are built on small, consistent steps over a long time.
