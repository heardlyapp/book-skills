# 3 — Solo Career, Family, and Achievements

> Source: "Beyoncé: A Little Golden Book Biography" by Lavaille Lavette

## Solo Breakthrough

- **Dangerously in Love** (2003): First solo album. "Crazy in Love" with Jay-Z won Grammy
- **28 Grammys**: Most-awarded woman in Grammy history (as of 2021)
- **Film career**: Austin Powers in Goldmember, Dreamgirls, The Lion King

## Family

Married Jay-Z in 2008. Three children: Blue Ivy (2012), twins Rumi and Sir (2017).

> **Case: The Most-Awarded Woman** (2021): Beyoncé became the most-awarded woman in Grammy history with 28 wins. From the little girl who swept floors at her mom's salon to the most decorated female artist ever. "I strongly believe if you work hard, whatever you want, it will come to you."
> **Key takeaway**: Records are not broken overnight. They are the result of years of work that nobody sees. Sweeping floors and accepting Grammys are two ends of the same journey of dedication.

> **Case: The Movie Career Pivot** (Solo Years): Beyoncé didn't just sing — she acted in Austin Powers in Goldmember (2002), Dreamgirls (2006), and voiced Nala in The Lion King (2019). Each move expanded her brand beyond music. She didn't stay in her lane; she created new lanes.
> **Key takeaway**: Don't let others define what you can do. If you have multiple talents, develop them all. Beyoncé the singer became Beyoncé the actress became Beyoncé the entrepreneur.
