# 2 — The Destiny's Child Era

> Source: "Beyoncé: A Little Golden Book Biography" by Lavaille Lavette

## Key Milestones

- **Girls Tyme**: Formed at age 9. Competed on Star Search. Didn't win but kept going.
- **First record deal**: Signed at 14, then dropped without releasing an album. Devastating but not fatal.
- **Destiny's Child**: Signed with Columbia Records. "No, No, No" was their first single.
- **"Say My Name"**: Won Grammy for Best R&B Song (2001). The group's breakthrough.
- **Lineup changes**: Original members left, new members joined. The group found its groove with Beyoncé, Kelly Rowland, and Michelle Williams.
- **Destiny Fulfilled**: Final album sold half a million copies in first week. World tour followed.

> **Case: The Setback** (Age 14): The record label dropped Girls Tyme after signing them. "The girls didn't let the setback stop them!" They changed their name to Destiny's Child, kept performing, and got a deal with Columbia Records.
> **Key takeaway**: Rejection is not the end. It's a detour to a better path.
