# 2 — Destiny's Child Era and Breakthrough

> Source: "Beyoncé: A Little Golden Book Biography" by Lavaille Lavette

## Key Milestones

- **Star Search**: Competed but didn't win. Loved the spotlight anyway
- **First record deal**: Signed at 14 — then dropped. Setback, not stop sign
- **Columbia Records**: Signed as Destiny's Child. First single: "No, No, No"
- **"Say My Name"**: Won Grammy for Best R&B Song (2001). Breakthrough moment
- **Lineup changes**: Original members left, new members joined. The group found its groove
- **Destiny Fulfilled**: Final album. Sold half a million copies in first week. World tour
- **Split**: The three members decided to pursue solo careers

> **Case: Father's Leap of Faith** (Girls Tyme Era): Beyoncé's father, Mathew Knowles, was so impressed with Girls Tyme that he left his job to become their manager. This meant Beyoncé got to work alongside her dad while chasing her dreams. It was a huge risk — leaving a steady job to manage a teenage girl group.
> **Key takeaway**: Having someone who believes in you enough to risk everything is invaluable. That level of support changes the trajectory of your life.

> **Case: Kelly Rowland — Best Friend and Bandmate** (Girls Tyme): Kelly and Beyoncé met in Girls Tyme at age 9. They became best friends instantly. Through lineup changes, label issues, and the eventual split of Destiny's Child, their friendship endured. Kelly was at Beyoncé's wedding. They've supported each other's solo careers for over two decades.
> **Key takeaway**: Building a career with people you genuinely love makes the journey sustainable. Your relationships are as important as your talent.
