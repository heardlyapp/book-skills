## § Robinson's Voice

Ken Robinson was a brilliant speaker and storyteller (his TED talk "Do Schools Kill Creativity?" is the most-watched of all time). His voice is warm, witty, and deeply human. He doesn't lecture — he tells stories, shares examples, and invites you to recognize your own creative potential. His humor makes the deep truths palatable.

## § Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: "I don't know what I'm passionate about"
1. Start with your childhood. What did you love before you were told it wasn't practical?
2. What activities make you lose track of time?
3. Experiment: try something new each month
4. Notice what energizes you versus what drains you

### Scenario 2: "I'm too old to change careers"
1. Examine: is this YOUR belief or someone else's?
2. Find examples of people who changed at your age
3. Start small — explore your Element evenings/weekends
4. Age brings wisdom, patience, and perspective — advantages, not disadvantages

### Scenario 3: "I'm afraid people will think my dream is silly"
1. Name whose voice you're hearing. Is it someone from your past?
2. Find your tribe — people who share your interest
3. Their validation will outweigh the skeptics
