# 3 — Systemic Racism: Policy Creates Inequality

> Source: "Stamped from the Beginning" by Ibram X. Kendi

## How Policy Creates Inequality

Kendi shows that racial inequality was created by specific policies:
- **Slavery**: 250 years of forced labor, family destruction, legal non-personhood.
- **Jim Crow**: Segregation laws after Reconstruction. Legal discrimination in every aspect of life.
- **Redlining**: Federal housing policy that denied mortgages to Black neighborhoods. Created the wealth gap.
- **Mass Incarceration**: War on Drugs, mandatory minimums, police targeting. Created a prison system disproportionately filled with Black Americans.
- **Voter Suppression**: Poll taxes, literacy tests, purges. Ongoing.

> **Case: The Housing Example** (20th Century Chapter): Kendi traces how the federal government created segregated housing. The FHA refused to insure mortgages in Black neighborhoods. Banks refused loans to Black families. This single policy created the racial wealth gap that persists today — and was justified by the "racist idea" that Black neighborhoods were "bad investments."
> **Key takeaway**: Policy creates inequality. Then racist ideas are invented to explain the inequality. The cycle continues until the policy is changed.

> **Case: The "Thug" Narrative** (Modern Chapter): Kendi traces how the word "thug" became a racial code word in the 21st century. When a young white man shot up a church, he was called "mentally ill." When a young Black man was killed by police, he was called a "thug." The racist idea: Black people are dangerous. The policy: aggressive policing, stop-and-frisk, mass incarceration.
> **Key takeaway**: Language is part of the system. The words we use for Black and white people in similar situations reveal the racist ideas that justify unequal treatment.
