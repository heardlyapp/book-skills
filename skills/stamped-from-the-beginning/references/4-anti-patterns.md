# 4 — Anti-Patterns

> Source: "Stamped from the Beginning" by Ibram X. Kendi

## Anti-Pattern 1: Biological Racism

**The mistake:** Believing racial groups have different inherent biological traits.

## Anti-Pattern 2: Cultural Racism

**The mistake:** Blaming racial inequality on cultural deficiencies rather than structural barriers.

## Anti-Pattern 3: Colorblindness

**The mistake:** "I don't see color." This ignores the reality of racial discrimination and prevents action.

## Anti-Pattern 4: Respectability Politics

**The mistake:** "If they just behaved better, they'd be treated equally." This places blame on the oppressed.

> **Case: The "Model Minority" Myth** (Modern Chapter): Kendi shows how the model minority myth (Asian Americans as the "good" minority) is a form of assimilationist racism. It says: "They succeeded, so why can't you?" — ignoring different histories, different policies, and different forms of discrimination.
> **Key takeaway**: Comparing racial groups' outcomes without acknowledging different histories and policies is a racist idea. It blames the victims of discrimination for not overcoming it fast enough.

> **Case: The "I Have Black Friends" Defense** (Contemporary): Kendi addresses the common deflection: "I can't be racist — I have Black friends." His response: having Black friends does not mean you don't hold racist ideas or support racist policies. Judge by policy, not by personal relationships.
> **Key takeaway**: Friendship with someone from a different race does not exempt you from examining your own biases or your support for racist policies. Actions matter more than associations.
