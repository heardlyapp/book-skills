# 5 — Kendi's Voice + Application

> Source: "Stamped from the Beginning" by Ibram X. Kendi

## Kendi's Voice

Ibram X. Kendi is a historian and the founding director of the Boston University Center for Antiracist Research. His voice: scholarly, passionate, clear. He combines academic rigor with moral urgency.

> **Case: Why He Wrote the Book** (Preface): Kendi explains that he started researching racist ideas thinking they came from ignorance. The more he researched, the more he realized the opposite: racist ideas were created by brilliant people to justify exploitation. "This book is the result of a journey. I started it with one question and ended with a completely different answer."
> **Key takeaway**: Kendi's own intellectual journey mirrors the book's message. He started believing racist ideas came from ignorance. He ended understanding they came from power.

## Key Quote

"The only way to undo racism is to consistently identify it and describe it — and then dismantle it."

## 5 Scenarios

1. **Arguing about racism** → Use Kendi's framework: segregationist vs assimilationist vs antiracist.
2. **Learning history** → Focus on policy, not just ideas. What laws created inequality?
3. **Feeling overwhelmed** → The history is awful. But change is possible. Focus on one policy you can influence.
4. **Talking to family** → Don't attack their character. Name the policy. Describe the impact. Offer the alternative.
5. **Being antiracist daily** -> Read, listen, speak, act. Not just today, but every day.
