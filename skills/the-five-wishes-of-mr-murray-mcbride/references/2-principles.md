# 2. Principles — Living Fully at 100

> Source: "The Five Wishes of Mr. Murray McBride" by Joe Siple (2018). Principles from Murray's wisdom.

## The 7 Principles

### Principle 1: Age Does Not End Desire
Murray is 100 and still wants things. The desire for meaning, connection, and purpose does not fade with age.

### Principle 2: Friendship Is the Engine of Fulfillment
Murray cannot fulfill his wishes alone. He needs Jason. Wishes require connection.

> **Case: The Bench Meeting** (Chapter 1): Murray and Jason meet on a park bench. Murray is alone. Jason is alone. They begin talking. This simple meeting leads to everything. The entire story hinges on one person being willing to speak and another willing to listen.
> **Key takeaway**: The most important relationships begin with the smallest gestures.

### Principle 3: Forgiveness Is for Yourself
Murray's wish to restore a relationship is not about the other person. It is about his own peace.

### Principle 4: Heroism Is Accessible
Murray's wish to do something heroic is fulfilled in a simple way — not by grand gesture but by showing up for someone.

### Principle 5: The Best Wishes Are for Others
The final wish is not for Murray. It is for Jason. Giving becomes more important than receiving.

### Principle 6: A Life Is Remembered by Its Depth, Not Its Length
Murray lived 100 years, but the months with Jason contain the meaning of his life.

### Principle 7: It Is Never Too Late
Murray makes his wishes at 100. He fulfills them at 100. As long as you are alive, it is not too late.

> **Case: The Ocean Wish** (Chapters 15-18): Murray's fourth wish is to see the ocean one last time. The trip is physically difficult for a 100-year-old man. But the moment he sees the water, his face transforms. The ocean does not care how old he is. It is the same ocean it always was.
> **Key takeaway**: Simple pleasures — the ocean, the sky, a sunset — are available at any age. They do not diminish.
