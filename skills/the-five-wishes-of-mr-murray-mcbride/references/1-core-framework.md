# 1. Core Framework — The Wish Journey

> Source: "The Five Wishes of Mr. Murray McBride" by Joe Siple (2018). The novel follows Murray's quest to fulfill five wishes.

## The Wish Structure

The novel's framework is simple: 100-year-old Murray McBride has five wishes he wants to fulfill before he dies. Each wish is a chapter, a milestone, and a lesson.

### The Five Wishes

1. To be seen as important — Murray wants to matter to someone before he dies.
2. To restore a broken relationship — Murray is estranged from his son and wants to reconcile.
3. To do something heroic — Murray wants one act of genuine courage.
4. To see the ocean one last time — A simple, beautiful wish for a man who has lived inland.
5. The unexpected wish — The wish Murray discovers he needs, different from what he planned.

> **Case: The First Wish** (Chapters 1-8): Murray's first wish — to be seen as important — is fulfilled simply by Jason listening to him. A boy sitting on a bench, paying attention, makes a 100-year-old man feel that his life mattered. This establishes the novel's theme: the smallest acts of attention are the most meaningful.
> **Key takeaway**: The simplest wish is often the deepest. Being seen is the most fundamental human need.

> **Case: The Fifth Wish** (Final chapters): Murray's final wish is not what he expected. It is not about him at all. It is about Jason. Murray wishes for Jason to live. This wish shifts the entire novel from "what an old man wants" to "what one person can give another."
> **Key takeaway**: The best wishes are for others. Love transforms wanting into giving.
