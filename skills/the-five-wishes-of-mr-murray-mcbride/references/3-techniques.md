# 3. Techniques — Dual Timeline Narrative

> Source: "The Five Wishes of Mr. Murray McBride" by Joe Siple (2018). Narrative craft techniques.

## The Dual Timeline

The novel has two timelines: the present (Murray and Jason) and the future (Jason as Prospero the Magician). The future timeline frames the story.

> **Case: The Opening Frame** (Prologue): The novel opens with adult Jason, now a famous magician named Prospero, about to perform the biggest show of his life. Two seats are empty. The reader does not know who they are for. This mystery carries through the entire book.
> **Key takeaway**: A framing device creates suspense. The reader keeps reading to understand the opening.

## The Magician Metaphor

Jason becomes a magician. This is not random — it symbolizes the miraculous nature of Murray's wishes. Things that seemed impossible (a 100-year-old man fulfilling his dreams) become possible. Magic is belief.

> **Case: Prospero's Name** (Prologue and Epilogue): Jason's stage name is Prospero — the magician from Shakespeare's The Tempest who creates a storm and then calms it. The name evokes transformation, loss, and redemption.
> **Key takeaway**: Character names should carry meaning. Prospero is not just a cool name — it is a literary reference that deepens the story.

## The Emotional Payoff Structure

Siple builds the wishes in ascending order of emotional difficulty. The first wish is simple (being seen). The fifth wish is the hardest (selflessness). Each wish prepares Murray — and the reader — for the next.

> **Case: The Wish Progression** (Chapters 1-38): Wish 1 (easy, surface-level), Wish 2 (harder, requires vulnerability), Wish 3 (requires courage), Wish 4 (simple beauty), Wish 5 (requires letting go). The pattern mirrors emotional growth: from ego to connection, from wanting to giving.
> **Key takeaway**: A satisfying narrative arc escalates stakes gradually. Each success makes the next challenge possible.
