# Techniques — Emotional Blackmail

## How to Break Free from Emotional Blackmail (Based on Dr. Susan Forward)

### Technique 1: The FOG Assessment
When you feel pressured, ask: "Am I feeling Fear? Obligation? Guilt?" Identify which one is being triggered. Case: Jim and Helen — when Jim sulked after Helen said no to moving in, Helen felt obligation (he's so lonely) and guilt (I'm being selfish). Naming the FOG is the first step to clearing it.

### Technique 2: S.O.S. — Stop, Observe, Strategize
When you feel the pressure, don't respond. Say: "I need to think about that" or "I'll get back to you." Buy time. Case: Sarah with her boyfriend Frank — whenever Frank implied she wasn't "committed enough," she immediately gave in. Forward taught her to say "I need some time to think about this." That pause broke the cycle.

### Technique 3: Non-Defensive Communication
Forward provides specific scripts that don't engage with the blackmailer's framing:
- "I'm sorry you feel that way."
- "I can see how you might think that."
- "I'll think about that."
- "That's your choice."
- "I understand. Still, this is my decision."
Case: Tom's mother guilt-tripped him about Christmas. Practice: "Mom, I understand you're disappointed. We've already bought the tickets and we're going."

### Technique 4: Identify Your Hot Buttons
Forward guides readers to identify what makes them vulnerable: fear of anger, fear of rejection, need for approval, need for peace at any price. Case: Liz's hot button was "being seen as selfish" — her husband knew exactly which label would make her comply. Once she identified this, she could resist the label.

### Technique 5: The Decision Framework
Not every blackmail is worth fighting. Forward provides questions: How important is this issue to me? What are the consequences of giving in? Of standing firm? Is this a pattern or a one-time thing? Case: Forward distinguishes between a partner who occasionally uses guilt (work on it) and a parent who uses threats as a way of life (consider distance).

### Technique 6: The Compliance Audit
Forward's checklist: "Do important people in your life: threaten to make your life difficult? threaten to end the relationship? threaten to hurt themselves? always want more? label you as selfish when you don't comply?" If you answered yes to even one, you're being emotionally blackmailed.

### Technique 7: The "What Will I Lose?" Exercise
When considering standing firm, ask: What will I actually lose? Often the "loss" is the illusion of the relationship, not the relationship itself. Case: Frank threatened to call off the wedding if Sarah didn't babysit his sister's kids. She realized: if he'd end the relationship over this, the relationship wasn't solid anyway.
