# Principles: Hemispheric Asymmetries

> Source: My Stroke of Insight, Chapters 2-3, 16

## Left Hemisphere Functions

Language, calculation, logic, sequence, time perception, and the narrative sense of "I" — a story about yourself that continues across time. The left brain sorts the world into categories: good/bad, mine/yours, past/future. It's the part that says "I am angry" or "I should be better."

> **Case: Losing Language** (Chapter 4): As the hemorrhage expanded, Taylor lost the ability to speak words. She could still understand language at first, then even that disappeared. She experienced the dismantling of left-hemisphere functions in the reverse order they had developed in childhood — the last thing to go was the deepest sense of "self."

> **Key takeaway**: The left hemisphere is brilliant at what it does, but it also generates suffering — judgment, comparison, fear of the future, regret about the past. When it went silent, Taylor felt peace.

## Right Hemisphere Functions

Present moment awareness, body sensation, energy perception, emotional processing. The right brain experiences nowness without judgment. It perceives the big picture and feels connected to everything.

> **Case: The Euphoria** (Chapter 6): As the left hemisphere shut down, Taylor entered pure right-hemisphere consciousness. She felt at one with the universe. No fear. No separate self. Just energy flowing. She later realized that this state — often called mystical or spiritual — is simply normal right-hemisphere function that anyone can access by quieting the left brain's chatter.

> **Key takeaway**: Inner peace is not a distant goal. It's the brain's natural state when the left hemisphere's constant narration pauses.
