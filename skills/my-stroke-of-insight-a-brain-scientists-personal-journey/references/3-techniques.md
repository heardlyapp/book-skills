# Techniques: Recovery Protocol

> Source: My Stroke of Insight, Chapters 9-14, Appendix B

## The 8-Year Recovery

Taylor's recovery was slow and methodical. She had to relearn everything from scratch: how to walk, talk, read, and write. She documented 50+ recommendations for recovery in Appendix B. The key principle: the brain heals itself — you just need to create the conditions.

> **Case: GG Comes to Town** (Chapter 10): Taylor's mother became her primary caregiver. GG created a peaceful environment, used simple affirmative language, and never tested her. She understood instinctively that the brain heals best in a safe, judgment-free space. Her presence was the single most important factor in Taylor's recovery.

> **Case: The Stereotactic Craniotomy** (Chapter 12): The surgery to remove the blood clot required Taylor to be awake so surgeons could map her language centers. She used her knowledge of brain anatomy to help guide them through her own brain. Even under this extreme stress, she was teaching.

## Recommendations for Recovery (Appendix B)

Specific techniques: create a calm quiet environment; use simple affirmative language; repeat everything (new neural pathways need repetition); never ask "remember this?" (it creates pressure); celebrate every tiny milestone; get sleep (the brain consolidates during sleep); provide unconditional positive regard.

> **Key takeaway**: Taylor's Appendix B is a practical guide for caregivers. The single most important factor: don't test the patient. Just love them.
