# Techniques: Capital Allocation Playbook

## The Five Options

| Option | When to Use | Example |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| Reinvest in business | High returns available, organic growth | Murdoch's News Corp |
| Acquire other companies | Good targets at reasonable prices | Murphy's Capital Cities |
| Buy back shares | Stock undervalued, no better option | Singleton's Teledyne |
| Pay dividends | Limited opportunities, tax-efficient for investors | Bill Anders at General Dynamics |
| Hold cash | No good opportunity, preserve flexibility | Buffett's Berkshire |

> **Case: Bill Anders at General Dynamics** (Chapter 7): Anders took over a struggling defense company. He didn't try to grow it. Instead, he divested non-core businesses, used the proceeds to buy back stock, and returned cash to shareholders. GD's stock went from $25 to $150. He proved that shrinking a company can create enormous value.
> **Key takeaway**: Growth is not always value creation. Sometimes the best strategy is to shrink and distribute. Anders showed that "selling off the family silver" can create enormous shareholder value.

## Contrarian Playbook

1. Build a cash reserve during good times
2. Wait for a downturn when others are desperate
3. Buy assets when sellers are panicking
4. Buy back your own stock when it's undervalued
5. Repeat

> **Case: Buffett's Sawdust** (Chapter 8): When asked for his advice on investing, Buffett said: "Be greedy when others are fearful." The Outsiders CEOs all had the courage to act against the crowd — buying when everyone else was selling.
> **Key takeaway**: Contrarian investing requires courage and liquidity. Most people talk about being contrarian but can't actually do it when the moment arrives.
