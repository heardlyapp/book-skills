# Author's Voice + Key Scenarios

> **Case: Thorndike's Analysis** (Author's Note): William Thorndike is a Harvard MBA and founder of a private investment firm. He studied these eight CEOs for years, analyzing their capital allocation decisions and the results. His unique contribution is synthesizing their common patterns into a coherent framework.
> **Key takeaway**: Thorndike's authority comes from deep analytical work. He didn't just tell stories — he quantified the results.

> **Case: The Outsiders Pattern** (Conclusion): Despite very different industries and strategies, the eight CEOs shared striking similarities: frugality, analytical rigor, patience, contrarian instincts, and a relentless focus on per-share value. Their combined record outperformed the S&P 500 and famous investors like Peter Lynch and George Soros.
> **Key takeaway**: The Outsiders framework works across industries and time periods. The principles of superior capital allocation are universal.

## Key Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Company Has Cash
Evaluate all options: reinvest, acquire, buy back, dividend, or hold. Choose the highest-return option.

### Scenario 2: Stock Is Undervalued
Buy back shares aggressively. Nothing creates value like buying your own stock at a discount.

### Scenario 3: Market Downturn
You've been holding cash for this moment. Buy when others are selling.

### Scenario 4: No Good Opportunities
Hold cash. Wait. Don't let pressure force a bad decision.

### Scenario 5: Pressure to Grow
Ignore the pressure. Focus on per-share value creation. Small can be beautiful.
