# Techniques: Practices for Presence and Awakening

## Simple Sitting Meditation

1. **Sit comfortably** — on a cushion or chair, spine relatively straight
2. **Bring attention to the breath** — feel the air entering and leaving the body
3. **When the mind wanders** (it will), gently bring it back — no judgment, no frustration
4. **Start with 5-10 minutes** — consistency matters more than duration
5. **Use a mantra** if helpful — Ram Dass recommends "Ram" or "Love"

## Breath Awareness Practice

- Inhale: silently say "here"
- Exhale: silently say "now"
- Let the breath and the phrase merge into one awareness
- When distracted, return to the breath

## The "One-ing" Practice

Ram Dass teaches "one-ing" — the practice of seeing unity in all things:

1. Look at another person and silently say: "You are me. I am you. We are one."
2. Look at a tree, the sky, or an animal and do the same
3. Feel the separation dissolve, even for a moment
4. If thoughts of separation arise, just note them and return to the feeling of connection

## The Loving Awareness Practice

1. Sit quietly and bring to mind someone you love easily
2. Let the feeling of love fill your heart
3. Extend that love outward — to yourself, to others, to the world
4. Notice: the love doesn't come from outside; it IS your nature

## The Karma Yoga Practice

"Service is the fullest expression of spiritual life." Practice:

1. Before any action, set the intention: "I do this as service"
2. During the action, stay present — don't rush to the next thing
3. After the action, let go of the result — the outcome is not yours to control
4. Notice: serving without attachment to outcome IS freedom

## The Cookbook's Daily Practices

The original Cookbook recommends:
- Morning meditation
- Mantra repetition throughout the day
- Service to others without expectation
- Reading spiritual texts (but not getting attached to them)
- Evening reflection and gratitude
