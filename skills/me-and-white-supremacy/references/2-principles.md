# Key Principles — Me and White Supremacy

## I. White Supremacy Is a System, Not Just Individual Bias

"White supremacy is a system of oppression that maintains the dominance of white people and the subordination of people of color. It operates in both overt and subtle ways." Case: it's not just white hoods — it's also "colorblindness," "exceptionalism," and "tone policing."

## II. The Work Starts With You, Not "Those White People Out There"

"This work is not about those white people 'out there.' It is about you. Just you." Case: Saad's workbook is structured as personal reflection — each day you journal about your own complicity.

## III. Discomfort Is Part of the Process

"Racial discomfort is inherent to an authentic examination of white supremacy." Case: Robin DiAngelo's foreword — "Building the racial stamina required to challenge the racist status quo is a critical part of our work as white people."

## IV. Ally Is a Practice, Not an Identity

"Ally is not a title you give yourself — it's a practice you engage in." Case: Saad distinguishes between performative allyship (posting on social media) and substantive allyship (taking action that risks comfort or privilege).

## V. Good Ancestor Is the Goal

"The primary drive is to become a good ancestor." Case: Saad quotes Octavia Butler's Earthseed — "To shape God with wisdom and forethought to benefit your world, your people, your life. Consider consequences. Minimize harm. Ask questions. Seek answers. Learn. Teach."

## VI. Avoidance Is a Choice

"Not choosing is also a choice." Case: Many white people "opt out" of engaging with racism because it's uncomfortable. Saad argues that this avoidance is itself a form of upholding white supremacy.

## VII. The Work Is Never Done

"Antiracism is not a destination — it's a continuous practice." Case: The 28 days are just the beginning. "After the 28 days, the real work begins."
