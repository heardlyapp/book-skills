# Techniques — Me and White Supremacy

## How to Do Antiracism Work (Based on Layla F. Saad)

### Technique 1: The Daily Journal Protocol
Each day, read the topic introduction, then answer the journal prompts in writing. No skimming — journal physically. Case: Saad's original Instagram challenge had thousands of people journaling daily for 28 days. The act of writing externalizes thoughts that can otherwise remain comfortably vague.

### Technique 2: The Complicity Audit
For each topic, ask: "How have I done this?" Not "Do I do this?" — "How have I done this?" Assume complicity and investigate. Case: For "white fragility," don't ask "Am I fragile?" — ask "When have I demonstrated white fragility?"

### Technique 3: The Circle Discussion
Saad provides guidance for group circles: set agreements (no fixing, no saving, no advising), use a talking piece, share vulnerably. Case: "Book circles" are a core part of the workbook's design — individual work + collective processing.

### Technique 4: The Good Ancestor Frame
Before acting, ask: "Will this make me a good ancestor? Will this benefit those who come after me?" This reframes antiracism from guilt-driven to purpose-driven. Case: Saad's entire purpose statement: "My purpose is to help create change, facilitate healing, and seed new possibilities for those who will come after I am gone."

### Technique 5: The "I Notice" Check
When you feel defensive, say to yourself: "I notice I'm feeling defensive. I'm going to sit with that discomfort instead of acting on it." Case: Tone policing often comes from discomfort. Noticing it without acting is the first step.

### Technique 6: The Resource Up
Don't ask BIPOC to educate you. Saad provides extensive resources at the end of the book. The technique: look it up. Google it. Read the books. "How have you managed not to know?" (DiAngelo).

### Technique 7: The Commitment Covenant
At the end of 28 days, write specific, actionable commitments. Not "I'll be less racist" — specific: "I will call out racist jokes in my family. I will read one book per month by a Black author. I will donate to this organization." Case: Specific commitments are measurable and accountable.
