# Core Framework — Me and White Supremacy

## Foreword by Robin DiAngelo

"All right! You say. I get it! Now what do I do?" DiAngelo notes this is the #1 question she gets from white people. The problem: "Most white people don't really want to know what to do about racism if it will require anything of them that is inconvenient or uncomfortable." Saad has created the road map. "This book is a gift of compassion from a brilliant Black woman."

## Part I: Welcome to the Work

Saad introduces the 28-day format. The book began as an Instagram challenge, then a free PDF downloaded by 100,000+ people. "The primary force that drives my work is a passionate desire to become a good ancestor."

**What is white supremacy?** Not just explicit racism (white hoods, slurs). "White supremacy is a system of oppression that maintains the dominance of white people and the subordination of people of color. It operates in both overt and subtle ways."

## Part II: The Work — 28 Days

**Week 1: The Basics** — Privilege, fragility, tone policing, colorblindness, exceptionalism. Journal prompts: "How have I benefited from white privilege?" "When have I demonstrated white fragility?"

**Week 2: Anti-Blackness, Stereotypes, Cultural Appropriation** — Anti-Blackness specifically, "a form of racism that is uniquely harmful to Black people." Stereotypes, cultural appropriation ("taking elements of a culture that is not your own without permission or credit").

**Week 3: Allyship** — What allyship is (action, not identity) and how to practice it effectively. "Ally is not a title you give yourself — it's a practice you engage in." Calling in vs calling out.

**Week 4: Power, Relationships, Commitments** — How power operates in relationships. Making specific, actionable commitments. "What will you do differently after these 28 days?"

## Key Concepts

**White Fragility:** "When white people experience even a small amount of racial stress, they become defensive and use a range of behaviors to restore their comfort." Coined by Robin DiAngelo.

**White Saviorism:** "The belief that white people need to 'save' BIPOC from their circumstances — often perpetuates the very systems they claim to fight."

**Tone Policing:** "Dismissing what BIPOC are saying because of the tone they use to say it." E.g., "I'd listen to you if you were calmer."

**Opting Out:** "Choosing not to engage with racism because it makes you uncomfortable." Saad: "Not choosing is also a choice."
