# Anti-Patterns — Me and White Supremacy

## The Central Error: "I'm a Good Person, So I'm Not Racist"

"White supremacy is not about good people — it's about a system." Case: "I'm a good person" is often used as a shield against examining one's own racial conditioning. Saad's framework: good intentions don't negate systemic participation.

### Mistake 1: "I don't see color"
"Colorblindness denies the reality of racial inequality." It's often used as a way to avoid talking about racism. "When you say you don't see color, you are saying you don't see me."

### Mistake 2: "White people experience racism too"
"Prejudice can happen to anyone. Racism requires systemic power." Saad distinguishes between individual prejudice (which anyone can experience) and systemic racism (which requires systemic advantage).

### Mistake 3: "You're being too sensitive"
This is tone policing — dismissing valid concerns based on the tone of delivery. "The issue is what I said, not how I said it." Case: "I'd listen to you if you were calmer."

### Mistake 4: "But I marched / donated / posted"
"Performative allyship is about looking like you care, not actually doing the work." Case: posting a black square on Instagram but not examining your own behavior.

### Mistake 5: "I feel so guilty — I can't do anything right"
"White guilt can be a form of resistance — it keeps the focus on your feelings rather than on the impact of your actions." Saad: "Guilt is not an action. Move through it."

### Mistake 6: "People of color should teach us"
"This is not the job of BIPOC." Saad provides extensive resources precisely so white people can educate themselves. "How have you managed not to know?"
