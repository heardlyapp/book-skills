# Techniques: The Totalitarian Movement

> Source: The Origins of Totalitarianism, Part Three

## The Movement Structure

Totalitarianism is not a static government — it's a permanent movement. The goal is not to solve problems but to keep moving.

> **Case: The Nazi March** (Part Three): Nazism was never satisfied. Conquering Germany wasn't enough — it needed Austria, then Czechoslovakia, then Poland. The movement must always expand or die. This endless motion is essential to totalitarianism.

> **Key takeaway**: Totalitarianism is a movement, not a government. Its goal is perpetual motion, not stability.

## Propaganda and Organization

Totalitarian movements use propaganda to attract followers and organization to dominate them.

> **Case: The SS and the Party** (Part Three): The Nazi party had two structures: the public propaganda machine (attracting mass support) and the secret SS (creating elite terror). Both were necessary.

> **Key takeaway**: Propaganda attracts the masses. Organization (police, camps, party) dominates them. Both are essential.

## The Role of Loneliness

Loneliness — not isolation — is the deepest root of totalitarianism. Isolated people can't act together. Lonely people are desperate for any belonging.

> **Case: The Mass Man** (Part Three): The person who feels they belong nowhere, who has no relationships in which they matter, is the perfect candidate for totalitarian belonging.

> **Key takeaway**: Loneliness is the deepest condition for totalitarianism. It makes people give up independent thought for the security of the movement.
