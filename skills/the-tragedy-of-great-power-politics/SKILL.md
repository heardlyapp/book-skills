## Philosophy — 5 rules to remember

1. **The international system is anarchic.** No world government.
2. **All great powers have offensive capability.** Every state can threaten others.
3. **No state can be certain of others' intentions.** Trust is impossible.
4. **Survival is the primary goal.** Everything else is secondary.
5. **To survive, be the most powerful.** Great powers seek hegemony.

## Core Framework Quick Reference

- **Offensive Realism** = Great powers maximize power to guarantee survival.
- **Anarchy** = No world government — states must help themselves.
- **Security Dilemma** = One state's security threatens others.
- **Hegemony** = Dominance of one state over a region.
- **Balance of Power** = Alliances to counter the strongest.

## Key Principles

1. **Great powers are always looking for opportunities to gain power.** Offensive realism says they don't wait to be threatened.
2. **Fear, self-help, and power maximization drive state behavior.** The system shapes behavior more than individual leaders.
3. **Regional hegemony is the best any power can achieve.** Global hegemony is impossible due to the "stopping power of water."
4. **Bipolarity is most stable; multipolarity is most dangerous.** Two superpowers can balance each other; many create miscalculation.
5. **The rising power will challenge the dominant one.** The US-China relationship is the defining case of our time.
6. **Cooperation is limited and temporary.** States cooperate only when it serves their interests.
