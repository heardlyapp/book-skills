# Author's Voice + Key Scenarios

> **Case: Mearsheimer's Career** (Introduction): John Mearsheimer is one of the most influential international relations scholars alive. His offensive realism theory is taught in universities worldwide. He is known for his controversial predictions about Ukraine and China.
> **Key takeaway**: Mearsheimer is not a journalist or policy maker — he's a theorist. His power is in providing a framework for understanding events, not predicting them.

> **Case: The China Prediction** (Conclusion): Mearsheimer predicts that China will attempt to become a regional hegemon in Asia and the US will resist it. This will lead to intense security competition and potentially war. This is not a prediction about Chinese leaders — it's a prediction about structural pressures.
> **Key takeaway**: The most important geopolitical relationship of our time is the US-China rivalry. Mearsheimer's framework suggests this will intensify, regardless of diplomacy or goodwill.

## Key Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Understanding Geopolitics
View international events through the lens of structural pressures, not just leadership.

### Scenario 2: Analyzing US-China Relations
Expect intense competition. Cooperation will be limited and temporary.

### Scenario 3: NATO and Russia
NATO expansion was perceived as a threat by Russia. Conflict was predictable.

### Scenario 4: The Future of War
Great power war is not a thing of the past. The system still produces the same pressures.
