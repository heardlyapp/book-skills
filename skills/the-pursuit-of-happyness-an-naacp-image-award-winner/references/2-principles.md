# Principles: Fatherhood and Persistence

> Source: The Pursuit of Happyness, Chapters 4-7

## Fatherhood as Fuel

Gardner's son Christopher was his reason to keep going. He was determined to be the father he never had.

> **Case: The Fatherless Boy** (Chapter 4): Gardner's father was absent. He vowed his son would never feel that abandonment. Every night in a shelter, every cold call, every rejection — he did it all for Christopher.

> **Key takeaway**: Love for his child was Gardner's superpower. Purpose is more powerful than any obstacle.

## The Training Program

Gardner got into a stockbroker training program at Dean Witter. The problem: it paid nothing during training, and he was homeless.

> **Case: Working for Free** (Chapter 5): Gardner arrived at Dean Witter every day in the same suit, with a smile, making cold calls. No one knew he'd slept in a train station. He worked harder than anyone because he had to.

> **Key takeaway**: Gardner's persistence was unmatched. He cold-called harder, worked longer, and refused to quit because quitting was not an option.

## Asking for Help

Gardner accepted help from shelters, churches, and strangers. He never let pride stand in the way of survival.

> **Case: The Shelter System** (Chapter 6): Gardner learned the system of homeless shelters. He'd race across town after work to secure a bed for the night. He never complained.

> **Key takeaway**: Pride is not worth more than survival. Ask for help. Accept help. There is no shame in struggle.
