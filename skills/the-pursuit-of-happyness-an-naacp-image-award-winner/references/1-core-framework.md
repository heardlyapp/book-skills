# Core Framework: Gardner's Journey

> Source: The Pursuit of Happyness, Prologue and Chapters 1-3

## The Starting Point

Chris Gardner grew up in poverty in Milwaukee. His father was absent. His mother taught him to believe in himself.

> **Case: The Mother's Gift** (Chapter 1): Gardner's mother, Bettye Jean, read him Langston Hughes' "Mother to Son" — "Life for me ain't been no crystal stair." Those words became his anthem. She told him he could be anything. This belief from one person was the foundation of everything.

> **Key takeaway**: One person's belief can change a life. Gardner's mother planted the seed of possibility.

## The Ferrari Decision

Gardner saw a man in a red Ferrari and asked two questions that changed his life.

> **Case: The Two Questions** (Chapter 3): Instead of envying the man, Gardner asked: "What do you do?" and "How do I get into that?" The man was a stockbroker at Dean Witter. Gardner had no college degree, no finance background — but he now had a direction. That one conversation opened the door.

> **Key takeaway**: Ask the right questions. Gardner changed his entire future with two simple questions.

## The Homeless Period

Gardner's wife left him. He was broke, alone with his toddler son, and soon homeless.

> **Case: The Subway Shelter** (Prologue): Gardner slept with his son in a locked bathroom at a train station. He held him through the night, telling him stories to keep him calm. He never let his son know how desperate their situation was.

> **Key takeaway**: Even in the lowest moment, Gardner kept his dignity and protected his son. He refused to let despair win.
