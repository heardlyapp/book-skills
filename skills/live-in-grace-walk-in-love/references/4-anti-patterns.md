# 4 — Anti-Patterns

> Source: "Live in Grace, Walk in Love" by Bob Goff

## Anti-Pattern 1: Performance Christianity

**The mistake:** Thinking you need to earn God's love through good behavior, church attendance, or spiritual disciplines.

## Anti-Pattern 2: Fear-Based Faith

**The mistake:** Serving God out of fear rather than love. Fear is not a healthy motivator.

## Anti-Pattern 3: Spiritual Isolation

**The mistake:** Trying to grow spiritually alone. Faith is meant to be lived in community.

> **Case: The Lonely Christian** (Throughout): Goff describes the Christian who goes to church but has no real relationships. The devotional is a daily reminder: love requires others. You can't be a Christian in isolation.
> **Key takeaway**: If your faith has no community, it's incomplete. Find people to walk with.

> **Case: The Checklist Christian** (Devotion on Legalism): Goff talks about the person who reads their Bible, prays, goes to church, gives to the poor — but their heart is far from God. The checklist has replaced the relationship.
> **Key takeaway**: Spiritual habits are not the goal. They are the means to the goal: a relationship with God. If you're checking boxes but not connecting, you've missed the point.
