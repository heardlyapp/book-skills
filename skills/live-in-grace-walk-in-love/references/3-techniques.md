# 3 — Love in Action

> Source: "Live in Grace, Walk in Love" by Bob Goff

## Love Is a Verb

Goff's signature message: love is not a feeling. It's something you do. Every devotion ends with an action step because reading without doing is incomplete.

## Practical Ways to Love

- Call someone who's lonely
- Forgive someone who hasn't asked for it
- Give without expecting anything back
- Listen more than you speak
- Show up when it's inconvenient

> **Case: The Hard Conversation** (Throughout): Multiple devotions describe Goff's practice of having hard conversations — with people he disagreed with, people who hurt him, people he had wronged. His method: show up, tell the truth, ask for forgiveness, offer grace.
> **Key takeaway**: The most loving thing you can do is often the hardest. Don't avoid the hard conversation. It's where love grows.

> **Case: The Unsent Letter** (Devotion on Forgiveness): Goff describes writing a letter to someone who wronged him, then not sending it. The act of writing was enough to release the bitterness. He didn't need the other person to respond. He needed to let go.
> **Key takeaway**: Forgiveness is for you, not for them. You don't need their apology to release your resentment.
