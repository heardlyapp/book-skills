# 1 — Core Framework

> Source: *Pedagogy of the Oppressed* by Paulo Freire — Chapters 1-2.

## The Two Frameworks

### Framework A: The Oppressor-Oppressed Contradiction (Chapter 1)

Freire's foundational insight: the world is divided between those who oppress and those who are oppressed. The oppressors dehumanize both themselves and the oppressed. The oppressed, having internalized the oppressor, are caught in a duality — they are themselves and the image of their oppressor simultaneously. Liberation is not a gift but a painful childbirth.

> **Case: The Peasant Who Became Overseer** (Chapter 1): "It is a rare peasant who, once 'promoted' to overseer, does not become more of a tyrant towards his former comrades than the owner himself." The oppressed, having the oppressor within them, replicate the oppressive structure. They do not seek liberation — they seek to become the oppressor. This is the "tragic dilemma of the oppressed."
> **Key takeaway**: The oppressed are not simply victims waiting to be liberated. They carry their oppressor within them. True liberation requires recognizing and ejecting this internalized oppressor.

### Framework B: The Banking Model vs. Problem-Posing Education (Chapter 2)

Freire's critique of traditional education: "Education is suffering from narration sickness." The teacher narrates, the student listens. Knowledge is a deposit. The more thoroughly students are filled, the better they are. This is not accidental — it is designed to produce passive, adaptable citizens who accept the world as it is.

**The banking model's characteristics:**
- The teacher teaches, the students are taught
- The teacher knows everything, the students know nothing
- The teacher thinks, the students are thought about
- The teacher talks, the students listen
- The teacher acts, the students have the illusion of acting
- The teacher chooses, the students comply

**The alternative — problem-posing education:**
- Students become critical co-investigators in dialogue with the teacher
- Both are Subjects who know and act upon the world
- Reality is presented as a process of transformation, not a static given
- "People educate each other through the mediation of the world"

> **Case: The Illiterate Peasant's Awakening** (Chapter 1, Preface): Freire describes peasants who, after learning to read through his method, said: "I now realize I am a person, an educated person." "We were blind, now our eyes have been opened." "Before this, words meant nothing to me; now they speak to me and I can make them speak." The difference was not literacy in itself — it was the realization that they could name their own world.
> **Key takeaway**: The banking model teaches people that knowledge belongs to experts. Problem-posing education teaches people that knowledge belongs to them. "To name the world is to transform it."
