# 3 — Techniques

> Source: *Pedagogy of the Oppressed* by Paulo Freire — Chapters 3-4.

## Practical Tools

### 1. The Generative Theme Investigation

Investigate the contradictions in people's concrete reality — not abstract problems.

### 2. Codification

Present a situation from their lives. Ask: What do you see? Why is this happening? Who benefits? Who suffers?

### 3. The Dialogue Circle

Replace lectures with circles. Educator is coordinator. Content emerges from the group.

> **Case: The Peasant Who Wanted the Educator to Explain First** (Chapter 1): "Why don't you explain the pictures first? That way it'll take less time and won't give us a headache." Banking model conditioning. The educator refused to shortcut. The peasant eventually spoke his own word.
> **Key takeaway**: Resist the pressure to "explain." The process must be slow.

> **Case: The Sixteen-Year-Old Who Understood** (Introduction by Donaldo Macedo): Freire gave a chapter of Pedagogy of the Oppressed to an African American student at Harvard. She gave it to her sixteen-year-old son. He read the whole chapter overnight. In the morning he said: "I want to meet the man who wrote this. He is talking about me."
> **Key takeaway**: Oppressed people recognize Freire's analysis immediately regardless of education level. The language is not difficult for those who have lived it.

### 4. The Counter-Codification

After analyzing a situation, imagine an alternative. Move from critique to creation.

### 5. The Praxis Check

Before action: have I reflected enough? After action: what did the action teach me?
