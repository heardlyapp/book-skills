# 4 — Anti-Patterns

> Source: *Pedagogy of the Oppressed* by Paulo Freire — Chapters 1, 2, 4.

## Common Errors

### 1. False Generosity

Giving to the poor while the system that creates their poverty remains unchanged is not generosity — it's maintenance.

### 2. Banking Model Education

> **Case: The Seminary Student Who Feared Freedom** (Preface): "Why deny it? I was afraid of freedom. I am no longer afraid!" The fear of freedom is the internalized oppressor resisting liberation.

> **Case: The Educator Who Couldn't Stop Lecturing** (Chapter 4 — Anti-Dialogical Matrix): The antidialogical educator uses conquest, divide and rule, manipulation, and cultural invasion. Even when claiming to be progressive, the educator who cannot engage in genuine dialogue with students is practicing domination under a new name.
> **Key takeaway**: Claiming to be progressive does not make you progressive. If you lecture at the people instead of dialoguing with them, you are oppressing them.

### 3. The False Convert

Members of the oppressor class who join the oppressed "almost always bring with them the marks of their origin."

### 4. Activism Without Reflection

"Action without reflection is pure activism." Rushing to action without understanding reinforces structures.

### 5. Verbalism Without Action

"Reflection without action is verbalism." Armchair revolutionaries change nothing.
