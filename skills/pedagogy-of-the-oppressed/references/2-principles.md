# 2 — Key Principles

> Source: *Pedagogy of the Oppressed* by Paulo Freire — Preface, Chapters 1-4.

## The 7 Principles

### 1. Education is Never Neutral

Every educational act either domesticates or liberates. No middle ground.

### 2. True Dialogue Requires Love, Humility, and Faith

> **Case: The Fearful Seminarians** (Preface): Participants feared conscientização would lead to "destructive fanaticism." A factory worker responded: "When I began this course I was naïve, and when I found out how naïve I was, I started to get critical. But this discovery hasn't made me a fanatic." Trust in the people is vindicated by experience.
> **Key takeaway**: The people are capable of critical thought. The fear that they will become fanatical is a prejudice of those who do not trust them.

> **Case: The Peasant Who Distrusted Himself** (Chapter 1): "The peasant feels inferior to the boss because the boss seems to be the only one who knows things and is able to run things." Freire observes how self-depreciation changes with the first changes in the situation of oppression: "They used to say we were unproductive because we were lazy and drunkards. All lies. Now that we are respected as men, we're going to show everyone that we were never drunkards or lazy. We were exploited!"
> **Key takeaway**: Self-depreciation is not a personality trait — it is a symptom of oppression. When the conditions change, confidence emerges.

### 3. Praxis is Reflection + Action

Reflection without action is verbalism. Action without reflection is activism.

### 4. The Oppressed Must Be Their Own Liberation

No one liberates anyone else. People liberate each other in communion.

### 5. False Generosity Perpetuates Injustice

"True generosity consists precisely in fighting to destroy the causes which nourish false charity."

### 6. Conscientização is a Painful Birth

"Liberation is thus a childbirth, and a painful one." Developing critical consciousness means confronting the oppressor within.

### 7. To Name the World is to Transform It

Those who have been silenced must find their voice.
