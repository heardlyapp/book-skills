# Core Framework: The Flash Crash Story

> Source: Flash Crash, Prologue and Chapters 1-2

## May 6, 2010

At 2:32 PM, the Dow began falling. By 3:08 PM, it dropped nearly 1,000 points — the largest intraday point decline in history. Then it recovered just as quickly. $1 trillion in market value was erased and restored in 36 minutes.

> **Case: The Waddell & Reed Order** (Chapter 1): A mutual fund placed a $4.1 billion sell order for E-mini S&P 500 futures. The algorithm was programmed to sell at any price without regard for market impact. HFT algorithms detected the selling and began selling themselves, creating a cascade that fed on itself.

> **Case: The Hot Potato Effect** (Chapter 1): As prices dropped, HFT algorithms passed contracts back and forth at lightning speed — sometimes 27,000 contracts traded in 14 seconds. This "hot potato" effect made the crash self-reinforcing.

> **Key takeaway**: The Flash Crash was a systems failure. A large order, combined with algorithmic trading strategies that all behaved the same way, created a cascading panic.

## Navinder Sarao

An unemployed computer science graduate living with his parents in Hounslow. He taught himself to trade futures from his bedroom, eventually moving markets worth trillions.

> **Case: The Arrest Scene** (Prologue): At 8 AM, FBI agents and UK police surrounded the Sarao family home. Neighbors had no idea the quiet man next door was accused of causing a global financial crisis.

> **Key takeaway**: The most powerful trader was not on Wall Street. He was a self-taught 30-something in a London suburb.
