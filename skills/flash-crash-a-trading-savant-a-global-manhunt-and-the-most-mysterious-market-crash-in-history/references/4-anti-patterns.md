# Anti-Patterns: Misunderstanding the Crash

> Source: Flash Crash, Chapters 10-12

## The Single Cause Fallacy

Believing the Flash Crash had one cause ignores the complexity of the system. It was a convergence of multiple factors.

> **Case: The Waddell & Reed Blame** (Chapter 10): Initially blamed entirely on the mutual fund's $4.1B sell order. Their order was a trigger, but without Sarao's spoofing, HFT algorithms, and fragile market structure, the same order on a different day might have gone unnoticed.

> **Case: The Sarao Blame** (Chapter 11): After Sarao's arrest, media blamed him entirely. Vaughan argues Sarao contributed to market conditions but the crash was a structural failure.

> **Key takeaway**: Complex system failures never have a single cause. Anyone saying "X caused the Flash Crash" is oversimplifying.

## Technology Solves Everything

Post-crash reforms helped but manipulation continues.

> **Case: Post-Crash Flash Events** (Chapter 12): The dollar flash crash (2011), the treasury flash crash (2014), the pound flash crash (2016). New forms of manipulation (spoofing now illegal, but quote-stuffing and other techniques continue).

> **Key takeaway**: Regulation always lags behind innovation. The system is safer but not safe.

## Sarao Was a Criminal Genius

Sarao was smart but not a mastermind. He created a simple algorithm and used it relentlessly. The real story is the system that allowed it to work for years.

> **Key takeaway**: The Flash Crash reveals system vulnerabilities, not individual villainy. Focus on structure, not personalities.
