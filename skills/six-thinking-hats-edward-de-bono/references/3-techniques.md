# Techniques: Black Hat and Yellow Hat

> Source: Six Thinking Hats, Chapters 17-28

## Black Hat Thinking

The most valuable hat. Caution, critical judgment, risk assessment. Prevents mistakes, excesses, and nonsense. The Black Hat is the foundation of Western critical thinking — but it should never be used alone. Used without Yellow and Green, it kills ideas without building anything.

> **Case: The Overuse Problem** (Chapter 20): De Bono warns that Western culture overuses the Black Hat because our educational system is built on critical thinking. A group that stays in Black Hat mode becomes destructive. The solution: force a switch to Yellow Hat or Green Hat after Black Hat.

> **Key takeaway**: The Black Hat is essential for survival but dangerous as the only thinking mode. Always pair with Yellow and Green.

## Yellow Hat Thinking

Constructive optimism. Benefits, values, positive possibilities. The Yellow Hat asks "Why will this work?" instead of "Why won't it work?" It requires logical support — it's not blind optimism but constructive positive thinking backed by reasoning.

> **Case: Speculative-Positive Thinking** (Chapter 26): Yellow Hat thinking includes speculation about future benefits. De Bono gives the example of a company considering a risky new product. Under Yellow Hat, they focus on the potential market, the unique value proposition, and the positive scenarios — not to ignore risks but to balance the Black Hat's caution.

> **Key takeaway**: Yellow Hat thinking is harder than Black Hat because finding benefits requires constructive effort, while finding problems is automatic.
