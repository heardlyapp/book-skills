# 2 — Key Principles

> Source: *Black Elk Speaks* — Chapters 1, 3, 24-25, Author's Postscript.

## The 7 Principles

### 1. A Vision is a Responsibility, Not an Entertainment

Black Elk received his Great Vision at 9. He did not tell anyone for fear of being called crazy. But the vision weighed on him. The thunder beings called him. He could not refuse. "A man must realize his vision."

### 2. The Hoop Can Be Broken — But Not Destroyed

Wounded Knee broke the Lakota hoop physically. The massacre scattered the survivors. But the vision — "the story of a mighty vision given to a man too weak to use it" — remained true.

> **Case: The Prayer on Harney Peak** (Author's Postscript): In his old age, near-blind, Black Elk climbed Harney Peak with Neihardt. He offered the pipe to the six directions and prayed: "Grandfather, Great Spirit, behold me on earth!… There is no center any longer, and the sacred tree is dead." But then he added: "Hear me, not for myself, but for my people." Even at the moment of deepest despair, he prayed not for himself.
> **Key takeaway**: The hoop may be broken, but the prayer continues.

> **Case: The Vision of the Other World** (Chapter 22): Black Elk participated in the Ghost Dance and had a vision of the Messiah — a dark-skinned being with wounds in his palms."It seems to me on thinking it over that I have seen the son of the Great Spirit himself."
> **Key takeaway**: The Lakota people integrated the Christ story into their own framework. They did not abandon their vision — they expanded it.

### 3. The Great Spirit is Above and Within

The pipe ceremony addresses the six directions as "the powers that are one Power." The sacred is immanent in the world — in every rock, river, tree.

### 4. All Life is Related

"All living things with feet or wings or roots are children of one mother and their father is one Spirit."

### 5. Truth Requires Sacrifice

Black Elk gave his sacred pipe to Neihardt. He gave his story. He knew that sharing might make him vulnerable. He did it anyway.

### 6. Silence is a Language

Black Elk often sat in long silence before speaking. The silence was not empty — it was full of preparation.

### 7. The Story Must Be Told

Black Elk: "What I know was given to me for men and it is true and it is beautiful. Soon I shall be under the grass and it will be lost." He told Neihardt because the vision was not his to keep.
