# 4 — Anti-Patterns

> Source: *Black Elk Speaks* — Chapters 3, 7, 24-25.

## Common Errors

### 1. Confusing Physical Defeat with Spiritual Death

**The trap:** "They won, so our spirit is broken."

**Why it fails:** Wounded Knee was a massacre. But Black Elk's vision — "If the vision was true and mighty, as I know, it is true and mighty yet" — survived the bullets.

### 2. Keeping the Vision for Yourself

**The trap:** "This experience is too sacred to share."

**Why it fails:** Black Elk struggled for decades alone with his vision. He shared it with Neihardt only when he realized it would be lost otherwise.

> **Case: Black Elk's Repeated Refusals** (Preface 1961): A woman came to interview Black Elk about Crazy Horse. He refused. "I can see that you are a nice-looking woman, and I can feel that you are good; but I do not care to talk to you about these things." But he felt something different when Neihardt arrived — a "strong desire to know the things of the Other World." He knew he was meant to tell his vision.
> **Key takeaway**: Discernment is not the same as secrecy. Know who you are meant to tell — but tell someone.

> **Case: The Vision in the Fog** (Chapter 3, Author's Postscript): The Great Vision was given to Black Elk, but he spent much of his life feeling he had failed it. He wept on Harney Peak. "I have fallen away and have done nothing." Yet Neihardt's book — read by millions, translated into 20 languages — was the fulfillment Black Elk could not see. The vision was not given to one man; it was given through him.
> **Key takeaway**: You may not live to see your vision fulfilled. That does not mean it has failed.

### 3. Losing the Center

**The trap:** "The hoop is broken. There is no hope."

**Why it fails:** "There is no center any longer," Black Elk prayed on Harney Peak. But Neihardt adds: the book itself became the fulfillment. The tree flowered in translation, reaching millions.

### 4. Forgetting the Relatives

The two-legged are not the only people. To ignore the four-legged, the winged, the rooted — is to break the hoop.
