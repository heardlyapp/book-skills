# Criticisms — Anti-Patterns

**Anti-Pattern 1: Dismissal Without Examination.** Mainstream scholars dismiss von Däniken without engaging with his evidence.

**Case: The Academic Response.** Most archaeologists have not read von Däniken's books but dismiss his claims as pseudoscience without addressing specific evidence.

**Case: The Circular Argument.** "It cannot be true because it does not fit our understanding of history" — this is circular reasoning, not a refutation.

**Anti-Pattern 2: Confirmation Bias.** Seeing only what supports the official narrative and ignoring evidence that contradicts it.

**Case: The Pyramid Builders.** The official story emphasizes slave labor, but evidence of skilled workers with medical care and good nutrition is available.

**Anti-Pattern 3: Ignoring the Unexplained.** The gaps in the official story are ignored rather than investigated.

**Case: The Missing Technology.** How did ancient cultures achieve precision that modern engineers cannot replicate without power tools? This question is not answered.

**Anti-Pattern 4: Ad Hominem.** Attacking the messenger rather than the message. This is a logical fallacy.

**Case: Von Däniken's Credibility.** Critics attack von Däniken personally rather than addressing his evidence. The question is not about his character but about whether his evidence supports his claims. The ad hominem fallacy avoids the real debate.
