# Reading the Evidence — Techniques

**Technique 1: Question Official Narratives.** Conventional archaeology has answers for everything. Those answers are not always correct.

**Case: Easter Island.** Official explanation: the moai were moved using logs and ropes. But the island had few trees. The statues weigh 80 tons. The official story has gaps that are not acknowledged.

**Case: The Pyramids.** Official explanation: thousands of workers dragged blocks up ramps. But no ramp traces have been found. The precision of the cuts requires tools that did not exist according to the historical record.

**Technique 2: Compare Across Cultures.** The same pattern appears everywhere: beings from the sky, advanced knowledge, worship as gods.

**Case: Global Pyramids.** Egypt, Mexico, Mesopotamia, China — pyramid structures appear in every ancient civilization. Von Däniken argues this indicates a common source of knowledge.

**Case: Global Flood Stories.** Every culture has a flood story. The Bible, the Epic of Gilgamesh, Hindu texts, Chinese legends. Von Däniken argues this points to a single real event.

**Technique 3: Read the Texts Literally.** Ancient texts describe events that sound like modern technology. Read them as descriptions of real events rather than symbolic mythology.

**Case: Ezekiel's Wheel.** "A wheel within a wheel, full of eyes round about." This could describe a spacecraft with portholes and landing gear.
