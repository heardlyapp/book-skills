**Case: The Koala. Koalas eat eucalyptus, which is toxic. Their gut microbes detoxify the poison. Without their microbes, koalas could not exist.**

**Case: The Coral. Coral reefs depend on symbiotic algae. When the partnership breaks, coral bleaches and dies.**

**Case: The Hoatzin. The hoatzin bird has a digestive system like a cow. It uses microbes to ferment leaves in its crop.**

**Case: The Gut-Brain Axis. Studies show that gut bacteria produce serotonin, dopamine, and GABA. These neurotransmitters affect mood and behavior. The gut is sometimes called the second brain.**

**Case: The Mitochondrial Origin. Mitochondria were once free-living bacteria. They were engulfed by a host cell. This symbiosis created all complex life.**

**Case: The Chloroplast. Like mitochondria, chloroplasts in plants were once free-living cyanobacteria. Photosynthesis itself began as symbiosis.**
