# Reference: The Origin Story

## Overview
Netflix was founded in 1997 by Marc Randolph and Reed Hastings. The idea came from a car ride. The business model was tested with a single CD and a $500 website.

## Case: The Car Ride
Randolph and Hastings worked together at Pure Atria, a software company. They carpooled from Santa Cruz to Silicon Valley every day. During one car ride, the idea emerged: what if you could rent movies by mail, like Amazon sold books? DVDs had just been introduced. They were small enough to mail cheaply. The idea sounded crazy. "That will never work," people said. The phrase became the book's title.

## Case: The DVD Test
Randolph bought a CD at Tower Records, put it in a paper envelope, and mailed it to his home address. The CD arrived intact. The postage was $0.32. This was the critical data point: if DVDs could be shipped for the price of a stamp, the business could work. He spent $500 on a basic website and tested the concept. The test was not about proving the business would succeed — it was about learning whether the basic assumption was correct.

## Case: The First Customers
Netflix launched on April 14, 1998, with 30 employees and 925 DVDs. The first customer was Randolph himself (he ordered a DVD to test the system). The early days were chaotic: orders were processed manually, DVDs were shipped from Randolph's garage, and the website crashed frequently. But customers loved it. The idea was not brilliant. The execution was everything.

## Case: The Name
The name "Netflix" was chosen for its simplicity. "Net" for internet, "flix" for flicks (movies). The founders wanted a name that was easy to remember, easy to spell, and suggested both internet and movies. They rejected dozens of other names before settling on Netflix.
