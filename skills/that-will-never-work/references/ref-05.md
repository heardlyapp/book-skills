# Reference: The Streaming Pivot

## Overview
The decision to shift from DVD rentals to streaming was the most important strategic decision in Netflix's history. It was also the most painful.

## Case: The Streaming Launch
Netflix launched streaming in 2007 with 1,000 movies available instantly. The selection was limited, the quality was poor, and most customers still preferred DVDs. But the decision was strategic: start early, learn fast, and improve before it becomes a competitive necessity. The early streaming service was terrible by today's standards, but it was the beginning of the future.

## Case: The Qwikster Disaster
In 2011, Netflix announced it was splitting its DVD and streaming businesses into separate companies. The DVD business would be called Qwikster. The plan was a disaster. Customers were confused and angry. They did not want to manage two separate queues, two separate websites, and two separate payments. Netflix lost 800,000 subscribers. The company's stock price collapsed. Within weeks, Netflix apologized and reversed the decision. The Qwikster episode was a painful reminder that customers care about simplicity.

## Case: Original Content
The pivot to original content (House of Cards, Orange Is the New Black) was another existential gamble. Netflix was spending $100 million on an untested series with no pilot. If it failed, the company would have wasted a huge sum and damaged its relationship with studios. House of Cards succeeded, proving that Netflix could compete with HBO and the networks. The original content strategy transformed Netflix from a distributor into a studio.
