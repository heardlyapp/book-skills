# Anti-Patterns: What Not to Do

## Overview

Palmer's book is filled with examples of bad asking — including her own.

## Anti-Pattern 1: The Apologetic Ask

"I'm so sorry to bother you, but..." This is the most common anti-pattern. The apology undermines the ask before it begins.

**Why it fails:** It signals that the asker believes they're doing something wrong. This creates discomfort for both parties.

**Case — Crowdfunding apology videos:** Friends starting Kickstarters would stammer into the camera, "We're so sorry to be asking." It made viewers uncomfortable.

**Fix:** Delete the apology. State the ask cleanly.

## Anti-Pattern 2: The Martyr Complex

Refusing to ask for help because you believe you should do everything yourself.

**Why it fails:** It isolates you. It denies others the opportunity to help. It leads to burnout.

**Case — Palmer's early career:** She worked dozens of jobs before learning that asking her community for support was okay.

**Fix:** Ask yourself: "Am I refusing to ask because I genuinely don't need help, or because I'm afraid?"

## Anti-Pattern 3: Asking Without Relationship

Cold asks — asking strangers for significant help without having built any connection.

**Why it fails:** People help people they trust. A cold ask has no trust foundation.

**Case — Kickstarter comparison:** The campaigns that succeeded were from creators who had built relationships with their communities over years. The ones that failed were from people who expected a platform to do the relationship-building for them.

**Fix:** Build the relationship before you need the ask.

## Anti-Pattern 4: The Guilt Trip

Asking in a way that implies refusal would be unkind: "I know you're busy, but..."

**Why it fails:** It manipulates. If people say yes to avoid guilt, they'll resent it.

**Fix:** Ask cleanly. Accept no graciously.

## Anti-Pattern 5: Refusing to Receive

When help is offered, refusing it. "Oh, I couldn't possibly..."

**Why it fails:** It denies both you and the giver a meaningful exchange.

**Case — Palmer's initial refusal:** She struggled to accept Neil's offer of help with the wedding. She had to learn that receiving was partnership.

**Fix:** When someone offers help, say "thank you." Let yourself receive.
