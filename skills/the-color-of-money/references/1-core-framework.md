# 1 — The Racial Wealth Gap

> Source: "The Color of Money" by Mehrsa Baradaran

## The Core Argument

The racial wealth gap was created by government policy, not by individual behavior or cultural differences. Black banks were promoted as a solution but were structurally incapable of closing the gap.

## Key Statistics

- Median white family wealth: ~$170,000
- Median Black family wealth: ~$17,000
- 10:1 ratio. Created by 400 years of discriminatory policy.

> **Case: The Freedman's Bank Collapse** (Chapter 1): The Freedman's Savings Bank was established in 1865 to help former slaves build financial security. By 1874, it had collapsed due to fraud, mismanagement, and a broader financial panic. 61,000 Black depositors lost their savings — $3 million (equivalent to $60 million today). "The destruction of the Freedman's Bank was a catastrophic blow to Black economic progress from which it has never fully recovered."
> **Key takeaway**: The first major attempt at Black banking ended in disaster — and it wasn't the fault of the depositors. The lesson: Black people learned that banks could not be trusted with their money.

> **Case: The 10:1 Ratio** (Introduction): Baradaran opens with the central fact: "White families have roughly ten times the wealth of Black families. This gap has persisted for generations." The gap is not shrinking. It is not explained by education, work ethic, or family structure. It is explained by policy.
> **Key takeaway**: Before proposing solutions, understand the scale of the problem. Small solutions cannot fix a problem this large.
