## 6 Anti-Patterns of Conventional Thinking

### 1. "The System Works — Follow It"

The system works for some people. But the extraordinary mind creates its own path. Following the default path is safe — and a recipe for mediocrity.

### 2. "Hard Work Is Enough"

Hard work on the wrong things is wasted effort. Extraordinary results come from questioning which work actually matters.

> **Case: The Corporate Grind** (Introduction): Vishen worked 60-hour weeks at his corporate job. He was working hard — on the wrong thing. The Brule said: work hard and you will be rewarded. But he was building someone else's dream, not his own.
> **Key takeaway**: Hard work is not enough. You must work on the right things — the things that align with your purpose.

### 3. "Happiness Comes from Achievement"

Happiness is not the result of achievement — it is the prerequisite. Blissipline means being happy before the achievement, not after.

### 4. "You Must Be Realistic"

Realistic thinking is safe and limited. Extraordinary achievement requires "unrealistic" vision.

### 5. "Beliefs Are Fixed"

Beliefs can be changed. Consciousness engineering proves that we can choose what we believe. The idea that beliefs are permanent is itself a belief — and it is a limiting one.

### 6. "You Need Permission"

Who gave you permission to live the life you want? If the answer is no one — you are waiting for someone else to authorize your life. Extraordinary people authorize themselves.

> **Case: The Meditation Skeptic** (Chapter 6): Vishen was initially skeptical of meditation. He thought it was a waste of time. But after trying it consistently, he found it was the single most transformative practice in his life. The belief that "meditation is for monks, not entrepreneurs" was a Brule he had to overcome.
> **Key takeaway**: The most powerful practices often look unproductive. Judging a practice by its appearance is a Brule.
