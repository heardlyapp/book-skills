## Practical Techniques

### 1. The Brule Audit

List your most deeply held beliefs about career, money, relationships, and health. For each, ask: "Where did I learn this? Does it still serve me? Is it true, or was I told it was true?"

### 2. The Gratitude Reset

When feeling down, list three things you are grateful for — specific, concrete things. Do this daily, not just when you feel like it. Blissipline is the discipline of doing it anyway.

> **Case: The Morning Practice** (Chapter 7): Vishen writes that his non-negotiable morning practice is: 1) Six-phase meditation, 2) Gratitude listing, 3) Reading for 20 minutes. This set of systems for living creates the foundation for an extraordinary day.
> **Key takeaway**: Systems are more reliable than willpower. Build the morning practice into a non-negotiable routine.

### 3. The Fear-Setting Exercise

Write down the worst possible outcome of a decision you are afraid to make. Then ask: "Could I survive that?" The answer is almost always yes.

### 4. The Vision Canvas

Create a vision for your life 5-10 years out. Do not limit yourself by what seems "realistic." Let the vision excite you. Then set goals that serve the vision.

> **Case: The Mindvalley Vision** (Chapter 8): Vishen started Mindvalley with a vision of transforming global education. He did not know how he would get there. He just knew the vision excited him more than anything else. The vision pulled him forward.
> **Key takeaway**: A compelling vision is more powerful than a detailed plan. Plan from vision, not from fear.
