# Techniques: Practical Brain-Healthy Eating

> Source: This Is Your Brain on Food, Chapters 5-11

## The Mediterranean Eating Pattern

Practical steps: eat vegetables with every meal, include fatty fish 2-3 times per week, use olive oil as your main cooking fat, snack on nuts and seeds, limit red meat to a few times per month. The pattern is more important than any single food.

> **Case: The MIND Diet** (Chapter 6): The Mediterranean-DASH Intervention for Neurodegenerative Delay. Combines the best of both diets into one practical eating pattern. In studies, strict adherence to MIND reduced Alzheimer's risk by 53%. Even moderate adherence reduced it by 35%. This is one of the most powerful dietary interventions for brain health.

> **Key takeaway**: The MIND diet is the most practical eating pattern for lifelong brain health. It's easier to follow than strict Mediterranean or DASH alone.

## ADHD and Elimination Diets

Some individuals with ADHD respond to eliminating gluten and casein (milk protein). Not everyone benefits, but it's worth a trial.

> **Case: The Gluten-ADHD Connection** (Chapter 5): Studies show higher rates of celiac disease and gluten sensitivity in people with ADHD. A gluten-free trial of 3-4 weeks is a reasonable diagnostic test. Polyphenols from berries and dark chocolate also support focus.

## Cooking for Brain Health

Naidoo's practical advice: eat a colorful plate (different colors = different polyphenols), use herbs and spices (turmeric, rosemary, ginger are anti-inflammatory), and include fermented foods daily.

> **Key takeaway**: The most practical rule: eat 5-7 different colors of plant foods each day. Each color provides different brain-protective compounds.
