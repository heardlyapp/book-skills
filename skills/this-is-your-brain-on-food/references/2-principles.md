# Principles: Depression, Anxiety, and Targeted Nutrition

> Source: This Is Your Brain on Food, Chapters 2-4

## Depression and the Mediterranean Diet

Depression responds strongly to dietary change. Three key nutrients: omega-3 fatty acids (reduce inflammation in the brain), probiotics (support serotonin production in the gut), and B vitamins (support neurotransmitter synthesis).

> **Case: The SMILES Trial** (Chapter 2): The landmark study on diet and depression. Participants with major depression who followed a modified Mediterranean diet for 12 weeks had significantly greater improvement than those who received only social support. One of the first randomized controlled trials showing diet can treat depression.

> **Key takeaway**: Fatty fish (salmon, sardines), leafy greens, olive oil, nuts, and fermented foods should be staples for anyone with depression.

## Anxiety and Fermented Foods

Anxiety responds to fermented foods (which contain probiotics), magnesium (relaxes the nervous system), zinc (supports neurotransmitter function), and dietary fiber (stabilizes blood sugar).

> **Case: The Fermented Food Study** (Chapter 3): Participants who ate more fermented foods had lower social anxiety. The link: fermented foods support a healthy microbiome, which produces GABA — the brain's main inhibitory neurotransmitter that calms neural activity.

## PTSD and Blueberries

PTSD involves inflammation and glutamate excitotoxicity. Blueberries are rich in polyphenols that reduce inflammation and protect against glutamate damage.

> **Case: Blueberry Neuroprotection** (Chapter 4): Animal studies show that blueberry supplementation reduces the behavioral effects of trauma exposure. The anthocyanins in blueberries cross the blood-brain barrier and concentrate in brain regions involved in memory and emotion.
