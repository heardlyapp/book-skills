# Voice and Application: Naidoo's Approach

> Source: This Is Your Brain on Food, throughout

## Naidoo's Voice

Dr. Uma Naidoo is a Harvard-trained psychiatrist and professional chef. She writes as a clinician who cooks — scientific but accessible, evidence-based but practical. Her central message: food is medicine for the mind, and the kitchen is a pharmacy.

## Application: Depression

Start with the Mediterranean diet. Add omega-3s (fatty fish, walnuts, flaxseeds). Include probiotics (yogurt, kefir, kimchi). Consider supplementing vitamin D and B vitamins. The SMILES trial showed diet alone can significantly improve depression.

## Application: Anxiety

Increase fermented foods (kimchi, sauerkraut, kombucha). Add magnesium-rich foods (leafy greens, pumpkin seeds, dark chocolate). Balance blood sugar with fiber and protein at every meal.

## Application: Brain Fog

Follow the MIND diet which combines Mediterranean and DASH approaches. Increase polyphenols from berries and dark chocolate. Reduce processed foods and sugar. The MIND diet reduced Alzheimer's risk by 53% in studies.

## Application: ADHD

Consider a gluten-free trial for 3-4 weeks. Increase polyphenols from berries. Reduce artificial colors and food additives. The MIND diet is the best starting point.

## Application: PTSD

Add blueberries and other polyphenol-rich foods. Reduce glutamate excitotoxicity through anti-inflammatory eating. Support the microbiome with prebiotics and probiotics.
