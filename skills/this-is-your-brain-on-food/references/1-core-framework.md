# Core Framework: The Gut-Brain Axis

> Source: This Is Your Brain on Food, Introduction & Chapter 1

## The Gut-Brain Connection

The gut and brain are connected through three pathways: the vagus nerve (physical connection), neurotransmitters (chemical signaling), and the immune system (inflammation). The gut produces 90-95% of the body's serotonin — the "feel-good" neurotransmitter. When the gut is inflamed or its microbiome is disrupted, the brain suffers.

> **Case: The Vagus Nerve** (Chapter 1): The vagus nerve runs from the brainstem through the neck, chest, and into the abdomen. It's the main physical highway between the two brains. Signals travel both directions — an upset gut sends stress signals to the brain, and anxiety sends stress signals to the gut. This is why anxious people often have digestive problems.

> **Case: The Microbiome** (Chapter 1): Each person has a unique microbial fingerprint. The balance of good vs bad bacteria directly affects mood, cognition, and stress response. Diet is the fastest way to shift the microbiome — microbial changes happen within 24 hours of changing what you eat.

## The Mediterranean Foundation

The Mediterranean eating pattern — vegetables, fruits, whole grains, fish, olive oil, nuts — is the most studied diet for mental health. Large-scale studies show it reduces depression risk by 25-35%. It's the foundation for all other recommendations.

> **Key takeaway**: Start with the Mediterranean diet. Add targeted nutrients for specific conditions. That's the entire framework in one sentence.
