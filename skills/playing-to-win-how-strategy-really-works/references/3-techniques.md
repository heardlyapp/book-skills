# Techniques: Capabilities and Systems

> Source: Playing to Win, Chapters 5-6

## The Capability Question

"What capabilities must we have to win where we've chosen to play?" This forces strategy into concrete organizational terms.

> **Case: P&G's Consumer Understanding Capability** (Chapter 5): P&G invested deeply in understanding consumers — not just surveys but ethnography. Researchers lived with families, watched how they cleaned, bathed, and cared for babies. This capability directly enabled their HTW of superior innovation driven by consumer insight.

> **Case: The Innovation Capability** (Chapter 5): P&G developed a systematic innovation capability under "Connect + Develop" — sourcing ideas from outside as well as inside. This capability was deliberately built over years, not acquired overnight.

> **Key takeaway**: Capabilities are the bridge between strategy and execution. Without the right capabilities, the best strategy is just a wish.

## Management Systems

Structure, incentives, metrics, and processes must reinforce the strategy.

> **Case: The Category Management System** (Chapter 6): P&G restructured around categories (laundry, beauty, baby care) instead of functions (marketing, R&D, sales). Each category had a CEO-like leader with P&L responsibility. This system aligned structure with the strategic logic flow — each category could make its own WTP/HTW choices.

> **Case: The OGSM Tool** (Chapter 6): Objectives, Goals, Strategies, Measures — P&G's system for cascading strategy through the organization. It translated high-level strategic choices into specific goals and measures for every team.

> **Key takeaway**: Management systems deliver strategy. If systems conflict with strategy, the systems win. Align them or change them.
