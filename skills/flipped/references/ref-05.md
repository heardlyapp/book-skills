# Reference: Family

## Overview
The contrast between Juli's family and Bryce's family is essential to the novel.

## Case: The Bakers
Juli's family is warm, creative, and accepting. Her father is an artist who paints landscapes. Her mother works hard to support the family. They have an uncle with special needs. They love each other.

## Case: The Loskis
Bryce's family is cold and critical. His father is the dominant figure. He is narrow-minded and emotionally abusive. The mother is gentle but powerless. The home is tense.

## Case: The Father's Influence
Bryce's father influences how Bryce sees the world. Bryce must overcome his father's example to become his own person.

## Case: Grandpa Duncan
The grandfather moves in with the Loskis. He is the moral center. He teaches Bryce to see Juli's value. He is the bridge between the two families.

## Case: Uncle David
Juli's uncle has special needs. The family cares for him. Juli learns compassion from watching her parents. She does not judge people who are different.

## Case: The Contrast
The Bakers have less money but more love. The Loskis have more money but less warmth. The novel suggests that the quality of family life is not measured by material wealth.

## Case: The Growth
Bryce learns to question his father's values. He chooses Grandpa Duncan's wisdom over his father's narrowness. This is how he becomes worthy of Juli.
Flipped argues that the family you come from shapes who you become — but you can choose to become something different.
