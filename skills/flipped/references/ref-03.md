# Reference: The Sycamore Tree

## Overview
The sycamore tree is the novel's central symbol. It represents perspective, beauty, and loss.

## Case: Juli's Refuge
Juli discovers the sycamore tree when she is young. She climbs it. From the top, she can see the whole town. She stays there for hours.

## Case: The View
From the tree, Juli sees the world differently. The town is not just buildings. It is a whole. She understands "the whole being greater than the sum of its parts" from the tree.

## Case: The Fight
The tree is scheduled to be cut down. Juli sits in it to protect it. She refuses to come down. The police are called. She is not enough to save it.

## Case: The Loss
When the tree is cut, Juli loses something essential. She loses her perspective. She loses her place of peace. She loses her innocence.

## Case: The Sapling
At the end, Bryce plants a new sycamore in Juli's yard. It is a promise. It is a symbol of hope. The tree will grow again.

## Case: The Symbol
The sycamore tree is Juli's way of seeing. Without it, she must find another way to understand the world. The tree is not just a tree — it is her perspective.

## Case: The New Beginning
Bryce's sycamore sapling is his apology and his declaration. He has finally understood what Juli values. He plants it so she can see again.
The sycamore tree is the novel's most powerful image. It represents the view from above — the ability to see the whole, not just the parts.
When Juli loses the tree, she loses her perspective. When Bryce plants a new one, he gives it back.
