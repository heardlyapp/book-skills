# Reference: Bryce Loski

## Overview
Bryce Loski is the other narrator. He begins as a boy who cannot see Juli clearly. His arc is about learning to see.

## Case: The Beginning
Bryce wants Juli to leave him alone. He is embarrassed by her attention. He does not understand her. He is a typical boy who wants to fit in.

## Case: The Father
Bryce's father is critical and narrow-minded. He disapproves of the Bakers. Bryce absorbs his father's prejudices without thinking.

## Case: The Eggs
Bryce throws away Juli's eggs because his father says they may be unsanitary. This is a betrayal. Juli finds out. It changes everything.

## Case: The Grandfather
Grandpa Duncan moves in. He tells Bryce about Juli's true value. He is the first person who helps Bryce see clearly.

## Case: The Flip
Bryce's perspective on Juli finally changes. He begins to see her as extraordinary. The boy who wanted her to leave him alone now wants her attention.

## Case: The Basketball Auction
Bryce is auctioned in the Basket Boys fundraiser. Juli does not buy him. He is devastated. For the first time, he realizes he wants her.

## Case: The Sycamore Tree
At the end, Bryce plants a sycamore sapling in Juli's yard. This is his declaration. He has finally understood what she values.
Bryce's growth is the heart of the novel. He starts as a typical boy and ends as someone who can see what is truly valuable. That is the flip.
The boy who dove under now wants to be seen. That is the arc of Bryce Loski.
Bryce Loski: the boy who learned to flip.
