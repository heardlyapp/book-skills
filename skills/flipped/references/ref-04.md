# Reference: Perspective

## Overview
The novel's most important theme is perspective. The same events look different from Juli's side and Bryce's side.

## Case: The Dual Narrative
Each chapter alternates between Juli's voice and Bryce's voice. The reader sees the same story from two perspectives. The truth is somewhere between.

## Case: The First Interaction
Juli remembers meeting Bryce as romantic. Bryce remembers it as awkward. They experienced the same moment completely differently.

## Case: The Eggs
Juli gives eggs as a gesture of friendship. Bryce sees them as a burden. He throws them away. The same act means different things to different people.

## Case: The Flip
"Flipped" means the moment when everything you thought about someone reverses. Juli flips on Bryce. Bryce flips on Juli. The timing is everything.

## Case: The Basket Auction
Juli decides not to bid on Bryce. Bryce expects her to. The moment reverses their dynamic. Bryce, for the first time, realizes he wants her attention.

## Case: The Whole
The phrase "the whole is greater than the sum of its parts" applies to how we see people. You cannot judge someone by individual traits. You must see the whole person.

## Case: The Ending
When Bryce plants the sycamore, Juli sees him differently. He has finally understood. The flip has happened. They are seeing each other clearly for the first time.
The novel's structure — two narrators, two perspectives — is its argument: you cannot understand another person until you are willing to see from their side.
Flipped is about the hardest thing in the world: seeing someone clearly.
