# Reference: Juli Baker

## Overview
Juli Baker is one of YA literature's most memorable heroines. She is passionate, independent, and unafraid to be herself.

## Case: The Sycamore Tree
Juli loves the sycamore tree. She sits in it for hours, watching the world from above. She fights the men who try to cut it down. When it is gone, she is devastated.

## Case: The Chickens
Juli raises chickens in her backyard. She sells the eggs. She gives eggs to the Loskis. She is responsible, entrepreneurial, and independent.

## Case: The Scientist
Juli has a scientific mind. She conducts experiments. She thinks systematically. She is not the typical romantic heroine — she is a thinker.

## Case: The Unconventional Girl
Juli is not like other girls. She does not care what people think. She climbs trees in dresses. She speaks her mind. She is herself completely.

## Case: The Growth
Juli learns that love requires more than feeling. It requires seeing the whole person. The whole that is greater than the sum of its parts.

## Case: The Determination
When Juli decides something, she does not give up. She decided to love the sycamore tree. She decided to raise chickens. She decided to protect the tree from the chainsaw. Her determination is her defining trait.

## Case: The Love
Juli's love for Bryce is real but not blind. Over time, she begins to see him clearly. She realizes that the boy she loved may not be the boy she thought he was. This is the "flip."
Juli Baker is the kind of girl who will change the world — not by being what the world wants, but by being exactly who she is.
