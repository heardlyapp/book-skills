# Reference: Pope Francis's Vision

## Overview
Pope Francis made mercy the defining theme of his pontificate. From his first Angelus address to the declaration of the Jubilee Year of Mercy, he has consistently called the Church to prioritize mercy over judgment.

## Case: The First Homily
In his first homily as Pope (March 14, 2013), Francis said: "The Lord never tires of forgiving. It is we who tire of asking for forgiveness." This sentence became the motto of his papacy. He has repeated it countless times. It captures his conviction that the Church's primary mission is not to condemn the world but to announce God's inexhaustible mercy.

## Case: The Jubilee Year of Mercy
On December 8, 2015, Pope Francis opened the Holy Door of St. Peter's Basilica, proclaiming an extraordinary Jubilee Year of Mercy. During this year, he encouraged priests to prioritize the sacrament of reconciliation, opened "Doorways of Mercy" in prisons and hospitals, and sent out "Missionaries of Mercy" — priests authorized to forgive sins normally reserved to the Holy See.

## Case: The Confessor Pope
Francis is known for his own practice of confession (he confesses every two weeks) and his emphasis on the sacrament. He has said that confession is not a "dry cleaning" that removes stains but an encounter with God's love that transforms the heart. He revolutionized the practice by allowing priests to forgive the sin of abortion (normally reserved to bishops) during the Jubilee Year.

## Case: The Field Hospital
Pope Francis often says the Church should be a "field hospital after battle" — tending the wounded rather than guarding the gates. This image shapes everything: the Church must go to the margins, seek out those who have been hurt or excluded, and offer healing before doctrine. Mercy is not a program; it is the Church's fundamental orientation toward the world.
