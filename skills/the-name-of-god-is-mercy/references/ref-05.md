# Reference: Mercy in Action

## Overview
Mercy is not an abstract concept — it must be lived. Pope Francis calls every Christian to practice the works of mercy, which translate God's love into concrete action.

## Case: The Corporal Works of Mercy
The traditional seven corporal works of mercy are drawn from Matthew 25 — Jesus's teaching that whatever we do to the least of our brothers and sisters, we do to Him. Feed the hungry, give drink to the thirsty, clothe the naked, shelter the homeless, visit the sick, visit the imprisoned, bury the dead. Francis emphasizes that these are not optional acts of charity but essential expressions of faith. How we treat the poor is how we treat Christ.

## Case: The Spiritual Works of Mercy
The spiritual works of mercy address the needs of the soul: counsel the doubtful, instruct the ignorant, admonish sinners, comfort the afflicted, forgive offenses, bear wrongs patiently, pray for the living and the dead. Francis notes that these are often harder than the corporal works because they require emotional and spiritual investment. It is easier to give money than to listen patiently to someone in distress.

## Case: Mercy to the Imprisoned
Pope Francis has made visiting prisoners a hallmark of his papacy. On Holy Thursday 2013, he washed the feet of young prisoners in a juvenile detention center. He has visited prisons in Rome, Philadelphia, and Bolivia. He argues that prisons should be places of rehabilitation, not punishment, and that the dignity of prisoners must be respected. A society is measured by how it treats its most marginalized members — including those who have broken the law.
