# Anti-Patterns · Why Checklists Fail

> Source: *The Checklist Manifesto*, Chapters 6-8

---

## 1. The Checklist is Too Long

The #1 killer of checklists. If it doesn't fit on one page, it won't be used. Every item must earn its place.

**Fix:** Cut mercilessly. Only keep what would be catastrophic to miss.

## 2. Wrong Type of Checklist

Using DO-CONFIRM when READ-DO is needed (or vice versa). A READ-DO checklist for a routine procedure slows everyone down. A DO-CONFIRM for a crisis misses the point.

**Fix:** Match the type to the situation. READ-DO for unfamiliar/crisis. DO-CONFIRM for routine/check.

## 3. No Real-World Testing

A checklist designed in a conference room has never been tested under pressure. It will fail.

**Fix:** Test in the field. Revise. Repeat.

## 4. The "I Don't Need This" Mentality

Experts often resist checklists because they feel the steps are obvious. But that's exactly when checklists are most valuable — catching the obvious thing you missed under pressure.

## 5. Ignoring Team Communication

A checklist is only as good as the team using it. If the culture doesn't support pausing to check, the checklist is useless.

**Fix:** Make pausing for the checklist a ritual. The most senior person should model stopping to check.
