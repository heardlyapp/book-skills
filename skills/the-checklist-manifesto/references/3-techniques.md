# Techniques · Applying Checklists in Practice

> Source: *The Checklist Manifesto*, Chapters 2, 5, 7

---

## Aviation: The Model

Aviation pioneered the modern checklist. Before takeoff, pilots use a READ-DO checklist. After engine start, a DO-CONFIRM. Every critical phase has a corresponding checklist.

**Lesson for other fields:** Start with the highest-risk moments. What are the "takeoffs and landings" of your work?

## Surgery: The Proof (Chapters 5, 7)

The WHO Surgical Safety Checklist:
1. **Before anesthesia** — patient identity, procedure site, equipment check
2. **Before incision** — team introductions, anticipated critical events
3. **Before leaving OR** — instrument count, specimen labeling, post-op plan

**Results from 8 hospitals worldwide:**
- Major complications: reduced by 36%
- Deaths: reduced by 47%
- The improvements held across all settings, from rich to poor countries

## Construction: The Master Builder (Chapter 3)

Construction uses "master builder" checklists to coordinate hundreds of subcontractors. Each morning, the foreman runs through a brief checklist of who does what that day — preventing miscommunication that could lead to structural failure.

## Your Personal/Team Implementation

**Step 1:** Identify the highest-risk, most error-prone part of your work.
**Step 2:** Draft a DO-CONFIRM checklist for routine steps.
**Step 3:** Draft a READ-DO checklist for unfamiliar or crisis situations.
**Step 4:** Test it in real conditions for one week.
**Step 5:** Revise based on what you missed or what was unnecessary.
