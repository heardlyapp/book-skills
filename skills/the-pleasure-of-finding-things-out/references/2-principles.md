# Key Principles

## 1. Science Is a Method, Not a Body of Facts

"Science is a way of trying not to fool yourself. The first principle is that you must not fool yourself — and you are the easiest person to fool."

## 2. Doubt Is a Feature, Not a Bug

"If you're not uncertain about everything you know, you're not doing science. Scientific knowledge is always provisional, always open to revision."

## 3. Nature Is the Final Authority

"No matter how beautiful your theory, no matter how smart you are, if it doesn't match experiment, it's wrong."

> **Case: The Beauty Trap** (Chapter 5): Feynman describes how physicists often fall in love with elegant mathematical theories. But nature doesn't care about elegance. The most beautiful theory can be wrong if it doesn't match observation. Physics is not about what we think should be true — it's about what is.
> **Key takeaway**: Beauty and elegance are not evidence. Nature is the only judge that matters. A homely theory that matches data beats a beautiful one that doesn't.

## 4. Curiosity Is the Engine

"The only thing that drives real science is curiosity. Not career advancement, not funding — just wanting to know how things work."

## 5. You Must Be Your Own Harshest Critic

"The first principle of honest thinking: you must not fool yourself. And you are the easiest person to fool."

> **Case: The Cargo Cult Scientist** (Chapter 6): Feynman describes airplanes dropping supplies on Pacific islands during WWII. After the war, islanders built mock runways and control towers, hoping the planes would return. They looked like real airfields but didn't work. Some scientific research is the same — it looks like science but lacks the essential ingredient: honest testing.
> **Key takeaway**: Beware of cargo cult science — research that has all the trappings of science but none of the substance. The difference is rigorous, honest testing of hypotheses.
