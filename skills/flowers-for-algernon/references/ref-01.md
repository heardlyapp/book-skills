# Reference: Charlie Gordon

## Overview
Charlie Gordon is one of the most unforgettable characters in literature. His journey from disability to genius to regression is told through his own progress reports.

## Case: Before the Surgery
Charlie works at Donner's bakery. His coworkers mock him. He does not realize it. He thinks they are his friends. He wants to be smart so people will like him.

## Case: The Intelligence
After the surgery, Charlie's IQ skyrockets. He learns languages in days. He reads everything. He sees the cruelty of the world. He becomes lonely.

## Case: The Discovery
Charlie realizes his "friends" were laughing at him, not with him. He realizes Nemur and Strauss see him as a scientific result, not a person. He becomes angry.

## Case: The Love
Charlie tries to love Alice. He is too intelligent for her. He tries to love Fay. She does not want to understand him. He is alone.

## Case: The Regression
Charlie watches himself decline. He writes desperate progress reports. He tries to hold onto his intelligence. It slips away.

## Case: The Final Report
Charlie's last progress report is barely coherent. He has forgotten how to write. He says goodbye. He asks someone to put flowers on Algernon's grave. He disappears.

## Case: Charlie's Legacy
Charlie is a person, not a case study. He wanted to be smart to be loved. He became smart and was lonelier than ever. He regressed and was taken care of.
Charlie Gordon is the proof that intelligence and humanity are not the same thing. He is worth more at IQ 68 than at IQ 185.
