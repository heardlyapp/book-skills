# Reference: The Regression

## Overview
The regression is the novel's devastating second half. Charlie watches his own decline.

## Case: Algernon's Death
Algernon dies. Charlie buries him in the backyard. He places flowers on the grave. He knows he will follow.

## Case: The Awareness
Charlie can feel his intelligence fading. He forgets things. He struggles to read. He cannot solve problems. He is aware of every loss.

## Case: The Desperation
Charlie writes desperate progress reports. He tries to hold on. He tries to document everything before it is gone. It is not enough.

## Case: The Withdrawal
Charlie withdraws from people. He does not want them to see him decline. He pushes Alice away. He isolates himself.

## Case: The Return
Charlie returns to the bakery. Donner gives him his old job back. His coworkers protect him. They do not mock him anymore. He does not remember why.

## Case: The Lesson
Charlie's regression teaches him what humans are. At the peak of his intelligence, he is lonely and bitter. In the regression, he is cared for. He learns that connection matters more than intellect.

## Case: The Courage
Charlie's courage is not in becoming intelligent. It is in facing the loss. He knows what is coming. He does not run. He writes until he cannot.
The regression is the hardest part because Charlie is aware of every loss. He watches himself disappear. That is the tragedy of the novel.
He is not destroyed by intelligence. He is destroyed by the awareness of losing it.
