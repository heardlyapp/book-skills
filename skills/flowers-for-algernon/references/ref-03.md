# Reference: The Progress Reports

## Overview
The progress reports are the novel's narrative voice. Charlie's writing reflects his changing intelligence.

## Case: The Beginning
"Progris riport 1 martch 3. Dr Strauss says I shoud rite down what I think and remembir." The misspellings show Charlie's disability. He tries hard.

## Case: The Improvement
As Charlie gets smarter, his writing improves. The misspellings disappear. The sentences become complex. He writes like a scholar.

## Case: The Peak
At his peak, Charlie writes about scientific research, literary analysis, and his own psychology. The writing is brilliant.

## Case: The Decline
"Im having truble spelling. Its like somthing is fading." The misspellings return. The sentences break. The mind is crumbling.

## Case: The Final
"P.S. please if you get a chance put some flowers on Algernons grave." The last line. Simple. Heartbreaking. Charlie has forgotten everything except kindness.

## Case: The Innovation
The progress reports are an innovation in narrative form. The reader experiences Charlie's transformation directly. We do not read about his change — we experience it through his words.

## Case: The Voice
Charlie's voice is consistent even as his intelligence changes. He remains kind. He remains vulnerable. The progress reports prove that the person is the same, even when the intelligence is not.
The progress reports are not just a narrative technique. They are the moral argument of the novel: Charlie is a person, with a voice, at every stage of his life.
