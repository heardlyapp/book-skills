# Reference: The Experiment

## Overview
The intelligence experiment is the engine of the novel. Charlie is the first human subject. Algernon is the mouse who went before him.

## Case: Algernon
Algernon is the laboratory mouse. He receives the surgery before Charlie. He becomes a maze-solving genius. He then regresses. He dies. His fate is Charlie's fate.

## Case: Nemur and Strauss
Nemur is arrogant. He sees Charlie as a subject. Strauss is more humane. Charlie becomes smarter than both of them. He sees their limitations.

## Case: The Surgery
The surgical procedure is experimental. Charlie volunteers. He wants to be smart. He signs papers. He does not fully understand the risks.

## Case: The Peak
At his peak, Charlie is a genius. He designs experiments. He discovers that Algernon's regression is inevitable. He realizes the same will happen to him.

## Case: The Failure
The experiment fails. Charlie regresses. He knows he is regressing. He cannot stop it. The experiment made him brilliant and broke him.

## Case: The International Symposium
Charlie attends a scientific conference. He is treated as a specimen. He refuses to participate unless treated as a colleague. He takes Algernon and leaves.

## Case: The Paper
Charlie writes a scientific paper about his own regression. He discovers the flaw in the experiment. He cannot save himself. But he contributes to knowledge.
The experiment is not just science. It is a metaphor for the human condition: we all peak, we all decline. The novel asks what we do with the time in between.
