## The Secret to Relationships

### The Most Important Relationship Principle

The single most important teaching about relationships in The Secret: **You must love yourself first.** The quality of your relationships is a direct reflection of your relationship with yourself. You cannot attract someone who loves, respects, and cherishes you if you do not love, respect, and cherish yourself.

**Why this works by law:** Like attracts like. If you feel unworthy of love, you broadcast the frequency of unworthiness. The universe responds by delivering situations and people that confirm your unworthiness. If you feel worthy of deep, loving, respectful partnership, you broadcast that frequency, and matching partners appear in your life.

### Practical Steps for Attracting a Loving Relationship

**1. Make a list of qualities you want in a partner.**
Be specific. Write it in present tense: "My partner is kind, intelligent, emotionally available, shares my values, makes me laugh, supports my dreams." Every quality you list is an instruction to the universe.

**2. Focus on the qualities, not the person.**
Do not fixate on a specific person. This constrains the universe. The universe has infinite resources to bring you the perfect person. Let the universe decide who it is — you just specify the qualities.

**3. Feel the feeling of being in that relationship now.**
This is the most important step. Every day, spend time feeling as if you are already in the relationship you desire. Feel the love. Feel the companionship. Feel the gratitude. If you can't feel those feelings now, you are broadcasting the absence of relationship.

**4. Prepare for the relationship.**
Clear space in your closet. Sleep in the middle of the bed. Buy new sheets. These physical acts signal "I am ready for a relationship" and put you in the frequency of receiving.

**5. Do not focus on what's wrong with your current situation.**
If you're single, don't focus on loneliness. That attracts more loneliness. If you're in a difficult relationship, don't focus on the problems. Focus on the ideal relationship. The universe will either transform your current relationship or bring you a new one — but you must not control the path.

**6. Love yourself first.**
Take yourself on dates. Treat yourself the way you want a partner to treat you. Speak to yourself with kindness. Cultivate your own interests and passions. When you are complete in yourself, you attract someone who complements your completeness, not someone who completes your incompleteness.

### Case Study 12: Marci Shimoff's Relationship Reversal

Marci Shimoff, one of the teachers in The Secret, shares a personal story about transforming her relationship with her mother. She had a difficult relationship with her mother for years — constant criticism, judgment, and emotional pain. Marci realized that continuing to focus on what her mother was doing wrong was only attracting more of the same.

She decided to apply The Secret to the relationship. She began visualizing her mother as loving, supportive, and kind. Every day, she imagined moments of warmth and connection. She felt the gratitude of having a wonderful mother-daughter relationship. She stopped focusing on what her mother did wrong and focused only on the ideal.

Within months, the relationship began to transform. Her mother's behavior shifted. Small but significant changes occurred — her mother started being more supportive, less critical. The relationship gradually healed not because Marci confronted her mother or demanded change, but because she changed her own frequency.

**Lesson:** You cannot change other people directly. But by changing your frequency, the Law of Attraction brings about a shift in how others respond to you. The person who annoyed you yesterday may become kind to you tomorrow — not because they changed, but because you did.

## Action in the Inspired Way

The Secret is not about sitting on a couch and daydreaming. Action is required — but it must be **inspired action**, not forced action.

| Forced Action | Inspired Action |
|---|---|
| Driven by worry, fear, or panic | Driven by joy, excitement, or knowing |
| Feels heavy, hard, like pushing | Feels light, natural, like flow |
| Comes from "I have to" | Comes from "I am guided to" |
| Usually involves controlling or forcing outcomes | Usually involves responding to opportunities |
| Leads to burnout, exhaustion | Leads to energy, expansion |

### How to Recognize Inspired Action

Inspired action feels good. It feels like a "yes" in your body. It often comes as an unexpected impulse — call this person, take this route, make this choice. These impulses are the universe guiding you through the "how" that you don't need to figure out logically.

**Example:** You want a new job. You've been visualizing, feeling grateful, staying in the frequency of having a great job. One day, a friend calls out of the blue and mentions an opening at their company. That's inspired action — the call. You then choose to follow up. That's inspired action — following the impulse.

### What Inspired Action Looks Like in Daily Life

- You feel a pull to go somewhere you don't normally go
- You randomly think of someone and feel moved to text them
- You hear about an opportunity and feel excited, not obligated
- A solution to a problem comes to you while in the shower, walking, or meditating
- You take a small step and the next step becomes clear
