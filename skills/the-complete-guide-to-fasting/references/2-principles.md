## 7 Principles of Therapeutic Fasting

### 1. Insulin Drives Fat Storage

Insulin is the fat-storage hormone. When insulin is high (after eating), the body stores energy as fat. When insulin is low (fasting), the body burns fat for fuel.

### 2. Fasting Lowers Insulin Directly

No food = no insulin response. The body switches from glucose-burning to fat-burning. This is the mechanism. Everything else is secondary.

### 3. Hunger Is Temporary

Hunger comes in waves. Most people find that after 2-3 fasts, hunger decreases significantly. Ghrelin (hunger hormone) follows habit, not necessity.

> **Case: Jimmy Moore's Experience** (Jimmy's Section): Jimmy Moore, co-author, weighed over 400 pounds. He tried every diet — low-fat, low-carb, calorie restriction — and always regained. Extended fasting (up to 7 days) finally broke the cycle. He lost 170 pounds and kept it off. His hunger actually decreased as he fasted longer.
> **Key takeaway**: The experience of hunger does not match the expectation. Longer fasts are often easier than shorter ones because the body adapts.

### 4. Autophagy Is a Key Benefit

During extended fasts (24+ hours), the body initiates autophagy — cellular cleanup. Old and damaged proteins are recycled. This is the mechanism behind fasting's longevity and anti-aging benefits.

### 5. Growth Hormone Protects Muscle

During a 24-hour fast, growth hormone increases up to 5x. This preserves muscle mass while the body burns fat. The fear that fasting causes muscle loss is scientifically unfounded for short-term fasts.

### 6. The "Breakfast Myth"

The idea that breakfast is "the most important meal of the day" is a marketing invention, not a scientific finding. Extending the overnight fast by skipping breakfast is a simple way to practice intermittent fasting.

> **Case: The Historical Norm** (Chapter 2): Humans fasted regularly throughout history — by necessity, by religious practice, by culture. Three meals a day plus snacks is a modern invention. The human body evolved to function optimally with periods of fasting.
> **Key takeaway**: Fasting is not a fad — it is a return to how humans have eaten for most of our evolutionary history.

### 7. Consistency Beats Intensity

Fasting two days a week consistently is better than fasting five days and burning out. Start with 16:8. Build from there.
