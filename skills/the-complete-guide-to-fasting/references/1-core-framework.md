## The Fung Framework: Insulin Is the Key

Dr. Fung's central argument: obesity is a hormonal disease, not a caloric one. The primary driver of fat storage is insulin. Fasting is the most effective way to lower insulin and allow the body to burn stored fat. Calorie restriction without addressing insulin is fighting the symptom, not the cause.

**The Two-Compartment Model**: The body has two compartments — the food compartment (what you just ate) and the body compartment (stored fat). Most diets only affect the food compartment. Fasting forces the body to access the body compartment.

> **Case: The Diabetes Reversal** (Chapter 6): Fung treated a 60-year-old man with Type 2 diabetes who was on insulin injections. Through a program of intermittent fasting (24-hour fasts, 3 times per week), the patient lost weight, normalized his blood sugar, and was able to stop all diabetes medication within 3 months. Fung has replicated this result with hundreds of patients.
> **Key takeaway**: Type 2 diabetes is reversible. The standard treatment — more insulin — makes the underlying problem (hyperinsulinemia) worse. Fasting addresses the root cause.

> **Case: The Calorie Restriction Failure** (Chapter 3): Fung cites a study where participants restricted calories by 25% for 2 years. They lost an average of 5 pounds — and most regained it. The problem: calorie restriction alone does not lower insulin. The body adapts by reducing metabolism and increasing hunger. Fasting avoids this adaptation.
> **Key takeaway**: The failure of calorie restriction is not a lack of willpower — it is biology. Fasting works with biology, not against it.
