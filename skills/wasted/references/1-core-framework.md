# 1. Core Framework — The Persephone Arc

> Source: "Wasted" by Marya Hornbacher (1998). The memoir is structured around the Persephone myth — the descent into the underworld and the return.

## The Mythic Framework

Hornbacher frames her eating disorder through the Persephone myth: Persephone is taken to the underworld by Hades, forced to eat pomegranate seeds, and must return each year for six months. This cycle of descent and return mirrors the cycle of the disorder.

> **Case: The Persephone Chapter** (Chapter 5): "Persephone Herself Is but a Voice" — Hornbacher explicitly connects her experience to the myth. She is the girl taken to the underworld (the eating disorder), forced to eat (the seeds, the calories), doomed to return. The myth gives shape to her chaos.
> **Key takeaway**: Framing personal trauma through myth gives it universal meaning. The reader understands that this is not just one girl's story — it is a story as old as storytelling.

## The Arc

1. Childhood (innocence, before the fall)
2. Descent (the disorder takes hold)
3. The Underworld (hospitalization, starvation, near-death)
4. Return (survival, writing, recovery)

> **Case: The 52-Pound Body** (Chapter 6): Hornbacher describes her body at 52 pounds: "I looked like a corpse. I was a corpse that hadn't quite stopped breathing." This is the bottom of the descent — the point where the body can go no further.
> **Key takeaway**: The body has limits that the mind does not recognize. The disorder would continue until the body stopped it.
