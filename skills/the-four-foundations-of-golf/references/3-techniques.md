# Techniques: Golf Improvement Practices

## The Pre-Shot Routine

1. Stand behind the ball — pick your target
2. Take one practice swing — feel the shot
3. Step in and align
4. Look at the target one more time
5. Take a deep breath
6. Hit the shot
7. Accept the result — good or bad — and move to the next shot

> **Case: The Routine Under Pressure** (Part IV): A golfer who had no pre-shot routine would rush under pressure. On the 18th tee of a tournament, he stepped up without a routine, made a poor swing, and lost. After developing a consistent routine, he could perform under pressure because the routine gave him something to focus on besides the outcome.
> **Key takeaway**: A pre-shot routine is not just preparation — it's a pressure shield. When you have a routine, you focus on the process, not the result.

> **Case: The 100-Yard Shot Drill** (Part III): A golfer practiced 100-yard approach shots exclusively for a month. He hit 50 shots per session, tracked his dispersion pattern, and noted his average miss. After the month, his approach shots were tighter, his scoring improved, and he knew exactly where his miss would be — which made course management easier.
>
> **Key takeaway**: Practice that tracks results and identifies patterns is infinitely more valuable than mindless repetition. Know your miss, plan for it, and improve it.

## Purposeful Practice Session Structure

1. Warm up (10 minutes) — loosen up, no specific goal
2. Drills (20 minutes) — specific skill focus with feedback
3. Practice play (20 minutes) — simulate course conditions
4. Short game (20 minutes) — chipping, pitching, putting
5. Cool down (10 minutes) — review, note areas for next session

## Course Strategy Guide

- Off the tee: hit the club that keeps the ball in play, not the longest club
- Into the green: aim for the center, not the pin
- Around the green: get it on the green, don't try to hole it
- On the green: lag putt first, make putt second
- Choose the shot you can execute 8/10 times
