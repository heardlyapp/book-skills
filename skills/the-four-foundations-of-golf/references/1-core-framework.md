# Core Framework: The Four Foundations

## The Central Thesis

"Most golfers focus on the wrong things. They chase swing perfection while ignoring the four areas that actually determine their scores." Sherman's framework is built on a simple truth: golf improvement is not about swinging better — it's about managing expectations, making smarter decisions, practicing purposefully, and training the mind.

> **Case: The Frustrated Golfer** (Introduction): A golfer with a 15 handicap expected to shoot in the 70s on his good days. He was constantly disappointed. When he tracked his actual scores over 20 rounds, his average was 89. His expectations were completely unrealistic. The first step to improving was accepting where he actually was.
> **Key takeaway**: You cannot improve until you know where you actually are. Track your scores honestly. Your average is your truth.

## The Four Foundations

| Foundation | Key Question |
|-----------|--------------|
| Managing Expectations | What is realistic for my ability? |
| Course Strategy | What decision gives me the best outcome? |
| Purposeful Practice | How do I practice to actually improve? |
| Mental Game | How do I stay composed under pressure? |

> **Case: The Course Management Transformation** (Part II): A 12-handicap golfer started making smarter decisions: aiming for the center of every green, laying up to his favorite yardage, never trying hero shots. His scores dropped by an average of 4 strokes per round — without changing his swing at all.
> **Key takeaway**: Strategy improvements produce immediate results. You don't need to change your swing to shoot lower scores. You need to change your decisions.
