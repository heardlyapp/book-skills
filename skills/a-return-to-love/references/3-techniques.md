> Source: A Return to Love, Parts I-IV

## Practical Techniques

### 1. The Pause and Choose

When you feel fear (anger, anxiety, jealousy), pause. Recognize the ego's voice. Ask: "What would love do now?" Then choose love. The pause is the miracle.

### 2. The Forgiveness Practice

Write down a grievance. Say: "I forgive myself for believing [person] could take my peace. I release this grievance." Repeat daily until the weight lifts.

> **Case: The Daily Forgiveness** (Chapter 6): Williamson teaches a daily forgiveness practice: at the end of each day, review any grievances that arose. For each one, say: "I choose to see this differently. I choose to forgive." The practice rewires the mind toward love.
> **Key takeaway**: Forgiveness is not a one-time event — it is a daily practice. The mind must be trained to release.

### 3. The Inner Voice Distinction

When you hear an inner voice, ask: "Does this voice come from love or fear?" Love's voice is gentle, patient, and kind. Fear's voice is urgent, judgmental, and harsh. Learn to distinguish them.

### 4. The Holy Instant

Take a moment of complete stillness. Do nothing. Want nothing. Be present. This is the "holy instant" — a gap in the ego's constant chatter. In that gap, peace is available.

> **Case: The Five-Minute Miracle** (Introduction): Williamson describes beginning each day with five minutes of stillness. Just sitting. Not asking for anything. Not thinking about anything. This practice sets the tone for the entire day.
> **Key takeaway**: Stillness is not empty — it is full. In the silence, guidance can be heard. The practice of stillness is the foundation of spiritual life.
