## 7 Principles of Effective Follow-Up

### 1. Speed Matters

The first follow-up within 24 hours dramatically increases close rates. Delay kills momentum.

### 2. Value Before Ask

Every contact must provide something useful. Never contact a prospect without offering value.

> **Case: The Article Share** (Chapter 7): A salesperson sent a prospect a relevant industry article — no pitch, no ask. The prospect responded: "Thanks for thinking of me." Two weeks later, the prospect called back and bought. The value-first contact built trust.
> **Key takeaway**: Giving without asking creates trust. Trust leads to sales.

### 3. Persistence Is Professional

Most sales require 5+ follow-ups. Stopping at 2-3 is leaving money on the table.

### 4. Emotion Drives Decisions

Buyers buy on emotion, then justify with logic. Follow-up must address emotions — fear, trust, excitement.

### 5. Track the Process

Measure: response rate, time to close, number of touches. What you measure, you improve.

### 6. "Not Now" Is Not "No"

"Not now" means the timing is wrong. Ask when to reconnect. Set a specific time.

### 7. The Close Is a Service

If the prospect is ready, not asking them to decide is a disservice. Ask directly. No tricks.

> **Case: The 5-Email Rule** (Chapter 9): Shore tracked a sales team and found that the average sale required 7 follow-ups. The team was averaging 2. When they increased to 5, close rates tripled. The data was clear.
> **Key takeaway**: Your instincts about how many follow-ups to send are wrong. The data says more. Trust the data, not your discomfort.
