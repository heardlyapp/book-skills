## 6 Anti-Patterns of Poor Follow-Up

### 1. "I Don't Want to Be a Nuisance"

Persistence with value is not nuisance — it is service. The prospect who needs what you offer will thank you for following up.

### 2. "If They Are Interested, They Will Call Back"

They will not. They are busy. Your follow-up is not pressure — it is a reminder that you are available.

### 3. "One Follow-Up Is Enough"

Most sales require 5+ follow-ups. One is not enough.

> **Case: The Stolen Deal** (Chapter 3): A salesperson made a great first impression. The prospect said "call me next week." The salesperson called once, got voicemail, and stopped. Three months later, the prospect bought from a competitor who had called 4 times.
> **Key takeaway**: One follow-up is not enough. The competitor who persisted won the deal you started.

### 4. "A Check-In Email Is Fine"

A "just checking in" email is not fine. It provides no value. It wastes a contact opportunity.

### 5. "The Close Is a Technique"

The close is not a technique — it is a direct invitation. The best salespeople do not "close" — they ask clearly and respectfully.

### 6. "Emotions Don't Matter in B2B"

Business buyers are human. Emotions — trust, fear, excitement, status — drive every purchase.

> **Case: The Value Test** (Chapter 5): Shore says to ask yourself before every contact: "If I received this message, would I find it useful?" If the answer is no, do not send it.
> **Key takeaway**: The value test eliminates annoying follow-up. If you would not want to receive it, do not send it.
