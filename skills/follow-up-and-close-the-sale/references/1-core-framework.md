## The Shore Framework: Value-Driven Follow-Up

Shore's framework: follow-up is not an afterthought to sales — it is the core of the sales process. Most salespeople are judged on their pitch. They should be judged on their follow-up.

**The Key Insight**: The prospect's decision timeline is rarely aligned with the salesperson's urgency. Follow-up bridges the gap between "not now" and "yes."

> **Case: The 24-Hour Rule** (Chapter 2): Shore studied sales teams and found that follow-up within 24 hours dramatically increased close rates. Prospects who received a same-day follow-up were 4x more likely to convert than those who received follow-up after 48 hours.
> **Key takeaway**: Speed matters. The first follow-up is not just valuable — it is critical. 24 hours is the window.

> **Case: The Value Email** (Chapter 6): Shore distinguishes between "value emails" (sharing a relevant article, a case study, a solution to a problem) and "check-in emails" (just checking in). Value emails get responses. Check-in emails get deleted.
> **Key takeaway**: Every contact must provide value. If you cannot think of what value to offer, do not contact them yet.
The core framework: follow-up is the bridge between the initial interest and the final decision. Build the bridge with value, not pressure.
Bridge building is the metaphor that makes the framework stick.
 Every follow-up is a plank on the bridge.
 The goal: a complete bridge.
