# Reference: Terraforming Mars

## Overview
Mars is the only planet in the solar system that could be terraformed with foreseeable technology. Kaku presents a phased plan for making the Red Planet habitable.

## Case: The First Phase — Raising the Temperature
Mars's average temperature is -63°C. The first step is warming it. Kaku describes two methods: orbital mirrors (giant reflectors in orbit that concentrate sunlight on the poles) and super-greenhouse gases (perfluorocarbons manufactured on Mars that are 10,000 times more effective than CO2). Both are physically feasible with current technology. The challenge is scale — manufacturing mirrors kilometers wide on Mars is an enormous engineering project.

## Case: The Respiration Phase
Once Mars is warm enough for liquid water, the next challenge is breathable air. Kaku proposes genetically engineered bacteria and algae that can convert Mars's CO2-rich atmosphere into oxygen. This is the same process that created Earth's oxygen atmosphere 2.4 billion years ago. On Earth it took hundreds of millions of years. On Mars, with engineered organisms, it could take centuries.

## Case: SpaceX and the Mars Timeline
Kaku interviews Elon Musk and documents SpaceX's Mars architecture. The Starship is designed to carry 100 passengers to Mars. Musk's goal is to build a self-sustaining city of 1 million people on Mars within 50-100 years. Kaku notes that the physics is feasible but the biology (radiation, low gravity, psychological effects) presents harder challenges than the engineering.
