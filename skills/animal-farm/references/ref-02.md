# Reference: Napoleon and Snowball

## Overview
The power struggle between Napoleon and Snowball is the central conflict of the novel. It mirrors the struggle between Stalin and Trotsky.

## Case: Snowball's Windmill
Snowball plans a windmill to bring electricity to the farm. He represents the idealist revolutionary who believes progress is possible. His plans are detailed and visionary.

## Case: Napoleon's Dogs
Napoleon raises puppies in secret, training them into fierce enforcers. When he is ready, he unleashes them at a meeting, driving Snowball off the farm. This is the Stalinist purges.

## Case: The Scapegoat
After Snowball's exile, Napoleon blames everything on him. When the windmill collapses, Napoleon says Snowball sabotaged it. When anything goes wrong, Snowball is the excuse.

## Case: The Absolute Ruler
Napoleon becomes indistinguishable from a human dictator. He sleeps in a bed, drinks alcohol, trades with humans, and eventually walks on two legs. He is the tyrant the revolution was supposed to overthrow.

## Case: The Cult of Personality
Napoleon creates a cult around himself. Songs are sung about him. Slogans praise him. The animals must celebrate his leadership. This mirrors the Stalin cult.

## Case: The Purge
The dogs execute animals who confess to "collaborating" with Snowball. The confessions are forced. The trials are show trials. The executions are terror.
The pattern is familiar: the revolutionary who eliminates rivals, creates scapegoats, and demands absolute loyalty.
