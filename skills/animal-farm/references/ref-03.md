# Reference: The Seven Commandments

## Overview
The Seven Commandments are the laws of Animalism. They are the revolution's constitution. They are gradually rewritten until they mean the opposite.

## Case: The Original Commandments
1. Whatever goes upon two legs is an enemy. 2. Whatever goes upon four legs, or has wings, is a friend. 3. No animal shall wear clothes. 4. No animal shall sleep in a bed with sheets. 5. No animal shall drink alcohol. 6. No animal shall kill any other animal. 7. All animals are equal.

## Case: The First Alteration
The pigs begin sleeping in beds. Squealer argues that sheets are the problem, not beds themselves. The commandment becomes "No animal shall sleep in a bed with sheets." The erosion begins.

## Case: The Second Alteration
The pigs begin drinking alcohol. The commandment becomes "No animal shall drink alcohol to excess." Each change is justified with rationalization.

## Case: The Final Commandment
The remaining commandment is altered overnight. The animals wake to see: "ALL ANIMALS ARE EQUAL. BUT SOME ANIMALS ARE MORE EQUAL THAN OTHERS." The original ideal has been destroyed.

## Case: The Gradual Erosion
The commandments are not changed overnight. Each change is small enough to be rationalized. The animals notice but tell themselves it is not important. This is how tyranny grows.

## Case: The End of Equality
The pigs walk on two legs, wear clothes, drink alcohol, and rule the farm. All Seven Commandments are now meaningless. Equality is dead.
