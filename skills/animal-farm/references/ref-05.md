# Reference: Language and Propaganda

## Overview
Squealer is the master of propaganda. He can justify anything. Language is the most powerful weapon on Animal Farm.

## Case: The Milk and Apples
The pigs take the milk and apples. Squealer explains that pigs need them to think. "Jones would reduce us to the old slavery." The animals accept.

## Case: The Windmill Destruction
When the windmill collapses, Napoleon blames Snowball. Squealer convinces the animals that building it again will make them stronger. The lie becomes the truth.

## Case: The Boxer Lie
Boxer is sent to the knacker. Squealer says he died in a hospital, waving good-bye. He even writes a poem. The animals cry. They believe.

## Case: The Rewritten Commandments
The Commandments are altered one by one. Squealer's rationalizations make each change seem reasonable. The animals cannot remember the originals.

## Case: The Final Slogan
"All animals are equal, but some are more equal than others." This is the ultimate perversion. Language no longer describes reality. Language creates reality.

## Case: The Sheep
The sheep are trained to chant "Four legs good, two legs better." They drown out dissent with slogans. They are the masses who repeat propaganda without understanding it.

## Case: The Memory
The animals cannot remember the original Commandments. Writing was changed. Their own memories were overwritten. Once language is controlled, history is controlled.
Orwell's warning: control language, and you control thought. This is the same insight that drives 1984's Newspeak.
