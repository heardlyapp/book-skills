# Anti-Patterns — Coach Wooden and Me

## The Central Error: "Coach Wooden Was Always a Saint"

"Not the kindly, soft-spoken, fount of loving aphorisms that he later became." Wooden started his coaching career punching players and coaches. He was human. "We like to think of our heroes as perfect, but the harsh truth is that they became heroes by making mistakes and learning from them."

### Mistake 1: "The Wizard of Westwood was a genius who never lost"
Wooden hated the nickname "Wizard of Westwood." Before his championship run, he had seventeen consecutive winning seasons but zero national championships. He was good for years before becoming great. "Genius is one percent inspiration and ninety-nine percent perspiration" — Edison, quoted by Wooden.

### Mistake 2: "Winning is everything"
"The scoreboard is meaningless." Wooden meant this literally. He hated that sports movies always end with the underdog winning. "Showing the team winning sends the wrong message: that life lessons exist to serve as a guide for acquiring things that make you feel like a success."

### Mistake 3: "Black and white can never understand each other"
The entire book disproves this. Wooden grew up in Klan territory. Kareem grew up in the civil rights struggle. Neither pretended their experiences were the same. But they respected each other's differences deeply. "I'm not sure I ever understood being black in America," Wooden told Kareem, "but I always understood being treated unfairly."

### Mistake 4: "A good coach should give lots of advice"
Wooden's greatest gift to Kareem was often silence. After MLK's assassination: "He never said a word. He just sat there with me." When Kareem's marriage failed, Wooden called every week and "didn't give advice. He just listened."

### Mistake 5: "Religion has to divide us"
Kareem was a devout Muslim; Wooden was a devout Christian. Wooden didn't just tolerate the difference — he asked genuine questions. "Tell me what being a Muslim means to you." Their faiths didn't divide them. The technique: genuine curiosity bridges religious divides.

### Mistake 6: "Friendship is about being the same"
"If ever two people could be called polar opposites, John Wooden and I would be at the top of the list." White Midwestern farm boy / black Harlem kid. Christian / Muslim. Big band / jazz. They had almost nothing in common and built one of the great friendships in sports history.
