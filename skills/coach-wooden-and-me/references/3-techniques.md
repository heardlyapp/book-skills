# Techniques — Coach Wooden and Me

## How to Build a Lifelong Friendship (Based on Kareem Abdul-Jabbar and John Wooden)

### Technique 1: The Snug-and-Tug — Start With Fundamentals
Wooden's first lesson was putting on socks and sneakers. "Tug. And. Snug." This wasn't trivial — it was about preventing blisters that would keep you from practicing. "For want of a nail..." The technique: don't skip the basics. Every great achievement rests on properly executed fundamentals.

### Technique 2: The Rafter View — Step Back for Perspective
During practices, Wooden would climb to the top rafters of Pauley Pavilion. "From there he would look down on us like a benevolent god, watching us scuttle up and down the court. He liked the fresh perspective." Kareem had to do the same for this book — climb above his memories to see the big picture.

### Technique 3: Recruit Character, Not Just Talent
Wooden crossed off a top recruit who snapped at his mother. He talked Kareem's parents about grades before mentioning basketball. The technique: choose your collaborators, employees, and friends based on who they are, not just what they can do. "I wanted young men who wanted to play for UCLA."

### Technique 4: Presence Over Advice
When Kareem was grieving MLK's assassination, Wooden came to his apartment and sat in silence. No advice, no platitudes, no Bible verses. "He never said a word. He just sat there with me because he knew that's what I needed." The technique: sometimes the most powerful support is simply showing up.

### Technique 5: Let the Role Reverse
After Nell Wooden died, the friendship shifted. Kareem began checking on Coach. "I would sit with him at his home, quietly watching a Western movie or baseball game on his small TV, just enjoying the familiar warmth and comfort of his presence." The technique: as relationships age, let the care flow both ways.

### Technique 6: Play Basketball Jazz
Kareem realized their basketball system was jazz. "You taught us to play basketball jazz." Structured freedom — individual expression within a framework. "We soloed here and there, expressed our individuality, but all within the framework of what the other players were doing." The technique: in teamwork, balance structure with creative freedom.

### Technique 7: Keep Your Word
Both Wooden and Kareem demonstrated this. Wooden turned down Minnesota because he'd given his word to UCLA. Kareem turned down $3.25 million from the ABA because he'd given his word to the Milwaukee Bucks. "I had given my word. That was stronger than any written contract." The technique: your word is your bond, even when it costs you.
