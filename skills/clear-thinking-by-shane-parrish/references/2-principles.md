# Building Strength

> Source: Clear Thinking, Part 2, Chapters 2.1-2.7

## The Foundations

### The Four Strengths

| Strength | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Self-Accountability | Own your decisions and their outcomes |
| Self-Knowledge | Know your strengths, weaknesses, and defaults |
| Self-Control | Manage impulses and create space |
| Self-Confidence | Trust your judgment when you have prepared |

> **Case: Self-Accountability (Chapter 2.1)**
> Parrish argues that self-accountability is the foundation of clear thinking. If you do not own your decisions, you cannot learn from them. Blame is the enemy of improvement. When something goes wrong, ask: "What did I contribute to this outcome?" Even if the cause was external, your response was yours. Owning it gives you power over it. Deflecting it gives you nothing.
> **Key takeaway:** Self-accountability is the difference between victims and agents. Victims are acted upon. Agents act. The lesson: you cannot control what happens to you. You can control how you respond. That is where clear thinking begins.

> **Case: Setting Standards (Chapter 2.6)**
> Most people do not set standards for their thinking. They hope to make good decisions in the moment. Parrish argues: you must set your standards before the moment. What kind of person do you want to be? How do you want to make decisions? Write it down. Commit to it. When the pressure comes, your standards will guide you — if you set them ahead of time.
> **Key takeaway:** Standards are not constraints — they are freedoms. They free you from having to decide in the moment. When you have set your standards, the decision is already made. The lesson: prepare for the moments before they arrive.
