# Managing Weakness

> Source: Clear Thinking, Part 3, Chapters 3.1-3.3

## Safeguards

### The Technique

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Know your specific weaknesses |
| 2 | Design safeguards to prevent them |
| 3 | Review and update regularly |

> **Case: Knowing Your Weaknesses (Chapter 3.1)**
> Parrish asks: what are the situations where you consistently make bad decisions? For some, it is when they are tired. For others, it is when they are hungry. For others, it is when they are in a meeting with a particular person. The first step is to know your triggers. Keep a decision journal. Review your bad decisions. Look for patterns. Your weaknesses are not random — they are predictable.
> **Key takeaway:** The most effective way to improve decision-making is not to build more strength — it is to manage your weaknesses. The lesson: your weaknesses are knowable. Study them. Design around them.

> **Case: Safeguards (Chapter 3.2)**
> A safeguard is a rule or system that prevents you from making a bad decision when your defenses are down. Examples: never send an email when you are angry (wait 24 hours). Never make a major purchase without sleeping on it. Never say yes to a request without checking your calendar. Safeguards are not restrictions — they are freedom from your own worst impulses.
> **Key takeaway:** Safeguards work because they do not rely on willpower. Willpower is unreliable. Safeguards are automatic. The lesson: design your environment so that your weaknesses do not matter.
