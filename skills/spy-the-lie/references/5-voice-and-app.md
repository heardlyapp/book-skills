## Author's Voice

The authors write as former CIA officers who have done this for real. No theorizing — they tell stories from actual operations. The tone is direct, authoritative, and grounded. They stress the responsibility that comes with deception detection — false accusations are dangerous.

## Signature Phrases

- "Spy the lie" — the title and method
- "Baseline, cluster, question" — the framework
- "No Pinocchio effect" — no single tell
- "Cognitive load" — the key mechanism
- "The reverse timeline" — the best technique
- "The oops word" — guilty knowledge

## Application to 5 Scenarios

### Scenario 1: A manager investigating a workplace issue
**Partial response**: "Establish baseline first. Ask neutral questions. Then ask about the issue. Look for clusters of deviation. Do not accuse based on one behavior."

### Scenario 2: A parent wondering if a child is lying
**Partial response**: "Do not ask accusatory questions. Ask open-ended questions. Use the timeline technique — ask what happened in order, then ask in reverse."

### Scenario 3: A professional in negotiations
**Partial response**: "Listen for the oops word — information they should not have. Use the bait question to test specific knowledge."

### Scenario 4: A journalist interviewing a source
**Partial response**: "Use the baseline technique. Ask easy questions first. Then ask the hard question. Watch for deviations from their normal behavior."

### Scenario 5: Someone in a personal relationship
**Partial response**: "Be careful. These techniques can damage trust if misapplied. The CIA method is for high-stakes situations. Do not treat your partner like a suspect."

> **Case: The Unfaithful Spouse** (Chapter 6): The reverse timeline technique exposed a partner's lie about their evening. Forward: plausible. Backward: contradictions.

> **Key takeaway**: The reverse timeline works in personal situations too — when trust is on the line.

> **Case: The Oops Word** (Chapter 5): "I did not take the $10,000" before anyone mentioned the amount. The oops word revealed guilty knowledge.

> **Key takeaway**: Liars reveal what they should not know. Listen for the oops word.
