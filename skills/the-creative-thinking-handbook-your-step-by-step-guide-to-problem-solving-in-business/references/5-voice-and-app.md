# Voice and Application: Griffiths' Approach

> Source: The Creative Thinking Handbook, throughout

## Their Voice

Griffiths, Costi, and Medlicott write as practical business consultants. The tone is direct, structured, and results-focused. The book is designed as a workbook — each chapter has learning objectives, technique explanations, real examples, and exercises.

> **Case: The Workbook Format** (throughout): Each chapter opens with objectives, explains techniques, shows examples from real companies, then provides space for readers to apply them. This makes the book immediately actionable.

> **Case: The Company Case Studies** (throughout): Real business examples illustrate each technique — a packaging redesign using SCAMPER, a strategy session using mind mapping, a product launch using the Decision Radar. These make abstract techniques concrete.

> **Key takeaway**: The book is designed for doing, not just reading. Its value comes from applying the techniques, not absorbing concepts.

## Application: Problem Solving

Use the Solution Finder for any complex problem. Start with definition, not solutions.

## Application: Team Creativity

Use SCAMPER and mind mapping in group sessions. Structure helps groups think together.

## Application: Innovation Culture

Build creative time into your schedule. Reward implementation, not just ideas.

## Key Quotes

- "The right answer to the wrong problem is useless."
- "Creative thinking is a skill, not a fixed gift."
- "Separate ideation from evaluation completely."
- "Implementation is part of the creative process."
