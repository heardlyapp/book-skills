# Core Framework: The Solution Finder

> Source: The Creative Thinking Handbook, Introduction and Chapters 1-2

## Mental Models and Creative Blocks

Most creative blocks come from invisible assumptions. The book begins by helping readers identify and challenge them.

> **Case: The Assumption Challenge** (Chapter 1): A team was stuck on a problem. Someone asked "what assumptions are we making?" They realized they'd assumed the solution had to cost money. Once that assumption was named, dozens of low-cost ideas emerged immediately.

> **Key takeaway**: The most limiting assumptions are invisible. Make them explicit and question them. That's where creative breakthroughs begin.

## The Solution Finder Framework

Five stages: Understand the problem, Ideate ideas, Evaluate options, Plan action, Implement. Each has specific tools and techniques.

> **Case: The Company Turnaround** (Chapter 2): A struggling company used the Solution Finder. They thought the problem was "not enough customers." The real problem was "we don't know why customers leave." That reframing saved months of wasted effort on the wrong solution.

> **Case: The Product Launch Decision** (Chapter 2): A product team used the Solution Finder to evaluate three competing ideas. The Decision Radar showed which had the best balance of feasibility, impact, and resource requirements. They chose the winner.

> **Key takeaway**: The Solution Finder prevents jumping to solutions. Each stage builds on the previous one. Rigor in creative thinking produces better results.
