# Anti-Patterns: Creative Thinking Traps

> Source: The Creative Thinking Handbook, Chapters 7-9

## Jumping to Solutions

The most common creative failure. People hear a problem and immediately propose solutions without understanding the real issue.

> **Case: The Sales Decline** (Chapter 7): A company's sales were declining. Everyone proposed solutions: lower prices, more ads, new packaging. Only when they used the Solution Finder did they discover the real problem: customer service response time was too slow. All the "solutions" were solving the wrong thing.

> **Key takeaway**: Solution jumping wastes time and resources. Define the problem properly before generating ideas.

## Killing Ideas Too Early

Evaluating ideas during ideation shuts down creative flow.

> **Case: The Killed Idea** (Chapter 8): Someone suggested an idea that seemed impractical. The group dismissed it. Later, someone built on that "impractical" idea to create a breakthrough. If they hadn't killed it early, they'd have arrived at the solution sooner.

> **Key takeaway**: Separate ideation and evaluation completely. During ideation, all ideas are welcome. Judgment comes later.

## No Implementation Plan

A brilliant idea without execution is just an interesting thought.

> **Case: The Great Idea That Died** (Chapter 9): An employee had a brilliant product idea. It was approved but never implemented — no owner, no timeline, no accountability. The idea died on a spreadsheet.

> **Key takeaway**: An idea is only valuable when implemented. Build execution into every creative project.
