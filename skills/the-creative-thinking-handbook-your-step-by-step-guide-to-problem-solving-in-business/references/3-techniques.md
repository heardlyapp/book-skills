# Techniques: Evaluating and Implementing Ideas

> Source: The Creative Thinking Handbook, Chapters 6-9

## The Decision Radar

A visual evaluation tool for comparing ideas across multiple criteria: feasibility, impact, strategic alignment, and resources.

> **Case: The Three Proposals** (Chapter 6): A team had three competing proposals. Using the Decision Radar, each was plotted against four criteria. One idea clearly scored highest across all dimensions. The visual made the decision objective and defensible — no politics, just data.

> **Key takeaway**: The Decision Radar makes trade-offs visible. Different ideas rarely rank the same across all criteria. The radar reveals the best overall balance.

## Action Planning

Moving from idea to implementation. A great idea without a plan is just a wish.

> **Case: The Stalled Project** (Chapter 7): A creative team had a great idea but no execution plan. Using the Solution Finder's planning stage, they identified owners, timelines, milestones, and success metrics. The idea became a successful product within six months.

> **Key takeaway**: Implementation belongs in the creative process. Build planning and execution into your framework from day one.

## Building a Creative Culture

Sustained creativity requires specific organizational practices: time for exploration, psychological safety, and rewards for smart risk-taking.

> **Case: The Innovation Time** (Chapter 9): A company that allocated 10% of work time to creative exploration produced three times more patentable ideas than peers. Structured creative time is not wasted time.

> **Key takeaway**: Creativity needs structure, culture, and execution support. Each is necessary.
