# Principles: Creative Techniques

> Source: The Creative Thinking Handbook, Chapters 3-5

## SCAMPER

A structured technique for generating ideas by asking seven types of questions about any product, service, or problem.

> **Case: The Packaging Innovation** (Chapter 3): A packaging team used SCAMPER. The "Eliminate" prompt asked: what if we removed the outer box entirely? This led to a minimalist packaging design that saved costs and appealed to eco-conscious customers.

> **Key takeaway**: SCAMPER forces you to look at problems from multiple angles. Each prompt breaks habitual thinking.

## Mind Mapping

A visual technique that mirrors how the brain naturally connects ideas. Central topic, radiating branches, colors and images.

> **Case: The Strategy Session** (Chapter 4): A leadership team mind-mapped their annual strategy. The visual format revealed connections between departments they'd never seen in a linear document. New collaborative opportunities emerged.

> **Key takeaway**: Mind mapping reveals connections that linear thinking misses. The visual structure is the insight.

## Random Stimulus

Using an unrelated word or image to spark new connections.

> **Case: The Random Word** (Chapter 5): A team stuck on a logistics problem used "butterfly" as a random stimulus. This led to thinking about lightness and transformation — inspiring a solution involving modular, lightweight containers.

> **Key takeaway**: Random stimulus disrupts habitual thinking patterns. Forcing unexpected connections generates novel ideas.
