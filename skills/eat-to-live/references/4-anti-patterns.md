# Anti-Patterns

## 1. Counting Calories Instead of Nutrients

**The mistake**: Eating 1200 calories of processed food and thinking it's a "diet."

**Why it fails**: Calorie restriction without nutrient density creates hunger, fatigue, and failure. The body needs micronutrients to function. When it doesn't get them, it triggers cravings.

**Fix**: Ignore calories. Focus on ANDI scores. Eat as much as you want of high-ANDI foods.

## 2. The All-or-Nothing Trap

**The mistake**: One slip-up → "I failed → might as well eat whatever I want."

**Why it fails**: Perfection is not required. One meal off plan does not undo a week of healthy eating.

**Fix**: Get right back on track at the next meal. The Nutritarian diet is forgiving.

> **Case: The Moderate Failure** (Chapter 6): Dr. Fuhrman cautions against the "moderation" approach to processed food. "A little bit of sugar" keeps the addiction alive. A "small amount of junk" prevents taste bud retraining. Moderation of unhealthy foods is harder than complete elimination for the first 6 weeks.
> **Key takeaway**: Moderation of addictive substances keeps the addiction alive. Complete elimination for 6 weeks allows true freedom from cravings.

> **Case: The Oil Trap** (Chapter 8): Many people start eating more vegetables but cook them in oil. Dr. Fuhrman explains that oil is the most calorie-dense food on the planet — 120 calories per tablespoon — with zero nutrients. "Healthy" oils like olive oil are still pure fat with no fiber or micronutrients. Water-sauteing or broth-sauteing instead of oil is a critical technique.
> **Key takeaway**: Even healthy oils are empty calories. The Nutritarian diet minimizes all oils, including olive and coconut.

## 3. Ignoring the 6-Week Minimum

**The mistake**: Trying the diet for a few days, deciding it's too hard, and quitting.

**Why it fails**: The first 3-5 days are the hardest due to toxic hunger. Quitting before that passes means you never experience the benefits.

**Fix**: Commit to 6 weeks minimum. The first week is withdrawal. The remaining 5 are transformative.

## 4. Confusing Toxic Hunger with True Hunger

**The mistake**: Giving in to cravings because "I'm starving."

**Why it fails**: The desperate, demanding feeling is withdrawal from processed food, not genuine hunger.

**Fix**: Eat a large salad or a piece of fruit. If true hunger, it will be satisfied. If toxic hunger, the whole-food meal will calm the craving without perpetuating the addiction.
