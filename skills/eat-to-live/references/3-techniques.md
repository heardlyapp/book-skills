# Techniques: Practical Nutritarian Tools

## The 6-Week Jumpstart

1. **Day 1-3**: Withdrawal phase. May experience headaches, fatigue, cravings. This is toxic hunger.
2. **Day 4-7**: Energy returns. Cravings diminish. Taste buds begin to shift.
3. **Week 2-3**: Weight loss accelerates. Cravings minimal. Natural foods taste better.
4. **Week 4-6**: New normal established. Results visible. Health markers improving.

> **Case: The 6-Week Patient** (Chapter 7): A patient who couldn't stick to any diet committed to the full 6-week plan. In the first 3 days: severe cravings and headaches (toxic hunger). By day 5: energy returned. By week 3: vegetables tasted "sweet" — his taste buds had changed. He lost 18 pounds in 6 weeks.
> **Key takeaway**: The first 3-5 days are withdrawal. Push through it. Your taste buds will literally change.

> **Case: The Grocery Store Transformation** (Chapter 10): After 2-3 weeks on the Nutritarian diet, patients report their shopping habits change. The produce section becomes the most appealing part of the store. This is not willpower — it's biology. When your body gets the nutrients it needs, it stops craving empty calories.
> **Key takeaway**: Your food preferences adapt to what you eat. Change what you eat, and your cravings follow.

## Building the Nutritarian Plate

- Large salad (at least 6 cups of greens) — the foundation
- Cooked vegetables — grilled, steamed, or stir-fried
- Beans or legumes — lentil soup, bean chili, chickpea curry
- Mushrooms — any variety, cooked
- Berries for dessert — fresh or frozen, no added sugar
- Seeds — flax, chia, hemp, sunflower

## Grocery Priority List

Always stock:
- Dark leafy greens (kale, arugula, romaine, collards)
- All varieties of mushrooms
- All varieties of beans and lentils
- Onions, garlic, leeks
- Fresh or frozen berries
- Seeds: flax, chia, hemp, sunflower
- Cruciferous vegetables: broccoli, cabbage, cauliflower

## Handling Cravings

When a craving hits: wait 15 minutes, drink water, eat fruit, recognize it as toxic hunger.
