# Principles of Scientific Discovery

**Principle 1: See the Pattern First, Worry About the Math Later.** Faraday saw the pattern of lines of force without mathematics. The pattern came first.

**Principle 2: Mathematics Makes the Pattern Precise.** Maxwell gave Faraday's pictures mathematical form. The equations made the theory testable.

**Principle 3: Experiment Demands Theory; Theory Demands Experiment.** Neither Faraday nor Maxwell could have done it alone. They needed each other.

**Case: The Royal Institution.** Faraday's laboratory at the Royal Institution was a model of experimental excellence. He kept meticulous notes of every experiment.

**Case: The Cambridge Education.** Maxwell's mathematical training at Cambridge gave him the tools Faraday lacked.

**Case: The Speed of Light. Maxwell's equations predicted that electromagnetic waves travel at the speed of light. He concluded: light is an electromagnetic wave. This was one of the greatest unifications in physics.**

**Case: The Dynamo. Faraday invented the first dynamo in 1831. It converted mechanical energy into electrical energy. The entire electrical industry is based on this device.**
