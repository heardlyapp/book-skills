# Scientific Techniques

**Technique 1: Visualize.** Faraday's greatest strength: he could visualize physical processes that no one had ever seen. He saw lines of force.

**Technique 2: Quantify.** Maxwell's greatest strength: he translated visualization into equations. Equations can be tested.

**Technique 3: Connect.** Maxwell saw that electricity, magnetism, and light were the same thing. The connection was genius.

**Case: The Plug.** Faraday connected a magnet and a wire coil. The result was the first electric generator.

**Case: The Prediction.** Maxwell's equations predicted radio waves. No one had seen radio waves. They existed in the equations first.

**Case: The Color Photograph. Maxwell demonstrated the first color photograph in 1861. He used three colored filters to capture red, green, and blue images, then projected them together.**

**Case: The Electric Motor. Faraday also made the first electric motor. A wire carrying current rotated around a magnet. This was the beginning of electric power.

**Case: The Light Experiment. Faraday rotated polarized light using a magnetic field. This was the first evidence that light and magnetism were connected.**

**Case: The Prediction. Maxwell's equations predicted that electromagnetic waves exist. No one had detected them. The prediction was made purely from mathematics.**
