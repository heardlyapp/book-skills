# Techniques: Mindfulness in Daily Life

## Basic Sitting Meditation

1. Find a comfortable seated position
2. Close your eyes or lower your gaze
3. Bring attention to your breath
4. When thoughts arise, observe them without judgment
5. Gently return attention to the breath

> **Case: The Pebble Meditation** (Chapter 5): Imagine yourself as a pebble dropped into a river. You sink slowly, without effort, settling into the soft mud at the bottom. You rest there, surrounded by the flowing water. This is the quality of mind in meditation — settled, at rest, while life flows around you.
> **Key takeaway**: Meditation is not about effort or control. It's about allowing yourself to settle naturally, like a pebble sinking in water.

## Walking Meditation Instructions

1. Walk slowly, naturally
2. Coordinate each step with your breath
3. Be aware of the sensations in your feet
4. Notice the ground beneath you
5. When the mind wanders, return to the steps

> **Case: The Airport Walk** (Chapter 6): Thich Nhat Hanh describes practicing walking meditation in an airport. While everyone rushed past, he walked mindfully — aware of each step, each breath. People around him seemed stressed and hurried. He was calm and present. The difference wasn't the environment — it was his state of mind.
> **Key takeaway**: You can practice mindfulness anywhere — even in a busy airport. The environment doesn't determine your peace; your presence does.
