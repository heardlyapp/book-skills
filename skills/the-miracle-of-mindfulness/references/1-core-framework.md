# Core Framework: Mindfulness as a Way of Life

## The Central Thesis

"Mindfulness is the miracle by which we master and restore ourselves. It is the energy of being aware of what is happening in the present moment, both inside and outside of us."

> **Case: The Washing Dishes Story** (Chapter 1): A person washes dishes to get them done, rushing through the task to move on to something "more important." Thich Nhat Hanh says this misses the point. When you wash dishes, wash dishes. Be fully present with the water, the soap, the movement of your hands. The dishwashing IS the meditation.
> **Key takeaway**: There is no separation between meditation and daily life. Every task is an opportunity for practice.

## Key Concepts

| Concept | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| Mindfulness | Being fully present in the moment, aware without judgment |
| Consciousness Breathing | Using breath as anchor for attention |
| Sitting Meditation | Formal practice of sitting in stillness |
| Walking Meditation | Meditation through mindful walking |
| Gatha | Short verse or poem for meditation focus |

> **Case: The Cup of Tea** (Chapter 2): When you drink tea, just drink tea. Don't think about the past or future. Feel the warmth of the cup, smell the aroma, taste the tea on your tongue. This is mindfulness. If you drink tea while thinking about work, you are not truly drinking tea.
> **Key takeaway**: Most of us go through life half-present. Mindfulness means fully inhabiting each moment. Even a simple cup of tea can be a profound meditation when done with full awareness.
