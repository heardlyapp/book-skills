# Author Voice and Application Scenarios — Feinman's Voice and Consumer Empowerment

## Jay M. Feinman's Voice

Feinman is a law professor who writes with academic rigor and righteous anger. He is outraged by the insurance industry's tactics, but his outrage is channeled into practical guidance. He wants readers to understand the system so they can fight it. His tone is: "They are taking advantage of you. Here is how they do it. Here is how to stop them."

## Five Common Application Scenarios

> **Case 1: The Accident Victim** — "I was in a car accident. The other driver's insurance is stalling. I need my car repaired." Activate `1-core-framework.md`. You are experiencing the Delay tactic. Send a demand letter. File a complaint. Consider hiring a lawyer.

> **Case 2: The Homeowner After a Disaster** — "Our house was damaged in a storm. The insurance company is lowballing us." Activate `4-anti-patterns.md`. The lowball offer is the first move. Do not accept it. Get your own contractor estimate. Negotiate.

> **Case 3: The Small Business Owner** — "My business was damaged. The insurance company is refusing to pay." Activate `2-principles.md`. Document everything. Appeal in writing. File a complaint with your state insurance department. Consider a lawyer.

> **Case 4: The Health Insurance Patient** — "My health insurance denied coverage for a procedure my doctor says I need." Activate `3-techniques.md`. The same Three D's apply. Appeal through your insurance company's internal process. Then external review. Then state regulator.

> **Case 5: The Concerned Consumer** — "I want to know what insurance companies are doing before I need to file a claim." Activate `1-core-framework.md`. Read your policy. Understand what is covered. Document your possessions. Keep records. The time to prepare is before the loss, not after.

## Classic Quotes

- "Document everything. The party with the better records wins."
- "The system counts on you giving up. Do not give up."
- "Most people accept the first offer. That is exactly what the insurance company expects."
