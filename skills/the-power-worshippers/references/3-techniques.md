## Practical Techniques

### 1. The Local Watch Strategy

Identify the school board, city council, and state legislative bodies in your area. Monitor their agendas and meetings. Christian nationalist groups often start at the local level where turnout is low and a small, organized group can have outsized influence.

### 2. The Model Bill Check

When you see a "religious liberty" bill in your state legislature, check if it matches a model bill from a national organization. Project Blitz bills often appear in multiple states simultaneously. Identify the pattern.

> **Case: The "License to Discriminate" Bills** (Chapter 7): Stewart shows how model bills from Project Blitz appeared in over 20 states simultaneously. The bills were almost identical — varying only in sponsor names and state-specific formatting. The same playbook was being run everywhere.
> **Key takeaway**: When you see the same bill in multiple states, it is not coincidence — it is coordination. Trace the bill back to its source.

### 3. The Legal Strategy Audit

When a religious liberty case makes headlines, trace the organizations behind it. ADF, Liberty Counsel, and similar groups often recruit plaintiffs, provide legal representation, and fund the litigation. The "ordinary person" in the news is often part of a coordinated strategy.

### 4. The Narrative Challenge

When someone claims "Christians are being persecuted," ask: "What specific right is being denied, and to whom?" The persecution narrative often collapses under scrutiny — but only if you ask the question.

> **Case: The "War on Christmas"** (Chapter 4): Stewart describes how Christian nationalist groups manufactured a "War on Christmas" narrative. Stores using "Happy Holidays" instead of "Merry Christmas" were framed as persecution. The narrative was designed to mobilize the base, not to address any actual denial of rights.
> **Key takeaway**: Cultural grievances are often manufactured for political mobilization. Ask: is this a real denial of rights, or a complaint about cultural change?
