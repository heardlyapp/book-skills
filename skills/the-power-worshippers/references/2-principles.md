## 7 Principles of Christian Nationalism

### 1. The Movement Is Political, Not Religious

Christian nationalism is not about salvation or worship. It is about taking and holding political power. The religious language is the vehicle, not the destination.

### 2. The Local Level Is the Front Line

National attention focuses on Washington — but the movement's most effective work happens in state legislatures and school boards. Project Blitz specifically targets states.

> **Case: The Project Blitz Playbook** (Chapter 7): Stewart obtained internal documents from Project Blitz, a coalition of Christian nationalist organizations. The playbook includes model bills for state legislatures — bills that look like routine religious liberty protections but are designed to create legal carve-outs for discrimination. The bills were being introduced simultaneously in multiple states, coordinated from a central source.
> **Key takeaway**: The movement thinks locally and acts nationally. Model bills allow them to fight on 50 fronts at once.

### 3. The Persecution Narrative Is Essential

To justify theocratic measures, Christian nationalists must portray Christians as an oppressed minority. The narrative of persecution creates the urgency and moral authority for their agenda.

### 4. "Religious Liberty" Has Been Redefined

The term used to mean freedom of conscience for everyone. Christian nationalists have redefined it to mean the right to impose one's religious beliefs on others through law.

### 5. The Legal Infrastructure Is Formidable

The Alliance Defending Freedom, Liberty Counsel, and other organizations have built a sophisticated legal machine. They file hundreds of lawsuits, train thousands of lawyers, and have won major victories at the Supreme Court.

> **Case: Masterpiece Cakeshop** (Chapter 10): Stewart analyzes the Masterpiece Cakeshop case (2018), where the Supreme Court ruled in favor of a baker who refused to make a wedding cake for a same-sex couple. The case was litigated by ADF. Stewart shows how the legal strategy worked: create a sympathetic plaintiff, frame the case as religious persecution, and win a narrow victory that can be expanded later.
> **Key takeaway**: Every legal victory is a building block. The movement does not need to win every case — it needs to shift the legal framework over time.

### 6. The Education Battle Is Central

If you control what children learn, you control the future. School boards, curriculum, libraries — these are the most intense battlefields.

### 7. The Goal Is Not Fairness — It Is Dominion

The ultimate goal is not equal treatment for Christians — it is Christian control of society. The Seven Mountains Mandate makes this explicit.
