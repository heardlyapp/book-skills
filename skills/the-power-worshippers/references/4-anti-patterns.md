## 6 Anti-Patterns of Misunderstanding

### 1. "This Is About Religion"

Christian nationalism is not about faith — it is about power. Treating it as a religious movement misunderstands its purpose and overestimates its sincerity.

### 2. "It's Just a Few Extremists"

The movement is large, organized, and funded. Project Blitz operates in 50 states. ADF has a billion-dollar budget. This is not a fringe — it is a network.

> **Case: The Billion-Dollar Network** (Chapter 2): Stewart traces the funding of the Christian nationalist network. ADF alone has an annual budget of over $100 million. The network includes dozens of organizations with combined budgets in the billions. This is not a grassroots movement — it is an industry.
> **Key takeaway**: The "fringe" narrative is comforting but false. The movement has resources that rival major political parties.

### 3. "They Just Want Equal Treatment"

The movement does not want equal treatment — it wants dominance. The Seven Mountains Mandate makes this explicit. Equal treatment is the rhetorical cover, not the goal.

### 4. "The Courts Will Protect Us"

The courts have been a major target of the movement. The Federalist Society has transformed the judiciary. The courts are not a neutral arbiter — they are a battlefield.

### 5. "This Is Only an American Problem"

Christian nationalism is a global movement. Stewart documents connections to similar movements in Russia, Hungary, Brazil, and elsewhere. The networks are international.

### 6. "It Will Fade Away"

The movement has been growing for 50 years. It is not a temporary phenomenon. It has survived Republican defeats and Democratic presidencies. It adapts and persists.

> **Case: The Post-Dobbs Strategy** (Chapter 10, updated epilogue): Stewart shows that the Christian nationalist movement did not stop after Dobbs (overturning Roe v. Wade). The goal was always broader: personhood laws, national abortion bans, and the criminalization of contraception.
> **Key takeaway**: The movement does not declare victory and stop. It takes what it wins and pushes for the next objective.
