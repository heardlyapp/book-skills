## Core Framework Quick Reference

1. **Christian Nationalism**: A political movement that believes America was founded as a Christian nation and should be governed by Christian law. It is distinct from Christianity as a faith — it is a political ideology that uses religious language.
2. **The Seven Mountains Mandate**: A dominionist theology that Christians must take control of seven "mountains" of culture: government, education, media, arts/entertainment, family, religion, and business. This is the theological framework for political takeover.
3. **Project Blitz**: A coordinated effort to pass hundreds of "religious liberty" bills in state legislatures across America. The bills are designed to look like reasonable accommodations but actually create legal carve-outs that allow discrimination.
4. **The Legal Strategy**: Using the Religious Freedom Restoration Act (RFRA) and "religious liberty" claims to win in court what cannot be won at the ballot box. The strategy: create a conflict between religious liberty and other rights (LGBTQ, reproductive), and claim religious liberty must win.
5. **The Education Battle**: Christian nationalists target school boards and public education. They push for school prayer, curriculum control, and the elimination of "critical race theory." The goal: shape what children learn.
6. **The Anti-Abortion Connection**: The Christian nationalist network is deeply intertwined with the anti-abortion movement. Crisis pregnancy centers, personhood laws, and the legal strategy that ultimately overturned Roe v. Wade were built by this network.

## Key Principles

1. Christian nationalism is not a fringe movement — it is organized, well-funded, and has a long-term strategy for taking power.
2. The movement uses the language of "religious liberty" to justify discrimination. The term has been redefined to mean the right to impose one's religious beliefs on others.
3. The most effective work happens at the state and local level — state legislatures, school boards, courts. The movement thinks locally and acts strategically.
4. The theology of dominionism is the engine. Without understanding the Seven Mountains Mandate, the political strategy makes no sense.
5. The movement is not monolithic. It includes theological dominionists, pragmatic political operatives, and millions of ordinary Christians who do not know they are being used.
6. The legal strategy depends on creating victims — Christians must be portrayed as persecuted to justify theocratic remedies. The persecution narrative is central.
7. The goal is not the Republican Party — the party is a vehicle. The goal is the transformation of American government into a Christian theocracy.
