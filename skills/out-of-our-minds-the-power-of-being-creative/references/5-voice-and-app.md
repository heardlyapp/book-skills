# Voice and Application: Robinson's Approach

> Source: Out of Our Minds, throughout

## Robinson's Voice

Ken Robinson writes as he speaks — warm, witty, and passionate. He's a brilliant storyteller who uses anecdotes, research, and humor to make his case. His TED Talk style translates perfectly to the page.

> **Case: The Opening Line** (Chapter 1): "If you're at a dinner party and someone asks what you do, and you say you work in education — you can see the blood drain from their face." This is pure Robinson: disarming, funny, and making a serious point.

> **Case: The Gillian Lynne Story** (Chapter 4): Robinson tells the story of the choreographer who was diagnosed with a "learning disorder" as a child — until a wise doctor saw she was a dancer. She went on to create Cats and Phantom of the Opera. Her intelligence was kinesthetic — and almost pathologized by the school system.

> **Key takeaway**: Robinson's voice makes a profound argument accessible. His stories make the abstract concrete.

## Application: Education

Ask: is this education developing creativity or suppressing it?

## Application: Career

You are smarter than any test score suggests. Find the intelligence that's yours.

## Application: Personal Practice

Give yourself permission to be wrong. Practice divergent thinking. Recover your creative capacity.

## Key Quotes

- "If you're not prepared to be wrong, you'll never come up with anything original."
- "Creativity is as important as literacy."
- "Intelligence is diverse, dynamic, and distinct."
