# Core Framework: The Creativity Crisis

> Source: Out of Our Minds, Chapter 1

## The Problem

Robinson opens with a paradox: we need creativity more than ever to solve unprecedented global challenges — yet our education systems systematically suppress it. Schools were designed for the Industrial Age to produce conformity, not originality.

> **Case: The TED Talk Phenomenon** (Chapter 1): Robinson's "Do schools kill creativity?" is the most-viewed TED talk of all time. The global resonance proves people sense their creative potential was educated out of them. The message crosses every cultural border.

> **Key takeaway**: The problem is universal. People everywhere feel they are capable of more than their education allowed them to become.

## Three Themes and the Industrial Model

Robinson builds the book around three themes: why creativity matters, why people think they're not creative, and how to create conditions where creativity flourishes.

> **Case: The Factory School** (Chapter 1): Modern education traces to the Industrial Revolution — factory-like schools with bells, age grouping, standardized curriculum, and conformity. This model suppressed creativity by design. It needed compliant workers and obedient citizens.

> **Case: The Changing World** (Chapter 2): Robinson argues the 21st century is defined by accelerating change — technology, demographics, climate, economics. Preparing students for a static world is not just inadequate — it's dangerous.

> **Key takeaway**: The industrial model of education is not just obsolete — it's actively harmful. We're preparing students for a world that no longer exists.
