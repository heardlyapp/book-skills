# 1 — Month-by-Month Baby Development

> Source: "What to Expect the First Year" by Heidi Murkoff

## The First Year at a Glance

The book is organized into 18 monthly chapters, each covering what to expect in terms of growth, feeding, sleep, and development. Key themes: every baby is different, but there are predictable patterns.

> **Case: The Newborn Phase** (Chapter 5 — The Newborn): "Your newborn has just spent nine months in a warm, dark, quiet environment. Now they're in a world of bright lights, loud noises, and new sensations. Be patient. The first few weeks are about adjustment, not achievement." Murkoff emphasizes that the newborn period is about bonding and basic care, not development.
> **Key takeaway**: The first month is about survival, not milestones. Feed on demand, sleep when baby sleeps, and don't worry about "falling behind." Everything comes in time.

> **Case: The 4-Month Leap** (Chapter 8 — The Fourth Month): "Around 4 months, your baby will go through a major developmental leap. They'll become much more aware of their surroundings, start reaching for objects, and may begin rolling over. This is also when sleep regression often hits." Murkoff prepares parents for this transition by marking it as a phase, not a problem.
> **Key takeaway**: Development happens in bursts, not linear progress. The 4-month mark is a common inflection point. The sleep regression that often accompanies it is a sign of neurological development, not a step backward.
