# 2 — Feeding and Nutrition

> Source: "What to Expect the First Year" by Heidi Murkoff

## Feeding as a Foundation

Feeding is the primary activity of the first year. The book covers breastfeeding, bottle-feeding, starting solids, and the transition to table food — all with the philosophy that fed is best.

> **Case: Breastfeeding 101** (Chapter on Breastfeeding): "Breastfeeding is a learning process for both mother and baby. It takes time to get the latch right, to establish supply, and to feel comfortable. Most problems — sore nipples, engorgement, low supply — can be solved with proper positioning and frequent feeding." Murkoff provides detailed guidance on latch, positioning, and troubleshooting.
> **Key takeaway**: Breastfeeding is learned, not automatic. Pain is a sign something is wrong — seek help from a lactation consultant. Most common problems have simple solutions, but they require patience and support.

> **Case: Starting Solids** (Chapter 11 — The Sixth Month): "Around 6 months, your baby will show signs of readiness: sitting with support, good head control, interest in what you're eating, and loss of the tongue-thrust reflex. Start with single-ingredient purees — one new food every 3–5 days — to watch for allergic reactions." Murkoff emphasizes the gradual, observational approach to introducing solids.
> **Key takeaway**: The readiness signs matter more than the exact age. Start simple, go slow, and don't worry about quantity in the beginning. The first few months of solids are about exploration and practice, not nutrition.
