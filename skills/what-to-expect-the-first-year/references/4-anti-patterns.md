# 4 — Health, Safety, and Common Mistakes

> Source: "What to Expect the First Year" by Heidi Murkoff

## Anti-Pattern: Delaying or Skipping Vaccines

The most dangerous mistake a parent can make. The book follows CDC/APA guidelines and is emphatic that vaccines are safe, effective, and essential.

> **Case: The Vaccine Schedule** (Chapter on Vaccines): "The CDC-recommended vaccine schedule is designed to protect babies when they are most vulnerable. Delaying vaccines leaves your baby unprotected during a critical period. The schedule is backed by decades of research and millions of data points." Murkoff addresses vaccine concerns directly and provides the standard vaccination timeline (2, 4, 6, 12 months).
> **Key takeaway**: The vaccine schedule is not arbitrary — it's designed to protect babies at the ages when they are most at risk from preventable diseases. Delaying or skipping vaccines increases risk without evidence of benefit.

## Anti-Pattern: Underestimating Baby Mobility

Parents often don't realize how quickly babies become mobile. A baby who couldn't roll yesterday might be crawling today.

> **Case: Baby-Proofing Before Baby Moves** (Chapter on Safety): "Baby-proof your home before your baby becomes mobile — not after. By the time you see your baby heading for the stairs or reaching for an electrical outlet, it's already too late. Think ahead: get down on your hands and knees and see your home from your baby's perspective." Murkoff provides a room-by-room safety checklist.
> **Key takeaway**: Baby-proofing must be proactive, not reactive. Once your baby is mobile, they will find hazards faster than you can intervene. The time to install gates, cover outlets, and secure furniture is before baby needs them.
