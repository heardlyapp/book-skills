# 3 — Sleep Guidance

> Source: "What to Expect the First Year" by Heidi Murkoff

## Sleep Is Developmental

Sleep patterns in the first year change dramatically. Newborns sleep in short bursts around the clock. By 6 months, many babies can sleep through the night. The book provides a reassuring, evidence-based approach to sleep.

> **Case: The Safe Sleep Position** (Chapter on Sleeping): "The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends placing babies on their backs to sleep — every sleep, every time. This single recommendation has reduced SIDS rates by over 50% since the 1990s." Murkoff is emphatic about back-sleeping as the single most important safety measure.
> **Key takeaway**: Always put baby on their back. No exceptions. Not on the side, not on the stomach. This applies to naps and nighttime sleep. Remove soft bedding, pillows, and stuffed animals from the crib.

> **Case: The 4-Month Sleep Regression** (Chapter 8 — The Fourth Month): "Just when you think you've got this sleep thing figured out, your baby's sleep patterns change. The 4-month sleep regression is actually a sign of healthy neurological development — your baby is becoming more aware of their environment." Murkoff frames sleep regression as a normal developmental stage, not a problem to be solved.
> **Key takeaway**: Sleep regression is temporary (typically 2–6 weeks) and developmentally normal. Maintain consistent routines, resist the urge to introduce new sleep crutches, and know that this phase will pass.
