# Letting Go — The Core Practice

> Source: The Untethered Soul, Chapters 8, 9, 11, 12

## The Technique

### How to Let Go

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Notice the thought or emotion |
| 2 | Do not resist. Do not suppress. Do not analyze. |
| 3 | Relax the muscles that are holding it |
| 4 | Let it move through you and out |
| 5 | Return to openness |

> **Case: Let Go or Fall (Chapter 8)**
> Singer uses the analogy of catching yourself before you fall. When you start to fall emotionally, you can catch yourself — but you will be stiff and rigid. Or you can relax and let yourself fall. The fall is the same either way. The difference is resistance. Letting go is not about avoiding the experience — it is about not fighting it. Singer writes: "Let go now, or you will fall. Actually, you will fall either way. The only difference is whether you will be relaxed when you hit."
> **Key takeaway:** Letting go is the opposite of controlling. You cannot control your thoughts and emotions. You can only release them. The paradox: the more you try to control, the more trapped you become. The more you release, the freer you are.

> **Case: Death and Letting Go (Chapter 10)**
> The ultimate letting go is death. Singer teaches that the fear of death is the root of all other fears. If you can release your attachment to the self — the "me" that thinks it is separate — then death is not something to fear. It is the final release. Singer does not promise an afterlife. He teaches: the moment of death is the final opportunity to let go completely. If you have practiced letting go your whole life, you will be ready.
> **Key takeaway:** Singer's teaching on death is the most radical part of the book. He does not offer comfort — he offers freedom. The lesson: practice letting go now, so you are ready for the final letting go.
