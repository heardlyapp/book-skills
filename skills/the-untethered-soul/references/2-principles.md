# Energy and the Spiritual Heart

> Source: The Untethered Soul, Chapters 5, 6, 7

## The Heart Center

### Opening and Closing

| State | Experience |
|-------|-----------|
| Heart open | Love, connection, beauty, joy, presence |
| Heart closing | Fear, defense, separation, contraction, protection |

> **Case: The Spiritual Heart (Chapter 6)**
> Singer describes the heart as an energy center, not just a physical organ. When the heart is open, you feel love, beauty, and connection spontaneously. When it is closed, you feel separate, defended, and alone. Most people's hearts are partially closed — not from maliciousness but from survival. The spiritual path is learning to release the defenses that close the heart.
> **Key takeaway:** The spiritual heart is the key to the book. Singer's teaching: your heart naturally wants to be open. Closing is a defense against pain. The path is to release the defenses and let the heart open naturally.

> **Case: The Tendency to Close (Chapter 7)**
> When you experience pain — emotional or physical — your heart closes as a reflex. This reflex is useful in the moment: it protects you. But if you do not release the contraction, it becomes a permanent blockage. You cannot feel deep love when your heart is protected. Singer teaches: the pain passes if you do not hold it. The contraction is optional. Relax. Let the pain pass through.
> **Key takeaway:** Closing is a habit that can be unlearned. The reflex to close is not the problem — the problem is that we forget to reopen. The lesson: after every contraction, consciously choose to open again.
