# Anti-Patterns — The Heat Will Kill You First

## The Central Error: Thinking Heat Is Just Uncomfortable

"Who can tell the difference between a 77-degree day and an 81-degree day?" 3.6°C (2°C) of warming sounds modest. It is not. "Extreme heat is a force beyond anything we have reckoned with before."

### Mistake 1: "I'm young and fit, so I'll be fine"
Kelly Watt (18, track star). Philip Kreycik (37, ultramarathoner). Michael Popov (34, world-class ultrarunner carrying 4 bottles of water and ice). All died. "Being young and strong allows you to fight off warning signs until it's too late." Most heatstroke victims are young men.

### Mistake 2: "Heat waves are just hot days"
Goodell: "Temperature is a merry-go-round we are used to riding." But extreme heat is different. It bends railroad tracks. It kills a billion sea creatures in 3 days. It turns a town to ash. "This is heat as an active force."

### Mistake 3: "If I drink enough water, I'll be safe"
"Both heat exhaustion and heatstroke can occur in the absence of dehydration." Hydration helps, but it does not prevent heatstroke. Only cooling the core body temperature can do that. "The only effective treatment for heatstroke is to get core body temperature down, fast."

### Mistake 4: "We can adapt our way out of this"
AC works — but it consumes fossil fuels and leaks super-potent refrigerants. "We're cooking ourselves to cool ourselves." Shade trees help — but "planting a few thousand trees is not going to save" the billion people in Pakistan and India. "If you have enough money, you can engineer your way out of anything" — but most people don't.

### Mistake 5: "Extreme cold is worse"
This is a common argument from climate skeptics. Goodell acknowledges cold kills too. But "the number of cold-related deaths is declining as the planet warms, while heat-related deaths are rising." And the net effect: more death.

### Mistake 6: "If we stop emitting CO2, things will cool off"
"CO2 stays in the atmosphere for thousands of years. All that will do is stop the increase in warming. It will not reverse it." The heat already baked in will persist for centuries.

### Mistake 7: "My city is safe from this"
The Pacific Northwest was considered a "climate refuge." Then it hit 114°F. "A killer heat wave in the Pacific Northwest seemed about as likely as snow in the Sahara." No place is safe. Heat doesn't respect borders or climate models.
