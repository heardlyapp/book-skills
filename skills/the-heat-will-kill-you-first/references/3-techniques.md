# Techniques — The Heat Will Kill You First

## How to Survive and Understand Extreme Heat (Based on Jeff Goodell)

### Technique 1: Know the Wet Bulb Temperature
The air temperature is only half the story. Wet bulb (temperature + humidity) determines whether sweat can evaporate to cool your body. 95°F wet bulb = survival limit. Check wet bulb, not just temperature, before outdoor activity in extreme heat.

### Technique 2: The Water Trap
"Proper hydration cannot prevent heatstroke." Drinking water keeps sweat flowing but doesn't cool core temperature. Goodell: "Dehydration can exacerbate heat exhaustion, but you can still die of heatstroke and be well hydrated." Hydration is necessary but not sufficient. The real solution: get out of the heat.

### Technique 3: The Twenty-Five-Degree Test
During a heat wave, check the temperature difference between the poorest and richest parts of your city. Portland: 124°F in Lents vs 99°F in Willamette Heights. The technique: heat vulnerability maps reveal where trees are missing, where concrete dominates, and where investment is needed. "If you want to know who's most at risk, follow the money."

### Technique 4: The Muscle Heat Ratio
"Only 20% of the energy you expend goes to muscle contraction; the other 80% is released as heat." This is why exertional heatstroke kills athletes, not just the elderly. The technique: in extreme heat, don't exercise. Period.

### Technique 5: Early Warning Saves Lives
Gerrish and Chung died despite being warned. But explicit warnings (not just "it's going to be hot") can save lives. Heat alerts should specify: "Do not go outside between 10 AM and 4 PM. Do not exercise. Without AC, your home may reach lethal temperatures."

### Technique 6: The Canary in the Coal Mine
Salmon disintegrating in warm rivers. Starfish dying by the billions. Trees "screaming" (according to scientists with microphones). These are early warnings of system collapse. The technique: don't wait for human death tolls to act. When nature breaks, the breaking has already begun.

### Technique 7: Plan for the Worst, Hope for the Best
"One to three billion people will be left outside the climate conditions that gave rise to civilization." Goodell's technique: accept that adaptation has limits, but do it anyway. Cool roofs. Shade trees. Heat pumps. Resilient crops. "We have to act as if we can solve this."
