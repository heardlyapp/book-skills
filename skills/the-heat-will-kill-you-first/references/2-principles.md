# Key Principles — The Heat Will Kill You First

## I. Heat Is the Engine of Planetary Chaos

"All the climate impacts you hear about — sea-level rise, drought, wildfires — are second-order effects. The first-order effect is heat." Heat melts ice sheets, dries soil, revs up bugs, thaws permafrost, and drives the next pandemic. Understanding heat means understanding everything.

## II. Heat Is Invisible, Sudden, and Lethal

"Extreme heat penetrates every living cell and melts them like a Popsicle on a summer sidewalk." 489,000 people died from heat in 2019 — more than guns and illegal drugs combined. The Pacific Northwest heat dome killed 1,000 people in 72 hours. Lytton BC reached 121°F and burned to the ground the next day. Goodell: "When heat comes, it's invisible."

## III. Wealth = Cool. Poverty = Vulnerable.

The twenty-five-degree difference between Lents ($30K/year) and Willamette Heights ($1M homes) in Portland during the heat dome is not an accident. It's the legacy of redlining, disinvestment, and tree canopy distribution. "Heat exposes deep fissures of inequity and injustice." In India, families burn their hands opening doors in concrete slums. In Pakistan, only the rich have AC.

## IV. The Body Has Limits — And We Are Reaching Them

95°F wet bulb = the theoretical limit of human survival. Gerrish and Chung were young, fit, and had 85 oz of water. They died. Kelly Watt, Philip Kreycik, Michael Popov — ultrarunners who died from hyperthermia. "Being young or in great shape won't save you." Hydration alone does not prevent heatstroke.

## V. The Energy Paradox: We Are Cooking Ourselves to Cool Ourselves

AC saves lives but consumes enormous fossil-fuel energy and leaks super-potent greenhouse gases. "Cheap cold air" is both the solution and the problem. The ocean absorbs heat equivalent to 3 nuclear bombs per second. We are in "the terraforming business now, whether we like it or not."

## VI. The Great Migration Has Already Begun

Animals move north at 1 mile/year. Mosquitoes at 2.5 miles/year. Humans will follow. "One to three billion people will be left outside the climate conditions that gave rise to civilization." Wealthy people move to cooler places. Poor people get left behind. "We're all in the storm, but we're not in the same boat."

## VII. Adaptation Has Limits — But We Must Try

Goodell is skeptical of techno-optimism. "Planting a few thousand trees is not going to save them." But he also acknowledges adaptation is necessary: shade trees, white roofs, heat warnings, behavioral changes, geoengineering. "We have to act as if we can solve this, because the alternative is unthinkable." The book ends with an open question: can we?
