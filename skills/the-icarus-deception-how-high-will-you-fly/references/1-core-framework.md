# Core Framework: The Icarus Deception

> Source: Icarus Deception, Introduction & Part Zero

## The Misunderstood Myth

The story of Icarus is taught as a cautionary tale against hubris — don't fly too close to the sun or your wings will melt. Godin argues the real lesson is the opposite: don't fly too low. The danger is safety, mediocrity, and conformity. The sea (playing it safe) is as dangerous as the sun.

> **Case: The Myth Retold** (Introduction): Daedalus warned Icarus about two dangers: flying too high toward the sun AND flying too low where the sea would wet his wings and drag him down. We've been taught only one side — don't aim too high. The real risk is hovering in the safe middle, never daring to take off at all.

> **Case: Industrial Age Thinking** (Part One): The factory system was designed for compliance. Follow instructions. Don't question authority. Fit in. Get your ticket punched. This mindset was safe on the assembly line but is dangerous in the connection economy. The skills that made you safe in the industrial age — obedience, conformity, reliability — make you invisible now.

## We Are All Artists Now

Art is "the truly human act of creating something new that matters to another person." It doesn't require paint or a gallery. You can make art as a CEO, barista, engineer, or parent.

> **Key takeaway**: "Your ability to follow instructions is not the secret to your success." The connection economy rewards creators, not followers. The only truly safe path is to make art.
