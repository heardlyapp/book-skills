# Techniques: Grit, The Dip, and 87 Ideas

> Source: Icarus Deception, Part Five & Appendix

## Grit and the Dip

The Dip is the difficult middle between starting and succeeding. It's the long, boring, difficult stretch where most people quit. The ones who push through win — not because they're more talented, but because they have more grit.

> **Case: The 14 Real Artists** (Appendix 1): Godin profiles 14 artists who persisted through rejection and failure. Each faced the Dip and chose to keep going — musicians who kept playing empty rooms, writers who collected rejection letters, entrepreneurs whose first three businesses failed. Their common trait: not talent, but grit.

> **Key takeaway**: The Dip is where careers are made. Push through one more day, one more try.

## 87 Ideas for Thinking Like an Artist

Part Five contains 87+ short provocations. Each is designed to jolt you into seeing differently. Examples: "Your art is what you do when no one is watching" and "The opposite of art is not bad art — it's indifference."

> **Case: V Is for Vulnerable** (Appendix 2): An abecedary — an alphabet for artists. A is for Anxiety ("the feeling you get right before you make something new"), B is for Brave, C is for Courage. Each letter is a creative prompt.

## The Artist's Practice

Godin's practical advice: show up every day, do the work, share it, ignore the critics, do it again. The discipline of daily practice matters more than any single creation.

> **Key takeaway**: Art is a practice, not a product. Show up every day. Make something. Put it out there. Repeat.
