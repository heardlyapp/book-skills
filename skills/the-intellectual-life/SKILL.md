### Philosophy — 5 Rules

1. **Truth serves only its slaves.** Knowledge demands an initial outlay most are unwilling to pay. The price is your whole life, not your spare time.
2. **Everything in you must become intellectual.** Not just your brain — your body, your habits, your home, your friendships, your sleep.
3. **Not reading much, but reading well.** A page assimilated is worth a library skimmed. Digestion over accumulation.
4. **The intellect cannot produce without solitude.** You must create a "zone of silence" around your work. Society is the enemy of depth.
5. **Do not seek what is beyond your reach.** Accept your measure. Your vocation is irreplaceable — play your part, not another's.

### Core Framework Quick Reference

- **The Vocation** — Not a self-elected title but a sacred call discerned through instinct, aptitude, and Providence. You don't choose it; you recognize it.
- **Studiousness (Temperance of Knowledge)** — Know when to pursue knowledge and when to stop. Not enough = negligence. Too much = vain curiosity.
- **The Three Solitudes** — (1) Physical solitude (quiet place). (2) Intellectual solitude (freedom from conventional thinking). (3) Interior silence (stillness before truth).
- **Digestive Reading** — Four modes: (1) exploratory, (2) assimilative/RCR (read, copy, reflect), (3) creative consultation, (4) reading for pleasure.
- **The Writer's Discipline** — Write daily. Write despite mood. Finish what you start. "Set your work before you like a task and accomplish it."

### Key Principles

1. **Simplify your life.** Cut possessions, distractions, social obligations. The intellectual needs a "scantily furnished dwelling."
2. **Guard your mornings.** The first hours are sacred. Do not let correspondence, visitors, or trivialities steal them.
3. **Read with pen in hand.** Books are not an end — they are fuel for your own thinking. React, excerpt, annotate.
4. **Do not pursue two hares.** Specialize within a broad foundation. Depth before breadth.
5. **Finish everything.** An incomplete work is a betrayal of the truth glimpsed but not delivered.
6. **Keep contact with life.** The intellectual does not live in an ivory tower. Action refreshes thought.
7. **Pray before you think.** Not religious ritual — but the disposition of humility before truth that transcends you.

### Anti-Pattern Summary

The central error: **believing intellectual work is purely a mental exercise that can succeed without moral and physical discipline.** The intellect does not function in isolation from the soul. Character, body, and environment are not accessories — they are the foundation. See `references/4-anti-patterns.md`.
