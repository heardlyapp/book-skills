# 3. Techniques — Weapons Technology of the AK-47

> Source: "The Gun" by C. J. Chivers (2010). Technical features that made the AK-47 revolutionary.

## The Gas Piston

The AK-47 uses a long-stroke gas piston system. When a bullet is fired, expanding gas is tapped from the barrel and drives a piston that cycles the action. This system is powerful enough to force the action through dirt, sand, and ice.

> **Case: The Mud Test** (Chapter 5): In Soviet testing, the AK-47 was dragged through mud, buried in sand, and frozen in ice. It continued to fire. Western rifles subjected to the same tests jammed. This reliability became the AK-47's signature.
> **Key takeaway**: Test your product in the conditions where it will actually be used. Laboratory conditions lie.

## Stamped vs. Milled Receivers

Early AK-47s had milled receivers (machined from solid steel). Later versions used stamped receivers (pressed from sheet metal). The stamped receiver was cheaper and faster to produce.

## The 7.62x39mm Cartridge

The AK-47 fires a medium-power cartridge that balances range, stopping power, and controllability in automatic fire. The cartridge was specifically designed for the assault rifle concept — a weapon capable of both semi-automatic and fully automatic fire.

> **Case: The Cartridge Choice** (Chapter 5): The 7.62x39mm round was a compromise between the power of a full rifle round and the controllability of a pistol round. This compromise defined the assault rifle category.
> **Key takeaway**: The most successful designs are often compromises that get the balance right.
