# 1. Core Framework — Three Eras of Automatic Weapons

> Source: "The Gun" by C. J. Chivers (2010). The book traces three technological eras: Maxim, Kalashnikov, and the global aftermath.

## Era 1: The Maxim Revolution (1884-1914)

Hiram Maxim invented the first fully automatic machine gun in 1884. Using the recoil energy of the cartridge to eject the spent round and load the next, his gun could fire 600 rounds per minute — the equivalent of 100 riflemen.

> **Case: Maxim's Demonstration** (Chapter 3): Maxim demonstrated his gun to European military attachés in 1885. He cut down a tree with automatic fire. The officers were horrified and impressed. Within a decade, every European power had adopted machine guns.
> **Key takeaway**: A single invention can obsolete centuries of military tactics in a single demonstration.

## Era 2: Kalashnikov and the AK-47 (1947-1991)

Mikhail Kalashnikov, a wounded Soviet tank commander, designed the AK-47 to meet the Red Army's need for a reliable automatic rifle. His design was simple, durable, and easy to manufacture.

> **Case: Kalashnikov's Design Process** (Chapter 5): Kalashnikov had no formal engineering training. He was a self-taught inventor who examined German and American designs and created something new. His AK-47 was not a copy — it was a synthesis of existing ideas refined through relentless testing.
> **Key takeaway**: Innovation often comes from outsiders who are not constrained by "how things are done."

## Era 3: The Global Spread (1950s-present)

The USSR distributed AK-47s to allied states, liberation movements, and revolutionary groups worldwide. Licensed production in China, Eastern Europe, and the Middle East. Black market proliferation after the Cold War.
