# Techniques: The First Day Script

## The First Day Plan

1. **Before students arrive**: Write a meaningful assignment on the board. Place it on each desk. Organize materials.
2. **Greet students at the door**: Smile, make eye contact, hand them any materials.
3. **Start immediately**: The assignment on the desk tells students to begin working. No waiting for everyone to arrive.
4. **Teach your first procedure**: How to begin class. Poster visible. Demonstrate. Practice.
5. **Communicate your commitment**: "I'm here to help every one of you succeed."

> **Case: The Seating Chart Procedure** (Chapter 11): A teacher assigns seats on day one. Students enter, find their seat, and begin the assignment. This simple procedure eliminates chaos and signals that the classroom is organized.
> **Key takeaway**: Every procedure, no matter how small, contributes to the overall environment. Assigning seats is not authoritarian — it's efficient.

> **Case: The Morning Routine** (Chapter 13): A third-grade teacher spent the first week teaching one procedure: the morning entry routine. Students entered, hung coats, turned in homework, checked the schedule, and began morning work — all without a word from the teacher. By week two, it was automatic. The procedure saved 10 minutes every morning.
>
> **Key takeaway**: Time invested in teaching procedures pays back multiplied over the school year. Ten minutes saved per day equals over 30 hours saved per year.

## Procedure Inventory

Procedures needed for:
- Entering the classroom
- Starting the day's work
- Asking a question
- Participating in discussion
- Turning in work
- Working in groups
- Transitions between activities
- What to do when finished early
- Exiting the classroom

## Teaching a Procedure

1. **Explain** — State the procedure clearly
2. **Demonstrate** — Show what it looks like
3. **Practice** — Have students practice it
4. **Reinforce** — Praise correct practice, reteach if needed
5. **Repeat** — Continue until it's automatic

## The Script for the First Day

Time | Activity
-----|----------
Before | Assignment on desks, materials ready
Bell | Greet at door, students begin assignment
First 5 min | Take attendance while students work
5-10 min | Welcome, introduce first procedure
10-20 min | Teach/ practice procedure
20-30 min | Continue assignment or introduce content
30-45 min | Teach second procedure
45-60 min | Content activity using procedures taught
