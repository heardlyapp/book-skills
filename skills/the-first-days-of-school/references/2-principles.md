> Source: The First Days of School, Chapters 4-9

# Key Principles

## 1. Procedures, Not Rules

"Rules tell students what NOT to do. Procedures tell students what TO do. Effective classrooms run on procedures." The Wongs emphasize that procedures should be taught, practiced, and reinforced — the same way a coach teaches a team.

## 2. Teach Procedures Explicitly

"Students don't know the procedures unless you teach them. They're not being difficult — they just don't know what to do." The effective teacher spends the first week teaching procedures: how to enter, how to ask a question, how to turn in work, how to transition between activities.

## 3. The First Day Sets the Tone

"The first day of school is not the time to read the syllabus, discuss rules, or give a lecture. It's the time to begin learning." Students form a lasting impression of the class based on the first few minutes.

> **Case: The Teacher Who Started Late** (Chapter 8): A teacher spent the first class period going over rules, reading the syllabus, and telling students about herself. Students were bored and disengaged. The following year, she started with an engaging assignment on each desk. The difference in student engagement was dramatic.
> **Key takeaway**: Engagement starts on day one, minute one. Don't waste the first day on logistics. Start learning immediately.

## 4. Positive Expectations Drive Achievement

"What you expect is what you get. Students perform to the level you expect of them." The Wongs cite research showing that teacher expectations have a powerful effect on student achievement.

## 5. Consistency Creates Safety

"Students feel safe when they know what to expect. Consistency is more important than severity." A predictable classroom environment reduces anxiety and increases learning.

> **Case: The Substitute Teacher** (Chapter 9): A well-managed classroom runs so smoothly that a substitute teacher can step in and procedures continue automatically. This is the test of effective classroom management: does the class function without you?
> **Key takeaway**: The goal of classroom management is a classroom that runs itself. When procedures are automatic, learning is maximized.
