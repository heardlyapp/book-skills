# Core Framework: The Three Characteristics of Effective Teachers

## The Central Insight

"The single most important factor in student achievement is the effectiveness of the teacher." The Wong's framework identifies three characteristics that all effective teachers share — and these can be learned.

> **Case: The Effective Teacher Study** (Chapter 1): Studies consistently show that students taught by effective teachers learn significantly more than those taught by ineffective teachers — regardless of class size, funding, or student background. The teacher is the variable that matters most.
> **Key takeaway**: Improving teacher effectiveness is the most powerful lever for improving student outcomes. The good news: effectiveness can be learned.

## The Three Characteristics

| Characteristic | What It Means |
|----------------|---------------|
| Good classroom management | Has clear procedures and routines that create a productive environment |
| Teaches for mastery | Designs lessons that ensure every student learns |
| Positive expectations | Believes all students can succeed and communicates that belief |

> **Case: The First Day Assignment** (Chapter 3): An effective teacher hands out a meaningful assignment as students enter the room on the first day. While students work, the teacher takes attendance and prepares. The message: "This class is about learning, and it starts right now." The ineffective teacher gives a syllabus, talks about rules, and wastes the first day.
> **Key takeaway**: The first minute of the first day sends a powerful message. Effective teachers use that minute to signal: learning starts now.
