# Author's Voice + Key Scenarios

> **Case: The Wongs' Teaching Legacy** (Foreword): Harry Wong was a high school science teacher before becoming one of the most influential figures in American education. His book has sold millions of copies and is used in teacher preparation programs nationwide.
> **Key takeaway**: The Wongs' authority comes from decades of classroom experience, not academic theory. Their principles have been tested in thousands of classrooms.

> **Case: The Teacher Who Transformed** (Chapter 19): A struggling teacher was told by her principal to read The First Days of School. She implemented the procedures systematically. Within two weeks, her classroom was transformed. Students who had been "impossible" were now on task. Procedures changed everything.
> **Key takeaway**: If you're struggling in the classroom, the problem is likely not your students or your subject knowledge — it's your procedures. Fix those, and everything else follows.

## Key Scenarios

### Scenario 1: New Teacher
Script your first three days. Teach procedures. Start with an assignment on each desk.

### Scenario 2: Classroom Chaos
Identify the procedures that are missing. Teach them. Practice them. Enforce them consistently.

### Scenario 3: Disengaged Students
Check your procedures. Are students clear on what to do at every moment? Chaos causes disengagement.

### Scenario 4: Difficult Parent
Procedures protect you. When a parent questions your approach, you can point to the established, consistent system.

### Scenario 5: Improving as a Teacher
Focus on the three characteristics: management, mastery teaching, positive expectations.
