# Anti-Patterns: Gathering Mistakes

> Source: The Art of Gathering, Chapters 8 and Conclusion

## Pretending There Is No Power Dynamic

Every gathering has unspoken power dynamics. Ignoring them doesn't help.

> **Case: The Boss's Party** (Chapter 8): When a boss hosts, hierarchy is present even if unstated. A good host names it: "Tonight I'm not your boss — I'm your host." Explicitly setting a different norm for the gathering transforms the dynamic.

> **Key takeaway**: Acknowledge power dynamics rather than pretending they don't exist. Rename the roles explicitly.

## Not Closing Properly

Most gatherings fade awkwardly. This undermines the entire experience.

> **Case: The Lingering Dinner** (Chapter 8): After the main event, guests linger, phones come out, energy drains. Parker's advice: close decisively. "Thank you all for coming. I'll see you to the door." The ending shapes how the gathering is remembered.

> **Key takeaway**: Close deliberately and decisively. A strong ending preserves the gathering's meaning.

## Inviting Everyone

Fear of excluding someone dilutes the experience for everyone.

> **Case: The Overflowing Party** (Conclusion): A host invites everyone they know to avoid offending anyone. Result: no deep conversations, no intimacy, no connection. Fewer guests, clearer purpose, better experience for everyone.

> **Key takeaway**: Every extra guest dilutes the gathering. Invite with purpose, not obligation. Exclusion is kindness.
