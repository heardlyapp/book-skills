# Manipulation Framework — Covert Aggression

Simon's central idea: aggression is not always obvious. Covert-aggressive people hide their intent.

**Case: The Guilt Trip. A manipulator makes you feel responsible for their unhappiness. "If you really cared, you would do this."**

**Case: The Victim. The manipulator portrays themselves as the injured party. You become the aggressor for setting boundaries.**

**Case: Gaslighting. The manipulator denies reality. "That never happened. You are imagining things." You begin to doubt yourself.**

**Case: The Blame Shift. Everything is your fault. "If you weren't so sensitive, I would not have to do this."**

**Case: Minimizing. Your concerns are dismissed. "You are overreacting. It was not that bad."**
