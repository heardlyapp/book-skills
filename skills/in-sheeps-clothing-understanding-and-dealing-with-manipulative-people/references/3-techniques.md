# Techniques for Dealing with Manipulators

**Technique 1: Name the Tactic. When you identify a tactic, name it. "I recognize you are trying to make me feel guilty."**

**Technique 2: Set Clear Boundaries. State what you will and will not accept. Stick to it.**

**Technique 3: Stay Calm. Manipulators feed on emotional reactions. Do not give them one.**

**Case: The Broken Record. Repeat your boundary calmly. "I understand you feel that way. My answer is no."**

**Case: The Time-Out. "I need to think about this. We can talk later." Remove yourself from the pressure.**

**Case: The Direct Question. "What exactly are you asking me to do?" Force them to be explicit.**

**Case: The Written Record. Writing down conversations counters gaslighting.**

**Case: The Witness. A neutral party for important conversations helps.**

**Case: The Script. Prepare a script for difficult conversations. Practice it.**

**Case: The Pause. Before responding, pause. Count to three. This breaks the reactive cycle.**

**Case: The Phone. If a conversation becomes manipulative on the phone, say: I need to go. We can talk later.**

**Case: The Email. Written communication gives you time to think. Use email for important boundaries.**

**Case: The Ultimatum. Set a clear boundary: If X happens again, I will do Y. Then follow through.**
