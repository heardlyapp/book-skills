# Principles of Self-Defense Against Manipulation

**Principle 1: Recognize Covert Aggression. Not all aggression is obvious. Hidden aggression is the most dangerous kind.**

**Principle 2: Do Not Try to Change Them. Manipulators do not change because they do not think they are wrong.**

**Principle 3: Use Assertiveness, Not Aggression. Stand up for yourself without attacking back.**

**Case: The Boss. A manager who uses guilt to get employees to work overtime. Recognize the tactic, set boundaries.**

**Case: The Partner. A romantic partner who uses shame to control behavior. "You are not good enough." Recognize the tactic.**

**Case: The Parent. A parent who uses obligation. "After everything I have done for you." Recognize the tactic.**
