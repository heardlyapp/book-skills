## Harris's Voice

Harris writes in short, punchy sentences. She does not use jargon. She tells the story as it happened, without unnecessary adornment.

**Case: The Opening.** "A small fist rapped gently on my bedroom door." The book opens not with a policy statement but with a child waking her up. The voice establishes: this is a personal story, not a political treatise.

**Case: The Phone Call.** "Hi, Joe,' I said. 'I need to talk to you.' He was calling from his home in Rehoboth Beach." The dialogue is direct. No description of the weather, no setting of the scene. Just the conversation.

## The Structure as Countdown

The book is organized as a countdown from 107 days to Election Day. Each chapter is headed with the number of days remaining. The countdown structure creates a sense of urgency — the reader experiences the same pressure the candidate felt.

**Case: Day 107.** The first chapter covers the phone call, the transition, and the first hours of the campaign. By the end, the countdown is already at Day 106.

**Case: Day 1.** The final chapter covers Election Day itself. After all the drama, all the work, all the sacrifice — the voters decide. The countdown ends not with a conclusion but with an opening: the beginning of something new.
