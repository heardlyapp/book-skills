## 7 Principles of Direct Seeing

### 1. Truth Cannot Be Taught

Truth is not a piece of information that can be transmitted. It must be seen. The book can point — but only you can see.

### 2. The Mind Is the Obstacle

The mind is not the instrument of truth — it is the barrier. Thinking, analyzing, strategizing — these are the problem.

### 3. Seeking Is an Evasion

The search for truth is a way of avoiding truth. The seeker is always looking ahead — and missing what is here.

> **Case: The Library of Wisdom** (Observation): A person who has read every spiritual text but has not seen the truth is no closer than when they started. The books are not the problem — but the belief that the next book will give you the answer is.
> **Key takeaway**: Reading about truth is not the same as seeing it. The library is not the destination.

### 4. Mastery Is Perception, Not Practice

Practice improves technique. It does not create mastery. Mastery arises when perception is so clear that action is effortless.

### 5. The Ego Must Be Seen, Not Improved

The ego cannot be improved. It can only be seen. Seeing the ego is the end of the ego's power.

### 6. Happiness Is the End of Seeking

Seeking happiness guarantees it will not be found. Happiness is what remains when the search ends.

### 7. There Is No Path

A path implies distance. Truth is not distant — it is here. The path is a distraction from what is already present.

> **Case: The Knowledge Accumulator** (Truth section): A student who accumulates techniques and teachings is like a man who collects maps but never travels. The map is not the territory.
> **Key takeaway**: Knowledge is not understanding. Accumulating ideas about truth is a way of avoiding the direct experience of it.
