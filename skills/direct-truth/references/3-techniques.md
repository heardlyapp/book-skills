## Practical Techniques

Gupta would reject the very idea of "techniques." The following are not methods to practice — they are invitations to see directly.

### 1. The Seeing Practice

When a problem arises, do not try to solve it. Do not think about it. Sit with it. Look at it. See it clearly. The seeing itself is the solution.

### 2. The Stopping of Seeking

Notice when you are seeking — for answers, for happiness, for improvement. Just notice. Do not try to stop. The observation itself may reveal the futility of seeking.

> **Case: The Meditator's Trap** (Observation): A person who meditates for years to achieve peace is still seeking. The peace is not in the meditation — it is in the stopping of seeking. The meditation becomes another method, another path.
> **Key takeaway**: Even spiritual practices can be traps. The goal is not to practice — it is to see.

### 3. The Ego Observation

When you feel hurt, offended, or threatened, observe: "Who is hurt?" The answer is the ego. Just see it. Do not fix it.

### 4. The Question of Mastery

Before you practice anything, ask: "Do I really need more practice, or do I need to see more clearly?" The answer may surprise you.

> **Case: The Golfer's Insight** (Performance): The golfer who stopped trying and started seeing found that his game improved without practice. The seeing revealed what the effort was hiding.
> **Key takeaway**: Sometimes the best thing you can do is stop trying. Direct perception reveals what effort conceals.
