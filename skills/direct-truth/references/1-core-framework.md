## The Gupta Framework: Seeing, Not Doing

Gupta's framework is anti-framework. It rejects all systems, methods, and prescriptions. The only "method" is direct perception — seeing the truth of a thing without the mind's interference.

**The Key Insight**: Everything you have been told about how to improve yourself is wrong. Self-improvement is a trap. The self that wants to improve is the problem.

> **Case: The Master Golfer** (Performance section): Gupta describes working with a professional golfer who was struggling with his game. The golfer wanted a new technique, a new method. Gupta told him: "Your striving is the problem. You already know how to hit the ball. The issue is that your mind gets in the way." The golfer had to stop trying and start seeing.
> **Key takeaway**: Mastery is not about learning something new — it is about removing the interference of the mind.

> **Case: The Perpetual Seeker** (Truth section): Gupta describes a person who spends years studying spiritual teachings, attending retreats, collecting methods from different traditions. This person is a collector of paths, not a walker. The seeking itself is the barrier. The seeker must see that seeking is an evasion.
> **Key takeaway**: Seeking truth is a way of avoiding truth. The seeker is always one step away from seeing — and will remain so until seeking stops.
The mind cannot solve the problem of the mind. Only seeing — direct perception — can.
 This is Gupta's fundamental teaching.
