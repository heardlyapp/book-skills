# Characters and Community

> Source: American Dirt, all chapters

## The People Lydia Meets

### Key Characters on the Journey

| Character | Role | Significance |
|-----------|------|-------------|
| Soledad | Fellow migrant, young woman | Becomes Lydia's close friend, shows the vulnerability of women on the journey |
| Choncho | Mysterious man on the train | Helps Lydia and Luca without explanation. Could be good — or could be a cartel lookout. |
| Miriam | Journalist investigating migration | A parallel to Lydia's husband; represents the witness role |
| Beto | Coyote (border guide) | A complex figure: he helps people cross but also exploits them |

> **Case: Soledad's Story** (Chapters 15-17)
> Soledad is a young woman traveling alone. She joins Lydia and Luca. She is smart, resourceful, and determined. But on the train, women are targets. She tells Lydia: "They look at me and I know what they're thinking." Soledad carries a knife. She has used it before. Her presence on the journey shows an additional layer of danger: for women, the journey is not just exhausting — it is sexually dangerous. The migrant community protects its own, but not always.
> **Key takeaway:** Soledad represents the invisible vulnerability of women migrants. The dangers of La Bestia are not the same for everyone. Women face threats that men do not. Soledad's strength is that she knows this and prepares for it.

> **Case: The Coyote Beto** (Chapters 25-30)
> Beto is the coyote who will guide Lydia and Luca across the border. He is not a villain — he is a businessman. He charges $7,000 per person. He has rules. He has a system. He navigates the cartels, the immigration authorities, and the geography. He is good at his job. But his job exists because the border exists. The coyote is a product of the system, not the cause of it.
> **Key takeaway:** The coyote is not the villain of the migrant story. He is a symptom of the problem. When the border is impassable legally, people will find illegal ways through — and someone will profit. The coyote is a market response to a policy failure.
