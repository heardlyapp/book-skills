# Techniques — The Book of Basketball

## How to Think About Basketball Like Bill Simmons

### Technique 1: The Pyramid Method
Rank players by tiers, not linear numbers. The difference between #4 and #5 is marginal; the difference between Pantheon and Level 4 is enormous. Simmons' Pantheon (6 players) vs. Level 4 (18 players) vs. Level 3 (24 players). This creates natural groupings for debate.

### Technique 2: The "Would I Want to Play with Him?" Test
The Secret in action. Simmons judges sidekicks by one question: "Would you have wanted to play with everyone on this list?" The list of championship sidekicks since 1980 — Magic, Parish/McHale, Kareem (older), Worthy, Pippen, Dumars, etc. — passes. Stephon Marbury, Carmelo Anthony, Allen Iverson — fail. "You wouldn't want to play with guys who don't make you better."

### Technique 3: The Goosebump Scale
Simmons' informal metric for memorable moments: "On the Goosebump Scale, it's about a 9.8." Isiah's 25-point quarter on a sprained ankle. Bird's steal from Isiah. Havlicek's game-winner in double OT. The scale captures what stats can't: emotional impact.

### Technique 4: The "What Would This Look Like in the Internet Era?" Fit
Simmons constantly imagines how past moments would play today. Dave Cowens driving a cab around Boston during the playoffs. Marvin Barnes in a full-length mink coat. Bird's "Merry Christmas" to Chuck Person. The technique forces you to re-evaluate nostalgia vs. reality.

### Technique 5: The Bailey's Barometer
Named after the ice cream shop Simmons and his father went to after Bird's 1988 Game 7 against Dominique: "We didn't say anything for twenty minutes. We just kept eating ice cream and shaking our heads." The measure of true greatness: moments that leave you speechless. "You can't walk away from the potential of more Bailey's moments."

### Technique 6: The Goldman Memory Trap
Goldman's thesis: "The greatest struggle an athlete undergoes is the battle for our memories." Simmons fights this by researching old games (watching Havlicek's 1978 farewell game on NBA TV, finding Hondo was 3rd in scoring all-time). The technique: go back and watch the actual games. Don't rely on fading memories.

### Technique 7: Arguments Over Answers
Simmons: "This book is supposed to start arguments." The Pyramid ranking, the What-If Game, the Wine Cellar — all designed to provoke debate. The point is not to have the "correct" answer, but to engage deeply with basketball history. "I'd tell you more, but I snoozed through the fourth quarter."
