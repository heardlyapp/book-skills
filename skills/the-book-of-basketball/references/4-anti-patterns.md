# Anti-Patterns — The Book of Basketball

## The Central Error: Believing Basketball Can Be Understood Through Statistics Alone

"You can't grasp basketball through statistics alone. You have to watch the games. You have to pay attention."

### Mistake 1: "The best player always wins the title"
Talent alone doesn't win. The '75 Warriors imploded because of Rick Barry's grating personality. The '86 Lakers lost to a worse Rockets team because Kareem and Magic couldn't co-exist yet. The '08 Lakers lost to Boston despite being 3-to-1 favorites. "Talent-wise, the Lakers were better. Team-wise, the Celtics were better."

### Mistake 2: "The highest scorer is the most valuable player"
Amar'e Stoudemire scored 22-25 PPG but "doesn't make anyone else better, doesn't create shots for anyone else." The '89 Pistons had no 20-PPG scorer and won the title — the first team in history to do so. "Every night we found a different person to win it for us."

### Mistake 3: "Past greatness is forever secure"
Goldman: "Greatness has a shelf life." Havlicek was 3rd in all-time scoring when he retired. Name the players who were 1st, 2nd, and 3rd in scoring in 1978. You can't. Because our memories fade. "It's easy to forget anything if you stop thinking about it long enough."

### Mistake 4: "If you can build one great team, you can build another"
Isiah Thomas is the cautionary tale. He won twice as a player because of chemistry and sacrifice — then became a GM and ignored everything he knew. He paid Stephon Marbury, signed Zach Randolph to play next to Eddy Curry, and gave away two lottery picks. "How could someone learn The Secret for that long and still screw up?"

### Mistake 5: "The best team always wins the championship"
Parity and randomness matter. "You need luck in the lottery, luck with young players, luck with trades, luck with everything." The coin flip that sent Elvin Hayes to the Bulls instead of the Celtics. Len Bias dying two days after the draft. Portland having 5.3% odds and getting Greg Oden. "You need to be smart and lucky."

### Mistake 6: "More stats = better understanding"
Simmons devotes an entire chapter to the limits of statistics. Russell's most important stat: "how much better I'd made my teammates play." Duncan's Spurs won 70% for two decades without a single defining statistical season. "Where's the statistic for that?"

### Mistake 7: "The present is always better than the past"
Goldman predicted: "Sure [Bird] was good, and so was Magic — but they couldn't play today." Simmons: "We'd like to believe that our current stars are better than the guys we once watched because the single best thing about sports is the unknown." The urge to replace MJ with LeBron is driven by hope, not evidence.
