> Source: The Girl from the Train, Irma Joubert (2015). A historical novel.

# Core Framework — The Train, the Rescue, and the Two Lives

## The Model

Gretl's life is divided by a single event: the train. Before: Poland, family, war. After: South Africa, Aunt Maria, a new identity.

> **Case: The Train** (Opening): Gretl is six years old. She is on a transport train with her family. She does not understand where they are going. The train stops. A German soldier sees her. He hesitates. He pulls her off the train. He hides her in a wagon. She never sees her family again. The train departs. The novel's entire trajectory is set in this moment.
> **Key takeaway**: A single moment can divide your life into before and after. Before the train. After the train. The division is permanent.

> **Case: The Rescue** (Early chapters): Jakob, a German soldier, makes a decision that will haunt and define him. He saves Gretl. He does not know why. He does not plan it. He acts. He risks his life for a child he does not know. His act of courage becomes the foundation of Gretl's new life.
> **Key takeaway**: Courage is often spontaneous. Jakob does not think. He acts. Sometimes the best decisions are the ones you do not have time to overthink.

> **Case: The New Life** (Middle chapters): Gretl is sent to South Africa. She lives with Aunt Maria on a farm. She learns a new language. She goes to school. She makes friends. She becomes a different person. But the past is not erased. She dreams of Poland. She wonders about her family. She carries the train.
> **Key takeaway**: You can build a new life. You cannot erase the old one. The new life and the old life coexist inside you. That is not a contradiction. It is the reality of survival.
