# Core Framework — We Are Displaced

## Malala's Story (Part 1: Chapters 1-6)

### Before the Taliban (Chapter 1)
- Born in Mingora, Swat Valley, Pakistan — once called "the Switzerland of the East"
- Father Ziauddin opened first high school in 2003 (boys and girls together)
- By 2004, co-ed classes became impossible
- 2005 earthquake (73,000+ killed, 18,000 children) — extremists provided aid and gained influence
- Taliban began radio propaganda: women must cover faces, music and movies sinful, girls should not go to school
- 2007: TVs/computers burned; girls' education declared un-Islamic
- First brush with Taliban: on road to Shangla, men with machine guns checked for music cassettes

### The Descent (Chapter 2)
- 2008: Taliban began bombing girls' schools (at night, no casualties)
- Children's games banned, targeted killings — bodies found in "Bloody Square"
- Malala began BBC Urdu blog at age 11
- January 15, 2009: Taliban decree — all girls' schools must close
- Her class of 27 dwindled to 10; secret school operated for grades 1-4

### Internally Displaced (Chapter 3)
- May 4, 2009: Government ordered evacuation of Swat Valley
- 2 million people fled; family had 2 days' notice
- Malala packed schoolbooks; mother made her leave them behind
- Cousin killed in crossfire — that convinced her father to leave
- Three days of uncertain travel, stayed with kind strangers and in dirty hotels
- Last 15 miles walked with all belongings
- Even at uncle's house in Shangla: "The Taliban were just here"

### Life in Exile (Chapter 4)
- Went to school with cousin Sumbul in Shangla
- Shocked to see only 2 other girls in class of 15+ boys
- Girls covered faces when male teacher entered, never spoke in class
- After 6 weeks, joined father in Peshawar
- Moved between friends and family homes, fourth city in two months
- 12th birthday forgotten — cousin brought cake after midnight

### Return (Chapter 5)
- After almost 3 months, return to Mingora
- City devastated: bullet-riddled buildings, burned-out cars, silence
- Home intact but school used as army base (walls blasted for machine gun barrels)
- Chickens starved to death
- Taliban scattered but not destroyed — targeted killings continued
- October 9, 2012: Malala shot on school bus by Taliban
- Flown to Birmingham, England, while in induced coma

### New Life in Birmingham (Chapter 6)
- Woke up in a foreign country, alone in hospital
- Parents arrived with only clothes they were wearing
- Mother terrified of elevators, missed being on the ground
- Enrolled in local girls' school — felt out of place, lonely
- Thousands of letters from around the world gave her purpose
- "I am not a refugee. But I understand the experience of being displaced."

## The Nine Stories (Part 2)

### 1. Zaynab & Sabreen — Yemen → USA / Europe
- Zaynab (18) got US visa; Sabreen (16) was rejected
- Grew up with grandmother in Aden, Yemen; mother left for US when Zaynab was 4
- Grandmother died suddenly — the connecting piece to family
- 2011 Arab Spring protests → Yemen revolution → indiscriminate bombings
- Uncle shot, neighbor's floor destroyed by bomb
- Fled to Cairo; Zaynab diagnosed with TB, kicked out of relative's house
- 6 months of daily treatment → visa approved for Zaynab only
- Sabreen took boat to Italy: 9 days at sea, 3 different boats, ran out of fuel
- Italian coast guard rescued them
- Sabreen ended up in Netherlands camp, married a Yemeni man there
- Zaynab: skipped 10th and 11th grades, started girls' soccer team (lost every game)
- Met Malala at He Named Me Malala screening
- "I dream big. I want my sister and anyone else who has gone through these hard times to dream big, too."

### 2. Muzoon — Syria → Jordan Camp → UK
- War began in Syria 2011; schools forced to close
- "Even life in a refugee camp has to be better than this" — her father
- Zaatari camp: 12×12 tent for 8 people, no electricity
- Shocked to see so few students in camp school
- Went tent-to-tent convincing parents not to marry off daughters
- Convinced a 17-year-old girl not to marry a man in his 40s
- Became known as "the Malala of Syria" (but "I knew she was the Muzoon of Syria" — Malala)
- Youngest UNICEF Goodwill Ambassador, first refugee ever appointed
- Now studying international politics in UK

### 3. Najla — Yazidi, Iraq (ISIS Survivor)
- Yazidi community — small religious minority in northern Iraq
- Ran away to Sinjar Mountains for 5 days at age 14 to fight for education
- Father didn't speak to her for a year; she persisted and went back to school
- August 2014: ISIS took Mosul, attacked Yazidi villages — genocide
- 18 people in one car, fled to Sinjar Mountains for 8 days
- "Thousands of people, just like us, were on the run"
- Lived in unfinished building with 100+ families in Dohuk
- Taught younger kids to read and write
- Met Malala, asked: "If I lost my hope one day, what would I do?"
- Invited to speak at UN General Assembly
- Dream: go to college, open a hair salon

### 4. María — Colombia Internal Displacement
- Father (farmer) killed when she was 4; family fled to Cali
- Lived in makeshift camp of plastic sheets, 2 spigots for 800 people
- Mother washed clothes tent-to-tent for money
- Family discriminated against for darker skin and rural accent
- Joined community theater; wrote play Nobody Can Take Away What We Carry Inside
- Has moved 8 times since leaving the farm
- Made a documentary about what it means to be displaced
- "When I dream of home, I dream of mangoes I can pick off the trees."

### 5. Analisa — Guatemala → USA
- Mother died when she was 4; father took her in, gave her first birthday cake
- Father fell down stairs and died when she was 15
- Half brother Oscar moved in, took her money, trapped her
- Half brother Ernesto called from US: "Do you want to come live with me?"
- Prayed for a sign: rain on Saturday = yes. It rained.
- Journey: bus to Petén → raft across crocodile river → truck with 25 people → safe houses
- Crossed Rio Grande, immediately caught by ICE
- "Ice box" (freezing room, aluminum blankets) and "dog pound" (chain-link cages)
- 5 days in detention, 6 weeks in children's shelter
- Met other girls: one told of friend who died leaping onto train
- Reunited with Ernesto in Massachusetts, lives behind an Adventist church
- "Thank you, God. I am not alone."

### 6. Marie Claire — DRC → Zambia → USA
- War in Congo started year she was born
- First 4 years: constant running, sleeping under thorny bushes
- Boat to Zambia; refugee camp → one-room house for 8 people
- Started school at 11 in grade 3; other kids threw rocks, shouted "Go back!"
- Mother: "This is your life. Not theirs. Ignore them. Follow your dreams."
- Masked mob attacked family: mother killed, father stabbed in head multiple times
- Mother's prayer: "You can take my life as long as my children are safe"
- Father miraculously survived
- UNHCR application approved → resettled in Lancaster, Pennsylvania
- 18 years old, told she was too old for high school; convinced counselor to give her a chance
- Graduated in 5 months — first in family to finish high school
- Met Jennifer (American volunteer) who became her "American mom"
- Spoke at UN General Assembly with Malala
- Now studying nursing, dreams of working with refugees worldwide

### 7. Jennifer — American Volunteer (Lancaster, PA)
- Saw photo of 3-year-old Syrian boy Alan Kurdi washed up on Turkish beach
- Googled "refugee and volunteer" → found Church World Service
- Met Marie Claire's family at airport; moved by how thin and hopeful they were
- Showed them how to use stove, toilet, microwave (they thought it was magic)
- Bought girls necklaces → they cried, saying "I wish our mother were here"
- "That deep grief is something the girls carry around with them"
- Became known as "American mom" to Marie Claire's entire family
- "Refugees are ordinary people who got caught in the middle of a conflict"

### 8. Ajida — Rohingya, Myanmar → Bangladesh Camp
- Rohingya: Muslim minority in Buddhist Myanmar, persecuted for decades
- Midnight: military surrounded village, set fire to every house
- 300+ people fled together, only traveled at night for 9 days
- Husband's brother killed; bodies everywhere
- Crossed river to Bangladesh in a small rowboat (none could swim)
- Camp: plastic sheet tent, then bamboo hut on exposed mountain
- Bangladesh doesn't allow Rohingya to leave camps — "an open prison"
- Learned to make clay stoves from mother; Love Army hired her to make them for other refugees
- Has made 2,000+ stoves
- "The only way I could ever return to my home is if my family was guaranteed to be treated with dignity."

### 9. Farah — Born in Uganda, expelled by Idi Amin, raised in Canada
- 1972: Idi Amin expelled 50,000 Asian Ugandans — 90 days to leave
- Family's citizenship revoked; mother attacked by soldiers
- Arrived in Montreal with one bag per person
- Parents never used the word "refugee" — tried to protect their children
- Grew up in Canada, experienced racism ("go back home," "you people")
- In her 20s, learned truth from parents about the expulsion
- Returned to Uganda at 36 — first time back since age 2
- Cemetery and destruction; "I've seen poverty before, but I never saw poverty like this"
- Founded G(irls)20, later became CEO of Malala Fund
- "I still struggle to find out what I will do for the country where I was born."

### Epilogue — Return to Swat
- March 31, 2018: Malala returned to Swat Valley for first time in 5.5 years
- Her mother: "Malala left Pakistan with her eyes closed; now she returns with her eyes open"
- 500+ friends and relatives came to greet them
- Mountains where Taliban once headquartered now "only trees and green fields"
- "I didn't leave my country by choice, but I did return by choice."
