# Techniques — God Is Not Great

## How to Argue Against Religion (Based on Christopher Hitchens)

### Technique 1: The Childhood Epiphany
Hitchens' most effective argument comes from his own experience at age nine. Mrs. Watts claimed God made grass green for our eyes. "I simply knew... that my teacher had managed to get everything wrong in just two sentences." The technique: start with the simplest, most obvious falsehood and work outward. If the first claim is wrong, why trust any claim?

### Technique 2: The Moral Inversion
Hitchens inverts the moral claim of religion. "Religion has caused innumerable people not just to conduct themselves no better than others, but to award themselves permission to behave in ways that would make a brothel-keeper raise an eyebrow." Case: the Crusaders who carved crosses on their victims, the Inquisitors who tortured in Christ's name, the suicide bombers who believe they're earning paradise.

### Technique 3: The "If X Can Y, Why Not Y All?" Question
"If Jesus could heal a blind person, why not heal blindness?" If God answers prayers, why do children still die of cancer? If scripture is inerrant, why does it contain contradictions? This simple question exposes the logical failure at the heart of religious claims.

### Technique 4: The Health Hazard
Hitchens devotes a chapter to "A Note on Health, to Which Religion Can Be Hazardous." Faith healing kills. Circumcision removes sensitive tissue. Refusing blood transfusions (Jehovah's Witnesses) kills children. The virginity obsession causes genital mutilation. "Religion is the cause of immeasurable physical and psychic suffering."

### Technique 5: The "Why No Result?" Test
"With all this continual prayer, why no result?" Hitchens asks why, if millions pray daily, we have polio, famine, and war. The answer believers give (mysterious ways, free will, tests of faith) are rationalizations — not explanations. "Religion is ultimately grounded on wish-thinking."

### Technique 6: The Scriptural Inconsistency
Hitchens reads scripture like a literary critic. He exposes contradictions, absurdities, and moral atrocities. Case: the Old Testament's treatment of women as property versus its claims of divine justice. The Koran's scientific errors (semen from the backbone). The synoptic gospels' contradictions on the resurrection. "God did not create man in his own image. It was the other way about."

### Technique 7: The Secular Alternative
"We do not need any machinery of reinforcement." Hitchens offers the alternative: "Literature, not scripture, sustains the mind and — since there is no other metaphor — also the soul." The technique: don't just criticize — provide a positive vision of the secular life based on reason, science, art, and love.
