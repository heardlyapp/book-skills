# Anti-Patterns — God Is Not Great

## The Central Error: "Religion Is a Force for Good"

Hitchens' entire book is a refutation of this. "Past and present religious atrocities have occurred not because we are evil, but because it is a fact of nature that the human species is, biologically, only partly rational." The claim that religion makes people better is not supported by evidence.

### Mistake 1: "Atheists have no basis for morality"
"We believe with certainty that an ethical life can be lived without religion." Hitchens points to the great works of secular literature (Shakespeare, Tolstoy, Dostoyevsky) as guides to ethical life. "Literature, not scripture, sustains the mind."

### Mistake 2: "The private faith of the kindly believer does no harm"
"Even the most peaceful faith has a murderous potential, and it is a potential that is activated by many events." Moderate faith provides cover for extremism. "The lines of latitude that would hold the line against theocracy begin with the acceptance that faith is an impostor."

### Mistake 3: "Religion comforts the afflicted"
Hitchens' response to the headmaster: "That would be as much as saying that religion might not be true, but never mind that, since it can be relied upon for comfort. How contemptible." If a belief is false, it's not comforting — it's a lie. "The comfort that religion offers is the comfort of a lie."

### Mistake 4: "The New Testament is an improvement on the Old"
"Evils that exceed those of the Old." The New Testament's doctrine of eternal hell is morally worse than anything in the Old Testament. "Torture without end is the invention of the Prince of Peace."

### Mistake 5: "It is arrogant to deny God"
Hitchens inverts this: the arrogance is in claiming that you know God's will. "How much vanity must be concealed in order to pretend that one is the personal object of a divine plan?"

### Mistake 6: "Religion discourages violence"
"Religion has caused innumerable people to award themselves permission to behave in ways that would make a brothel-keeper raise an eyebrow." The Crusades, Inquisition, witch hunts, sectarian conflict, suicide bombing — all done in the name of faith. "Religion kills."
