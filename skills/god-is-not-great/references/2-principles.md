# Key Principles — God Is Not Great

## I. The Four Irreducible Objections

Hitchens' core argument: religion misrepresents origins, combines servility with solipsism, causes sexual repression, and is grounded in wish-thinking. "There still remain four irreducible objections to religious faith." Case: Mrs. Watts teaching that God made grass green because it's good for human eyes — "she had managed to get everything wrong in just two sentences."

## II. Religion Kills

"Religion has caused innumerable people to award themselves permission to behave in ways that would make a brothel-keeper or an ethnic cleanser raise an eyebrow." Hitchens catalogs the Crusades (sack of Constantinople by Christian crusaders), the Inquisition, witch hunts, the Thirty Years' War, sectarian violence in Ireland and the Balkans, and contemporary Middle Eastern conflicts. "Past and present religious atrocities have occurred not because we are evil, but because the human species is only partly rational."

## III. Scripture Is Man-Made and Fallible

The Old Testament: genocides, misogyny, absurdities. The New Testament: eternal hell, the demand to hate family. The Koran: borrowed myths, endorsement of slavery. "God did not create man in his own image. It was the other way about." Case: The doctrine of hell — "torture without end" — is morally worse than anything it claims to punish.

## IV. Religion Is a Form of Child Abuse

"Labeling children with a religion before they can consent is a form of abuse." Hitchens argues that circumcision without consent, the doctrine of original sin, and religious schooling that teaches biblical literalism instead of science are forms of child abuse. "The first thing to inflict on the child is the sin of Onan."

## V. An Ethical Life Can Be Lived Without Religion

"We believe with certainty that an ethical life can be lived without religion." Hitchens cites secular societies (Scandinavian countries) as evidence that morality does not depend on faith. "Literature, not scripture, sustains the mind and — since there is no other metaphor — also the soul."

## VI. The Argument from Design Is Inverted

"The eyes were adjusted to nature, and not the other way about." Evolution explains apparent design without a designer. Paley's watchmaker analogy is a false premise. Case: "If Jesus could heal a blind person, then why not heal blindness?"

## VII. Freedom of Inquiry Trumps Dogma

"We respect free inquiry, openmindedness, and the pursuit of ideas for their own sake." Hitchens' model is the scientific and intellectual freedom of the Enlightenment, set against the closed authoritarianism of organized religion. The alternative to faith is not nihilism — it's reason, literature, music, art, and love.
