# Common Mistakes — Anti-Patterns

**Anti-Pattern 1: Reacting Emotionally.** When attacked, our instinct is to fight back. This escalates the conflict.

**Anti-Pattern 2: Arguing Positions.** Positions are not negotiable. Interests are.

**Anti-Pattern 3: Pushing Too Hard.** Pushing creates resistance. The other side will push back.

**Case: The Car Salesman. A pushy salesperson makes you want to walk away.**

**Case: The Ultimatum. An ultimatum shuts down communication. It almost never works.**

**Case: The Argument. Two people arguing over positions never reach agreement. They must reframe to interests.**

**Case: The Deadline. Artificial deadlines create pressure but rarely lead to good agreements.**

**Case: The Blame Game. Blaming the other side makes them defensive. Focus on solutions, not fault.**

**Case: The Escalation Trap. Each time you respond to attack with attack, the conflict escalates. The only way out is to step back.**

**Case: The Power Imbalance. When one side has more power, the weaker side must improve their BATNA before negotiating.**

**Case: Additional Example.** The breakthrough strategy works in every domain: business negotiations, family disputes, customer complaints, international diplomacy, and everyday conversations. The principles are universal.
