# Key Principles — Cracked, Not Broken

## I. Suicide Is Impulsive — and Therefore Preventable

"I didn't jump because I wanted to die. There is a huge difference between believing you have to do something and wanting to do something." The instant his hands left the railing, Kevin realized he wanted to live. "I wonder how many other victims of the four-second fall felt as I did, that they had to die and then realized, after it was too late, that it was the worst mistake they would ever make."

## II. The Voices Are the Disease, Not the Truth

Kevin heard voices commanding him to kill himself. "The voice was like the one I heard as a boy but never told anyone about its existence." The critical insight: those voices were symptoms of bipolar 1 disorder with psychotic features, not expressions of his true self. Treating the illness weakened the voices.

## III. You Are Not Your Diagnosis

Kevin is not "bipolar" — he is a person living with bipolar disorder. "I have bipolar type 1 disorder with psychotic features. This illness convinced me that there was no way to ease the pain except through my death." The illness is part of him, but it does not define him. "I was Cracked... But will never be Broken."

## IV. Treatment Works — But You Have to Follow It

Kevin's recovery required medication, therapy, exercise, social connection, and education. "I followed my treatment plan." He read everything he could about his condition. "I no longer wanted to deny the reality of my condition. Instead, I wanted to learn as much as I could."

## V. Family Support Saves Lives

Kevin's adoptive parents Patrick and Debi, his uncle Kevin, and his siblings stood by him. His father visited daily. Uncle Kevin brought books and soup. "Faith, family, true friends, and those letters kept me going in my loneliest and most dismal of hospital days."

## VI. Helping Others Heals the Helper

"I love helping people." Kevin became a public speaker because he realized his story could give hope to others. "If you have a voice that you are willing to share with the public, one day it becomes your duty to look out for those who are hurting and in pain."

## VII. Recovery Is Not a Straight Line

Kevin has been in and out of psych wards, had relapses, and struggled. But each time he got back up. "Yesterday is History, Tomorrow is a Mystery, Today is a Gift — That is Why They Call it the Present." The goal is not perfection — it's progress.
