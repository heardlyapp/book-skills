# Anti-Patterns — Cracked, Not Broken

## The Central Error: "People Who Attempt Suicide Just Want Attention"

"Those driven to attempt suicide this way rarely survive." Kevin did not want attention. He wanted his pain to end. The voices convinced him death was his only option. "I believed, beyond a shadow of a doubt, that I had to die."

### Mistake 1: "Just think positive"
Mental illness is a brain disease, not a failure of will. "I had — and have — bipolar disorder." You cannot think your way out of a chemical imbalance. Kevin needed medication, therapy, and a structured recovery plan — not positive thinking.

### Mistake 2: "Suicide is selfish"
This is the most common reaction families hear. Kevin addresses it directly: "I was not thinking about the consequences of my actions on other people — my brain was chemically incapable of that reasoning." The suicidal mind has tunnel vision. Calling it selfish adds stigma and silences those who need help.

### Mistake 3: "Once someone talks about suicide, they won't do it"
This myth kills people. Kevin talked about suicide before his attempt — but no one recognized the warning signs. "Giving away prized possessions exemplifies suicidal ideation." Anyone who talks about suicide should be taken seriously.

### Mistake 4: "Psych wards are scary places for bad people"
Kevin's seven hospitalizations taught him that these are places of healing. "The patients without visitors were called 'The Forgotten.'" The people inside are not dangerous — they are suffering. "Acknowledge them, respect them, love them."

### Mistake 5: "After treatment, you're cured"
"Recovery is not a straight line." Kevin has had relapses. He still manages his condition daily. "I am not cured, I survive and thrive with chronic mental illness." The goal is management, not elimination.

### Mistake 6: "Keep your struggles to yourself"
Kevin's saving grace was speaking out. "If you have a voice that you are willing to share with the public, one day it becomes your duty." The opposite — silence and shame — almost killed him. "The silence around mental illness is deadly."
