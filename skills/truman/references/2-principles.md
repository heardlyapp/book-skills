# 2 — Key Principles

> Source: *Truman* by David McCullough — Chapters 1-18.

## The 7 Principles

### 1. The Buck Stops Here

Truman's desk sign became his philosophy. No passing blame. No making excuses. Every decision was his. Every failure was his.

### 2. Honesty Is the Only Policy That Works

"I never gave anybody hell. I just told the truth and they thought it was hell." Truman's directness was often called bluntness. It was actually integrity.

> **Case: The Music Critics' Letter** (Chapter 18): After leaving office, Truman wrote a letter to a music critic who had criticized his daughter Margaret's singing voice. He threatened to punch the critic in the nose. The press had a field day. But the letter was true to who he was — a father who defended his daughter. He didn't apologize.

### 3. You Don't Need Charisma to Lead

Truman was plain-spoken, uncharismatic, and undervalued. He is now rated among the top 10 presidents. Substance outlasts style.

### 4. The Right Decision Is Often Unpopular

The atomic bomb, the Truman Doctrine, the Marshall Plan, the firing of MacArthur — each was deeply unpopular at the time. Each was the right call.

> **Case: The 1948 Election** (Chapter 14): Everyone — the polls, the press, the pundits — said Truman would lose. The Chicago Tribune printed "DEWEY DEFEATS TRUMAN" prematurely. Truman held up the paper and smiled. He had won. He had trusted the people over the experts.

### 5. Integrity Is Not About Circumstances — It's About Choices

Pendergast was corrupt. Truman never was. The same environment, different choices.

### 6. Character Is What You Do After You Lose Power

Truman left the presidency almost broke. He refused to sit on corporate boards or accept speaking fees. He drove himself back to Missouri, paid off his debts with his memoirs, and walked the streets of Independence as a private citizen.

### 7. Common Sense Is Underrated

"Some presidents were geniuses. I'm not one of them. But I've got good common sense and I know how to use it."
