# 4 — Anti-Patterns

> Source: *Truman* by David McCullough.

## Common Errors

### 1. Believing Integrity and Success Are Incompatible

**The trap:** "You can't be successful in politics if you're honest."

**Why it fails:** Truman was the most honest president of the 20th century. He was also one of the most consequential.

> **Case: The $10,000 Cash Contribution** (Chapter 6): Pendergast gave Truman's campaign $10,000 in cash. Truman reported every dollar. Had he hidden it, the money would have become a scandal. Because he was transparent, the accusation never stuck. Integrity was his best political protection.
> **Key takeaway**: Transparency is not just ethical — it's strategic. A clean record is the best defense.

> **Case: The 1952 Steel Seizure** (Chapter 17): During the Korean War, Truman seized the steel mills to prevent a strike. The Supreme Court ruled his action unconstitutional. He complied immediately. A president who had fired Douglas MacArthur and used the atomic bomb accepted a court ruling against him without complaint. Constitutional order prevailed.
> **Key takeaway**: Even when you disagree with the ruling, you follow the law. That's what the rule of law means.

### 2. Letting Public Opinion Dictate Policy

**The trap:** "I need to do what's popular to stay in power."

**Why it fails:** Truman's approval rating hit 22%. He still fired MacArthur, proposed civil rights, and desegregated the military. He didn't care about the polls.

### 3. Over-Hesitating on Hard Decisions

Delays have their own costs. Truman authorized the bomb and sent troops to Korea in days.

### 4. Staying in Power at Any Cost

Truman could have run for a third term. He chose not to.

### 5. Forgetting Who You Are

"The presidency is not a prize — it's a sacred trust."
