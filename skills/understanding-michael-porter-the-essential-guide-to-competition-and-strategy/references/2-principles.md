# Principles: Strategy = Being Different

> Source: Understanding Michael Porter, Chapters 4-5

## The Core of Strategy

Strategy is not about being better at what everyone else does. It's about being different — choosing a unique position that delivers unique value to a specific set of customers. Strategic positioning means performing different activities from rivals, or performing the same activities in different ways.

> **Case: Southwest Airlines** (Chapter 4): Southwest chose a unique position: low-cost, short-haul, point-to-point flights serving secondary airports. This required distinct activities: no meals, no seat assignments, no baggage transfers, no hub-and-spoke system. They didn't try to be "better" — they chose to be different. Their strategy drove every operational decision they made.

> **Key takeaway**: Strategy is about choice. Choose who to serve, what to offer, and how to deliver. Accept the trade-offs those choices inevitably require.

## Trade-offs Are the Linchpin

Trade-offs are the essence of strategy. If there were no trade-offs — if you could serve all needs without sacrificing anything — any competitor could copy your position. Trade-offs make strategic choices meaningful and protect you from imitation.

> **Case: IKEA vs Ethan Allen** (Chapter 5): IKEA trades off service and selection for low price and self-service. Ethan Allen trades off low price for full service and premium design. Neither can copy the other without destroying its own strategy.
