# 3 — Type Dynamics

> Source: "Psychological Types" by C.G. Jung

## Projection

We project our own unconscious qualities onto others. The person who annoys you most often embodies something you haven't accepted in yourself.

## The Anima and Animus

- **Anima**: The inner feminine in a man. Men project their anima onto women, seeing their own unconscious feminine qualities reflected.
- **Animus**: The inner masculine in a woman. Women project their animus onto men.
- The goal: withdraw projection and integrate these inner figures.

## The Shadow

The parts of ourselves we reject — our weaknesses, our darker impulses, our undeveloped capacities. The shadow is not evil; it's simply what we've chosen not to be.

> **Case: The Opposites Attract** (General): Jung describes why we are drawn to people of opposite types. The extravert is fascinated by the introvert's depth. The thinking type admires the feeling type's warmth. But this same attraction creates conflict — because the other person doesn't actually embody what we project; they are simply different.
> **Key takeaway**: Relationship conflicts are often projection conflicts. The other person's difference triggers your unconscious. Your growth lies in withdrawing the projection and developing the quality yourself.

> **Case: The Anima Encounter** (Chapter on Anima): Jung describes a man's encounter with his anima as one of the most important psychological experiences. "She appears in dreams, in fantasies, in the women he is attracted to. Until he recognizes her as part of himself, he will be driven by unconscious fascination."
> **Key takeaway**: The people you are most drawn to and most repelled by are mirrors of your inner world. The work is to see them as mirrors, not as sources of fulfillment or frustration.
