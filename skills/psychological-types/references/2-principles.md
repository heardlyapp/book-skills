# 2 — The Four Functions

> Source: "Psychological Types" by C.G. Jung

## Rational vs Irrational

Jung divides the four functions into two categories:
- **Rational Functions** (judging): Thinking and Feeling. They involve making judgments and decisions.
- **Irrational Functions** (perceiving): Sensation and Intuition. They involve taking in information without immediate judgment.

## The Dominant Function

Everyone has a primary function that is most developed and conscious. This determines your general type orientation.

> **Case: The One-Sided Development** (General Theory): Jung warns that modern culture overvalues certain functions. Thinking types are rewarded in academia and business. Feeling types may appear "irrational." Sensing types are practical. Intuitive types are visionary. Healthy development requires all four functions, even the ones society undervalues.
> **Key takeaway**: Your natural strengths are real. But they can become a trap if you rely on them exclusively. True maturity is developing all four functions.

## The Auxiliary Function

The second most developed function. It complements the dominant: if your dominant is a rational function (T/F), your auxiliary is usually an irrational one (S/N), and vice versa. This provides balance.

## The Inferior Function

The least developed function. It operates largely unconsciously and can be a source of:
- Projection (seeing it in others instead of yourself)
- Fascination (being drawn to people who embody it)
- Immature behavior under stress (regressing to the inferior function)

> **Case: The Inferior Function in Action** (Chapter on Types): Jung describes how a dominant Thinking type, under stress, may suddenly act out in childish Feeling ways — becoming overly emotional, sentimental, or hurt. This is the inferior Feeling emerging unconsciously. Recognizing this pattern is key to personal growth.
> **Key takeaway**: Your weakest function will ambush you when you're stressed. The goal is not to make it equal to your dominant, but to be conscious of it so it doesn't control you.
