# 1 — The Core Framework

> Source: "Psychological Types" by C.G. Jung

## The Two Attitudes

**Extraversion**: The libido (psychic energy) flows outward. The object (people, things, the external world) is primary. Extraverts are energized by engagement with the outer world. They think, feel, and act in relation to others.

**Introversion**: The libido flows inward. The subject (inner world, ideas, subjective experience) is primary. Introverts are energized by solitude and inner reflection. They filter external experience through their internal framework.

## The Four Functions

**Thinking (T)**: Logical judgment. Concerned with truth, principles, consistency. Extraverted Thinking (Te) organizes the external world. Introverted Thinking (Ti) seeks internal logical consistency.

**Feeling (F)**: Value-based judgment. Concerned with harmony, relationships, personal values. Extraverted Feeling (Fe) attunes to collective values and others' feelings. Introverted Feeling (Fi) follows a deeply held internal value system.

**Sensation (S)**: Concrete perception. Concerned with facts, details, the present moment, physical reality. Extraverted Sensation (Se) engages directly with the sensory world. Introverted Sensation (Si) recalls and compares with past sensory impressions.

**Intuition (N)**: Abstract perception. Concerned with possibilities, patterns, future implications, meaning. Extraverted Intuition (Ne) sees possibilities in the external world. Introverted Intuition (Ni) perceives internal visions and future patterns.

> **Case: The Two Attitudes Exemplified** (Chapter on Extraversion/Introversion): Jung describes how extraverts and introverts approach the same situation differently. An extravert in a crisis seeks others, talks it through, and takes action in the world. An introvert in a crisis withdraws, reflects internally, and processes alone. Neither is wrong — they are different energy directions.
> **Key takeaway**: Extraversion and introversion are not about being "outgoing" or "shy." They are about where your energy comes from and where it goes.

## The Eight Types

Each function can be extraverted or introverted, producing eight cognitive functions: Te, Ti, Fe, Fi, Se, Si, Ne, Ni.
