# Reference: Bonnie's Hoarding

## Overview
Bonnie Snow suffers from severe hoarding disorder. Her house at 48 Arcadia Avenue is packed floor to ceiling with paper, boxes, and junk.

## Case: The Accumulation
Bonnie collects everything, but paper dominates: newspapers, greeting cards (hundreds, blank inside), magazines, receipts, leaflets, old bills. She cannot throw anything away. Every object is precious to her.

## Case: The Goat Paths
The hallways are reduced to narrow passages Ro calls "goat paths." She navigates sideways, the paper slippery under her feet. The carpet has not been seen in years.

## Case: The Fire Risk
The house is a fire waiting to happen. Bonnie smokes. Ro lives in constant fear that a stray ash will ignite the piles of paper. She imagines the house going up in flames with Bonnie in it.

## Case: The Denial
Bonnie refuses to acknowledge the problem. When Ro tries to talk about it, Bonnie deflects, gets angry, or changes the subject. She cannot face the reality of her situation.

## Case: The Bathroom
The bathtub is piled so high with junk that Ro has not taken a bath since she was five. She showers in the cramped plastic cubicle in the corner, one of the few spaces Bonnie has not colonized.

## Case: The Bedroom
Bonnie sleeps in the living room armchair. Her bedroom is unreachable, blocked by years of accumulation. She has not slept in a bed in years.
Hoarding is a mental illness. Bonnie does not choose this life. She is trapped by a disorder that convinces her every scrap of paper is essential.
