# Reference: Hope and Recovery

## Overview
The novel ends with the beginning of recovery. There is no easy fix, but there is hope.

## Case: The Intervention
Social services visits the house. Bonnie is defensive. Ro is terrified. But the process begins. The secret is out.

## Case: Bonnie's Resistance
Bonnie does not immediately cooperate. She resists. She denies. She blames Ro. Recovery from hoarding is slow and difficult.

## Case: Ro's Relief
Despite the fear, Ro feels relief. She no longer has to carry the secret alone. Adults are now involved. She is not the only one responsible.

## Case: The Future
The novel does not promise a happy ending. Bonnie may or may not recover. Ro may or may not stay with her. But there is possibility. There is hope.

## Case: The Title
"Paper Avalanche" is the thing that has buried Ro alive. The intervention is the first shovel. The avalanche will take time to clear. But it is no longer falling.

## Case: The Message
The novel's message: you are not alone. Hoarding is a family disease. Everyone in the house is affected. Help exists.

## Case: The Courage
Ro's courage to speak is the model. She shows that asking for help is not weakness. It is the strongest thing you can do.
Paper Avalanche is ultimately a hopeful book. It says: even when you are buried, there are people who will dig you out. You just have to let them.
The avalanche does not end. But the falling stops. And that is the beginning of everything.
That is the hope the book offers: not a clean resolution, but the possibility of one.
