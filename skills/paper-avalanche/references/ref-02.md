# Reference: Ro Snow

## Overview
Ro Snow is the protagonist. She is 14, quiet, responsible, and carrying a burden no child should carry.

## Case: The Parentified Child
Ro manages the household finances. She knows the bank account is overdrawn. Bonnie does not look at statements. Ro budgets, worries, and tries to keep them afloat.

## Case: The Secret
Ro lives in total secrecy. She sprays Febreze on her clothes before school. She never invites anyone home. She makes excuses when the house is mentioned. She is exhausted by the deception.

## Case: The Sanctuary
Ro's bedroom is her refuge. She painted it white, keeps it spotless, locks the door. It is the only space in the house that is not cluttered. It is the calm at the center of the storm.

## Case: The Nightmare
Ro has a recurring dream: being buried alive under piles of paper. The hoard is literally what she fears will kill her.

## Case: The Courage
Ro's arc is about finding the courage to speak. She tells her friend Goose. She tells a teacher. She lets social services in. This is the hardest thing she has ever done.

## Case: The Leaflet Job
Ro delivers takeaway leaflets on weekends. It gives her money, independence, and a reason to leave the house. Eric runs the distribution office.

## Case: Jodie
Jodie is Ro's friend from work. They are close, but Ro never tells her the truth about home. Jodie represents a normal life Ro cannot have.
Ro is the reluctant adult in a household where the parent has abdicated. She does not choose this role. It is forced on her by circumstance.
