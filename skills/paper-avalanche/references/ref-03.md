# Reference: Bonnie Snow

## Overview
Bonnie is Ro's mother. She is a singer, a hoarder, and a deeply troubled woman. She loves Ro but cannot care for her properly.

## Case: The Singer
Bonnie performs at pubs and events. She wears sequined dresses, sprays her hair, applies thick makeup. She is talented but unreliable. She sacked her manager and misses gigs.

## Case: The Hoarder
Bonnie's hoarding is severe. She cannot stop accumulating. She cannot throw anything away. She sleeps in an armchair because her bedroom is blocked. She smokes despite the fire risk.

## Case: The Denial
Bonnie avoids reality. She does not open bank statements. She lies to Ro about sacking her manager. She turns up the television when Ro confronts her. She cannot face her problems.

## Case: The Love
Despite everything, Bonnie loves Ro. The love is real but not enough. Love cannot protect Ro from the hoard. Love cannot pay the bills.

## Case: The Resistance
When social services intervenes, Bonnie resists. She does not want help. She does not believe she has a problem. Recovery will be long and difficult.

## Case: The Parallel
Bonnie and Ro are trapped together. Bonnie cannot stop collecting. Ro cannot stop cleaning up. They are stuck in a cycle that neither can break alone.

## Case: The Complexity
Bonnie is not a villain. She is a victim of her own mind. She hates what her life has become but cannot escape. The novel asks us to hold compassion and frustration together.
Bonnie is not bad. She is sick. Hoarding disorder is a mental illness, not a moral failing.
