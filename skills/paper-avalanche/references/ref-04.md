# Reference: Friendship and Connection

## Overview
Ro's isolation is the central theme. Her friendships are fragile because she cannot tell the truth.

## Case: The First Friend
Ro meets Jodie at work. Jodie is older, fun, chaotic. Ro enjoys her company but cannot tell her the truth about home. The friendship is real but limited.

## Case: Goose
Goose (Megan) is a new student. She is confident and persistent. She pushes past Ro's defenses. She is the first person Ro tells about the hoard.

## Case: The Confession
Telling Goose is the turning point. Goose does not judge Ro. She listens. She helps. She makes Ro believe that help is possible.

## Case: The Teacher
Ro eventually tells a teacher at school. This is the step that triggers the formal intervention. It is terrifying and necessary.

## Case: Jamie
Jamie Cannon is a boy from school who walks Ro home. He sees the house. Ro panics. Jamie's reaction is mixed — he is confused, then uncomfortable. The encounter is painful but reveals how impossible Ro's situation is.

## Case: The Shame
Ro's shame is the wall between her and everyone else. She cannot let anyone see where she lives. The shame is as damaging as the hoard itself.

## Case: The Hope
Goose's friendship is proof that connection is possible. Not everyone will reject Ro when they learn the truth. Some people will help.
The novel argues that isolation is the enemy of recovery. Connection is the only way out. Ro's courage to speak is the beginning of everything.
Silence protects the disease. Speaking aloud is the cure.
