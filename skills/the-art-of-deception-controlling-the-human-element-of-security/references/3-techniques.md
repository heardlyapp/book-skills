# Techniques: Phone, Email, and Physical Attacks

> Source: The Art of Deception, Chapters 7-13

## Phone Attacks (Vishing)

Social engineers use the phone constantly. Voice-only communication removes visual cues that might reveal deception.

> **Case: The Help Desk Call** (Chapter 7): A social engineer calls the IT help desk. He sounds frustrated and under pressure. "I'm the CFO's assistant and we're in a meeting with auditors — he needs his password reset NOW." Urgency + authority = the help desk complies without verification.

> **Key takeaway**: Urgency overrides normal security procedures. Social engineers always create urgency.

## Email Attacks (Phishing)

Emails that appear legitimate but contain malicious links or attachments.

> **Case: The Vendor Email** (Chapter 7): An email arrives that appears to be from a trusted vendor. It says "Please review the attached invoice." The attachment contains malware. The employee opens it because the vendor name is familiar.

## Physical Breaches (Tailgating)

Walking into a secure building behind an authorized person. Carrying a heavy box, wearing a fake badge, or just looking confident.

> **Case: The Smoker's Door** (Chapter 10): Employees who smoke often prop open a back door. A social engineer waits for a smoker, then walks in unchallenged. Once inside, he's just another person in the building.

> **Key takeaway**: Social engineers combine multiple techniques. A call to gather information, then an email, then a physical visit. Each step builds on the last.
