> Source: She's Come Undone, Wally Lamb (1992).

# Key Principles — Body Image, Self-Worth, and the Right to Take Up Space

## Core Principles

> **Case: The Wedding Dress** (Late chapter): Dolores gets married in a wedding dress that is too small. She cannot fit into it. She wears it anyway. The zipper does not close. She uses a safety pin. The dress is a metaphor: she is trying to fit into a life that does not fit her. The marriage fails. But the moment of standing in the dress — refusing to admit it does not fit — is the moment the reader understands. She has been trying to fit into expectations her whole life.
> **Key takeaway**: Stop trying to fit into a life that does not fit you. The dress is too small not because you are too big but because it is the wrong dress.

> **Case: The TV as Life** (Throughout): Dolores watches television obsessively. She watches other people live their lives while she hides in her apartment. The TV is escape. It is also a prison. She lives through characters on the screen instead of living her own life. The turning point comes when she realizes that watching life is not living it.
> **Key takeaway**: Watching other people live is not living. Whether it is television, social media, or the lives of others, you cannot live through someone else. You have to step into your own life.

> **Case: The Thin Woman Inside** (Throughout): Dolores believes there is a thin woman trapped inside her who will be happy once she loses weight. She learns, slowly, that the thin woman would have the same problems. Weight loss is not a cure for trauma, depression, or a difficult life. The work she needs to do is internal, not external.
> **Key takeaway**: Losing weight will not solve your problems. The problems are in your mind, your past, your relationships. Do the internal work. Your body will follow.
