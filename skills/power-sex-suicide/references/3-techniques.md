# Techniques: Mitochondrial Health

## Supporting Mitochondrial Function

1. **Exercise** — High-intensity interval training (HIIT) increases mitochondrial biogenesis. Moderate exercise improves efficiency.
2. **Cold exposure** — Brief cold exposure stimulates mitochondrial growth and efficiency.
3. **Caloric restriction** — Reducing calorie intake reduces oxidative stress on mitochondria.
4. **Sleep** — Mitochondrial repair and turnover occur during deep sleep.
5. **Nutrient density** — Mitochondria require specific nutrients: CoQ10, B vitamins, magnesium, iron, omega-3s.

> **Case: The Exercise Effect** (Chapter 10): Exercise doesn't just build muscle — it builds mitochondria. Regular exercisers have more and better-functioning mitochondria than sedentary people. The result: more energy, better health, slower aging.
> **Key takeaway**: Exercise is the single most effective intervention for mitochondrial health. It increases both the quantity and quality of your mitochondria.

## Understanding Mitochondrial Disease

Mitochondrial diseases are caused by mutations in mtDNA or nuclear DNA that affect mitochondrial function. They affect tissues with high energy demands: brain, muscle, heart, eyes, ears.

> **Case: The Leigh Syndrome Patient** (Chapter 12): Leigh syndrome is a severe mitochondrial disorder that affects children. It causes progressive loss of mental and movement abilities. It's caused by mutations that impair the mitochondrial energy production machinery.
> **Key takeaway**: Mitochondrial disease shows how essential these organelles are. When mitochondria fail, the most energy-hungry organs fail first.

## The Hormesis Principle

Low-level stress (exercise, cold, heat, fasting) triggers protective responses in mitochondria. This is called hormesis. A little stress makes mitochondria stronger.
