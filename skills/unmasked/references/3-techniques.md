# 3 — The Portland Protests

> Source: "Unmasked" by Andy Ngo

## Ground Zero

Portland became the center of Antifa's escalation in 2020. The protests following George Floyd's murder provided cover for Antifa's most sustained campaign of violence against a federal installation.

> **Case: The 100 Nights** (Chapter on Portland): "For over 100 consecutive nights, protesters gathered outside the federal courthouse in Portland. Each night, a core group of Antifa-affiliated individuals escalated the violence — smashing windows, launching fireworks, attempting to breach the building." Ngo documents this unprecedented duration of attack.
> **Key takeaway**: The duration and intensity were unprecedented in American history. A sustained assault on a federal building lasting over 100 nights represented a new level of domestic political violence.

> **Case: The Courthouse Siege** (Chapter on Federal Response): "Protesters attacked the federal courthouse with commercial-grade fireworks, improvised explosives, and chemical irritants. Federal officers inside described it as a siege." Ngo presents the courthouse siege as the best-documented evidence of Antifa's willingness to use paramilitary-style violence against government institutions.
> **Key takeaway**: The assault on the federal courthouse was not a protest — it was an organized attack on a federal installation. The documented video evidence, court records, and officer testimony make this the most extensively documented Antifa action.
