# 3 — The Portland Protests

> Source: "Unmasked" by Andy Ngo

## Ground Zero

Portland became the center of Antifa's escalation in 2020. The protests following George Floyd's murder provided cover for Antifa's most sustained campaign of violence.

## The Federal Courthouse

The Mark O. Hatfield Federal Courthouse became the focal point. For months, Antifa protesters attacked the building with fireworks, projectiles, and incendiary devices.

> **Case: The 100 Nights** (Chapter on Portland): "For over 100 consecutive nights, protesters gathered outside the federal courthouse in Portland. Each night, a core group of Antifa-affiliated individuals escalated the violence — smashing windows, launching fireworks, attempting to breach the building."
> **Key takeaway**: The duration and intensity of the Portland protests were unprecedented. The sustained assault on a federal building represented a new level of escalation for domestic political violence.

> **Case: The Courthouse Siege** (Chapter on Federal Response): "Protesters attacked the federal courthouse with commercial-grade fireworks, improvised explosives, and chemical irritants. Federal officers inside described it as a siege." Ngo presents this as a case study in how far Antifa was willing to go.
> **Key takeaway**: The assault on the federal courthouse was not a protest — it was an organized paramilitary-style attack on a federal installation. This is the most documented evidence of Antifa's violence.
