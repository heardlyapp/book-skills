# 1 — Antifa's Origins and Ideology

> Source: "Unmasked" by Andy Ngo

## Historical Roots

Antifa traces its origins to anti-fascist movements in 1920s-30s Europe, particularly in Italy and Germany. The modern American movement emerged in the 2010s.

## Core Ideology

Antifa is anti-capitalist and anti-fascist. It rejects liberal democracy as insufficient and argues that violence against fascists is justified self-defense of marginalized communities.

> **Case: The Rose City Antifa** (Chapter on Origins): "The Rose City Antifa, based in Portland, is one of the oldest and most organized Antifa groups in the United States. Founded in 2007, it has served as a model for groups across the country." Ngo traces how this group built infrastructure and relationships that would later fuel the 2020 escalation.
> **Key takeaway**: Antifa is not a spontaneous movement that emerged in 2020. Groups like Rose City Antifa had been organizing and developing tactics for over a decade.

> **Case: The 2017 Berkeley Riot** (Chapter on Early Escalation): "In 2017, Antifa groups violently shut down a planned speech by Milo Yiannopoulos at UC Berkeley. The riot caused over $100,000 in damage and left several people injured." This event marked a turning point in Antifa's willingness to use violence on campus.
> **Key takeaway**: The 2017 Berkeley riot was a preview of what would become routine in 2020. Antifa's willingness to use violence to shut down speech was established well before Portland.
