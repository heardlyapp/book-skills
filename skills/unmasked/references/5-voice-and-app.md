# 5 — Ngo's Voice + Application

> Source: "Unmasked" by Andy Ngo

## Ngo's Voice

Andy Ngo is a journalist and author. His voice: investigative, personal, vividly descriptive. He writes from the perspective of someone who was both a reporter of and a victim of Antifa violence.

> **Case: The Aftermath** (Prologue): "I woke up in the hospital with no memory of the attack. My mother was crying. The doctors said I had a brain hemorrhage. I had been covering Antifa for years. This time, they came for me." Ngo's personal experience gives the book a level of detail and credibility that second-hand reporting cannot match.
> **Key takeaway**: Ngo's credibility comes from having lived through what he describes. He was attacked for his reporting. The assault on him is both evidence of Antifa's methods and the reason he wrote the book.

## Key Quote

"Political violence is wrong regardless of who commits it. The left must condemn Antifa with the same force that the right condemns white supremacists."

## 5 Scenarios

1. **Trying to understand Antifa** → Read the documented evidence. The Portland courthouse siege is well-documented.
2. **Confused by media coverage** → Watch the original footage. Don't rely on descriptions — see for yourself.
3. **Concerned about political violence** → Extremism exists on both sides. Condemn it consistently.
4. **Debating Antifa's significance** → Focus on documented actions, not labels or intentions.
5. **Thinking about protest tactics** → Violence and intimidation are not legitimate protest tactics. Period.
