# 2 — Antifa Tactics and Organization

> Source: "Unmasked" by Andy Ngo

## The Black Bloc

The black bloc is Antifa's signature tactic. Participants wear black clothing and masks to avoid identification while committing violence and property destruction. The tactic originated in European protests in the 1980s.

> **Case: The Black Bloc Formation** (Chapter on Tactics): "The black bloc emerges from the crowd. Participants pull on black hoodies and bandanas, covering their faces. They move as a unit, targeting specific buildings, vehicles, or individuals." Ngo describes the choreographed nature of black bloc actions.
> **Key takeaway**: Antifa's violence is not random — it is organized and targeted. The black bloc functions as a tactical formation, not an angry mob. Participants know exactly what they are doing.

> **Case: The Doxxing Campaigns** (Chapter on Intimidation): "Antifa publishes personal information — home addresses, phone numbers, employer details — of political opponents, journalists, and even government officials. This is not activism — it is intimidation." Ngo documents how doxxing is used to terrify targets into silence.
> **Key takeaway**: Doxxing is a form of violence. By publishing personal information, Antifa exposes its targets to real physical danger from others who might act on the information. Ngo himself was doxxed and received death threats.
