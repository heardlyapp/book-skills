# 2 — Antifa Tactics and Organization

> Source: "Unmasked" by Andy Ngo

## The Black Bloc

The black bloc is Antifa's signature tactic. Participants wear black clothing and masks to avoid identification while committing violence and property destruction.

## Organization

Antifa is decentralized but not disorganized. Affinity groups coordinate through encrypted messaging apps. Tactics and strategies are shared across cities.

> **Case: The Black Bloc Formation** (Chapter on Tactics): "The black bloc emerges from the crowd. Participants pull on black hoodies and bandanas, covering their faces. They move as a unit, targeting specific buildings, vehicles, or individuals." Ngo describes the choreographed nature of black bloc actions.
> **Key takeaway**: Antifa's violence is not random — it is organized and targeted. The black bloc is a tactical formation, not an angry mob.

> **Case: The Doxxing Campaigns** (Chapter on Intimidation): "Antifa publishes personal information — home addresses, phone numbers, employer details — of political opponents, reporters, and even government officials. This is not activism — it is intimidation." Ngo documents how doxxing is used to terrorize targets into silence.
> **Key takeaway**: Doxxing is a form of violence. It exposes people to real physical danger from those who would act on the information. Ngo himself was doxxed and received death threats.
