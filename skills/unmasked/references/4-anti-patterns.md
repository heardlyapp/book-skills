# 4 — Media and Political Response

> Source: "Unmasked" by Andy Ngo

## Anti-Pattern: Minimization

**The mistake:** Dismissing Antifa as a fabrication or overblown concern, despite documented evidence of violence and organization.

## Anti-Pattern: Selective Coverage

**The mistake:** Covering violence from the right extensively while downplaying violence from the left.

> **Case: The Anonymous Attack** (Chapter on Ngo's Assault): "I was attacked by Antifa in Portland in June 2019. A group of masked individuals punched and kicked me, hitting me with a metal object that caused a brain hemorrhage. The assault was captured on video. Some media outlets described it as a 'scuffle.'"
> **Key takeaway**: The way media describes similar events differently based on the ideology of the perpetrators is evidence of bias. Ngo's assault was captured on video and was clearly a violent attack — calling it a "scuffle" is a choice.

> **Case: The Political Response** (Chapter on Politics): "When Trump called Antifa a terrorist organization, many on the left objected. But when city leaders in Portland refused to condemn Antifa violence, they enabled it." Ngo argues that the political response to Antifa has been inconsistent and insufficient.
> **Key takeaway**: The failure of political leaders to clearly condemn left-wing political violence creates an environment where it can flourish. Condemning violence must be consistent regardless of ideology.
