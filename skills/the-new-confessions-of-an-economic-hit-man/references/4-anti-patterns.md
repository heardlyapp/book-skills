# 4 — Anti-Patterns

> Source: *The New Confessions of an Economic Hit Man* by John Perkins.

## 4 Errors

### 1. Trusting Foreign Aid

Aid is often a weapon. Loans create dependency. "Foreign aid" is a euphemism for corporate subsidies.

### 2. The Growth Addiction

Believing that all growth is good. GNP can grow while the majority become poorer.

> **Case: The Panama Canal Negotiations** (Chapter 17): Torrijos wanted a fair deal. The US refused. Torrijos died in a plane crash — assassinated, Perkins believes.

### 3. The Single Conspiracy Fallacy

There is no one cabal. The system is more dangerous than a conspiracy: it is a self-perpetuating network of aligned interests.

### 4. The "I'm Too Small to Matter" Trap

Individuals can act. The brick makers did. Your choices to localize, reduce debt, and build community are acts of resistance.
