# 2 — Principles

> Source: *The New Confessions of an Economic Hit Man* by John Perkins.

## 6 Principles

1. **Debt is the primary tool of empire.** Loans that can never be repaid create permanent dependency.

2. **Follow the money.** Every economic decision benefits someone. Ask who.

> **Case: The Ecuadorian Brick Makers** (Chapter 1): Perkins helped the brick makers form a co-op that undercut the ricos who controlled them. The lesson: the people themselves must take action.

> **Case: Indonesia's Master Energy Plan** (Chapter 6): Perkins produced a 25-year forecast that justified billions in loans. The electricity went to oil companies and wealthy Javanese. The poor got debt.

3. **Fear drives the death economy.** We are told to be afraid of terrorists, immigrants, the other.

4. **The EHM system has come home.** Student loans, mortgages, credit cards.

5. **Transparency is protection.** Perkins' book became his insurance.

6. **A life economy is possible.** Local, sustainable, equitable.
