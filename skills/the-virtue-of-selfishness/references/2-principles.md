> Source: The Virtue of Selfishness, Chapters 1-15 — 7 principles of Objectivist ethics

## Principle 1: Life Is the Standard of Value

The foundation of Objectivist ethics: "good" is what serves the life of a rational being. Values are not subjective preferences — they are objective requirements of survival. This gives ethics a firm foundation in reality.

> **Case: The Alternative of Death** (Chapter 1): Rand argues that the concept of "value" is dependent on the concept of "life." For an inanimate object, nothing is good or evil — it simply exists. For a living being, things can help or harm its survival. Ethics is the science of choosing values that serve life.

## Principle 2: Reason Is the Only Guide to Action

Emotions, faith, and social convention are not valid guides to action. Only reason — the faculty that identifies and integrates the evidence of the senses — can guide human choices. Objectivism rejects all forms of mysticism and whim-worship.

## Principle 3: Rational Self-Interest Is Moral

Pursuing your own life and happiness is not selfishness in the conventional sense — it is the highest moral purpose. The conventional view that selfishness is evil is based on a false equation of selfishness with sadism or whim-worship.

## Principle 4: Altruism Is the Enemy

Altruism, as Rand defines it, is the doctrine that self-sacrifice is the moral ideal. It is not kindness or generosity — it is a code that demands you value others above yourself. Consistently applied, it destroys individuality and happiness.

## Principle 5: Individual Rights Are Absolute

Rights belong to individuals, not groups. The only proper function of government is to protect individual rights. Any government action beyond that — redistribution, regulation, conscription — is a violation of rights.

> **Case: The Nature of Government** (Chapter 4): Rand argues that government is properly an agency that protects individual rights through objective laws and enforcement. A government that initiates force against its citizens (through taxation, regulation, or conscription) violates the very rights it is supposed to protect.

## Principle 6: The Trader Principle Governs Relationships

All moral relationships are voluntary exchanges for mutual benefit. Both parties gain. Neither is sacrificed. This applies to economic exchange, friendship, love, and intellectual discourse.

## Principle 7: Happiness Is the Purpose of Morality

The purpose of morality is not to suppress happiness — it is to achieve it. A rational ethics tells you how to live so that you can achieve genuine, long-term happiness. Self-sacrifice cannot produce happiness because it denies the value of your own life.
