# Anti-Patterns

## 1. Perfectionism

**The mistake**: Waiting until the work is perfect before releasing it.

**Fix**: The finished imperfect piece is worth more than the unfinished masterpiece. Release and move on.

## 2. Comparison

**The mistake**: Measuring your work against others'.

**Fix**: Your voice is unique. The only comparison that matters is between your current work and your previous work.

> **Case: The Comparison Trap** (Chapter 22): Rubin describes artists who become paralyzed by comparing their early work to their heroes' finished work. They don't see the thousands of hours of practice, the failed attempts, the editing. They see only the final product and conclude they'll never be that good.
> **Key takeaway**: Don't compare your behind-the-scenes to someone else's highlight reel. Every great artist was once a beginner making bad work.

## 3. Overthinking

**The mistake**: Analyzing and judging your work while you're creating it.

**Fix**: Create first. Edit later. Don't mix the two modes.

## 4. Attachment to Outcome

**The mistake**: Needing the work to be successful, popular, or meaningful.

**Fix**: Create for the process, not the outcome. The result is out of your control.

> **Case: The Album That Almost Wasn't Released** (Chapter 15): Rubin worked with an artist who created a brilliant album but then doubted it. He kept tinkering, re-recording, second-guessing. The album was almost abandoned. Rubin eventually had to say: "It's done. Let it go." The album was released and became a classic.
> **Key takeaway**: Sometimes the artist is the worst judge of their own work. Trust the process and the people who believe in you.

## 5. Forcing Inspiration

**The mistake**: Sitting and waiting for inspiration to strike.

**Fix**: Inspiration comes from doing, not waiting. Start moving. The ideas will follow.
