# 1 — Classical Marxism and Its Evolution

> Source: "American Marxism" by Mark R. Levin

## The Original Ideology

Levin begins by defining classical Marxism — its core tenets, its ambitions, and its historical failures. He argues that understanding the original ideology is essential to understanding its modern descendants.

> **Case: The Core Tenets** (Chapter on What Marxism Is): "Marx argued that all of human history is the history of class struggle. The bourgeoisie (owners) exploit the proletariat (workers) by extracting surplus value from their labor. The solution: revolution, the abolition of private property, and the establishment of a communist society." Levin outlines the basic framework.
> **Key takeaway**: Marxism is not just a critique of capitalism — it is a comprehensive theory of history, a political program, and a prediction about the future. It claims to be scientific. Understanding these claims is necessary to understand why they remain appealing.

> **Case: The Historical Failures** (Chapter on Marxist History): "Every country that has implemented Marxism has produced the same result: poverty, tyranny, and mass death. The Soviet Union, China, Cuba, North Korea, Cambodia — the body count is in the hundreds of millions. Yet the ideology persists." Levin catalogs the historical record of Marxist regimes.
> **Key takeaway**: The persistence of Marxism despite its catastrophic results is one of the great paradoxes of modern intellectual history. Levin argues that this is because Marxism is not a practical system — it is a religion, complete with dogma, heretics, and a vision of paradise that justifies any means.
