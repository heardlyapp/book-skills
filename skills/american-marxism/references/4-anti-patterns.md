# 4 — The Assault on America's Founding

> Source: "American Marxism" by Mark R. Levin

## Anti-Pattern: Historical Revisionism

One of the most dangerous anti-patterns Levin identifies: the systematic effort to tear down America's founding, its founders, and its principles.

> **Case: The 1619 Project** (Chapter on Revisionism): "The New York Times' 1619 Project argued that America's true founding was not 1776 but 1619, when the first enslaved Africans arrived. It claimed that the American Revolution was fought to preserve slavery. These are not historical arguments — they are political attacks on the American idea." Levin critiques one of the most prominent examples of historical revisionism.
> **Key takeaway**: The 1619 Project is not history — it is a political document that uses historical material to advance a Marxist narrative. It replaces the story of American freedom with a story of American oppression. If successful, it will destroy the moral foundation of the Republic.

## Anti-Pattern: Equity Over Equality

Levin identifies the shift from equality of opportunity to equity of outcome as a core Marxist move.

> **Case: The Equity Trap** (Chapter on Social Justice): "The demand for 'equity' is a demand for equal outcomes. This is not a reform of the American system — it is a rejection of it. The American ideal is equality of opportunity under law. Equity requires the redistribution of resources until all groups have the same results. This is Marxism." Levin distinguishes between equality and equity.
> **Key takeaway**: The shift from "equality" to "equity" appears small but is revolutionary. Equality means the same rules for everyone (classical liberal). Equity means different rules to produce the same outcomes (Marxist collectivism). The language of equity is the poison pill.
