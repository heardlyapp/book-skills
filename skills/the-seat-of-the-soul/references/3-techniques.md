## Practical Techniques

### 1. The Intention-Setting Practice

Before any significant action, sit quietly and set an intention. Ask: "What is my deepest intention here?" If the intention comes from fear (control, avoidance, approval-seeking), reconsider. If it comes from love (learning, growth, service), proceed.

### 2. The Intuition Check

When facing a decision, check your body's response. Does your stomach tighten? Do you feel expansive or contracted? Your body is your soul's voice. Learn to trust it.

> **Case: The Business Decision** (Chapter 5): Zukav tells of a businesswoman who had a choice between two deals. The first deal offered more money but felt heavy. The second deal offered less money but felt light. She chose the second — following intuition over logic. The first deal fell through due to fraud. Intuition had protected her.
> **Key takeaway**: Intuition often knows what logic does not. The soul sees the bigger picture. When your body contracts, pay attention.

### 3. The Karma Reflection

When something painful happens, ask: "What is this teaching me?" Not "Why is this happening to me?" but "What is my soul learning from this?" The shift in question changes everything.

### 4. The Reverence Practice

At least once daily, consciously treat someone or something with reverence — a person, an animal, a plant, a task. Reverence is a practice, not a feeling.

> **Case: The Reverent Gardener** (Chapter 4): Zukav describes a gardener who tended her plants with reverence — speaking to them, touching them gently, thanking them. The plants thrived. Reverence is not sentiment — it is a spiritual practice that creates harmony.
> **Key takeaway**: Reverence can be practiced anywhere. A reverent act changes the energy of the moment. It aligns you with authentic power.
