# 1 — Origins and Framework

> Source: "The Cold War: A New History" by John Lewis Gaddis

## The Framework: 1945-1949

### The Division of Europe
WWII ended with Soviet and American armies meeting in the center of Europe. Stalin installed communist governments in Eastern Europe. The US responded with containment.

### Key Documents
- **Long Telegram** (1946): George Kennan's analysis of Soviet behavior — they understood only force.
- **Truman Doctrine** (1947): US would support free peoples resisting subjugation.
- **Marshall Plan** (1948): $13 billion to rebuild Western Europe. Kept it from going communist.

> **Case: The Long Telegram** (Chapter 1): George Kennan, a US diplomat in Moscow, sent an 8,000-word telegram analyzing Soviet behavior. His conclusion: the USSR was fundamentally insecure, expansionist, and understood only the language of force. This telegram became the foundation of US Cold War policy.
> **Key takeaway**: One cable from a mid-level diplomat shaped US strategy for 45 years. Ideas matter. Clear analysis can change history.

> **Case: Stalin's Miscalculation** (Chapter 2): Stalin believed the US would retreat into isolationism after WWII, as it had after WWI. He was wrong. The Marshall Plan, NATO, and the Berlin Airlift showed America would stay. Stalin's miscalculation was based on assuming the US would repeat history.
> **Key takeaway**: Leaders often fight the last war. Stalin expected the US to repeat 1919. Instead, the US learned from 1919 and stayed engaged. Adapting to new circumstances is the essence of strategy.
