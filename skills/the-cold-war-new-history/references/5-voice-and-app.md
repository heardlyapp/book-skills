# 5 — Gaddis' Voice + Application

> Source: "The Cold War: A New History" by John Lewis Gaddis

## Gaddis' Voice

John Lewis Gaddis is the preeminent Cold War historian. His voice: balanced, elegant, authoritative. He writes with the confidence of someone who has spent decades studying the subject and the clarity of someone who knows how to tell a story.

> **Case: Why He Wrote the Book** (Preface): Gaddis explains that the Cold War is the closest thing to a "laboratory" in international relations. "We can study it like scientists study a phenomenon — with the advantage of distance and access to archives." The book is his attempt to distill 45 years of history into a single readable volume.
> **Key takeaway**: The Cold War is the best-documented major historical event. We can study it more thoroughly than any previous conflict. The lessons are — or should be — deeply learned.

## Key Quote

"The Cold War was the product of two fears: fear of communism and fear of capitalism. Neither side was wrong about the other's capacity for evil."

## 5 Scenarios

1. **US-Russia relations today** → The Cold War never fully ended for Russia. Understanding the history helps explain Putin.
2. **Nuclear weapons policy** → The nuclear taboo is not guaranteed. We must actively maintain it.
3. **Great power competition** → The Cold War shows that competition is manageable if both sides are rational.
4. **Ideological conflict** → When values clash, understanding the other side's ideology is essential for managing the conflict.
5. **Change in history** → Gorbachev and Reagan showed that individuals can change the course of history. Leadership matters.
