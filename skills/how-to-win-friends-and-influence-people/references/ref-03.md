# Reference: Winning People to Your Way of Thinking

## Overview
Persuasion is not about argument. It is about creating an environment where the other person wants to agree with you.

## Case: Avoid Arguments
Carnegie observed that nine times out of ten, an argument ends with each party more convinced than ever that they are right. You cannot win an argument. If you lose, you lose. If you win, you have hurt the other person's pride, and they will resent you. The only way to get the best of an argument is to avoid it.

## Case: Admit Your Mistakes
When you are wrong, admit it quickly and emphatically. Carnegie tells the story of walking his dog without a leash in a park where it was illegal. A policeman was about to give him a ticket. Before the policeman could speak, Carnegie said, "You're right, Officer. I was wrong. I have no excuse." The policeman softened. Admitting you are wrong disarms criticism.

## Case: Get to Yes Early
The Socratic method: ask questions that the other person will answer yes to. Each yes makes them more receptive to the next question. By the end, they are agreeing with you without feeling pressured. Carnegie illustrates with a salesman who sold a difficult prospect by asking him questions that led to yes answers, building agreement gradually.

## Case: Let the Other Person Feel the Idea
People are more committed to ideas they discover themselves. Instead of telling someone what to do, Carnegie suggests planting a seed and letting them arrive at the conclusion. "I wonder what would happen if..." is more effective than "You should..."
