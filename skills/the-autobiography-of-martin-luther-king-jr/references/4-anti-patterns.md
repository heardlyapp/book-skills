# Anti-Patterns — The Autobiography of Martin Luther King, Jr.

## The Central Error: "MLK Was a Moderate Who Wanted Incremental Change"

This is the most common misreading of King. "White moderates" were, in fact, his harshest critics. The Letter from Birmingham Jail is a devastating critique of the "white moderate who is more devoted to 'order' than to justice."

### Mistake 1: "MLK's 'I Have a Dream' speech was his only message"
King is often reduced to that speech. But his message evolved: he opposed the Vietnam War, fought for economic justice, and was organizing the Poor People's Campaign when he died. Case: "I have a dream" is one speech. The "Beyond Vietnam" speech, the "Mountaintop" speech, the Letter from Birmingham Jail — each is equally important.

### Mistake 2: "Nonviolence means being passive"
"Nonviolent direct action seeks to create such a crisis and foster such a tension that a community is forced to confront the issue." Case: The Birmingham campaign deliberately provoked Bull Connor's violence to create national pressure. Nonviolence was confrontation, not passivity.

### Mistake 3: "The civil rights movement was only about race"
King's vision included economic justice from the beginning. The Poor People's Campaign was multiracial — poor whites, blacks, Native Americans, Latinos. Case: "What good does it do to be able to eat at a lunch counter if you can't afford a hamburger?"

### Mistake 4: "Change comes through polite persuasion"
"Freedom is never voluntarily given by the oppressor; it must be demanded by the oppressed." Case: The 381-day Montgomery bus boycott was not polite persuasion — it was economic pressure that forced change.

### Mistake 5: "Churches should stay out of politics"
King argued the opposite. "A church that is not a striking and growing force in social reform is nothing but a self-centered club." Case: King's activism was inseparable from his ministry.

### Mistake 6: "MLK was universally loved in his time"
At his death, King was one of the most hated men in America. The FBI surveilled him. Polls showed 75% of white Americans disapproved of him. Case: He was assassinated at 39. "The only way to deal with a man like King is to kill him," an FBI official reportedly said.
