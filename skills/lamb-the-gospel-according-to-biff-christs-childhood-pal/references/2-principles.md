> Source: Lamb: The Gospel According to Biff, Christopher Moore (2002).

# Key Principles — Doubt, Love, and the Human Side of Divinity

## Core Principles

> **Case: Josh's Love for Maggie** (Mid-novel): Josh loves Maggie. He cannot act on it because he is divine. He is torn. Biff tells him to follow his heart. Josh cannot. His love is real. His restraint is real. Both are true. This is the most human moment in the novel: the Son of God with a broken heart.
> **Key takeaway**: Even the Son of God struggled with love. Your struggles with love are not a sign of weakness. They are a sign of being human.

> **Case: The Forty Days** (Desert): Josh goes into the desert to fast and pray. Biff goes with him. Biff is not fasting. He hides food. He supports Josh. He does not judge. He is present. When Josh emerges from the desert, he has begun to understand his purpose. Biff has learned nothing. He is still Biff. That is fine.
> **Key takeaway**: When someone you love is going through something difficult, you do not have to do it exactly as they do. You just have to be there.

> **Case: Josh's Doubt** (Gethsemane): Josh knows what is coming. He is afraid. He asks if there is another way. He knows there is not. He accepts it. This is the most powerful moment in the novel because it shows that even Jesus had to choose. He chose willingly. He was not a puppet. He was a man making a terrible choice.
> **Key takeaway**: Doubt is not the opposite of faith. It is part of it. Even Jesus had doubts. The choice to go forward despite doubt is what makes faith meaningful.
