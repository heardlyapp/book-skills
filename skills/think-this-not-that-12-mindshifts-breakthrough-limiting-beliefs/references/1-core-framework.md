# Core Framework: The Memory Transplant and ABCDE Model

## The Memory Transplant

The most powerful technique in the book. When a limiting belief is blocking you, consciously replace it with a new narrative supported by evidence. Axe's high school teacher Mrs. Nobel laughed when he said he wanted to become a doctor. Combined with his 2.3 GPA and ADHD diagnosis, this created a belief that nearly destroyed his potential. Years later, college teacher Mrs. Williams returned his first paper with an A+ and said, "Have you ever thought about becoming a writer?" He put the paper on his bathroom mirror and saw it every morning. The new evidence eventually overwrote the old belief. He finished the summer program with a 3.5 GPA, got into the University of Kentucky, and went on to earn a doctorate.

## The Nocebo Effect

A 1970s patient was diagnosed with end-stage liver cancer and told he had months to live. He died within the predicted time frame. The autopsy revealed only a small, localized tumor that was not life-threatening. Doctors were wrong, but his belief killed him. Conversely, a patient in Axe's clinic was wheelchair-bound with what doctors said was multiple sclerosis. Axe discovered she actually had mold toxicity. Within days of the correct diagnosis, she walked again.

## The ABCDE Model

From Albert Ellis's rational emotive behavior therapy. A = Activating event (what triggered the belief). B = Belief (what you told yourself). C = Consequences (what happens if you keep believing). D = Disputation (challenge the belief rationally). E = Effects (new healthy outcome). Axe used this for his own belief: A = Mrs. Nobel's mockery. B = "I'm not smart." C = I won't try, I'll fail. D = "I can study harder. I've succeeded when I've applied myself." E = Medical school admission.

## The 10x Strategy

Visualize what you want ten times bigger than possible. Axe's client John believed business growth would mean less freedom. When he imagined the business 10x bigger, he realized he would hire people for the tasks he hated. The limiting belief dissolved and his business grew 10x.

## The 50,000 Thoughts Study

Research cited from Wharton School: the average person has 50,000 thoughts per day, 95 percent on repeat. Most are lies we've been told. This makes the memory transplant essential — you must break the loop.

## Jamie Kern Lima (IT Cosmetics)

An investor told Lima that "no one would want to buy makeup from someone who looked like her." She chose to believe otherwise. L'Oréal bought IT Cosmetics for $1.2 billion. Lima became the first female CEO in L'Oréal's 108-year history.
