# Becoming vs Accomplishing and Finding Purpose

## The Becoming Mindset

Axe's 96-year-old grandfather Howard had no wealth, no social media presence, no Forbes listing. At his funeral, person after person called him "my best friend." "Howard gave me money when I lost my job." "Howard showed me what a great father looked like." Axe realized: "I needed to reexamine my definition of success." He developed the formula: Success = maximizing your unique skill for the good of others.

## The Lance Armstrong Cautionary Tale

Armstrong was the most influential athlete in America in 2010 — seven Tour de France titles, massive wealth, global influence. The doping scandal cost him everything: his career, sponsorships, reputation, and personal relationships. "When you accumulate accomplishments while ignoring your character, it's like building a house on sand."

## Steve Jobs' Regrets

Jobs left behind a $10.2 billion fortune and world-changing products. But he told his biographer: "I wanted my kids to know me. I wasn't always there for them." Research cited: a Journal of Research in Personality study tracked 147 college graduates. Those who valued material goals over relationships had less life satisfaction.

## Axe's Mother's Cancer

When his mom was diagnosed with stage 4 breast cancer at 41, it became Axe's life purpose: using food as medicine. She adopted a holistic protocol — dietary changes, supplements, gratitude, prayer, visualization. Her tumors shrank 52 percent in four months. She later became a guide for other cancer patients. At 68, she still water-skis.

## The Isaac Meek Story (Five Daughters Bakery)

Axe's friend Isaac left a corporate job "crushing his family" to start a baking company. He woke at 3 AM every morning to make croissant-style donuts. He includes his five daughters in the business. He schedules date nights with each daughter and annual trips with his wife. His character guided his purpose.

## The Ikigai Framework

The Japanese concept of ikigai: where what you love, what you're good at, what the world needs, and what brings reward intersect. Axe used this framework to find his purpose. His purpose evolved: clinical practice → #1 natural health website → Ancient Nutrition → leaders.com.

## The STARS Goal Method

Specific, Trackable, Accountable, Realistic, Significant. Axe created this framework for pursuing purpose. His purpose changed over time, but the method stayed consistent.
