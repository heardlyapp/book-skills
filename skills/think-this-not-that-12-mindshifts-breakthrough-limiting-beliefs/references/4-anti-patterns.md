# Rewriting Your Story and Building Community

## Harriet Tubman's Hero Journey

Born into slavery in 1820, Minty (her birth name) was beaten as a child, rented out as a nursemaid at five, set to catch muskrats at seven. At twelve, she stepped between a slave owner and a slave he was about to strike. She sustained severe head injuries causing lifelong seizures. She escaped to Philadelphia in 1849 but returned to Maryland to free her family. She became a conductor of the Underground Railroad, helping approximately 70 enslaved people to freedom. Later she became the first woman to lead an armed Civil War expedition, freeing 700+ slaves. "Tubman had every right to remain a victim. She became a hero because she kept saying yes to the cycle of action."

## Axe's Failed Business

After moving to Florida and pouring everything into a business that failed — costing him "every penny of savings" — Axe had two months of savings left. He sought counsel, found a mentor who introduced him to a community of greatness, and relaunched. He went on to build Ancient Nutrition, one of the most successful supplement companies globally. "That opportunity-turned-failure was one of my greatest teachers."

## The Four Character Types

Axe identifies four character types in any story: Victim (self-focused, helpless, full of excuses), Villain (resentful, hurts others, narcissistic), Hero (willing to transform, accepts responsibility, uses pain for good), Guide (focuses on helping others transform, has compassion and wisdom). The goal is moving from victim to hero to guide.

## Joseph Campbell's Hero's Journey (Simplified)

Comfort → Awaken → Assemble a Team → Quest → Trials → Transformation → Triumph → Return with Wisdom. Axe adapted Campbell's 17 stages into 8.

## Community of Greatness

6-8 people sharing purpose and values. Axe's group had signed agreements: confidentiality, monthly meetings, one-on-ones between meetings, annual retreats, shared reading. Members prioritized the group above almost everything else.

## The Target Meeting

A Target executive wanted to meet about Axe's supplements. The only available time conflicted with his men's group retreat. Axe chose the retreat. Target waited.

## Dunbar's Number and Mentorship

Humans maintain ~150 relationships max, with only 5 in the core support group. Yet 76 percent of people believe mentors are important, while only 37 percent have one. Mentored employees are promoted five times more often.

## Tolkien and C.S. Lewis

J.R.R. Tolkien and C.S. Lewis were members of the Inklings, a group that gathered weekly to read and critique each other's works. Without this community, Lord of the Rings and Narnia might never have been completed.
