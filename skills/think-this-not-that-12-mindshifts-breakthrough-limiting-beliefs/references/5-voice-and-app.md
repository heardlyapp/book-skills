# Fear, Visualization, and Positive Perseverance

## Carol Dweck's Fifth Graders

The classic study: children were given easy problems and praised either for intelligence ("You must be smart") or effort ("You must have worked hard"). Next, they were given difficult problems. The intelligence-praised children gave up quickly and saw failure as evidence they weren't smart. The effort-praised children persisted. They saw failure as motivation. This is the difference between fear-based mindset and flourishing mindset.

## Axe's Spinal Infection

After a stem cell procedure went wrong, Axe was diagnosed with a spinal infection causing osteomyelitis. He couldn't walk. Doctors said he might never fully recover, could be permanently disabled, or could die. He spent a year learning to walk again — crawling first, then shuffling with a walker, then walking. He lost 40 pounds. He chose hope, grit, and gratitude. "Instead of feeling pity for myself, I strove to rise above my feelings."

## Sara Blakely (Spanx)

A former door-to-door fax machine salesperson, Blakely's father encouraged her to celebrate failures. When she came home with a D, he asked: "What did you learn?" This reframe allowed her to persist through constant rejection. She built a billion-dollar company. She credits visualization as "a top determining factor of SPANX's success." She even visualized being on Oprah's show before it happened — which came true.

## Charles Snyder's Hope Theory

Three components of hope: goals (what you want to achieve), pathways (the routes to reach them), and agency (the motivation to use those pathways). Develop all three for positive perseverance.

## Angela Duckworth's Grit Research

Grit — passion and persistence for long-term goals — is a greater predictor of success than IQ, talent, or socioeconomic status. Axe's spinal infection was a grit test: one year of crawling, shuffling, and walking.

## Deion Sanders

Two-time Super Bowl champion, two-sport professional athlete. At the height of his success, he attempted suicide. After surviving, he turned to coaching, teaching young men character, honor, and responsibility at the University of Colorado. His message: "You send us a boy, we'll send you back a man."

## The Free-Throw Visualization Study

Three groups of basketball players. Group 1 practiced physical free throws (24 percent improvement). Group 2 did nothing (no improvement). Group 3 visualized making free throws (23 percent improvement). Mental rehearsal nearly matches physical practice.

## Viktor Frankl's Concentration Camp Story

A female prisoner who knew she was going to die focused on a tree branch with two blossoms. She said the tree told her: "I am here — I am here — I am life, eternal life." Axe uses this as the ultimate example of finding hope in suffering.
