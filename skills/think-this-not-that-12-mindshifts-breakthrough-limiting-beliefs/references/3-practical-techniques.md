# Practical Techniques

## Overview

Axe provides an extensive toolkit of techniques drawn from clinical psychology, sports psychology, business strategy, and his personal experience. Each is designed to be practiced daily until it becomes habit.

## The Memory Transplant

Identify the specific event where a limiting belief was formed. Create a counter-memory that provides evidence against the old belief. Write it down. Display it where you'll see it daily. Repeat until the old belief loses power.

**Case — Axe's A+ paper:** After college teacher Mrs. Williams gave him an A+ and said he should consider writing, Axe put the paper on his bathroom mirror. Every morning, he saw evidence that Mrs. Nobel had been wrong. The new evidence eventually overwrote the old belief.

**Case — The 10x Strategy:** Axe's client John believed business growth would mean less freedom. Axe asked him to imagine the business ten times bigger. At that scale, John realized he would hire help. The limiting belief dissolved, and his business grew 10x.

## The ABCDE Model (Albert Ellis)

From rational emotive behavior therapy. A = Activating event (what triggered the belief). B = Belief (what you told yourself). C = Consequences (what happens if you keep believing). D = Disputation (challenge the belief rationally). E = Effects (new healthy outcome).

**Case — Axe's classroom belief:** A = Mrs. Nobel laughed. B = "I'm not smart." C = I won't try. D = "I can study harder. I've succeeded when I've applied myself." E = Better grades, medical school admission.

## Personal SWOT Analysis

Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats across five life areas: faith, fitness, finances/work, family, free time. Be brutally honest.

**Case — The deadlifts:** Axe hurt his back because his wife Chelsea showed him he was using poor form. His pride had prevented him from accepting instruction. The SWOT exercise revealed pride as his blind spot.

## Community of Greatness Protocol

6-8 people sharing purpose and values. Signed commitment: frequency, accountability, confidentiality. Monthly meetings, one-on-ones between meetings, annual retreats.

**Case — The Target meeting:** A Target executive wanted to meet about stocking Axe's supplements. It conflicted with his men's group retreat. Axe chose the retreat. Target waited.

## The Virtue Card Method

Write a virtue on an index card with supporting quotes on the other side. Practice it in every conversation for one month.

**Case — Encouragement practice:** Axe practiced saying encouraging words daily. "At first, it was uncomfortable." After months, people told him he "seemed like a different person."

## Visualization Protocol

5 minutes daily in a quiet space. Full sensory detail — sights, sounds, feelings. Repeat daily.

**Case — Sara Blakely:** She visualized being on Oprah's show before it happened. It became a "top determining factor of SPANX's success."

**Case — Free-throw study:** Three groups: physical practice (24% improvement), nothing (0%), visualization (23% improvement). Mental rehearsal nearly matches physical practice.
