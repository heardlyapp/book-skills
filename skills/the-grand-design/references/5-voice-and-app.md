# Author's Voice + Key Scenarios

> **Case: Hawking's Legacy** (Preface): Stephen Hawking was one of the most brilliant theoretical physicists since Einstein. Despite having ALS, he continued to work, write, and communicate his ideas to the public. The Grand Design was his last major book before his death.
> **Key takeaway**: Hawking's authority is unparalleled in modern cosmology. His willingness to address the biggest questions — the origin of the universe, the existence of God — made him a unique figure.

> **Case: The No-Boundary Proposal** (Chapter 9): Hawking proposed that the universe has no boundary in time — like the Earth has no boundary at the poles. At the Big Bang, time becomes like a dimension of space. There is no "before" the Big Bang because time didn't exist.
> **Key takeaway**: The question "What came before the Big Bang?" is meaningless, like asking "What is north of the North Pole?" The universe's beginning is not an event in time — time began at the Big Bang.

## Key Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Understanding the Big Bang
Time began with the Big Bang. There is no "before."

### Scenario 2: Quantum Curiosity
At the quantum level, reality is probabilistic. Observation determines outcome.

### Scenario 3: The God Question
Hawking's argument: physics can explain the universe without a creator.

### Scenario 4: Multiverse
Our universe may be one of many, each with different physical laws.

### Scenario 5: Purpose
The universe has no purpose — but our lives can.
