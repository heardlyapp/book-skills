# Techniques: Understanding Modern Cosmology

## The Big Bang Explained

1. 13.8 billion years ago, all matter/energy was concentrated in a single point
2. Space itself began expanding
3. Inflation caused exponential expansion in the first fraction of a second
4. As it cooled, matter, galaxies, and stars formed
5. The universe continues expanding today (accelerating)

> **Case: The Universe from Nothing** (Chapter 6): Hawking describes how the total energy of the universe is exactly zero. The positive energy of matter is balanced by the negative energy of gravity. This means the universe can be created from nothing without violating the law of conservation of energy.
> **Key takeaway**: The universe doesn't need a creator because its net energy is zero. Creating something from nothing is possible if the net result is nothing.

## Key Questions

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| Before the Big Bang? | No "before" — time began with the Big Bang |
| Why these laws? | In a multiverse, every set of laws exists somewhere |
| Any purpose? | According to physics, no |

> **Case: The Fishbowl Reality** (Chapter 5): A fish in a curved bowl develops physics based on its observations. The theory is correct for the fish's world but not the outside world. We are the fish. Model-dependent realism accepts this.
> **Key takeaway**: There is no "view from nowhere." Our physics describes our universe — but may not describe ultimate reality. Model-dependent realism focuses on predictions, not "truth."
