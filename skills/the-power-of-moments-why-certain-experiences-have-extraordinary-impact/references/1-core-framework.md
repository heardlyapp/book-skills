# Core Framework: The EPIC Framework

> Source: The Power of Moments, Introduction and Chapters 1-2

## Defining Moments

The Heaths open with a question: why do certain experiences stay with us forever while others fade? They identify four common elements that, when combined, create defining moments.

> **Case: The Scholar-Athlete Ceremony** (Introduction): A high school created an academic pep rally with the same energy as sports events — announcements, a keynote speaker, real emotion and drama. The student honored called it "the best night of my life." A designed defining moment.

> **Key takeaway**: Defining moments can be intentionally designed using the right elements. They are not accidents.

## The EPIC Framework

Elevation (building peaks through surprise and sensory appeal), Insight (creating breakthrough realizations), Pride (celebrating milestones and courage), Connection (building relationships through shared experiences).

> **Case: The Peak-End Rule** (Chapter 2): People judge experiences by their peak and their end only. A longer colonoscopy with a gentler ending was rated BETTER than a shorter one. The total experience matters less than the best and final moments.

> **Case: Breaking the Script** (Chapter 2): A hotel leaving a surprise treat for guests creates a peak moment by violating expectations. Breaking the script is the simplest and most powerful way to create elevation.

> **Key takeaway**: The more EPIC elements you include, the more powerful the moment. Even adding one element can transform an ordinary experience into something unforgettable.
