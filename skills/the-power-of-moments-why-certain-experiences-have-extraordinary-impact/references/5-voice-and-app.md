# Voice and Application: Heath Brothers' Approach

> Source: The Power of Moments, throughout

## Their Voice

Chip and Dan Heath write as storytellers and researchers. They combine compelling case studies with behavioral science. Their tone is warm, accessible, and practical. Each chapter opens with a story, then reveals the science, then gives actionable takeaways.

> **Case: The Popsicle Concierge** (Chapter 3): A hotel with a poolside phone labeled "FREE POPSICLES." A child calls. Someone answers with a French accent: "Popsicle concierge!" This tiny unexpected act is the most memorable part of the stay — a perfect example of "breaking the script."

> **Case: The Scholar-Athlete Ceremony** (Introduction): A school creates an academic pep rally equal to sports events — with a keynote speaker, announcements, and genuine emotion. The recipient called it "the best night of my life." Designed, not accidental.

> **Key takeaway**: The Heaths make research actionable. They show what works and how to do it.

## Application: Business

Create defining moments in customer experience, employee recognition, and product launches. Design peaks.

## Application: Personal Life

Create defining moments for family celebrations, birthdays, and holidays. Break the script.

## Application: Education

Design insight and pride moments for students. Stretch them and celebrate their effort.

## Key Quotes

- "Defining moments shape our lives — but we don't have to wait for them to happen."
- "Break the script."
- "Tripping over the truth is more powerful than being told."
- "Peaks and endings are what we remember. Design both."
