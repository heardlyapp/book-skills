# Principles: Elevation and Insight

> Source: The Power of Moments, Chapters 3-6

## Elevation — Building Peak Moments

Three ingredients: sensory appeal (make it special), raise the stakes (add competition or performance), break the script (violate expectations).

> **Case: The Popsicle Concierge** (Chapter 3): A hotel has a poolside phone. A sign says "FREE POPSICLES." When a child picks up the phone, someone answers with a French accent: "Popsicle concierge! May I send your popsicle out?" This tiny break from the script creates the most memorable moment of the stay.

> **Case: The Surprise Recognition** (Chapter 3): A manager who publicly recognizes an employee's work with genuine specificity and appreciation creates a peak moment that far exceeds any generic "employee of the month" program.

> **Key takeaway**: Breaking the script is the cheapest and most powerful way to create a peak moment. Unexpectedness is memorable.

## Insight — Trip Over the Truth

People don't change because they're told. They change because of realizations they reach themselves.

> **Case: The Stretch Assignment** (Chapter 5): A struggling student is given a responsibility beyond her comfort zone. She discovers her own capability. The insight came from the stretch, not from being told "you can do it."

> **Case: The Exposure Trip** (Chapter 6): A student visits a college campus she never considered. The visit changes her entire trajectory. Exposure to new possibilities creates insights that arguments cannot.

> **Key takeaway**: Create conditions for self-discovery. "Tripping over the truth" is more powerful than having the truth pointed out.
