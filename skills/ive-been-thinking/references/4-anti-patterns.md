# Anti-Patterns — I've Been Thinking...

## The Central Error: "My Life Is Over Because of What Happened to Me"

"I am not what happened to me. I am what I choose to become." Shriver's entire book pushes against the idea that circumstance defines destiny. After her divorce, her children leaving home, her parents aging and dying — she chose to re-create.

### Mistake 1: "I'll be happy when..."
"I used to spend so much time beating myself up for choices I'd made... or I deluded myself by focusing my attention only on an imagined future." The "I'll be happy when" trap keeps you out of the present. "Living in either the past or the future keeps me up in my head, out of reality, robbing me of the present."

### Mistake 2: "I need other people to validate my worth"
"We all want someone to see the jewel that is inside of us. We all long to be seen as valuable." But Shriver warns: "We work hard trying to get others to recognize our value, giving them the power to decide if we are, in fact, jewels. But that power is actually our own."

### Mistake 3: "Grief means I'm weak"
"It's okay — in fact, it's crucial — to grieve." Shriver pushes back against the idea that we should always be strong. "We all want to skip to the happy ending, but the only way through grief is through it." Case: She watched her parents age and die, and felt the full weight of that loss.

### Mistake 4: "I'm too old to start over"
Shriver wrote "Sixty Life Lessons for My Birthday" at sixty. Her friend reframed "empty nester" as a new beginning. "Don't call me an empty nester." She had to re-create her life in her fifties after her divorce. "Your life is yours to create and then re-create."

### Mistake 5: "Power comes from position and money"
Shriver's experience with the Benedictine nuns showed her otherwise. "When we talk about powerful women, we always focus on those in political office. But I want to focus on women whose power comes from within themselves." The nuns have "none of the trappings" but are deeply fulfilled.

### Mistake 6: "I have to go it alone"
"We're in need of a social kindness movement." "Why we should stop trying to 'go it alone.'" Shriver emphasizes community, friendship, asking for help. Case: the nuns created a community of support; Shriver herself relies on her circle.
