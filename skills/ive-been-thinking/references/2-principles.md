# Key Principles — I've Been Thinking...

## I. You Are Not Your Past

"I am not what happened to me. I am what I choose to become" (Carl Jung). Shriver emphasizes that every day offers the opportunity to begin anew. "No one's life follows a linear path. No one's life is devoid of mistakes, pain, and regret." Case: Shriver herself had to re-create her life after her divorce from Arnold Schwarzenegger. She stepped into the unknown and rebuilt.

## II. See the Jewel Inside

"The most fundamental harm we can do to ourselves is to remain ignorant by not having the courage and the respect to look at ourselves honestly and gently" (Pema Chödrön). Shriver's metaphor: we are jewels inside wrinkled gift wrap. "How do we turn our focus away from the glittering gift wrap we think is us and focus instead on the jewel inside?" Case: Shriver watched both her parents age and loved them no less — she decided to love herself the same way as she aged.

## III. Fortitude Comes from Faith

"What is intestinal fortitude? Faith is at the root of it." Shriver writes about the storm of modern culture — "people scream at each other on TV, trash-talking is applauded." The answer is not more rage — it's deeper faith. "People with intestinal fortitude are calm in the storm."

## IV. Power Comes from Within

Shriver's visit to the Benedictine nuns taught her that real power is not external. "Sister Joan and the others have helped me change my own opinion of power. Power comes from values, from beliefs, from purpose, from within." The nuns work in inner cities, teach, serve, and "don't make any money doing it" — yet they are happier and more fulfilled than most.

## V. Gratitude Is a Daily Practice

"Every morning when I open my eyes, and before my feet touch the ground, I thank God for the gift of my life." Shriver cites research: gratitude makes you happier and more hopeful. Case: she seeks out people who have a gratitude practice — "they look at the world through a clear lens. They're more joyful. When adversity arises, they bounce back faster."

## VI. Grief Is Not Optional — It's Crucial

"It's okay — in fact, it's crucial — to grieve." Shriver does not shy away from the hard parts of life. She writes about losing loved ones, about the need to mourn, about the importance of processing pain. "We all want to skip to the happy ending, but the only way through grief is through it."

## VII. Life Is Yours to Create and Re-Create

Drawing on Steve Jobs: "Everything around you that you call life was made up by people who were no smarter than you. And you can change it, you can influence it." Shriver's message: no matter how old you are or what has happened, you have the power to rebuild. "Your life is yours to create and then re-create."
