# Techniques — I've Been Thinking...

## How to Live a Meaningful Life (Based on Maria Shriver)

### Technique 1: The Gratitude Morning Practice
"Every morning when I open my eyes, and before my feet touch the ground, I thank God for the gift of my life." Shriver's daily practice: give thanks for health, family, friends, and country before starting the day. She also keeps a gratitude journal. "I've found that starting out that way makes for a better day and, in turn, I believe it makes for a better life."

### Technique 2: The Negative-to-Positive Thought Swap
"When a negative thought pops up in my mind, instead of dwelling and ruminating on it, I think about how I can turn that negative thought into a positive thought." Case: worrying about her youngest son's graduation. Instead of "I'm going to fall apart," she shifted to "I'm going to be absolutely fine. I've prepared him well." The technique: catch the negative thought, stop it, and redirect with a positive certainty.

### Technique 3: The Intestinal Fortitude Check
When the world feels unsteady, Shriver doesn't react with more anger. "I turn my focus away from all that and instead focus on strengthening my own intestinal fortitude by renewing my own faith." Case: When reporters are called "bimbo," when a journalist is gunned down on live TV — she asks herself: "What the—?!" then deliberately shifts to strengthening her own foundation.

### Technique 4: The Home Blessing
Shriver had a priest bless her home. He told her: "Create a peaceful home, a blessed home. Make that a priority. That way, when you go forth from it, you go forth from a place of peace out into the world." The technique: make your home a sanctuary of peace before you try to change the world.

### Technique 5: The Nun Power Model
Shriver's insight from visiting Sister Joan Chittister: real power is internal. The technique: when feeling powerless, ask yourself — what is my purpose? What are my values? Who am I serving? "Power comes from values, from beliefs, from purpose, from within."

### Technique 6: The "Go Back to Move Forward" Reflection
"Go back to move forward." Shriver recommends reflecting on your past — not to live there, but to understand how you got here. Case: she studied biographies to understand people's journeys. The technique: reflect on your own life story to see the patterns, the lessons, and the growth. Then use that understanding to inform your next steps.

### Technique 7: The "I Love You to the Moon and Back" Practice
Shriver dedicates the book to her children: "I love you all to the moon and back." Her technique: use specific, personal, affirmative language with loved ones. "Talk of love, not hate." "Bring joy into your home." Case: she writes about "The Power of Thank You" — a simple practice of expressing gratitude to those in your life.
