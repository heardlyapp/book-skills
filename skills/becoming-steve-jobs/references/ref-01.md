# Reference: Jobs's Early Years

## Overview
Steve Jobs was born in 1955 to Joanne Schieble and Abdulfattah Jandali, who gave him up for adoption. Paul and Clara Jobs raised him in Mountain View, California — the heart of what would become Silicon Valley.

## Case: Reed College and Calligraphy
Jobs dropped out of Reed College after one semester but continued auditing classes for 18 months. He sat in on a calligraphy class that later inspired the Mac's multiple typefaces and proportionally spaced fonts. "If I had never dropped in on that single course," Jobs later said, "the Mac would have never had multiple typefaces or proportionally spaced fonts."

## Case: The India Trip
At 19, Jobs traveled to India seeking spiritual enlightenment. He returned a Buddhist, shaved-headed, and wearing Indian robes. The trip deepened his appreciation for intuition over pure logic — a quality that distinguished him from engineering-focused Silicon Valley. Jobs later said India taught him that "the most important things in life are not things."

## Case: The Homebrew Computer Club
Jobs and Wozniak attended the Homebrew Computer Club, a regular gathering of hobbyists in Silicon Valley. Wozniak went for the engineering; Jobs went for the business opportunity. When Wozniak designed the Apple I, Jobs insisted they sell it as a completed board rather than a kit. He saw what Wozniak did not: the personal computer was a product, not a hobby.
