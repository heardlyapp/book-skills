# Reference: Apple's Renaissance

## Overview
When Jobs returned to Apple in 1997, the company was 90 days from bankruptcy. He orchestrated one of the most remarkable turnarounds in business history.

## Case: The iMac Saved Apple
The iMac (1998) was Jobs's first major product after returning. It was a translucent all-in-one with no floppy drive — an act of courage in 1998. The iMac sold 800,000 units in its first five months and returned Apple to profitability. Jobs insisted on the USB port, the CD-ROM drive, and the distinctive design. The design-oriented approach — form and function together — became Apple's signature.

## Case: The iPod + iTunes Ecosystem
The iPod (2001) was not the first MP3 player. But Jobs understood that the key was not the device but the ecosystem: iTunes, the iTunes Store, seamless syncing. The iPod + iTunes combination changed the music industry. It also laid the groundwork for the iPhone: Apple learned how to negotiate with content companies, manage digital rights, and create a platform business.

## Case: The iPhone Changed Everything
Jobs announced the iPhone in January 2007. It was three products in one: a widescreen iPod, a revolutionary mobile phone, and a breakthrough internet communicator. The touchscreen keyboard, the multi-touch gestures, the sensors — none of these were invented by Apple, but Apple was the first to combine them into a coherent product that ordinary people could use. The iPhone remains the most profitable product in history.
