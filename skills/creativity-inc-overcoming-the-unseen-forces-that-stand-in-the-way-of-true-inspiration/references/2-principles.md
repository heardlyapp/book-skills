# Principles: Candor and Protecting the Process

> Source: Creativity, Inc., Chapters 4-7

## Candor Over Harmony

Catmull argues that many organizations prioritize harmony over candor. They avoid difficult conversations. This kills creativity.

> **Case: The Honest Note** (Chapter 4): A Pixar artist receives a note that his work isn't working. The feedback is direct, specific, and respectful. He doesn't take it personally — he takes it as help. This is candor: telling the truth about the work without attacking the person.

> **Key takeaway**: Candor is not cruelty. It's respect for the work and the person. Without it, mediocrity takes hold.

## Protecting the Process

Leadership's primary job: shield the creative team from corporate pressure.

> **Case: The Disney Merger** (Chapter 6): When Disney acquired Pixar, the Pixar leadership had to protect Pixar's culture from Disney's bureaucracy. They succeeded by being clear about what couldn't change.

> **Key takeaway**: The leader's most important job is to create a container where creatives can take risks without fear.

## The Sucky First Version

Every Pixar film was bad before it was good. The goal is to surface problems early.

> **Case: The Toy Story 2 Crisis** (Chapter 7): The first version of Toy Story 2 was terrible enough that it could have been scrapped. Instead, the team reworked it relentlessly until it became a classic.

> **Key takeaway**: Expect the first version to be bad. The process of iteration and feedback makes it great.
