# Anti-Patterns: Creativity Killers

> Source: Creativity, Inc., Chapters 11-13

## Putting the Idea Before the Team

The most common mistake: obsessing over the idea and ignoring the people. Pixar proves the opposite.

> **Case: The Brilliant Jerk Hire** (Chapter 11): Pixar hired a brilliant director based on a great pitch. The person couldn't collaborate with the team. The project stalled and had to be restructured. Catmull learned: hire for team fit, not just brilliance.

> **Key takeaway**: Team before idea. A great team fixes bad ideas. A bad team ruins great ideas. Hire for collaboration, not just talent.

## Avoiding Difficult Feedback

Leaders who avoid tough conversations are destroying their creative potential.

> **Case: The Silent Meeting** (Chapter 12): Everyone in the room knew the project was failing, but no one said anything. Candor was absent. Months of work were wasted. The cost of silence was enormous.

> **Key takeaway**: Silence is not kindness. Avoiding candor wastes time, money, and creative energy. Speak the truth with respect.

## Success Breeds Complacency

After success, organizations stop questioning themselves. That's when decline begins.

> **Case: The Post-Hit Blindness** (Chapter 13): After Finding Nemo and The Incredibles, Pixar could have coasted. Catmull created Notes Day to surface problems. The commitment to improvement must be systemic and deliberate.

> **Key takeaway**: After success, question everything. Complacency is the enemy of sustained creative excellence.
