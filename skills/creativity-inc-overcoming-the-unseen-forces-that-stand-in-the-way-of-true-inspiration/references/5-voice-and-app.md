# Voice and Application: Catmull's Approach

> Source: Creativity, Inc., throughout

## Catmull's Voice

Ed Catmull writes as a thoughtful, humble leader who spent decades building a creative organization. His tone is reflective, honest, and generous — he shares failures as openly as successes. He doesn't claim to have all the answers.

> **Case: The Honest Introduction** (Introduction): Catmull opens with his own failures — projects that flopped, mistakes he made. This honesty sets the tone. He's a learner sharing what he's discovered.

> **Case: The Steve Jobs Afterword** (Afterword): Catmull's portrait of Steve Jobs is deeply human — showing both his brilliance and his flaws. He doesn't sugarcoat Jobs, but he shows how Jobs grew over time.

> **Key takeaway**: Catmull's humility makes his lessons powerful. His credibility comes from having done the work, not from claiming expertise.

## Application: Team Building

Hire for team fit, not just talent. A great team is worth more than a great idea.

## Application: Feedback

Create your own Braintrust. A group of peers who tell you the truth. No authority, just honest insight.

## Application: Culture

Culture requires constant maintenance. Success creates complacency. Actively improve how you work through postmortems and feedback days.
