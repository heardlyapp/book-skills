# Core Framework · The Creativity Birthright · Fear of Judgment

> Source: *Creative Confidence*, Chapters 1-3

## Chapter 1: The Creativity Birthright

"The belief that you can create meaningful change is the most powerful force in the world."

**The myth:** Creativity is a rare gift given to artists and geniuses.
**The truth:** Creativity is a fundamental human capacity. Everyone is born creative. It's trained out of us by schools, workplaces, and social pressure.

> **Case:** A woman who believed she wasn't creative her entire life. At a workshop, she was asked to make a simple prototype out of clay. The result was terrible — but the process unlocked a flood of ideas. She realized: creativity isn't about the quality of the first attempt. It's about the willingness to try.

## Chapter 2: Fear of Judgment

The single biggest obstacle to creativity is the fear of being judged. This fear manifests as:
- The inner critic ("that's a stupid idea")
- Social anxiety ("what will they think?")
- Perfectionism ("it's not good enough")

**The fix:** Separate idea generation from idea evaluation. Generate without judgment first. Evaluate later.

> **Case:** A team at IDEO was stuck on a design problem. The leader asked everyone to sketch their worst possible idea. The laughter broke the tension — and the terrible ideas sparked a genuinely good solution.
