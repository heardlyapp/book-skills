# 2 — Healing Emotional Wounds

> Source: "The Mastery of Love" by Don Miguel Ruiz

## The Wound

Everyone carries emotional wounds from childhood. These wounds shape how we see love and relationships.

## Healing

Healing doesn't mean forgetting. It means understanding, forgiving, and learning to love yourself through the pain.

> **Case: The Rejection Wound** (Chapter 5): Ruiz describes how the fear of rejection begins in childhood. "When we are rejected by a parent, we learn that we are not enough. We spend the rest of our lives trying to prove our worth." The healing: recognizing that you were always enough.
> **Key takeaway**: Rejection is a wound, not an identity. You were rejected, but that doesn't make you rejectable. Separating the event from your self-worth is the healing.

> **Case: The Emotional Body** (Chapter 7): Ruiz teaches that emotions have a physical presence in the body. "Grief lives in the chest. Fear in the stomach. Anger in the jaw. Healing is not mental — it's physical. You must feel the emotion in your body and let it move through."
> **Key takeaway**: Emotional healing is not intellectual. You can't think your way out of trauma. You must feel it in your body and allow it to release.
