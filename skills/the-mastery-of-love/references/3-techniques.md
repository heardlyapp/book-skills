# 3 — Self-Love Practices

> Source: "The Mastery of Love" by Don Miguel Ruiz

## Self-Love Is Not Selfish

Many people resist self-love because they think it's selfish. Ruiz: "You cannot love others until you love yourself. Self-love is the foundation, not the obstacle."

## Practices

1. Accept yourself as you are — not as you think you should be.
2. Forgive yourself for past mistakes. You did the best you could.
3. Stop comparing yourself to others. Your journey is unique.
4. Speak to yourself with kindness, not criticism.

> **Case: The Judge** (Chapter 6): Ruiz describes the "Judge" in our mind — the voice that constantly criticizes us. "The Judge says you're not good enough, not smart enough, not worthy. But the Judge is not you. It's a learned voice. You can stop listening."
> **Key takeaway**: You don't have to believe every thought. The inner critic is a learned pattern, not the truth. Distance yourself from it.

> **Case: The Victim** (Chapter 8): "When we blame others for our suffering, we become victims. The only way out of victimhood is to take responsibility for your own healing." Ruiz teaches that no one can hurt you without your permission.
> **Key takeaway**: Taking responsibility for your emotions is not blaming yourself — it's empowering yourself. You can't change others, but you can change your response.
