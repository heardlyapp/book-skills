# 4 — Anti-Patterns

> Source: "The Mastery of Love" by Don Miguel Ruiz

## Anti-Pattern 1: Need as Love

**The mistake:** Confusing need for love. "I need you" is not love — it's dependency.

## Anti-Pattern 2: Control as Care

**The mistake:** Trying to control your partner "because you care." Control is fear, not love.

## Anti-Pattern 3: Jealousy as Passion

**The mistake:** Believing jealousy is a sign of love. It's a sign of insecurity.

## Anti-Pattern 4: The Rescuer

**The mistake:** Trying to save or fix your partner. They are not broken. You are not their savior.

> **Case: The Neediness Trap** (Chapter 9): Ruiz describes the relationship where one person needs the other to feel complete. "When you need someone, you become dependent on them. You lose yourself. The relationship becomes a cage." True love is two whole people choosing to share their lives — not two halves trying to make a whole.
> **Key takeaway**: If you feel you can't live without someone, that's not love — that's attachment. Love is free. Attachment is a cage.

> **Case: The Jealousy Story** (Chapter 10): Ruiz tells a story about a couple where one partner is consumed by jealousy. "Jealousy has nothing to do with the other person. It's about your own fear — fear of not being enough, fear of being replaced. Healing the jealousy means healing your self-worth."
> **Key takeaway**: Jealousy is a mirror of your relationship with yourself. If you trust yourself, you can trust your partner.
