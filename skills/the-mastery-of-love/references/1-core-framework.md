# 1 — The Framework

> Source: "The Mastery of Love" by Don Miguel Ruiz

## Love Is Being, Not Getting

The central teaching: love is not something you find or receive. It's what you are. "You are love itself. You don't need to be loved — you are love."

## The Toltec View

The Toltec tradition sees the human mind as a "dream" — a collective agreement about reality. What we call love is often just a dream conditioned by society.

> **Case: The "Hole" in the Soul** (Chapter 2): Ruiz describes the belief that we have a hole in our soul that someone else must fill. "We believe we need someone to complete us. This is the root of all suffering in relationships. You are not missing anything — you are already whole."
> **Key takeaway**: The belief that you need someone to complete you is the source of codependency and suffering. You are already whole. Relationships are for sharing, not completing.

> **Case: The Dream of Perfection** (Chapter 4): Ruiz explains that we all carry an image of the "perfect partner." When real people don't match the image, we try to change them. "The image is a fantasy. The person is real. Choose reality over fantasy."
> **Key takeaway**: Let go of the perfect partner fantasy. It doesn't exist. Love the real person in front of you, not the image in your mind.
