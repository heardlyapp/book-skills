# Anti-Patterns: What Not to Do with Long Covid

> Source: Long Covid Handbook, Chapters 8-10

## Pushing Through Symptoms

The most dangerous mindset for Long Covid. "No pain, no gain" works for athletic training but destroys recovery from post-viral conditions.

> **Case: Medinger's Mistake** (Introduction): Gez tried to run through his initial COVID symptoms. This triggered the severe post-viral fatigue that took over two years to recover from. His marathon training mindset — push harder, don't give up — made his Long Covid significantly worse.

> **Key takeaway**: If you have PEM, pushing through is not bravery. It's self-harm. Rest is the most productive thing you can do.

## Assuming Normal Tests Mean Nothing Is Wrong

Standard blood tests, MRIs, and scans are often normal in Long Covid. But the pathology is in the immune system — which standard tests don't measure.

> **Case: The Normal Blood Work** (Chapter 4): Patients are told "everything is normal" and dismissed. But specialized tests (T-cell subsets, autoantibodies, viral fragments) often show significant abnormalities. Normal results don't mean nothing is wrong.

## Ignoring PEM Triggers

Continuing activities that consistently cause crashes — whether physical, cognitive, or emotional — prevents recovery. This includes social events, screen time, and even conversations for severe patients.

> **Key takeaway**: If an activity consistently causes PEM, you must stop or significantly reduce it. This is not giving up — it's strategic recovery.
