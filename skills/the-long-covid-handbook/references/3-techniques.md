# Techniques: Symptom Management

> Source: Long Covid Handbook, Chapters 9-13

## Brain Fog Strategies

Brain fog includes difficulty concentrating, memory problems, word-finding issues, and mental fatigue. Caused by brain inflammation and reduced blood flow. Practical strategies: reduce cognitive load (write everything down, use lists, simplify decisions), take brain breaks (5-minute rest every 30 minutes), eliminate multitasking (single-task only).

> **Case: The Post-It Note Method** (Chapter 9): Patients with brain fog learn to externalize memory — write everything down, use phone alarms, keep a master list visible at all times. This reduces the cognitive load of trying to remember. Accepting that you need these aids is part of recovery.

## Sleep Management

Long Covid often disrupts sleep architecture. Tips: maintain a consistent schedule (same bedtime and wake time every day), avoid screens for 2 hours before bed, use relaxation techniques, consider melatonin under doctor supervision.

> **Case: The Pacing Diary** (Chapter 9): Tracking sleep quality alongside activity levels helps identify patterns. Many patients find that poor sleep predicts a low-energy day, and over-exerting predicts poor sleep. This feedback loop is key to managing recovery.

## Dealing with Medical Gaslighting

Chapter 8 addresses a common challenge: being dismissed by doctors. Strategies: bring a detailed symptom diary to appointments, request specific tests, see a Long Covid specialist if possible, bring an advocate to appointments.

> **Key takeaway**: If a doctor tells you it's "just anxiety," seek a second opinion. Long Covid is a real physiological condition with measurable immune abnormalities.
