# Core Framework: Understanding Long Covid

> Source: Long Covid Handbook, Chapters 1-5

## What Is Long Covid

Symptoms persisting beyond 12 weeks after initial COVID-19 infection. Over 200 symptoms have been identified across every body system. The most common are fatigue, brain fog, breathlessness, chest pain, and post-exertional malaise (PEM).

> **Case: Gez Medinger's Journey** (Introduction): A marathon runner who got COVID in March 2020. He tried to run through it and crashed hard. Five weeks in, he recognized the feeling of post-viral fatigue from a previous glandular fever episode. He went from marathon training to barely able to walk around the block. His film about the experience went viral, and he built a YouTube channel of 80+ films.

> **Case: The Immunology Perspective** (Chapter 4): Prof. Altmann explains that Long Covid involves immune system abnormalities — T-cell exhaustion, altered B-cell responses, and persistent inflammation. Standard blood tests often appear completely normal despite significant symptoms.

## Causes

Four leading theories: immune dysfunction (abnormal T-cell and B-cell responses), viral persistence (SARS-CoV-2 fragments remaining in tissues), autoimmunity (immune system attacks the body's own cells), and microclots (small blood clots affecting circulation).

> **Key takeaway**: Long Covid is not one condition — it's likely several different subtypes with different underlying mechanisms. Treatment must be individualized to the patient's specific symptom profile.
