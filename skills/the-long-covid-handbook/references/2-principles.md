# Principles: Pacing and PEM Management

> Source: Long Covid Handbook, Chapters 9-11

## The Central Concept: PEM

Post-Exertional Malaise is the hallmark symptom of Long Covid. It's not just being tired after exercise — it's a systemic worsening of ALL symptoms (fatigue, brain fog, pain, flu-like feelings) after physical, cognitive, or emotional exertion. The worsening is often delayed by 24-72 hours, making it hard to connect cause and effect.

> **Case: The Crash Cycle** (Chapter 9): Many patients get stuck in a push-crash cycle. They feel good one day, push too hard (a walk, a social event, even a phone call), then crash for days or weeks. They recover slightly, then push again. Each crash sets recovery back further.

> **Key takeaway**: If you have PEM, the single most important thing is to stay within your energy envelope. Rest before you're exhausted.

## The Pacing Protocol

Find your baseline: the amount of activity you can do without causing PEM. Stay below this threshold consistently. When you're stable for 2-3 weeks, increase activity by 5-10%. If you crash, drop back down to your previous level.

> **Case: The Heart Rate Monitor Method** (Chapter 9): Some patients use a heart rate monitor to pace. They stay below their "anaerobic threshold" (often 100-110 bpm for severe cases). This prevents PEM by avoiding the metabolic state that triggers crashes. Even mental activity can raise heart rate.

## Energy Management Tools

Use a diary to track activities and symptoms. Identify what triggers your crashes. Learn your energy envelope. Schedule proactive rest periods — rest before you crash, not after.
