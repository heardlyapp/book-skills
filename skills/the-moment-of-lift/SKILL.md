## Philosophy — 5 rules to remember

1. **When you lift women, you lift everyone.** Empowering women creates more for everyone.
2. **Family planning changes everything.** The ability to control if and when to have children is foundational.
3. **Educating girls is the highest-return investment.** An educated girl marries later, earns more, has healthier children.
4. **Listen to women themselves.** Top-down solutions fail. Best interventions come from listening.
5. **Systemic change takes patience.** Real change takes generations. Quick fixes don't work.

## Core Framework Quick Reference

- **The Moment of Lift** = When a woman is empowered to make her own decisions about her life.
- **Family Planning** = Access to contraception and reproductive health care.
- **Child Marriage** = Marriage before age 18, barrier to empowerment.
- **Microfinance** = Small loans and financial services for women entrepreneurs.
- **The Gender Gap** = Systematic differences in opportunities, rights, and outcomes.

## Key Principles

1. **Empowering women lifts entire communities.** When women earn income, they invest in their children's education and health.
2. **Family planning is the foundation.** Without control over reproduction, other forms of empowerment are limited.
3. **Education is transformative.** Girls who stay in school longer marry later and have healthier families.
4. **Economic agency creates independence.** Women need access to credit, markets, and property rights.
5. **Men must be partners.** Gender equality is not a zero-sum game — it benefits everyone.
6. **Listen before acting.** The most effective programs come from women themselves.

## Anti-Pattern Summary

The book's core correction: Many well-intentioned development programs impose solutions from the outside without understanding local context. The most effective interventions start by listening to women. See `references/4-anti-patterns.md`.
