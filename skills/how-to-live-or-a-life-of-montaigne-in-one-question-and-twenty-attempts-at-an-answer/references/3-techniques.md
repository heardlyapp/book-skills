# Techniques from Montaigne

**Technique 1: Write to Think. Montaigne did not write because he had something to say. He wrote to discover what he thought.**

**Technique 2: Read Widely, Forget Most of It. Reading is not about accumulation. It is about digestion.**

**Technique 3: Keep a Private Room. Everyone needs a space where they can be alone with their thoughts.**

**Case: The Quotation. Montaigne filled his essays with quotations from ancient writers.**

**Case: The Revision. Montaigne kept revising his essays. They grew with him.**

**Case: The Conversation. Montaigne valued conversation as a form of thinking. Talking with others sharpens the mind.**

**Case: The Idleness. Montaigne wrote about the value of doing nothing. Idle time is productive for the soul.**
