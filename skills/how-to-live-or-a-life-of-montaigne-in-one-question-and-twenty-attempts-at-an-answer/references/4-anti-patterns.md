# Anti-Patterns — What Montaigne Rejected

**Anti-Pattern 1: Dogmatism. Certainty is the enemy of wisdom. Montaigne rejected anyone who claimed absolute truth.**

**Anti-Pattern 2: Self-Importance. Taking yourself too seriously is a mistake. Montaigne laughed at himself.**

**Case: The Pedants. Montaigne despised scholars who only quoted others and never thought for themselves.**

**Case: The Fanatics. He saw religious extremists kill each other over certainties they could not prove.**

**Case: The Stoics. Not the Stoics themselves, but those who pretended to be above human suffering.**

**Case: The Philosophers. Philosophers who claimed to have found the answer. Montaigne believed the question is more important.**

**Case: The Bigotry. Montaigne opposed bigotry in all forms. He believed in tolerance and humanity.**

**Case: The Cruelty. Montaigne hated cruelty above all. He wrote: "I hate cruelty, both by nature and by reason."**

**Case: The Pretense. Montaigne rejected intellectual pretense. He wrote plainly about ordinary things.**

**Case: The Withdrawal. Montaigne rejected the idea of complete withdrawal from the world. Live in the world but keep your freedom.**
