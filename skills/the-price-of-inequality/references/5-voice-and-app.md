# Author's Voice + Key Scenarios

> **Case: Stiglitz's Credentials** (Introduction): Joseph Stiglitz is a Nobel Prize-winning economist, former Chief Economist of the World Bank, and chair of the Council of Economic Advisers under President Clinton. He has spent decades studying inequality from both academic and policy perspectives.
> **Key takeaway**: Stiglitz brings rare credibility — he's not an activist but a world-class economist. His criticism of inequality is grounded in data, not ideology.

> **Case: The Market Fundamentalism Critique** (Conclusion): Stiglitz argues that the 2008 financial crisis was not a failure of markets but a failure of market fundamentalism — the belief that markets are always right. The same ideology that drove deregulation and inequality also caused the crash.
> **Key takeaway**: The ideology of "free markets" was used to justify policies that enriched the few at the expense of the many. The evidence shows that well-regulated markets outperform unregulated ones.
