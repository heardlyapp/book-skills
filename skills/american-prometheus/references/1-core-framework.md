# 1 — Oppenheimer's Life and Career

> Source: "American Prometheus" by Kai Bird and Martin J. Sherwin

## The Prodigy

Oppenheimer was born into wealth and privilege in New York City. He was a precocious, intense child who grew into one of the most brilliant and complex scientists of his generation.

> **Case: The Harvard Years** (Part I — The Early Years): "Oppenheimer arrived at Harvard already knowing more physics than most of his professors. He completed his degree in three years, published his first papers, and developed a reputation for intellectual intensity that bordered on the obsessive. Friends described him as brilliant but difficult — a young man who could not stop thinking." The authors paint a picture of a driven, eccentric prodigy.
> **Key takeaway**: Oppenheimer was not a normal student. His intellectual intensity was both a gift and a burden. He was drawn to the most difficult problems in physics precisely because they were difficult. This drive would make him perfect for Los Alamos.

> **Case: The European Training** (Part I — Europe): "In Göttingen, Oppenheimer worked with Max Born, one of the founders of quantum mechanics. He published groundbreaking papers, nearly had a breakdown from overwork, and eventually returned to America with a European polish and a deep understanding of the new physics." The authors trace his intellectual formation.
> **Key takeaway**: Oppenheimer's European training made him uniquely qualified to lead the Manhattan Project. He understood the cutting edge of quantum physics, had connections to the great scientists of Europe, and knew how to frame problems in ways that made them solvable. No other American scientist had his combination of depth and breadth.
