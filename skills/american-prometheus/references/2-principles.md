# 2 — The Manhattan Project

> Source: "American Prometheus" by Kai Bird and Martin J. Sherwin

## The Wizards of Los Alamos

The Manhattan Project was one of the greatest scientific and engineering achievements in history. Oppenheimer's leadership was essential to its success.

> **Case: Los Alamos Assembled** (Part II — The Project): "Oppenheimer gathered the greatest collection of scientific minds in history at Los Alamos. Feynman, Fermi, Bethe, Teller, Bohr — the list reads like a Nobel Prize directory. And he managed them not with military discipline but with intellectual respect and personal charisma." The authors describe Oppenheimer's leadership style.
> **Key takeaway**: The Los Alamos scientists were not soldiers. They were brilliant, eccentric, and impossible to command. Oppenheimer succeeded because he spoke their language, respected their work, and created an environment where the impossible seemed possible. This was perhaps his greatest achievement.

> **Case: Trinity** (Part III — The Test): "At 5:29 AM on July 16, 1945, the first atomic bomb exploded in the New Mexico desert. The light was brighter than a thousand suns. The heat was felt 10,000 yards away. Oppenheimer watched from the control bunker, his face illuminated by the blast. A line from the Bhagavad Gita came to him: 'Now I am become Death, the destroyer of worlds.'" The authors describe the Trinity test.
> **Key takeaway**: Trinity was the moment everything changed. Oppenheimer knew, in that instant, that the world would never be the same. His famous quotation reveals the complexity of his reaction — a mixture of awe, triumph, and horror. The Prometheus myth had begun.
