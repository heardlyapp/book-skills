# Reference: The Modern World

## Overview
The modern world has created conditions that suppress violence and activate the better angels. But these conditions are not guaranteed to persist.

## Case: The Leviathan (State)
The historical decline of violence is largely the story of the state's monopoly on force. In societies without effective government, individuals must be prepared to defend themselves, and disputes escalate into cycles of violence. In societies with effective government, the state punishes violence, creating an environment where individuals can resolve disputes without force. The decline of homicide in Europe from the Middle Ages to the present is primarily a story of state capacity.

## Case: Gentle Commerce
Trade reduces violence by creating positive-sum relationships. When two parties trade, both benefit. The relationship is valuable. Violence would destroy it. The expansion of global trade since WWII has created economic interdependence that makes war between trading partners economically irrational. This insight — "doux commerce" — is one of the most important forces for peace identified by Pinker.

## Case: Feminization
The decline of violence is also a story of the declining influence of male-dominated honor cultures. As women have gained political and economic power, the societies they influence have become less violent. Feminization reduces violence in multiple ways: women are less likely to approve of violence, less likely to commit violent crimes, and more likely to support peaceful resolution of conflicts.
