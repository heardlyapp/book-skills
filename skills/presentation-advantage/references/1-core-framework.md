# Core Framework — Presentation Advantage

## The Foundation: It's All About Connecting

Three kinds of connection: with your message, with yourself, with your audience. "Your presentation only has power when all three are present." Case: Peter the finance guy had the data (connected to his message) but failed to connect with himself (no passion) or his audience (boring delivery). The second time, he engaged them with a question and a clear story.

**Connect with Your Message.** "If your message is merely tactical, find some other way." If it's strategic, your energy matters. Raise the ante — connect your message to the organization's mission. Case: Peter's first version was data dump. His second version was about saving money AND making more money.

**Connect with Yourself.** Character + Competence = Credibility. "Your character includes your integrity, your motive, your intent. Your competence includes your capabilities, your skills, your track record." Stay out of the "spin cycle": no cherry-picking, doublespeak, or euphemisms. Case: The manufacturing VP who told new engineers: "Half your job is communicating your solutions."

**Connect with Your Audience.** "Average attention span in 2000: 12 seconds. In 2013: 8 seconds." People check their phones 110 times during daylight. You have 8 seconds to grab them. "Turn your presentation into a conversation."

## Chapter 2: Develop a Powerful Message

The 4-part structure: opening, key message, body, call-to-action. "The opening is your most critical moment." The key message must be one sentence — a single idea you want them to remember. Case: Peter's key message: "Our weak security is losing us money — here's how we fix it."

## Chapter 3: Design Impactful Visuals

"Poor PowerPoint is a crime against humanity." The three rules: simplicity (one idea per slide), clarity (the message should be obvious in 5 seconds), relevance (serve the audience, not your ego). Case: "Death by bullet point" — slides with dense text kill audience engagement.

## Chapter 4: Deliver with Excellence

"The medium IS the message." Delivery signals competence: eye contact (2-3 seconds per person), movement (purposeful, not pacing), voice (variety, pauses), and handling the room. Case: The same slide deck delivered by two different people can produce completely different results.
