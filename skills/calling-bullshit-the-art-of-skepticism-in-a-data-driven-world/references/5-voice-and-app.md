# Voice and Application: Bergstrom and West's Approach

> Source: Calling Bullshit, Chapters 10-11

## Their Voice

Bergstrom and West write as scientists and educators. Sharp, witty, and accessible. They respect the reader's intelligence without assuming statistical expertise. The book is full of concrete examples that anyone can relate to.

> **Case: The Closing Call to Action** (Chapter 11): The authors give specific strategies for refuting bullshit: be charitable (assume good faith), be specific (don't just shout "bullshit"), and provide a better frame. Their "truth sandwich" — state the truth, explain the bullshit, restate the truth.

> **Case: The Pre-Registration Solution** (Chapter 9): Bergstrom and West advocate for pre-registering studies — declaring hypotheses and methods before collecting data. This prevents p-hacking and selective reporting of results.

> **Key takeaway**: Calling bullshit is a craft. It requires knowledge of the subject, respect for the audience, and the ability to explain what's wrong — not just that something is wrong.

## Application: Everyday Life

Use the key questions: "Compared to what?" "Who gains?" "Is the data representative?"

## Application: Academic Work

Pre-register studies, report null results, be transparent about methods.

## Application: Journalism

Read past the headline. Check whether a study's conclusions match its data.

> **Key takeaway**: The best calling of bullshit is thorough and calm. One well-reasoned refutation beats a thousand angry tweets.
