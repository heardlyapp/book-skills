# Core Framework: What Bullshit Is and Why It Flourishes

> Source: Calling Bullshit, Chapters 1-3

## Bullshit vs. Lying

A liar knows the truth and hides it. A bullshitter doesn't care about truth — only persuasion. This is the defining distinction of the book, and its most important concept.

> **Case: The Lying Mantis Shrimp** (Chapter 3): Smaller mantis shrimp bluff about size when molting. They posture aggressively even when vulnerable. This isn't lying (they don't actually claim to be big) — it's bullshit (creating an impression regardless of truth). Bergstrom shows deception is ancient.

> **Case: The Caffeine-Free Water** (Chapter 2): "99.9% caffeine free" — technically true but meaningless. All water is 99.9% caffeine free. The statement exploits your expectation that something meaningful is being asserted.

> **Key takeaway**: Bullshitters exploit what they know you'll believe. Truth is irrelevant to them — only persuasion matters.

## The BS Asymmetry Principle (Brandolini's Law)

It takes far more energy to refute bullshit than to produce it. A false claim takes seconds to create but hours to disprove thoroughly. This asymmetry is why bullshit is so effective and so hard to fight.

> **Case: The Viral Falsehood** (Introduction): A false meme spreads to millions before any fact-check can be published. Even when thoroughly debunked, the correction reaches far fewer people.

> **Key takeaway**: The asymmetry means we must shift from reaction to prevention — building skepticism before accepting claims.
