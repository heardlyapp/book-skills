# Techniques: Data Visualization Tricks

> Source: Calling Bullshit, Chapter 7

## Truncated Axes

Starting a graph's y-axis above zero exaggerates differences. The most common visualization trick in media and politics.

> **Case: The Political Bar Chart** (Chapter 7): A bar chart showing economic growth under different presidents with the y-axis starting at 99% instead of 0. Tiny differences look enormous. Bergstrom's fix: show the full axis starting at 0.

> **Key takeaway**: Always check where axes start. If the y-axis doesn't include zero, the visual impression is likely misleading.

## Cherry-Picked Time Windows

Choosing start and end dates to maximize the apparent effect.

> **Case: The Stock Chart** (Chapter 7): A stock's performance from its low to its high looks amazing. The same data starting from any other point looks different. Bergstrom shows how to detect this: look at the full time range.

> **Case: The Temperature Graph** (Chapter 7): Climate change deniers pick short time windows to show cooling trends. The full 100-year record shows warming. Always ask: why was this time window chosen?

## 3-D Effects and Area Manipulation

3-D charts distort proportions. Changing aspect ratios changes visual impressions.

> **Case: The 3-D Pie Chart** (Chapter 7): 3-D pie charts make front slices look bigger and back slices smaller. Bergstrom recommends avoiding them entirely.

> **Key takeaway**: The goal of visualization is clarity. If a chart confuses you, that confusion may be intentional.
