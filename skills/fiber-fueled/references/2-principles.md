# Key Principles — Fiber Fueled

## I. You Are More Microbe Than Human

"There are more bacteria in your body than there are human cells. You are more microbe than human." Your gut microbiome is "the engine that drives human health." Take care of your microbes and they take care of you. Case: Leslie's health collapsed after antibiotics disrupted her microbiome — her acne treatment caused an iatrogenic (doctor-caused) gut crisis.

## II. Fiber Is Fuel for Your Microbes

"Fiber is not just a broom sweeping through your system." It's the preferred food of your gut bacteria. When microbes digest fiber, they produce short-chain fatty acids (SCFAs) — "the single most important component of the diet to optimize your health." SCFAs reduce inflammation, strengthen the gut barrier, regulate appetite, and support immune function.

## III. Diversity Is the Key to Microbiome Health

"The diversity of your diet directly determines the diversity of your microbiome." A healthy gut has hundreds of different bacterial species. Each species eats different types of fiber. Bulsiewicz's target: 30 different plant foods per week. Case: Leslie's restrictive diet (only arugula, avocado, meat, bone broth) created a depleted, mono-culture gut. Reintroducing diversity rebuilt her microbiome.

## IV. Most Food Sensitivities Are Fixable

Gas, bloating, and discomfort when eating beans and whole grains are not signs of inflammation — they're signs that your gut needs to rebuild its microbial population. "When you have a sensitivity to a food, the solution is not to eliminate it forever — it's to heal your gut so you can digest it." Case: Leslie had eliminated beans for years. Through gradual reintroduction, she was able to enjoy them again.

## V. The Standard American Diet Is the Problem

"The average American eats about 15 grams of fiber per day. The recommended amount is 30-35 grams. Our ancestors ate 100+ grams." Processed foods, animal-heavy diets, and lack of plant diversity starve the microbiome. The result: an epidemic of chronic disease directly linked to gut health.

## VI. Postbiotics Are the Endgame

Probiotics, prebiotics, and postbiotics are the holy trinity — but the goal is postbiotics. SCFAs (butyrate, propionate, acetate) are what actually heal your body. "Fiber is the fuel, your microbes are the engine, and SCFAs are the horsepower."

## VII. Food Sensitivities Can Be Resolved

Bulsiewicz's step-by-step approach: (1) Remove problematic foods temporarily. (2) Heal the gut with fiber diversity. (3) Reintroduce foods gradually, starting with small amounts. (4) Use preparation techniques (soaking, sprouting, cooking) to reduce gas-producing compounds. The goal is not restriction — it's liberation.
