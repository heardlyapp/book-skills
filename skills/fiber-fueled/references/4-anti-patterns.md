# Anti-Patterns — Fiber Fueled

## The Central Error: "Fiber Causes Inflammation and Bloating"

"Popular diet gurus have demonized fiber, claiming it causes inflammation, bloating, and gut damage." Bulsiewicz argues the opposite: gas and bloating are signs that your gut is underpopulated with the right bacteria, not that fiber is bad. "When you have a sensitivity to a food, the solution is not to eliminate it forever — it's to heal your gut so you can digest it."

### Mistake 1: "Paleo/Whole30/Keto is gut-healing"
"Bone broth and grass-fed meat are touted as gut healing. There's not a single study to support that, not even a crappy one." Case: Leslie tried all of these restrictive diets. Each one gave short-term relief followed by worsening symptoms. The common thread: they all restrict the diversity of plant foods, starving the microbiome.

### Mistake 2: "Lectins and phytates are dangerous"
"Phytates and lectins are demonized by some diet gurus, but the science shows they are actually beneficial." Phytates are antioxidants. Lectins are destroyed by cooking. The anti-nutrient narrative is not supported by evidence. "The longest-lived populations on earth eat beans and whole grains daily."

### Mistake 3: "Grains cause inflammation"
Bulsiewicz cites studies showing that whole grains reduce systemic inflammation. "The problem is not grains — it's refined grains." Leslie had eliminated wheat for 10 years. Reintroducing whole wheat bread (in the right amounts) improved her health.

### Mistake 4: "You need lots of protein"
"The obsession with protein is misplaced." Most Americans eat twice the recommended amount. Excess protein can be converted to byproducts that are harmful to the gut lining. "The real concern should be fiber deficiency, not protein deficiency."

### Mistake 5: "Probiotics are the most important"
"Probiotics are the supporting cast." The most important thing is feeding your existing microbes with fiber. Without prebiotics, probiotics cannot colonize. "The holy trinity is prebiotics, probiotics, postbiotics — in that order of priority."

### Mistake 6: "Fiber causes gas = fiber is bad"
Gas is a sign of fermentation — which is exactly what you want. "If you have gas after eating beans, it means your microbes are doing their job." The solution is not to stop eating fiber — it's to increase it gradually so your gut can adapt.
