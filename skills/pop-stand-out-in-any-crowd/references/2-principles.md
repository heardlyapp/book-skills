## 7 Principles of Standing Out

### 1. Purpose Is the Foundation

Without purpose, you are just another person in the crowd. Know what you stand for. Know why you exist.

### 2. Originality Creates Distinction

Being different is not enough — you must be different in a way that matters to your audience.

> **Case: The Consultant's Gap** (Chapter 5): A management consultant was one of hundreds. She identified a gap: no one was applying her methodology to non-profit organizations. She became the go-to expert for non-profits. Originality = focus.
> **Key takeaway**: Originality is not about being completely novel — it is about finding the intersection of your skills and an underserved audience.

### 3. Pizzazz Makes You Memorable

People forget what you say. They remember how you made them feel. Bring energy. Bring presence.

### 4. Create Your Own IP

A framework, a phrase, a method — your IP makes you referable. When people remember your concept, they remember you.

### 5. Words Are Your Differentiator

Craft your messaging. Use short, surprising, specific language. Every word counts.

### 6. Test and Refine

Show your messaging to three people. If they do not react, rework it. Feedback is essential.

### 7. POP Is a Daily Choice

Every interaction is an opportunity to POP. Not by being loud — by being purposeful, original, and present.

> **Case: The Job Seeker Who POPped** (Chapter 9): A job seeker used POP to stand out. Purpose: to join a company that aligned with his values. Originality: he created a "skills portfolio" instead of a resume. Pizzazz: his enthusiasm was genuine. He got the job.
> **Key takeaway**: POP works in every context — including job hunting. Purpose, originality, and presence are universal.
