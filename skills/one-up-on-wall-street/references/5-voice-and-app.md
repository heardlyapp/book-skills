# Author's Voice + Key Scenarios

> **Case: Lynch's Fidelity Magellan Record** (Introduction): Peter Lynch managed the Fidelity Magellan Fund from 1977 to 1990, achieving an average annual return of 29% — more than doubling the S&P 500. His record is one of the best in investment history.
> **Key takeaway**: Lynch's methods are not theoretical — they produced real, documented results over one of the most successful investing careers on record.

> **Case: Lynch's Retirement** (Epilogue): Lynch retired at 46 to spend more time with his family. He didn't need to keep working. But he wanted to share his investing wisdom with individual investors. His books have helped millions.
> **Key takeaway**: Lynch's motivation was not money but teaching. His desire to help ordinary investors succeed is genuine.

## Key Scenarios

### Scenario 1: First Stock Investment
Start with a company you know. Use the Two-Minute Drill. Check the category. Calculate the PEG.

### Scenario 2: Deciding Whether to Buy More
Re-check the story. Is it still intact? Is the stock cheaper or more expensive relative to earnings?

### Scenario 3: Considering Selling
Run the When to Sell Checklist. If 3+ signals, sell. If 1-2 signals, watch closely.

### Scenario 4: Building a Portfolio
Start with 3-5 stocks in different categories. Add as your conviction grows. 10-15 stocks is max for most individuals.

### Scenario 5: Market Drop
Don't panic. Re-check the stories of your holdings. If they're intact, the lower price is a buying opportunity.
