# Core Framework: Invest in What You Know

## The Central Insight

"Twenty years on Wall Street, I've learned that the best stocks are often found by people who aren't on Wall Street." Peter Lynch's framework is built on the idea that individual investors have a real edge — if they know how to use it.

> **Case: The L'Eggs Pantyhose Story** (Chapter 1): Lynch noticed that his wife brought home L'Eggs pantyhose, sold in a unique egg-shaped container at grocery stores. He researched the company (Hanes) and found a great investment. A consumer product he encountered in daily life led to one of his best investments.
> **Key takeaway**: The best investment ideas are often right in front of you — in the products you buy, the stores you visit, the services you use.

## The Six Stock Categories

| Type | Growth Rate | Key Metrics | What to Watch For |
|------|-------------|-------------|-------------------|
| Slow Grower | 2-4% | Dividend yield | Is the dividend safe? |
| Stalwart | 10-12% | P/E ratio, recent growth | When will the next growth phase come? |
| Fast Grower | 20-25% | PEG ratio, growth sustainability | Can it sustain this growth? |
| Cyclical | Fluctuates | Inventory, supply/demand | Timing the cycle matters |
| Turnaround | Variable | Debt levels, cash position | Is the turnaround real? |
| Asset Play | Variable | Book value, hidden assets | What are the hidden assets worth? |

> **Case: The Dunkin' Donuts Investment** (Chapter 6): Lynch invested in Dunkin' Donuts after noticing that customers were always lined up at his local store. He researched the company, found it had a strong franchise model and expansion potential. The stock became a multi-bagger. His consumer observation was the starting point.
> **Key takeaway**: Lynch's method starts with observation. Notice what's working in the real world. Then do the research to confirm the investment thesis.
