# Practical Methods — Techniques

## Technique 1: Show Up Every Day

The most important technique: establish a regular practice. Make art at the same time, in the same place, every day.

**How to do it:** Pick a time. Pick a place. Show up. Do not wait for inspiration. The practice itself is the point.

**Case: The Morning Pages.** Many artists write or draw first thing in the morning. The daily practice builds momentum.

**Case: The Studio Habit.** An artist who goes to the studio at the same time every day will produce more than one who waits for inspiration.

## Technique 2: Make More, Judge Less

The ceramics experiment principle: quantity over quality in the early stages.

**How to do it:** Do not judge your work while you are making it. Make a lot of work. Judge it later. The judgment comes after the work is done.

**Case: The Writer's 500 Words.** Write 500 words every day. Do not edit while writing. The editing comes later.

**Case: The Painter's Studies.** Painters make studies — quick, imperfect versions of what they want to paint. The studies are not the final work. They are practice.

## Technique 3: Finish Something

The fear of finishing is real. The only cure is to finish anyway.

**How to do it:** Set a deadline. Tell someone. Submit to a show. The external pressure can help overcome the fear.

**Case: The Exhibition Deadline.** An artist who has a show deadline will finish work that would otherwise remain incomplete.

**Case: The Submission Call.** A writer who submits to a magazine will finish the story.
