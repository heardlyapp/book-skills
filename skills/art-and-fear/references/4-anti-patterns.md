# Self-Sabotage — Anti-Patterns

## Anti-Pattern 1: Waiting for Inspiration

The belief that you need to feel inspired before you can work. This is a form of avoidance.

**Case: The Waiting Painter.** A painter who only works when she "feels inspired" will produce very little. The professional painter works regardless of inspiration.

**Case: The Blocked Writer.** Writer's block is often a fear response. The solution is not to wait for inspiration but to write anything — even bad writing.

## Anti-Pattern 2: Comparing Yourself to Others

The belief that your work should be as good as someone else's.

**Case: The Ansel Adams Comparison.** A beginning photographer compares his work to Ansel Adams and feels inadequate. The authors: Adams had decades of practice. The comparison is unfair.

**Case: The Competition Trap.** Entering competitions is useful. But believing that the results reflect your worth as an artist is destructive.

## Anti-Pattern 3: Perfectionism

The belief that your work must be perfect before it can be shown.

**Case: The Unfinished Masterpiece.** An artist works on a single piece for years, never satisfied. The piece is never finished. The perfectionism prevents the work from existing.

**Case: The Revisions That Never End.** A writer revises the same manuscript endlessly. Each revision improves the manuscript — but the manuscript is never published.
