## The Central Problem: Fear Disguised as Something Else

Artists rarely say "I'm afraid to make this painting." They say "I don't have time" or "I'm not good enough" or "I need more materials." The authors: these are fears in disguise.

**Case: The Procrastinating Artist.** An artist who spends more time preparing to make art than making it. The authors: the preparation is a form of avoidance. The fear of making imperfect work prevents the work from being made.

**Case: The Over-Researcher.** A writer who reads endlessly about writing but never writes. The research is a safe substitute for the risk of creating.

## The Solution: Show Up and Do the Work

The authors do not offer a cure for fear. They offer a practice: keep working despite fear.

**Case: The Ceramics Experiment.** The quantity group made more pots — and learned more. The quality group planned one perfect pot and produced nothing. The lesson: the best way to improve is to produce.

**Case: The Daily Practice.** The authors recommend establishing a regular practice. Show up at the same time, in the same place, every day. The work gets done because the practice becomes automatic.
