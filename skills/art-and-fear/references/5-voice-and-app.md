## The Authors' Voice

Bayles and Orland write as if they are talking to you across a table. They use "we" — they share their own struggles and fears. They do not preach. They commiserate.

**Case: The Dedication.** "To all those who have been making art, and to all those who have been meaning to make art." The dedication includes everyone — those who have done the work and those who are afraid to start.

## Why Art & Fear Endures

Art & Fear has remained in print for decades because it speaks to a universal experience: the terror of creating something and putting it into the world. The book does not offer easy answers. It offers companionship. The authors are in the same struggle. They understand.

**Case: The Universal Readership.** Art & Fear is read by painters, writers, musicians, photographers, architects, and anyone who has ever tried to make something. The fears are the same regardless of medium.

**Case: The Practical Advice.** The book is not theoretical. It is practical. The authors suggest specific actions: make more work, finish something, show up every day.
