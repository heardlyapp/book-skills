# Techniques: Leading Transformation

## Technique 1: Read and Learn Widely

Nadella is a voracious reader. He reads literature, poetry, philosophy, and science. He brings insights from outside technology into his leadership. Reading widely provides new frameworks and perspectives.

## Technique 2: Model the Behavior You Want

Leaders must model the behavior they want to see. If you want a learning culture, admit when you do not know something. If you want collaboration, collaborate publicly. Nadella demonstrated vulnerability and curiosity from day one.

> **Case: The Cricket Analogy** (Chapter 5): Nadella uses cricket as a metaphor for leadership. In cricket, you must be patient, strategic, and team-oriented. You cannot win alone. You must trust your teammates. The analogy is deeply personal to Nadella, who grew up playing cricket in India. It illustrates his leadership philosophy: patience, teamwork, and long-term thinking.
> **Key takeaway:** Personal metaphors make leadership authentic. Nadella uses his own experience to communicate his philosophy in a way that resonates.

## Technique 3: Make Strategic Bets

Identify three big bets that will define your organization's future. Invest in them disproportionately. Reorganize around them. Nadella's three bets were cloud, AI, and productivity.

## Technique 4: Break Down Silos

Internal competition destroys value. Nadella broke down Microsoft's silos. He merged divisions. He shared resources. He rewarded collaboration over internal competition. The one Microsoft approach was radical for a company that had celebrated internal competition.

## Technique 5: Embrace Paradox

Nadella holds contradictions: empathy and competition, learning and execution, purpose and profit. He refuses false choices. The best leaders hold tensions without resolving them.

> **Case: The HoloLens Bet** (Chapter 6): Microsoft invested in mixed reality through HoloLens. It was a bet on a technology that might not succeed for years. But the investment signaled that Microsoft was willing to think long-term. HoloLens became a leader in enterprise mixed reality. The bet showed that Microsoft could innovate beyond its core businesses. It was a symbol of the new Microsoft.
> **Key takeaway:** Long-term bets require patience and conviction. Not every bet succeeds, but the willingness to make bold bets signals a culture of innovation.
Nadella also introduced a new approach to product development: the garage model. Small teams with autonomy to experiment and fail. The garage teams were protected from the bureaucracy that had stifled innovation. They could move fast, take risks, and learn. Not all garage projects succeeded. But those that did — including parts of Azure and AI — became the foundation of Microsoft future growth.
The learn-it-all culture also changed how Microsoft hired. The company started looking for candidates who demonstrated curiosity and learning ability, not just technical expertise. It valued diversity of background and perspective. It hired for potential, not just past achievements. This hiring shift was essential for building a culture that could adapt to a rapidly changing industry.
Partnerships also extended to the open source community. Microsoft embraced open source, contributing to Linux, creating Visual Studio Code, and acquiring GitHub. This was a radical shift for a company that had famously opposed open source. The embrace of open source signaled that Microsoft was serious about partnership and learning.
