## 7 Principles of Evidence-Based Development

### 1. Test Everything

Assumptions about what works in poverty are often wrong. RCTs provide real evidence. Use them.

### 2. The Poor Are Rational

Poor people make the same kinds of decisions as everyone else — they just face tighter constraints. Behavior that looks irrational from the outside is often rational given the constraints.

> **Case: The Fertilizer Study** (Chapter 6): Farmers in Kenya knew fertilizer increased yields. Most did not use it. The reason: the decision was made at the wrong time — when money was short. A small intervention (delivering fertilizer at harvest time, when farmers had money) doubled usage.
> **Key takeaway**: The problem was not knowledge — it was timing. Small changes in when and how decisions are made can have large effects.

### 3. Free Works Best

Charging for essential goods (bed nets, deworming, vaccines) reduces uptake without increasing use. Free distribution is the most effective.

### 4. Simple Is Powerful

The most effective interventions are often the simplest: deworming, iodized salt, chlorine at water sources, iron supplements. Expensive, complex programs are not always better.

### 5. Quality Matters

Getting children into school is not enough. They must learn. Teacher attendance, curriculum quality, and classroom materials matter more than enrollment numbers.

### 6. Information Is Not Enough

Telling people that vaccines are safe or bed nets prevent malaria does not change behavior. Removing barriers (cost, distance, time) changes behavior.

> **Case: The Immunization Study** (Chapter 4): In Rajasthan, India, researchers tested two ways to increase childhood immunization: health camps with reliable service (addressing supply) and small incentives (lentils, plates). Both worked. Information campaigns did not.
> **Key takeaway**: People know vaccines work. The problem is not information — it is access. Fix the supply, and demand follows.

### 7. Corruption Is a Tax on the Poor

When officials steal resources or demand bribes, the poor pay the highest price. Fighting corruption is a development priority.
