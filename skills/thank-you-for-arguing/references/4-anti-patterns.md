## 6 Anti-Patterns of Bad Arguing

### 1. "Logic Alone Will Persuade"

Logic does not persuade. Emotion persuades. Logic supports. If you lead with logic and ignore emotion, you lose.

### 2. "Never Concede"

People who never concede look unreasonable. Strategic concession makes you look fair — and opens the door for your argument.

### 3. "Winning the Argument Means Defeating the Opponent"

The opponent is not the target. The audience is. If you defeat the opponent but lose the audience, you have not won.

> **Case: The Online Troll** (Observation): A person wins every factual point in an online argument but comes across as aggressive and dismissive. They defeat the opponent — and lose every reader.
> **Key takeaway**: How you argue matters more than what you argue. Style persuades. Attitude persuades. Facts alone do not.

### 4. "I Know What I Want to Say"

Rhetoric is not about what you want to say — it is about what the audience needs to hear. Audience analysis first.

### 5. "The Best Argument Is the Most Complex"

The best argument is the simplest. Complexity confuses. Clarity persuades.

### 6. "Rhetoric Is Manipulation"

Rhetoric is a tool. It can be used ethically or unethically. The fault is not in the tool — it is in the user.

> **Case: The Parent-Teen Argument** (Chapter 4): A parent says "Because I said so." That is an argument from authority — and it usually fails. A better approach: "Here is why I think this is important." That is ethos + logos. The teenager may still disagree — but you have a conversation, not a confrontation.
> **Key takeaway**: Authority without explanation breeds resistance. Even when you have the power, use persuasion.
