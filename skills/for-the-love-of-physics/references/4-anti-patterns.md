## 6 Anti-Patterns of Physics Learning

### 1. "Physics Is Equations"

Physics is not equations. Physics is understanding. The equations are the language, not the subject.

### 2. "Physics Is for Geniuses"

Lewin proved that anyone can learn physics. His MIT lectures were accessible to everyone. The only prerequisite is curiosity.

### 3. "Math First, Understanding Later"

Lewin did it backwards: wonder first, explanation second, equations last. The math is most meaningful when you already understand the concept.

> **Case: The Falling Apple** (Chapter 2): Lewin does not start with F = ma. He starts with an apple falling from a tree. Why does it fall? Why does it accelerate? Only after you understand the phenomenon does he introduce the equation.
> **Key takeaway**: Concepts come first. Equations are the summary of understanding, not the path to it.

### 4. "Physics Is Boring"

If physics is boring, you have not seen a good demonstration. Lewin's lectures were anything but boring.

### 5. "You Need Expensive Equipment"

A pendulum, a key, a string, and a bathtub are enough to explore the laws of physics.

### 6. "Understanding Kills Wonder"

The opposite is true. Understanding the rainbow makes it more beautiful. Understanding the pendulum makes it more fascinating. Knowledge enhances wonder.

> **Case: The Rocket Launch** (Chapter 12): Lewin describes watching a rocket launch. A non-physicist sees a beautiful fireball. Lewin sees Newton's third law in action — the exhaust pushes down, the rocket goes up. Both are in awe. The physicist's awe is deeper.
> **Key takeaway**: Physics adds a layer of meaning to every experience. Understanding does not diminish wonder — it deepens it.
