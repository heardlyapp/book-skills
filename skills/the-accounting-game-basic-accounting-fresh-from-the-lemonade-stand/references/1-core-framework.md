# Core Framework: The Balance Sheet

> Source: The Accounting Game, Chapter 1

## The Fundamental Equation

Assets = Liabilities + Owner's Equity. This equation must always balance. Every transaction affects at least two accounts. The book teaches this through the lemonade stand: you start with $5 cash (asset) and $5 owner's equity. Buy $3 of lemons: cash goes down $3, inventory goes up $3. Total assets unchanged. Still balanced.

> **Case: The Lemonade Stand Start** (Chapter 1): You empty your piggy bank: $5 in cash. That's your asset. Your parents gave you that money? No — you saved it. So it's your investment. Cash $5, Owner's Equity $5. Balance sheet balanced.

> **Case: Borrowing from Dad** (Chapter 2): You need a bigger sign but don't have cash. Dad loans you $10. Cash goes up $10, Notes Payable (liability) increases $10. Assets increased by the same amount as liabilities. Still balanced.

## The Three Statements

Balance Sheet (snapshot at one moment), Income Statement (profit over time), Cash Flow Statement (where cash actually moved). They interconnect: net profit flows into retained earnings on the balance sheet.

> **Key takeaway**: "The Balance Sheet always balances. If it doesn't, you've made a mistake."

## Key Terms

- **Cash**: Money in the cigar box (or bank account)
- **Inventory**: Lemons, sugar, cups you bought to make/sell
- **Assets**: Things you own that have value
- **Liabilities**: Money you owe others
- **Owner's Equity**: Your investment + accumulated profits
- **Retained Earnings**: Profits kept in the business (not paid to owner)
