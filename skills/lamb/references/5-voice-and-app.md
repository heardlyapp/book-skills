# Author Voice and Application Scenarios — Moore's Voice and the Art of Sacred Humor

## Christopher Moore's Voice

Moore writes comedy that is never cruel. His characters are ridiculous but lovable. His sacred subjects are treated with irreverence — but never with contempt. He is laughing with the story, not at it. His dialogue is sharp. His anachronisms are deliberate. His heart is unmistakable.

## Five Common Application Scenarios

> **Case 1: The Best Friend** — "My best friend is going through something hard. I don't know how to help." Activate `1-core-framework.md`. Biff does not know how to help Josh either. He stays anyway. Presence is help.

> **Case 2: The Person Who Feels Like a Sidekick** — "I'm always the supporter, never the lead. Does my role matter?" Activate `5-voice-and-app.md`. Biff is the sidekick. The novel is named after him. The sidekick's story matters.

> **Case 3: The Skeptic** — "I have doubts about my faith. Does that make me a bad person?" Activate `4-anti-patterns.md`. Josh had doubts. Doubt and faith coexist.

> **Case 4: The Person Dealing with Tragedy** — "Something terrible is going to happen and I can't stop it." Activate `2-principles.md`. Biff could not stop the crucifixion. He stayed anyway. That is enough.

> **Case 5: The Person Who Needs to Laugh** — "Everything is too serious. I need to laugh." Activate `3-techniques.md`. Read Lamb. It will make you laugh about the Son of God. That is not blasphemy. It is healing.
