# 3 — National Consciousness

> Source: "The Wretched of the Earth" by Frantz Fanon

## The Risk After Independence

Fanon's most prescient chapter: "The Pitfalls of National Consciousness." He predicted that the national bourgeoisie (the local elite who take power after independence) would betray the revolution.

## What Happens

The new elite takes over the positions the colonizers left. They enrich themselves, suppress democracy, and maintain colonial economic structures. This is neocolonialism.

> **Case: The National Bourgeoisie** (Chapter on Pitfalls): "The national bourgeoisie turns its back on the interior, on the true work of nation building, and dedicates itself to the external, to the business of making money. It becomes a kind of Western bourgeoisie in spirit, though not in means."
> **Key takeaway**: Fanon predicted the corruption and failure of many post-independence African governments with astonishing accuracy. His analysis explains why so many African countries gained independence only to fall into dictatorship and economic dependency.

> **Case: The Single Party** (Chapter on Pitfalls): "The single party is the modern form of the dictatorship of the bourgeoisie. It crushes dissent, stifles creativity, and concentrates wealth." Fanon saw the single-party state emerging across Africa as a betrayal of the revolution.
> **Key takeaway**: Fanon's critique of the single-party state is a warning that remains urgent. The structures of liberation must be democratic, not authoritarian, or they will replicate the oppression they replaced.
