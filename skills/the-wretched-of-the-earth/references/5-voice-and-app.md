# 5 — Fanon's Voice + Application

> Source: "The Wretched of the Earth" by Frantz Fanon

## Fanon's Voice

Frantz Fanon (1925-1961) was a psychiatrist, philosopher, and revolutionary from Martinique. His voice: clinical, passionate, prophetic. He wrote "The Wretched of the Earth" while dying of leukemia, completing it in 1961. He died shortly after.

> **Case: The Dying Prophet** (Preface): Fanon wrote the book in the last year of his life, knowing he was dying. "I am speaking of this world with the voice of a man who is about to die." The urgency and passion of the book reflect this. He was racing against death to finish his message.
> **Key takeaway**: Fanon wrote with the intensity of someone with nothing to lose. The book is not an academic exercise — it's the testament of a dying revolutionary.

## Key Quote

"Each generation must discover its mission, fulfill it or betray it, in relative opacity."

## 5 Application Scenarios

1. **Studying colonialism** → Fanon's framework is essential for understanding the psychological effects of colonialism.
2. **Analyzing postcolonial states** → His critique of the national bourgeoisie explains many of the failures of post-independence governments.
3. **Understanding contemporary movements** → Black Lives Matter, Palestinian liberation, and other movements continue Fanon's analysis.
4. **Examining cultural identity** → His three-stage model of the colonized intellectual helps understand cultural alienation and reclamation.
5. **Thinking about revolutionary change** → Fanon's argument about violence remains controversial but essential to understand.
