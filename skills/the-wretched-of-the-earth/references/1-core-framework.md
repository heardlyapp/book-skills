# 1 — The Manichean World

> Source: "The Wretched of the Earth" by Frantz Fanon

## The Divided World

Colonial society is a Manichean world — divided into two opposing zones: the colonizer's city (clean, orderly, prosperous) and the colonized's shantytown (crowded, dirty, brutalized).

## The Colonizer's Logic

The colonizer must dehumanize the colonized to justify exploitation. The colonized are portrayed as lazy, primitive, childlike. This projection justifies their subjugation.

> **Case: The Settler's City** (Chapter On Violence): Fanon describes the physical division of colonial cities. "The colonizer's sector is a well-fed, bright, and sturdy town. The colonized's sector is a hungry, dark, and wretched town. Between them, no concourse. They face each other across a divide created by ruthless force."
> **Key takeaway**: The spatial division of colonial cities reflects the psychological division of colonial society. The two worlds are not just separate — they are opposed. One exists by dominating the other.

> **Case: The Colonized in the Colonizer's Language** (Chapter on Culture): Fanon describes the colonized intellectual who speaks the colonizer's language perfectly and is accepted — but never fully. "The colonized who proves himself worthy is treated as an exception. He is not a 'true native.' He has been 'assimilated.' But even he will never be fully accepted."
> **Key takeaway**: Assimilation is a trap. No matter how perfectly the colonized adopts the colonizer's culture, they will always be seen as the Other. Full acceptance is impossible because the system requires their inferiority.
