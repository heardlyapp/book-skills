# 4 — Cultural Alienation

> Source: "The Wretched of the Earth" by Frantz Fanon

## The Colonized Intellectual

Fanon identifies three stages in the colonized intellectual's journey:
1. **Assimilation**: Trying to prove they are as good as the colonizer
2. **Reconnection**: Rediscovering and romanticizing pre-colonial culture
3. **Fighting**: Creating a revolutionary national culture

## The Trap

Many colonized intellectuals get stuck in stage 2 — romanticizing the past instead of fighting for the future.

> **Case: The Return to Tradition** (Chapter on National Culture): "The native intellectual who returns to his people through the arts behaves like a foreigner. He speaks of the past in abstract, idealized terms. He has not grasped the present struggle." Fanon criticizes intellectuals who romanticize pre-colonial culture instead of engaging with the living reality of the people's fight.
> **Key takeaway**: Reclaiming culture is necessary — but it must not be an escape from the present struggle. The past is a source of strength, not a refuge. Revolutionary culture is created in the act of fighting, not in the museum.

> **Case: The Westernized Elite** (Chapter on Culture): "The native intellectual has been steeped in Western culture. He often despises his own people. He must learn to re-immerse himself in the life of the nation, not as a tourist, but as a participant in the struggle."
> **Key takeaway**: The colonized intellectual's alienation from their own people is a form of psychological colonization. Liberation requires not just political independence but cultural reconnection.
