# Core Framework — The Hundred Years' War on Palestine

## Introduction: Yusuf Diya's Letter

In 1899, Yusuf Diya al-Khalidi, former mayor of Jerusalem and Ottoman deputy, wrote to the French chief rabbi to pass to Theodor Herzl. He understood Zionism's appeal — Jews were being persecuted in Europe, and a refuge was "natural, beautiful and just." But he warned: "Palestine is an integral part of the Ottoman Empire, and more gravely, it is inhabited by others." The indigenous population would never accept being supplanted. "In the name of God, let Palestine be left alone."

Herzl's reply ignored Yusuf Diya's central point. He argued that Jewish immigration would benefit the indigenous people. The most revealing line: "Who would think of sending them away?" — referencing his own diary entry about "spiriting the penniless population across the border."

Khalidi's thesis: "The modern history of Palestine can best be understood as a colonial war waged against the indigenous population, by a variety of parties, to force them to relinquish their homeland to another people against their will."

## Declaration 1: The Balfour Declaration (1917)

Britain promised a "national home for the Jewish people" in Palestine — while Palestinians constituted 90% of the population. The declaration never mentioned Palestinians. "A land without a people for a people without a land" was the slogan used by Christian Zionists. Arthur Balfour himself, when asked about the 600,000+ Arabs living there, dismissed them as "the non-Jewish communities in Palestine."

**The 1936-39 Arab Revolt:** Palestinians rebelled against British rule and Zionist colonization. Britain crushed the revolt with 100,000 troops and air power. 10% of the adult male Palestinian population was killed, wounded, imprisoned, or exiled. Simultaneously, Jewish immigration surged from 18% of the population in 1932 to 31% in 1939, driven by Nazi persecution.

**Ze'ev Jabotinsky's "Iron Wall":** The Revisionist Zionist leader explicitly acknowledged the colonial nature of the project: "Every native population in the world resists colonists as long as it has the slightest hope of being able to rid itself of the danger of being colonised. Zionist colonisation can proceed only under the protection of a power that is independent of the native population — behind an iron wall, which the native population cannot breach."

## Declaration 2: The Nakba (1948)

The ethnic cleansing of over half the Arab population of Palestine — some 750,000 people — by Zionist militias and then the Israeli army. Khalidi's family experienced it firsthand: his grandfather's library, the Khalidi Library in Jerusalem, survived only because it was a religious endowment.

**Plan Dalet:** The Zionist military plan for the conquest and depopulation of Palestinian areas. Towns like Lydda and Ramle were emptied at gunpoint. Villages were destroyed. "The expulsion of over half the Arab population of the country completed the military and political triumph of Zionism."

**The Palestinian narrative:** "Nakba" means "catastrophe." For Israelis, the same events are celebrated as the War of Independence. The two narratives are fundamentally irreconcilable.

## Declaration 3: The Six-Day War (1967)

Israel captured the West Bank, Gaza Strip, East Jerusalem, the Golan Heights, and the Sinai. The occupation of the West Bank and Gaza — now 50+ years — is the longest military occupation in modern history. Settlement construction began immediately, violating the Fourth Geneva Convention.

## Declaration 4: The Lebanon War (1982)

Israel invaded Lebanon to destroy the PLO. The siege of Beirut lasted two months. The Sabra and Shatila massacre — where Israeli-allied Christian militias killed hundreds of Palestinian civilians while the Israeli army surrounded the camps — became a symbol of the conflict's brutality.

## Declaration 5: The First Intifada and Oslo (1987-1993)

**The Intifada:** A spontaneous Palestinian uprising against the occupation — stone-throwing youth vs. the Israeli military. It forced Israel to recognize that the occupation was unsustainable.

**Oslo:** The 1993 Oslo Accords were sold as a peace agreement. Khalidi argues they were "a capitulation" — the PLO recognized Israel and got nothing concrete in return. No settlement freeze. No right of return. No sovereignty. The Palestinian Authority became "the subcontractor of the occupation."

## Declaration 6: Gaza and Jerusalem (2007-2017)

**Gaza:** After Hamas won the 2006 elections, Israel and Egypt imposed a siege on Gaza. Three wars (2008-09, 2012, 2014) devastated the Strip. "The siege of Gaza and the intermittent wars on its population became the defining feature of the conflict in the 21st century."

**Jerusalem:** In 2017, President Trump recognized Jerusalem as Israel's capital and moved the US embassy there. "This was the final nail in the coffin of the two-state solution."
