# Key Principles — The Hundred Years' War on Palestine

## I. The Conflict Is a Settler Colonial War

Khalidi's central thesis: "This conflict must be seen quite differently from most of the prevailing views of it." The Zionist project was a classic European settler colonial movement — like the colonization of North America, Australia, Algeria, and South Africa. It involved the displacement of an indigenous population by European settlers, with the support of great imperial powers. Jabotinsky himself acknowledged this: "Every native population in the world resists colonists."

## II. External Powers Made It Possible

Zionism succeeded because of Britain (1917-1948) and then the United States (1967-present). The Balfour Declaration was issued by a British cabinet. Britain crushed the 1936-39 Arab Revolt with overwhelming force. After 1967, the US provided diplomatic cover, military aid, and veto power at the UN. "This war could not have been waged without the support of the great imperial powers."

## III. The Indigenous Population Was Dismissed

Herzl's letter to Yusuf Diya ignored the central fact — Palestine was inhabited. The Balfour Declaration never mentioned Palestinians. The slogan "a land without a people" was a convenient fiction. "For over a century, the Palestinians have been depicted in precisely the same language by their colonizers as have other indigenous peoples."

## IV. The Nakba Was Not an Accident

The expulsion of 750,000 Palestinians in 1948 was not a spontaneous byproduct of war — it was planned and executed as part of a colonial project. Plan Dalet was a military blueprint. "The expulsion of over half the Arab population of the country completed the military and political triumph of Zionism."

## V. The Occupation Is Not Temporary

It has lasted over 50 years — the longest military occupation in modern history. Settlement construction violates the Fourth Geneva Convention. "There is no escaping the fact that this century-long colonial war is still ongoing."

## VI. Oslo Was a Capitulation, Not a Peace

The 1993 Oslo Accords recognized Israel without securing Palestinian statehood. Settlements continued. The Palestinian Authority became a subcontractor of the occupation. "The negotiations after 1993 were conducted on the basis of the complete subordination of the weaker party."

## VII. Palestinian Resilience Is the Counterweight

Khalidi acknowledges Palestinian mistakes, divisions, and poor leadership. But "we cannot overlook the formidable international and imperial forces arrayed against them." The book is dedicated to his grandchildren — "who will hopefully see the end of this hundred years' war." Despite everything, Palestinians have displayed remarkable resilience.
