# Anti-Patterns — The Hundred Years' War on Palestine

## The Central Error: Seeing the Conflict as a Symmetrical National Struggle

"Although this war shares many of the typical characteristics of other colonial campaigns, it has been portrayed as a straightforward national clash between two peoples with rights in the same land." Khalidi argues this framing obscures the fundamentally asymmetrical, colonial nature of the conflict.

### Mistake 1: "The conflict started in 1967"
1967 is important (occupation, settlements), but the war started in 1917 (Balfour) or arguably in 1897 (first Zionist congress). "The hundred years' war on Palestine began long before the occupation."

### Mistake 2: "A land without a people"
The slogan was used by Christian and early Zionist supporters. But by 1917, Palestine had over 600,000 Arab inhabitants — 90% of the population. By 1947, the Arab population was 1.2 million. "Palestine was terra nullius to those who came to settle it."

### Mistake 3: "Israel is the only democracy in the Middle East"
Israel is a democracy for its Jewish citizens. For Palestinians in the occupied territories, it is a military occupation. For Palestinian citizens of Israel, it is a state that defines itself as ethnically Jewish. "You can't say you're the only democracy in the Middle East when you're ruling over millions of people who have no rights."

### Mistake 4: "Oslo was a peace process"
Oslo created the Palestinian Authority as a subcontractor of the occupation. It did not stop settlements — settlements doubled between 1993 and 2000. Khalidi: "The negotiations were conducted on the basis of the complete subordination of the weaker party."

### Mistake 5: "Palestinians never miss an opportunity to miss an opportunity"
This phrase (attributed to Abba Eban) blames Palestinians for their own dispossession. Khalidi: "Palestinians have been accused by those who sympathize with their oppressors of wallowing in their own victimization. We cannot overlook the formidable international and imperial forces arrayed against them."

### Mistake 6: "The conflict is about religion"
"Palestinian nationalism has always been largely secular." The conflict is fundamentally about land, sovereignty, and rights — not religion.

### Mistake 7: "The two-state solution is the only possible outcome"
Khalidi suggests that continued settlement construction has made a viable Palestinian state impossible. "The two-state solution is dead, killed by fifty years of occupation and settlement." One state, binational or otherwise, is the only remaining framework.
