# Key Principles — Rising Strong

## I. Falling Is Inevitable

"If we're going to put ourselves out there and love with our whole hearts, we're going to experience heartbreak. If we're going to try new things, we're going to fail." The question is not "if" you'll fall — it's "what happens when you're facedown in the arena?" Case: Brené's interview with the reporter — "You're going to stumble, fall, and get your ass kicked."

## II. The Reckoning: Get Curious Before You Get Fierce

"The reckoning is the moment you become aware of what you're feeling." Instead of acting out the emotion, get curious about it. "What's happening in my body? What story am I making up?" Case: Brené in a meeting — a comment hits her, she feels defensive, but instead of reacting, she pauses and gets curious.

## III. The Rumble: Separate Fact from Fiction

"We are meaning-making machines. When something happens, we immediately create a story." The rumble is distinguishing what actually happened from the story you're telling about it. "The story I'm making up is..." Case: The colleague conflict — "I thought I was being disrespected. I was actually afraid I wasn't good enough."

## IV. Don't Gold-Plate Grit

"We like recovery stories to move quickly through the dark." This is dangerous. "Failing is painful. It fuels the 'shouldas and couldas,' which means judgment and shame are often lying in wait." Case: EWB's failure report — 14 stories of failure published openly, showing that real resilience requires honest emotion.

## V. Badassery Is Vulnerability, Not Swagger

"Swagger is not badassery. Perfection is about the furthest thing from badassery." Real courage is being honest about pain. Case: "The man who tells his son, 'It's okay to be sad. We all get sad.'"

## VI. Failure Is Compost

"Failure isn't something to be avoided or sanitized. It's raw material. You have to let it decompose and turn into something fertile." There's no shortcut. The decomposition process (feeling the hurt) is essential. Case: "The most useful knowledge about human behavior is based on people's lived experiences."

## VII. Own Your Story or Be Trapped in It

"When we own our stories, we avoid being trapped as characters in stories someone else is telling." The revolution is writing your own ending — actively choosing how to move forward rather than being defined by the fall. Case: "Owning our story and loving ourselves through that process is the bravest thing we'll ever do."
