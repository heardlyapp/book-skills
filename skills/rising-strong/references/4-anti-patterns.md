# Anti-Patterns — Rising Strong

## The Central Error: "I Can Skip the Hard Part"

"We like recovery stories to move quickly through the dark so we can get to the sweeping redemptive ending." This is gold-plating grit — trying to bypass the painful emotions. Case: Failure conferences that celebrate failure without acknowledging the real hurt. "To strip failure of its real emotional consequences is to scrub grit of everything that makes it important."

### Mistake 1: Immediate Problem-Solving
"Don't be sad — let's fix it." When someone is in pain, our first impulse is to solve the problem. But the rising strong process requires feeling first, fixing second. Case: "We're facedown in the arena. Before hearing anything about triumph or achievement, we need to figure out what happens next."

### Mistake 2: Emotional Stoicism as Strength
"I'm fine. It doesn't bother me." Emotional stoicism is not resilience — it's avoidance. Real badasses feel their feelings. Case: "Swagger is not badassery. Emotional stoicism is not badassery."

### Mistake 3: Acting Out Instead of Feeling
"Instead of feeling hurt, they're acting out their hurt. Instead of acknowledging pain, they're inflicting pain on others." This is the badassery deficit in action. Case: "Rather than risking feeling disappointed, they're choosing to live disappointed."

### Mistake 4: "Everything Happens for a Reason"
Polishing failure with platitudes prevents the real work of rumbling. If you jump to "it was meant to be," you skip the necessary pain. Case: "The compost needs to decompose before it becomes fertile. You can't skip the decomposition."

### Mistake 5: Confabulation — Believing Your Own Story
"The stories we make up feel true. That doesn't make them true." Humans are prone to confabulation — creating narratives that fit our pre-existing beliefs. Case: Brené's story about being disrespected by a colleague — she fully believed it until she rumbled.

### Mistake 6: Isolation in the Arena
"When we're facedown, we often isolate." The impulse is to hide. But rising strong requires connection. Case: "People who are brave enough to share their failures for the purpose of learning and growing are helping, generous, open, knowledgeable, brave, and courageous."
