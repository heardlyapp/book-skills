# Techniques — Rising Strong

## The Rising Strong Process in Practice

### Technique 1: The Reckoning — Body Scan
When you feel emotionally hooked, pause and scan your body. "What am I feeling? Where is it in my body? Tight jaw? Racing heart? Knot in my stomach?" Name the emotion. "I'm feeling shame. I'm feeling anger." Case: Brené in the meeting — the physical sensation came before the cognitive understanding.

### Technique 2: The Rumble Starter — "The Story I'm Making Up Is..."
This is Brown's signature phrase. When you feel hurt or defensive, say out loud: "The story I'm making up is..." This immediately shifts from certainty to curiosity. Case: "The story I'm making up is that she thinks I'm incompetent." Then ask: "What do I actually know to be true?"

### Technique 3: The Delta Check
After naming the story, check it against the facts. What actually happened? What did they say? What did I say? "Separating data from interpretation is the work of the rumble." Case: Brené's colleague conflict — the actual words exchanged were professional, but her interpretation was personal.

### Technique 4: The Compost Question
Instead of trying to rush past a failure, ask: "What can I learn from this that I couldn't learn any other way? What is this failure teaching me?" Case: "Failure isn't something to be avoided. It's raw material that needs to decompose."

### Technique 5: The Arena Check
When in a conflict or disappointment, ask: "Am I in the arena or on the sidelines?" If you're truly in the arena (risking, caring, showing up), then falling is part of the deal. Case: "Arena is any moment where we have risked showing up and being seen."

### Technique 6: Gold-Plating Detection
When you catch yourself or others polishing a failure story ("It was meant to be," "Everything happens for a reason"), pause. "Am I gold-plating grit? Am I skipping over the real emotion?" Case: "We like recovery stories to move quickly through the dark to get to the redemptive ending. This is dangerous."

### Technique 7: The Revolution Question
After reckoning and rumbling, ask: "What am I going to do differently? How is this going to change how I live, love, or lead?" This is the action step — "writing your own brave ending." Case: "The revolution is turning the learning into real, concrete change in how you show up."
