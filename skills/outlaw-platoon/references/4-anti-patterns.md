# Anti-Patterns — Outlaw Platoon

## The Central Error: Believing Combat Makes You Inhuman

"I could feel myself becoming hard. Was this the kind of man I was destined to become?" The Outlaws' greatest struggle was staying human while surviving war. It's possible — but it takes deliberate effort.

### Mistake 1: "Combat is glamorous"
Parnell describes war as confusion, fear, boredom, and terror punctuated by moments of chaos. "Hollywood got it wrong." The blood on his uniform was not cinematic. The little girl's leg was not special effects.

### Mistake 2: "A good leader doesn't feel fear"
Parnell was terrified constantly. "Combat leaders do not show weakness. They set the example." The difference is not whether you feel fear — it's whether you act despite it. "I was screaming inside. But I didn't let them see it."

### Mistake 3: "The enemy is evil"
Parnell respected the enemy's skill. "They are cunning, well led, and well equipped. Do not underestimate them. You won't be fighting goat herders. You're going to be up against their first team." Dehumanizing the enemy leads to tactical mistakes.

### Mistake 4: "I can do it alone"
Parnell could not have survived without Sabatke, Greeson, Baldwin, Taylor. "No one leads in combat alone. You lead with your men." The Outlaws were a team, not a one-man show.

### Mistake 5: "Home will be the same"
Coming home was harder than going to war. "For sixteen months, I had lived with these men. Now I was expected to just — stop." The transition from combat to civilian life is a battle fought alone, without weapons.

### Mistake 6: "More medals = better leader"
The Outlaws were highly decorated — but Parnell does not see this as proof of leadership. "The medals belong to the men. I just happened to be the one writing the citations."

### Mistake 7: "The war ends when you leave"
Parnell's war followed him home. The dreams. The hypervigilance. The emotional distance from people who hadn't been there. The little girl's eyes. "The war does not end when you board the plane. It lives inside you."
