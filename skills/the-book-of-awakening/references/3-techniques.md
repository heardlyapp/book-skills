# Techniques — The Book of Awakening

## How to Practice Presence (Based on Mark Nepo)

### Technique 1: The Sunrise Juice Ritual
Take one ordinary act — drinking juice, making tea, washing dishes — and do it with full presence. Wait for the light to catch it. "The light from the sun pierced the juice and diffused into orange, crystal light." Case: Nepo's morning ritual — squeezing juice, placing it on the table, waiting for the sun, then drinking. A sacrament of presence.

### Technique 2: The Daily Entry
Read one entry per day — no more, no less. Let it sit with you. "This book is meant to be of use, to be a companion, a soul friend." Case: The book is structured for daily use, not binge reading.

### Technique 3: The Broken Stone Collection
"Gathering the insights for this book has been like finding bits of stone that glistened on the path." Collect glistening moments throughout your day — jot them down, reflect on them later. Case: Nepo spent two years collecting "bits of stone" that became the book's entries.

### Technique 4: The Cancer Eye
"Brings with him the eyes of a dying person who is grateful simply to breathe." Practice seeing life as if you've just survived something. What would you notice if today were your last? Case: Nepo's cancer gave him this perspective — you can practice it without having cancer.

### Technique 5: The Silence After Reading
After reading the daily entry, sit in silence for one minute. Let the words settle. "The soul drinks from the same lake at center." Case: The book is designed for reflection, not just consumption.

### Technique 6: The Gift Mindset
Everything is a gift. "Like most gifts, it is the passing of something meaningful between people that awakens us to our potential." Approach each interaction as a gift exchange. Case: "More than the pages that follow, it is the living center that each page points to that I continue to be amazed by."

### Technique 7: The Glistening Stone
When something catches your attention — a sight, a sound, a feeling — pause. What makes this stone glisten? What is it teaching you? Case: "The bits that have glistened along the way are what make up this book."
