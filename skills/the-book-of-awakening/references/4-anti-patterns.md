# Anti-Patterns — The Book of Awakening

## The Central Error: "I Need More to Be Happy"

"Having the life you want by being present to the life you have." The central error is believing you need to acquire more, achieve more, or become more to be happy. The truth: you already have everything you need — you just need to wake up to it. Case: "Most sacraments are acts of breathtaking simplicity: a simple prayer, a sip of wine, a single breath."

### Mistake 1: Binge Reading
"I'll read this whole book this weekend." The book is designed for daily use, one entry per day. Reading too quickly skips the reflection that makes it work. Case: "A daybook has become inner food" — it needs to be digested one day at a time.

### Mistake 2: Waiting for Big Moments
"I'll be grateful when something big happens." Gratitude is a practice for ordinary moments. The big moments take care of themselves. Case: A glass of juice catching the morning sun.

### Mistake 3: Avoiding Pain
"This suffering is a problem to be solved." Nepo's cancer taught him that suffering, when faced, opens the heart. Case: "His descent into illness gave birth to an astonishing mindfulness."

### Mistake 4: Seeking Outside
"I need this book/teacher/practice to wake me up." The awakening is within. The book is just a pointer. Case: "More than the pages that follow, it is the living center that each page points to."

### Mistake 5: Rushing Through Life
"I'll slow down later." There is no later. Presence is available now or not at all. Case: Nepo waiting for the sun to hit his juice — a full minute that felt like a lifetime.

### Mistake 6: Forgetting Connection
"My awakening is personal." No — "The soul drinks from the same lake at center. When one of us bows toward that lake, the center is opened for us all."
