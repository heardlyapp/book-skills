# Key Principles — The Book of Awakening

## I. Presence Is the Path

"Having the life you want by being present to the life you have." You don't need more — you need to wake up to what's already here. The book is a training in presence. Case: Nepo waiting for the sun to pierce his orange juice — a full minute of presence before drinking.

## II. Suffering Is a Teacher

Cancer shook Nepo awake. "Mark had cancer, and it shook him awake. His descent into illness gave birth to an astonishing mindfulness." Suffering, when faced directly, opens the heart. Case: "Freshly on the other side of cancer, I was gentle and raw and eager to bottle light."

## III. The Ordinary Is Sacred

"An alchemist of the ordinary, he invites us to see, taste, touch, dance, and feel our way into the heart of life." The miraculous is hidden in plain sight. Case: A glass of juice, a stone on the path, a shared laugh — each contains the whole of life.

## IV. Gratitude Is a Way of Seeing

Gratitude isn't a response to receiving — it's a lens for perceiving. When you see life as gift, everything changes. Case: "The light from the sun pierced the juice and diffused into orange, crystal light."

## V. Life Is Made of Moments

"Just as a life is made of days, so are days made of moments." The quality of your life is the quality of your attention to each moment. Case: "A life well lived is firmly planted in the sweet soil of moments."

## VI. We Are All Connected

"The soul drinks from the same lake at center. Somehow when one of us bows our head toward that lake, the center is opened for us all." Your awakening helps everyone. Case: Passages in the book speak to readers as if Nepo knew them personally — because the soul is shared.

## VII. Stay Awake

The title is a command: stay awake. Awakening is not a one-time event — it's a daily practice. "Staying Awake" is the title of one of Nepo's recordings. Case: "The journey of unearthing and shaping these entries has helped me bring my inner and outer life more closely together."
