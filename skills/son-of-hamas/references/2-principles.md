# Key Principles — Son of Hamas

## I. The Conflict Is Not Simple

"Few Westerners can come close to understanding the complexities of the Middle East." The root of the conflict goes back to the animosity between Sarah and Hagar in Genesis, not just to 1948 or 1967. Case: "To understand the political realities, you don't have to look much further than the aftermath of World War I."

## II. Violence Begets Violence

The cycle of attack and retaliation is self-perpetuating. Hamas attacks, Israel responds, more Palestinians radicalize. "Every drop of innocent blood that has been saved gives me hope to carry on." Case: The Intifada, the suicide bombings, the military incursions — no side is without blood on its hands.

## III. Inside Hamas, There Is No Peace Option

"At the highest levels of Hamas, I saw the behind-the-scenes dealings of top Middle Eastern leaders." Hamas is not a political movement seeking compromise — it is a religious movement committed to Israel's destruction. Case: "I was trusted at the highest levels of Hamas, and I saw things no one should ever see."

## IV. Love Transcends Hate

Mosab's conversion to Christianity was driven by Jesus's teaching of loving your enemies. "When Middle Eastern nations start to understand some of what I understand, only then will there be peace." Case: Jesus's command to "love your enemies" was the revolutionary idea that changed Mosab's life.

## V. Truth Has a Cost

"I knew what kind of hero they were looking for — a fighter who dedicated his life." Telling the truth about Hamas meant betraying his family, his culture, and his people. Case: "I once brought pride to you, I now bring you only shame."

## VI. Espionage Is a Lonely Life

"Trust no one." As a double agent, Mosab could never fully trust anyone. His life was constantly at risk. Case: "Number 823" — even his identity was reduced to a number in the Shin Bet's files.

## VII. One Life Saved Is Worth Everything

"Every drop of innocent blood that has been saved gives me hope." The moral calculus: how many lives justify betraying your family? Mosab's answer: all of them. Case: "I believed in what I did and I still believe, which is my only fuel for this long journey."
