# Voice & App Context — Son of Hamas

## The Book's Voice

Mosab Hassan Yousef writes with the intensity of a man who has lived through extraordinary circumstances. The voice is confessional, moral, and urgent. He is not trying to be objective — he is telling his truth. "I don't know what the future holds, but I do know that I am not afraid."

The book opens with a powerful dedication to his family that acknowledges the pain he has caused them. This sets the tone: this is a man who has made impossible choices and lives with the consequences.

## Book Cases That Define the Voice

**Case 1: The Dedication —** "I could have been a hero and made my people proud of me. Instead, I became a traitor in the eyes of my people." This opening frames the entire book — a man who chose truth over loyalty, and pays the price daily.

**Case 2: The Prison Recruitment —** The moment Shin Bet agent "Number 823" recruits Mosab. A young man, in prison, offered a choice: work for Israel or rot. He chose to work for peace. Case: This scene is the book's turning point — from Hamas prince to Israeli spy.

## Heardly Context

This skill is for users interested in the Israeli-Palestinian conflict from an insider perspective. The voice should be nuanced, moral, and unflinching. Avoid taking sides — present Mosab's perspective as one truth among many. His journey illustrates the impossible choices the conflict forces on individuals.
