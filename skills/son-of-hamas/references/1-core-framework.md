# Core Framework — Son of Hamas

## Early Life: The Prince of Hamas

Mosab Hassan Yousef was born the oldest son of Sheikh Hassan Yousef, one of the founders and leaders of Hamas. "Before the age of twenty-one, I saw things no one should ever see: abject poverty, abuse of power, torture, and death." He grew up in the heart of the Palestinian resistance movement, trained in Islamic teachings, and watched the First Intifada unfold. Case: "I was the royal prince of Hamas."

## Captured and Recruited (Chapters 1, 11-12)

Arrested by Israeli authorities at age 17 for Hamas activity. In prison, he was approached by the Shin Bet (Israel Security Agency). They offered him a choice: become an informant or face life in prison. "Number 823" — his Shin Bet code name. Case: "I chose to work with the Israelis because I believed it was the only way to stop the killing on both sides."

## The Undercover Years (Chapters 13-25)

For over a decade, Mosab worked as an undercover agent inside Hamas. He brought intelligence that foiled dozens of suicide bombings and saved hundreds of lives. "The information revealed in this book in no way jeopardizes the ongoing global war on terrorism." Case: He moved freely inside Hamas leadership circles while working for the Shin Bet — "I was trusted at the highest levels of Hamas."

## The Conversion (Chapter 15: Damascus Road)

In prison, Mosab was given a Bible. Through reading the Gospels, he came to believe in Jesus. His conversion was the real turning point — not just changing sides in a conflict, but changing his entire worldview. Case: "I am a child of Islam and the son of an accused terrorist. I am also a follower of Jesus."

## The Price of Betrayal

"I could have been a hero and made my people proud of me. Instead, I became a traitor in the eyes of my people." His father disowned him. His family was publicly shamed. "I paid, you paid, and yet the bills of war and peace continue to come." Case: "I know you see me as a traitor; please understand it was not you I chose to betray, but your understanding of what it means to be a hero."
