# 1. Core Framework — The Epistolary Network

> Source: "The Guernsey Literary and Potato Peel Pie Society" by Mary Ann Shaffer and Annie Barrows (2008). The novel is structured as a web of correspondences.

## The Letter Network

The novel has no narrator. Every piece of information comes through letters between characters. The reader must piece together the story from multiple correspondents, each with their own voice, perspective, and reliability.

### The Main Correspondents

| Character | Role | Writing Style |
|-----------|------|---------------|
| **Juliet Ashton** | Protagonist, writer | Witty, self-deprecating, literary |
| **Dawsey Adams** | Farmer, Guernsey | Earnest, simple, deeply feeling |
| **Sidney Stark** | Publisher, friend | Busy, affectionate, practical |
| **Amelia Maugery** | Island elder | Blunt, wise, warm |
| **Isola Pribby** | Friend, eccentric | Rambling, loving, full of news |
| **Kit McKenna** | Elizabeth's daughter | Brief, childlike, heartbreaking |

> **Case: Dawsey's First Letter** (Part One): "I was introduced to your name and address by a bookseller in London..." This single letter — from a stranger to a writer he has never met — launches the entire novel. Dawsey found Juliet's name written inside a used copy of Charles Lamb's essays. He writes to ask if she can help him find more books by Lamb. This letter is the seed from which the entire story grows.
> **Key takeaway**: A great story can begin with the smallest act — a letter written to a stranger about a book.

> **Case: The Formation of the Society** (Mid-novel): Elizabeth McKenna and her friends are caught breaking curfew by German soldiers. To avoid punishment, Elizabeth claims they were at a meeting of their literary society. The soldiers demand to see documentation. The friends scramble to create a real book club, complete with minutes. "The Guernsey Literary and Potato Peel Pie Society" is born from this lie — and becomes a real community.
> **Key takeaway**: The best communities are born from necessity. A lie becomes the truth when people live it.
