## 1. The 8 Markers Scorecard

Score your country on each of Dalio's 8 markers. 1 = strong, 10 = declining. A score above 50 (out of 80) signals advanced decline. This gives you an objective measure of where your nation is in the Big Cycle.

## 2. The Diversification Imperative

Dalio's investment rule: do not bet on any single country or currency. Hold a mixture of US stocks, international stocks, commodities, gold, and inflation-linked bonds. In a changing world order, no single asset class is safe.

> **Case: The Lost Decade** (Chapter 9): The 1970s was a period of changing world order — the end of Bretton Woods, the decline of US economic dominance, high inflation. A 60/40 US stock/bond portfolio lost money in real terms. Gold returned 30%+ annually. Diversification across assets was essential.

## 3. The Historical Compass

When faced with uncertainty, ask: "What is the historical precedent?" Dalio's team studied every major empire cycle over 500 years. When they identified a pattern, they used it to understand the present.

## 4. The Three Force Check

Before any major decision, check against the three forces: (1) Where are we in the debt cycle? (2) Where are we in the internal order cycle? (3) Where are we in the external order cycle? If all three are in a late stage, prepare for turbulence.

## 5. The Paradigm Shift Detection

A paradigm shift is occurring when the old assumptions stop working. Watch for: sustained inflation, loss of reserve currency confidence, rising internal conflict. When these converge, the paradigm has shifted.

> **Case: The 1971 Nixon Shock** (Chapter 9): When Nixon ended dollar convertibility to gold in 1971, Dalio was a young investor. The old paradigm (fixed exchange rates, gold-backed dollar) ended. A new paradigm (floating exchange rates, fiat currency) began. Investors who understood the paradigm shift positioned for inflation and gold. Those who didn't lost purchasing power.
> **Key takeaway**: Paradigm shifts create big winners and big losers. Understanding the cycle helps you identify which paradigm is ending and which is beginning.
