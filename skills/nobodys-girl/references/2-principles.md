# Key Principles — Nobody's Girl

## I. Survivors Must Be Believed

"For years, powerful people called me a liar." Virginia Roberts Giuffre was called a fantasist, a gold-digger, a prostitute. She was sued, harassed, and threatened. The Miami Herald investigation proved she was telling the truth. Case: Her flight logs, her sworn testimony, and the dozen other women who came forward — corroborated.

## II. The Powerful Protect Each Other

Epstein operated with impunity for decades. He had friends in high places — Prince Andrew, former Senators, billionaires. "The bottom of the pyramid" — the operation was designed to recruit vulnerable girls for powerful men. Case: Prince Andrew's friendship with Epstein — Virginia alleges she was trafficked to him. The palace denied it. She testified under oath.

## III. Trafficking Happens in Plain Sight

"I was recruited at a country club." Virginia was not kidnapped from a van — she was groomed. Maxwell approached her at Mar-a-Lago where she worked as a spa attendant. "She was charming. She seemed like she wanted to help." Case: Epstein's house in Palm Beach, his private jet, his island — none of it was hidden.

## IV. The System Failed the Victims

Epstein's first plea deal (2008) was a travesty. Federal prosecutors agreed to let him plead to state charges, serve 13 months, and register as a sex offender. Federal charges were hidden from victims. Case: "I was told the deal was done. I wasn't told that federal charges were being dropped."

## V. Going Public Is Terrifying — And Necessary

"I was terrified. But I was also angry." Virginia knew that going public would subject her to attacks. She was called a liar. She was sued for defamation. But she did it anyway. Case: "I couldn't hide anymore. The truth had to come out."

## VI. Justice Is Possible — But Not Guaranteed

Epstein died before facing full justice. Maxwell was convicted. "Maxwell on trial" — Virginia testified. "I looked her in the eye. She wouldn't look back." Case: Maxwell was sentenced to 20 years. Epstein's suicide meant his full network was never exposed.

## VII. Your Story Is Your Power

"I believe it has the potential to impact many lives." Virginia's last email, written days before her death: the book must be published. She knew her story would help other survivors. "I want the book published regardless of my circumstances." Case: Her collaborator: "She wanted the world to know who she really was so that survivors of abuse would feel less alone."
