# Core Framework — Nobody's Girl

## A Note from the Collaborator

The book's collaborator writes a devastating note: Virginia Roberts Giuffre died on April 1, 2025, shortly before publication. Her last email: "I am reaching out to discuss an important matter regarding my book. It is my heartfelt wish that this work be published, regardless of my circumstances at the time." She had been assaulted by her husband, separated from her children, and suffered a car accident in the weeks before her death. "If it's in God's hands, I'm not scared. If it's my time to go, it's my time to go, but I want the book published."

## Introduction

Virginia Lee Roberts was born in California. Her parents divorced when she was young. She was raised primarily by her mother, Lynn. Her father, Clay, was largely absent. She was sexually abused by a family friend starting at age 7.

## Part I: Daughter

**Childhood trauma:** "I was less than nothing." Virginia was sexually abused by her father's friend, a man she called "Uncle" Ron. Her abuser was later convicted for abusing another minor. "I learned early that my body wasn't mine."

**The escape attempt:** At 14, she ran away from home. She was placed in foster care, then a group home. She was desperate for love, attention, and safety. "I was a girl looking for someone to save me."

## Part II: Prisoner

**The Pink House:** Palm Beach, Florida. Ghislaine Maxwell recruited her at the Mar-a-Lago club — where Virginia worked as a spa attendant. "She was charming. She seemed like she wanted to help me."

**Enter Jeffrey Epstein:** Epstein was introduced as a "very important man." He paid Virginia for massages. The massages became sexual. She was trafficked to other powerful men. "I was a puppet on a string."

**The network:** Epstein's private jet, his island in the Caribbean, his townhouse in New York. "The bottom of the pyramid" — other girls recruited the same way. Case: "I met other girls. We called ourselves 'Epstein's girls.' We didn't know what else to call ourselves."

## Part III: Survivor

**Escaping:** Virginia finally broke free of Epstein's control. She married Robert Giuffre, an Australian security guard. They had three children. "I wanted to be nobody. Just a wife, just a mother."

**The past catches up:** In 2009, the FBI contacted her. They were investigating Epstein. She decided to tell her story. "I was terrified. But I was also angry."

**Becoming a witness:** She gave a sworn deposition in 2009. Epstein's first plea deal (2008) was already a scandal — he served just 13 months on a state charge while federal charges were hidden.

## Part IV: Warrior

**Going public:** In 2014, the Miami Herald's Julie K. Brown broke the story. Virginia decided to go public. "I couldn't hide anymore."

**The lawsuit against Maxwell:** Virginia filed a defamation lawsuit against Ghislaine Maxwell. Maxwell denied everything. "She called me a liar. She said I made it all up."

**Epstein's arrest:** July 6, 2019. Epstein was arrested on federal sex trafficking charges. He died in jail one month later — ruled a suicide. "I didn't believe it was suicide. I still don't."

**Maxwell's trial:** November 2021. Ghislaine Maxwell was convicted on five of six counts, including sex trafficking of a minor. "I testified. I looked her in the eye. She wouldn't look back."

**The aftermath:** Virginia continued to advocate for victims. She co-wrote this book. She died before seeing it published. "I want the book published regardless of my circumstances."
