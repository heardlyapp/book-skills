# Anti-Patterns — Nobody's Girl

## The Central Error: "She Was a Willing Participant"

This is the most common and most damaging accusation against trafficking survivors. "They called me a prostitute. They said I was a willing participant." Virginia was a child — 16 years old — when she was recruited. She was groomed, controlled, and trafficked. "I was a child. I didn't know what was happening to me."

### Mistake 1: "Epstein was just a billionaire who liked young girls"
"He was a predator who built an international trafficking network." Epstein had a system: recruitment (Maxwell found girls), transportation (private jet), locations (Palm Beach, NYC, the island), and supply (other powerful men). It was organized crime.

### Mistake 2: "Ghislaine Maxwell was just Epstein's girlfriend"
"She was his recruiter." Maxwell found and groomed teenage girls. She told Virginia that massaging Epstein was normal. She participated in the abuse. Case: Maxwell was convicted of sex trafficking of a minor.

### Mistake 3: "The girls were paid — they chose this"
"You can't consent when you're a child being controlled by adults who hold all the power." Virginia was given money, told what to do, and threatened if she didn't comply. "I was a puppet on a string."

### Mistake 4: "The justice system worked"
"Epstein died before facing trial." His first plea deal was a disgrace — 13 months for a lifetime of crimes. The full extent of his network was never investigated. Case: "The system failed us."

### Mistake 5: "She was after money"
"I never wanted money. I wanted the truth." Virginia filed a civil suit for damages, but her primary motivation was never financial. "I wanted Ghislaine Maxwell held accountable."

### Mistake 6: "She made it up for attention"
"Nobody would choose this life." The publicity brought death threats, harassment, and ongoing trauma. "Going public was the hardest thing I've ever done."
