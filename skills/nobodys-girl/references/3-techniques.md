# Techniques — Nobody's Girl

## How to Survive and Fight Back (Based on Virginia Roberts Giuffre)

### Technique 1: The Escape Plan
Virginia eventually escaped Epstein's orbit. The technique: find a moment of safety — a trip, a new city, a person you trust — and use it to break free. Case: Virginia met Robert Giuffre in Australia. She chose to stay, build a life, and never return to Epstein.

### Technique 2: The Written Record
Virginia kept journals. She remembered names, places, dates. "I wrote everything down because I thought someday I'd need to prove it." The technique: document everything. Flight logs, receipts, text messages — all became evidence.

### Technique 3: The Sisterhood of Survivors
"Survivor sisters unite." Virginia connected with other women. "I met other girls. We called ourselves 'Epstein's girls.' We didn't know what else to call ourselves." The technique: find your people. Other survivors understand in ways that others cannot.

### Technique 4: The Journalist Partnership
When the Miami Herald's Julie K. Brown investigated Epstein, she partnered with survivors. "The Miami Herald series changed everything." The technique: journalists can do what individuals cannot — investigate, corroborate, and publish.

### Technique 5: The Sworn Testimony
Virginia gave a sworn deposition in 2009. She testified at Maxwell's trial. "I looked her in the eye." The technique: tell the truth under oath. It's terrifying, but it's the most powerful thing you can do.

### Technique 6: The Timing of Going Public
Going public is a strategic decision. Virginia waited until she was ready — until she had support, until the investigation was underway, until she could no longer stay silent. Case: She went public in 2014 after the Miami Herald investigation was already ongoing.

### Technique 7: The Last Request
Virginia's final email: "I am reaching out to discuss an important matter regarding my book. It is my heartfelt wish that this work be published, regardless of my circumstances at the time." The technique: leave a clear written record of your wishes. Make sure your story will be told even if you can't tell it yourself.
