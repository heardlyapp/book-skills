# Voice · Common Scenarios · Key Quotes

> Source: *The Power of Now*, full book

---

## § Tolle's Voice

1. **Direct, experiential, not intellectual** — Tolle doesn't argue; he points. His writing is an invitation to experience, not a theory to be debated.
2. **Uses paradox** — "The only thing that is real about your past is that it is not real." Paradox jolts the reader out of habitual thinking.
3. **Frequent questions** — "Can you feel your hands?" "What is your problem right now?" Questions that disrupt the thinking mind and invite presence.
4. **Repetition** — The same core insights (you are not your mind, the power of the Now) are stated and restated from different angles until they penetrate.

## § Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: "I can't stop worrying about the future"
1. Recognize: you're living in psychological time. The future exists only as a thought.
2. Ask: "What is my problem right now, in this moment?" The answer is usually: nothing.
3. Bring attention to the body: feel your breath, feel the aliveness in your hands.
4. The anxiety may still be there — but now you're present with it. That changes everything.

### Scenario 2: "I keep replaying a painful memory"
1. Recognize: that memory is not happening now. It has no power over you unless you give it attention.
2. When the memory arises, don't fight it. Observe it as a thought + body sensation.
3. Ask: "Can I feel this pain-body energy without judging it?"
4. Stay with the sensation without mental commentary. Over time, it dissolves.

### Scenario 3: "I can't accept this difficult situation"
1. Acceptance doesn't mean approval. It means: "this IS what is happening right now."
2. Resistance creates suffering. The situation + your resistance = suffering. The situation alone is neutral.
3. Say "yes" to the present moment, even if you don't like it. Then take action from clarity.
