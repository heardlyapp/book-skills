# Core Framework · The Mind · The Pain-Body

> Source: *The Power of Now*, Chapters 1-2

---

## You Are Not Your Mind (Chapter 1)

**Core insight:** The single greatest obstacle to enlightenment is identification with your mind.

> *"The mind is a superb instrument if used rightly. Used wrongly, however, it becomes very destructive."*

The incessant thinking mind runs on autopilot. Most people are possessed by their thoughts — they can't stop thinking, and they believe they ARE their thoughts.

**The Practice:** Watch the thinker. Listen to the voice in your head. When you hear it without judgment, you realize: "I am not the thinker — I am the awareness behind the thought."

> **Key distinction:** You are not your thoughts. You are the space in which thoughts appear and disappear.

**Emotion:** Emotions are the body's reaction to what your mind is doing. A thought creates an emotion in the body. Watching both thought and emotion without judgment is the way out.

---

## The Pain-Body (Chapter 2)

**Core insight:** Every person carries an energy field of accumulated emotional pain from the past — the pain-body.

**How it forms:** Every painful experience that wasn't fully accepted and processed leaves a residue of pain. This accumulates over a lifetime (and, Tolle suggests, across lifetimes). It becomes a semi-autonomous entity that lives within you.

**How it feeds:**
- Negative thinking
- Emotional drama in relationships
- Replaying painful memories
- Feeling offended, victimized, resentful

**The Practice:** When you feel the pain-body activate (strong emotional reaction), **become aware of it as an energy field within you**. Observe it without labeling it. Don't fight it — just watch. The moment you observe it consciously, its power over you diminishes.

> *"The pain-body doesn't want you to observe it directly — to see it as it is. The moment you observe it, it begins to dissolve."*

**Key insight:** The pain-body feeds on your unconscious identification with it. Consciousness dissolves it like light dissolving darkness.
