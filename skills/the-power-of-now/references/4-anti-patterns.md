# Anti-Patterns · What Keeps You Out of the Now

> Source: *The Power of Now*, Chapters 1-10

---

## Identification with the Mind

**Anti-pattern:** Believing you ARE your thoughts. Living in a continuous internal monologue that you take to be yourself.

**Examples:**
- "I'm an anxious person" (identifying with anxious thoughts)
- "I can't stop my mind — there's too much to think about"
- Defending your opinions as if they were your identity

**Tolle's fix:** Watch the thinker. The awareness behind thought is who you really are.

---

## Living in Psychological Time

**Anti-pattern:** Constantly dwelling on the past or future, missing the only moment that exists.

**Examples:**
- Replaying old conversations and regrets
- Anxious about things that haven't happened (and may never)
- "I'll be happy when…" (always waiting for the future)

**Tolle's fix:** Ask yourself: "What is my problem right now, in this moment?" The answer is usually: nothing.

---

## Feeding the Pain-Body

**Anti-pattern:** Unconsciously seeking negativity — complaining, feeling offended, creating drama — because the pain-body needs negative emotion to survive.

**Examples:**
- Feeling a secret satisfaction when bad things happen (proving "life is unfair")
- Replaying grievances in your mind
- Getting defensive over small criticisms

**Tolle's fix:** Recognize the pain-body when it activates. Observe it without judgment. Don't feed it by adding mental commentary.

---

## Non-Surrender

**Anti-pattern:** Arguing with reality. Saying "this shouldn't be happening" to what already is.

**Examples:**
- Complaining about traffic, weather, illness
- "Why is this happening to me?"
- Resisting the present moment emotionally

**Tolle's fix:** Accept the present moment as if you had chosen it. Then act from that acceptance.

---

## Negative Emotions as Resistance

**Anti-pattern:** Any negative emotion — anxiety, anger, resentment, impatience — is a form of non-surrender. It's the mind saying "no" to what is.

**Tolle's fix:** The moment you realize you're unhappy, that realization itself is freedom. Watch the unhappiness without judgment.
