# Core Framework: The Learning Loop

## The Central Insight

"Failure is not an event — it's a judgment." The book's core teaching is that what we call failure is simply an outcome we didn't want. The difference between those who succeed and those who don't is not the absence of failure — it's how they interpret and respond to it.

> **Case: The Inventor's Iterations** (Chapter 1): Thomas Edison didn't fail 10,000 times — he found 10,000 ways that didn't work. His framing of each unsuccessful attempt as data rather than defeat is the essence of failing forward.
> **Key takeaway**: The same event can be failure or feedback. Your interpretation determines whether you stop or continue.

## The Learning Loop

Try → Fail → Extract Lesson → Adjust → Try Again

"The faster you move through this loop, the faster you succeed." Most people get stuck at Step 2 (fail) and never reach Step 3 (extract lesson) or Step 5 (try again). The speed of the loop determines your growth rate.

> **Case: The Sales Rejection** (Chapter 3): A salesperson received 47 rejections in one week. She could have interpreted this as "I'm not good at sales." Instead, she asked: "What pattern do these rejections share? What can I change?" She identified that her opening was weak, adjusted it, and saw her close rate improve.
> **Key takeaway**: Failure without analysis is wasted. Each failure contains a pattern. Find the pattern, and you've found the lesson.
