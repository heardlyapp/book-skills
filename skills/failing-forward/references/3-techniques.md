# Techniques: Failing Forward Practices

## The Post-Failure Debrief

After any significant failure:
1. What happened? (facts only, no interpretation)
2. What did I contribute to this outcome? (ownership)
3. What can I learn from this? (extraction)
4. What will I do differently next time? (adjustment)
5. What's my next step? (forward motion)

> **Case: The Athlete's Comeback** (Chapter 9): An athlete lost the biggest match of her career. Instead of making excuses, she used the 5-question debrief. She realized she had neglected her mental preparation. She hired a sports psychologist and won the same match the following year.
> **Key takeaway**: The 5-question debrief transforms a painful loss into actionable intelligence. Every failure is data for your next attempt.

## The Small Bet Strategy

When fear of failure is paralyzing:
1. Make the smallest possible bet — minimal time, money, ego
2. Define success and failure clearly before starting
3. Execute and observe the outcome
4. Learn and adjust
5. Increase the bet size gradually as confidence grows

> **Case: The Aspiring Speaker** (Chapter 10): A man wanted to become a public speaker but was terrified of failing on stage. Instead of booking a keynote, he gave a 5-minute talk at his local Toastmasters. No one hated it. He gave another. A year later, he was speaking professionally.
> **Key takeaway**: Start so small that failure is meaningless. Each small success builds the confidence to take bigger risks.

## Overcoming the Blame Reflex

When you catch yourself blaming:
1. Pause and notice: "I'm blaming."
2. Reframe: "What was MY role in this outcome?"
3. Ask: "What can I learn from my contribution?"
4. Even if only 5% was your fault, focus on that 5%.
