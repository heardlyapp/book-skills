# Core Framework: The 5 Parts of Every Business

> Source: The Personal MBA, Chapters 2-7

## The 5 Parts

Every business, from a lemonade stand to Apple, consists of five interconnected parts: Value Creation (creating something people want), Marketing (attracting attention and interest), Sales (converting interest into a transaction), Value Delivery (actually delivering what you promised), and Finance (managing money, revenue, costs, and cash flow). All five must function for the business to succeed. If any part breaks, the business breaks.

> **Case: The Lemonade Stand** (Chapter 2): The simplest business. A recipe people enjoy = value creation. A sidewalk sign = marketing. Asking passersby to buy = sales. Making and serving the lemonade = value delivery. Tracking the cost of lemons vs money from sales = finance. All five parts must work or the stand fails.

> **Key takeaway**: Struggling in business? Check the five parts. The weakness is in one of them. Find it and fix it.

## The Core Human Drives

Four drives motivate all human behavior: Acquire (status, money, material goods), Bond (relationships, belonging, love), Learn (curiosity, mastery, skill development), Defend (security, health, safety). Every successful product satisfies at least one.

## The Fogg Behavior Model

B = MAT. Behavior happens when Motivation, Ability, and Trigger converge. To sell more, simplify the purchase process (ability) and create compelling triggers (calls to action, scarcity).

> **Key takeaway**: People don't buy features. They buy satisfaction of drives. Make it easy and they'll buy.
