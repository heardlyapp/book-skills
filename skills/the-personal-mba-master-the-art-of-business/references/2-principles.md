# Principles: Marketing, Sales, and Finance

> Source: The Personal MBA, Chapters 3-6

## The 4Ps of Marketing

Product, Price, Place, Promotion. The classic marketing framework. Kaufman adds: your marketing must address the customer's core human drive. A product serving Acquire (luxury watch for status) should be marketed differently than one serving Defend (home security system).

> **Case: The Positioning Problem** (Chapter 3): A great product with wrong positioning will fail. Kaufman's example: a high-quality pen positioned as "expensive writing instrument" (Acquire for status) vs "tool for better ideas" (Learn for creativity). Same pen, different positioning, different customers.

> **Key takeaway**: How you position your product matters as much as the product itself.

## The Sales Process

Five steps: Approach (initiate contact), Ask (understand their needs and situation), Listen (the most important step — understand before offering), Present (offer your solution), Close (ask for the commitment). Most salespeople skip Ask and Listen — and fail.

> **Case: The Listen Failure** (Chapter 4): Salespeople who pitch before understanding lose the sale. Kaufman emphasizes that listening is the single most important sales skill. The best salespeople ask great questions and listen carefully.

## Finance Fundamentals

Revenue - Cost = Profit. Cash flow is money available right now. You can be profitable on paper (accrual accounting) while running out of money (cash accounting).

> **Key takeaway**: Track revenue, costs, AND cash flow separately. They tell different stories about your business.
