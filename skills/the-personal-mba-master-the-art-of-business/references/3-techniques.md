# Techniques: Human Mind and Systems Thinking

> Source: The Personal MBA, Chapters 7-12

## The Fogg Behavior Model

B = MAT. For any desired behavior to occur, three elements must converge: Motivation (the person wants to do it), Ability (it's easy enough to do), and Trigger (a cue to act). If customers aren't buying, check which element is missing. Often it's Ability — the purchase process is too hard, takes too long, or requires too many decisions.

> **Case: The One-Click Solution** (Chapter 7): Amazon's 1-Click ordering is a masterful optimization of Ability. Buying a book was already fairly easy, but reducing the process to a single click dramatically increased conversion rates. Kaufman's lesson: small reductions in friction produce large increases in behavior.

> **Key takeaway**: Make it as easy as possible for customers to take the action you want. Reduce every step you can.

## The 3-System Problem

Every business has three systems: Create Demand (marketing and sales), Fulfill Demand (taking orders through delivery), and Support Demand (customer service and retention). Most business problems trace to a breakdown in one system.

## Mental Models for Business

Kaufman provides dozens of mental models to sharpen business thinking: The 80/20 Rule (most results come from few efforts), The Iron Law of the Market (must solve a real problem), The Salience Bias (what's vivid feels important), The Meridian of Mind (people hold ~7 items in working memory).

> **Key takeaway**: Mental models sharpen business judgment. The more models you have, the better your decisions.
