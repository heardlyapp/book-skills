# Anti-Patterns: Common Business Mistakes

> Source: The Personal MBA, Chapters 8-10

## Ignoring Cash Flow

The most dangerous mistake: obsessing about profit while ignoring cash flow. You can show healthy profit on paper while running out of money because customers haven't paid their invoices yet.

> **Case: The Profitable Bankruptcy** (Chapter 6): A business that sells on credit books profit when the sale is made (accrual accounting) but has no cash to pay the rent until customers pay. Many companies fail despite strong revenue. Cash flow is oxygen — profit is food. You can live a while without food, but not without oxygen.

> **Key takeaway**: Watch cash flow first and obsessively. It's the earliest indicator of trouble.

## Building Before Understanding Demand

Creating a product before knowing anyone wants it. The fix: minimum viable products and customer conversations before building.

> **Case: The Empty Product** (Chapter 2): Entrepreneurs often build elaborate products without checking demand. Kaufman's rule: talk to potential customers first. Ask about their real problems. Only build what they'll actually pay for.

## Solving the Wrong Problem

The Iron Law of the Market: if you're not solving a real problem, you don't have a business. Marketing and sales won't save a product nobody actually needs.

## Confusing Activity with Progress

Being endlessly busy — meetings, emails, planning — isn't the same as making progress. Real progress means creating value and getting paid for it.

> **Key takeaway**: Don't confuse motion with action. Are you creating value or just keeping busy?
