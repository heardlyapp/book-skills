# 1. Core Framework — The Eating Disorder Arc

> Source: "Unbearable Lightness" by Portia de Rossi (2010). The memoir traces the arc of her eating disorder from onset through recovery.

## The Arc of the Disorder

### Phase 1: Onset (Age 12)
A comment about her weight triggers "the voice" — an internal drill sergeant that will control her eating for the next decade. She begins secretly restricting food.

### Phase 2: Deepening (Teenage Years)
She moves from restriction to bingeing and purging. She learns to hide her behaviors. The secret becomes her closest companion.

> **Case: The Secret Life** (Part One): Portia describes the elaborate rituals of hiding her eating disorder: flushing food down the toilet, exercising in secret, lying about what she ate. She becomes an expert at appearing normal while slowly starving herself.
> **Key takeaway**: Eating disorders thrive in secrecy. The ability to hide the disorder is part of the disorder's power.

### Phase 3: Hollywood and Escalation (Ally McBeal Years)
As an actress under constant scrutiny, Portia's disorder escalates. She loses weight to meet Hollywood's standards — and then keeps losing. At 5'7", she drops to 82 pounds.

> **Case: The 82-Pound Moment** (Part Two): Portia describes looking at her 82-pound body and still seeing fat. This is the hallmark of body dysmorphia: the image in the mirror does not match reality. The disorder has completely distorted her perception.
> **Key takeaway**: An eating disorder is not a problem of weight — it is a problem of perception. Changing the body does not change the perception of the body.

### Phase 4: Intervention and Recovery
Ellen, friends, and medical professionals intervene. Portia enters treatment, gains weight, and begins the slow process of recovery.

> **Case: The First Confession** (Part Three): Portia tells Ellen her actual weight for the first time. "It was the first time I'd told anyone the truth." The confession breaks the seal of secrecy.
> **Key takeaway**: Truth-telling is the first act of recovery. The secret must be spoken before it can be defeated.
