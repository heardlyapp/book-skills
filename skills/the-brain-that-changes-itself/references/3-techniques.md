# Techniques — The Brain That Changes Itself

## How to Harness Neuroplasticity

### Technique 1: The Sensory Substitution Method (Bach-y-Rita)
If one sense is damaged, provide input through another channel. The brain will learn to process it. "The brain doesn't care where sensory information comes from — it just needs data to work with." Case: Cheryl Schiltz wearing the tongue device that substituted balance signals — her brain learned to "hear" balance through her tongue.

### Technique 2: The Targeted Cognitive Exercise (Arrowsmith)
Identify the specific cognitive weakness (memory, reasoning, spatial perception). Design exercises that repeatedly challenge that exact function. "The brain strengthens what it repeatedly practices." Case: Arrowsmith's exercises targeted her specific learning disabilities and literally rebuilt her neural architecture.

### Technique 3: Constraint-Induced Movement Therapy (Taub)
Restrict the functioning limb. Force use of the impaired limb. This prevents "learned non-use" and rewires the brain. Case: A stroke patient who hadn't used his arm in 20 years regained function after two weeks of CI therapy.

### Technique 4: The OCD Relabeling Method (Schwartz)
When an obsessive thought arises: 1) Relabel it as OCD, 2) Reattribute it to a brain glitch, 3) Refocus on a positive activity, 4) Revalue the thought as unimportant. "Change the channel in your brain." Case: OCD patients who practiced this method for 12 weeks showed measurable brain changes on fMRI scans.

### Technique 5: The Mirror Box for Phantom Limb Pain (Ramachandran)
Place a mirror so the patient sees the reflection of their intact limb over the phantom. The brain "sees" the limb moving and updates its body map. Case: A patient with phantom arm pain placed his arm in the mirror box — the pain disappeared as his brain saw the "phantom" arm moving.

### Technique 6: Mental Practice
Visualize a movement in detail. Imagining piano practice activates the same motor circuits as actual practice. Case: Pianists who mentally practiced showed almost as much improvement as those who physically practiced.

### Technique 7: The Focused Attention Rule
Passive exposure is not enough. Learning requires focused attention. "Brain change is effortful." Case: Background TV doesn't teach language — active engagement does.
