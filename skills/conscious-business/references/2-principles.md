# Key Principles — Conscious Business

## I. Consciousness Is a Choice

"Living consciously is a state of being mentally active rather than passive. It is the ability to look at the world through fresh eyes." Kofman's journey from Argentina's Dirty War taught him that unconsciousness is not innocent — it enables evil. "Nobody's more blind than the one who doesn't want to see."

## II. Unconditional Responsibility

"Responsibility is the ability to respond." Not to blame — to respond. Kofman's Argentinean soccer story: losing at halftime, the coach said "You can blame the referee, the field, the other team — or you can take responsibility for the second half." They won 3-0. "Between stimulus and response there is a space. In that space is our power to choose our response."

## III. Integrity Is Wholeness, Not Morality

"Integrity is about wholeness — being undivided." Enron had a code of ethics. The problem wasn't lack of rules, it was lack of alignment. "A person of integrity aligns their actions with their values, even when no one is watching."

## IV. You Are Not Your Map

"Ontological humility means acknowledging that your map of reality is not the territory." We all have blind spots. The path to wisdom begins with "I could be wrong." As Solzhenitsyn wrote: "The line dividing good and evil cuts through the heart of every human being."

## V. Speak the Truth, But Take Care Not to Wound

Authentic communication requires distinguishing opinions from facts. "A thought is never a fact. A fact is a thought that many people agree on." When you speak from "I" — "I think," "I feel," "I see" — you own your perspective without imposing it.

## VI. Win-Win Is a Way of Being, Not a Technique

The Orange dispute: Two sisters wanted one orange. They compromised — half each. One squeezed her half for juice; the other used the peel for cake. If they'd explored interests, both could have had 100%. "Win-win requires genuine curiosity about what the other person truly needs."

## VII. Success Beyond Success

"The best way to do is to be" — Lao Tzu. Kofman's ultimate message: business success is hollow without human development. "Success beyond success means achieving results while growing as a human being." The highest purpose of business is service.
