# 5 — Hicks's Voice + 5 Application Scenarios

> Source: "Ask and It Is Given" by Esther and Jerry Hicks

## Abraham's Voice

The book is channeled from "Abraham" — a group of non-physical beings speaking through Esther Hicks. The voice: loving, authoritative, playful, and clear. Abraham speaks in a distinctive cadence that is simultaneously gentle and powerful.

> **Case: The Foreword** (Foreword by Wayne Dyer): "Dr. Wayne Dyer writes: 'Abraham's words have been a powerful part of my own spiritual journey. The teachings are simple, profound, and practical. They have helped millions of people understand the power of their own thoughts and feelings.'" The endorsement from Dyer establishes the book's credibility in the spiritual self-help world.
> **Key takeaway**: The book has been endorsed by some of the most respected names in personal development. Its influence extends far beyond the "Law of Attraction" niche. Dyer, a highly respected author, considered Abraham's teachings among the most important.

> **Case: Preface** (Preface by Jerry Hicks): "Jerry Hicks writes: 'Esther and I have been sharing these teachings since 1986. We have seen thousands of people transform their lives — heal their bodies, improve their relationships, find financial freedom. The Law of Attraction is not a theory. It is a practical, demonstrable truth.'" The Hickses present their experience.
> **Key takeaway**: Jerry and Esther Hicks did not just write a book. They spent decades teaching workshops, giving seminars, and witnessing real transformations. The book is the distillation of that experience. It is meant to be practiced, not just read.

## Key Quote

"Your only work is to feel good. The universe will do the rest."

## 5 Application Scenarios

1. **Feeling stuck in negative emotions** → Use the Emotional Scale. Identify where you are and find one small step up. Don't try to jump to joy.
2. **Wanting to manifest something specific** → Try the Scripting process. Write your desire in present tense with feeling.
3. **Feeling overwhelmed** → Try the Rampage of Appreciation. Start with one thing and build.
4. **Overthinking and worrying** → Practice segment intending. Set your intention for the next activity and let go.
5. **Doubtful about manifestation** → Start with small, easy manifestations (a parking spot, a phone call). Build confidence through experience.
