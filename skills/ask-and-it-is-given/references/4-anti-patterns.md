# 4 — Resistance and the Art of Allowing

> Source: "Ask and It Is Given" by Esther and Jerry Hicks

## Anti-Pattern: Forcing Outcomes

The most common mistake: trying to make things happen through effort, control, and force. This is the opposite of allowing.

> **Case: The Forcing Trap** (Part II — Allowing): "Many people try to manifest through sheer willpower. They visualize, affirm, and drive themselves crazy trying to make things happen. But the Law of Attraction responds to your vibration, not your effort. Forcing creates resistance. Allowing creates flow." Abraham distinguishes between effort and alignment.
> **Key takeaway**: If manifestation feels like work, you're doing it wrong. The feeling of effort means you're forcing against resistance. True alignment feels like relief, like coming home, like letting go. The most productive thing you can do is to relax and feel good.

## Anti-Pattern: Worrying About the How

Another common block: obsessing over how your desire will manifest. This keeps you in a vibration of lack and doubt.

> **Case: The How Trap** (Part II — Allowing): "You don't need to know how your desire will manifest. The universe has resources you cannot imagine. Your only job is to clarify what you want and get into vibrational alignment with it. The universe will orchestrate the how in ways that will amaze you." Abraham addresses the most common question.
> **Key takeaway**: Worrying about how is a form of resistance. It implies doubt — you don't trust that the universe can deliver. The truth: the universe is infinite in its creativity. Your limited human mind cannot imagine the paths that are available. Trust the how. Focus on the what and the feeling.
