# Techniques — Multiple Identities, Compartmentalization, and the Art of Being Forgettable

## Practical Methods

> **Case: The Six Identities** (Early): The Jackal has six separate identities, each with its own passport, backstory, and purpose. He uses one to enter France, another to rent a hotel room, another to order the rifle parts. If one identity is compromised, the others remain clean.
> **Key takeaway**: Compartmentalize everything. No single identity knows the whole plan. If one piece is discovered, the rest survive.

> **Case: The Clean Method** (Throughout): The Jackal does not leave fingerprints. He does not use traceable weapons. He pays in cash. He does not contact anyone who can identify him. When his contact Kowalski becomes a liability, the Jackal eliminates him without hesitation.
> **Key takeaway**: Clean methods are not about morality. They are about operational security. Every contact you make is a potential point of exposure. Minimize them.

> **Case: The Unremarkable Presence** (Throughout): The Jackal does not look like an assassin. He looks like a businessman, a tourist, a civil servant. He is not memorable. People do not remember him because there is nothing to remember. The best disguise is being average.
> **Key takeaway**: If you need to move through a space without being noticed, the key is not to stand out in any way. Average clothes. Average manner. Average everything. Be forgettable.
