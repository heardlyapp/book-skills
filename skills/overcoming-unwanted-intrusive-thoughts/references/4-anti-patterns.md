# Anti-Patterns — Overcoming Unwanted Intrusive Thoughts

## The Central Error: "Intrusive Thoughts Mean Something About Me"

This is the core mistake. "A thought is not a fact. A thought is not a wish. A thought is not a plan." Intrusive thoughts are meaningless mental noise — but sufferers interpret them as meaningful. Case: "If you were actually going to push someone, you wouldn't be worried about it."

### Mistake 1: Thought Suppression
"I need to stop having this thought." Trying to suppress a thought guarantees it returns stronger. Case: The white bear experiment. Practicing thought suppression creates a rebound effect. "The harder you try not to think about it, the more you think about it."

### Mistake 2: Reassurance Seeking
"Just tell me I'm not a bad person." Reassurance provides temporary relief but trains the brain to need more reassurance. Case: "Asking your partner 'Am I a good person?' twenty times a day doesn't help — it fuels the doubt."

### Mistake 3: Mental Rituals
"I have to replace the bad thought with a good one." Compensating with "good" thoughts maintains the belief that the original thought is dangerous. Case: "I had a violent thought, so now I have to think 'I love everyone' ten times." This strengthens the obsession.

### Mistake 4: Avoidance
"I can't be around knives / children / heights." Avoidance confirms to your brain that the thought signals real danger. Case: A mother who avoids being in the kitchen with her child because she had a harm thought — this avoidance confirms the thought's "danger."

### Mistake 5: Arguing With the Thought
"I need to prove this thought wrong." You can't argue away an intrusive thought because it's not based on logic. Case: "Trying to logically disprove an irrational thought is like trying to reason with a toddler having a tantrum."

### Mistake 6: Checking and Confessing
"I need to check if I actually did something bad." Intrusive thoughts about past actions lead to compulsive checking and confessing. Case: "Did I run someone over? I need to go back and check." The checking never satisfies.
