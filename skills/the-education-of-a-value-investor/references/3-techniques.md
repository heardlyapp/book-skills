# Techniques: The Value Investor's Practice

## The Decision Journal

1. Before each investment, write down: why you're buying, what you think it's worth, what would make you sell
2. After each investment, write down: what happened and why
3. Review the journal quarterly — look for patterns in your mistakes
4. Share the journal with a trusted mentor — external perspective is invaluable

> **Case: The Mistake Journal** (Chapter 8): Spier kept a detailed journal of every investment decision. Reviewing it revealed patterns: he bought companies he didn't fully understand when he was impatient, he sold too early when he was anxious. The journal didn't prevent mistakes but helped him recognize his patterns.
> **Key takeaway**: A decision journal is not about being right — it's about understanding why you make the decisions you do. The patterns you discover are more valuable than any individual trade.

> **Case: The Mohnish Pabrai Partnership** (Chapter 10): Spier formed an investment partnership with Mohnish Pabrai, a fellow value investor. They shared ideas, debated each other's theses, and provided mutual accountability. The partnership improved both their decision-making.
>
> **Key takeaway**: Investing alone amplifies your biases. A trusted partner who shares your principles but challenges your conclusions improves your process.

## Expanding Your Circle of Competence

1. Identify one industry you want to understand deeply
2. Read everything about it — annual reports, history, competitors
3. Talk to people who work in the industry
4. Study the industry's best and worst performers over decades
5. Write down your understanding — can you explain it clearly?
6. Only invest once you genuinely understand the business model

## The Network-Building Practice

- Attend value investing conferences (Berkshire AGM, Columbia, etc.)
- Write thoughtful letters to people you admire
- Join or form an investment club with like-minded people
- Share your analysis publicly — it invites feedback
- Be generous with your own knowledge

## The Reading Practice

- 30 minutes minimum daily from physical books
- Mix of investing, history, psychology, and biography
- Take notes on what you read
- Review your notes regularly
- Discuss what you're reading with others
