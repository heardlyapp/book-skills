# Reference: Esmeralda

## Overview
Esmeralda is the beautiful Romani dancer. She is the novel's innocent victim.

## Case: The Street Dancer
Esmeralda dances in the Place de Greve. She is joyful, free, and beloved by the crowd. Her goat Djali performs tricks. She represents life and beauty in grim medieval Paris.

## Case: The Innocent
Esmeralda is pure. She falls in love with Phoebus because he is handsome. She saves Gringoire from hanging by marrying him. She gives water to Quasimodo when he is humiliated. She does not deserve her fate.

## Case: The Victim
Esmeralda is persecuted. Frollo's obsession destroys her. She is accused of murder, tortured, and hanged. The system — Church, law, and society — all conspire against her.

## Case: The Mother
Esmeralda was stolen from her mother as a child. Her mother, Sister Gudule, lives in madness. They are reunited moments before Esmeralda's execution. The reunion is devastating.

## Case: The Symbol
Esmeralda represents innocence, beauty, and freedom. She is destroyed by the forces of repression, obsession, and injustice. Her fate is the novel's central tragedy.
Esmeralda is the light that shines briefly in the darkness of medieval Paris. Her dance, her voice, her kindness — all are extinguished by the cruelty of fate and the malice of men.
She is the emerald — precious, beautiful, and crushed under the weight of a society that cannot value what it does not understand.
The name Esmeralda means emerald. She is precious, beautiful, and crushed.
