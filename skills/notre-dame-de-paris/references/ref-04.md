# Reference: Medieval Paris

## Overview
Notre Dame de Paris is as much about the city and its cathedral as about the characters. Hugo's descriptions of medieval Paris are historically rich.

## Case: The Cathedral
Notre Dame is the soul of the novel. Hugo describes every detail: the towers, the bells, the gargoyles, the rose windows. He wanted to save the cathedral from neglect and destruction.

## Case: The Court of Miracles
The underworld of Romani and outcasts. A hidden city within Paris. The "miracles" are the deceptions of beggars.

## Case: The Printing Press
Hugo believed that printing would kill architecture. The printed book would replace the cathedral as the vessel of human knowledge.

## Case: Ananke (Fate)
The word scratched into the stone of Notre Dame. The novel is about fate — the forces that trap the characters and drive them to their ends.

## Case: The Architecture
The novel is also about the Gothic architecture of Paris. Hugo saw cathedrals as the great books of civilization.
Hugo's description of Paris from the towers of Notre Dame is one of the great passages in French literature. He sees the city as a living organism, growing and changing through centuries.
The contrast between the medieval city, with its church spires and narrow streets, and the Renaissance city that followed, is a central theme of the novel.
The Court of Miracles is the hidden Paris — the Paris of the outcast and the damned. It is where Esmeralda belongs and where society fears to go.
