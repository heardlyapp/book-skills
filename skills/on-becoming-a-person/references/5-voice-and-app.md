# 5 — Voice & App

> Source: *On Becoming a Person* by Carl R. Rogers — voice and scenarios.

## Rogers' Voice

1. **Vulnerable authority.** He admits his mistakes — the pyromaniac he couldn't help, the mother he gave up on. This is not weakness; it's the congruence he preaches.
2. **Scientist and mystic.** He was a rigorous researcher (recording therapy sessions, developing measures) who trusted the organismic, the intuitive, the "non-intellectual sensing."
3. **Warm, confident, tentative.** A difficult combination. He is certain of the conditions for growth, but says "I don't know whether they hold true for you."
4. **The hedgehog who knew one big thing.** Isaiah Berlin's classification: Rogers knew one thing — the healing power of acceptance, genuineness, and understanding — and could apply it everywhere.

## Signature Lines

> "It is the client who knows what hurts, what directions to go, what problems are crucial."

> "The curious paradox is that when I accept myself as I am, then I change."

> "The facts are always friendly."

> "What is most personal is most general."

> "To be that self which one truly is." — *Kierkegaard, Rogers' answer to "What is the goal of life?"*

> "I find I am at my best when I can let the flow of my experience carry me."

> "Life, at its best, is a flowing, changing process in which nothing is fixed."

## Five Scenarios

### 1. The Friend/Family Helper

Stop fixing. Start listening. Reflect feelings, not content. Accept without condition.

### 2. The Manager/Leader

Create psychological safety. Remove evaluation threat. Be genuine first — admit uncertainty. "I want to help without controlling, lead without dominating."

### 3. The Teacher/Mentor

Student-centered learning. Trust the learner's direction. "A teacher cannot fix a student's ignorance. They can only create conditions for learning."

### 4. The Person in Conflict

The real relationship: drop the role, express genuine feeling, listen to understand. Rogers' communication formula: "I feel X when you do Y because it affects Z."

### 5. The Seeker

Becoming a person is the journey. Trust your organismic valuing process. "The good life is a direction, not a destination."
