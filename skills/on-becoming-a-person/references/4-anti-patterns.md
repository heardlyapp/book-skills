# 4 — Anti-Patterns

> Source: *On Becoming a Person* by Carl R. Rogers — Chapters 3, 5, 13.

## Common Errors

### 1. The Fix-It Reflex

"The failure of any approach through the intellect has forced me to recognize that change appears to come about through experience in a relationship." The most common error is trying to solve another person's problem through advice, interpretation, or instruction. None of these work in the long run.

### 2. The Professional Mask

"When we develop elaborate diagnostic formulations, seeing the person as an object... we can keep ourselves from experiencing the caring which would exist if we recognized the relationship as one between two persons." The professional mask — hiding behind diagnoses, theories, or roles — prevents genuine connection.

### 3. The Evaluation Trap

Positive evaluations are as threatening as negative ones. "To inform someone that he is good implies that you also have the right to tell him he is bad." The helper who constantly evaluates — even positively — keeps the other person dependent on external judgment.

### 4. Conditional Acceptance

"The more I can keep a relationship free of judgment and evaluation, the more this will permit the other person to reach the point where he recognizes that the locus of evaluation lies within himself." When acceptance is conditional ("I accept you when you behave well"), no true growth can occur.

### 5. The Sympathy Confusion

Sympathy ("I feel sorry for you") is not empathy ("I understand how that feels for you"). Sympathy creates distance; empathy creates connection.
