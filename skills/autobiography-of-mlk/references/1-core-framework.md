# 1 — The Civil Rights Movement

> Source: "The Autobiography of Martin Luther King, Jr."

## Key Events Timeline

**Montgomery Bus Boycott (1955-56)**: 381 days. MLK's first leadership role. The city's buses were desegregated. The movement was born.

**Birmingham Campaign (1963)**: "The most segregated city in America." MLK was arrested. Wrote "Letter from Birmingham Jail." Children's March. Fire hoses and police dogs. National outrage.

**March on Washington (1963)**: 250,000 people. "I Have a Dream." The high point of the movement.

**Selma (1965)**: Voting rights. Bloody Sunday. The march from Selma to Montgomery. Led directly to the Voting Rights Act.

> **Case: The Montgomery Bus Boycott** (Chapter 3): MLK describes the night Rosa Parks was arrested. "She was seated in the front of the 'colored' section. When the bus driver ordered her to give up her seat for a white passenger, she refused. She was not physically tired. She was tired — tired of being pushed around." MLK was 26 years old. He was chosen to lead the boycott that night.
> **Key takeaway**: Leadership is often thrust upon us. MLK didn't plan to lead a movement. He was prepared, but he didn't choose it. Sometimes the moment chooses you.

> **Case: The Bombing of His Home** (Chapter 4): During the Montgomery boycott, MLK's house was bombed. His wife and baby daughter were inside. A mob gathered, ready to retaliate in violence. MLK stood on his porch and told them: "He who lives by the sword will die by the sword. We must meet hate with love." The mob dispersed. The principle of nonviolence was tested and held.
> **Key takeaway**: Nonviolence is not theoretical. It's tested in moments of crisis. MLK's response to the bombing of his own home is the most powerful demonstration of his philosophy.
