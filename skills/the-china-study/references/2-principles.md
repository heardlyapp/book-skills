# Key Principles — The China Study

## I. Diet Is the Master Key

"The science is conclusive." Diet is the single most important factor in determining health. Genetics plays a minor role compared to nutrition. Case: The geographic variation in cancer rates within China — same genetics, different diets, dramatically different outcomes.

## II. Animal Protein Promotes Cancer

"Casein, the protein in cow's milk, promoted cancer growth in laboratory rats at 20% but not 5%." The same protein that strengthens bones feeds cancer cells. This is Campbell's most controversial and important finding. Case: Plant protein (wheat, soy) did NOT promote cancer even at high levels.

## III. Whole Foods Beat Supplements

"Nutrients work synergistically." Isolated nutrients (vitamin pills, protein powders) don't work the same way as whole foods. The whole is greater than the sum of its parts. Case: Beta-carotene supplements actually increased cancer risk in some studies, while whole vegetables reduced it.

## IV. Prevention Is Better Than Cure

"The Western medical establishment focuses on treatment, not prevention." Treatment is profitable. Prevention is not. The system is stacked against the dietary approach. Case: "Every health care provider and researcher in the world must read it."

## V. Chronic Diseases Are Reversible

"Heart disease, diabetes, obesity — these can be reversed through diet." Not just prevented — reversed. Case: Dean Ornish's work (cited in the book) showed that heart disease can be reversed with a plant-based diet.

## VI. The Public Has Been Misled

"The public has been misled by the food industry, the medical establishment, and government policy." Conflicting nutrition advice serves corporate interests. Case: The "meat and milk are essential" message is a product of industry lobbying, not science.

## VII. One Diet Fits All

"The optimal diet for human health is a whole-food, plant-based diet." Not Mediterranean, not Paleo, not Keto. This diet works for everyone regardless of genetics. Case: Populations around the world that eat traditional plant-based diets have low rates of chronic disease.
