# Core Framework — The China Study

## The China Project

The largest study of nutrition ever conducted. Led by T. Colin Campbell (Cornell) in partnership with Chinese and British researchers. Surveyed diet and death rates from cancer in over 2,400 Chinese counties. "The China Study is the account of a ground-breaking research study that provides the answers long sought by physicians, scientists and health-conscious readers." Case: The project revealed dramatic geographic variations in disease rates within China — variations that correlated with diet, not genetics.

## The Animal Protein-Cancer Link

Campbell's laboratory research showed that low levels of casein (the protein in cow's milk) inhibited cancer growth, while higher levels promoted it. "The impact of these findings is enormous." Case: Rats fed 20% casein developed tumors. Rats fed 5% casein did not. The same protein that strengthens bones also feeds cancer.

## The Diseases of Affluence

Cancer, heart disease, diabetes, obesity, autoimmune diseases. These are not inevitable — they are caused by the Western diet. "The science is conclusive." Case: Rural Chinese populations eating traditional plant-based diets had dramatically lower rates of these diseases than Western populations.

## The Whole-Food Plant-Based Solution

The answer: a diet of whole plant foods (fruits, vegetables, whole grains, legumes). No animal products, no processed foods, no added oils. "All concerned with the obesity epidemic will find wise and practical solutions in Dr. Campbell's The China Study." Case: Patients who adopted this diet reversed heart disease, diabetes, and obesity.

## The Medical Establishment Problem

Campbell argues that the medical and research establishment is corrupted by funding from the pharmaceutical and food industries. Treatment is more profitable than prevention. Case: "The book is a moving and insightful history of the struggle to understand and explain the vital connection between our health and what we eat."
