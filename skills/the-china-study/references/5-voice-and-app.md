# Voice & App Context — The China Study

## The Book's Voice

T. Colin Campbell writes with the authority of a lifetime of research and the passion of a convert. He started his career promoting high-protein diets before his own research convinced him otherwise. "Dr. Campbell's exposé of the research and medical establishment makes this book a fascinating read."

The book is part scientific treatise, part memoir, part exposé. Campbell positions himself as a whistleblower — a scientist who followed the evidence where it led, even when it contradicted everything he was taught.

## Book Cases That Define the Voice

**Case 1: The Rat Study —** Campbell's own laboratory research. Rats fed 20% casein developed tumors. Rats fed 5% casein did not. "The impact of these findings is enormous." This single experiment changed Campbell's life and career.

**Case 2: The China Rural-Urban Divide —** Rural Chinese eating traditional plant-based diets had dramatically lower rates of breast cancer, heart disease, and diabetes than urban Chinese eating Western diets. Case: "The geographic variation in disease rates within China correlated with diet, not genetics."

## Key Quotes

- "Genetics loads the gun, but diet pulls the trigger." — T. Colin Campbell
- "The science is conclusive." — T. Colin Campbell
- "Rural Chinese populations eating traditional plant-based diets had dramatically lower rates of chronic disease." — T. Colin Campbell
- "If it has a mother or a face, don't eat it. If it came from a plant, eat it." — T. Colin Campbell (commonly quoted summary)
- "Nutrients work synergistically in whole foods. Isolated supplements often have opposite effects." — T. Colin Campbell

## Heardly Context

This skill is for users interested in nutrition, plant-based eating, disease prevention, or chronic disease reversal. The voice should be evidence-based and passionate but not dogmatic. Acknowledge that the dietary recommendations are demanding.
