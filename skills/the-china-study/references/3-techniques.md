# Techniques — The China Study

## How to Eat for Optimal Health

### Technique 1: The Whole-Food Plant-Based Plate
Fill your plate with: fruits, vegetables, whole grains, legumes, nuts and seeds. No animal products. No processed foods. No added oils. "If it has a mother or a face, don't eat it. If it came from a plant, eat it." Case: Rural Chinese farmers eating rice, vegetables, and soy — with virtually no Western diseases.

### Technique 2: The Protein Shift
Reduce total protein intake to 10% of calories. Source protein exclusively from plants. "The casein-cancer link is dose-dependent: the more casein, the more cancer." Case: Campbell's rat study — 20% casein fed tumors, 5% casein starved them.

### Technique 3: The Prevention Assessment
Look at disease rates in populations eating different diets. "If a disease exists at 1/100th the rate in another population eating a different diet, the cause is likely dietary." Case: Rural China vs. urban America — breast cancer rates correlated with animal product consumption.

### Technique 4: The Reversal Challenge
For existing chronic disease, adopt the whole-food plant-based diet strictly for 4-12 weeks. Measure changes: cholesterol, blood pressure, blood sugar, weight. Case: Patients who followed this approach reversed heart disease and normalized blood sugar.

### Technique 5: The Supplement Skepticism
Don't rely on supplements. "Nutrients work synergistically in whole foods. Isolated supplements often have opposite effects." Case: Beta-carotene supplements increased lung cancer risk in smokers, while beta-carotene-rich vegetables protected against it.

### Technique 6: The Industry Bias Detector
When you read nutrition advice, ask: "Who funds this research? Who benefits from this recommendation?" Case: The dairy industry funded studies that "proved" milk is essential — studies that Campbell's research contradicted.

### Technique 7: The Long Game
Dietary change is not a quick fix — it's a lifelong approach. "The most important changes take time to manifest. But the evidence shows they are worth it." Case: Chinese populations eating traditional diets for generations had consistently low disease rates.
