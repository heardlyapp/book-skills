# Principles: Water, Flight, and Anatomy

> Source: The Science of Leonardo, Part Two

## Water and Flow

Leonardo was obsessed with water. He studied rivers, turbulence, vortices, and the movement of water for decades.

> **Case: The Turbulence Drawings** (Chapter 5): Leonardo's drawings of turbulent water are scientifically accurate centuries before fluid dynamics existed. He observed how water swirls, spirals, and flows — patterns he later saw in blood, air, and hair.

> **Key takeaway**: Leonardo saw water as the fundamental force shaping the earth. His water studies are masterpieces of scientific observation.

## Flight

Leonardo spent decades studying bird flight, hoping to build a flying machine.

> **Case: The Bird Anatomy** (Chapter 6): Leonardo dissected birds to understand the mechanics of flight. He studied wing shape, feather structure, and the movement of air. He understood lift and drag centuries before they were formalized.

> **Key takeaway**: Leonardo's flight studies were scientifically rigorous. He failed to build a flying machine because he lacked lightweight materials, not understanding.

## Anatomy

Leonardo dissected over 30 human bodies, creating the most accurate anatomical drawings of his era.

> **Case: The Heart Valves** (Chapter 7): Leonardo described the function of heart valves with extraordinary accuracy. He understood blood flow and arterial function centuries before Harvey.

> **Key takeaway**: Leonardo's anatomy was not just art — it was science. His dissections revealed how the body works.
