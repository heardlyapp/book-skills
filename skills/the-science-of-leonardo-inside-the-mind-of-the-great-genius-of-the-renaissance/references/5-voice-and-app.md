# Voice and Application: Capra's Approach

> Source: The Science of Leonardo, throughout

## Capra's Voice

Fritjof Capra writes as both a physicist and a systems thinker. He brings a unique perspective: he understands both modern science and Leonardo's different approach. His tone is passionate and reverent — not towards Leonardo the man but towards Leonardo's way of knowing.

> **Case: The Systems Lens** (throughout): Capra views Leonardo through modern systems theory. He shows Leonardo's approach is more compatible with modern ecology than Newton was. Leonardo saw the earth as alive.

> **Case: The Newton Contrast** (Introduction): Capra argues that judging Leonardo by Newton's standards misses the point. Leonardo's science was qualitative and visual, not mathematical. Both are valid ways of knowing.

> **Key takeaway**: Capra sees Leonardo as a kindred spirit — a systems thinker before the term existed.

## Application: Observation

Slow down. Look carefully. Leonardo trusted his eyes above all. Modern science needs more observation.

## Application: Integration

Don't separate disciplines. Art, science, engineering — Leonardo didn't separate them. Neither should you.

## Application: Patterns

Look for recurring patterns. The spiral in water is the spiral in your ear. Nature repeats itself.

## Key Quotes

- "Leonardo's science was a science of qualities, not quantities."
- "He saw patterns where others saw chaos."
- "The earth is a living organism — with a circulatory system like our own."
- "Study the science of art and the art of science."
- "Everything comes from everything and everything is made of everything."
