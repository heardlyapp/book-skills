# Core Framework: Leonardo's Science

> Source: The Science of Leonardo, Introduction and Part One

## A Different Kind of Science

Capra argues Leonardo's science was not a failed precursor to Newton. It was a fundamentally different approach — qualitative, visual, and systemic rather than quantitative and mathematical.

> **Case: The Self-Portrait Observer** (Introduction): Leonardo's self-portrait shows an old man who has spent a lifetime observing. Capra uses it to frame Leonardo's method: trust your eyes, observe directly, see the patterns that connect all things.

> **Key takeaway**: Leonardo's science was different from Newton's — not inferior, but a complementary approach. He asked different questions about patterns and relationships rather than quantities and forces. Both ways of knowing are valid.

## The Notebooks

Over 6,000 pages of observations, sketches, and ideas. Leonardo's true legacy as a scientist.

> **Case: The Mirror Script** (Chapter 1): Leonardo wrote backwards in mirror writing. Not a code but practical for a left-handed writer. The notebooks reveal a mind constantly questioning, connecting, learning.

> **Case: The Unfinished Synthesis** (Chapter 1): Leonardo planned to publish his findings but never did. He was always more interested in discovering new things than organizing what he already knew.

> **Key takeaway**: The notebooks are Leonardo's masterpiece as a scientist. They document a mind that never stopped asking questions and making connections between everything it observed.
