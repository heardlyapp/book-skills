# Techniques: The Shooting and Survival

> Source: Serpico, Part II

## The Botched Drug Bust

February 3, 1971. Serpico and three other officers raid a building in Williamsburg, Brooklyn, for a drug bust. Inside, Serpico is shot in the face at close range.

> **Case: The Door Opens** (Part II): The officers enter the apartment. Serpico is first through the door. A man emerges from a bedroom with a gun. Serpico is shot in the face. The other officers — who were right behind him — do not fire. They do not call for backup.

> **Key takeaway:** The shooting was suspicious. Fellow officers who should have been right behind Serpico were inexplicably delayed. The failure to call for backup may have been deliberate.

## The Cover-Up

Serpico survived surgery and extensive facial reconstruction. The NYPD investigated the shooting. The investigation was superficial.

> **Case: The Hospital Room** (Part II): Serpico wakes up in the hospital. A police official visits him. The official suggests the shooting was Serpico's fault — he was too aggressive. Serpico realizes the NYPD is building a narrative to protect itself.

> **Key takeaway:** Even after nearly dying, the institution was more concerned with covering itself than with justice. The cover-up continued even as Serpico lay in a hospital bed.

## The Aftermath

Serpico recovered but was permanently damaged — physically and psychologically. He retired from the NYPD and moved to Switzerland.

> **Case: The Retreat** (Part II): Serpico leaves the United States. He can't live in New York anymore. The city that he tried to make better became impossible to inhabit. He finds peace in a remote village.

> **Key takeaway:** Serpico did his job and paid a terrible price. He changed the NYPD, but the system that nearly killed him remained unreconciled.
