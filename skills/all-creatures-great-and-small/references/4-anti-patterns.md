## Anti-Pattern 1: Overconfidence

The worst mistakes come from thinking you know more than you do.

**Case: The Textbook Confidence.** Herriot arrived in Yorkshire thinking his education had prepared him for everything. It had not. The real world was messier, harder, and more complicated than any textbook.

**Case: The Farmer Who Knew Better.** When a farmer contradicted Herriot's diagnosis, Herriot sometimes dismissed his opinion. He learned that the farmer often knew more than he did.

## Anti-Pattern 2: Going It Alone

Herriot tried to handle everything himself. He learned to ask for help.

**Case: The Impossible Calving.** Herriot nearly exhausted himself trying to deliver a calf alone. Finally, he called for help. A neighboring farmer came and together they succeeded.

**Case: The Siegfried Rescue.** When Herriot was stuck on a case, Siegfried would sometimes take over. It was humiliating — but necessary.

## Anti-Pattern 3: Taking It Personally

Herriot took every death personally at first. He learned that not every animal could be saved.

**Case: The Lost Calf.** When a calf died despite his best efforts, Herriot blamed himself. The farmer told him: "You did your best, lad. That's all anyone can do."

**Case: The Euthanasia Guilt.** Putting an animal down was always hard. Herriot learned that sometimes the kindest thing was to end suffering.
