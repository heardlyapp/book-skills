# Practical Lessons — Techniques

## Technique 1: Prepare for the Worst

Herriot learned that every call could be a disaster. He packed his bag carefully, checked his equipment, and prepared for anything.

**How to do it:** Before any critical task, check your equipment. What could go wrong? What would you need if it did? Herriot carries ropes, calving instruments, medication, and clean water.

**Case: The Frozen Rope.** Herriot once arrived at a calving to find his ropes frozen stiff. He had to thaw them in the farmer's kitchen before he could begin. After that, he always carried extra equipment.

## Technique 2: Earn Trust Slowly

The farmers of the Dales did not trust strangers. Herriot earned their trust one case at a time.

**How to do it:** Do not expect trust immediately. Show up. Do good work. Be honest about what you know and what you do not. Trust comes from consistency over time.

**Case: The Silent Farmer.** A farmer watched Herriot work without saying a word for weeks. Then, one day, he offered him a cup of tea. It was a sign of acceptance.

**Case: The Referral.** When a farmer called Herriot a second time, it meant he had earned his trust. Each repeat call was a vote of confidence.

## Technique 3: Admit When You Are Wrong

Herriot made mistakes. He misdiagnosed, he failed, he was wrong. He admitted it.

**How to do it:** When you are wrong, say so. Do not make excuses. Do not blame the patient. The farmer will respect honesty more than false confidence.

**Case: The Misdiagnosis.** Herriot once treated a horse for the wrong condition. The farmer had to call him back. Herriot admitted the mistake and treated the horse correctly.

## Technique 4: Find Joy in the Work

Herriot loved his work despite the hardship. The joy was in the animals, the people, the landscape, the humor.

**How to do it:** Focus on what you love about your work. The hard parts are real. But so are the rewards. Herriot found joy in a calf safely delivered, a grateful farmer, a beautiful morning in the Dales.

**Case: The Spring Morning.** After a long winter of exhausting calls, Herriot describes a spring morning in the Dales — the green hills, the new lambs, the sense of renewal. The beauty of the work sustained him.
