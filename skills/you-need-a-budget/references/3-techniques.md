# 3. Techniques — Budgeting in Practice

> Source: "You Need a Budget" by Jesse Mecham (2017). Practical budgeting techniques.

## Setting Up Your Categories

Mecham recommends organizing categories into three groups:
- **Immediate Obligations**: Rent, utilities, insurance, minimum debt payments
- **True Expenses**: Car maintenance, medical, gifts, annual subscriptions — broken down into monthly amounts
- **Savings and Fun**: Emergency fund, retirement, dining out, entertainment

> **Case: The Category Audit** (Chapter 4): Mecham recommends a monthly "category audit" — reviewing your categories and adjusting them based on actual spending patterns. If you consistently overspend on dining out, your category is too small, not your willpower too weak.
> **Key takeaway**: Categories should reflect reality, not aspiration. A budget based on what you wish you spent is a budget that will fail.

## The Monthly Budgeting Cycle

1. **Plan**: Before the month starts, assign every dollar to a category
2. **Track**: Enter every transaction as it happens
3. **Review**: At month end, review and adjust
4. **Repeat**: Every month, every year

## Debt Payoff Strategy

YNAB recommends the debt snowball: pay minimums on all debts, throw extra money at the smallest debt first. The psychological wins keep you motivated.

## The YNAB App Integration

While the method works on paper, the YNAB software syncs with bank accounts, imports transactions, and calculates Rule 4 (Age Your Money) automatically.

> **Case: The Automation Benefit** (Appendix): Mecham notes that users who sync their bank accounts and use the mobile app stick with budgeting 3x longer than those who don't. Automation reduces the friction of tracking.
> **Key takeaway**: Use tools that reduce friction. The best budgeting system is the one you will actually use.
