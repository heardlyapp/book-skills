# 1. Core Framework — The 4 Rules

> Source: "You Need a Budget" by Jesse Mecham (2017). The core framework is the 4 Rules.

## The Four Rules

YNAB's framework is elegantly simple: four rules that transform your relationship with money.

### Rule 1: Give Every Dollar a Job
Before the month begins, assign every dollar to a category. Zero-based budgeting: income minus outflows equals zero.

> **Case: The First Budget** (Chapter 2): Mecham describes creating his first budget as a college student. He had $500 and assigned every dollar: $200 for food, $100 for rent, $50 for gas, $50 for fun, $100 for savings. The act of assigning transformed his relationship with money.
> **Key takeaway**: A budget is not about restriction. It is about intentionality. When every dollar has a job, you are in control.

### Rule 2: Embrace Your True Expenses
Big, irregular expenses are not emergencies. Car repairs, Christmas, annual insurance — they are predictable.

> **Case: The Car Repair** (Chapter 3): When Mecham's car needed $1,200 in repairs, it wasn't an emergency — it was a true expense he had been saving for. He had been setting aside $100/month for car maintenance. The repair was covered.
> **Key takeaway**: True expenses are not surprises. They are predictable events you chose not to prepare for.

### Rule 3: Roll with the Punches
If you overspend, move money from another category. Adjust your budget. Don't abandon it.

### Rule 4: Age Your Money
The goal: spend money you earned 30+ days ago. When your money is old, you are no longer living paycheck to paycheck.
