# 2. Principles — Money Mindset

> Source: "You Need a Budget" by Jesse Mecham (2017). Financial mindset shifts.

## The 7 Principles

### Principle 1: Money Is a Tool
Money is not a goal. It is a tool for achieving the life you want. A budget is a plan for using that tool effectively.

### Principle 2: Intentionality Beats Restriction
A budget is not about saying no. It is about knowing what you are saying yes to.

> **Case: The Fun Category** (Chapter 6): Mecham insists that budgets include a "Fun" category. If you don't budget for fun, you will overspend on fun guiltily. A budget that doesn't allow enjoyment is a budget that will fail.
> **Key takeaway**: A realistic budget includes money for things you enjoy. Denial is not sustainable.

### Principle 3: Predictability Is Power
True expenses (car repairs, medical bills, holidays) are predictable. Preparing for them transforms them from crises to minor adjustments.

### Principle 4: Flexibility Is Not Failure
Rolling with the punches is Rule 3. Changing your budget is not giving up. It is staying in control.

### Principle 5: Debt Is a Symptom
Debt is not the problem. It is a symptom of living beyond your means or not preparing for true expenses. Fix the budget, and the debt will follow.

### Principle 6: Community Matters
Budgeting is easier with support. Spouses, partners, YNAB community — sharing the journey makes it sustainable.

### Principle 7: Small Steps Lead to Big Changes
You don't need to fix everything at once. Start with one month. Then another. The habits compound.

> **Case: The Paycheck-to-Paycheck Break** (Chapter 6): Mecham describes the moment his family's money reached 30 days old. They were spending money earned a month ago. The feeling of being ahead — even by one month — transformed their relationship with money.
> **Key takeaway**: Aging your money is not just financial — it is psychological. Being ahead changes how you feel about money.
