# Techniques: The 14-Day Program

> Source: Breathe, Week 1-2

## Week 1: Awareness and Foundation

Days 1-7 focus entirely on awareness. The goal is not perfect breathing — it's noticing the difference between how you breathe now and how you could breathe. Learn the diaphragm. Feel it for the first time. Most clients report this alone changes their relationship to their own body.

> **Case: The Book Exercise** (Introduction): Lie on your back, place a book on your belly, breathe so the book rises and falls. This is the single most effective exercise for learning diaphragmatic breathing. Vranich recommends 5 minutes, twice daily for the entire first week. Most people first feel their belly move on day 3 or 4.

> **Case: The Hand Cue**: During the day, touch your belly as a reminder to breathe into it. This creates a physical anchor. Vranich suggests setting phone reminders every 2 hours in week 1. Just one conscious belly breath can reset your nervous system.

## Week 2: Resistance and Integration

Days 8-14 add resistance and integrate correct breathing into daily activities — walking, climbing stairs, exercise. The diaphragm strengthens quickly with practice.

> **Case: The Straw Breathing**: Breathe in through nose, out through a narrow straw. This creates back-pressure (positive end-expiratory pressure) that strengthens the diaphragm and improves CO2 tolerance. 5 minutes daily.

> **Case: Breath Holds for CO2 Tolerance**: After a normal exhale, pinch your nose and hold until you feel a moderate urge to breathe. Start at 10-15 seconds. Work up to 30-40 seconds over the second week. This directly increases your BOLT score.
