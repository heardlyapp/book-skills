# Principles: The Exhale and CO2 Tolerance

> Source: Breathe, Week 1-2

## The Exhale is the Relaxation Switch

Lengthening the exhale activates the parasympathetic nervous system. Inhale stimulates the sympathetic system (alertness). Exhale stimulates the parasympathetic (calm). This is the physiological basis for every breathing meditation technique in existence.

> **Case: The Anxious Client** (throughout): Vranich's clients with anxiety are almost always chest breathers. Their inhale is longer than their exhale — stuck in sympathetic mode. The first intervention: extend the exhale to 6-8 seconds. Most report feeling calmer within 60 seconds of their first attempt.

> **Key takeaway**: If you're anxious, your exhale is probably too short. Lengthen it. 4 seconds in, 6 seconds out. That's the simplest and most effective breathing exercise.

## CO2 Tolerance (BOLT Score)

CO2 is not waste. It's necessary for oxygen release from hemoglobin — the Bohr effect. Without adequate CO2, hemoglobin holds onto oxygen instead of releasing it to cells. Over-breathing reduces CO2 and paradoxically reduces oxygen delivery.

> **Case: The Asthma Client** (throughout): Clients with asthma often have BOLT scores under 10 seconds. Retraining breathing — focusing on nose breathing and building CO2 tolerance through breath holds — can significantly reduce symptoms and reliance on rescue inhalers.

> **Key takeaway**: If you can't comfortably hold your breath for 20 seconds after a normal exhale, your breathing efficiency is low. The 14-day program increases this score through progressive exercises.
