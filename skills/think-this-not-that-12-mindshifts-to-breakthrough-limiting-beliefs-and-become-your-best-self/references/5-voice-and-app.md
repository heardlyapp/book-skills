# Voice and Application

## Overview

Axe writes as a coach — direct, personal, grounded in his own stories. The book draws from three domains: functional medicine (how beliefs affect physical health), entrepreneurial leadership (how mindset affects team performance), and Christian spirituality (how faith shapes identity and purpose). Each chapter opens with a personal story — the laughing teacher, the failed business, his mother's cancer, his own spinal infection.

## Application: Personal Growth

Use the 12 mindshifts as a 12-week curriculum. Start with Mindshift 1 (memory transplant). Then move to Mindshift 4 (find your why). These two provide the foundation.

**Case — Axe's own sequence:** He didn't start rebuilding after his business failure with strategy or networking. He started with Mindshift 1 (replacing the belief that "I failed, therefore I'm a failure") and Mindshift 5 (rewriting his story from victim to hero). Only then did he move to practical action.

## Application: Health and Wellness

Axe saw mindset as the foundation of physical health. Patients with a "becoming" mindset (who focused on who they were becoming) maintained their results. Those focused only on outcomes (losing weight, lowering blood pressure) relapsed.

**Case — The mother who healed:** Axe's mother combined nutritional changes with visualization, gratitude, and prayer. Her tumors shrank 52 percent in four months. The mindset work was as important as the dietary changes.

**Case — The stress epidemic:** With 75-90 percent of doctor visits stress-related, the fear flip (Mindshift 10) is the most immediately applicable health principle.

## Application: Career and Business

Entrepreneurs face constant uncertainty and rejection. The mindshifts provide a framework for maintaining motivation.

**Case — From clinic to Ancient Nutrition:** Axe moved from seeing patients to building a global supplement brand. Key mindshifts: M2 (becoming over accomplishing), M6 (build a community), M10 (flip fear of failure).

**Case — The failed business lesson:** The failure that cost him "every penny of savings" taught him investment principles, the importance of team, and the value of data-driven decisions.

## Application: Leadership

Leaders who practice the mindshifts create healthier, more productive organizations. Key mindshifts: M3 (self-awareness through SWOT), M6 (building community), M9 (principles over opinions).

**Case — The multiplication of mentorship:** Axe's mentor created a group. Axe created his own. His wife created hers. His friend created his. Each group multiplied, scaling impact exponentially.

## Application: Relationships

Limiting beliefs about relationships become self-fulfilling prophecies. The key is M1 (identifying the belief) and M5 (rewriting the story).

**Case — The men's group impact:** Axe's community of greatness taught him how to be a better husband and father. "This group has helped me become a better husband by teaching me how to serve and love my wife better."
