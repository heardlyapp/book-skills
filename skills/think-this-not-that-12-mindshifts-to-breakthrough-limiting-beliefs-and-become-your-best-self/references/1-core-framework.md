# Core Framework: The 12 Mindshifts

## Overview

Josh Axe's framework is built around 12 specific mindshifts — conscious shifts from limiting beliefs to empowering ones. Each chapter follows the same structure: identify the false belief, understand where it came from, challenge it with evidence, and replace it with a truthful belief. The book draws from Axe's work as a functional medicine physician, his entrepreneurial experience building multimillion-dollar businesses, his personal health crisis (spinal infection that left him unable to walk for a year), and his Christian faith.

## The 12 Mindshifts

**1. Create a Breakthrough by Unlimiting Your Beliefs** — Memory transplant technique. ABCDE model (Albert Ellis). Nocebo effect case: the man diagnosed with terminal liver cancer who died within the predicted timeframe despite having only a small, non-life-threatening tumor. Jamie Kern Lima case: investor told her no one would buy makeup from her; L'Oréal bought IT Cosmetics for $1.2 billion.

**2. Redefine Success by Becoming, Not Accomplishing** — Grandfather Howard's funeral: no wealth or fame, but called "best friend" by dozens. Success = maximizing unique skill for the good of others. Lance Armstrong case: accomplishments without character leads to collapse. *Journal of Research in Personality* study: material goals produce less life satisfaction.

**3. Become Self-Aware to Get Where You Need to Go** — Tasha Eurich's research: 90 percent think they're self-aware; only 10-15 percent are. The Batman Effect study: children who took on superhero identity worked 23 percent better. SWOT analysis adapted for personal growth. Four barriers: pride, busyness, character sabotage, entertainment addiction.

**4. Find a Why or Risk Wasting It All** — Axe's mother's stage 4 breast cancer diagnosis at 41 became his purpose: food as medicine. Her tumors shrank 52 percent in four months. Japanese ikigai framework. STARS goal-setting method (Specific, Trackable, Accountable, Realistic, Significant).

**5. Rewrite Your Role in the Story** — Harriet Tubman: born into slavery, beatings, lifelong seizures. Rather than remain victim, she became conductor of the Underground Railroad. Joseph Campbell's 8-stage hero's journey adapted: Comfort → Awaken → Team → Quest → Trials → Transformation → Triumph → Return with Wisdom. Four character types: victim, villain, hero, guide.

**6. Assemble a Team to Fulfill Your Dreams** — Community of greatness: 6-8 people sharing purpose and values. Shawn Johnson East & Andrew East story: chance meeting in a Nashville coffee shop led to a four-person community. Dunbar's number: humans maintain ~150 relationships, with 5 in the core group. J.R.R. Tolkien and C.S. Lewis: the Inklings group that made Lord of the Rings and Narnia possible.

**7. Build an Unshakable Identity** — Three identity foundations: modern (sand), traditional (soil), divine (stone). Michael Jordan as childhood identity model. Carl Jung quote: "The world will ask you who you are, and if you don't know, the world will tell you."

**8. Bust Vices by Building Virtues** — Seven virtues (wisdom, justice, self-control, courage, faith, hope, love) vs. seven vices (pride, envy, wrath, greed, lust, gluttony, sloth). Axe's virtue card method: wrote "encouragement" on an index card, practiced it daily. True story of Michelangelo: "Every block of stone has a statue inside it."

**9. Turn Off Opinions and Turn On Principles** — 10 life principles including: recognize the wise by their results, you reap what you sow, the greatest of all is the servant of all, love (agape) wins. Warren Buffett example: ignoring media hype, investing based on principles. Good fruit vs. rotten fruit test for evaluating mentors.

**10. Flip the Fear to Turn On the Growth** — Flourishing mindset vs. fear-based mindset. Carol Dweck study: children praised for effort outperform those praised for intelligence. 75-90 percent of doctor visits are stress-related. Axe's business failure that led to Ancient Nutrition.

**11. Visualize to Realize** — Sara Blakely visualized being on Oprah before it happened. Basketball free-throw study: mental rehearsal produced 23 percent improvement, nearly matching physical practice's 24 percent. Vision board method. Time-boxing technique. Stop-Start-Keep system.

**12. Power Up Your Potential with Positive Perseverance** — Axe's spinal infection: couldn't walk for a year, doctors warned of permanent disability. He chose hope, grit, and gratitude. Charles Snyder's hope theory: goals + pathways + agency. Angela Duckworth's grit research. Deion Sanders: attempted suicide at peak, transformed to coaching young men's character. Viktor Frankl's concentration camp story: the woman who found hope in a tree branch with two blossoms.
