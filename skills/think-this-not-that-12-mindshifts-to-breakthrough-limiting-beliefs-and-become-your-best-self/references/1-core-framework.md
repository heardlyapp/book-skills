# Core Framework: The 12 Mindshifts

## Overview

Josh Axe's framework is built around 12 specific mindshifts — conscious shifts from limiting beliefs to empowering ones. Each chapter follows the same structure: identify the false belief, understand where it came from, challenge it with evidence, and replace it with a truthful, empowering belief. The book draws from Axe's work as a functional medicine physician, his entrepreneurial experience building multimillion-dollar businesses, his master's degree in leadership from Johns Hopkins, and his Christian faith.

## The 12 Mindshifts

### Mindshift 1: Create a Breakthrough by Unlimiting Your Beliefs
**Not That:** "I can't because..." → **Think This:** "I can because..."

**Key techniques:** Memory transplant, ABCDE model (Albert Ellis), pain-pleasure reframe.

**Case — The memory transplant:** After Axe's college teacher Mrs. Williams gave him an A+ and said he should consider writing, he experienced what he calls a "memory transplant" — replacing the old limiting belief ("I'm not smart") with an unlimited one ("I am smart. I can do this."). He put the A+ paper on his bathroom mirror for daily reinforcement.

### Mindshift 2: Redefine Success by Becoming, Not Accomplishing
**Not That:** "My value comes from what I achieve." → **Think This:** "My value comes from who I become."

**Key concepts:** Becoming over doing. Character development as the real measure of success.

**Case — Business teams:** Team members who focused on personal growth outperformed those focused on revenue targets. The former built sustainable skills; the latter burned out.

### Mindshift 3: Become Self-Aware to Get Where You Need to Go
**Not That:** "I know what I'm doing." → **Think This:** "I need to understand myself better."

**Four barriers to self-awareness:** Pride, busyness, character sabotage, entertainment addiction.

**Case — SWOT analysis:** Axe adapted the Stanford business tool for personal development — assessing Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats across career, relationships, faith, finances, and health.

### Mindshift 4: Find a Why or Risk Wasting It All
**Not That:** "I'm just going through the motions." → **Think This:** "I have a purpose that gives meaning to my efforts."

**Case — Axe's mother's cancer:** Stage 4 breast cancer diagnosis inspired Axe to study functional medicine. Her subsequent healing through diet and mindset became the foundation of his life's purpose.

### Mindshift 5: Rewrite Your Role in the Story
**Not That:** "I am what happened to me." → **Think This:** "I am the author of my own story."

**Case — From failure to Ancient Nutrition:** Axe's failed business cost him his life savings and nearly ended his career. He rewrote the story from "I failed" to "I learned what doesn't work" and launched the business that became Ancient Nutrition.

### Mindshift 6: Assemble a Team to Fulfill Your Dreams
**Not That:** "I have to do this alone." → **Think This:** "I need a community of greatness."

**Key numbers:** 76% believe mentors are important; only 37% have one. Mentored employees are promoted 5x more often. Axe recommends groups of 6-8 people.

**Case — The multiplication formula:** Axe's mentor group led to him starting his own group, which led to his friend starting a group, and his wife starting one. The formula: 1+1=3.

### Mindshift 7: Build an Unshakable Identity
**Not That:** "I am what others say I am." → **Think This:** "I am who I choose to be."

**Three foundations:** Modern identity (sand — based on feelings and culture), Traditional identity (soil — based on family and duty), Divine identity (stone — based on higher power and unchanging principles).

### Mindshift 8: Bust Vices by Building Virtues
**Not That:** "I need to stop this bad habit." → **Think This:** "I need to build the opposing virtue."

**The principle:** "We are what we repeatedly do; therefore, virtue is a habit." Instead of fighting laziness, build diligence. Instead of fighting dishonesty, build integrity.

### Mindshift 9: Turn Off Opinions and Turn On Principles
**Not That:** "What will people think?" → **Think This:** "What do my principles say?"

**10 life principles:** Recognize the wise by their results. Model the wise. You reap what you sow. The word is mightier than the sword. The greatest is the servant of all. Find the truth. Iron sharpens iron. Virtue is a habit. Be the change. Love (agape) wins.

**Case — Warren Buffett:** Buffett's secret is ignoring opinions, trends, and media hype and investing based on simple principles: great people, great products, great potential for profit.

### Mindshift 10: Flip the Fear to Turn On the Growth
**Not That:** "Fear means I should stop." → **Think This:** "Fear means I'm growing."

**Key concepts:** Fear-based mindset vs. flourishing mindset. Carol Dweck's growth mindset research. 75-90% of doctor visits are stress-related.

### Mindshift 11: Visualize to Realize
**Not That:** "I'll believe it when I see it." → **Think This:** "I'll see it when I believe it."

**Key technique:** Daily visualization of desired outcomes, combined with gratitude and prayer.

**Case — Mother's healing:** Axe's mother used visualization, gratitude, and prayer alongside dietary changes during her cancer recovery. The combination of mindset medicine and nutritional medicine produced results that conventional treatment alone had failed to achieve.

### Mindshift 12: Power Up Your Potential with Positive Perseverance
**Not That:** "It's too hard, I quit." → **Think This:** "This is hard, but I'm growing."

**Key technique:** Process over performance. Celebrate small wins. Maintain long-term vision.

**Case — Sara Blakely (Spanx):** Her father encouraged her to celebrate failures. This mindset allowed her to persist through countless rejections and build a billion-dollar company.
