# Seven Principles in Depth

## Principle 1: Beliefs Are Self-Fulfilling Prophecies

Axe's own story is the clearest evidence. A high school teacher laughed at his dream of becoming a doctor. Combined with his 2.3 GPA and ADHD diagnosis, this created a belief that nearly destroyed his potential. The research cited: the average person has 50,000 thoughts per day, 95 percent on repeat (Wharton School). Dr. Henry Beecher's quantification of the placebo effect at Harvard: one-third of patients respond significantly to placebo or nocebo. The man who died from a non-life-threatening tumor because he believed he was dying. The MS patient who couldn't walk until she learned her diagnosis was wrong.

## Principle 2: Becoming Over Accomplishing

The attaining of accomplishments without character development is building on sand. Axe's grandfather Howard had nothing by society's standards — no wealth, no fame, no Forbes ranking. But dozens of people at his funeral called him their best friend. Axe's formula: Success = maximizing your unique skill for the good of others. The accomplishing mindset asks "What's next to achieve?" while the becoming mindset asks "Who am I becoming?" Research cited: a *Journal of Research in Personality* study of 147 college graduates found that those who valued material goals had less life satisfaction.

## Principle 3: Self-Awareness Is the Foundation of Growth

Tasha Eurich's research: 90 percent of people believe they are self-aware; only 10-15 percent actually are. The four barriers: pride (power begets pride), busyness (constant motion prevents reflection), character sabotage (vices create blinders), entertainment addiction (dopamine replaces growth). The Batman Effect study: children who took on a superhero identity showed 23 percent better work performance. Axe's personal SWOT analysis adapted from Stanford. Pennebaker's writing therapy: 15-20 minutes of expressive writing reduces anxiety.

## Principle 4: Purpose Gives Meaning to Suffering

Axe's mother's stage 4 breast cancer became his life's mission. The ikigai framework. Purpose must be revisited throughout life: Axe's purpose evolved from clinical practice, to natural health education (#1 website in its category), to supplement company (Ancient Nutrition), to leadership development (leaders.com). The STARS goal method. Without purpose, effort is scattered and motivation fades.

## Principle 5: Rewrite Your Story and Build Your Community

Harriet Tubman's transformation from enslaved to conductor of the Underground Railroad. Four character types: victim, villain, hero, guide. Axe's hero's journey 8-stage model. Community of greatness: 6-8 people sharing purpose and values. Dunbar's number: humans maintain 150 relationships, with 5 in the core group. J.R.R. Tolkien and C.S. Lewis: the Inklings community that produced Lord of the Rings and Narnia.

## Principle 6: Build Identity on Virtues and Principles

Three foundations: modern (sand), traditional (soil), divine (stone). Seven virtues vs. seven vices. The virtue card method: Axe practiced encouragement daily until it became habitual. 10 life principles including agape love. Warren Buffett ignores media and bets on principles. Michael Burry predicted the 2008 crash by recognizing broken economic principles.

## Principle 7: Fear Is a Growth Signal; Persevere Positively

Carol Dweck's fifth graders: praise for effort produces better outcomes than praise for intelligence. 75-90 percent of doctor visits are stress-related. Axe's spinal infection: told he might never walk again, he chose hope-grit-gratitude. Snyder's hope theory: goals + pathways + agency. Duckworth's grit: greater predictor of success than IQ. Deion Sanders: from suicide attempt to coaching young men in character.
