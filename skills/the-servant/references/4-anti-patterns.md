# Anti-Patterns · What Destroys Leadership

> Source: *The Servant*, full book

---

## 1. Using Power Instead of Authority

"I'm the boss, so do what I say." This creates compliance, not commitment. Your team will do the minimum required — and nothing more.

**Fix:** Ask "How can I serve this person?" instead of "How can I make this person do what I want?"

## 2. Selfishness

Putting your needs (recognition, credit, comfort) ahead of your team's. The servant leader puts others first.

**Fix:** Make your team's success your success. Celebrate their wins. Take responsibility for their failures.

## 3. Impatience

Quick to anger. Quick to judge. Quick to interrupt. Impatience signals that you value your time more than the other person's dignity.

**Fix:** Patience is the first principle for a reason. Without it, none of the others can operate. When you feel impatience rising, pause. Breathe. Choose differently.

## 4. Hypocrisy

Saying one thing, doing another. Leaders are watched constantly — your actions teach more than your words.

**Fix:** Align your behavior with your stated values. If you say "people matter," treat people as if they matter — even when it's inconvenient.

## 5. Blaming Others

When a leader blames the team, they lose authority. The leader is ultimately responsible for the conditions that led to the failure.

**Fix:** The next time something goes wrong, start with "What could I have done differently?" This single question transforms your leadership.

> **Case:** The executive who entered the retreat blaming his team for the division's poor performance. By the end, he realized: the division's problems started with his leadership. His blame was a defense mechanism hiding his own failures.
