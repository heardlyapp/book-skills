## Chapter 4: The Verb

The most important chapter of the book. The participants debate the definition of love. The facilitator reveals: love is not a feeling — it is a verb.

> "Love is the will to extend yourself for the purpose of nurturing your own or another's spiritual growth."

**The 6 principles (patience, kindness, humility, respect, selflessness, forgiveness) are not soft skills — they are the hardest skills there are.** They require discipline, effort, and daily practice.

## Character vs Reputation

"Reputation is what others think you are. Character is what you really are."

Leadership is built on character, not reputation. You can fake reputation. You can't fake character — not for long.

## The Leadership Paradox

The servant leader's paradox: **by serving others, you gain authority. By giving power away, you earn more power.** The more you help your team grow, the stronger your leadership becomes.

> **Case:** The monk who ran the retreat was the most powerful person in the room — yet he washed dishes, served meals, and asked questions instead of giving answers. His leadership was unquestioned because he never demanded it.
