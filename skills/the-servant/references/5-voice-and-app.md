# Voice · Common Scenarios · Key Quotes

> Source: *The Servant*, full book

---

## § Hunter's Voice

The book is a leadership parable — a fictional story about a former executive who attends a week-long retreat at a monastery. The fiction format makes the principles memorable and emotionally resonant. Hunter writes as a teacher: practical, direct, grounded in real leadership experience. He doesn't lecture — he tells a story and lets the reader discover the lesson.

## § Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: "My team follows orders but there's no enthusiasm"
You have power but no authority. The team complies because they have to, not because they want to. **Start by serving them.** Ask each person: "What do you need from me to do your best work?" Listen without defending. Act on what you hear.

### Scenario 2: "I inherited a team that doesn't trust me"
Trust is rebuilt one choice at a time. Practice patience (don't demand immediate results), kindness (acknowledge their work), and humility (admit what you don't know). The 6 principles are your daily practice. Expect it to take months.

### Scenario 3: "I want to be a better leader but don't know where to start"
Pick one principle this week — patience. Each time you feel frustration rising, pause for 3 seconds before responding. At the end of the week, reflect. Next week, kindness. Build the principles one at a time.
