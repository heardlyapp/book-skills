# Techniques · Daily Practices · The Leadership Test

> Source: *The Servant*, Chapters 4-5

---

## Daily Leadership Practices

**Start with yourself.** You cannot lead others until you lead yourself. Each day:

1. **Pick one principle** — Focus on patience today. Or kindness. Or humility. Practice it consciously.
2. **Ask serving questions** — "What does my team need from me today?" "Who can I help grow?"
3. **Take responsibility** — When something goes wrong, ask "What could I have done differently?" instead of blaming.
4. **Apologize quickly** — When you fail (and you will), apologize sincerely. This builds trust faster than never making mistakes.
5. **Reflect daily** — At the end of each day, review: Did I lead with authority or power? Did I serve or did I demand?

## The Leadership Test

> "The true test of leadership is: are people better off because they worked with you? Are they growing? Are they becoming better people?"

Apply this test to your leadership quarterly. If the answer is "no" or "I'm not sure," something needs to change.

## Developing the 6 Principles

Pick one principle each week. Practice it deliberately. Notice when you fail — and try again the next day. Over 6 weeks, you'll have practiced all 6. Then start the cycle again.

> **Case:** The executive who spent the entire retreat silent because he was afraid of looking weak. The facilitator didn't push him — he simply waited with patience. By the end, the executive spoke. The patience of the leader created the space for him to grow.
