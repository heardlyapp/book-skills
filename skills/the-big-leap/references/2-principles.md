## 7 Principles of the Big Leap

### 1. The Upper Limit Is Universal

Everyone has an internal thermostat. No one is immune. The question is not whether you have one — it is where yours is set.

### 2. The Zone of Genius Is Your Highest Contribution

Your genius zone is where your unique talents and deepest joy meet. It is the place where you create maximum value with minimum effort.

> **Case: The Professor Who Did Busywork** (Chapter 3): Hendricks describes a university professor who spent 90% of his time on administrative tasks (Zone of Competence) and only 10% on his research (Zone of Genius). When he inverted those percentages, his research output and happiness both increased dramatically.
> **Key takeaway**: Most people spend their lives in the wrong zone. The leap is not about working harder — it is about shifting where you spend your time.

### 3. Excellence Is a Trap

Zone of Excellence feels great — you get praised, you feel valuable. But it keeps you from your genius. Excellence is comfortable. Genius is scary.

### 4. The Four Hidden Fears Must Be Faced

The fear of being seen, the fear of expanding, the fear of being a burden, and the fear of being a separate self — these four fears create the upper limit. Face them.

### 5. Relationships Have Their Own Thermostats

Couples unconsciously regulate intimacy. When things get too good, one partner creates a problem. Awareness is the cure.

> **Case: The Couple Who Learned to Notice** (Chapter 8): Hendricks' own practice: after a peak experience, he and his wife learned to say: "We are upper limiting. Let's not create a problem. Let's just enjoy." The simple act of saying it stopped the pattern.
> **Key takeaway**: The most powerful relationship tool is shared awareness. When both partners know about the upper limit, they can choose not to trigger it.

### 6. Money and Body Are Upper Limit Clues

If you have money issues or chronic health issues that come and go, look at them as upper limit signals. Money ceilings and body symptoms often appear when you are about to expand.

### 7. The Leap Requires Vulnerability

Taking the leap to your Zone of Genius means being seen in a new way. It means being visible, taking up space, and making a contribution that is uniquely yours. This is terrifying — and necessary.
