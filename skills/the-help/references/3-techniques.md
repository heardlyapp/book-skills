# Techniques — Small Acts of Resistance, the Secret Project, and Surviving the System

## Practical Methods

> **Case: The Home Help Sanitation Initiative** (Mid-novel): Miss Hilly pushes for a law requiring white families to install separate bathrooms for their Black maids. It is humiliating, dehumanizing, and perfectly legal. The maids have no political power to stop it. Their resistance is not in the legislature. It is in the way they hold their heads, the way they go on working, the way Minny refuses to use the outhouse.
> **Key takeaway**: Not every act of resistance is public and political. Sometimes resistance is simply refusing to be humiliated. Minny's dignity is her weapon.

> **Case: The Secret Collaboration** (Throughout): Aibileen, Minny, and the other maids meet in secret. They cannot be seen together. They pass messages through Aibileen. They are building a bomb — a book that will explode Jackson's self-image. The secrecy is necessary and terrifying.
> **Key takeaway**: The most powerful movements often begin in secret. You do not need to announce what you are doing until you are ready.

> **Case: The Terrible Awful** (Minny's secret): Minny's worst act of resistance — a chocolate pie served to Miss Hilly with a hidden ingredient — is also her most effective. It is crude, it is disgusting, and it is the only thing that stops Hilly from destroying Minny's life. Minny uses the leverage. She does not apologize.
> **Key takeaway**: Sometimes the only power you have is the power to embarrass your enemy. Use it. You do not have to fight fair when the system is rigged against you.
