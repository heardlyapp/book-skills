# 2 — Prospecting Principles

> Source: "Eat What You Kill" by Sam Taggart

## The Four Customer Profiles

Taggart identifies four personality types that determine how you should approach, pitch, and close:

| Profile | Traits | Approach |
|---------|--------|----------|
| **Driver** | Decisive, impatient, results-focused | Get to the point fast. Don't waste time. Give them options and let them choose. |
| **Analyzer** | Detail-oriented, cautious, data-driven | Bring evidence. Explain the logic. Give them time to process. |
| **Passive** | Agreeable, avoids conflict, relationship-focused | Be warm and friendly. Don't push. Help them feel safe saying yes. |
| **Socializer** | Talkative, enthusiastic, people-focused | Match their energy. Let them talk. Make it fun. |

> **Case: Adapting to the Prospect** (Chapter 28-31): Taggart explains that a Driver will walk away if you spend 10 minutes on small talk. An Analyzer will distrust you if you can't answer technical questions. A Passive will feel pressured if you're too aggressive. A Socializer will get bored if you're too serious. The best carnivores learn to read the prospect within the first 30 seconds and adjust their approach.
> **Key takeaway**: One pitch does not fit all. Carnivores adapt their communication style to the prospect's personality type.

## Prospecting Strategy

1. **Define your ideal customer profile (ICP).** Who has the problem you solve? Who can afford you? Where do they exist?
2. **Create a prospecting strategy.** How will you reach them? Door knocking? Cold calling? Social media? Referrals? Use all seven doors.
3. **Use a CRM religiously.** Data is king. Track every interaction, every follow-up, every outcome.

## The Seven Doors to Reach Customers

1. Door knocking (D2D)
2. Cold calling
3. Social media prospecting
4. Email outreach
5. Referrals
6. Networking / events
7. Strategic partnerships

> **Case: The Four-Hour Late Pickup** (Introduction): On Taggart's first day at a D2D alarm company in Dallas, his manager Luke was supposed to pick him up at the airport but was four hours late — because he was too busy closing sales. Taggart shadowed Luke for 2 hours, watched him close 2 sales, then was dropped off at an apartment with 3 blank contracts and an assigned neighborhood. The next morning, he was a professional D2D rep with no training. "I didn't even know how an alarm system worked."
> **Key takeaway**: Carnivores are thrown into the deep end and learn to swim. The prospecting strategy is simple: find the door, knock on it, learn as you go.
