## Taggart's Voice

Sam Taggart is founder of D2D Experts, a sales training company that runs D2DCon (4,000+ attendees) and serves 10,000+ active monthly users on its online platform. His voice:
- **Direct and aggressive.** "Carnivore. Kill. Hunt. Prey." He uses aggressive metaphors deliberately — to shock complacent salespeople out of their passivity.
- **Empathetic underneath.** Despite the tough talk, he constantly reminds readers that sales is about serving, not exploiting. "You want them to love you so much that they'll recommend you to their friends."
- **Self-made credibility.** His story starts at age 7 selling candy. By 13 he was managing a team. He quit a $600K/year job to start a training company. He's not a theorist.

> **Case: Quitting $600K for a Dream** (Introduction): In 2017, Taggart quit his $600,000/year job as VP of Sales to start D2D Experts. "I felt a strong urge to do something bigger than just continuing to boost my sales income. I wanted to share my success as widely as possible." Most people would see $600K as the finish line. Taggart saw it as a starting point for something bigger.
> **Key takeaway**: True carnivores aren't motivated by comfort. They're driven by purpose, impact, and the thrill of the hunt. If you're only in sales for the money, you'll quit when it gets hard.

> **Case: The #1 Sales Skill Is Not What You Think** (Chapter 1, Mindset): Taggart opens Part One with a quote from Brian Tracy: "Sales success is 80 percent attitude and only 20 percent aptitude." He knew readers would be impatient for tactical content — but insisted the book starts with mindset. "Success has to start in your head before it can be manifested in reality." The reps who skip the mindset section are the ones who won't implement the techniques.
> **Key takeaway**: The hardest thing to change is not your technique — it's the story you tell yourself about who you are. Before you can become a carnivore, you must believe you are one.

## Application Scenario 1: Fear of Cold Calling

*"I freeze up before every cold call. My heart races, I stall, and sometimes I just don't dial."*

**Use:** Mindset (reference/1) + Opening (reference/3)

**Playbook:**
1. **30-second state change.** Before every call, stand up, breathe deep, and repeat: "I'm a carnivore. I'm here to help."
2. **The Mental Governor.** Your racing heart is your brain trying to "protect" you from rejection. Recognize it. Push through anyway. The governor resets after action.
3. **Selective amnesia.** After each call — win or lose — forget it immediately. The next prospect is a clean slate.
4. **Rejection is tuition.** Each "no" is a data point. Adjust your approach based on what you learn.

## Application Scenario 2: Prospecting Drought

*"I have no leads. The marketing team isn't delivering. What do I do?"*

**Use:** Carnivore mindset (reference/1) + Prospecting (reference/2)

**Playbook:**
1. Stop waiting. You're a carnivore — you hunt for your own food.
2. Use the Seven Doors. If door knocking isn't working, try cold calling. If cold calling isn't working, try social media. Use all seven.
3. Talk to existing customers for referrals. The best leads come from people who already trust you.
4. Don't prejudge who will buy. Your job is to present, not to decide for the prospect.

## Application Scenario 3: Prospect Says "Not Interested"

*"Every time I start my pitch, they cut me off with 'not interested.'"*

**Use:** Opening (reference/3) + Objection Handling (reference/4)

**Playbook:**
1. **The Pullback.** Give them an out: "Totally fair. If this isn't for you, I'll be on my way." This disarms resistance.
2. **The Transition.** Follow with: "Can I ask you a quick question?" A question is easier to say yes to than a pitch.
3. **Identify the real objection.** Is it a deal-breaker, smoke screen, or true objection? Probe gently.
4. **Selective hearing.** Don't check out after the first "no." The real signal might come right after the resistance.

## Application Scenario 4: Need to Close

*"I get to the end of the presentation and I don't know how to ask for the sale."**

**Use:** Closing (reference/5)

**Playbook:**
1. **Tie-Downs.** Throughout the pitch, use Tie-Downs to check: "Does that make sense?" "Would that be valuable?" By the end, you've already closed in 10 small steps.
2. **The Yes Train.** Get a series of small "yeses" leading to the final yes.
3. **Assume the close.** Stop asking "Would you like to buy?" Start saying "Great, let's get you started." Fill out the order form.
4. **Ask for referrals immediately after the close.** Right when they're happiest.

## Application Scenario 5: Sales Slump

*"I was on fire last month. This month I can't close anything. What's wrong?"**

**Use:** Mindset (reference/1) + Prey Drive (reference/1)

**Playbook:**
1. **Emotional stability.** Carnivores maintain the same emotional state whether they're on a hot streak or a cold streak. Don't get high on wins or low on losses.
2. **Back to basics.** Are you prospecting enough? Are you using your opening? Are you doing the fundamentals?
3. **Check the Mental Governor.** You might be unconsciously limiting yourself. Push harder today. One more door.
4. **Activate your prey drive.** Read your goals. Visualize your why. Remember why you chose sales. The fire is still there — you just need to relight it.
