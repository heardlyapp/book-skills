# The Wilderness Years

> Source: Churchill, Part One, Chapters 14-20

## The Prophet Ignored

### The 1930s

| Year | Churchill's Warning | Government Response |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|
| 1933 | Hitler is a threat | Disarmament continues |
| 1935 | Germany is rearming | Appeasement begins |
| 1938 | Munich is a disaster | Chamberlain returns with "peace for our time" |
| 1939 | War is inevitable | Poland invaded |

> **Case: The Voice in the Wilderness (Chapters 15-18)**
> Throughout the 1930s, Churchill was a voice in the wilderness. He wrote articles, gave speeches, warned Parliament. Germany is rearming. Hitler is dangerous. Britain must rearm. He was ignored, mocked, and dismissed as a warmonger. The Conservative Party would not have him. His own constituency was hostile. He was isolated — but he never stopped warning. When war came in 1939, Churchill was proven right. The country turned to him.
> **Key takeaway:** The wilderness years are the most important period of Churchill's life. Being right was not enough. He had to survive being right while being ignored. The lesson: if you are right about something important, do not stop saying it — even if no one is listening.

> **Case: The Norway Debate (Chapter 19)**
> In May 1940, after the failed Norway campaign, Parliament held a debate that destroyed Chamberlain's government. Churchill was not the target — he was the beneficiary. The Labour Party forced a vote. Chamberlain resigned. Churchill became Prime Minister. He wrote: "I felt as if I were walking with destiny, and that all my past life had been but a preparation for this hour and this trial."
> **Key takeaway:** The phrase "walking with destiny" is the book's title and its thesis. Churchill believed his entire life — the ambition, the failure, the isolation — was preparation for May 1940. The lesson: your whole life is training for a moment you cannot predict.
