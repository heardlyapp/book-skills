# The Institutional Struggle — Core Framework

The book is organized around a central conflict: a president who did not accept the constraints of the rule of law, and the officials who tried to enforce those constraints.

**Case: The Sessions Recusal.** Jeff Sessions, Trump's first Attorney General, recused himself from the Russia investigation because he had been part of the Trump campaign. Trump never forgave him. Sessions' decision was the first major act of institutional independence in the administration.

**Case: The Comey Firing.** Trump fired FBI Director James Comey because — as he later admitted on television — of "this Russia thing." The firing triggered the appointment of a special counsel.

**Case: The Whistleblower.** A CIA officer filed a whistleblower complaint about Trump's phone call with Ukraine's president. The complaint led to the impeachment.

**Case: The Mueller Report. Mueller's team charged 34 individuals and 3 companies. They obtained 7 guilty pleas and 2 trial convictions. The report did not exonerate Trump on obstruction — it presented evidence and left the conclusion to Congress.**

**Case: The Impeachment. The House impeached Trump for abuse of power and obstruction of Congress. The Senate acquitted him. Only one Republican senator voted to convict.**

**Case: The Institutionalists. Officials like Rod Rosenstein, Don McGahn, and Jeff Sessions believed in the rule of law even when it meant opposing the president. Their choices constrained Trump's actions.**
