# Anti-Patterns: Offer Mistakes

## ❌ Pricing Based on Cost
The most common mistake: cost-plus pricing. "This costs me $50 to deliver, so I'll charge $100." Price should be based on value delivered, not cost incurred. A $10 ebook that saves someone $10,000 is worth $1,000. Price for the value, not the cost.

## ❌ Selling to Everyone
"If you're selling to everyone, you're selling to no one." The more specific your offer, the more valuable it is to the right person. A "weight loss program" is generic. A "30-day post-pregnancy weight loss program for busy working moms" is an offer that a specific starving crowd will pay a premium for.

> **Case: The Generic Coach** (Section I): Hormozi describes a coach who was selling "business coaching" to anyone who would pay. He made $2,000/month. When he niched down to "helping dental practices add $100k in monthly revenue," he charged $5,000/month and had a waiting list. Same coaching skills. Different niche. Different result.
> **Key takeaway:** The riches are in the niches. The more specific your target, the higher your price.

## ❌ Weak Guarantees
"I'll try to help you" is not a guarantee. A weak guarantee protects you but doesn't reassure the customer. Strong guarantees remove fear and increase sales.

## ❌ Fake Scarcity
Using false scarcity destroys long-term trust. If you say "only 3 spots left" but then accept the fourth customer, you've trained your customers that you lie. Once trust is gone, no offer can save you.

## ❌ Overcomplicating the Offer
Too many options paralyze buyers. Hormozi recommends ONE Grand Slam Offer. Not three tiers. Not custom packages. One offer that's so good it's the obvious choice.

> **Case: The Menu Trap** (Section IV): Hormozi describes a consultant who had 12 different service packages. Customers were confused and bought nothing. He consolidated to ONE offer: "The 90-Day Business Transformation" — everything included, one price. Revenue tripled.
> **Key takeaway:** Choice is the enemy of conversion. One great offer beats five good ones.


## ❌ Ignoring the Post-Purchase Experience
A great offer that leads to a poor experience destroys your business. If customers feel cheated after buying, they'll leave bad reviews and never return. The offer must deliver on its promise — not just in marketing, but in actual results.

## ❌ Not Testing Your Price
Hormozi's rule: if no one complains about your price, it's too low. You should get some resistance. If everyone says yes immediately, you're leaving money on the table. Test higher prices until you get some pushback.

The lesson is simple: build a great offer, deliver on it, and keep improving. The market rewards businesses that consistently provide exceptional value.

Avoid these mistakes and you will be ahead of most competitors.
Your customers will tell you exactly what they want if you ask and listen.
A well-designed offer with strong guarantees and compelling bonuses will always outperform a mediocre one, regardless of price.
