# Techniques — The Strike, The Sanction Revealed, and Thinking for Yourself

## Practical Methods

> **Case: The Pullman Coach** (Dagny's discovery): Dagny finds an abandoned train coach on a sidetrack — built with a revolutionary design that is decades ahead of its time. The inventor has vanished. The prototype sits rusting. Dagny realizes: this is what the strike looks like. The best work is being hidden because the best minds have stopped producing. She decides to find them. Her response to the strike is not to join it — she wants to save Taggart Transcontinental. But her search leads her to Galt's Gulch, where she must decide: stay and fight in the corrupt world, or join the strikers.
> **Key takeaway**: When you see something brilliant being wasted, ask why. The answer will tell you who is benefiting from the waste.

> **Case: Rearden Metal** (Rearden's struggle): Hank Rearden invents a new metal alloy that is stronger, lighter, and cheaper than steel. The establishment rejects it. The government regulates it. The intellectuals attack it. Rearden is forced to spend more time defending his metal than developing it. This is the pattern: innovation is always attacked by those who benefit from the status quo. The inventor must fight two battles — the technical battle to create, and the political battle to defend the creation.
> **Key takeaway**: Creating something new means fighting the existing system. This is normal. Do not expect gratitude from the people whose world you are disrupting.

> **Case: The Money Speech** (Francisco's lesson): Francisco d'Anconia teaches a young man: "Do not let the villains tell you that money is the root of all evil. Money is a tool of exchange. It exists because people produce different things and want to trade. When money is honest, it represents the value of what the person who earned it has produced. When money is debased — created out of thin air by governments — it becomes a tool of plunder." The speech connects economics to ethics: honest money is the physical representation of the trader principle.
> **Key takeaway**: Money is not evil. The debasement of money is evil. Earn your money honestly. Spend it proudly. You traded value for it.
