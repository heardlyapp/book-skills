# Core Framework — The Office BFFs

## Jenna's Story — Getting Cast as Pam

### Before The Office
- Grew up in St. Louis, Missouri. Wanted to be an actor since age 7.
- Graduated Truman State University with theater degree. Drove Mazda 323 hatchback to LA.
- Spent 8 years as struggling actor: receptionist jobs, Commedia del Arte, background work.
- Booked pilot "Rubbing Charlie" (massage therapist for Scott Wolf) — didn't make it to series.
- Told manager she wanted to quit and apply to vet technician school. Manager: "The right role will find you."
- Season after that: auditioned for The Office remake of BBC show. Role: receptionist Pam — "a worn-down yet still hopeful young woman with artistic dreams beyond her depressing day job. In other words, me."

### The Audition
- Table read with Ricky Gervais and Stephen Merchant present. Stephen: "Jim and Pam are the heart of the show."
- Steve Carell: "One day this photo will be worth money. Especially after they fire me and replace me for episode two."
- Decorated desk with label maker, message pad, file folders — all from Office Depot trip with John Krasinski.
- First day on set: "I tried to imagine I was driving to my real job at a real paper company in Scranton."

## Angela's Story — Getting Cast as Angela

### Before The Office
- Grew up in Indonesia, Louisiana, Texas — dad was drilling engineer.
- At age 4, told mom she wanted to be Carol Burnett.
- Baylor University degree → intern at Late Night with Conan O'Brien (lied about music experience to become "band's intern").
- Moved to LA: two jobs (1-800-DENTIST operator and iO West intern program), constantly auditioning.
- Related by marriage to Greg Daniels (his wife, Susanne, was her husband's sister).
- Greg called her in to audition for The Office. One caveat: no one could know they were related.
- November 17, 2003: auditioned for Pam. The 16th person that day. Wore pink sweater and black pencil skirt. Sweated through armpits (used toilet paper). Read with Phyllis. Did three scenes. Felt great.
- Next day: Allison Jones called. Did NOT get Pam — "too feisty."
- Got cast in Salon Royale (Oxygen network). Didn't make it to series.
- A few weeks later: Allison's office called. New role — "the prickly lady in accounting."
- Wore light blue turtleneck, gray cardigan, bare face. Sat in corner. Got the part.

### First Days
- First day: call time 4:30 AM. Freezing cold hair and makeup trailer.
- Walked onto set not knowing where to sit. Ken Kwapis showed her to accounting corner.
- Discovered Oscar Nuñez was her deskmate (knew him from Groundlings and Hot Towel sketch comedy).
- Yelled "Holy crap, Oscar!" — neither knew the other had been cast.
- Wore her own clothes in the pilot (Payless shoes, the chunky ones).
- Two scripted lines in pilot: "I bet it's gonna be me. Probably gonna be me."
- Sprinkles the cat: born from a green Post-it note doodle during pilot week. Handed out birthday party invitations during background walk.

## The Friendship
- **"Bench Week"** (Season 1, "Basketball"): seated together on metal bench in warehouse. Told each other their life stories. Shared childhoods, moves to Hollywood, same Target. "This was the birthplace of our best friendship."
- Linked arms walking to trailers, sang Laverne & Shirley theme song. Steve Carell walked behind them: "No matter what happens with the show... you will have this. This friendship is what you will take with you."
- Lunch dates in Jenna's trailer (brown velour couch, Bed Bath & Beyond TV trays). "Lady, can I be snarky for a second?"
- Rainn Wilson once asked: "What more do you have to talk about?!"
- Both "mom the same way" — Midwestern/Southern upbringings. Now 5 kids between them, go to same school.
- Angela was in Jenna's wedding. Their kids hug at recess.

## Booze Cruise (Season 2)
- First episode moved to Thursday "Must See TV." First location shoot (Long Beach harbor).
- Angela and supporting cast became series regulars — found out on the boat, ran down gangway screaming to tell Jenna.
- Three nights on a boat. Rainn got seasick ("most pukey"). Jenna got sick — captain gave her magic oil behind ears.
- Fake puking scene: cream of mushroom and minestrone soup. Angela chose minestrone.
- Steve's dance scene: improvised, no music playing, leg-slapping move and slow worm on floor.
- "27 Seconds of Silence" — Jim and Pam scene. Script only said "They look at each other." Greg fought network to keep the silence.
- **The Dinghy Incident**: Angela, Rainn, Brian, Phyllis chose dinghy to shore. Engine died. Drifted in darkness for 20 minutes. Second dinghy rescued them. "Next time you watch 'Booze Cruise' and see Jim and Pam stare longingly... think of us huddled together on that tiny dinghy."
- Angela's car died at intersection on drive home. Towed. "Normally this would have completely stressed me out, but that day, I knew I'd be okay."

## Behind the Scenes (Chapter 4)
- **Hair and Makeup**: Kim Ferry's chair had JENNA'S DISH for clips. Bedhead is "the great equalizer." Five red wigs = Meredith's hair catches on fire. B. J. Novak became blond.
- Hair and makeup vault: "what's discussed in hair and makeup stays in hair and makeup."
- **Postpartum support**: Jenna returned 5 weeks after C-section (no paid maternity leave). Hair and makeup team cheered. Angela got her postpartum underwear. "The truth is it felt like a double standard."
- **Wardrobe**: Carey Bennett visited real paper company for research. Wolf shirt inspiration. Costumes by paycheck: Pam = Ann Taylor Factory, Phyllis = Dress Barn, Meredith = Liz Claiborne, men = Mervyn's and JCPenney.
- Angela's vintage clothes: no doubles. She wore paper towel bibs at lunch.
- **Food**: Peter and Vartan's snack shack (Peter was John Travolta's craft service man, never told stories). "Hot snack" at 10:30 AM. Sergio's catering: "the Rashida" (black beans, turkey bacon, avocado, egg whites), "Angela size" portions.
- **Greg Daniels' innovation**: Writers as actors (B. J., Mindy, Paul, Mike). Open writers' room — actors welcome. Notecards on walls for storylines.

## Holidays (Chapter 5)
- **Halloween**: Angela's first house — bought GIANT orange tub of candy. No one came. Ate candy and cupcakes themselves.
- On set: costumes in August (100°F). Rainn's makeup melting. Accounting department group photos.
- **Sprinkles Post-it note**: doodled on green Post-it during pilot. Jenna used it as improv fuel: "Are you going to Angela's cat party on Sunday?"
- **Christmas Party**: Yankee Swap. Babies Playing Jazz poster. Angela trying to fit in gift bag. Soap snow (Angela tasted it — confirmed).
- Real-life Yankee Swap party at Angela's house every year. Oscar Nuñez makes chants. "Give him the hose!"
- Jenna almost missed Halloween with her kids (Splitting Up Together schedule). Angela: "I've talked to a couple of neighbors. We're going trick-or-treating. You will not miss Halloween on my watch."

## The Women of Dunder Mifflin (Chapter 6)
- **Kate Flannery** (Meredith): Second City, Annoyance Theatre, Lampshades. Did her own stunts (bat attack, set on fire, hit by car, head shaved, hand sanitizer). "She gives 110% to every performance."
- **Phyllis Smith**: from casting associate to actor. Ken Kwapis was so taken by her he told Greg she should play a role. St. Louis connection with Jenna (Cardinals, toasted raviolis, gooey butter cake).
- **Melora Hardin** (Jan): acting since age 6. Originally cast in Back to the Future (too tall to play Michael J. Fox's girlfriend). Advice to Jenna: ask for a hotel room during press junkets. At 2005 Emmys: "Hold up. We are all part of the cast." First working mom/actress Angela ever met.
- **Mindy Kaling**: only female writer. Wrote "The Dundies," "The Injury," "Niagara," "Diwali" (first US TV comedy to depict Diwali). Subtle campaign to transform Kelly Kapoor from quiet to her own personality. Picked most expensive item on Jenna's baby registry — breast pump.

## The Men (Chapter 7)
- **John Krasinski**: childhood friends with B. J. Novak (Little League). Office Depot trip for desk supplies. "We were both pretty boring people. In a good way. He felt stable."
- **Rainn Wilson**: Shakespeare actor. Ten years on the road. First line to Angela: "This is Holly. Bearer of my seed." Helped Angela through karaoke anxiety in "Benihana Christmas" (knelt down, sang "Pa rum pum pum pum" with her). Tuna fish sandwich before kissing scene with pregnant Angela.
- **Steve Carell**: requested early call times (two young kids). "I'd rather get started early and get home for family dinner." Never wanted to be treated differently. Hockey player IRL — became inspiration for "Michael's Birthday" and "Threat Level Midnight."

## JAM — Jim and Pam (Chapter 9)
- Kasie (Jenna's friend from college) got hired as production assistant. When she heard about Jim and Pam's first kiss at Casino Night, she locked herself in bathroom and wept: "I have been waiting three years for this moment!"
- Wedding episode ("Niagara"): flew to Niagara Falls for real location. Jenna was nervous about dancing in front of hundreds of extras. Mindy wrote the dance specifically for Jenna's skill level: "She didn't put anything in the script that she knew I couldn't do."
- Only two people knew Pam was pregnant (writer + Greg). Revealed in talking head.
- Jim and Pam's wedding necklace — Alysia Raycraft gave it to Jenna after wrap.

## Dwangela — Dwight and Angela (Chapter 11, by Angela)
- First interaction: "Has anyone seen the first-aid kit?" — "How many times have I told you? I'm the safety officer, not you."
- Truth or Dare truth: Dwight once said Angela's biggest secret is she's a "slut for a good irrigation system."
- Tiny Birkenstock feet in playhouse scene: Angela had to scooch down Rainn's torso, face in chest. Rainn did plank so he wouldn't crush her. "I'm not sure either of us knew what to say." During the 12+ takes they became friends.
- "The Little Drummer Boy": Angela (real) terrified of singing. Rainn knelt down and sang the "pa rum pum pum pum" to help her find the beat.
- Proposal scene: Dwight pours heart out through bullhorn. Everyone on crew crying. "Did it start with a car chase? Did Pam yell 'What the fuck is wrong with you?'?"
- Fans get Dwight and Angela tattoos. "I have had strangers at airports show me their Dwight and Angela tattoos... including upper thigh."

## Death Bus — The "Work Bus" Episode (Chapter 12)
- Directed by Bryan Cranston (breaking from Breaking Bad — went from RV to bus).
- Small airport shuttle with desks, chairs, lamps, watercooler, microwave, 14 actors, 4 crew.
- **The Pee Incident**: Ellie Kemper laughed so hard at "Shabooya Roll Call" she peed her pants. "More like a large tinkle."
- **The Swerve**: stunt driver swerved HARD. Nothing was tied down. Desks, lamps, printers, water dispenser, laptops went flying. Cast piled on top of each other. "Creed, John, lamps, laptops, chairs were all on top of Phyllis."
- **The Exhaust Poisoning**: portable AC unit's intake hose was next to bus exhaust pipe. Almost gassed entire cast with carbon monoxide. "STOP THE BUS!!!"
- After all this: ate lunch in a field. Realized they looked like Seurat painting. Took photos. "A Sunday Afternoon on the Island of La Grande Jatte."

## The Finale (Chapter 13)
- Final day: cast and crew in tears. Creed's final speech: "We opened a show about nothing and turned it into something."
- Oscar, Brian, Angela: last scene together. "It was really quiet in the office."
- Jenna's photo of the bullpen on the final day — everyone hugging.
- Final crew picture: "The show was the sum of all of us."

## Boss Ladies — After The Office (Chapter 14)
- Both cofounded the podcast "Office Ladies" — episode-by-episode rewatch.
- Jenna starred in "Splitting Up Together" (ABC). Directed episodes of the show.
- Angela runs a production company, produces content for various networks.
- "What is a pleasure to write is a pleasure to read" — Angela's college writing professor. "It was a pleasure for US to make the show, and I think because of that, it is a pleasure to watch."
