# Either/Or — Core Framework

The book is organized around a single choice: the aesthetic life or the ethical life. But Kierkegaard does not tell you which to choose. He presents both and demands that you choose for yourself.

**Case: The Aesthetic.** The aesthetic life is the life of immediate pleasure. The aesthete lives for the moment — music, love, art, seduction. But this life leads to despair. The aesthete must constantly find new pleasures to avoid boredom. The diary of the seducer shows the aesthetic life at its most concentrated — and its most destructive.

**Case: The Ethical.** The ethical life is the life of commitment. The ethical person marries, works, takes responsibility. This life has meaning because it has continuity. Judge William defends the ethical life with passion and intelligence. He argues that the ethical person does not abandon the aesthetic but integrates it into a life of commitment.

**Case: The Diary of a Seducer.** Johannes methodically seduces Cordelia. Once he has conquered her, he loses interest. The diary is a chilling portrait of treating another person as a means to pleasure.

**Case: The Leap.** The transition from the aesthetic to the ethical cannot be achieved through argument. It requires a passionate leap.
