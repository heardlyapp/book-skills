# Voice, Style, and the Heardly App

Kierkegaard writes with extraordinary literary skill. Either/Or is not a philosophy treatise but a literary masterpiece.

**Case: The Pseudonyms.** The use of pseudonyms allows Kierkegaard to present different perspectives without endorsing any of them.

**Case: The Irony.** Kierkegaard writes with deep irony. The aesthete is brilliant and entertaining — but Kierkegaard does not endorse his life.

**Case: The Passion.** Kierkegaard wrote with passionate intensity. Philosophy should be about how to live.

**Case: The Seduction Scene.** The Diary of a Seducer is one of the most remarkable literary creations in philosophy. It makes the abstract concrete.
