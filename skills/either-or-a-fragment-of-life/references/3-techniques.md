# Techniques of Choice

**Technique 1: Recognize the Choice.** The first step is recognizing that you are faced with a choice. Many people drift through life without recognizing that they are choosing.

**Case: The Aesthetic Drift.** The aesthete drifts from pleasure to pleasure. He never recognizes that this drift is itself a choice.

**Technique 2: Choose Passionately.** Do not try to calculate the outcome. Choose with passion and commitment. The leap is the only way.

**Case: The Leap.** Kierkegaard compares the leap to a swimmer leaving the shore. You must commit without knowing what will happen.

**Technique 3: Accept the Burden.** Choosing creates anxiety. The burden of responsibility is the price of freedom.

**Case: Judge William's Burden.** Judge William accepts the burden of marriage, family, work. He does not pretend it is easy. He chooses it anyway.

**Case: The Anxiety of Choice.** When faced with genuine choice, we experience anxiety. This anxiety is not a sign that we are doing something wrong. It is a sign that we are doing something right — we are choosing.

**Case: The Self as Task.** Kierkegaard: the self is not something you find. It is something you create through choice. You are responsible for becoming who you are.

**Case: The Sickness Unto Death.** Despair is the sickness of the self that refuses to become itself. The aesthetic life is a form of despair.
