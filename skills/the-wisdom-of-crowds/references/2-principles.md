# 2 — Key Principles

> Source: *The Wisdom of Crowds* by Joe Abercrombie — Parts VII-IX.

## The 7 Principles

### 1. Know Who You're Marching With

The Breakers included ex-soldiers fighting for freedom, mill workers fighting for bread, and thugs fighting for loot. They were not the same movement — they were three movements marching in the same direction for different reasons.

> **Case: Jakib the Shoemaker** (Part VII, "The Little People"): Jakib joins the Breakers with his grandfather's rusty sword, full of hope about bread prices and freedom. Within hours he's part of a mob kicking a man to death. "Regrets were for tomorrow."

### 2. When Institutions Fall, Protection Falls With Them

The prisons were full of innocent people. Opening them freed the innocent — and the guilty. The courts were corrupt. Burning them removed the corrupt — and the protection they provided from mob justice.

### 3. The Moderates Must Move Fast or Die

When the Breakers entered the Agriont, Risinau was carried to the Lords' Round on a gilded chair while Judge's Burners were already in the streets killing people. The moderates had one afternoon to establish order. They spent it cheering.

### 4. Every Revolution Produces Its Own Contradictions

Adnes kissed a soldier she didn't know, filled with joy at the "Great Change." Twenty minutes later, she stepped over a dead body. The revolution is liberation and murder in the same breath.

### 5. Surrender Can Be the Strongest Move

Orso's decision to surrender alone — unarmed, in full regalia — disarmed the mob more effectively than any defense. "Well," he said, "Here I am." The crowd that had been screaming for his blood fell silent.

### 6. The Coin-Toss Between Hero and Villain

Broad's realization after the battle: "There was only a coin-toss between him and these corpses." He could have been the soldier in the red line. The dead soldier could have been the hero.

### 7. The Morning After Is Harder Than the Night Before

Rikke wins half the North, captures Stour Nightfall, sits in Skarling's Chair. Her first act? Sending Shivers to burn a valley. Winning is easy. Governing is not.
