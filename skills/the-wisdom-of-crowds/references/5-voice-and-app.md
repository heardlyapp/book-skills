# 5 — Voice & App

> Source: *The Wisdom of Crowds* by Joe Abercrombie.

## Abercrombie's Voice

1. **Cynical but compassionate.** He shows every character's worst impulses — and still makes you care about them. Orso is a spoiled king, but his surrender is genuinely noble.
2. **The "little people" chorus.** The interludes showing ordinary citizens (Jakib, Shawley, Lilott, Adnes) make the politics personal. History is not made by leaders alone.
3. **Dark humor in dark moments.** "Wish I could get him down," Broad mutters, while the crowd carries Brock on their shoulders. The absurdity of hero worship in the middle of a revolution.
4. **No heroes, no villains — just people making choices.** Even Judge, the closest thing to pure evil, is understandable in her rage. Even Orso, the most sympathetic character, is a privileged ruler who benefited from the system.

## Five Scenarios

### 1. The Activist in a Splintering Movement

See the factions. Control the extremists or lose the movement. Plan for day one.

### 2. The Leader Watching Institutions Fail

Build one salvageable institution. Process over vengeance. Identify what must be preserved even as everything changes.

### 3. The Person Complicit in Violence

Jakib's arc: he wanted bread prices to go down. By midnight he'd helped murder a man. Small choices compound.

### 4. The Survivor of a Purge

Ettenbeck was a tax clerk. He was killed because someone decided "people like him" were the enemy. The definition of "the enemy" always expands.

### 5. The Victor Who Doesn't Know What to Do Next

Rikke won. She captured Stour. She sat in Skarling's Chair. Then she had to govern. The hardest work comes after the victory.
