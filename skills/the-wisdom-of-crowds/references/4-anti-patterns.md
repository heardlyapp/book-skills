# 4 — Anti-Patterns

> Source: *The Wisdom of Crowds* by Joe Abercrombie.

## Common Errors

### 1. The "This Time It's Different" Delusion

**The trap:** "Our revolution is different. We've learned from history. We won't make the same mistakes."

**Why it fails:** Every revolutionary movement believes this. The Breakers' banners said "The Great Change Is Come." Fifteen minutes after taking the gates, they were looting, killing, and flying severed heads on spikes. The pattern is not broken by belief.

### 2. The "Burn It Down and Figure It Out Later" Fallacy

**The trap:** "First we destroy the old system. Then we'll build the new one."

**Why it fails:** You need the old system's infrastructure to build anything new. The Breakers burned the courts — then had no mechanism for justice. They opened the prisons — then had no way to protect anyone.

### 3. The Moderate's Pause

**The trap:** "I'll wait and see which way this goes before committing."

**Why it fails:** Risinau's moderates had one afternoon after storming the Lords' Round to establish some order. They spent it making speeches while Judge's Burners were settling scores. The pause costs you the initiative.

### 4. The "Just the Bad Ones" Trap

**The trap:** "We'll only go after the truly corrupt. Everyone else has nothing to fear."

**Why it fails:** Ettenbeck was a tax clerk, not a war criminal. The fountain execution shows that once you start "purging the corrupt," the definition of "corrupt" expands to include anyone who had a better job than you.

### 5. The Noble Name for Nasty Work

**The trap:** "This isn't murder — it's people's justice." "This isn't looting — it's redistribution."

**Why it fails:** Call it what it is. Shawley killed Furnevelt for a debt, not for freedom. The Breakers who drowned Ettenbeck were settling class scores, not delivering justice. A beautiful name doesn't make a brutal act beautiful.
