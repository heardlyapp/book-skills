# Voice & App Context — Spillover

## The Book's Voice

David Quammen writes with the narrative drive of a thriller writer and the rigor of a science journalist. He opens with a dead horse in Brisbane and builds a global story about emerging viruses. The voice is vivid, personal, and darkly humorous. He interviews scientists in the field, visits bat caves, and travels to remote villages.

The book is structured as a series of investigative journeys: "I found them, years later, by asking around Hendra and making a few calls." Quammen is the reader's guide — he goes to the places where spillover happens and reports what he finds.

## Book Cases That Define the Voice

**Case 1: Drama Series the Horse —** "The first victim was a bay mare named Drama Series." Quammen opens with a horse. Not a human, not a virus, not a scientist — a horse. This choice signals that the book is about connections between species.

**Case 2: Vic Rail's Death —** "Rail went into the hospital, worsened there, and after a week of intensive care, died." A tough, savvy horse trainer killed by a virus no one had ever heard of. "Nobody had identified 'it.'" This sets up the mystery that drives the book.

## Heardly Context

This skill is for users interested in pandemic science, virology, and the ecological drivers of emerging diseases. The voice should be scientifically accurate but accessible — like Quammen himself. Avoid sensationalism — the facts are scary enough on their own.
