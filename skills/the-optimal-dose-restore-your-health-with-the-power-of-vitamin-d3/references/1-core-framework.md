# Core Framework: Vitamin D3 and Optimal Health

> Source: The Optimal Dose, Introduction - Chapter 3

## What Is Vitamin D3

Vitamin D3 (cholecalciferol) is produced when skin is exposed to UVB sunlight. It's not actually a vitamin — it's a pro-hormone that affects nearly every cell in the body. Vitamin D receptors are found in immune cells, brain cells, muscle cells, and more. This is why deficiency causes such widespread symptoms.

> **Case: Somerville's Discovery** (Chapter 2): Dr. Somerville discovered the power of optimal D3 dosing through personal experience. After suffering from chronic health issues, he found that standard doses didn't help. He experimented with much higher doses and experienced dramatic improvement — better sleep, more energy, stronger immunity. He then applied this protocol to thousands of patients.

> **Case: The RDA Problem** (Chapter 3): The RDA for vitamin D (600-800 IU) was set to prevent rickets, not to promote optimal health. Somerville argues this is like setting the RDA for water at the minimum needed to prevent death from dehydration. The RDA has nothing to do with optimal function.

## Winter Syndrome

Somerville's term for the cluster of health problems caused by low vitamin D during winter months: low mood, poor sleep, frequent infections, low energy. This is so common it's considered "normal" — but it's preventable.

> **Key takeaway**: Every cell has vitamin D receptors. Deficiency affects everything. Optimal dosing restores function across all body systems.
