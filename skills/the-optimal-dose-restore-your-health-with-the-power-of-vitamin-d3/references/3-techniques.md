# Techniques: Starting Your D3 Protocol

> Source: The Optimal Dose, Chapters 8, Appendix

## How to Start Your Protocol

Step 1: Get a blood test for 25-hydroxyvitamin D. Step 2: Calculate your starting dose using the formula (30-50 IU per pound of body weight daily). Step 3: Take D3 with a fatty meal for best absorption since it's fat-soluble. Step 4: Take magnesium glycinate 200-400 mg at night — it's essential for D activation. Step 5: Re-test after 3 months and adjust your dose as needed.

> **Case: The 3-Month Check** (Chapter 8): Somerville recommends re-testing after 3 months. Most patients need at least one dose adjustment. The target blood level is 50-80 ng/mL. If below, increase the dose. If above, reduce it.

> **Key takeaway**: This is a process. Start, test, adjust. It takes 3-6 months to dial in your personal optimal dose. Don't expect perfection on the first try.

## Summer vs Winter Dosing

In summer, sun exposure naturally raises D levels — you may need less oral supplementation. In winter, you need more. "Winter Syndrome" is Somerville's name for the cluster of low mood, poor sleep, and frequent infections from low D in winter months.

## Sleep Optimization

Take D3 in the morning, not at night, to support healthy circadian rhythm. Morning sunlight exposure provides natural D and reinforces the sleep-wake cycle naturally.

## Choosing the Right Supplement

D3 (cholecalciferol) is superior to D2 (ergocalciferol) — more bioavailable and longer-lasting. Choose liquid drops or softgels. Consider a combined D3 + K2 supplement for convenience.
