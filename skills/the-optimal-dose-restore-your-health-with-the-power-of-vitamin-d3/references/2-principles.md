# Principles: Optimal Dosing Protocol

> Source: The Optimal Dose, Chapters 5-8

## The Dosing Formula

Somerville's core formula: 30-50 IU per pound of body weight daily. A 200 lb person takes 6,000-10,000 IU/day — 10-15 times the standard RDA of 600-800 IU. For severely deficient patients, doses up to 50,000 IU/day have been used safely under medical supervision with excellent results.

> **Case: Patient Results** (Appendix): Somerville presents case studies of patients who normalized vitamin D levels using high-dose D3. Chronic pain, debilitating fatigue, and frequent infections all improved within weeks of reaching optimal blood levels. These patients had previously failed to respond to standard RDA-level dosing.

> **Key takeaway**: The optimal dose is personal. You must test blood, adjust dose, and re-test. The target is 50-80 ng/mL of 25-hydroxyvitamin D.

## Co-Factors Are Essential

Magnesium is required for vitamin D activation — D3 simply can't work without it. Vitamin K2 directs calcium to bones instead of soft tissues. Zinc works synergistically with D3 for immune function.

## Benefits of Optimal Dosing

Somerville documents improved immune function (fewer colds and infections), better sleep quality, more stable mood, weight loss support, and reduced chronic pain in his patients. Many reported these benefits within 2-4 weeks of reaching optimal blood levels.

## The Testing Protocol

Test your 25-hydroxyvitamin D level. Below 30 ng/mL = deficient. 30-50 = insufficient. Optimal: 50-80 ng/mL. Re-test after 3 months on your protocol and adjust your dose accordingly.
