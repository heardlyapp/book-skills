# Anti-Patterns: Vitamin D Mistakes

> Source: The Optimal Dose, Chapters 3-5

## Taking the Standard RDA

The most common mistake: following the RDA of 600-800 IU and believing it's enough for optimal health. The RDA prevents rickets but does nothing for immune function, sleep, or metabolic health.

> **Case: The Standard Dose Failure** (Chapter 3): Patients come to Somerville already taking vitamin D at RDA levels, yet their blood tests still show deficiency. The RDA was designed to prevent severe deficiency in the general population — it's not an optimal dose for any specific individual.

> **Key takeaway**: The RDA is a minimum, not a target. Most adults need 5,000-10,000 IU/day to reach optimal levels of 50-80 ng/mL.

## Forgetting Magnesium

Vitamin D requires magnesium for activation. Without adequate magnesium, the body can't convert D3 into its active form. Most people are magnesium deficient.

> **Case: The Non-Responder** (Chapter 5): Patients who take high-dose D but don't see improvement are often magnesium deficient. Once they add magnesium glycinate 200-400 mg at night, they start responding.

## Not Testing Blood Levels

Guessing your dose without blood testing is like driving without a map. You can't know if you're on track without data.

## Taking D at Night

Vitamin D can interfere with melatonin production when taken before bed. Take it in the morning with breakfast.

## Ignoring Co-Factors

Taking D3 without K2 can lead to calcium depositing in soft tissues instead of bones. K2 guides calcium to where it belongs.
