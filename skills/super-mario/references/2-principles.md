# Key Principles — Simple Design, Joy First, and the Character Franchise

## Core Principles

> **Case: The Mario Jump** (Throughput): The most important mechanic in video game history: the jump. Mario's jump is perfectly tuned. It is responsive, satisfying, and endlessly varied. Miyamoto spent months getting the jump right. He understood that the core mechanic must be perfect because everything else is built on it.
> **Key takeaway**: The core mechanic of your product must be perfect. Not good. Perfect. Everything else is decoration.

> **Case: The Level Design Philosophy** (Chapter 8): Miyamoto designed Super Mario Bros levels as a series of "gulps" — small challenges that the player can see and immediately understand. Each screen is a puzzle. The player learns by dying. The levels teach without words.
> **Key takeaway**: Great design teaches without instructions. Your user should know what to do instinctively. If you need to explain it, redesign it.

> **Case: Mario as Franchise** (Throughput): Mario has appeared in over 200 games. He is the most recognizable character in entertainment history. The key: Mario is not the same in every game. He adapts. He drives karts, plays tennis, practices medicine. The character is consistent. The applications are infinite.
> **Key takeaway**: Build your brand on a consistent core. Apply it in different contexts. Mario is always Mario, whether he is saving a princess or playing golf.
> **Key takeaway**: The core mechanic is the soul of your product. Get it right before you add anything else. A perfect core makes everything else easy.
