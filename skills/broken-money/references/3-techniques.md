# Techniques: Protecting Your Wealth

## Asset Allocation Across Monetary Regimes

- Short-term (0-2 years): Cash, savings accounts, money market funds
- Medium-term (2-10 years): Stocks, bonds, real estate
- Long-term (10+ years): Gold, Bitcoin, diversified index funds
- Diversify across sound money assets

> **Case: The 1970s Inflation Playbook** (Chapters 9-10): During the 1970s, the US experienced high inflation. Stocks had a "lost decade." But gold rose over 2,000%. Real estate performed well. Those who diversified across asset classes preserved wealth. Those who held only stocks or cash struggled.
> **Key takeaway**: Different assets perform differently in different monetary regimes. The key is owning assets that thrive in the CURRENT regime.

## The Inflation Protection Checklist

- Do I hold more than 6 months of expenses in cash? Move excess to inflation-protected assets
- Am I earning interest that at least matches inflation?
- Do I have exposure to hard assets (gold, real estate, Bitcoin)?
- Am I invested in productive assets (stocks, businesses)?
- Have I considered geographic diversification?

> **Case: The Japan Lost Decade** (Chapter 12): Japan experienced deflation, not inflation, for decades after its bubble burst. Stocks fell. Real estate crashed. Cash held its value. This shows that monetary conditions vary by country and era. A diversified global portfolio protects against any single country's monetary failure.
> **Key takeaway**: Don't bet on one monetary outcome. A globally diversified portfolio prepares you for inflation, deflation, or anything in between.

## Understanding Central Bank Actions

When the central bank:
- Lowers rates: Bonds and stocks tend to rise, cash loses value
- Raises rates: Bonds and stocks may fall, cash earns more
- Quantitative easing: Money supply expands, hard assets rise
- Quantitative tightening: Money supply contracts, cash becomes scarcer
