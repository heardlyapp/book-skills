# Anti-Patterns

## 1. Believing Cash Is Safe

**The mistake**: Keeping all savings in a bank account and assuming it's preserved.

**Fix**: Cash is safe in nominal terms (the number doesn't change) but risky in real terms (purchasing power declines). Cash is not a long-term savings vehicle.

> **Case: The Saver's Loss** (Chapter 5): A diligent saver kept $100,000 in a savings account from 2000 to 2020. With average interest of 1% and average inflation of 3%, her real purchasing power fell from $100,000 to roughly $74,000. She did everything "right" and still lost 26%.
> **Key takeaway**: Cash is not safety. In an inflationary system, cash is a depreciating asset.

## 2. Ignoring History

**The mistake**: Assuming "this time is different" and that fiat money will somehow maintain its value indefinitely.

**Fix**: Study monetary history. Every fiat currency has eventually failed. The US dollar has held up better than most, but it has still lost 85% of its value since 1971.

## 3. Bitcoin Maximalism

**The mistake**: Believing Bitcoin will replace all fiat money and that other assets are worthless.

**Why it fails**: Bitcoin may succeed or fail, but betting everything on one outcome is risky. The monetary future is uncertain. Diversification is wisdom.

> **Case: The Crypto Crash** (Chapter 13): Bitcoin fell over 75% from its 2021 peak to its 2022 trough. Investors who put all their savings in Bitcoin suffered enormous losses. Those who diversified across assets weathered the storm better.
> **Key takeaway**: No asset is guaranteed. Bitcoin's scarcity is attractive, but its volatility is real. Diversify across sound money assets, don't go all-in on one.

## 4. Ignoring Political Risk

**The mistake**: Assuming the monetary system will remain stable regardless of political developments.

**Fix**: Monetary policy is political. Government decisions about debt, spending, and regulation shape the value of money. Stay informed about political risks.

## 5. Timing the Market

**The mistake**: Trying to predict the exact moment to buy or sell.

**Fix**: Time in the market beats timing the market. Dollar-cost average into your positions and hold for the long term.
