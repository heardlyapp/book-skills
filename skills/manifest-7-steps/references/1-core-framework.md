# Core Framework — Manifest: 7 Steps

## Introduction: Roxie's Story

Roxie Nafousi was 27, jobless, depressed for over a decade, in the grip of addiction. After returning from a yoga teacher training in Thailand, she was back to drinking, smoking, and drugs within 24 hours. A friend sent her a podcast on manifesting. "I sat and read and researched and listened and learned and absorbed everything I could."

One week later, she received a message on a dating app from an Australian actor named Wade Briggs. Two weeks later they met. Three months later she was pregnant. One year to the day after his message, their son Wolfe was born. "Three years later, I am free of all addiction, I have carved out a successful career, I am happier and more content than I could put into words."

**The science:** "Everything in the universe is made up of energy. Different emotions have different frequencies. Like attracts like." The law of attraction: high frequency attracts high frequency. Through neuroplasticity, we can rewire our brains to raise self-worth, override limiting beliefs, and align behavior toward goals.

## Step 1: Be Clear in Your Vision

"You can't get to where you want to go if you don't know where it is you are headed." Visualization works because the brain responds to imagined experiences almost identically to real ones. "If we visualize ourselves in a stressful scenario, our brain releases stress hormones. If we imagine ourselves in a peaceful setting, our body relaxes."

Technique: create a vision board, write down exactly what you want (present tense), visualize daily with all five senses engaged.

## Step 2: Remove Fear and Doubt

Fear and doubt are the biggest obstacles to manifestation. They lower your vibration and block what you're asking for. **Cultivate self-love** — "self-love is the foundation of all manifestation. If you don't believe you deserve what you're asking for, the universe won't give it to you."

Nafousi's practice: affirmations, journaling, speaking to yourself as you would to a beloved friend. "You cannot manifest a life you don't believe you deserve."

## Step 3: Align Your Behavior

"You can't expect different results if you keep doing the same things." Once you've clarified your vision and removed fear, you must act as if. If you want to manifest a new job, update your resume. If you want to manifest love, make space in your life for it. "Your behavior must match your intentions."

## Step 4: Overcome Tests from the Universe

"When you start manifesting, the universe will test you." Tests come as obstacles, setbacks, moments of doubt. "A test from the universe is not a sign that you're on the wrong path — it's a sign that you're on the right one. Don't let a rejection, a closed door, or a disappointment derail you."

## Step 5: Embrace Gratitude (Without Caveats)

"Gratitude raises your vibration immediately." Not "I'll be grateful when I get what I want" — gratitude for what you already have opens the door for more. Nafousi's daily practice: write down three things you're grateful for every morning.

## Step 6: Turn Envy into Inspiration

"Envy is a map to what you really want." When you feel envious of someone, ask: "What do they have that I want?" That feeling is information. Use it as inspiration, not resentment. "If they can have it, so can you."

## Step 7: Trust in the Universe

"The final step is letting go." After you've done the work — clarity, removing fear, aligned behavior, passed the tests, gratitude, inspiration — you must surrender. "Trust that the universe is working on your behalf. Let go of the need to control exactly how and when it arrives."

## Epilogue

Nafousi's final message: "Manifesting is not magic. It's a meeting of science and wisdom. It's a philosophy to live by and a self-development practice to help you live your best life."
