# 4 — Anti-Patterns

> Source: *Go for No!* by Richard Fenton & Andrea Waltz.

## 5 Errors

### 1. The Success Slump

When you hit your quota or goal, you stop. This is the most common success killer. The fix: set no-goals.

> **Example: Eric's Monday** (The Book's Frame): Eric closed 3 sales on Monday and stopped for the week. "Success has ruined many a man." The no-goal would have kept him working.

> **Example: The 80% Dropout Rate** (The Golf Course, Chapter 3): Cheryl stated that 80% of salespeople don't make it through their first year "for the simple reason that they failed to make enough calls." Nothing else. Not skill. Not training. Just insufficient rejection.

Past failures create invisible barriers. You believe the obstacle is still there when it has been removed.

### 3. The 44% Problem

44% of salespeople quit after 1 no. Another 22% quit after 2 no's. By the 4th no, 92% have quit. But 60% of sales happen after the 4th no.

### 4. Confusing No with Never

No means "not yet." It is a request for more information, not a final rejection.

### 5. The Small Account Trap

Fear of the big no leads salespeople to focus on small accounts. "If you're going to get a no, get it from the client who needs 20 copiers, not 2."
