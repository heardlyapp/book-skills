# 3 — Techniques

> Source: *Go for No!* by Richard Fenton & Andrea Waltz.

## 5 Tools

### 1. The "Next!" Method

When you get a no, say "next" and move on. Doctors are trained to say "next" when they lose a patient. Do the same for every rejection.

> **Example: The Doctor's Magic Word** (The Flight, Chapter 3): "Every doctor knows that eventually they are going to lose a patient. In medical school, every doctor is taught the magic word: next!"

> **Example: The 4th No That Was a Yes** (The Golf Course, Chapter 3): Kurt told the story of a prospect who said no 3 times. Most salespeople would have quit. Kurt went for the 4th and 5th no's. On the 5th attempt, the prospect said yes to a deal 3x larger than originally proposed.
> **Key takeaway**: No means not yet.

Statistically, 60% of customers say no 4 times before saying yes. 92% of salespeople give up before the 5th attempt. Go for at least 5 no's from every prospect.

### 3. The No-Goal Calculator

If your closing ratio is 1 in 10, and your target is 4 sales/week, set a goal of 36 no's/week. This guarantees you keep prospecting even when you're on a roll.

### 4. The Pike Syndrome Test

Ask yourself: "Is the glass partition still there, or was it removed years ago?" Most people's barriers are self-imposed and no longer real.

### 5. The Failure Celebration

Create a "Go for No!" award for your team or yourself. Celebrate the person who got the most rejections. Make the award equal in prestige to the success award.
