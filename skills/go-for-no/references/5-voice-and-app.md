# 5 — Voice & App

> Source: *Go for No!* by Richard Fenton & Andrea Waltz.

## Voice

The book is a fable — a fictional story about a salesman named Eric who falls out of bed, time-travels 10 years into the future, and meets a successful version of himself. The conversational tone makes the concepts memorable.

> **Example: The Fable Structure** (The Frame): Eric wakes up in 1990, meets his 2000 self, and learns the Go for No! philosophy through conversations, a round of golf, and a speaking engagement. The reader learns alongside him.

> **Example: The Proof Page** (The Ending): After returning to 1990, Eric finds a folded piece of paper under his chair — the page from the book he was beginning to write. Was it all a dream? Or was it real?

## 5 Scenarios

1. **The cold-call averse salesperson** — Set no-goals, not yes-goals
2. **The entrepreneur facing repeated rejection** — Increase your FQ
3. **The team leader whose team won't take risks** — Create a Go for No! award
4. **The person stuck after past failures** — Pike Syndrome recovery
5. **The high achiever who slows down after success** — The Success Slump cure
