# 2 — Principles

> Source: *Go for No!* by Richard Fenton & Andrea Waltz.

## 7 Principles

### 1. Set No-Goals, Not Yes-Goals

"The only way I set goals anymore is by the number of no's I'm going to get." If you focus on no-goals, you never pull back from success.

### 2. Success Tempts You to Stop — Resist It

"When I hit a hot streak, I actually step up my sales attempts to hit my no goal."

### 3. Reward Failure, Not Just Success

> **Case: The Go for No! Award** (The Golf Course): CopyQuest gave out a Producers Pin for top sales AND a Go for No! trophy for most rejections. The Go for No! award often meant more to recipients because it validated their internal desire.

### 4. No Means Not Yet, Not Never

"When someone says no, most salespeople hear never. I hear not yet."

> **Case: Allen Breed's 30-Year Wait** (The Golf Course, Chapter 3): Breed invented the airbag sensor in 1967. Detroit said no for 20 years. Congress mandated airbags. It still took 10 more years before Breed's first big sale — $120 million. Thirty years of no's.

### 5. Fail Fast, Fail Big, Fail Exponentially

One person can only fail so fast. Great leaders help their entire organization fail faster. Edison hired other inventors not because they were smarter — but because he wasn't failing fast enough alone.

### 6. Courage is Acting in the Face of Fear

"The comfort zone is never static. It's either expanding or shrinking."

### 7. Success and Failure Are Not Opposites

"If you're not succeeding fast enough, you're probably not failing fast enough, and you can't have one without the other."
