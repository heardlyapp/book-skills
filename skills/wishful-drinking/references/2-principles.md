# Principles: Fame, Princess Leia, and the Dark Side

> Source: Wishful Drinking, Chapters 2-3

## The Accidental Icon

Fisher never set out to be Princess Leia. She auditioned at 19, got the part, and became one of the most recognizable women on the planet. The role defined her public identity for the rest of her life — a complicated gift.

> **Case: The Gold Bikini** (Chapter 2): Fisher wore the iconic gold bikini "against my will." She found the fan obsession with it bizarre and uncomfortable. The image followed her for decades, long after she wanted to be known for her writing.

> **Key takeaway**: Fame is accidental. You don't choose how the world sees you — but you choose how you live with it.

## Cocaine on Set

During Star Wars, Fisher was young, under pressure, and using cocaine. She didn't yet understand addiction.

> **Case: The Empire Strikes Back Cocaine Story** (Chapter 2): Fisher openly discusses doing coke on set. She tells this story with self-deprecating humor — not pride. It's one of the most famous anecdotes from her Star Wars years, illustrating how early the addiction started.

## The Family Legacy

Her parents' divorce was a national scandal that shaped her worldview about love, trust, and fame.

> **Case: The Cuckolded Family** (Chapter 3): Fisher describes growing up in the shadow of the Eddie-Elizabeth-Debbie triangle. She learned early that privacy didn't exist and that your family's story could be told by strangers.

> **Key takeaway**: Fisher's humor about darkness is not denial — it's mastery. She owned every part of her story.
