# Techniques: Addiction, Recovery, and Survival

> Source: Wishful Drinking, Chapters 4-6

## The Alcoholism

Fisher was a high-functioning alcoholic. She could drink heavily and still perform, write, and appear in public — but it destroyed her health and relationships over time.

> **Case: The Binge Cycle** (Chapter 4): Fisher describes the relentless cycle of drinking, getting sober, relapsing, and repeating. The highs of mania combined with alcohol created a destructive pattern that took decades to manage.

> **Case: The "Get Better, Never Get Well" Philosophy** (Chapter 6): Fisher's most famous recovery insight: "We get better but we never get well." Addiction is not cured — it's managed. The goal is progress, not perfection.

> **Key takeaway**: Recovery is not linear. Relapse is part of the story, not the end of it.

## The Hospitalizations

Fisher spent time in psychiatric hospitals. She describes them with her signature dark wit, never minimizing the seriousness.

> **Case: The Psych Ward** (Chapter 5): Fisher recounts being hospitalized for bipolar episodes. She found the experience both terrifying and absurd — hospital gowns, group therapy, and rules that felt like being a child again.

## ECT (Electroconvulsive Therapy)

Fisher was open about receiving ECT. She discussed the memory loss and confusion with her characteristic frankness, helping destigmatize the treatment.

> **Key takeaway**: Fisher shared everything — the relapses, the hospitals, the ECT. Hiding nothing was her form of freedom.
