# Core Framework: The Fisher Family and Hollywood Royalty

> Source: Wishful Drinking, Introduction and Chapter 1

## Hollywood Royalty

Carrie Fisher was born into celebrity. Her father Eddie Fisher was a famous crooner with many gold records. Her mother Debbie Reynolds starred in Singin' in the Rain. Together they were "the Brad Pitt and Jennifer Aniston of the late '50s" — adored by millions and followed by the tabloid press everywhere.

> **Case: The Scandal of the Century** (Chapter 1): Eddie Fisher left Debbie Reynolds for Elizabeth Taylor after Taylor's husband Mike Todd died in a plane crash. The tabloids exploded. Carrie grew up with this as the defining story of her family — her father more famous for his private life than his music.

> **Key takeaway**: "Scandal outshining celebrity" — the Fisher family was more famous for their scandals than their accomplishments. This shaped everything.

## The One-Woman Show

Wishful Drinking was a stage show first. The book keeps Fisher's stand-up comedy voice — she addresses readers like a live audience in her living room.

> **Case: A Friend Dies in My Bed** (Introduction): A gay friend died in Fisher's bed during Oscar week. She turns tragedy into comedy: "If my life wasn't funny it would just be true, and that is unacceptable." She hasn't been naked in fifteen years: "I haven't even gone sleeveless in twenty."

> **Key takeaway**: Tragedy + time = comedy. Fisher finds the funny angle on the darkest material. That is the method.
