## 7 Principles of Feynman's Life

### 1. The Pleasure of Finding Things Out

Science is joy. The goal is not to know — it is to discover. Feynman never lost the childlike wonder of asking "why?"

### 2. You Must Not Fool Yourself

The first principle of science — and of life. You are the easiest person to deceive. Rigorous honesty with yourself is essential.

> **Case: The Textbook Letter** (1960): A student wrote to Feynman that a textbook claimed "when you boil water, the spaces between the molecules get bigger." Feynman wrote back: "That is wrong. The molecules themselves do not change. The spaces between them get bigger." He could not let a scientific error pass — even from a student's textbook.
> **Key takeaway**: Accuracy matters. Feynman could not tolerate scientific inaccuracy — even in small things.

### 3. Love Is the Most Important Thing

Feynman's letters to Arline prove that his heart was as big as his mind. Physics was his career. Love was his life.

### 4. Deviate from the Beaten Track

Do not do what is expected. Do what interests you. The beaten track is beaten because everyone walks it. Feynman chose the path that fascinated him.

### 5. Play Is Not a Distraction

Feynman played bongo drums, learned to draw, cracked safes, and studied Mayan hieroglyphics. These were not distractions from his physics — they were expressions of the same curiosity.

### 6. Science and Religion Are Different Domains

Feynman was an agnostic. He respected religious people but did not share their certainty. He was comfortable not knowing.

### 7. Reality Trumps Public Relations

The Challenger report showed Feynman's commitment to truth — even when the truth was uncomfortable for NASA. Reality does not care about appearances.

> **Case: The Drum Letter** (1960): A fan wrote asking about Feynman's hobbies. He replied that he played bongo drums. He was not showing off — he genuinely loved music. The letter reveals a man who did not compartmentalize his life.
> **Key takeaway**: Your interests are not separate from your work. They are expressions of the same person. Be whole.
