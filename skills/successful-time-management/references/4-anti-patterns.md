# Anti-Patterns — Successful Time Management

## The Central Error: "I Need to Work Longer Hours"

"Work smarter, not longer." Most people respond to being overwhelmed by working more hours. This is counterproductive — fatigue reduces quality, creativity, and decision-making. Case: The perfectionist trap — "To be, or not to be (perfect)" — chasing perfection wastes time that could be spent on higher-value work.

### Mistake 1: Multitasking
"I can do several things at once." Humans cannot truly multitask. What feels like multitasking is rapid context-switching, which reduces efficiency and increases errors. Case: "Batch your tasks" — single-tasking is faster and more accurate.

### Mistake 2: Cherry-Picking Easy Tasks First
"Do the easy things first to get momentum." This is the "cherry picking" trap. You end up with a list of only hard tasks left and no time for them. Case: "Avoid 'cherry picking'" — do the important tasks first, not the easy ones.

### Mistake 3: Email Addiction
"I need to check email constantly." Constant email checking destroys focus. Most emails are not urgent. Case: "Turning off email notifications is the single most effective productivity change you can make."

### Mistake 4: Filing Never
"I'll deal with this paper later." Piles grow into mountains. A document that takes 30 seconds to file now will take 30 minutes to find later. Case: "Do not let files and filing waste time — file immediately, don't pile."

### Mistake 5: Saying Yes to Everything
"I don't want to let people down." Saying yes to everything means saying no to your own priorities. Case: "Simply the most time-saving phrase in the language is 'No'."

### Mistake 6: Doing Work Others Should Do
"It's faster if I just do it myself." In the short term, yes. In the long term, you've trained everyone to bring you their work. Case: "Don't do it — delegate" — invest time now to save more time later.
