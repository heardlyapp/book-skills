# Author's Voice + Key Scenarios

> **Case: The Author's Consulting Practice** (Introduction): Marilee Adams built her career on Question Thinking after discovering how powerful questions transform coaching conversations. Her clients — from Fortune 500 executives to struggling teams — consistently report that learning to ask better questions changes more than their communication; it changes how they see themselves.
> **Key takeaway**: The framework was developed through decades of real coaching, not academic theory. It works because it's been tested in thousands of real conversations.

> **Case: The CEO's Question** (Chapter 3): A CEO facing declining employee engagement asks himself: "What am I doing that's causing this?" — a Learner question about his own role. This question leads him to discover that his silence in meetings (intended to let others speak) was interpreted as disinterest. A simple switching question changed his understanding of the problem.
> **Key takeaway**: Learner questions directed at yourself are the most powerful. "What am I doing?" opens more possibilities than "What are they doing?"

## Key Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Receiving Critical Feedback
Use the QT Pause. Notice the Judger. Ask: "What can I learn from this?"

### Scenario 2: Leading a Stuck Team
Model Learner questions. Ask: "What's possible? What do we want?"

### Scenario 3: Coaching a Direct Report
Replace advice with questions. "What do you think? What options do you see?"

### Scenario 4: Self-Criticism Loop
Notice: "There's the Judger." Ask: "Is this helping?"

### Scenario 5: Recurring Conflict
Instead of "What's wrong with them?" ask: "What do I really want here? What am I missing?"
