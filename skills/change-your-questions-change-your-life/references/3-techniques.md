# Techniques: Using Question Thinking Daily

## The QT Pause

When you feel triggered, frustrated, or stuck:
1. **Stop** — physically pause. Take a breath.
2. **Notice** — "Am I in Judger right now?"
3. **Switch** — Ask your switching question: "What do I really want?"
4. **Choose** — Proceed from Learner.

> **Case: The Beginner's Practice** (Chapter 9): A new manager starts using the QT Pause. His first attempts are clumsy — he forgets, falls back into Judger, and gets frustrated. But each time he catches himself, the pause gets faster. By the third week, the QT Pause becomes automatic.
> **Key takeaway**: Question Thinking is a skill, not a concept. You learn it by practicing, not by understanding.

> **Case: The Leadership Team Breakthrough** (Chapter 11): A leadership team stuck in conflict — each blaming others. A facilitator introduced the Choice Map. Leaders shifted from "Who dropped the ball?" to "What do we need to learn?" The meeting went from blame-storming to problem-solving in 20 minutes.
> **Key takeaway**: One switching question can transform an entire meeting.

## Common Switching Questions

Find the one that works for you:
- "Is this helping?"
- "What do I really want here?"
- "What can I learn from this?"
- "What's possible?"
- "What would I ask if I were curious?"
- "How can I contribute?"

## The Learner Journal

Daily practice:
- Morning: Set intention with one Learner question for the day
- Throughout the day: Notice Judger moments and practice switching
- Evening: Review — "What questions served me? What questions got in my way?"

## Coaching with Questions

When helping someone solve a problem:
1. Ask instead of tell. "What's the situation?" not "Here's what you should do."
2. Follow their answer with another question. "What else?" "What matters most?"
3. Let them arrive at their own insight.
