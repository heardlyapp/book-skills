# Core Framework: The Choice Map

## The Central Framework

The Choice Map is the book's core tool: a simple visual representation of two mindsets, Judger and Learner, and the path between them. Every moment, you have a choice which path to take.

**Judger Mindset**: Asks questions like "Who's to blame? Why can't I do anything right? What's wrong with me?" Judger is reactive, automatic, self-critical, other-critical, and problem-focused. It feels stuck.

**Learner Mindset**: Asks questions like "What can I learn? What's possible? What do I want?" Learner is curious, intentional, accepting, creative, and solution-focused. It feels open.

**The Switching Question**: When you notice you're in Judger, ask yourself a switching question: "Is this helping? What do I really want? What can I learn here?" This question is the bridge.

> **Case: Ben's Leadership Crisis** (Chapters 2-3): Ben is a newly promoted executive who defaults to Judger under pressure. He blames his team, criticizes himself, and creates a toxic atmosphere. Through Question Thinking, he learns to recognize his Judger patterns and uses switching questions to lead from a Learner mindset. His team's performance and morale dramatically improve.
> **Key takeaway**: Leadership is not about having all the answers — it's about asking the right questions. Ben's transformation came not from learning new techniques but from changing the questions he asked himself and his team.

> **Case: The Choice Map Discovery** (Chapter 1): Marilee Adams introduces the Choice Map through the story of a coaching client who realized she was stuck in Judger mode about a difficult employee. When she asked herself "What do I really want here?" instead of "What's wrong with him?", she shifted from trying to fix the employee to understanding his perspective. The relationship transformed.
>
> **Key takeaway**: One switching question can change an entire relationship. The Choice Map is not a theory — it's a tool that produces different outcomes the moment you use it.

## The 12 Tools

The book presents 12 practical tools organized around the Choice Map framework. Each tool is a specific question sequence designed for a particular situation (coaching, feedback, conflict, decision-making, goal-setting).
