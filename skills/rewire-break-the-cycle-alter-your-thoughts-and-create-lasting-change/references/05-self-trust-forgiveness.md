# Reference: Self-Trust, Intuition & Forgiveness

## Concept

The final movement of *Rewire* connects everything to one actionable foundation: **self-trust.** Confidence is not a feeling you wait for — it is the residue of kept promises. Every time you say "I will" and follow through, you deposit into your self-trust bank. Every time you hit snooze, skip the workout, or break a commitment to yourself, you make a withdrawal. Overdraft leads to low self-esteem, chronic self-doubt, and paralysis.

## Self-Trust = Personal Accountability

Nicole defines self-trust as: "Having confidence in your abilities, building personal accountability, and making sure you keep the promises you make to yourself."

This is concrete, not abstract. It is not about affirmations — it is about behavior. The mechanism:

1. **Make a small promise to yourself** ("I will wake up at 6 AM and stretch for 5 minutes")
2. **Keep the promise** (it helps if it is realistic and achievable)
3. **Notice how it feels** (you trusted yourself, and your brain updates its model of who you are)
4. **Repeat** (self-trust compounds like compound interest)

**Steps to building self-trust:**

1. **Honor your commitments** — Start absurdly small. Read one page. Walk for 5 minutes. Do one pushup.
2. **Set realistic goals** — Not what you *should* want — what you *actually* want. If the goal doesn't align with your values, you will subconsciously sabotage it.
3. **Celebrate accomplishments** — Even minor wins. The brain's negativity bias will skip over them unless you actively pause to acknowledge them.
4. **Growth mindset** — Failure is data, not identity. You either win or you learn.
5. **Listen to yourself** — Your body and intuition are constantly sending signals. Learn to hear them.

## Case: Nicole's Book Deal

When the email from one of the biggest publishing houses in the world arrived, Nicole was ready. She says: "Had the opportunity come a year earlier, I don't know if I would have been ready for it. But given the transformation I had undergone in the years leading up to this, I was ready and I was calm."

She wrote her book proposal from the gut. No fear. No self-doubt about whether she was "good enough." She knew: "If it doesn't work out this time, it will work out at another time." This level of self-trust allowed her to produce her best work without the anxiety of attachment to outcome.

**How she built this:** She kept promises to herself. She woke up at 6 AM and ran when she said she would. She meditated. She limited social media. Over time, she built evidence that she could trust herself. When the big moment came, her self-trust bank was full.

## Case: The Self-Sabotaging Patterns in Relationships

The book walks through a common scenario: you have been hurt or cheated on. You enter a new relationship believing "all relationships fail." This belief becomes a self-fulfilling prophecy. You look for evidence of betrayal. You interrogate your partner. You create tension. Your partner, trying to keep the peace, starts hiding things. When the relationship collapses, you say "see, I knew it."

Your beliefs, mental rehearsal, negativity bias, and attentional bias all work together to shape how you interact with the world. The stories you repeat to yourself are extremely powerful. Be mindful of what self-fulfilling prophecy you are shaping.

## Intuition Is Subconscious Processing

Nicole explains that your gut feeling is your subconscious brain making decisions without conscious awareness. Your brain has stored every memory, every experience, every pattern. When you "have a feeling" about someone or something, it is the result of your brain processing information below the threshold of consciousness.

**Studies show** that we can still make decisions even when we are not consciously thinking about it (Gut feeling research cited in the book). Your subconscious is a network of intertwined neurons communicating constantly. Most of this processing never reaches conscious awareness — if it did, you would be overwhelmed.

When you trust yourself, you trust the signals your intuition sends. It is a reciprocal relationship: the more you lean into your intuition and see positive results, the more clear and reliable it becomes.

**Matthew McConaughey quote:** "Most of the problems in my life have come from letting my head get too far ahead from my heart."

## Motivation Is Temporary — Non-Negotiables Are Forever

One of the most practical insights in the book: motivation will disappear. Expect it. Plan for it. Do not rely on it.

**Non-negotiables** (Nicole's list):
- Exercise Monday–Friday (even if just stretching or walking)
- Phone off at 9 PM
- 8 hours of sleep
- Daily meditation/visualization (even 1 minute)
- Limit social media
- No phone first thing in the morning
- Swim in the sea at least twice a week

**The principle:** When motivation leaves, routine keeps you going. Like clockwork, the cogs keep ticking in repetition to wire the brain the way you want it.

## The Forgiveness Chapter

The final step of the Rewire journey — and arguably the hardest. Forgiveness is not for the other person; it is for you.

**Who to forgive:** Those who unknowingly or unintentionally programmed you during your formative years — parents, teachers, peers, authority figures who attached labels to you or limited your potential.

**How:** Empathize with their intentions and circumstances. They were operating within the confines of their own knowledge, experience, and limitations. They were driven by their own fears and constraints. By shifting focus from blame to self-responsibility, you release the grip of resentment.

**Why it matters:** Without forgiveness, you risk becoming the people who hurt you. Resentment keeps you tethered to the past. Forgiveness creates space for a new narrative.
