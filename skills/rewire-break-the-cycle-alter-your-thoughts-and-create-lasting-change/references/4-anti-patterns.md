# Anti-Patterns: What Undermines Rewiring

> Source: Rewire, Phase 1 & 3

## Chronically Ignoring Stress

Treating all stress as "bad" or powering through it destroys neuroplasticity. Chronic maladaptive stress shrinks the frontal cortex, enlarges the amygdala, and damages the hippocampus.

> **Case: The Stress Triage Mistake:** Many people try to build new habits while in survival mode. This is like trying to renovate a house while it's on fire. You must distinguish alert stress (good), acute adaptive stress (builds resilience), and maladaptive/chronic stress (needs intervention before any rewiring).

> **Key takeaway:** You cannot rewire when your hardware is in survival mode. Fix stress first.

## The Arrival Fallacy

"I'll be happy when I reach X." Dopamine drives wanting, not satisfaction. Once you achieve the goal, dopamine drops and you need a new target.

> **Case: The High-Achiever Trap:** Martha was top of her class at a top digital marketing firm but deeply insecure. She attached her identity to being "intelligent" — so every work mistake felt like a personal attack. She had achieved the goal but felt empty. True fulfillment comes from the journey, not the arrival.

## The "You're So Smart" Trap

Carol Dweck's experiment: students praised for intelligence ("You're so smart!") later chose easier problems and lied about their scores. Students praised for effort chose harder problems. Praising intelligence creates a fixed mindset that avoids challenge.

> **Key takeaway:** If you're waiting to feel confident before you act, you'll wait forever. Confidence comes from action, not the other way around.
