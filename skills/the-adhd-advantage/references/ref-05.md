# Reference: ADHD in Specific Contexts

## Overview
ADHD manifests differently at different life stages and in different contexts. Tailored strategies are essential.

## Case: ADHD in Children
ADHD in children often presents as hyperactivity, impulsivity, and difficulty following instructions. Archer recommends behavioral strategies first: structure, routine, clear expectations, and positive reinforcement. Medication can be effective but should be carefully considered.

## Case: ADHD in Education
Traditional education (lectures, homework, testing) is often a poor fit for the ADHD brain. Strategies include: sitting at the front, taking frequent breaks, using fidget tools, recording lectures, and breaking study sessions into short blocks.

## Case: Adult Late Diagnosis
Many adults are diagnosed with ADHD later in life, often after their children are diagnosed. The late diagnosis can be a relief: suddenly, a lifetime of struggles makes sense. The path forward involves learning about the ADHD brain, developing strategies, and letting go of the shame accumulated over years of undiagnosed difficulties.

## Case: ADHD and Entrepreneurship
Entrepreneurship is a natural ADHD environment. The autonomy, variety, risk, and stimulation match the ADHD brain. Many successful entrepreneurs attribute their success in part to ADHD traits. The risks — impulsivity, difficulty with routine tasks, burnout — can be managed with systems and support.

## Case: ADHD and Creativity
The ADHD brain's tendency to make unusual connections is a creative gift. Many artists, musicians, and writers have ADHD. The key is creating a structure that allows creativity to flourish while managing the practical aspects of creative work.
