# Reference: The Seven Traits

## Overview
Archer identifies seven ADHD traits that can be advantages when channeled correctly.

## Case: Creativity
The ADHD brain makes unusual connections. It thinks outside the box because there is no box. This creativity is a gift for problem-solving, innovation, and the arts. Many entrepreneurs and artists have ADHD. The creativity is fueled by the ADHD brain's tendency to wander and explore.

## Case: Hyperfocus
When someone with ADHD is engaged in something they find genuinely interesting, their focus can be extraordinary. They enter a state of flow where nothing else exists. Hyperfocus is the opposite of distraction — it is concentration so deep that the world disappears. The trick is directing hyperfocus toward productive activities.

## Case: Resilience
People with ADHD develop resilience from a lifetime of overcoming challenges. They have learned to fail, get up, and try again. This resilience is invaluable in entrepreneurship, sales, and any field where rejection is common. The ADHD brain does not give up easily when it is engaged.

## Case: Risk-Taking
The ADHD brain seeks stimulation. This drives risk-taking — a trait that can lead to disaster or extraordinary success. In entrepreneurship, the willingness to take calculated risks is essential. The ADHD entrepreneur is more likely to start a business, launch a product, and take the leap that others avoid.

## Case: Empathy
Many people with ADHD are highly attuned to others' emotions. The constant scanning for novelty and social cues can make them perceptive and empathetic. This is a gift in leadership, sales, therapy, and any field that requires understanding people.
