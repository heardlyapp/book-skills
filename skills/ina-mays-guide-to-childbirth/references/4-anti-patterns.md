# 4 — Anti-Patterns

> Source: *Ina May's Guide to Childbirth* by Ina May Gaskin — Part II, Chapters 5, 8, 11-13.

## Common Errors

### 1. The "Hospital is Always Safer" Assumption

**The trap:** "Birth must happen in a hospital because that's where it's safest."

**Why it fails:** The U.S. has one of the highest maternal mortality rates in the developed world — despite spending more on birth technology than any other country. Hospital births come with routine interventions that themselves carry risk. The right place for birth depends on your specific risk profile, not on cultural assumptions.

### 2. Routine Episiotomy

**The trap:** "Cutting speeds things up and prevents tearing."

**Why it fails:** Research shows that routine episiotomy does not prevent severe tears — in some studies it increases them. Ina May's midwives rarely perform episiotomies. They use slower, gentler techniques — warm compresses, perineal support, position changes, and patience.

> **Case: The Unnecessary Episiotomy** (Part I, Kim Trainor's First Birth): Kim was given an episiotomy — 12 stitches — for a hospital birth in New York. The baby shot out. The stitches were unnecessary. She had to be knocked out with ether to be sewn up. Her second and third births at The Farm — without episiotomy — produced no tearing at all. The difference was not her body. It was the care.

### 3. The Supine Position

**The trap:** "You must lie on your back to give birth."

**Why it fails:** Lying flat works against gravity, compresses major blood vessels, and narrows the pelvic outlet. It is the most mechanically difficult position for birth. It persists because it is convenient for the caregiver. Ina May: "No mammal gives birth on her back."

### 4. The Cascade of Interventions

**The trap:** Inducing for convenience → stronger contractions → epidural for pain → diminished pushing urge → Pitocin to strengthen contractions → fetal distress → C-section. Each intervention creates the "need" for the next.

### 5. The "Too Old" Myth

Women over 35 are routinely labeled "advanced maternal age" and treated as high-risk. Ina May's Farm mothers over 35 (like Bernadette at 38) had normal, unmedicated births. Age alone is not a medical condition.

### 6. The "One Cesarean, Always Cesarean" Fallacy

VBAC is safe for 70-80% of women who try. Many hospitals still ban VBAC or make it difficult. The evidence supports VBAC. The policy often does not.
