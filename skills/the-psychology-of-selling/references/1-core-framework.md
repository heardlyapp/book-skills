# Core Framework: Selling as a Science

## The Central Thesis

"Selling is not an art reserved for the naturally gifted — it's a science that can be learned, practiced, and mastered by anyone who is willing to study and apply the principles."

> **Case: The Enthusiastic Salesman** (Chapter 1): A salesman with average product knowledge but exceptional enthusiasm consistently outsold his more knowledgeable peers. His energy was contagious. Customers felt his confidence and wanted to be part of it. Tracy's point: attitude beats knowledge in selling.
> **Key takeaway**: Enthusiasm is the most powerful sales tool. If you believe in your product, your customer will too.

## Key Concepts

| Concept | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| Consultative Selling | Solving customer problems, not pushing products |
| Features vs Benefits | Features describe; benefits create desire |
| The Feel-Felt-Found Method | Acknowledge → empathize → provide solution |
| Trial Close | Testing buying readiness before final close |
| Assumptive Close | Acting as if the customer has already decided |

> **Case: The Lost Prospect** (Chapter 3): A salesperson spent 40% of their time prospecting but wasn't systematic. Tracy showed them to dedicate specific time blocks exclusively to prospecting. Result: they found 3x more qualified leads. The difference was discipline, not talent.
> **Key takeaway**: Prospecting is not something you do when you have time — it's something you schedule. Regular, dedicated prospecting is the foundation of sales success.
