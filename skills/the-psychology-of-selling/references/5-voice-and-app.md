# Author's Voice + Key Scenarios

> **Case: Brian Tracy's Background** (Introduction): Brian Tracy is one of the world's most respected sales trainers. He started as a salesman, struggled initially, and through study and practice became a top performer. His system is tested through thousands of clients.
> **Key takeaway**: Tracy's authority comes from experience. He's not a theorist — he practiced what he teaches.

> **Case: The Million-Dollar Question** (Chapter 10): Tracy teaches one question that transformed his sales career: "If I could show you a way to [solve their problem], would you be interested?" This question opens conversations without pressure.
> **Key takeaway**: The right question can open any door. Frame everything around the customer's benefit.

## Key Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Cold Calling
Get to the point. Show you understand their need. Ask for a meeting.

### Scenario 2: The Presentation
Focus on benefits, not features. Use stories. Ask trial close questions.

### Scenario 3: Handling Price Objections
Reframe: price is what you pay, value is what you get.

### Scenario 4: The Close
Assume the sale. Ask for the order. Then be quiet and wait.
