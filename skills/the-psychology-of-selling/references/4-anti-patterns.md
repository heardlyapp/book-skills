# Anti-Patterns

## 1. Talking Too Much

**The mistake**: Dominating the conversation with product features.

**Fix**: Listen 70% of the time, talk 30%. Your goal is to understand, not to perform.

> **Case: The Nonstop Talker** (Chapter 2): A salesperson knew every feature of his product. He would launch into a 30-minute monologue on every call. His prospects tuned out. When he was trained to ask questions and listen, his close rate doubled.
> **Key takeaway**: Your product knowledge doesn't matter until the customer feels understood. Talk less, ask more.

## 2. Fear of Rejection

**The mistake**: Taking objections personally and avoiding calls to protect your ego.

**Fix**: Rejection is not personal — it's part of the process. Every "no" gets you closer to "yes."

## 3. No Follow-Up

**The mistake**: Making contact once and giving up if there's no immediate sale.

**Fix**: Most sales need 5+ touches. Implement a systematic follow-up process.

> **Case: The One-Call Wonder** (Chapter 6): A salesperson made one call and if there was hesitation, moved on. He thought he was being "efficient." Tracy showed him that the most successful salespeople follow up 5-8 times. When he started following up systematically, his results tripled.
> **Key takeaway**: The fortune is in the follow-up. Most salespeople give up too early. Persistence is the most underrated sales skill.
