# Techniques: The Sales Process

## The Prospecting Pipeline

1. Define your ideal customer
2. Create a list of prospects
3. Reach out (call, email, LinkedIn)
4. Qualify: does this prospect have a need and budget?
5. Move qualified leads to the presentation stage

> **Case: The 80/20 Prospector** (Chapter 8): A salesperson applied the 80/20 rule: 80% of sales come from 20% of prospects. She stopped chasing unqualified leads and focused only on ideal customers. Her conversion rate doubled and her stress halved.
> **Key takeaway**: Not all prospects are equal. Focus your energy on prospects who match your ideal customer profile. Saying no to the wrong prospects is as important as saying yes to the right ones.

## Handling Objections: The LAARC Method

1. **Listen** fully without interrupting
2. **Acknowledge** the objection
3. **Ask** clarifying questions
4. **Respond** with facts and benefits
5. **Confirm** the answer satisfied them

## The Trial Close

Ask a question that tests buying readiness without asking for the order directly:
- "If we could solve this problem, would you move forward?"
- "How does this solution look to you so far?"

> **Case: The Assumptive Close** (Chapter 9): A real estate agent showed a house and asked: "Which bedroom would be yours?" instead of "Do you like the house?" She assumed the sale and acted as if the customer had already decided. Her close rate was 3x the industry average.
> **Key takeaway**: The assumptive close works because it bypasses the conscious decision and focuses on the experience of ownership. Act "as if" and the customer follows.
