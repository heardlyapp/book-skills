# The Search for Life in the Universe

> Source: Cosmos, Chapters 7, 10, 12

## SETI and the Drake Equation

### The Drake Equation

N = R* × fp × ne × fl × fi × fc × L

| Variable | Meaning | Sagan's estimate |
|----------|---------|-----------------|
| N | Number of civilizations we could contact | ? |
| R* | Rate of star formation | 10/year |
| fp | Fraction of stars with planets | 0.5 |
| ne | Planets per system that could support life | 2 |
| fl | Fraction where life actually evolves | 1 |
| fi | Fraction where intelligent life evolves | 0.01 |
| fc | Fraction that develop detectable technology | 0.01 |
| L | Lifetime of such a civilization | 100-10,000 years |

> **Case: The Message from Earth** (Chapters 10, 12)
> Sagan helped design the plaques on the Pioneer spacecraft (1972, 1973) and the Golden Record on Voyager (1977) — messages intended for any intelligent extraterrestrial civilization that might find them. The plaques show a man and a woman, the location of Earth relative to pulsars, and a diagram of the solar system. The Golden Record contains sounds and music from Earth.
> **Key takeaway:** The Voyager Golden Record is a message in a bottle cast into the cosmic ocean. It represents our hope that we are not alone — and our willingness to reach out.

> **Case: The Oldest Story** (Chapter 10)
> Sagan explores the evolution of life on Earth. The oldest fossils are 3.5 billion years old — single-celled organisms preserved in rock. For most of Earth's history, life was microscopic. The evolution of complex life was not inevitable — it depended on a series of improbable events. Sagan's point: if intelligent life evolved here, it might have evolved elsewhere. But the conditions must be right.
> **Key takeaway:** Life on Earth shows that the evolution of intelligence is possible — but rare. The universe may be teeming with life, or it may be mostly empty. The only way to find out is to look.
