# Anti-Patterns

## 1. Thinking It's About Being Fake

**The mistake**: Believing an alter ego is inauthentic.

**Fix**: It's not fake — it's strategic. You're not pretending to be someone else. You're accessing a part of yourself that you can't access through your ordinary identity.

> **Case: The Authenticity Worry** (Chapter 2): A leader worried that using an alter ego would make her seem fake. She tried it anyway for a difficult negotiation. Her alter ego allowed her to be more direct and assertive — qualities she actually had, but couldn't access naturally in that situation.
> **Key takeaway**: The alter ego doesn't make you someone else. It unlocks qualities you already possess. Authenticity is not about being your everyday self — it's about being true to who you can become.

## 2. Letting the Alter Ego Take Over

**The mistake**: Using the alter ego so much that you lose connection to your core self.

**Fix**: The alter ego is a tool, not an identity. Use it intentionally and put it aside when you don't need it. Separate the performance from the person.

## 3. Not Creating a Clear Transition

**The mistake**: Wandering into performance mode without a clear ritual.

**Fix**: The phone booth moment is essential. Without it, you can't clearly separate your alter ego from your ordinary self.

## 4. Choosing the Wrong Qualities

**The mistake**: Creating an alter ego based on qualities you don't actually have or want.

**Fix**: Your alter ego should amplify strengths you already have, not impose qualities that don't fit. Choose qualities that feel like an expanded version of you, not a completely different person.

> **Case: The Mismatched Persona** (Chapter 7): A quiet, thoughtful person tried to create an alter ego based on a loud, aggressive salesperson. It felt wrong and didn't work. When he created an alter ego based on a calm, wise mentor — someone who listens first — it aligned with his natural strengths and was effective.
> **Key takeaway**: Your alter ego should be an extension of your strengths, not a contradiction of your nature. Choose qualities that feel like a stretch, not a break.

## 5. Forgetting to Integrate

**The mistake**: Never bringing the qualities back into your ordinary self.

**Fix**: After performing as your alter ego, reflect on what qualities you accessed. Practice bringing those qualities into everyday situations. The alter ego is a bridge, not a destination.
