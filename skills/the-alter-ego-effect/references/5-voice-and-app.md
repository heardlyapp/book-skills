# Author's Voice + Key Scenarios

> **Case: Todd Herman's Background** (Preface): Todd Herman is a performance coach who has worked with Olympic athletes, Fortune 500 executives, and entrepreneurs. The alter ego approach emerged from his work with clients who struggled with self-doubt and performance anxiety.
> **Key takeaway**: The alter ego framework is not theoretical — it's been tested with world-class performers across multiple domains.

> **Case: The Shy Salesperson** (Chapter 10): A naturally shy salesperson used an alter ego to transform her performance. She created "The Educator" — someone who teaches rather than sells. Her phone booth moment was simply changing her posture and saying "Time to teach."
> **Key takeaway**: The alter ego doesn't have to be dramatic. A simple shift in identity — from "salesperson" to "educator" — can transform performance without feeling fake.

## Key Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Big Presentation
Name your presenter alter ego. Create a phone booth moment. Practice the transition.

### Scenario 2: Tough Conversation
Create an alter ego who is calm and direct. Choose a name that embodies those qualities.

### Scenario 3: Competitive Event
Design an alter ego with the qualities needed. Use a physical ritual to switch modes.

### Scenario 4: Creative Work
If your inner critic blocks creativity, create an alter ego who creates without judgment.

### Scenario 5: Job Interview
Your "Interviewee" alter ego is confident and prepared. Phone booth moment before walking in.
