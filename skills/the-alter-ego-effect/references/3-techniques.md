# Techniques: Creating and Using Your Alter Ego

## The Alter Ego Creation Process

1. Identify a situation where you need to perform better (presentations, tough conversations, sports)
2. List the qualities you need (confidence, calm, assertiveness, focus)
3. Think of a character — real or fictional — who embodies those qualities
4. Give it a name — the name creates psychological distance
5. Design a phone booth moment — a ritual that transitions you

## The Phone Booth Moment

Your transition ritual should include:
- A physical trigger (change posture, adjust clothing, touch an object)
- A mental trigger (a phrase, a visualization, a mantra)
- A sensory trigger (a song, a scent, a specific taste)
- Practice it consistently until it becomes automatic

> **Case: The Speaker's Transformation** (Chapter 8): A shy professional created an alter ego called "The Professor" for giving presentations — knowledgeable, authoritative, engaging. Her phone booth moment was putting on a specific pair of glasses. The glasses signaled: "Now I'm The Professor. I know my material. I'm confident."
> **Key takeaway**: The phone booth moment can be as simple as putting on glasses, changing your shirt, or playing a song. It doesn't need to be dramatic — it just needs to be consistent.

## The Performance Prep Routine

1. Before the event, step away — find a private moment
2. Do your phone booth ritual
3. Visualize yourself performing as your alter ego
4. Step into the situation as that identity
5. Debrief afterward — what worked? What needs adjustment?

> **Case: The Sales Call Alter Ego** (Chapter 9): A salesperson who was nervous on calls created an alter ego named "The Consultant." His phone booth moment was taking a deep breath and saying "Time to consult." He stopped trying to "sell" and started trying to "advise." His close rate improved dramatically.
> **Key takeaway**: The alter ego changes not just how you feel but how you approach the task. A "salesperson" mindset is different from a "consultant" mindset.
