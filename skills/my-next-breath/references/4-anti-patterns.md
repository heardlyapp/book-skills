# Anti-Patterns — My Next Breath

## The Central Error: "Wait Until You're Ready"

"If you wait until you're ready, you'll never start." Renner's entire life disproves the "wait until conditions are perfect" approach. Case: He jumped to save Alex without computing distances, without preparing his body, without "being ready." He missed — but he tried.

### Mistake 1: "It's too late — I missed my chance"
Renner missed his flight by seconds. Most people would give up. He drove 8 hours through a blizzard. Case: "Screw it. Alex, let's just drive up there." The technique: there's almost always another way.

### Mistake 2: "Small problems are small — ignore them"
The key fob was a tiny mistake — putting it on the roof of the car. It could have ruined the whole trip. Case: Renner didn't ignore it or get paralyzed — he identified the problem and sent Alex back in an Uber to find it. "A Christmas miracle."

### Mistake 3: "When things go wrong, it's a sign to stop"
Everything went wrong trying to get to Tahoe. Missed flight. Key fob. Blizzard. But Renner kept going. Case: "Nothing could stop us now." The mindset: obstacles are not signs to give up — they're challenges to overcome.

### Mistake 4: "Celebrity = invincibility"
Renner is a movie star who does his own stunts. But he learned: being famous doesn't make you immune. The snowcat didn't care who he was. "This man faced with one shot is not, at that moment, a Hollywood actor."

### Mistake 5: "I'm not a writer, so I shouldn't write a book"
"I did not want to write this book. I'm not a writer." But he wrote it anyway. Case: "Even though I'm not a writer, and didn't want to write a book, here it is." The technique: don't let lack of qualification stop you from telling your story.

### Mistake 6: "Recovery is a straight line"
Renner's recovery was brutal — multiple surgeries, 38+ broken bones, depression, doubt. Case: He doesn't pretend it was easy. "I didn't want to relive the incident and its violence." But he did — and that's why the book exists.
