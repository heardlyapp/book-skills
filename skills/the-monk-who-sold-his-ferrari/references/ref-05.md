# Reference: Daily Practices

## Overview
The 10 ancient rituals for radiant living are the practical practices that make the seven virtues real. They must be practiced daily.

## Case: The Ritual of Solitude
Spend at least 15 minutes each day in complete solitude. No phone, no book, no music. Just you, your thoughts, and silence. This is a lost art in the modern world. Solitude allows you to reconnect with yourself, hear your inner voice, and gain perspective. It is the most important ritual.

## Case: The Ritual of Physical Exercise
Exercise the physical body daily. The body is the temple of the spirit. Physical vitality creates mental vitality. Sharma recommends at least 30 minutes of exercise daily. The ritual is not optional — it is a non-negotiable part of living fully.

## Case: The Ritual of Learning
The habit of continuous learning is essential for growth. Read books, listen to podcasts, take courses. Never stop learning. Sharma reads for at least 30 minutes each day. The knowledge you gain compounds over a lifetime. Learning is the ultimate investment in yourself.

## Case: The Ritual of Service
Do at least one act of kindness each day. Sharma says that the fastest way to feel good about yourself is to do good for others. The ritual of service can be small — holding a door, listening to a friend, giving a compliment. The key is consistency. A daily act of service trains the heart to be generous.

## Case: The Evening Ritual
End each day with reflection. Review what went well. Review what you could improve. Express gratitude for the day. The evening ritual closes the day intentionally, allowing you to learn from the past and prepare for tomorrow. Without reflection, there is no growth.
