# Reference: The Parable

## Overview
The story of Julian Mantle is the narrative framework for Sharma's teachings. Understanding the parable makes the seven virtues meaningful.

## Case: Julian Mantle's Collapse
Julian Mantle was a brilliant, wealthy trial lawyer. He had a Ferrari, a mansion, and more money than he could spend. But he was unhappy, stressed, and spiritually empty. In the middle of a trial, he collapsed from a heart attack. The collapse was his wake-up call — a realization that his external success meant nothing without internal peace.

## Case: The Himalayan Journey
Julian left everything — his job, his Ferrari, his possessions — and traveled to the Himalayas. There he found the Sages of Sivana, a community of monks living in a remote mountain village. The Sages taught him the principles of enlightened living. The journey is a metaphor for the inner journey we must all make.

## Case: The Return
Julian returned to the West transformed. He did not return to his old life. He began teaching what he had learned, starting with the story of the monk who sold his Ferrari. The parable is the book — Julian's teacher became a teacher of millions.

## Case: The Symbolism of the Ferrari
Julian's Ferrari represents the material world — success, status, possessions. Selling it represents letting go of attachment to the external and focusing on the internal. The monk who buys the Ferrari lives simply. The title's message: you must be willing to give up what you think you want to find what you truly need.
