# Reference: Purpose and Service

## Overview
The second virtue — follow your purpose — and the sixth virtue — selflessly serve others — are the heart of the book. Without purpose, life is directionless. Without service, it is empty.

## Case: Discovering Your Purpose
Sharma teaches that purpose is not found — it is uncovered. It lies within you already. The question is not "What is my purpose?" but "What am I here to give?" Your purpose is at the intersection of your unique gifts and the needs of the world around you. Purpose is revealed through introspection, not external searching.

## Case: The Wagon of Service
The story of the wagon of service: a man stops pushing his own wagon and begins helping others push theirs. He finds that as he helps others, his own wagon moves forward too. The metaphor: serving others is not a distraction from your own success — it is the path to it.

## Case: Living Fully
Sharma's seventh virtue is "Embrace the Present." The past is gone. The future is not guaranteed. The only moment that exists is now. Living fully requires presence. Most people live somewhere else — in the past (regret) or the future (anxiety). The present is where life happens.

## Case: The Three Questions
Sharma teaches three questions for finding purpose: What would I do if I knew I could not fail? What gives me the deepest sense of fulfillment? What would I do with my life if money were no object? The answers to these questions point toward your purpose. Most people never ask them.
