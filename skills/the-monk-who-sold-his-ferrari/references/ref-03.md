# Reference: Kaizen and Discipline

## Overview
Kaizen — continuous improvement through small incremental changes — is one of the most powerful principles for personal growth.

## Case: The Kaizen Principle
Kaizen originated in Japan. It means "continuous improvement through small, consistent steps." Sharma applies it to personal development. Instead of trying to change your life overnight, focus on improving 1% each day. Small wins build momentum. 1% improvement each day compounds into 37x improvement over a year.

## Case: The Power of Rituals
Sharma teaches that rituals — not goals — are the key to lasting change. A goal is a destination. A ritual is a daily practice. The 10 ancient rituals for radiant living include: solitude, physical exercise, nutritious eating, continuous learning, acts of service, morning reflection, and evening reflection.

## Case: Discipline
Discipline is the ability to do what needs to be done when it needs to be done, whether you feel like it or not. Sharma calls it "the bridge between goals and accomplishment." Without discipline, knowledge is useless. The discipline of daily rituals — even when you do not feel like it — is the path to mastery.

## Case: The Morning Ritual
The morning ritual is the most important daily practice. Before the world intrudes, spend time in solitude. Set your intention for the day. Reflect on your purpose. Visualize your success. The first hour of the day sets the tone for the remaining 23. Winners start their day with intention.
