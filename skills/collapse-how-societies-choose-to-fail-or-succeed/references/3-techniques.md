# Techniques for Avoiding Collapse

**Technique 1: Long-Term Thinking.** Past societies focused on short-term gains and collapsed. Sustainable societies plan for the long term — decades and centuries ahead, not just the next season.

**Technique 2: Monitor the Environment.** Know what is happening to your resource base. The Easter Islanders did not realize they were cutting down their last tree. Modern societies have the tools to measure environmental change — but often choose not to use them.

**Technique 3: Be Willing to Change.** The most critical factor. When evidence shows a problem, change course. The Greenland Vikings had evidence that their way of life was failing. They did not change. Icelanders had the same evidence. They did change. The difference was choice.

**Technique 4: Listen to Dissent.** Societies that suppress dissenting voices are more likely to collapse. The Greenland Vikings did not tolerate those who suggested adopting Inuit ways. Icelanders were more open to new ideas.

**Case: Iceland. Changed from European farming to Inuit-influenced techniques. Changed their social structure. Their willingness to change saved them from the fate of Greenland.**

**Case: Japan's Response to the West. Japan recognized the threat of Western colonization in the 1850s. They modernized rapidly — changing their military, economy, and social structure. The willingness to change preserved their independence.**
**Case: Tikopia. The Polynesian island of Tikopia managed its resources sustainably for 3,000 years by recognizing limits and living within them.**
