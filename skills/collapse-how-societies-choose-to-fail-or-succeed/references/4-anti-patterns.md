# Common Mistakes — Anti-Patterns

**Anti-Pattern 1: Elite Isolation.** The rich and powerful are insulated from the consequences of their decisions. They do not feel the effects of environmental degradation because they can import food and goods. They ignore warning signs that they themselves do not experience.

**Anti-Pattern 2: Short-Term Thinking.** The next election, the next quarterly report, the next harvest. Leaders focus on immediate problems and defer long-term issues. By the time the long-term problem becomes urgent, it may be too late.

**Anti-Pattern 3: Denial.** "It cannot happen here." Every collapsed society believed it was special. The Maya had survived for centuries. Easter Island had always had trees. Greenland had always been habitable. Denial is a powerful force.

**Anti-Pattern 4: Groupthink.** When everyone in power agrees, dissenting views are suppressed. The Greenland Norse leaders rejected Inuit techniques because they threatened their cultural identity.

**Case: The Maya Elite. Continued building monuments while their civilization collapsed. They did not recognize the crisis because they did not experience its effects.**

**Case: Modern Denial of Climate Change. The pattern is identical to past societies: evidence is available, but powerful interests deny it, delay action, and suppress dissent. The outcome will be the same unless the pattern is broken.**
**Case: Modern China. China faces environmental challenges at unprecedented scale. Diamond asks whether it can adapt in time.**
