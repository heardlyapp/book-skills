# Principles of Societal Survival

**Principle 1: Adapt or Die.** The Greenland Vikings stuck to European farming practices in a non-European environment. They raised cattle and built churches while the climate turned against them. The Icelanders, faced with similar conditions, adapted their farming and survived. The willingness to change is the difference between survival and collapse.

**Principle 2: Environmental Limits Are Real.** Every society operates within environmental constraints. Ignoring those limits does not make them disappear. Easter Island had a finite number of trees. When they were gone, the society collapsed. There was no backup plan, no alternative.

**Principle 3: Elite Signals Are Deceptive.** When a society's elite are insulated from the consequences of their decisions, they continue making bad choices. The Maya kings built more monuments while their population starved. The Greenland chiefs imported luxury goods from Europe while their colony froze.

**Principle 4: Long-Term Thinking Is Rare.** Most societies focus on short-term survival. The next harvest, the next season, the next king. Problems that develop over decades or centuries are invisible to leaders who think in years.

**Case: Greenland vs. Iceland. Same Norse culture, different outcomes. Iceland adapted. Greenland did not. The difference was the willingness to change.**

**Case: The Maya. The elite built monumental architecture while the environment degraded. They did not recognize the crisis until it was too late.**
