# 2. Principles — Learning to Be Seen

> Source: "Keeping the Moon" by Sarah Dessen (1999). Principles drawn from Colie's summer journey.

## The 7 Principles of Keeping the Moon

### Principle 1: External Change Does Not Create Internal Change
Colie lost 25 pounds. She still felt like the fat girl. The novel's central insight: the body changes faster than the self-image. Losing weight does not automatically heal the wounds that made you hate yourself.

### Principle 2: Friendship Is a Skill You Can Learn
Colie arrives in Colby unable to make friends. She doesn't know how to talk to people, how to be vulnerable, how to accept kindness. Isabel and Morgan teach her — not by explaining but by including her.

### Principle 3: Standing Up for Yourself Is a Single Decision
The climax is a moment. Colie decides to fight back. The moment is terrifying — but it's also simple. Courage is not the absence of fear; it's acting despite it.

> **Case: The Party Confrontation** (Chapters 14-15): At a party, the popular girls mock Colie's weight loss, accusing her of being anorexic. The old Colie would have shrunk away. Instead, she faces them: "I used to be afraid of you. But I'm not anymore." The confrontation is brief but decisive. It is the moment everything changes.
> **Key takeaway**: One act of courage can undo years of self-doubt.

### Principle 4: Eccentricity Is Often Wisdom in Disguise
Aunt Mira collects broken items and turns them into art. She is the town oddball. But she is also the most centered, wisest character in the book.

### Principle 5: Mothers Can Be Wrong
Kiki Sparks is not a villain — she truly loves her daughter. But she is also self-absorbed, image-obsessed, and emotionally absent. The novel does not demonize her but shows the damage that even well-intentioned neglect can cause.

> **Case: Kiki's Visit** (Chapter 10): When Kiki visits Colby, she is horrified by Mira's house and concerned about Colie's job. She offers to take Colie to Europe. Colie's choice to stay in Colby — to finish her summer working at the Burrito — is her first act of independence.
> **Key takeaway**: Saying no to what is comfortable is sometimes the most important yes you can say to yourself.

### Principle 6: You Can Choose Your Family
Aunt Mira is not blood-close — she is Kiki's sister, but Colie barely knows her. By the end of the summer, Mira has become more of a mother to Colie than Kiki ever was. Found family is real family.

### Principle 7: The Moon Cannot Be Kept — Only Enjoyed
The title's meaning: you can't possess the moon. You can't keep it in a jar. But you can sit in its light and appreciate it. This is also the novel's message about life: you can't hold onto summer, beauty, or youth. You can only experience them while they last.
