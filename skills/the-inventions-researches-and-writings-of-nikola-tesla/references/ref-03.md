# Reference: Wireless Transmission & Radio

## Overview
Tesla was a pioneer of wireless technology whose contributions were long overshadowed by Marconi. The story of how Tesla invented the fundamental principles of radio — and how Marconi used them without credit — is one of the great scientific controversies of the early 20th century.

## Case: The Tuned Circuit Patent
Tesla filed US Patent No. 645,576 in 1897 for a "System of Transmission of Electrical Energy" using tuned circuits — matched oscillators on the transmitter and receiver. This is the fundamental principle behind radio. Marconi's early system used a spark-gap transmitter (unpredictable frequency) and a coherer receiver (unreliable). Marconi's later, successful transatlantic transmission (1901) used Tesla's tuned-circuit technology. When Marconi won the Nobel Prize in 1909, Tesla sued. In 1943, the US Supreme Court upheld Tesla's patent, ruling that Marconi had infringed.

## Case: Colorado Springs Experiments
In 1899, Tesla built an experimental station in Colorado Springs. He generated 130-foot artificial lightning bolts, produced the first man-made ball lightning, and measured standing waves in the earth — proving that the planet itself could resonate electrically. He attempted to transmit power wirelessly by lighting 200 lamps from a coil 25 miles away. The experiment demonstrated that wireless power transmission was physically possible, but the project was abandoned when the local power plant's generators were damaged by Tesla's experiments.

## Case: Wardenclyffe Tower
Tesla's most ambitious project was Wardenclyffe Tower on Long Island (1901-1905). Funded by J.P. Morgan, the tower was intended to transmit both signals and power wirelessly around the world. Tesla envisioned it as the beginning of a global wireless energy system. But Marconi's transatlantic transmission in 1901 made Morgan reconsider: if signals could be sent without expensive towers, why fund Tesla's grand vision? Morgan withdrew support, and the tower was never completed. Tesla spent his remaining years trying (and failing) to secure funding for its completion.
