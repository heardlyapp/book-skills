# Reference: Tesla's Life & Character

## Overview
Tesla's life story is the core narrative that gives context to all his inventions. Born in 1856 to Serbian parents in the Austrian Empire (modern Croatia), he showed early signs of his extraordinary visual memory and ability to conceptualize machines without drawings.

## Case: The Edison Rivalry
Tesla worked for Edison in 1884-1885. Edison promised him $50,000 to redesign the DC generators. When Tesla succeeded, Edison reneged, saying: "When you become a full-fledged American, you will appreciate an American joke." Tesla quit and spent his next year digging ditches. This experience shaped his lifelong contempt for Edison and his commitment to AC, which would defeat Edison's DC system.

## Case: Westinghouse Patronage
George Westinghouse bought Tesla's AC patents for $60,000 plus royalties ($2.50 per horsepower). When Westinghouse faced financial pressure during the 1890s panic, Tesla voluntarily tore up his royalty contract to save the company. Westinghouse kept the Tesla name on the AC system. This was Tesla's most generous act — and his most financially disastrous.

## Case: The Later Years
By the 1930s, Tesla was living in poverty at the New Yorker Hotel, feeding pigeons, and making increasingly fantastic claims about death rays and interplanetary communication. His birthday parties became media events where he announced new "inventions" that never materialized. The tragedy was not that his mind failed but that his credibility failed while his mind remained sharp.
