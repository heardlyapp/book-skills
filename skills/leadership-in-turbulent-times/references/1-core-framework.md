# The Four Presidents

> Source: Leadership, Foreword, Parts I, II, III

## Core Framework

### The Four Paths to Leadership

| President | Background | Ambition | Crisis |
|-----------|------------|----------|--------|
| Abraham Lincoln | Poverty, self-educated | "Every man is said to have his peculiar ambition" | Civil War |
| Theodore Roosevelt | Privilege, Harvard | "I rose like a rocket" | Coal Strike of 1902 |
| Franklin Roosevelt | Wealth, political family | "No, call me Franklin" | Great Depression |
| Lyndon Johnson | Texas hill country | "A steam engine in pants" | Civil Rights |

> **Case: Lincoln's Depression** (Chapter 5)
> In 1835, Lincoln was a promising young legislator in Illinois. He fell in love with Ann Rutledge. She died. Then his political career suffered a devastating defeat. Then his engagement to Mary Todd collapsed. Lincoln fell into a profound depression that his friends called "the hypo" — a near-suicidal despondency. He was bedridden for weeks. His neighbors feared for his life. He recovered by making himself useful: he threw himself into his legal practice, borrowed books on mathematics and science, and emerged with a deeper understanding of human suffering.
> **Key takeaway:** Lincoln's depression was not a weakness — it was a forge. The man who emerged from "the hypo" had an empathy for suffering that would sustain him through the Civil War. He became the most compassionate of presidents because he had been the most despairing of men.

> **Case: TR's Double Blow** (Chapter 6)
> On February 14, 1884, Theodore Roosevelt lost his mother (to typhoid) and his wife (to kidney failure) in the same house, on the same day. He wrote in his diary: "The light has gone out of my life." He left politics and moved to the Badlands of North Dakota to ranch, hunt, and grieve. But he also wrote books, built a cattle business, and formed a new vision of himself. When he returned to politics two years later, he was transformed: more patient, more focused, and with a profound awareness of life's fragility.
> **Key takeaway:** TR could have stayed in the Badlands — many would have. He chose to return. The two years of solitude were not escape — they were preparation. The grief did not break him; it deepened him.
