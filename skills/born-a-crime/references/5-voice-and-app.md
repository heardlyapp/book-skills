# 5 — Voice & App

> Source: *Born a Crime* by Trevor Noah — voice and scenarios.

## Trevor's Voice

1. **Comedian's distance on tragedy.** Trevor can tell you about getting thrown from a moving car — and argue with his mom about God's will while bleeding on the sidewalk. The humor doesn't diminish the trauma; it makes it survivable.
2. **The lawyer inside.** Even as a child, he found loopholes. He questioned everything. "I felt like every rule that didn't make sense should be challenged."
3. **Raw honesty without self-pity.** He describes Mopane worms, the garage floor, Abel's beatings — and never asks for sympathy. He just reports it.
4. **Unconditional love for his mother, without idealization.** He loves Patricia. He also shows her flaws: her stubbornness, her inability to leave Abel, her religious rigidity that once almost got them killed.

## Five Scenarios

### 1. The Person Caught Between Two Worlds

**Situation:** You grew up in one culture but now live in another. You don't fully belong to either.

**Playbook:** The Chameleon strategy — learn the languages of both worlds. Code-switch without losing yourself. Choose your tribe. At H.A. Jack, Trevor chose black. You get to choose too.

### 2. The Person in an Abusive Relationship

**Situation:** Your partner is charming in public, but hurts you in private. You keep making excuses.

**Playbook:** Recognize the Abel Cycle. You can't fix him. The police may not help. You need an exit plan. The pantry check: always keep the door within reach. Find your person (your "Andrew" who will stand with you, or your "Trevor" who will run with you).

### 3. The First-Generation Success

**Situation:** You're the first in your family to achieve something. Everyone wants a piece.

**Playbook:** Patricia's path — you must build your foundation before you can lift anyone. The Black Tax is real. Sometimes you must run away to build your own life. You can come back for your family later.

### 4. The Parent Trying to Raise a Confident Child

**Situation:** You want your child to believe the world is open to them. But the world keeps telling them no.

**Playbook:** Patricia showed Trevor the ice rink even though they couldn't skate. She took him to the drive-in. She gave him books. She made him feel that the ghetto was not the world. That's the job.

### 5. The Survivor Who Can't Stop Looking Back

**Situation:** You went through terrible things. You can't stop thinking about them.

**Playbook:** "Forget the pain but remember the lesson." Trevor didn't hold onto the trauma of the fires he set or the beatings he received. He didn't deny them. He just didn't carry them forward. Every day is a new day to get up to some shit.
