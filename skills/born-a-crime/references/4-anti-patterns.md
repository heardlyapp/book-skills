# 4 — Anti-Patterns

> Source: *Born a Crime* by Trevor Noah — Chapters 1-18.

## Common Errors

### 1. Believing the Labels You Were Given

**The trap:** "I am what the system says I am." Apartheid said Trevor was illegal. The church said he couldn't take communion. The school said he belonged in the A classes.

**Why it fails:** Labels are tools of control. Trevor's mother taught him to examine every label and question its validity. "I was born a crime" — but the crime was the system's, not his.

### 2. The "He's Charming with Everyone Else So It Must Be Me" Trap

**The trap:** "He's so nice to everyone. The problem must be me."

**Why it fails:** Abel was beloved by the world. That made it nearly impossible for Patricia and Trevor to process his violence. "If you think someone is a monster and the whole world says he's a saint, you begin to think that you're the bad person."

### 3. Paying the Black Tax Indefinitely

**The trap:** "My family sacrificed for me. I owe them everything I earn."

**Why it fails:** Patricia's mother took her entire salary. She had to run away to build her own life. Supporting family is noble — but if it prevents you from ever getting ahead, the system is broken.

### 4. Trusting the System to Protect You

**The trap:** "The police will help. The law is on my side."

**Why it fails:** The police refused to file Patricia's charge against Abel. "This is a family thing." The system that was built to maintain apartheid was not going to save a black woman from her abusive husband.

### 5. The "I Can Fix Him / Save Him" Delusion

**The trap:** "If I just love him enough, he'll change."

**Why it fails:** Patricia believed she could help Abel succeed. She poured her savings into his business. He continued drinking. He continued hitting. "She poured herself into his failed dream."

### 6. Confusing Discipline with Abuse

**The trap:** "All punishment is the same. All violence is the same."

**Why it fails:** Trevor distinguishes clearly. His mother beat him with love and purpose. Abel beat him with rage and terror. The difference is not in the blow — it's in the intent. One says "you made a mistake." The other says "I want to destroy you."
