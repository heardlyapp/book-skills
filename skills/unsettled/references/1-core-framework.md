# Core Framework — Unsettled

## The Thesis

Steven E. Koonin (Caltech PhD, Obama Undersecretary for Science at DOE, former BP chief scientist) argues that "The Science" is far less settled than the public believes. The chain from research → assessment reports → media summaries → public understanding distorts the truth at every step. The result: "I do not think 'The Science' says what you think it says."

**The four climate questions:** (1) How have humans influenced the climate? (2) How does the climate respond? (3) What will the impact be? (4) What can we do about it? Each answer depends on the previous one, so the uncertainty compounds.

**The APS workshop (2014):** Koonin convened six leading climate experts and six physicists to "stress test" climate science. Findings: models disagree with each other and with observations; data is poor; expert judgment was used to paper over gaps; press releases don't match the reports. "I came away not only surprised, but shaken."

## Part I: The Science

### Chapter 1: What We Know About Warming
The globe has warmed about 1ºC (1.8ºF) since 1900. CO2 has risen from 280 ppm to 410 ppm. These are not disputed. But: "Most of the warming occurred before 1950 or after 1980, with a pause (the 'hiatus') from roughly 2000-2014." The data before 1979 (satellite era) is sparse and uncertain.

### Chapter 2: Humble Human Influences
Human greenhouse gas emissions exert a warming influence. But that influence is "physically small" — about 2% of the natural greenhouse effect. The climate's "sensitivity" (how much warming results from doubled CO2) is uncertain by a factor of 3 or more. Natural variability (volcanoes, solar, ocean cycles) is poorly understood and may be as large as human influences.

### Chapter 3: Emissions Explained and Extrapolated
CO2 stays in the atmosphere for centuries. Even stabilizing emissions at current levels won't stabilize concentrations — they'll keep rising. "The connection between CO2 emissions and concentration diminishes the prospect of even stabilizing growing human influences."

### Chapter 4: Many Muddled Models
Models are the primary tool for projecting future climate. "They give results that differ significantly not only from each other but from observations." As models have become more elaborate (higher resolution, more physics), their projections have actually become more divergent, not less. "In fact, the results have become more divergent with each generation of models."

### Chapter 5: Hyping the Heat
US record high temperatures: "No more common today than they were in 1900." The warmest temperatures in the US have not risen in the past 50 years. Koonin traces how an NCA report claimed the opposite — through selective statistics and wording. "When I tell people this, most are incredulous. Some gasp. And some get downright hostile."

### Chapter 6: Tempest Terrors
Hurricanes: "Humans have had no detectable impact on hurricanes over the past century." The IPCC itself says "low confidence" in any human influence on hurricane activity. The data shows no trend in frequency or intensity of landfalling US hurricanes since 1900. Yet the media reports the opposite.

### Chapter 7: Precipitation Perils
Global burned area: "The global area burned by fires each year has declined by 25 percent since observations began in 1998." Floods: no clear trend in US flooding over the past century. Drought: the US has not become more drought-prone.

### Chapter 8: Sea Level Scares
Sea levels have been rising for millennia at about 1 foot/century. The current rate is perhaps 1.5 feet/century — modest acceleration, but not unprecedented. The IPCC projects 1-4 feet by 2100. "It's very hard to believe that surging seas will drown the coasts anytime soon."

### Chapter 9: Apocalypses That Ain't
Three claimed catastrophic impacts:
- **Fatalities:** Climate-related deaths have declined 98% over the past century (from improved resilience, not despite warming)
- **Famine:** Global food production per capita has risen 25% since 1970, even as the population grew
- **Economic impact:** The IPCC projects net economic impact of human-induced climate change will be "modest through at least the end of this century" — about 0.2-2% of GDP

### Chapter 10: Who Broke "The Science"?
Multiple actors: environmental activists (Paul Watson: "It doesn't matter what is true, it only matters what people believe is true"), scientists who confuse advocacy with objectivity (Schneider's "double ethical bind"), media that profits from alarm, and politicians who exploit fear. "Activism masquerading as The Science is pernicious."

## Part II: The Response

### Chapter 12: The Chimera of Carbon-Free
The Paris Agreement goals are not being met. Global CO2 emissions continued rising through 2019. Even if all nations met their pledges, warming would be reduced by only ~0.2ºC by 2100. "Meaningful emission reductions are far harder than the public realizes."

### Chapter 14: Plans B
Adaptation is inevitable and already happening — sea walls, resilient crops, air conditioning. Geoengineering (solar radiation management, carbon capture) could be deployed in extremis. These are not alternatives to emissions reduction but pragmatic complements.

### Closing Thoughts
Koonin's recommendations: (1) Improve climate science, especially observations and models. (2) Subject assessment reports to adversarial ("Red Team") review. (3) Separate science from advocacy. (4) Pursue adaptation and geoengineering alongside emissions reduction. (5) Be honest about uncertainties.
