# Anti-Patterns — Unsettled

## The Central Error: Treating Climate Science as Settled

"The science is insufficient to make useful projections about how the climate will change over the coming decades, much less what effect our actions will have on it." The fundamental mistake is claiming certainty where uncertainty exists.

### Mistake 1: "The models are reliable"
Models disagree with each other and with observations. They've become more divergent as they've become more elaborate. Yet the media treats model projections as gospel. "As our models have become more elaborate, their descriptions of the future have become less certain."

### Mistake 2: "Climate change is an existential crisis"
The IPCC itself projects modest economic impacts (0.2-2% of GDP through 2100). Climate-related deaths have declined 98%. Food production is up 25%. Koonin: "If you're like most people, after the surprise wears off, you'll wonder why you're surprised."

### Mistake 3: "We need to shut down fossil fuels immediately"
"Even if all nations met their Paris pledges, warming would be reduced by only ~0.2ºC by 2100." A rapid transition to zero-carbon is a "chimera." The energy system is enormous and slow to change.

### Mistake 4: "Average temperature tells the whole story"
Global average temperature is useful but limited. "Most of the warming occurred before 1950 or after 1980, with a pause from 2000-2014." Regional changes matter more than global averages, and they vary enormously.

### Mistake 5: "Jason-3 certification" — political capture of science
Koonin cites the infamous "Jason-3 certification" episode: a group of well-known scientists refused to certify a sea-level satellite (Jason-3) because they disagreed with Trump administration policy. "When scientists use science to score political points, they corrupt it."

### Mistake 6: "There's no harm in exaggeration if it saves the planet"
Schneider's "double ethical bind" — exaggerating to get attention. Koonin: "It is the height of hubris." Philip Handler: "When scientists fail to recognize these boundaries, their own ideological beliefs, usually unspoken, easily becloud seemingly scientific debate."

### Mistake 7: "Adaptation means giving up"
Koonin: "Plan B strategies are not defeatist — they are pragmatic." Adaptation (sea walls, resilient crops) and geoengineering are necessary complements to emissions reduction. Ignoring them is not virtuous — it's negligent.
