# Key Principles — Unsettled

## I. The Science Is Less Settled Than You Think

Koonin's central claim: "The science is insufficient to make useful projections about how the climate will change over the coming decades, much less what effect our actions will have on it." The "consensus" narrative obscures the real uncertainties in climate sensitivity, model projections, and natural variability.

## II. The Chain of Communication Distorts the Truth

Research → assessment reports → summaries → press releases → media reporting → public understanding. At each step, caveats are dropped, nuances vanish, and uncertainties become certainties. Koonin: "The public gets their climate information almost exclusively from the media; very few people actually read the assessment summaries, let alone the reports."

## III. Models Are Useful But Not Reliable

"Multitudes of climate models disagree with, or even contradict, each other and many kinds of observations." As models have become more elaborate, their results have become more divergent. Policymakers should not base trillion-dollar decisions on models that "differ significantly not only from each other but from observations."

## IV. Uncertainty Is Not Ignorance — It's Information

Schneider's "double ethical bind" is wrong. Scientists should not exaggerate for effect. "It is the height of hubris for a scientist even to consider deliberately misinforming policy discussions in service of what they believe to be ethical." Every measurement has a margin of error. Hiding it is dishonest.

## V. Climate Impacts Are Modest and Manageable

Koonin's most provocative claims: US heat records are not increasing. Hurricanes show no human signal. Global burned area has declined 25% since 1998. Climate-related deaths have dropped 98% over a century. Food production per capita is up 25%. Economic impact is projected at 0.2-2% of GDP. "The net economic impact of human-induced climate change will be minimal through at least the end of this century."

## VI. The Energy Transition Is Harder Than Promised

"Meaningful emission reductions are far harder than the public realizes." Paris Agreement goals are not being met. Even if they were, the effect on warming would be ~0.2ºC by 2100. Zero-carbon is "the chimera." The public has been sold a false hope that a rapid transition is easy and cheap.

## VII. Adaptation and Geoengineering Are Necessary

"Plans B" are not defeatist — they are pragmatic. Sea walls, resilient crops, air conditioning, solar radiation management, carbon capture. "We need to prepare for future climate changes, whether natural or caused by humans." Emissions reduction alone is insufficient.
