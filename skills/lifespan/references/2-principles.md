# 2. Principles — The Science of Longevity

> Source: "Lifespan" by David Sinclair (2019). Principles drawn from the book.

## The 7 Principles

### Principle 1: Aging Is Reversible
The central claim of the book: aging is plastic, not fixed. It can be slowed, stopped, and potentially reversed.

### Principle 2: Stress Activates Repair
Hormesis — mild stress — triggers the body's repair mechanisms. Exercise, fasting, cold exposure, and heat all activate longevity pathways.

> **Case: The Caloric Restriction Studies** (Part I): Mice fed 30% fewer calories live 30% longer. The survival circuit evolved to protect organisms during famine. When food is scarce, the body prioritizes repair over reproduction.
> **Key takeaway**: What doesn't kill you makes you live longer. Mild stress is beneficial; extreme stress is harmful.

### Principle 3: NAD+ Is the Fuel of Longevity
NAD+ is a molecule that sirtuins need to function. NAD+ levels decline with age. Boosting NAD+ (with NMN or NR) activates sirtuins and may slow aging.

### Principle 4: Epigenetic Information Is Fragile
The epigenome is not as stable as DNA. It can be disrupted by diet, toxins, radiation, and simply the passage of time.

### Principle 5: Longevity Genes Are Conserved
The same longevity genes (sirtuins, mTOR, AMPK) exist in yeast, worms, flies, and humans. What works in animals likely works in us.

> **Case: The Yeast Experiment** (Part I): Sinclair first identified sirtuins in yeast. By adding an extra copy of the SIR2 gene, yeast lived 30% longer. This single experiment launched a field.
> **Key takeaway**: Fundamental biology is conserved across species. Discoveries in simple organisms often apply to humans.

### Principle 6: The Regulatory System Blocks Progress
The FDA does not recognize aging as a disease. This means longevity treatments cannot be approved for "aging." They must be approved for specific diseases.

### Principle 7: Longevity Requires a Multi-Pronged Approach
There is no single pill. Sinclair takes multiple supplements, practices intermittent fasting, exercises, and monitors his biomarkers. Longevity is a system, not a substance.
