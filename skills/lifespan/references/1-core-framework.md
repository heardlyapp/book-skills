# 1. Core Framework — The Information Theory of Aging

> Source: "Lifespan" by David Sinclair (2019). Part I presents the core scientific framework.

## The Information Theory

Sinclair's central argument: aging is not caused by accumulated damage (the traditional view). It is caused by loss of epigenetic information.

**The analogy**: Your DNA is a hard drive containing all the data needed to build and maintain your body. The epigenome is the operating system that reads the hard drive. Over time, the OS becomes corrupted. DNA is read incorrectly. Cells forget their function.

> **Case: The Vision Reversal Experiment** (Part I): Sinclair's lab reversed age-related vision loss in old mice. They delivered three Yamanaka factors (genes that reprogram cells) to the optic nerve via gene therapy. The old mice could see again. This demonstrated that aging is reversible at the tissue level.
> **Key takeaway**: If aging were irreversible damage, you could not restore vision. The fact that Sinclair reversed it proves that aging is an information problem, not a damage problem.

## The Survival Circuit

The body has a built-in longevity system — the survival circuit. When the body senses stress (lack of food, cold, heat, exercise), it activates sirtuins and other repair mechanisms.

> **Case: The Resveratrol Discovery** (Part I): Sinclair discovered that resveratrol (a molecule in red wine) activates sirtuins. This led to a decade of research showing that sirtuin activation extends lifespan in yeast, worms, flies, and mice.
> **Key takeaway**: Molecules that activate the body's survival circuit can extend lifespan across multiple species. This suggests the mechanism is fundamental.
