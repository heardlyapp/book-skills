# 3. Techniques — The Longevity Protocol

> Source: "Lifespan" by David Sinclair (2019). Practical longevity interventions.

## Sinclair's Personal Protocol

David Sinclair takes the following supplements (as described in the book):
- 1 gram NMN (nicotinamide mononucleotide) daily
- 1 gram resveratrol daily
- 1 gram metformin daily (prescription)
- Vitamin D, vitamin K2

> **Case: The NMN Study** (Part II): Sinclair's lab showed that NMN reverses multiple markers of aging in mice. In human trials (ongoing at publication), early results suggest NMN improves metabolic health.
> **Key takeaway**: NAD+ boosters like NMN are among the most promising longevity interventions, but human data is still emerging.

## Lifestyle Interventions

- **Intermittent fasting**: Sinclair skips breakfast, eats within an 8-hour window
- **Cold exposure**: Cold showers and ice baths activate brown fat and survival pathways
- **Exercise**: High-intensity interval training and resistance training
- **Temperature variation**: Sauna and cold exposure both activate hormetic pathways

> **Case: The Cold Bath** (Part I): Sinclair describes taking cold baths to activate his survival circuit. Cold exposure increases norepinephrine and activates sirtuins. He recommends 11 minutes of cold exposure per week.
> **Key takeaway**: Simple, inexpensive interventions can activate the same pathways as expensive supplements.

## The Metabolic Approach

Sinclair recommends monitoring several biomarkers:
- **Fasting glucose**: Should be low (under 90 mg/dL)
- **Insulin levels**: Should be low, indicating insulin sensitivity
- **Inflammation markers**: CRP and others should be in the optimal range
- **NAD+ levels**: Can be measured and boosted with NMN

> **Case: The Biomarker Tracking** (Part II): Sinclair tracks his own biomarkers religiously. He adjusts his protocol based on the data. This personalized approach — measure, intervene, measure again — is the scientific method applied to one's own body.
> **Key takeaway**: Measure what matters. You cannot manage what you do not measure. Personal biomarkers are the foundation of a longevity protocol.
