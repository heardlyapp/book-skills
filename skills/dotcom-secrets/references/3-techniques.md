# 3 — Techniques

> Source: *DotCom Secrets* by Russell Brunson.

## Practical Tools

### 1. The Value Ladder Blueprint

Map out 5+ rungs of your offer sequence:
- **Bait** — Free or shipping-only (free book, free session, free assessment)
- **Frontend** — Low-ticket ($7-$97, the core product)
- **Middle** — Mid-ticket ($100-$1,000, home study course, group coaching)
- **Backend** — High-ticket ($1,000-$10,000+, intensive, retreat, consulting)
- **Continuity** — Recurring ($27-$97/month, membership, subscription, retainer)

> **Case: Brunson's Own Value Ladder** (Secret #2): Brunson's company ladder: free book (shipping only, $9.95) → home study courses → live events ($500-$2,000) → Ignite program ($10,000) → Inner Circle ($25,000) → Done-With-You consulting ($100,000 + 10% of profits). The total value from one customer: potentially $1 million+.
> **Key takeaway**: Build your ladder before you need it. You don't need all rungs on day one, but you need the blueprint.

> **Case: The 7-Game Soap Opera** (Secret #7): Brunson used a 7-email sequence to sell a continuity program. Each email introduced one "game" — a segment of the larger curriculum. Email 1 ended with: "In email 2, I'll show you Game 1's secret weapon." The sequence turned a simple program into a 7-day event. By email 7, the anticipation was unbearable.
> **Key takeaway**: Break your product into its component parts and reveal them one per email. Each reveal is a reason to open the next one.

Every funnel needs these phases: (1) Traffic source, (2) Opt-in (squeeze page), (3) Frontend sale (the core offer), (4) Upsell (enhance the frontend), (5) Downsell (discounted version of the upsell), (6) Continuity (recurring commitment), (7) Backend (highest-ticket offer).

### 3. The Soap Opera Sequence Structure

7 emails: Email 1 — Open a loop (introduce a problem or story). Email 2 — Tease more. Email 3 — Deepen the story. Email 4 — Delay and build tension. Email 5 — Almost resolve. Email 6 — The twist. Email 7 — Close the loop and present the offer. Each email ends with a cliffhanger.

### 4. The Dream Customer Avatar

Write out: age, gender, income, location, education, job title, goals, fears, frustrations, aspirations, what they hate, what they desire most, what they read, where they hang out online, who they follow. Build the product for this ONE person.

### 5. The Daily Seinfeld Sequence

A variation of the Soap Opera Sequence for ongoing daily engagement. Not a story with an arc — just entertaining, connection-building content that keeps you top of mind until the customer is ready to buy.
