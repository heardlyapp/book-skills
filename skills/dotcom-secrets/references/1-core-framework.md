# 1 — Core Framework

> Source: *DotCom Secrets* by Russell Brunson.

## The Three Frameworks

### Framework A: The Value Ladder (Secrets 1-3, Introduction)

The core concept of the book: a sequenced offer system where customers start with low/no-cost "bait" and gradually ascend to higher-ticket offers. Each rung delivers value that justifies the price increase.

> **Case: The Dentist's Value Ladder** (Secret #2): Brunson went to a new dentist for a free teeth cleaning. The dentist noticed his yellow teeth and sold him custom whitening trays. Then noticed his shifting bottom teeth and sold him retainers. Total bill: over $2,000. Brunson had walked in for a free cleaning. The dentist had built a perfect Value Ladder: free frontend → low-ticket whitening → mid-ticket retainers → high-ticket cosmetic surgery (potential) → continuity (6-month checkup).

> **Case: The Chiropractor's Transformation** (Secret #2): Chiropractor Chad Woolner only did $50 adjustments. He had nothing else to offer. Brunson helped him see that his real value was in his full knowledge — nutrition, natural healing, fibromyalgia treatment. They created a Value Ladder: free massage (bait) → adjustment ($50) → wellness program ($5,000). The frontend massage therapists were trained to identify when a client needed an adjustment. The practice was transformed.

### Framework B: The Attractive Character (Secret #6)

A persona — real or semi-fictional — that attracts your dream customers. Not a brand, not a logo. A person with a story, a journey, and an arc. Your customers want to become this person, be their friend, or have their life.

> **Case: The Online Marketing Wizard** (Foreword, Secret #6): Brunson built his Attractive Character as "the tall, goofy guy who figured out online marketing." Dan Kennedy's foreword frames the book as revealing the secrets of the "Online Marketing Wizard Fraternity." Brunson positions himself not as a guru, but as the wizard who pulled back the curtain. His origin story: he sold potato gun instructions online. That vulnerability and relatability became his Attractive Character.

### Framework C: The Soap Opera Sequence (Secret #7)

A 7-email storytelling sequence that opens a loop in email 1, keeps it open through sequential cliffhangers, and doesn't close it until the final email — where the sales pitch lives. Built on the principle that an open loop creates tension that the brain wants to resolve.

> **Case: The 7-Game Email Sequence** (Secret #7): Brunson describes selling a continuity program called the "7-Game." The Soap Opera Sequence opened with "Game 1" in email 1, teased Game 2, delayed resolution, built anticipation over 7 emails. The prospect had to read all 7 to get the full picture. By email 7, the customer had invested so much time and emotional energy that the purchase felt natural.
