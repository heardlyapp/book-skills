# 5 — Voice & App

> Source: *DotCom Secrets* by Russell Brunson.

## Brunson's Voice

Brunson writes as the "tall, goofy guy who figured out online marketing." His tone is conversational, direct, and unpolished. He tells personal stories with self-deprecating humor. The book feels like sitting across from a friend who happens to know a ton about sales funnels. He's not a guru lecturing from a mountain — he's the guy who tried 500 things, failed at 400, and is sharing what worked.

> **Case: The Potato Gun Origin** (Introduction, Secret #6): Brunson's first online product was a DVD about building potato guns. He doesn't hide this. He uses it as his origin story: "And that just goes to show you can sell anything online!" The story establishes that he started from nothing, had ridiculous ideas, and figured it out. It's humble, relatable, and disarming.

> **Case: The Yellow Teeth Story** (Secret #2): Brunson shares how a dentist upsold him from a free cleaning to $2,000+ in services. He doesn't present himself as the savvy marketer who saw through it. He presents himself as the normal guy who fell for a good funnel. The vulnerability makes the lesson stick: "This dentist had strategically taken me through a powerful process that I call a Value Ladder."

## Five Scenarios

### 1. The Solopreneur Starting from Zero

Build a Value Ladder starting with free content (bait), a low-ticket product ($7-$47), and an ascent plan. Don't worry about backend until the frontend works.

### 2. The Coach/Consultant

Your consultation is the backend high-ticket. The frontend needs a low-cost entry: a book, a course, a workshop. The Value Ladder lets people try you cheap before committing.

### 3. The SaaS Founder

Frontend: free trial or low monthly plan. Backend: enterprise plan. Continuity: monthly subscription is built in. The challenge is making the frontend so valuable that users naturally upgrade.

### 4. The Ecommerce Seller

Bait: free sample or discount code. Frontend: best-selling product. Upsell: accessories or bundles. Continuity: subscription for consumables.

### 5. The Content Creator

Your free content is the bait. Your paid course or membership is the middle. Your 1-on-1 coaching is the backend. The Soap Opera Sequence turns followers into customers.
