# 2 — Key Principles

> Source: *DotCom Secrets* by Russell Brunson.

## The 7 Principles

### 1. Fix the Funnel First

When your business isn't working, it's almost never a traffic or conversion problem. It's a funnel problem. Before tweaking headlines or ad targeting, fix the offer sequence.

> **Case: FitLife.tv Rescue** (Introduction): Drew Canole had 1.2 million Facebook followers. A Facebook algorithm change dropped his traffic 90%. He was spending $116 to sell a $97 product. Brunson's diagnosis: not a traffic or conversion problem. Fix the funnel to increase customer value. Add backend offers. Increase the amount he could spend to acquire a customer. The fix: build a Value Ladder so the $97 frontend became the start of a much larger customer relationship.

### 2. The Business That Can Spend the Most Wins

Your goal isn't to minimize customer acquisition cost (CAC). It's to maximize how much you can afford to spend. A better funnel = higher LTV = you can outspend every competitor.

### 3. Value Must Precede Price

Every step of the Value Ladder must deliver disproportionate value relative to price. If your $9.95 shipping-only offer doesn't feel like a steal, no one will ascend. If your $5,000 program doesn't feel like $50,000 of value, no one will buy.

### 4. Dream Customer First, Product Second

Don't create a product and search for buyers. Define your dream customer — age, income, desires, fears, worldviews — then create what they want.

### 5. The Character Is the Magnet

> **Case: The Potato Gun Story** (Secret #6): Brunson's first product online was a DVD on how to build a potato gun. He wasn't a "potato gun expert." But he built an Attractive Character around the idea: "the guy who builds things and has fun doing it." The character attracted a specific audience: DIY enthusiasts, teenagers, dads wanting projects. The product was secondary to the persona.

### 6. Continuity Is King

One-time sales fund today. Continuity (subscriptions, memberships, coaching retainers) funds every month. Build your Value Ladder with a recurring revenue rung.

### 7. Split Test Everything

Never assume you know what works. The only way to know is to test. Headlines, prices, offers, sequences — test them all.
