# 4 — Anti-Patterns

> Source: *DotCom Secrets* by Russell Brunson.

## Common Errors

### 1. Diagnosing the Wrong Problem

**The trap:** "My ads aren't working. I need better copy. I need more traffic."

**Why it fails:** Brunson's core thesis: it's almost never a traffic or conversion problem. It's a funnel problem. FitLife.tv had 1.2 million followers and was bleeding money. The fix wasn't more traffic or better headlines. It was building a proper Value Ladder.

> **Case: The $116-to-$97 Death Spiral** (Introduction): Drew Canole's company spent $116 to acquire a customer who bought a $97 product. Every sale lost $19. The instinct: cheaper ads, better targeting, more traffic. The real problem: no Value Ladder. Once they added backend offers, the $97 loss leader became profitable because customers bought more later.
> **Key takeaway**: Don't look at the first sale in isolation. One loss-making frontend sale is fine IF the backend recoups it.

**The trap:** "I only have one thing to sell. I don't need a funnel."

**Why it fails:** Brunson's chiropractor friend only did $50 adjustments. He didn't think he had a Value Ladder. Once he mapped out free massages (bait) and a $5,000 wellness program (backend), his business transformed. Everyone has more value to offer.

### 3. The Faceless Brand

**The trap:** "I'll build a professional company brand. No personal stuff."

**Why it fails:** People buy from people. The Attractive Character — a real persona with a story, not a logo — builds trust faster than any corporate brand ever could.

### 4. Selling Before Giving Value

**The trap:** "I'll pitch my product on the first email/website visit."

**Why it fails:** The Value Ladder starts with free or near-free offers. You must deliver value before you ask for money. The dentist cleaned Brunson's teeth before selling whitening.

### 5. The No-Continuity Mistake

**The trap:** "I'll make all my money from one-time sales."

**Why it fails:** One-time sales are unpredictable. Continuity — subscriptions, memberships, coaching retainers — creates predictable recurring revenue. Brunson's continuity program (ClickFunnels subscription) is the financial foundation of his entire business.

> **Case: ClickFunnels as Continuity** (Secret #2, Section 5): ClickFunnels was the continuity rung of Brunson's entire Value Ladder. Customers paid monthly for access to the software — long after the initial book purchase and training programs. This recurring revenue made the entire funnel sustainable. Without it, Brunson would have to constantly find new customers just to maintain revenue.
