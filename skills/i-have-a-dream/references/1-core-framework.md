# The Speech — Core Framework

King's speech moves through three movements: the promissory note, the urgency of now, and the dream.

**Case: The Promissory Note. King calls the Declaration of Independence a promissory note. America has defaulted on its promise to Black citizens.**

**Case: The Bank of Justice. The metaphor: America has given Black people a bad check marked insufficient funds. But the bank of justice is not bankrupt.**

**Case: The Fierce Urgency of Now. King rejects gradualism. He demands action now, not later.**

**Case: The Dream. The most famous passage. A vision of racial harmony and character-based judgment.**

**Case: Let Freedom Ring. The close: freedom ringing from every mountainside, from every hillside, from every state.**

**Case: The Island of Poverty. Black Americans live on a lonely island of poverty in the midst of a vast ocean of prosperity.**
