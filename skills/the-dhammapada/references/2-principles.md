# Key Principles — The Dhammapada

## I. Mind Is the Forerunner

"All that we are is the result of what we have thought. It is founded on our thoughts. It is made up of our thoughts." The mind is the source of all experience. Purity of mind leads to happiness; impurity leads to suffering. Case: Twins chapter — the same external circumstances can produce completely different experiences depending on the state of mind.

## II. Awareness Is the Path

"Awareness is the path to the deathless." Mindfulness is not just a practice — it is the path itself. The aware person lives differently from the unaware. Case: "The awake are always active, striving for peace, never resting."

## III. Non-Violence Toward All Beings

"All beings tremble at violence. Life is dear to all." The principle of ahimsa (non-harming) extends to all living beings. Case: "Putting yourself in the place of another, you should neither kill nor cause to kill."

## IV. Impermanence Is Universal

"This body is worn out with age, a nest of diseases, fragile and breaking." Everything changes. Clinging to what is impermanent causes suffering. Case: Old Age chapter — "What is laughter, what is joy, when the world is always burning?"

## V. The Eightfold Path Is the Way

"Of all paths, the Eightfold Path is the best." The path to liberation is practical and specific: Right View, Right Intention, Right Speech, Right Action, Right Livelihood, Right Effort, Right Mindfulness, Right Concentration. Case: The Path chapter — eight specific practices.

## VI. Anger Must Be Overcome

"Give up anger, abandon pride, overcome all fetters." Anger is a poison that destroys the angry person before it harms anyone else. Case: Anger chapter — "One who holds back rising anger as a charioteer holds back a chariot, that one I call a true charioteer."

## VII. The Goal Is Freedom

"Happiness is the freedom from craving." The ultimate goal is not pleasure or achievement but liberation from suffering. This freedom is attainable here and now. Case: Happiness chapter — "There is no fire like passion, no evil like hatred, no suffering like the aggregates, no happiness greater than peace."
