# Anti-Patterns — The Dhammapada

## The Central Error: "Happiness Comes from External Things"

The Dhammapada teaches that happiness comes from within — from a pure mind, not from acquiring external goods or status. Case: "All that we are is the result of what we have thought."

### Mistake 1: Blaming Others
"He hurt me, he beat me, he defeated me, he robbed me." The Dhammapada says letting go of resentment is the only way to peace. Holding onto anger harms the holder. Case: "Those who hold such thoughts never become free from hatred."

### Mistake 2: Clinging to Youth and Beauty
"I will enjoy my body while it lasts." The body decays. Clinging to impermanent things causes suffering. Case: Old Age chapter — "This body is worn out with age."

### Mistake 3: Following Fools
"Better to travel alone than with a fool." The company we keep shapes our mind. Case: Fools chapter — "If you find a wise companion, go with them rejoicing. If not, travel alone."

### Mistake 4: Neglecting the Mind
"My actions don't matter as long as I think good thoughts." No — all actions follow from the mind. Case: Twins chapter — "If with an impure mind you speak or act, suffering follows."

### Mistake 5: Intellectualizing Instead of Practicing
"One who speaks many verses but does not act accordingly is like a cowherd counting others' cattle." Study without practice is worthless. Case: "One verse of truth that leads to peace is better than a thousand useless verses."

### Mistake 6: Craving More
"I will be happy when I have more." The Dhammapada says the opposite: happiness comes from releasing craving. Case: "There is no fire like passion, no happiness greater than peace."
