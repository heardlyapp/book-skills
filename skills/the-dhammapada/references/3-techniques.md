# Techniques — The Dhammapada

## How to Apply the Buddha's Teachings

### Technique 1: The Mind Check (from Twins chapter)
Throughout the day, check your mind. "All that we are is the result of what we have thought." If the mind is impure (angry, greedy, deluded), suffering follows. If pure (kind, generous, clear), happiness follows. Case: "If with an impure mind you speak or act, then suffering follows you like the cart follows the ox."

### Technique 2: The Awareness Practice (Awareness chapter)
Cultivate mindfulness in all activities. "The awake are always active." Awareness is not occasional — it is a way of being. Case: "Awareness is the path to the deathless. Unawareness is the path to death."

### Technique 3: The Empathy Practice (Rod chapter)
When tempted to harm, ask: "How would I feel if someone did this to me?" "All beings tremble at violence. Life is dear to all." Case: "Putting yourself in the place of another, you should neither kill nor cause to kill."

### Technique 4: The Impermanence Reflection (Old Age chapter)
Regularly reflect on impermanence. "This body is worn out with age, a nest of diseases." This is not morbid — it is liberating. Attachment to what will pass causes suffering. Case: "What is laughter, what is joy, when the world is always burning?"

### Technique 5: The Eightfold Path Check
Use the Eightfold Path as a daily checklist: Is my view right? My intention? My speech? My action? My livelihood? My effort? My mindfulness? My concentration? Case: The Path chapter — eight specific practices for daily life.

### Technique 6: The Anger Pause (Anger chapter)
When anger arises, pause. "One who holds back rising anger as a charioteer holds back a chariot, that one I call a true charioteer." Case: Anger is compared to rust that destroys the iron it arises from.

### Technique 7: The Craving Release (Craving chapter)
"Cut down the whole forest of craving, not just a tree." Craving is a network, not isolated desires. Freedom comes from releasing the root, not managing individual cravings. Case: "There is no fire like passion, no evil like hatred, no suffering like the aggregates, no happiness greater than peace."
