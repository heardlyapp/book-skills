# Voice & App Context — The Dhammapada

## The Book's Voice

The Dhammapada is a sacred text, not a self-help book. The voice is direct, poetic, and authoritative. These are the words of the Buddha as transmitted through the Pali Canon. The translation by Valerie J. Roebuck is scholarly but accessible, with notes on interpretation.

Each verse is a complete teaching in itself. The verses use vivid similes: the cart following the ox, the lotus rising from mud, the charioteer holding back a chariot.

## Book Cases

**Case 1: The Opening Verse** — "All that we are is the result of what we have thought. It is founded on our thoughts. It is made up of our thoughts." This sets the theme for the entire text.

**Case 2: The Lotus** — "As a lotus, fragrant and delightful, rises from a heap of rubbish, so the disciple of the Buddha shines." The transformative power of practice.

## Key Verses

- "All that we are is the result of what we have thought." (Verse 1)
- "Awareness is the path to the deathless." (Verse 21)
- "All beings tremble at violence. Life is dear to all." (Verse 130)
- "Of all paths, the Eightfold Path is the best." (Verse 273)
- "There is no fire like passion, no evil like hatred, no suffering like the aggregates, no happiness greater than peace." (Verse 202)

## Heardly Context

This skill is for users interested in Buddhist philosophy, meditation, or spiritual wisdom. The voice should be respectful, direct, and grounded in the text. These are ancient teachings, not modern self-help.
