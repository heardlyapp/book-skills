# Techniques: Daily Mind Training

## The Basic Meditation

1. Sit comfortably, back straight, shoulders relaxed
2. Bring attention to the breath
3. When mind wanders, gently bring it back — no judgment
4. Start with 5 minutes — consistency > duration

> **Case: The Five-Minute Meditator** (Chapter 7): A busy professional insisted she had "no time" to meditate. She started with just 2 minutes daily. Over three months, those 2 minutes added up to real change. She became calmer, more focused, less reactive.
> **Key takeaway**: Five minutes daily is worth more than an hour once a week. Consistency builds the habit.

> **Case: The Emotion Watcher** (Chapter 8): A woman with anxiety learned to watch her anxiety as it arose — noticing where she felt it in her body, watching it shift, letting it pass. The anxiety didn't disappear, but it stopped controlling her.
> **Key takeaway**: The goal is not to eliminate difficult emotions but to change your relationship with them. When you can watch without being controlled, you are free.

## Working with Difficult Emotions

1. Notice the emotion — name it
2. Feel it in the body
3. Don't push it away — let it be present
4. Watch it change
5. Let it pass

## The Compassion Break

Stop. Hand on heart. Silently: "May I be happy. May I be peaceful." Extend to others.

## The Mind Training Reminder

Pause and ask: What is my mind doing right now? Is this serving me?
