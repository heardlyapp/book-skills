# 3 — Policy History

> Source: "The Sum of Us" by Heather McGhee

## How Policy Created Division

McGhee traces specific policies that created and maintained racial and economic division.

## Redlining

FHA refused to insure mortgages in Black neighborhoods from the 1930s.

## The New Deal

Social Security excluded agricultural and domestic workers — most Black workers.

## The GI Bill

Local administrators denied benefits to Black veterans.

> **Case: The Homeownership Gap** (Chapter 5): McGhee traces the racial wealth gap to specific housing policies. "Black families were systematically excluded from the largest wealth-building program in American history — the federally insured mortgage. This one policy explains a large portion of the racial wealth gap."
> **Key takeaway**: The racial wealth gap is not a mystery or an accident. It was created by policy. Understanding this is the first step to fixing it.

> **Case: The Auto Insurance Story** (Chapter on Consumer Goods): McGhee discovered that Black families pay more for car insurance than white families in the same risk category. "I found that ZIP code-based pricing meant that Black families paid hundreds more per year for the same coverage. This is not about risk — it's about race."
> **Key takeaway**: Racial discrimination is baked into systems that most people assume are colorblind. ZIP code pricing. Credit scoring. These systems reproduce inequality without explicitly mentioning race.
