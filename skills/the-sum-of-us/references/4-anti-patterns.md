# 4 — Anti-Patterns

> Source: "The Sum of Us" by Heather McGhee

## Anti-Pattern 1: Zero-Sum Thinking

**The mistake:** Believing that there's only so much to go around. This belief makes it easy to turn groups against each other.

## Anti-Pattern 2: Racial Wedge Politics

**The mistake:** Using race to divide voters who share economic interests. This is the most effective tool for blocking progressive policy.

## Anti-Pattern 3: Scarcity Mindset

**The mistake:** Assuming the pie is fixed. Solidarity can grow the pie.

> **Case: The Southern Strategy** (Chapter 7): McGhee traces the political strategy of using race to divide poor white and Black voters. "The Southern Strategy was not about race — it was about power. If you can convince poor white people that their enemy is poor Black people, they won't notice that the rich are robbing them both."
> **Key takeaway**: The most effective tool of the 1% is dividing the 99% along racial lines. When we fight each other, we don't notice that we're all losing.

> **Case: The Healthcare Story** (Chapter 8): McGhee shows how the fear of "socialized medicine" is partly a racial fear. "When Medicare was proposed, opponents argued it would lead to integration. The same with the ACA. Every effort to expand healthcare has been met with racial appeals to defeat it."
> **Key takeaway**: The fight against universal healthcare in America cannot be understood without understanding race. The fear of sharing resources across racial lines has shaped every major social policy debate.
