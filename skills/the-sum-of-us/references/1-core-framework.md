# 1 — Zero-Sum and Solidarity

> Source: "The Sum of Us" by Heather McGhee

## The Zero-Sum Myth

The belief that there is only so much to go around — and that one group's gain must be another's loss. This myth is the foundation of racist politics.

## The Solidarity Dividend

The opposite of zero-sum: when groups work together, they create more for everyone. McGhee documents this with data and stories.

> **Case: The Public Pool** (Chapter 1): Montgomery, Alabama closed all its public pools rather than integrate them after a court order. "The city chose to drain a $2 million pool rather than share it with Black residents." The pool sat empty for decades. Everyone lost. This is the paradigm case of how racism hurts everyone.
> **Key takeaway**: The decision to drain the pool is not irrational — it's the logic of racism. If we can't have it exclusively, we'll destroy it. This logic has played out across American public life.

> **Case: The Union Solidarity** (Chapter on Labor): McGhee tells the story of unions in the 1930s that organized across racial lines. "When the CIO chose to organize Black and white workers together, membership exploded and wages rose for everyone. The solidarity dividend was real." The subsequent effort to divide workers along racial lines was a deliberate strategy to weaken unions.
> **Key takeaway**: Solidarity pays. When workers organize together, they win more. When they're divided by race, everyone loses. This is the solidarity dividend in action.
