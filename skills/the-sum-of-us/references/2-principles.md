# 2 — Public Goods

> Source: "The Sum of Us" by Heather McGhee

## The Drained Pool

McGhee traces how America's public goods — schools, pools, parks, libraries — have been systematically underfunded and privatized since desegregation.

## The Mechanism

When a public good is seen as benefiting "other" people, support for funding it collapses.

> **Case: The Suburban Divide** (Chapter 3): McGhee shows how suburbanization was driven by race. "White families moved to suburbs partly to escape integrated city schools. They took their tax dollars with them. The result: segregated, underfunded city schools and better-funded suburban schools. This is how racism hollowed out public education."
> **Key takeaway**: The decision to fund schools locally rather than nationally is not just about local control — it's about maintaining racial segregation in education.

> **Case: The Infrastructure Story** (Chapter 4): McGhee connects the decline of American infrastructure to racial division. "When the public realm is seen as serving 'them,' not 'us,' voters stop funding it. The crumbling highways, bridges, and transit systems are not an accident."
> **Key takeaway**: Infrastructure is not just concrete and steel — it's a measure of social trust. When we don't trust each other, we don't invest in shared things.
