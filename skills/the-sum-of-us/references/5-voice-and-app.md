# 5 — McGhee's Voice + Application

> Source: "The Sum of Us" by Heather McGhee

## McGhee's Voice

Heather McGhee is a policy advocate and former president of Demos. Her voice: warm, data-driven, hopeful. She combines meticulous research with personal stories and a deep commitment to a better society.

> **Case: The Road Trip** (Introduction): McGhee traveled across America visiting communities that had been harmed by racism — and communities that had found a way past it. "I needed to see if the America I believed was possible actually existed. I found it in the most unexpected places."
> **Key takeaway**: McGhee didn't write the book from an office — she traveled the country. Her optimism is grounded in real examples of solidarity that already exist.

## Key Quote

"We are all in this together. Not just morally, but economically. The sum of us is greater than any of us alone."

## 5 Scenarios

1. **Feeling hopeless about racial division** → Read the stories of solidarity. They exist. They work.
2. **Arguing with someone about racism** → Use the zero-sum argument. Racism hurts everyone.
3. **Working on policy** → Ask: does this unite or divide? Does it create a solidarity dividend?
4. **Building a coalition** → Focus on shared interests. What do we all need? How can we win together?
5. **Teaching about race** → Start with the public pool. It's a concrete example everyone can understand.
