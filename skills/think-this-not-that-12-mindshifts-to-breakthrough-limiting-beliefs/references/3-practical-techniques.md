# Practical Techniques

## The Mindshift Practice Method

Axe provides a five-step method for each mindshift:

1. **Identify the false narrative** — What have you been telling yourself?
2. **Challenge it with evidence** — Is it actually true? What's the counter-evidence?
3. **Replace with an empowering truth** — What would you tell a friend in your situation?
4. **Repeat daily** — Say it, write it, meditate on it
5. **Act as if** — Behave as though the new belief is true

## The Awareness Exercise

Most limiting beliefs operate below conscious awareness. Axe recommends a daily check-in:

- What's the first thought I have about myself today?
- What story am I telling myself about this challenge?
- Is this story true? How do I know?
- What would a more empowered version of me think?

## The Replacement Journal

For each mindshift, write:
- **Old belief**: What I've been telling myself
- **New truth**: What I know to be true
- **Evidence**: Proof that the new truth is real
- **Action**: One thing I'll do today based on the new truth

## Book Case — The functional medicine transformation protocol

Axe describes his clinical protocol for mindset change:
- Week 1: Identify one limiting belief (Mindshift 1)
- Week 2: Write the replacement story (Mindshift 5)
- Week 3: Visualize the new reality daily (Mindshift 11)
- Week 4: Take one action based on the new belief
- The physical and emotional improvements followed the mindset shift, not the other way around.

## Book Case — Entrepreneurship and the team principle

When Axe stopped trying to do everything himself and assembled a team (Mindshift 6), his businesses grew exponentially. The practical application: identify your weaknesses and hire people who are strong where you're weak. Don't try to be good at everything.
