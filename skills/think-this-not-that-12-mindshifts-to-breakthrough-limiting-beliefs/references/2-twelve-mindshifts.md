# The 12 Mindshifts in Depth

## Mindshift 1: Create a Breakthrough by Unlimiting Your Beliefs

**The barrier**: Self-limiting beliefs feel like facts. "I'm not smart enough." "I don't have what it takes." "People like me don't do things like that." These are stories you've been telling yourself.

**The shift**: Your beliefs shape your reality. If you believe you can't, you won't. If you believe you can, you'll find a way.

**Book case — Axe's teacher Mrs. Nobel**: She told 14-year-old Josh that his thinking was wrong — he could go to college. That intervention changed his life.

## Mindshift 2: Redefine Success by Becoming, Not Accomplishing

**The barrier**: We measure success by external markers — money, title, status. But these are lagging indicators of who you've become.

**The shift**: Focus on becoming the person who can achieve the goal. The achievement follows.

**Book case — Axe's business growth**: He stopped chasing revenue targets and focused on becoming a better leader, communicator, and servant. Revenue followed naturally.

## Mindshift 3: Become Self-Aware to Get Where You Need to Go

**The barrier**: Blind spots. Everyone has patterns they can't see in themselves.

**The shift**: Seek feedback. Practice reflection. Understand your triggers and tendencies.

**Book case — Johns Hopkins leadership studies**: Self-awareness was identified as the single most important leadership skill. Without it, all other skills are undermined.

## Mindshift 4: Find a Why or Risk Wasting It All

**The barrier**: Without purpose, you drift. When challenges come, you quit.

**The shift**: A compelling "why" sustains you when motivation fades.

**Book case — Healing as calling**: Axe's purpose of helping people heal naturally sustained him through the difficult early years of building his practice.

## Mindshift 5: Rewrite Your Role in the Story

**The barrier**: You've been cast in a role by family, culture, or past experiences.

**The shift**: You are the author, not the character. Rewrite your role.

**Book case — From "sick person" to "healing journey"**: Patients transformed when they rewrote their identity narrative.

## Mindshift 6: Assemble a Team to Fulfill Your Dreams

**The barrier**: The myth of the self-made individual.

**The shift**: Nobody succeeds alone. Build a team with complementary strengths.

## Mindshift 7: Build an Unshakable Identity

**The barrier**: If you don't define yourself, others will define you.

**The shift**: Root your identity in values, not outcomes.

## Mindshift 8: Bust Vices by Building Virtues

**The barrier**: Fighting bad habits directly is exhausting and rarely works.

**The shift**: Replace vices with virtues. Focus on what you want to become, not what you want to stop.

## Mindshift 9: Turn Off Opinions, Turn On Principles

**The barrier**: Fear of other people's judgment keeps you small.

**The shift**: Let timeless principles guide you, not the opinions of others.

## Mindshift 10: Flip the Fear to Turn On the Growth

**The barrier**: Fear is seen as a stop sign.

**The shift**: Fear is a green light. It signals you're entering growth territory.

**Book case — Public speaking**: Axe started speaking despite terror. The fear never fully left, but it stopped controlling him.

## Mindshift 11: Visualize to Realize

**The barrier**: Hope without visualization is wishful thinking.

**The shift**: Visualization is mental rehearsal. It activates the same neural pathways as actual performance.

## Mindshift 12: Power Up Your Potential with Positive Perseverance

**The barrier**: Grit without hope is just suffering.

**The shift**: Combine determination with optimism.
