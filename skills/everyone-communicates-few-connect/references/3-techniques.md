# Techniques: The 5 Practices of Connecting

## Practice 1: Find Common Ground

Research your audience before you speak. Open with shared experience. Mirror their language and energy. Ask questions that reveal common values.

> **Case: The Sales Presentation** (Chapter 6): A salesperson was failing to close deals until he started researching his prospects' backgrounds before meetings. He'd find one shared interest — a college, a hobby, a professional connection — and open with it. His close rate tripled.
> **Key takeaway**: Common ground doesn't have to be deep. One genuine point of shared experience is enough to open the door to connection.

## Practice 2: Simplify Your Message

Write your complete message, cut it in half, cut it again. Can you say it in one sentence? Can a 10-year-old understand it?

> **Case: The Complex Product Pitch** (Chapter 7): A tech founder had a brilliant but complex product. His pitch was full of jargon and details. Maxwell helped him find the one-sentence simplification: "We help companies save 30% on their biggest expense." That was it. The product didn't change — the communication did.
> **Key takeaway**: Complexity hides lack of clarity. If you can't say it simply, you haven't thought it through enough.

## Practice 3: Create an Experience

Use stories, multimedia, participation, and surprise. People remember how you made them feel more than what you said.

## Practice 4: Always Add Value

Encouragement, insight, resources, appreciation. Every interaction leaves people better off.

## Practice 5: Inspire People

Believe in people more than they believe in themselves. Share a vision. Model the behavior you want to see.
