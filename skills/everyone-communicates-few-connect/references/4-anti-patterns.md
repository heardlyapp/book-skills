# Anti-Patterns

## 1. Making Yourself the Hero

**The mistake**: Focusing on your achievements, credentials, and story.

**Fix**: The audience is the hero. Your role is to serve them.

> **Case: The Boring Expert** (Chapter 2): A world-class expert was hired to give a keynote. He spent 20 minutes talking about his credentials and his journey. The audience was bored. When a coach told him to start with "What does this mean for YOU?" — the audience perked up. Same expert. Same topic. Different focus.
> **Key takeaway**: Your expertise doesn't matter until the audience sees why it matters to THEM. Make them the hero.

## 2. Overcomplicating Your Message

**The mistake**: Jargon, details, and complexity to sound impressive.

**Fix**: The goal is not to impress but to be understood.

## 3. Rushing Past Rapport

**The mistake**: Jumping straight to business without building connection.

**Fix**: Five minutes of genuine rapport is the most efficient investment you can make.

> **Case: The Agenda-Obsessed Manager** (Chapter 5): A manager who always started meetings with "Let's get right to business" wondered why his team was disengaged. When he started each meeting with 3 minutes of personal connection — "How was your weekend? How's your daughter's soccer team?" — engagement doubled.
> **Key takeaway**: Connection time is not wasted time. The most efficient meeting starts with rapport.

## 4. Neglecting Your Energy

**The mistake**: Showing up tired, flat, or distracted.

**Fix**: Energy is a prerequisite for connection. If you don't bring it, people won't give you attention.

## 5. Faking Authenticity

**The mistake**: Using techniques without genuine care.

**Fix**: People can sense insincerity. All technique without heart is manipulation.
