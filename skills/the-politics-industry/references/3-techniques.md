## 1. The Five Forces Political Diagnosis

**The technique**: Use Porter's Five Forces to analyze any political system. Identify where competition is suppressed and where structural power is concentrated.

**How to do it**: For any political system, ask: Who competes? Who can enter? Who are the buyers and what power do they have? Who are the suppliers and what power do they have? What are the substitutes? The answers reveal where the system is broken.

> **Case: The Duopoly Diagnosis** (Chapter 2): Applying Five Forces to U.S. politics reveals that the real problem is not polarization — it is the absence of competition. The two parties collude to maintain high entry barriers and low buyer power. The diagnosis points to the cure: increase competition through Final-Five Voting.

## 2. The Final-Five Voting Framework

**The technique**: A two-step reform that combines nonpartisan open primaries with ranked-choice voting.

**How it works**: Step 1: All candidates, regardless of party, appear on a single nonpartisan primary ballot. The top five finishers advance. Step 2: In the general election, voters rank the five candidates. If no one gets 50%+1, the last-place candidate is eliminated and votes are redistributed until someone reaches a majority.

> **Case: The Alaska Model** (Chapter 7): Alaska adopted Final-Four Voting (similar to Final-Five) in 2020. In the 2022 special election, Democrat Mary Peltola won a traditionally Republican seat. The reform produced a result that reflected voters' preferences rather than party dominance.

## 3. The Political Value Chain Analysis

**The technique**: Map the legislative process as a value chain — identify where the chain is broken.

**How to do it**: Trace how a bill becomes a law. At each step, identify where party leadership, committee chairs, or special interests have the power to block or distort outcomes. The broken links in the chain reveal the intervention points for reform.

## 4. The Citizen Action Checklist

**The technique**: A practical list of actions citizens can take to support structural political reform.

**What to do**: Support Final-Five Voting initiatives in your state. Vote in every election, including primaries. Educate others about structural reform. Support organizations working on political innovation. Run for local office.
