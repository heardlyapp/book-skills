# 4 — Anti-Patterns

> Source: *Dumbing Us Down* by John Taylor Gatto — Chapters 1, 2, 4.

## Common Errors

### 1. Believing School Reform Will Fix Things

"You can't reform a prison by painting the walls." Schools are not broken — they are working as designed.

### 2. More School = Better Education

When families struggle, the instinct is to extend school. Gatto's argument: "We need less school, not more."

> **Case: The Barnstable Holdout** (Chapter 2): Compulsory schooling was imposed in Massachusetts around 1850. It was resisted by an estimated 80% of the population. Barnstable on Cape Cod did not surrender its children until the 1880s — when the militia marched children to school under guard. The idea that schooling is natural is a historical myth.
> **Key takeaway**: Schooling was never embraced — it was enforced by military force.

### 3. Confusing Networks with Communities

Schools look like communities but are networks: narrow, functional, temporary. "Everyone I know is lonely because of that."

### 4. The Expert Trap

"Good people wait for an expert to tell them what to do." This is the fifth lesson of schooling. Parents who defer to certified professionals about their own children have been well-schooled.

> **Case: The Literacy Paradox** (Chapter 2): Before compulsory schooling, Massachusetts had 98% literacy. After compulsory schooling, the figure never exceeded 91%. "Reading, writing, and arithmetic only take about one hundred hours to transmit as long as the audience is eager and willing to learn."
> **Key takeaway**: The expert is not necessary. Before the experts took over, people learned just fine.

### 5. Treating Symptoms, Not Causes

Drugs, suicide, violence, indifference — symptoms of school-induced dependency and alienation. More school-based interventions will not solve problems school itself created.
