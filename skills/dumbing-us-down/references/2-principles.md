# 2 — Key Principles

> Source: *Dumbing Us Down* by John Taylor Gatto — all 5 chapters.

## The 7 Principles

### 1. Schooling ≠ Education

"Schooling teaches you to obey orders. Education teaches you to think for yourself." The two are not just different — they are often opposed. The more schooling you have, the less educated you may be.

### 2. The Hidden Curriculum is the Real Curriculum

What children learn from the structure — bells, grades, age-segregation, surveillance — is more powerful than anything they learn from textbooks. "Schools teach exactly what they are intended to teach, and they do it well."

### 3. Children Need Time to Themselves

Gatto's calculation: out of 168 hours per week, his students had 9 hours of private time. "It's not enough." Real growth requires solitude, privacy, and the freedom to be bored.

> **Case: The Calculus of School Time** (Chapter 2): 56 hours sleep + 55 hours TV + 30 hours school + 8 hours commuting + 7 hours homework + 3 hours eating = 159 hours. Of the remaining 9 hours, "they have no private time or private space and are disciplined if they try to assert individuality." The system consumes 90% of childhood.
> **Key takeaway**: If children have no time to themselves, they cannot develop self-knowledge. Self-knowledge is the only basis for real education.

### 4. Self-Knowledge is the Basis of All Real Knowledge

Elite education has always been built on this principle. "Everywhere in this system you will find arrangements that work to place the child alone in an unguided setting with a problem to solve." Solitude, risk, and self-direction.

### 5. You Don't Need an Expert

Before compulsory schooling, Massachusetts had 98% literacy. Homeschoolers today outperform schooled peers. "Reading, writing, and arithmetic only take about one hundred hours to transmit as long as the audience is eager and willing to learn."

### 6. Community is the Real Classroom

Children learn best when they participate in real life alongside people of all ages. Gatto's childhood in Monongahela — the river, the railroad men, the police officer who took him to jail — was more educational than any school.

> **Case: The Calculus of School Time** (Chapter 2): 56 hours sleep + 55 hours TV + 30 hours school + 8 hours commuting + 7 hours homework + 3 hours eating = 159 hours. Of the remaining 9 hours per week, Gatto notes: "they have no private time or private space." The system consumes 90% of childhood.
> **Key takeaway**: If children have no time to themselves, they cannot develop self-knowledge. Self-knowledge is the only basis for true education.

### 7. You Can Opt Out

Gatto won Teacher of the Year. His advice: take your children out of school. "Schools are structurally unreformable." The solution is not better schools — it is less school.
