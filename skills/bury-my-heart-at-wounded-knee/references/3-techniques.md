# 3 — Techniques

> Source: *Bury My Heart at Wounded Knee* — practical methods from the book.

## Practical Tools

### 1. The Treaty Audit

**When presented with an agreement by a more powerful party:**
1. Who interprets? Never accept the other side's translator
2. What is "permanent"? Identify what can be changed, by whom, under what conditions
3. Who enforces? If the enforcer is the same party that benefits from breaking the agreement, the agreement is worthless
4. What is the agreed-upon dispute mechanism?
5. Have there been previous agreements with this party? If so, what happened?

### 2. The Council Record Method

When you must negotiate from a weaker position: speak in your own voice, have a recorder YOU trust present, create a written record of what you said. This was the single most powerful tool available to Indian leaders.

### 3. The Resistance Calculus

Before choosing to fight: assess your enemy's resources honestly. Little Crow had seen Washington. He knew the white men's numbers. But he also knew what his people would face if he refused to lead them. The calculus is not "can we win?" but "what is the cost of not fighting?"

### 4. The Peach Tree Strategy

When Carson's soldiers cut down the Navahos' peach trees at Canyon de Chelly, they were attacking culture, not just agriculture. Protect the symbolic resources that sustain identity.

### 5. The Ghost Dance Assessment

When a social movement arises in your community, ask: is it being dismissed as "primitive" or "threatening" by outside authorities? If so, it may be treated as a conspiracy regardless of its peaceful intentions. Protect your people's spiritual movements.
