## Practical Techniques

### 1. The 2x50 Rule

For each exercise step, aim to complete 2 sets of 50 perfect reps before moving to the next step. This ensures mastery and prevents injury.

### 2. The Every-Other-Day Schedule

Train every other day. Muscles grow during recovery. A day off is not laziness — it is part of the program.

> **Case: The Overtrained Prisoner** (Chapter 9): Wade describes inmates who trained too hard, every day, pushing through pain. They plateaued or got injured. The ones who rested between sessions progressed faster.
> **Key takeaway**: More is not better. Better is better. Rest is training.

### 3. The Perfect Rep Test

If you cannot do a rep with perfect form, you are not strong enough for that step. Drop back to the previous step. Mastery before advancement.

### 4. The 15-Minute Minimum

If you are short on time, do 15 minutes — one or two Big Six exercises. Consistency trumps session length.

> **Case: The Daily Minimum** (Chapter 10): Wade tells of a prisoner who did only 100 pushups and 100 squats every day — 10 minutes of work. Over years, this built an incredible physique. The daily minimum beats the occasional marathon.
> **Key takeaway**: Short, consistent training over long periods beats infrequent marathon sessions.

### 5. The Breath Rule

Exhale on the exertion. Inhale on the release. Never hold your breath during strength work. Proper breathing stabilizes your core and protects your spine.
