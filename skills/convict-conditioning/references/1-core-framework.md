## The Wade Framework: Progressive Bodyweight Mastery

Wade's framework is simple: six fundamental movements, ten levels of progression for each, and a philosophy of consistency and discipline derived from prison training. The goal is not just strength — it is mastery.

**The Key Insight**: You do not need weights to get strong. Your body is the weight. Progressive overload comes from increasing leverage (making the movement harder against your own bodyweight).

> **Case: The One-Arm Pushup Journey** (Chapter 4): A beginner cannot do a one-arm pushup. But by progressing through wall pushups → incline pushups → knee pushups → half pushups → full pushups → close pushups → uneven pushups → half one-arm → lever pushups → one-arm, anyone can reach the goal. Each step uses the same movement pattern at a harder angle.
> **Key takeaway**: Progressive calisthenics works because each step is achievable. You never attempt what you cannot do — you only attempt the next step.

> **Case: The Prison Yard Proof** (Introduction): Wade describes observing men in prison who built incredible strength with only bodyweight exercises. No barbells, no machines, no supplements — just pushups, pullups, squats, and the will to improve. The proof that bodyweight training works is walking around every prison yard.
> **Key takeaway**: The prison context proves the method. If men with no equipment can build extraordinary strength, equipment is not the limiting factor — effort is.
