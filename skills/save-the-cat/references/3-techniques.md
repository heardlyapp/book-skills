# Techniques — Save the Cat!

## How to Write a Hollywood Screenplay (Based on Blake Snyder)

### Technique 1: The Logline Test
Before writing a word of your script, write one sentence describing your movie. Four requirements: irony, compelling mental picture, audience/cost indicator, killer title. "If you can't do this, you don't have a movie yet." The logline is the medicine to test whether your idea works.

### Technique 2: The Beat Sheet (BS2)
Create a 15-beat outline on a single page. Open Image → Theme Stated → Set-Up → Catalyst → Debate → Break into Two → B Story → Fun and Games → Midpoint → Bad Guys Close In → All Is Lost → Dark Night → Break into Three → Finale → Final Image. "When you get lost, go back to the beats."

### Technique 3: The 40-Card Board
Buy a corkboard and 40 index cards. Write one scene per card. Arrange in 4 rows of 10. Act One = first row. Act Two = rows 2-3. Act Three = row 4. "If a card doesn't advance the story, throw it away." Trouble-shoot at the card stage.

### Technique 4: The "Save the Cat" Check
Read your first 10 pages. Ask: "Does my hero do something that makes the audience like them?" If not, add a scene. Sea of Love: Al Pacino lets father off. Snyder: "Doesn't matter what movie you're writing. Romance, thriller, comedy — your hero must be likable."

### Technique 5: The Pope in the Pool
Exposition is boring. "The Pope in the Pool" technique: deliver important information while the audience is distracted by something entertaining. Miss Congeniality: Sandra Bullock learns her assignment while having a makeover. "Bad Guy explans his plan at gunpoint" is lazy.

### Technique 6: The Negative Space Test
Snyder uses the analogy of Michael Jordan's jump shot: "You don't prove he's great by showing him scoring points. You prove it by showing him missing and then watching him work to compensate." Show the hero's struggle. Show the failure. That's how we know they've earned the victory.

### Technique 7: The Emotional Color Wheel
Are you varying the audience's emotions across the script? Laughter, fear, tears, triumph, love — they need to cycle. If page 30 and page 60 feel the same emotionally, the script is flat. "Make me laugh, make me cry, then make me laugh again."
