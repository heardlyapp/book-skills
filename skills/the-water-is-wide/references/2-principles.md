# Key Principles — Fighting the System, Teaching What Matters, and Paying the Price

## Core Principles

> **Case: The Curriculum Battle** (Throughout): The prescribed curriculum is irrelevant to the children of Yamacraw. It assumes knowledge they do not have. It does not address their reality. Conroy abandons it. He teaches them about the world: music, history, science, stories. The school board demands he follow the curriculum. He refuses.
> **Key takeaway**: When the curriculum fails the students, the teacher must choose. Follow the curriculum and fail your students, or teach what matters and face the consequences.

> **Case: The Gullah Culture** (Throughout): The children speak Gullah, a creole language with African roots. The school system treats it as inferior English. Conroy respects it. He does not try to eliminate it. He builds on it. He teaches standard English as another tool, not a replacement.
> **Key takeaway**: Respect the culture of your students. Do not treat their language or heritage as something to be corrected. Build on what they bring.

> **Case: The Firing** (End): Conroy is fired for what the school board calls "conduct unbecoming a teacher." His real crime: he taught his students to think. He showed them the world beyond the island. He refused to accept the system's definition of what they could achieve.
> **Key takeaway**: If you challenge the system, the system will push back. Expect it. Prepare for it. Decide in advance whether it is worth it. For Conroy, it was.
