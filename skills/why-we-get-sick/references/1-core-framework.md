# 1 — The Insulin Resistance Framework

> Source: "Why We Get Sick" by Benjamin Bikman

## The Hidden Epidemic

Insulin resistance is the condition where the body's cells stop responding normally to insulin. To compensate, the pancreas produces more and more insulin. This chronic hyperinsulinemia drives most chronic diseases.

> **Case: The Seven Deadly Diseases** (Introduction): "Insulin resistance is the common thread connecting heart disease, Alzheimer's, cancer, NAFLD, PCOS, type 2 diabetes, and kidney disease. These are not separate conditions with different causes — they are different manifestations of the same underlying metabolic dysfunction." Bikman presents this as the central thesis of the book, supported by hundreds of studies.
> **Key takeaway**: The conventional medical model treats each disease separately (cardiologist for heart, neurologist for Alzheimer's, oncologist for cancer). Bikman argues this siloed approach misses the underlying connection. Treat insulin resistance, and you address all of them.

> **Case: The TG/HDL Ratio** (Chapter on Measuring Insulin Resistance): "A simple blood test can reveal insulin resistance years before symptoms appear. The ratio of triglycerides to HDL cholesterol (TG/HDL) is surprisingly accurate. A ratio above 2.0 is concerning; above 4.0 indicates significant insulin resistance." Bikman argues this simple marker is more predictive than many expensive tests.
> **Key takeaway**: You don't need a specialist to know if you're insulin resistant. Any standard lipid panel gives you the TG/HDL ratio. If it's above 2, your metabolic health needs attention.
