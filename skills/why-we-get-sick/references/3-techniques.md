# 3 — Alzheimer's as a Metabolic Disease

> Source: "Why We Get Sick" by Benjamin Bikman

## Type 3 Diabetes

Alzheimer's disease shares fundamental mechanisms with type 2 diabetes. Brain cells become resistant to insulin and cannot take up glucose for energy. The brain starves.

> **Case: The Brain Insulin Connection** (Chapter on the Brain): "Just 20 years ago, medical texts listed the brain as an organ that had no need for insulin. We now know that's wrong. The brain is exquisitely sensitive to insulin. Brain insulin resistance is a key feature of Alzheimer's disease." Bikman presents the research showing that Alzheimer's patients have significantly reduced brain insulin signaling.
> **Key takeaway**: The brain is metabolically active and depends on insulin to function. When brain cells become insulin resistant, they cannot access glucose. This metabolic starvation may be the fundamental mechanism behind cognitive decline.

> **Case: Ketones as Alternative Fuel** (Chapter on Brain Metabolism): "Unlike glucose, ketones can enter brain cells without needing insulin. This is why ketogenic diets show promise for Alzheimer's — they provide the brain with an alternative fuel source that bypasses the insulin resistance." Bikman explains that ketosis is not just a weight loss tool; it is a potential therapeutic intervention for neurodegenerative disease.
> **Key takeaway**: The therapeutic potential of ketosis extends beyond weight loss. For people with brain insulin resistance, ketones provide a metabolic workaround. This is why the ketogenic diet is being studied for Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, and other neurodegenerative conditions.
