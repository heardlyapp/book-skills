# 2 — Heart Disease and Metabolic Health

> Source: "Why We Get Sick" by Benjamin Bikman

## Rethinking Heart Disease

Heart disease is the leading cause of death globally. The conventional model blames LDL cholesterol. Bikman challenges this, presenting evidence that insulin resistance is the true driver.

> **Case: The Failed LDL Hypothesis** (Chapter on Heart Disease): "LDL cholesterol is not the reliable predictor we've been told it is. Half of all heart attacks occur in people with normal LDL. What correlates far better is the pattern of LDL particles — small, dense LDL particles, which are driven by insulin resistance." Bikman explains that high triglycerides and low HDL are better markers than LDL alone.
> **Key takeaway**: The TG/HDL ratio reflects LDL particle size. High TG + low HDL = small, dense LDL = dangerous. Low TG + high HDL = large, fluffy LDL = benign. Total LDL number tells you almost nothing about particle quality.

> **Case: Insulin Damages Arteries Directly** (Chapter on Vascular Damage): "High insulin levels directly damage the endothelium — the inner lining of blood vessels. This damage initiates the atherosclerotic process. This happens independently of cholesterol. Insulin is not just a marker — it is a causal factor in heart disease." Bikman presents studies showing that hyperinsulinemia precedes arterial damage by years.
> **Key takeaway**: The cholesterol-centric model of heart disease misses the real driver. Lowering insulin addresses the root cause. Lowering LDL alone (with statins) manages a symptom, not the disease.
