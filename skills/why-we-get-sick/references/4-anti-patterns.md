# 4 — Cancer, PCOS, and Environmental Toxins

> Source: "Why We Get Sick" by Benjamin Bikman

## Anti-Pattern: Ignoring the Insulin-Cancer Link

The conventional model of cancer focuses on genetics and mutations. Bikman argues that insulin and IGF-1 create the metabolic environment in which cancer can grow.

> **Case: Insulin Promotes Cancer Growth** (Chapter on Cancer): "Higher levels of insulin and IGF-1 (insulin-like growth factor) are consistently associated with increased cancer risk. Cancer cells have insulin receptors. When insulin is high, cancer cells receive a signal to grow and divide." Bikman cites studies showing that people with type 2 diabetes have significantly higher rates of several cancers.
> **Key takeaway**: Cancer is not just a genetic disease — it is a metabolic disease. Hyperinsulinemia creates a permissive environment for cancer. Reducing insulin levels may be a powerful cancer prevention strategy.

## Anti-Pattern: Treating PCOS Without Addressing Insulin

PCOS (Polycystic Ovary Syndrome) is routinely treated with birth control pills or fertility drugs. Bikman argues this misses the root cause: insulin resistance drives the hormonal dysfunction of PCOS.

> **Case: The PCOS-Insulin Connection** (Chapter on Reproduction): "Up to 70% of women with PCOS have insulin resistance. High insulin drives the ovaries to produce excess testosterone, which prevents ovulation. Treating the insulin resistance — through diet, exercise, and medications like metformin — can restore fertility naturally." Bikman presents this as a metabolic condition that is frequently mislabeled as a reproductive disorder.
> **Key takeaway**: PCOS is fundamentally a metabolic condition. When insulin resistance is addressed, many women ovulate normally. Treating the root cause is more effective than managing symptoms with hormonal contraceptives.
