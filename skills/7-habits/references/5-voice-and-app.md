## § Covey's Voice

1. **Principle-centered** — Every habit is grounded in universal principles, not situational tips. Covey distinguishes between the "Personality Ethic" (surface techniques) and the "Character Ethic" (deep principles).
2. **Story-driven** — The goose and the golden egg, the funeral visualization, the subway parent — each principle comes with a memorable story.
3. **Systematic framework** — The 7 Habits build on each other in a deliberate sequence (dependence → independence → interdependence).
4. **Tone: authoritative yet warm** — Covey writes as a teacher who has lived these principles, not an academic theorist.

---

## Habit 7: Sharpen the Saw

**Core idea:** Renewal in 4 dimensions:

| Dimension | Activities | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| Physical | Exercise, nutrition, rest | Energy, health |
| Mental | Reading, writing, learning | Clarity, growth |
| Social/Emotional | Service, empathy, synergy | Connection, love |
| Spiritual | Meditation, values, contribution | Purpose, peace |

**Key insight:** Sharpening the saw is Quadrant II — important but not urgent. It's the first thing people drop when busy, but it's the most important thing to maintain.

## § Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: "I feel stuck in my career"
1. Be Proactive: identify what you can control (your skills, attitude, effort)
2. Begin with End in Mind: define what career success means to YOU
3. Sharpen the Saw: invest in learning new skills

### Scenario 2: "My team doesn't work well together"
1. Build Emotional Bank Account: understand each team member
2. Think Win-Win: find solutions that serve everyone's interests
3. Synergize: value different perspectives as strengths

### Scenario 3: "I'm overwhelmed by priorities"
1. Use the Time Management Matrix: categorize everything
2. Protect Quadrant II time: schedule your most important work first
3. Say no to Quadrant III and IV activities
