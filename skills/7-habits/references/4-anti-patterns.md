# Anti-Patterns · What Blocks Effectiveness

> Source: *The 7 Habits*, full book

---

## Reactive Mindset

**Anti-pattern:** Blaming circumstances, other people, or genetics for your situation.

**Examples:**
- "There's nothing I can do — my boss won't let me"
- "I'm just not a morning person"
- "That's just how I am"

**Covey's fix:** Focus on your Circle of Influence. Between stimulus and response is your freedom to choose.

---

## Living Without a Mission

**Anti-pattern:** Drifting through life guided by other people's agendas, social pressures, or the urgent demands of the moment.

**Examples:**
- Climbing the ladder of success only to find it's against the wrong wall
- Achieving goals that don't matter to you personally
- Living someone else's idea of success

**Covey's fix:** Begin with the End in Mind. Write a personal mission statement.

---

## Urgency Addiction

**Anti-pattern:** Living in Quadrants I and III — always reacting to the urgent, never doing what's important.

**Examples:**
- The inbox drives your day
- You feel productive because you're busy
- Important projects (planning, relationship building) never get done

**Covey's fix:** Schedule your Quadrant II activities first. Put the big rocks in before the sand.

---

## Win-Lose Thinking

**Anti-pattern:** Seeing life as zero-sum. If someone else wins, you lose.

**Examples:**
- Hoarding credit for work
- Not sharing information
- Competing instead of collaborating

**Covey's fix:** Abundance Mentality. There's enough for everyone. Win/Win is more powerful in the long run.

---

## Listening to Reply, Not to Understand

**Anti-pattern:** Half-listening while formulating your response. Responding with autobiography (judging, advising, probing, interpreting).

**Covey's fix:** Seek first to understand. Listen empathically before trying to be understood.

---

## Neglecting Renewal

**Anti-pattern:** Grinding without rest, learning, or reflection. Burning out from neglecting any of the 4 dimensions.

**Covey's fix:** Sharpen the Saw. Spend time renewing physically, mentally, socially, and spiritually — it's the investment that makes all other habits possible.
