# Techniques · Habits 4-6: Public Victory

> Source: *The 7 Habits*, Part Three

---

## Habit 4: Think Win-Win

**Core idea:** There's plenty for everyone. Seek mutually beneficial solutions.

**The 6 Paradigms of Human Interaction:**
1. Win/Win — mutually beneficial (the only sustainable choice for interdependence)
2. Win/Lose — "I win, you lose" (authoritarian)
3. Lose/Win — "I lose, you win" (appeaser)  
4. Lose/Lose — "if I can't win, neither can you"
5. Win — only care about my own win (independent)
6. Win/Win or No Deal — if we can't find a solution that works for both, we agree not to deal

**Character foundation for Win/Win:**
- Integrity (stay true to your values)
- Maturity (courage to express feelings + consideration for others)
- Abundance Mentality (there's enough for everyone)

## Habit 5: Seek First to Understand, Then to Be Understood

**Core idea:** Most people listen with the intent to reply, not to understand. Empathic listening — listening with the intent to truly understand the other person — is the key to influence.

**Four Levels of Listening:**
1. Ignoring — not listening at all
2. Pretending — "yeah, uh-huh"
3. Selective listening — hearing only parts
4. Attentive listening — paying attention to words
5. Empathic listening — listening with your ears, eyes, and heart

**Diagnose before you prescribe:** A doctor who prescribes without diagnosing is incompetent. A communicator who gives advice without understanding is the same.

## Habit 6: Synergize

**Core idea:** 1+1 = 3 (or more). When two people combine their strengths, they can create solutions neither could create alone.

**Synergy in action:** Two people with different perspectives. Instead of compromising (each gives up something), they create a third alternative that's better than either original idea.

**Prerequisites:** High trust (full Emotional Bank Account) + genuine respect for differences.

**Valuing differences:** The key to synergy. Different perspectives aren't threats — they're opportunities.
