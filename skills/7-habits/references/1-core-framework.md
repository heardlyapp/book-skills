# Core Framework · Habits 1-3: Private Victory

> Source: *The 7 Habits*, Part Two

---

## The Maturity Continuum

```
Dependence → Independence → Interdependence
     |            |                |
  "You"        "I"              "We"
  Blame        Take             Collaborate
  others       responsibility   
```

Habits 1-3 move you from dependence to independence (Private Victory).
Habits 4-6 move you from independence to interdependence (Public Victory).
Habit 7 enables all six.

---

## Habit 1: Be Proactive

**Core idea:** Between stimulus and response, there is a space. In that space is your freedom to choose your response.

**Circle of Concern vs Circle of Influence:**
- Proactive people focus their energy on the **Circle of Influence** (things they can control)
- Reactive people focus on the **Circle of Concern** (things they can't control but worry about)

**Language matters:** "I can" vs "I can't". "I choose" vs "I have to". "Let's look at alternatives" vs "There's nothing I can do."

**Story:** Victor Frankl in Nazi concentration camps — found meaning and chose his response even in unimaginable circumstances.

---

## Habit 2: Begin with the End in Mind

**Core idea:** Mentally create before physically create. All things are created twice — first in the mind, then in reality.

**Application:** Write a personal mission statement. Define what you want to be (character), what you want to do (contributions), and the values/principles that guide both.

**Story:** Covey asks readers to imagine their own funeral — what would you want people to say about you? That vision becomes your north star.

---

## Habit 3: Put First Things First

**Core idea:** Organize and execute around priorities, not around the urgent.

**Time Management Matrix:**

| | Urgent | Not Urgent |
|---|---|---|
| **Important** | I: Crises, deadlines (do now) | II: Planning, relationships (do more of this) |
| **Not Important** | III: Interruptions, some mail (delegate) | IV: Time wasters, trivia (eliminate) |

**Key insight:** Effectiveness lives in Quadrant II — things that are important but not urgent. Most people live in Quadrant I (crisis mode) or Quadrant III (busy but not productive).
