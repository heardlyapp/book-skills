# Our Fishy Anatomy

> Source: Your Inner Fish, Chapters 2-4, 8-10

## Body as Time Machine

### Where Our Parts Come From

| Human Structure | Evolved From |
|----------------|-------------|
| Hands | Fins of lobe-finned fish |
| Ears | Gill arches of ancient fish |
| Teeth | Fish scales |
| Head bones | Jawless fish armor |
| Eyes | Light-sensitive cells in ancient invertebrates |

> **Case: Hands from Fins (Chapters 2-3)**
> Shubin shows that the bones in our hands — one bone in the upper arm, two bones in the forearm, multiple small bones in the wrist, and five fingers — match the pattern in the fins of ancient lobe-finned fish. Tiktaalik's fin has bones that look like a wrist. The genetic instructions for building a hand existed in fish long before hands evolved. The Hox genes that build our fingers are the same genes that built the fin rays of a Devonian fish.
> **Key takeaway:** Your hand is a modified fish fin. The proof is in the bones, the muscles, and the genes. This is not analogy — it is direct evolutionary inheritance. When you wave your hand, you are waving a fin that your 375-million-year-old ancestor used to paddle through Devonian swamps.

> **Case: From Gill Arches to Ears (Chapter 4, 8)**
> Fish have gill arches — bony supports between their gill slits. In humans, the first gill arch became our jaw. The second gill arch became the bones of our middle ear (the hammer, anvil, and stirrup). These tiny ear bones evolved from fish jaw bones. The hearing apparatus of mammals is a repurposed fish mouth. The proof: in early human embryos, the gill arches appear — and then transform into jaw and ear bones.
> **Key takeaway:** The bones that let you hear this sentence were once part of a fish's jaw. Evolution does not build from scratch — it repurposes what already exists. Your ear is a modified fish mouth.
