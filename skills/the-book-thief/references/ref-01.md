# Reference: Plot and Characters

## Overview
The Book Thief follows Liesel Meminger from 1939 to 1945. Her story is told by Death, who has followed her through life.

## Case: Liesel's Journey
Liesel's brother dies on the train to Molching. At his funeral, she steals her first book — "The Grave Digger's Handbook" — though she cannot read. She arrives at Himmel Street terrified. Hans Hubermann teaches her to read. She meets Rudy Steiner, her best friend. She steals more books. Max arrives. The story traces her transformation from illiterate child to writer.

## Case: Hans Hubermann
Hans is an accordion player, a painter, and a man of profound kindness. He hides Max at enormous risk. He gives bread to a Jewish prisoner being marched through Molching — a moment of compassion that leads to his punishment. Hans represents the moral center of the novel. He is not a hero who fights the Nazis; he is an ordinary man who chooses kindness.

## Case: Max Vandenburg
Max, the son of a Jewish man who saved Hans's life in WWI, arrives at the Hubermanns' door starving and desperate. He hides in their basement for nearly two years. He and Liesel become close friends. He writes two books for her: "The Standover Man" and "The Word Shaker." Max represents the hidden humanity that Nazi Germany tried to destroy.

## Case: Rosa Hubermann
Rosa appears as a coarse, foul-mouthed woman who calls everyone names. But she is the one who agrees to hide Max, who feeds him, who keeps the family alive. Her harsh exterior conceals a fierce love. The novel's emotional power comes partly from Liesel realizing how much Rosa sacrificed.

## Case: Rudy Steiner
Rudy is Liesel's neighbor, best friend, and secret admirer. He wants to be like Jesse Owens. He is brave (he jumps into a frozen river), loyal (he follows Liesel everywhere), and ultimately tragic. His death in the bombing is one of the novel's most painful moments.
