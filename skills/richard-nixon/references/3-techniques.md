# Techniques: Lessons from Nixon

## The Comeback Method

1. After defeat, don't disappear — stay visible but change your approach
2. Study why you lost — Nixon analyzed the 1960 election in obsessive detail
3. Build relationships while out of power — travel, speak, meet people
4. Reposition yourself — Nixon moved from fierce anti-communist to statesman
5. Wait for the right moment — and be ready when it comes

> **Case: The 1968 Victory** (Chapter 12): After losing in 1960 and 1962, Nixon spent 6 years rebuilding. He traveled the world, built relationships with Republican leaders, raised money, and positioned himself as a statesman. When the Democrats tore themselves apart over Vietnam in 1968, Nixon was ready. His comeback was not luck — it was preparation meeting opportunity.
> **Key takeaway**: The comeback was years in the making. Nixon didn't just "bounce back" — he methodically built the foundation for his return.

## The Strategic Opening Method

When dealing with adversaries:
1. Understand their incentives — what do they want?
2. Find the division — exploit splits in the opposing camp
3. Create mutual benefit — both sides must gain
4. Surprise everyone — the dramatic gesture changes the game

## Avoiding the Paranoia Trap

- Seek out people who disagree with you — they may be right
- Don't create enemies — you'll find them everywhere
- Ask: "Am I acting from fear or from strategy?"
- Remember that the cover-up is worse than any mistake
- Build a system of accountability before you need one

> **Case: The Saturday Night Massacre** (Chapter 20): Nixon ordered the firing of Special Prosecutor Archibald Cox. His Attorney General refused and resigned. His Deputy Attorney General also refused and was fired. Finally, the Solicitor General carried out the order. The "Saturday Night Massacre" turned public opinion decisively against Nixon. His attempt to control the investigation destroyed him.
> **Key takeaway**: When you try to stop an investigation by firing everyone involved, you prove you have something to hide. The cover-up becomes the story.
