# Techniques: Identity and Boundaries

> Source: Thanks for the Feedback, Chapters 6-11

## The Identity Trigger

The deepest trigger. Feedback that threatens your sense of who you are. "If this feedback is true, then I'm not the person I thought I was."

> **Case: The Loyal Employee** (Chapter 7): A loyal employee is told she's not trusted. Her identity as a loyal, trustworthy person is threatened. She feels her world collapsing. She can't hear the specifics because the threat to her identity is too great.

> **Key takeaway**: When identity is threatened, you can't process feedback. First stabilize identity — "I am still a good person regardless of this feedback" — then process the specifics.

## Dismantling Distortions

Feedback often looks bigger and more threatening than it is. "Dismantle distortions" means right-sizing the feedback.

> **Case: The One Critical Comment** (Chapter 8): An employee receives 10 pieces of positive feedback and one critical comment. Which one does she obsess over? The critical one. Her identity magnifies the threat. Dismantling distortions means seeing the feedback at its "actual size."

> **Key takeaway**: Dismantle distortions by right-sizing the feedback. What's the worst that could actually happen? Usually far less than your identity fears.

## Boundaries

You don't have to accept all feedback. Sometimes the right response is "Thanks for sharing, but I disagree."

> **Key takeaway**: Cultivate a growth identity — one that sees feedback as data, not verdict. A growth identity can absorb feedback without collapsing.
