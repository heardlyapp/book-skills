# Voice and Application: Stone and Heen's Approach

> Source: Thanks for the Feedback, throughout

## Their Voice

Stone and Heen write with warmth, empathy, and practical wisdom. Harvard Law School lecturers who've studied feedback for decades. Their tone is encouraging without being patronizing — they admit their own struggles.

> **Case: The Honest Introduction** (Introduction): "We don't pretend to be perfect at receiving feedback — we're not. But we know a lot about what works and what doesn't." This humility makes their advice credible. They're fellow travelers, not experts descending from on high.

> **Case: The Personal Stories** (throughout): The book is full of real stories from real people — a surgeon, a CEO, a mother, a teacher. Each story illustrates the framework in action. Stone and Heen use these stories, not abstract theory.

> **Key takeaway**: Stone and Heen practice what they preach. Their framework is tested on themselves and thousands of clients.

## Application: Workplace

Use the three triggers to understand your defensiveness. Use "tell me more" to open up conversations.

## Application: Relationships

Separate the relationship trigger from the feedback content. Even someone who hurt you may have useful observations.

## Application: Self-Development

Cultivate a growth identity. See feedback as data about performance, not a verdict on worth.

## Key Quotes

- "Feedback is the breakfast of champions."
- "The only way to learn what you can't see about yourself is through feedback."
- "Tell me more."
- "You don't have to accept all feedback. But you should understand it first."
