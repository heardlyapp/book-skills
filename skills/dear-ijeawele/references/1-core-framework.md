# 1 — The 15 Suggestions

> Source: "Dear Ijeawele" by Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie

1. **Be a full person.** Motherhood is a glorious gift, but don't define yourself by it.
2. **Do it together.** Parenting is shared. Not "helping" — sharing.
3. **Don't use gender as a starting point.** A child is a child. Start with her humanity.
4. **Challenge gender roles.** "Bossy" is leadership. Let her lead.
5. **Teach her about female role models.** She needs to see what women can be.
6. **Reject likeability.** Please don't raise your daughter to please.
7. **Teach her about difference.** Difference is normal, not threatening.
8. **Be careful with language.** "Act like a lady" teaches performance, not authenticity.
9. **Don't marry her to marriage.** Her life is not about finding a husband.
10. **Teach her about sexuality.** Shame-free, honest, respectful.
11. **Teach her romantic love.** But teach her that love doesn't mean losing herself.
12. **Reject anti-men feminism.** Feminism is about equality.
13. **Teach her about culture critically.** "Culture is made by people. It can be remade."
14. **Teach her to question.** Curiosity is more important than compliance.
15. **Teach her about money.** Financial independence is the foundation of freedom.
