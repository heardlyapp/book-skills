# 4 — Activist Journalism and Credibility

> Source: "Unfreedom of the Press" by Mark Levin

## Anti-Pattern: Journalist as Activist

**The mistake:** Journalists believing their role is to advance political causes rather than report facts.

## Anti-Pattern: Pack Journalism

**The mistake:** All major outlets covering the same stories in the same way. The loss of diverse editorial voices.

> **Case: The Journalist as Advocate** (Chapter on Modern Media): "Many journalists now openly identify as activists. They march in protests, donate to political campaigns, and advocate for causes on social media. They have abandoned any pretense of neutrality." Levin argues this is not journalism — it's advocacy masquerading as news.
> **Key takeaway**: When journalists become activists, they abandon their role as neutral arbiters of fact. The public can no longer trust that what they are reading is news rather than opinion.

> **Case: The Credibility Gap** (Chapter on Trust): "Trust in media has fallen to historic lows. A 2019 Gallup poll found that only 13% of Americans have a great deal or quite a lot of confidence in newspapers. This is not a public relations problem — it's a credibility problem. The media brought it on itself."
> **Key takeaway**: The decline in trust is not a media literacy problem — it's a media behavior problem. When news organizations abandon objectivity, the public notices.
