# Techniques — Future Bright

## How to Enhance Your Intelligence (Based on Michael E. Martinez)

### Technique 1: The Ten-Strategy Intelligence Workout
Martinez's 10 strategies aren't optional — they're a system. Commit to at least 3-4. The most powerful: (1) Broaden experiences — travel, learn new skills, meet diverse people. (2) Push beyond the easy — deliberate practice on challenging material. (5) Seek feedback — find someone who will tell you the truth about your blind spots. Case: Martinez himself embodied this — he pursued intellectual problems for decades "with a deeply critical spirit and a complete open mind."

### Technique 2: The Cognitive Complexity Audit
Assess the cognitive complexity of your daily environment. "Enriched environments raise IQ; deprived ones lower it." Ask: Does my work challenge me intellectually? Do I read complex material? Do I have conversations that push my thinking? Case: The "Brain Boost" project raised children's IQs by increasing the cognitive complexity of their after-school environments.

### Technique 3: The Flynn Effect Benchmark
Understand that IQs rise 3 points per decade on average. This means a person with a 100 IQ today would have scored about 130 on the same test from 1920. The technique: use this as evidence that intelligence is responsive to environment — and then ask what specific environmental factors (more education, more technology, better nutrition, more complex work) drove that rise.

### Technique 4: The Effective Character Check
"Intelligence is not just about IQ. It includes the dispositions of the heart." Assess your: Curiosity (do you ask why?), Openness (do you consider alternative perspectives?), Persistence (do you stick with hard problems?), Self-regulation (can you delay gratification?), and Intellectual humility (do you admit when you're wrong?). Case: Martinez's colleagues describe him as "radiating effective character" — he pursued problems for decades while staying open-minded.

### Technique 5: The Structural Brain Invest
Martinez cites London taxi drivers: their hippocampi physically enlarged as they learned "The Knowledge" (25,000 streets). The technique: choose a domain-specific skill that requires spatial or complex learning. Your brain will physically adapt. Case: Taxi drivers studied for 2-4 years to acquire The Knowledge — and their brains changed measurably.

### Technique 6: The Crystallized Intelligence Reading Stack
Crystallized intelligence accumulates with knowledge. The most efficient way to build Gc: read widely and deeply. Non-fiction, academic work, history, science, philosophy. Case: Martinez himself was described as having extraordinary crystallized intelligence — "accumulated wisdom" that he drew on in his research and teaching.

### Technique 7: The Synaptic Social Circle
"Build relationships that stimulate thinking." Surround yourself with people who challenge your ideas, introduce you to new perspectives, and model intellectual curiosity. Case: Martinez's research group involved "scores of students" — he didn't work in isolation but in a community of inquiry.
