# Anti-Patterns — Future Bright

## The Central Error: "Intelligence Is Fixed at Birth"

"Almost everyone assumes that intelligence is a genetically programmed trait like eye color — set from birth and unalterable." This is the single most damaging misconception about intelligence. It leads people to give up on themselves and others. "Your mind is not a bucket to be filled, but a fire to be kindled."

### Mistake 1: "IQ tests measure innate intelligence"
"IQ scores are changeable." They fluctuate with education, environment, nutrition, stress, and cognitive stimulation. "The discovery of rising IQs in the 20th century" (the Flynn Effect) proves that IQ measures current functioning, not fixed potential.

### Mistake 2: "You can't teach an old dog new tricks"
"Fluid intelligence peaks in young adulthood and then declines." But crystallized intelligence increases throughout life. And even fluid intelligence can be maintained through cognitive engagement. "The brain is not a static organ. It changes in response to experience."

### Mistake 3: "Genes are destiny"
"IQ heritability is about 50%." That means half the variance is environmental. Adoption studies show gains of 12-16 IQ points when children move to enriched environments. "If genes alone determined IQ, IQs would not rise over generations."

### Mistake 4: "Intelligence is just IQ"
"Intelligence is not just about IQ. It includes the dispositions of the heart." Martinez's model includes effective character — curiosity, persistence, humility — as a necessary component of intelligent action.

### Mistake 5: "School doesn't matter"
"Formal education reliably raises IQ." Each year of schooling adds an estimated 2-5 IQ points. But it's not just any schooling — it's quality education that challenges students to think critically. Case: Students who drop out of school see IQ decline; students who complete higher education see IQ increase.

### Mistake 6: "Brain training games will make you smarter"
"Quick fixes don't work." Martinez's strategies require sustained engagement with challenging material. "Pushing beyond the easy" — not playing brain games — is what enhances intelligence. Case: Martinez's Brain Boost project was a multi-year, multi-component intervention, not a quick fix.
