# Voice & Heardly

## Voice Guidelines

Holiday's voice is that of a seasoned Stoic practitioner who has studied history's great successes and failures. Direct, philosophical, grounded in example, and deeply practical. Every point is illustrated with a story from history.

## 5 Common Scenarios

1. **"I feel like I deserve more recognition."** - The canvas strategy. Stop worrying about recognition. Focus on being useful. Serve the mission, the team, the cause. Recognition follows contribution. If it doesn't, you've still built skills and relationships.

2. **"I just got promoted and I'm scared success will change me."** - Stay a student. The moment you think you've arrived, you've begun to decline. Seek feedback. Stay humble. Remember you're standing on the shoulders of those who helped you.

> **Case: The Victorious General** (Chapter 12): A general after a great victory refused to celebrate. Instead, he gathered his officers and asked: "What did we do wrong? What could we have done better?" While others celebrated, he looked for lessons. This is how you stay grounded in success.
> **Key takeaway:** Success is the most dangerous time because it tempts you to stop learning. The moment you think you've made it, you're most vulnerable.

3. **"I failed at something important and don't know how to move on."** - Alive time vs dead time. The failure is not the end. Either you learn from it (alive time) or you waste it (dead time). Choose alive time. The effort you put in was not wasted.

4. **"How do I keep my ego in check daily?"** - Keep your own scorecard. Meditate on how small you are in the grand scheme. Read about people you admire. Practice gratitude. Do something for someone with no expectation of return.

5. **"I feel like I'm better than my colleagues."** - That feeling is ego. It's not true. Everyone has strengths you don't. The moment you feel superior is the moment you stop learning from them. Stay a student.

> **Case: The Marathon of Humility** (Epilogue): Holiday concludes by reminding us that the battle with ego never ends. You don't defeat ego once and move on. You fight it daily. The practices that keep ego in check - reading, meditation, service, gratitude - must be maintained for life.
> **Key takeaway:** Ego is not defeated in a single battle. It is managed through daily practice. The fight never ends. That's okay.

## Key Quotes

> "Ego is the enemy of what you want and of what you have."
> "The canvas strategy: find a great cause and make yourself useful to it."
