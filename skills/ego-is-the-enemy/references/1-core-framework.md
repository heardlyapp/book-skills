# Core Framework: The Three Stages of Ego

Holiday organizes the book into three stages where ego attacks: Aspire, Success, and Failure. Each stage requires a different response. The ego is never satisfied - it sabotages you before you start, when you succeed, and when you fail.

## The Canvas Strategy

Before you can succeed, you must make yourself useful. The canvas strategy means finding a great person, a great cause, or a great organization and making them better. You don't need credit. You need to contribute. Your ego wants recognition before contribution. The path demands contribution first, recognition eventually. This is counterintuitive for ambitious people who feel they deserve to be noticed.

> **Case: John D. Rockefeller** (Chapter 5): The young Rockefeller started as a bookkeeper. He didn't act like he was above the work. He studied every aspect of the business obsessively. He made himself so valuable that his employer eventually made him a partner. He didn't demand a title. He earned one through relentless contribution.
> **Key takeaway:** The canvas strategy works because it builds skills, relationships, and reputation simultaneously. You don't need the credit. You need the experience.

## Talk vs. Do

Holiday's central warning: the more you talk about what you're going to do, the less you actually do. Talking gives you the dopamine hit of accomplishment without the actual effort. The ego loves this. The work requires silence.

> **Case: The Young Writer** (Chapter 1): Holiday describes a young man who announced to everyone he was writing a novel. He talked about his ideas, his themes, his characters. Everyone was impressed. He never wrote a single page. The talking had replaced the doing entirely.
> **Key takeaway:** If you want to accomplish something, keep it to yourself. The satisfaction of sharing your plans reduces your motivation to execute. Talk less. Do more.

The canvas strategy requires patience. It may take years of working behind the scenes before you get recognition. But the skills, relationships, and reputation you build during that time are worth more than any title. The ego wants the title now. The path builds the foundation first. Do not rush this. The foundation determines how high you can climb.

The talk vs do principle applies to every area of life. Social media, announcing goals, telling friends about your plans - all of these provide the satisfaction of progress without the work. The most productive people are often the quietest. They let their results speak.

The core framework of ego as the enemy across three life stages is the organizing principle of Holiday book. Understanding which stage you are in determines which antidote you need. In aspiration, use the canvas strategy and silence. In success, stay a student and maintain sobriety. In failure, choose alive time and keep your own scorecard.
The three stages model is not linear. You can cycle through them multiple times in a career or even a single year. The key is recognizing which stage you are in and applying the right response. Ego adapts. Your awareness must too.
