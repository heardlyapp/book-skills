# Techniques from Barbarian Days

## Technique 1: Reading the Ocean

The most important skill in surfing is reading the ocean — understanding currents, tides, swells, winds, and how they combine to create waves.

**How to do it:** Sit on the beach. Watch the waves. Notice the patterns. Learn to predict where the next set will break.

**Case: Finnegan's Surfing Eye.** Finnegan could read a break from the shore. He knew where to position himself. He could see what others could not.

## Technique 2: Paddling Out

Getting past the breaking waves to the lineup requires timing, technique, and courage.

**How to do it:** Watch the sets. Paddle when there is a lull. Duck dive under breaking waves. Keep paddling. Do not stop.

**Case: The First Paddle Out.** Finnegan describes his first time paddling out at a big break. He was terrified. He learned that the paddle out is always harder than the ride in.

## Technique 3: The Drop

The moment of truth — standing up on a wave that is moving at 20 miles per hour.

**How to do it:** Paddle hard to match the wave's speed. Pop up. Stay low. Look where you want to go.

**Case: The First Barrel.** Finnegan describes his first time being fully inside a wave — the barrel. The moment of being surrounded by water, in a moving cave. The wave makes a sound like a freight train.

**Case: The Wipeout.** Finnegan also describes the wipeout — the moment the wave takes control. The board is ripped away. The body is tumbled. The air is gone. The wave decides when to let you go.
