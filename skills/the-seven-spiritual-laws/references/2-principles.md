# 2 — Giving, Karma, and Least Effort

> Source: "The Seven Spiritual Laws of Success" by Deepak Chopra

## Law 2: Giving

The universe operates through dynamic exchange. Giving and receiving are two sides of the same coin. Give the thing you want to receive.

## Law 3: Karma

Every action generates a consequence. Dharma means skillful action — choices that create happiness.

## Law 4: Least Effort

Nature functions with effortless ease. The three components: acceptance, responsibility, defenselessness.

> **Case: The Giving Practice** (Chapter 2): "Whenever you meet someone, give them something — a compliment, a flower, a prayer. Don't go into any interaction without knowing what you will give." Chopra teaches that the act of giving opens the flow of abundance.
> **Key takeaway**: Giving is not about losing — it's about circulating. What you give returns to you multiplied. Don't wait until you have enough to give.

> **Case: The Defenselessness Practice** (Chapter 4): "The third component of least effort is defenselessness — the willingness to stop defending your point of view. When you stop defending, you stop creating conflict and resistance." Chopra argues that defending our opinions consumes enormous energy.
> **Key takeaway**: You don't need to defend your point of view. Your truth doesn't require defense. Releasing the need to be right frees enormous energy for creation.
