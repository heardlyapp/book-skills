# 4 — Detachment

> Source: "The Seven Spiritual Laws of Success" by Deepak Chopra

## Law 6: Detachment

Detachment is not indifference. It's the ability to be fully engaged while remaining unattached to outcomes.

## The Paradox

Desire strongly. Commit fully. Release completely. This is the path to manifestation.

> **Case: The Need to Know** (Chapter 6): Chopra describes the common mistake of needing to know how things will work out. "The need to know is attachment to the known. The known is the past. You cannot create the new while clinging to the old. Uncertainty is the ground of freedom."
> **Key takeaway**: Not knowing how things will work out is not a problem — it's an opportunity. The uncertainty means anything is possible.

> **Case: The Butterfly** (Chapter on Detachment): Chopra tells the story of a butterfly emerging from a cocoon. "If you try to help the butterfly by opening the cocoon, you kill it. The struggle is necessary for its wings to strengthen. Letting go means trusting the process."
> **Key takeaway**: Your attachment to how things "should" happen can block them from happening at all. Trust the process. Allow the unfolding.
