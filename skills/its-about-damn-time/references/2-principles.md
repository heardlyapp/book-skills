# Key Principles — It's About Damn Time

## I. You Need Only One "Yes" Out of a Hundred

"Save for the extremely rare positive response, I was met with a barrage of nos or was blatantly ignored." Hamilton sent hundreds of cold emails. Most were ignored. A tiny fraction replied. That was enough. "There's never been a time in my life when one person's no has made me give up entirely."

## II. If They Won't Let You In, Write Your Own Invitation

"I was one of only two Black people in the class." When Hamilton couldn't find a seat at the venture capital table, she built her own. Her blog post "Dear White Venture Capitalists" went viral. "Dear White Venture Capitalists: If You're Reading This, It's (Almost!) Too Late."

## III. Turn Being Underestimated Into Your Advantage

"Just as with stocks, companies, or horses, betting on the people everyone else is betting on is not often the savviest way." Hamilton looked for founders who reminded her of herself. Case: Backstage Capital's thesis — "What if the next Mark Zuckerberg is a little Black girl from the South?"

## IV. "Hustle" Looks Different Depending on Your Circumstances

"For most affluent White men, hustle might be pulling an all-nighter. For the people I know, hustle is sacrificing sleep to work on their side project after they've spent ten hours at their day job, come home and fed their kids." Case: Hamilton bought a $1 doughnut to sit in an all-night doughnut shop when she had nowhere else to go.

## V. Forgive to Free Yourself

"Forgiveness is a productivity hack." Hamilton argues that holding grudges is not righteous — it's exhausting. "Forgiveness is not about the other person. It's about freeing yourself from carrying that weight." Case: After being repeatedly rejected by Silicon Valley, she didn't let bitterness consume her — she redirected that energy into building Backstage.

## VI. You Are Your Greatest Investment

"Take care of yourself first. If you're running on empty, you can't help anyone." Hamilton's chapter on "The Danger of Hustle Porn" pushes back against the culture of glorifying burnout. She gave up drinking, focused on hobbies, and learned to say no.

## VII. Unapologetic Authenticity Is Your Superpower

"Don't deny the world your voice." Hamilton never tried to fit the VC mold. She showed up as a Black gay woman from a non-traditional background — and that became her competitive advantage. Case: When she invested in 130+ underestimated founders, it was because she had lived experience that no White male VC could replicate.
