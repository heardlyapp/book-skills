# Core Framework — It's About Damn Time

## Introduction: From Food Stamps to Fast Company

Arlan Hamilton was sleeping on the floor of the San Francisco airport, telling herself: "You are a venture capitalist. You are a venture capitalist." She had no money, no home, certainly no investment capital. She had a suitcase and backpack, rolled-up jeans for a pillow near the Virgin Atlantic check-in desk.

Five years later, she was founder and managing partner of Backstage Capital — a multimillion-dollar fund investing in underestimated founders. In October 2018, she became the first Black female noncelebrity on the cover of Fast Company magazine.

"I'd always had ideas. I'd always felt like an entrepreneur. But my ideas either hadn't led anywhere or hadn't brought me success. When I discovered Silicon Valley and heard about all the ups and downs that founders went through, their failures and successes, it reminded me of myself."

## Part I: Becoming Money

**Chapter 1: Let's Get (In)formation.** Hamilton taught herself venture capital from scratch. "I didn't go to college, and everything I knew about venture capital I learned myself, at home, with a whiteboard, handmade index cards, and that blow-up mattress." She read every book she could find, watched YouTube videos, sent hundreds of cold emails.

**Chapter 3: You Need Only One 'Yes' Out of a Hundred.** "There's never been a time in my life when one person's no has made me give up entirely." She sent hundreds of emails asking for internships, mentorship — most were ignored. A tiny fraction replied. That was enough.

**Chapter 5: Write Your Own Headlines.** "Dear White Venture Capitalists: If You're Reading This, It's (Almost!) Too Late" went viral. "I wanted to turn the idea of pattern matching for White male nerds on its head."

## Part II: Relationships

**Chapter 6: Optimize for People.** "Venture capital is a people business. The best deals come from relationships built long before a term sheet." Hamilton's approach: connect people before you need anything from them.

**Chapter 8: Let Someone Shorter Stand in Front of You.** Use your privilege (whatever it is) to help someone with less. "If you're further along, step back and let someone who hasn't had your advantages take your place."

## Part III: Resilience

**Chapter 10: The Road Trip That Lasted Forever.** Hamilton and her mom shared a hotel room, then she slept on a blow-up mattress in her mom's apartment in Pearland, Texas. "I barely left my room, spending all day and night reading books and blogs, watching YouTube videos, and sending emails."

**Chapter 13: Forgiveness Is a Productivity Hack.** Holding grudges drains energy. "Forgiveness is not about the other person — it's about freeing yourself from carrying that weight."

## Part V: Creativity

**Chapter 19: Natural Ingenuity.** "I've taken the grapes off the stems in the grocery store so I could afford them. I've bought a $1 doughnut so I could sit in an all-night doughnut shop when I've had nowhere else to go." When you have no resources, you develop resourcefulness.

## Part VI: Confidence

**Chapter 22: Imposter Syndrome.** "If you're the only person who looks like you in the room, you're not an imposter — you're a pioneer."

## Part VII: Self-Care

**Chapter 26: The Danger of Hustle Porn.** "Hustle looks completely different depending on your circumstances. For most affluent White men, hustle might be pulling an all-nighter. For the people I know, hustle is sacrificing sleep to work on their side project after they've spent ten hours at their day job, come home and fed their kids."

**Chapter 29: You Are Your Greatest Investment.** "Take care of yourself first. If you're running on empty, you can't help anyone."
