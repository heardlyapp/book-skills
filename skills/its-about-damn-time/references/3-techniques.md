# Techniques — It's About Damn Time

## How to Turn Being Underestimated Into Your Advantage (Based on Arlan Hamilton)

### Technique 1: The Self-Affirmation Ascension
Hamilton's practice: "In order to become, I needed to be." She rode up an escalator in a hotel she couldn't afford to stay in, repeating "You are a venture capitalist." Case: While homeless and sleeping at SFO, she kept telling herself who she was becoming — before she had the money, the title, or the results. The technique: affirm your future identity before it's externally validated.

### Technique 2: The Cold Outreach Machine
Hamilton sent hundreds of cold emails to investors. She asked for internships, mentorship, meetings. Most were ignored. A few replied. "You need only one yes out of a hundred." The technique: volume makes rejection irrelevant. Five years later, Chris Sacca (who contributed $500 to her crowdfunding campaign) was one of those yeses.

### Technique 3: The Viral Content Catalyst
When Hamilton's blog post "Dear White Venture Capitalists" went viral, it changed everything. "I wanted to turn the idea of pattern matching for White male nerds on its head." The technique: write what only you can write. Don't try to sound like everyone else. Your unique perspective is your content strategy.

### Technique 4: The Underdog Investment Thesis
Backstage Capital's thesis: invest in founders who are women, people of color, or LGBTQ. "Just as with stocks, betting on the people everyone else is betting on is not often the savviest way." The technique: your personal experience of being underestimated is market intelligence. Case: Hamilton had lived experience that no White male VC could replicate — she knew these founders, could relate to them, and understood their markets.

### Technique 5: The $1 Doughnut Strategy
When Hamilton had nowhere to go, she bought a $1 doughnut at an all-night shop so she could sit there and work on her laptop. The technique: don't let lack of resources stop you — let it force you to be creative. "I've taken the grapes off the stems in the grocery store so I could afford them."

### Technique 6: The Forgiveness Reset
"Forgiveness is a productivity hack." When someone rejects you, disappoints you, or underestimates you, don't carry the resentment. Release it. Case: Silicon Valley ignored Hamilton for years. She didn't stay angry — she channeled that energy into building her fund.

### Technique 7: The Privilege Pass-Through
"Let someone shorter stand in front of you." When you have any advantage — even a small one — use it to help someone with less. Case: Chris Sacca used his privilege to contribute $500 to an unknown Black woman's crowdfunding campaign. That small act of passing privilege forward helped launch Backstage Capital.
