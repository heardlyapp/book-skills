# 5 — Voice & App

> Source: *Letting Go* by David R. Hawkins.

## Hawkins' Voice

Hawkins writes as a physician, psychiatrist, and spiritual teacher. His tone is clinical yet compassionate — grounded in medical authority (MD, PhD) while addressing the deepest spiritual questions. He presents letting go not as a spiritual belief but as a verifiable mechanism with measurable effects on the body (muscle strength, blood pressure, skin color). This combination of scientific rigor and spiritual depth is what makes his work distinctive.

> **Case: The Opening Example (Chapter 2):** Hawkins doesn't start with philosophy. He starts with a concrete experience everyone has had: "You are in the midst of an intense argument; you are angry and upset, when suddenly the whole thing strikes you as absurd and ridiculous. You start to laugh. The pressure is relieved." The reader immediately recognizes the experience. Hawkins then builds the mechanism from that recognition.

> **Case: The Clinical Framing (Chapter 3):** Hawkins describes the Scale of Consciousness with calibration numbers (Shame=20, Guilt=30, etc.), presenting it as a research finding, not a belief system. He references "scientific research" (Gray-LaViolette theory) that "feeling tones organize thoughts and memory." The clinical framing makes the spiritual content accessible to skeptical, intellectual readers.

## Five Scenarios

### 1. The Chronically Anxious Person

You live with a baseline of anxiety. You've tried meditation, medication, therapy. Hawkins' approach: the anxiety is not the problem — the resistance to the anxiety is. Every time you feel anxious, instead of trying to make it go away, feel it fully and surrender. The reservoir of fear will gradually deplete. You will find your baseline rising on the Scale.

### 2. The Person Holding onto Resentment

You were wronged. You have every right to be angry. But the anger is destroying you, not them. Hawkins' key insight: letting go of the anger does not mean the event was okay. It means you stop carrying the weight. The knowing remains. The rage dissolves.

### 3. The Grieving Person

You've lost someone or something important. Grief is not a problem to be solved — it is a level on the Scale that must be passed through. Allow the grief fully. Do not rush. Each wave of grief that is surrendered brings you closer to acceptance.

### 4. The Spiritual Seeker

You've been on a spiritual path and feel stuck. You've meditated, read, practiced. Hawkins' diagnosis: you're likely stuck on one of the lower levels — Pride (175) or Desire (125). The spiritual path is not about acquiring more knowledge. It is about releasing what is below. Surrender is the vehicle.

### 5. The Person in Emotional Crisis

You're going through a divorce, a job loss, a death, a betrayal. Feelings are overwhelming. Hawkins' crisis protocol: don't resist. Don't act. Surrender repeatedly. The crisis is not a disaster — it is an opportunity for massive purification. Each wave of feeling released is permanent progress.
