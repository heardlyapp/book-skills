# 2 — Key Principles

> Source: *Letting Go* by David R. Hawkins.

## The 7 Principles

### 1. The Resistance IS the Pain

It's not the feeling itself that hurts — it's the resistance to the feeling. When you stop resisting and allow the emotion to be fully present in your body, the pain dissolves. The struggle against the feeling is what creates suffering.

> **Case: The Physical Sensation Experiment (Chapter 2):** Hawkins invites the reader to notice that a feeling is just a physical sensation — a tightness in the chest, a knot in the stomach, heat in the face. The story attached to it (the grievance, the fear scenario) is the mind's creation. The physical sensation itself is neutral. When you drop the story and feel only the sensation, it releases.

### 2. Expression Propagates the Feeling

Contrary to popular belief, venting anger does not release it — it energizes it. Expression allows the surface pressure to release while the reservoir remains intact. True release happens when you surrender the feeling inward, not dump it outward.

### 3. You Cannot Skip Levels

The Scale of Consciousness is sequential. You cannot go from Fear (100) to Love (500) directly. You must move through Desire, Anger, Pride, Courage, Neutrality, Willingness, and Acceptance first. Each released feeling reveals the next one underneath.

### 4. Healing the Past Is Possible Now

Hawkins' key insight: the past is not stored as events — it is stored as feelings. When you surrender the feeling, the associated memories lose their charge. You don't need to process the story. You need to release the feeling. The story fades on its own.

### 5. Surrender Is Active, Not Passive

Letting go is not giving up. It is not resignation. It is a conscious, deliberate act of releasing inner pressure. It takes effort and intention — especially at first.

### 6. Letting Go Is Continuous

You don't "let go once and it's done." You let go continuously throughout the day. Each surrender releases a small portion of the reservoir. Over time, the accumulated pressure drops significantly.

### 7. The Body Knows

> **Case: Muscle Strength Test (Chapter 2):** Hawkins notes that a negative feeling instantly causes a loss of 50% of the body's muscle strength. Positive feelings increase strength. There are detectable changes in skin color, breathing, pulse, blood pressure, and muscle tension during letting go. The body is the feedback system.
