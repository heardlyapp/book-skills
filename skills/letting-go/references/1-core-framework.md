# 1 — Core Framework

> Source: *Letting Go* by David R. Hawkins — Chapters 1-11.

## The Three Frameworks

### Framework A: The Mechanism of Letting Go (Chapter 2)

Hawkins defines letting go as "the sudden cessation of an inner pressure or the dropping of a weight." It's an actual mechanism of the mind — not a concept, not a belief. The process: allow the feeling to be present, give up resisting it, surrender the inner pressure, and the feeling releases. The result: immediate relief, lightness, and happiness.

> **Case: The Argument That Becomes Absurd (Chapter 2):** Hawkins' opening example: you're in an intense argument, angry and upset. Suddenly the whole thing strikes you as absurd. You start to laugh. The pressure is relieved. "You come up from anger, fear, and feeling attacked to feeling suddenly free and happy." Everyone has experienced this. Hawkins' insight: you can learn to do it at will, in any situation.

> **Case: The Accumulated Reservoir (Chapter 2):** Hawkins describes the "huge reservoir of accumulated negative feelings, attitudes, and beliefs" we all carry. This reservoir creates pressure that makes us miserable. It is the basis of "many of our illnesses and problems." One feeling can create thousands of associated thoughts over years. "If we could surrender the underlying painful feeling, all of those thoughts would disappear instantly and we would forget the event."

### Framework B: The Three Wrong Ways (Chapter 2)

Hawkins identifies three common but ineffective ways people handle feelings: suppression (pushing down, leading to physical illness), expression (venting, which propagates the feeling), and escape (distraction through addiction, entertainment, work). Surrender is the fourth path — the only one that actually releases the energy.

> **Case: The Freudian Misinterpretation (Chapter 2):** Hawkins argues that the popular belief in "expressing your feelings" is a misinterpretation of Freud. "What Freud actually said, in classical psychoanalysis, was that the repressed impulse or feeling was to be neutralized, sublimated, socialized, and channeled into constructive drives of love, work, and creativity." Expression does not free you — it propagates the feeling. Surrender is what neutralizes it.

### Framework C: The Scale of Consciousness (Chapter 3)

Hawkins presents a calibrated scale from 0 to 1000, where each level represents a specific energy frequency. Shame (20), Guilt (30), Apathy (50), Grief (75), Fear (100), Desire (125), Anger (150), Pride (175) — all below 200, which is the level of force. Courage (200) is the first level of power. Beyond that: Neutrality (250), Willingness (310), Acceptance (350), Reason (400), Love (500), Joy (540), Peace (600), Enlightenment (700+).

> **Case: The Sudden Shift in an Argument (Chapter 2):** Hawkins uses the example of a fight that suddenly becomes funny to illustrate moving up the Scale in an instant — from Anger (150) or Fear (100) to Acceptance (350) or even Love (500). The letting go mechanism IS the vehicle for moving up the Scale. Each release of a lower-level feeling allows a higher level to emerge naturally.
