# 3 — Techniques

> Source: *Letting Go* by David R. Hawkins.

## Practical Tools

### 1. The 30-Second Letting Go Exercise

When a negative feeling arises: (1) Stop. Don't react. (2) Locate the feeling in your body — chest, throat, stomach, jaw. (3) Drop the story. Focus only on the physical sensation. (4) Consciously surrender the resistance. Say inwardly: "I let this go." (5) Notice the release — a sigh, a softening, a sense of relief. Repeat as needed.

> **Case: The Sudden Release in an Argument (Chapter 2):** "You are in the midst of an intense argument; you are angry and upset, when suddenly the whole thing strikes you as absurd and ridiculous. You start to laugh. The pressure is relieved." This is the mechanism working spontaneously. Hawkins' technique makes it available at will.
> **Key takeaway**: You've already done this thousands of times. The skill just needs to be made conscious and repeatable.

> **Case: The Fear of Public Speaking (Chapter 6):** Hawkins uses fear of public speaking as an example of how surrender works for specific phobias. The fear is not about the speaking event itself — it's about the accumulated pressure of fear within the reservoir being triggered. By surrendering the fear sensation repeatedly, the charge diminishes. Eventually, the event loses its power.
> **Key takeaway**: The external trigger is irrelevant. The feeling is internal. Process the feeling, and the trigger loses its power.

Set a timer for 3 minutes, three times a day. Close your eyes. Bring attention to whatever feeling is present. Let go. Wait for the next feeling to surface. Let go. Continue. Over days, the reservoir of accumulated feelings begins to drain, and you feel progressively lighter.

### 3. The Crisis Protocol

In an emotional crisis: feelings may surge with overwhelming intensity. Do not resist. Do not act. Surrender repeatedly. Each wave of feeling that is released leaves you stronger. The crisis becomes a purification — not a breakdown.

### 4. The Level Calibration

To identify your current level on the Scale of Consciousness: notice what you feel when you're not trying to feel anything. Is there underlying shame? Guilt? Apathy? Grief? Fear? Desire? Anger? Pride? Your baseline emotion is your current calibration. Accept it. Work on releasing the level you're on — don't reach for the next level.

### 5. The "Let Go of Letting Go" Paradox

If you're stuck and "can't let go": the resistance to letting go is itself a feeling. Surrender the resistance. You don't need to force release. You only need to stop holding on. Simply allow whatever is happening to happen. The letting go happens by itself.
