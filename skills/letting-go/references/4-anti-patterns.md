# 4 — Anti-Patterns

> Source: *Letting Go* by David R. Hawkins.

## Common Errors

### 1. Believing Expression Releases the Feeling

**The trap:** "If I scream into a pillow, punch a wall, vent to a friend, I'll feel better."

**Why it fails:** Expression gives the feeling more energy. It releases surface pressure while the reservoir stays full. Worse, expression hurts others and damages relationships. The only true release is inward surrender.

> **Case: The Freudian Misunderstanding (Chapter 2):** "The expression of a feeling, first, tends to propagate that feeling and give it greater energy. Second, the expression of the feeling merely allows the remainder to be suppressed out of awareness." This is why people can vent about the same grievance for years and never feel better.
> **Key takeaway**: If venting worked, you'd be free by now. The fact that you're still angry after talking about it 50 times proves expression is not release.

> **Case: The Workaholic's Escape (Chapter 2):** Hawkins describes the person who stays constantly busy to avoid feeling. "Escape is the avoidance of feelings through diversion. This avoidance is the backbone of the entertainment and liquor industries, and also the route of the workaholic." The person who works 80 hours a week is not productive — they are terrified of what they would feel if they stopped.
> **Key takeaway**: The most socially rewarded escape (workaholism) is still an escape. Productivity can be a mask for emotional avoidance.

**The trap:** "I'm handling this. I'm just not letting it get to me."

**Why it fails:** Suppression drives the feeling underground. It does not release it. The suppressed feeling builds pressure and eventually manifests as physical symptoms: headaches, back pain, hypertension, colitis, insomnia.

### 3. The "I Can't Let Go" Trap

**The trap:** "I've tried. I just can't let it go. There must be something wrong with me."

**Why it fails:** "I can't let go" is itself a feeling (often shame or apathy). It can be surrendered like any other. The resistance to letting go is just another layer of the same reservoir. Surrender the resistance, and the underlying emotion becomes accessible.

### 4. Mistaking Pride for Progress

**The trap:** "I've let go of my anger. I'm now a very forgiving person. Look how evolved I am."

**Why it fails:** Pride (175) is still below Courage (200). It is a negative level — based on dependence on others' opinions. True surrender leads to humility, not self-congratulation.

### 5. Rushing the Process

**The trap:** "I want to get to Love (500) as fast as possible. Why is it taking so long?"

**Why it fails:** You cannot skip levels. Each level must be fully released before the next can emerge. Trying to bypass grief to reach love only buries grief deeper. Patience and continuous surrender are the only way.
