# Key Principles — Dreamstate: A Conspiracy Theory

## I. The Universe Does Not Exist

"The central premise of this book is the admittedly ridiculous but incontrovertibly true assertion that the universe does not exist." This is not a metaphor. Reality as we perceive it is a dream. The dead planet analogy: we are asleep on a featureless rock, dreaming a rich dreamworld to avoid the boredom of existence. "The only thing you can say for sure about the dreamstate is that you can't say anything for sure about it, and you can't even say that for sure."

## II. Awareness and Appearance Are the Only Things

Only two things exist (one of which doesn't): awareness (consciousness, which is true) and appearance (the content of consciousness, which is not true). "It doesn't get much simpler than that." You cannot have awareness without something to be aware of, and appearance cannot appear without awareness. "Yin and yang."

## III. All Knowledge Beyond I-Am Is Belief

"Beyond I Am, there is no knowledge to be found in the dreamstate, only anti-knowledge." Philosophy, religion, science — all are belief systems masquerading as knowledge. "I-Am/Consciousness is the weapon that destroys the universe." The only thing any sentient being can truly know is "I am aware."

## IV. Thinking Threatens the Dream

"The thing that threatens our repose is thinking. Thinking is something we can do and which we believe we do, but which we really don't do. It's like Adam and Eve with the apple — you are given this magnificent paradise to enjoy, and all you have to do in return is not eat the apple." Biting the apple of inquiry risks waking up.

## V. Waking Up Is Boring

"Boredom is the enemy of awareness." Once awake from the dreamstate, you realize it's boring — and you want back in. "You traded the dream of something for the truth of nothing, and now you want your something back." But you can't have it — you can't unsee the truth.

## VI. Spirituality Is the Enemy of Awakening

"Spirituality is about staying comfortably asleep in the dreamstate. Waking up in the dreamstate might be thought of as trans-spiritual and waking from it as anti-spiritual." Buddhism produces Buddhists, not Buddhas. "Ditto all -isms." Institutions exist to perpetuate themselves, not to produce enlightened beings.

## VII. There Is No Self

"The journey of awakening is the journey to no-self, meaning that self itself is but a dream." There is no one to wake up. "Who is all this for? Who's dream is this, and who wants to wake up?" The question undermines itself.
