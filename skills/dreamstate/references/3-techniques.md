# Techniques — Dreamstate: A Conspiracy Theory

## How to Approach the Dreamstate (Based on Jed McKenna)

### Technique 1: The Self-Inquiry Attack
McKenna presents I-Am/Consciousness as the only weapon against the dreamstate. Ask: "Who is aware of this experience?" Not intellectually — directly. The question "Who am I?" pursued ruthlessly leads to the collapse of the sense of separate self. Case: "Beyond I Am, there is no knowledge to be found. The only thing any sentient being can know is I Am. There is no higher someone that somehow knows something you don't."

### Technique 2: The Belief Excision
McKenna's method: identify anything you believe and recognize it as belief, not knowledge. "All supposed knowledge is really belief masquerading as knowledge, and the word for belief masquerading as knowledge is delusion." Case: McKenna dispatches God in a few paragraphs: "With one diamond bullet, we have killed God, gods, science, philosophy, religion, and any and all other claimants to knowledge."

### Technique 3: The Never-Focus
"Never focus, that's the key. Just let your vision go soft and let the dreamworld shimmer back into place." After waking up, you must learn to re-enter the dream carefully. The analogy: sleeping when you're not tired — "any thought, any sound, any disturbance, and you snap back into alertness and the delicate dreamworld vanishes."

### Technique 4: The Solitude Strategy
McKenna retreated to a remote Appalachian cabin with only his dog Maya. He cut firewood, read, and wrote. "I start my days slowly, ramping up the fire and having coffee and a light meal, then Maya and I set out for a walk." The strategy: strip away all distraction and social conditioning to face the bare reality of existence.

### Technique 5: The Apple Bite (Dangerous Inquiry)
"Simply by thinking about your situation, by inquiring, by biting the apple, you risk spontaneous emergence from the sub-lucid dreamstate into desolation." McKenna warns: this can happen accidentally. One minute you're "sleepwalking" and the next you've walked off a cliff. "There's no undo button, no do-overs, no going back."

### Technique 6: The Lava Lamp Test
McKenna keeps a lava lamp in his cabin. "It makes a nice nightlight and makes the cabin feel more groovy than spooky at night, and gives it a cool alien glow from a distance." The technique: find small aesthetic pleasures that keep the dreamstate tolerable without trying to find deeper meaning in them.

### Technique 7: The Play-Within-the-Dream
McKenna writes plays (Fedallah, A Nice Game of Chess). He knows they're ultimately meaningless but does them anyway. "All my clever idea-play has kept me amused." The technique accept the dream as entertainment, participate without attachment, and don't mistake any of it for truth.
