# Voice and Application: Dougherty's Perspective

> Source: Golden Gates, throughout

## Dougherty's Voice

Conor Dougherty writes as a journalist — fair, curious, and non-ideological. He spent years covering housing for the New York Times, and the book reflects that: he profiles both YIMBY activists and NIMBY homeowners, developers and tenant organizers, without taking sides.

## Application: Housing Advocates

If you're fighting for more housing, the book offers a playbook: show up at planning meetings (Sonja Trauss started by just showing up), build coalitions across traditional divides, use state law to preempt local obstruction, and tell human stories.

## Application: Policymakers

The book shows that state preemption of local zoning is the most effective tool for increasing housing supply. Cities won't solve the crisis on their own — they need state mandates.

## Application: Concerned Citizens

Attend your local planning commission meeting. Listen to both sides. The housing debate is happening in every growing city, and your voice matters.

## Application: Developers

The book profiles the challenges of building in a hostile regulatory environment. The lesson: engage with communities early, build affordable units without being required to, and tell your story.
