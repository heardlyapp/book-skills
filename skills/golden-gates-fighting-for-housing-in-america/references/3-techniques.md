# Techniques: Rent Control, Tenants, and Development

> Source: Golden Gates, Chapters 7-10

## Rent Control Debate

Rent control protects existing tenants but can discourage new building. Landlords convert rental units to condos or owner-occupied rather than renting at controlled rates. The book presents both sides without endorsing either — rent control is a tool with real tradeoffs.

> **Case: The 15-Year-Old Organizer** (Chapter 7): A fifteen-year-old girl led her apartment complex in a fight against eviction after a new owner bought the building. She organized tenants, attended hearings, and became a local activist. Her story shows that tenant organizing can work at the grassroots level — even with the most unlikely leaders.

> **Case: Sonja for Supervisor** (Chapter 9): Sonja Trauss ran for San Francisco Supervisor. Her campaign was a test of whether YIMBY politics could win at the ballot box. She lost, but the campaign showed that housing had become a central political issue that could no longer be ignored.

## Development as Solution

Building more housing is the only long-term solution. But who builds it, for whom, and at what cost? The book profiles developers, architects, and activists trying to build affordable housing at scale. The challenge: high land costs, lengthy permitting, community opposition, and financing challenges.

> **Case: The Value-Add Investor** (Chapter 8): A developer profile shows the economics of housing construction in San Francisco. Land is expensive, permits take years, and every project faces community opposition. The developer's goal was to build housing for middle-income families — but economics forced them to build luxury units to make the numbers work.
