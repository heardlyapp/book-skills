# Anti-Patterns: Housing Debate Traps

> Source: Golden Gates, Chapters 5, 6, Epilogue

## Single-Villain Theories

Attributing the crisis to one cause: greedy developers, tech workers, rent control, or foreign investors. The reality is more complex.

> **Case: The Tech Worker Scapegoat** (throughout): Tech workers are often blamed for rising rents. But the real problem is the housing shortage — which exists because local zoning prevented building enough homes for decades before tech arrived. Blaming tech workers is a distraction.

> **Case: The Developer Villain** (Chapter 8): Developers are necessary to build housing. The tension between profit-motivated building and public good is real, but demonizing developers doesn't create more homes.

## NIMBY as Progressivism

Some progressive activists oppose development in the name of protecting low-income communities from gentrification. The book shows this can backfire — blocking new housing in wealthy areas preserves exclusion.

> **Key takeaway**: The most progressive-sounding housing policies can have regressive effects. Rent control without new building protects some tenants but squeezes the rental market for everyone else.

## Planning Paralysis

The planning process creates years of delays and uncertainty. Every project requires approvals from multiple bodies, faces lawsuits from neighbors, and can be killed at any stage.

> **Key takeaway**: The current system gives opponents infinite veto points and supporters no path to certainty. This is by design — and it's why we can't build.
