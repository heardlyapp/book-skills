# 3 — Spiritual Traps and Delusions

> Source: "The End of Your World" by Adyashanti

## Common Traps

- **Spiritual ego**: "I am awakened." The most subtle trap.
- **The special one**: Believing you have a unique realization others don't.
- **Fixation on experiences**: Trying to recreate the initial awakening.
- **Escape from the ordinary**: Using spirituality to avoid normal life.
- **False emptiness**: Mistaking dissociation or numbness for non-attachment.

> **Case: The Spiritual Ego** (Chapter 8): Adyashanti is direct: "The spiritual ego is the last and most subtle fortress of the separate self. It uses the language of enlightenment to maintain a sense of superiority."
> **Key takeaway**: If you find yourself thinking "I am more awake than others," check for spiritual ego. True awakening humbles, not elevates.

> **Case: The Escape Artist** (Chapter 9): Some spiritual seekers use enlightenment as an escape from ordinary life. Adyashanti: "If your spirituality makes you less present in your relationships, less engaged with life, it's not true awakening — it's dissociation. Awakening brings you more fully into life, not away from it."
> **Key takeaway**: True awakening doesn't take you out of the world. It brings you fully into it. If you're using spirituality to escape, check your motivation.
