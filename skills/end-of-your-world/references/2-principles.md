# 2 — The "I Got It, I Lost It" Phenomenon

> Source: "The End of Your World" by Adyashanti

## The Pattern

You have a profound spiritual experience. You feel completely free. You think: "I've got it." Then the experience fades. You think: "I lost it." You start seeking again.

## The Truth

You didn't lose it. You never had it. What you had was an experience of truth — not truth itself. The realization is still there. It's the experience that passed.

> **Case: The Return of the Ego** (Chapter 5): Adyashanti explains that after awakening, the ego doesn't disappear — it reconfigures. "The ego is very clever. It will pretend to be enlightened. It will say 'I am that.' But the 'I' that says that is the same old self in new clothing."
> **Key takeaway**: The ego can mimic enlightenment. The real test is not the experience — it's whether the seeking has ended.

> **Case: The Ceiling of Experience** (Chapter 4): Adyashanti describes spiritual experiences as having a ceiling. "You can have the most profound experience, and it will still be an experience. It will end. What is beyond experience cannot be experienced — it can only be realized."
> **Key takeaway**: Don't chase experiences. Even the most profound ones pass. What remains is the realization of your true nature — and that doesn't come and go.
