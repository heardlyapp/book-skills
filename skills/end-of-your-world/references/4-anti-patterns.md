# 4 — Practice and Self-Inquiry

> Source: "The End of Your World" by Adyashanti

## Anti-Pattern: Seeking as Avoidance

**The mistake:** Using spiritual practice to avoid being present. It's possible to meditate, inquire, and seek for decades without ever actually facing the truth.

## The Real Practice

- **Self-inquiry**: "Who am I?" Not as a question to answer, but as a tool to look.
- **Presence**: Rest as awareness. Not doing — being.
- **Letting go of the seeker**: The final step is to stop seeking and simply live.

> **Case: The End of Seeking** (Chapter 10): Adyashanti's final teaching: "The end of your world is the end of seeking. When seeking stops, what remains? Not a better seeker. Not an enlightened person. Just this — ordinary, immediate, alive."
> **Key takeaway**: The goal is not to become a good seeker. It's to end seeking. When the seeker collapses, what's left is your true nature.

> **Case: The Sierra Mountains** (Dedication): Adyashanti dedicates the book to the Sierra Nevada mountains. "In your high places, where the air gets thin, I found my natural church and cathedral." The natural world is a direct pointer to the truth — no concepts, no doctrine, just presence.
> **Key takeaway**: Sometimes the most direct teaching is not in a book or a teacher — it's in the silence of nature. Let the mountains teach you.
