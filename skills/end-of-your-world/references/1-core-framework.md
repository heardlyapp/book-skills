# 1 — Awakening and What Comes After

> Source: "The End of Your World" by Adyashanti

## The Framework: Awakening → Disorientation → Integration → Living

Awakening is the first step, not the last. Adyashanti is ruthlessly honest about this.

### Awakening
A direct realization of your true nature. The recognition that you are not the separate self you thought you were. This can be sudden or gradual.

### Disorientation
After awakening, the old framework is gone. You don't know how to operate. This is normal.

### Integration
The real work. Bringing the realization into every aspect of life — relationships, work, emotions, the body.

### Living
When integration is complete, there's no more seeking. Just living. Ordinary life, seen clearly.

> **Case: The Disorientation** (Chapter 2): Adyashanti describes the period after awakening as profoundly disorienting. "The world you knew is gone. The person you thought you were is gone. But nothing has taken their place yet. It's a kind of spiritual limbo."
> **Key takeaway**: Disorientation after awakening is not a problem — it's a necessary stage. Don't rush through it. The discomfort is the dissolution of the old self.

> **Case: The First Three Years** (Introduction): Adyashanti reflects on his own awakening. "The initial realization was profound. But it took years to fully integrate. I thought I was done. I was just beginning." This is the book's central message: awakening is the start.
> **Key takeaway**: If you've awakened and feel like you're not "done," you're not doing anything wrong. The realization is real. The integration takes time.
