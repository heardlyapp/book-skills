# 3 — Techniques

> Source: *The Book of Life* by Jiddu Krishnamurti — practical methods from the daily meditations.

## Notes on "Technique" in Krishnamurti

Krishnamurti would insist there are no techniques — only direct perception. What follows are descriptions of *what happens* when one observes correctly, not methods to be practiced.

### Practice 1: The Writing Practice (December 25)

If awareness is difficult, Krishnamurti suggests: write down every thought and feeling that arises throughout the day. Record your reactions of jealousy, envy, vanity, sensuality, the intentions behind your words. In the evening, study what you wrote — not to judge or analyze, but just to see.

**What this does:** It brings the unconscious to the conscious. Patterns become visible. "In the flame of self-awareness, the causes of conflict are discovered and consumed."

> **Daily reading: December 25** — "Writing down what one thinks and feels, one's desires and reactions, brings about an inward awareness, the cooperation of the unconscious with the conscious."

### Practice 2: Stay With a Feeling (May 14)

When a feeling arises — anger, jealousy, envy — try to remain with it, pure and simple, without naming it, without saying "this is bad" or "I must overcome it."

**What happens:** The mind, not being able to do anything, becomes quiet. In that quietness, the feeling transforms.

> **Daily reading: May 14** — "Try remaining with the feeling of hate, with the feeling of envy, jealousy, with the venom of ambition. See if you can stay with that feeling. You will find it amazingly difficult."

### Practice 3: The Gap Between Thoughts (September 9)

Watch the interval between two thoughts. Though it may be an infinitesimal fraction of a second, there is a gap. In that gap, there is silence — and that silence is not of time.

**What happens:** By discovering the interval, you contact a space that is not conditioned by the past. "That period of silence, that interval, is not of time."

> **Daily reading: September 9** — "If you watch very carefully you will see that it is not a constant but that there is an interval between two thoughts… in that interval there is a quietness, there is a silence in which alone there is a comprehension."

### Practice 4: Die to Things During Life (November 11-15)

To end the fear of death, die every day — die to your possessions, your habits, your images, your relationships. Not physically, but psychologically: let go of the known each day.

**What happens:** You meet life fresh. The fear of death disappears because you're already dying — and being reborn.

> **Daily reading: November 15** — "You have to die to death while living, by dying to your name, to your house, to your property, to your cause, so that you are fresh, young, clear."

### Practice 5: The Pre-Breakfast Self-Study (December 25)

Set aside time in the morning — before breakfast — to write down your thoughts, feelings, and reactions from the previous day. Do this without judgment. Over days and weeks, you become aware of patterns you never saw.

**What happens:** The unconscious cooperates with the conscious. Integration and understanding arise naturally.

### Practice 6: Observe Without the Word (May 15-18)

When you look at a tree, a person, a feeling — can you look without the word? The word "tree" is not the tree. The word "love" is not love. The word creates a frontier.

**What happens:** Without the word, the mind has no boundary. It is open to what is.

> **Daily reading: May 17** — "The word creates the limitation, the boundary. A mind that is not functioning in words has no limitation; it has no frontiers."
