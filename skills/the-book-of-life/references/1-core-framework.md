# 1 — Core Framework

> Source: *The Book of Life* by Jiddu Krishnamurti — distributed across all 12 months of daily meditations.

## The Six Interlocking Frameworks

### Framework A: Choiceless Awareness (January, May, June)

Krishnamurti's central method (which is not a method): observation without selection, condemnation, or justification. You don't choose what to observe — you see everything as it is.

**How it differs from concentration:** Concentration forces the mind to a point. Awareness has no boundaries. "When there is a motive for attention, is there attention? If I pay attention to acquire something, the acquisition is not attention — it is a distraction."

> **Daily reading: January 1** — "Have you ever sat very silently, not with your attention fixed on anything, not making an effort to concentrate, but with the mind very quiet, really still? Then you hear everything…"

> **Daily reading: June 10** — "Concentration is a process of forcing the mind to narrow down to a point, whereas attention is without frontiers."

### Framework B: The Observer is the Observed (August, October)

The most radical insight in the book. The thinker, the watcher, the censor, the one who "experiences" — is not separate from the thought, the feeling, the experience. They are one. As long as the observer thinks he's separate, he tries to control the observed — which creates conflict, effort, and suffering.

**When this is seen deeply:** The division collapses. The thinker is the thought. The experiencer is the experience. There is no entity controlling — and the conflict between "what I am" and "what I should be" ends.

> **Daily reading: August 15** — "When there is a division between the observer and the observed, there is time, and therefore there is no ending of sorrow."

> **Daily reading: August 16** — "The thinker is made up of qualities; his qualities cannot be separated from himself. The controller is the controlled."

### Framework C: Freedom from the Known (February, September)

All knowledge is the past. The mind is the result of time, memory, conditioning. To meet life anew, the mind must be free of the known — not rejecting knowledge but not being burdened by it psychologically.

**The key question:** "Can the mind be free from the past, free from thought — not from good or bad thought, but from the very process of thought as the only response?"

> **Daily reading: September 16** — "To discover anything new you must start on your own; you must start on a journey completely denuded, especially of knowledge."

> **Daily reading: September 20** — "Learning is never cumulative; it is a movement of knowing which has no beginning and no end."

### Framework D: Self-Knowledge (January, October)

Not knowledge of a "higher self" or Atman — but direct, moment-to-moment observation of the actual self: the bundle of memories, fears, ambitions, envy, greed that constitute the "me."

**Why it's essential:** Without self-knowledge, experience breeds illusion. Without knowing yourself, whatever you do — pray, meditate, serve, renounce — is founded on ignorance.

> **Daily reading: January 23** — "Self-knowledge is obviously a process, not an end in itself; and to know oneself, one must be aware of oneself in action, which is relationship."

### Framework E: Relationship as Mirror (March, April)

"You cannot know yourself in isolation." Relationship — with people, with property, with ideas — is the mirror in which the self is revealed. Most people use relationship to escape, to possess, to feel secure. But the real function of relationship is self-revelation.

> **Daily reading: March 15** — "Relationship is a mirror in which I see myself as I am; but as most of us do not like what we are, we begin to discipline what we perceive."

> **Daily reading: March 17** — "How can two images which are the result of thought, of pleasure… have any affection or love?"

### Framework F: The Ending of Sorrow (July, November)

Sorrow is not a problem to be solved but a fact to be understood. Every escape — belief, work, amusement, religion — delays the ending of sorrow. The ending comes when you stay completely with sorrow, without naming it, without running from it.

> **Daily reading: July 23** — "Sorrow has an ending, but it does not come about through any system or method. There is no sorrow when there is perception of what is."

> **Daily reading: July 31** — "If you would understand sorrow, surely you must love it — that is, you must be in direct communion with it."
