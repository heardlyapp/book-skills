# 4 — Anti-Patterns

> Source: *The Book of Life* by Jiddu Krishnamurti — across all 12 months.

## Common Errors Exposed

### Anti-Pattern 1: Seeking a Method or System

**The trap:** "If I find the right method, practice, guru, or technique, I will reach truth or enlightenment."

**Why it fails:** Every method conditions the mind according to the result it desires. A mind conditioned by a system cannot be free. "Method only conditions the mind according to the result which is desired."

> **Daily reading: October 18** — "Will a method produce a new mind? Any method, a system, a practice, a repetitive thing day after day — will it produce a new mind? Surely, a method implies a continuity of a practice… which becomes mechanical."

### Anti-Pattern 2: The Ideal (Becoming Non-Violent)

**The trap:** "I am violent, so I will practice non-violence. I will become non-violent."

**Why it fails:** The ideal of non-violence is a projection of what the mind *wants* to be. It prevents you from looking at the fact of violence directly. The energy that goes into pursuing the ideal could be used to understand the fact.

> **Daily reading: June 25** — "When you are actually violent and you have an ideal of non-violence, you have a conflict. You are always trying to become non-violent — which is a part of the conflict. To realize that you are violent is the first thing to do — not try to become non-violent."

### Anti-Pattern 3: Psychological Time (Gradual Change)

**The trap:** "I will change gradually, over time. Today I am this, but eventually I will become that."

**Why it fails:** Psychological time — the "future" created by thought — is a trick. The "me" that wants to change and the "me" that will change are the same. "Time is a poison; it creates disorder."

> **Daily reading: October 1** — "The question is whether psychological time can bring not only order within the individual but also social order. To a man who is in sorrow, there is no significance in looking to time for a solution."

### Anti-Pattern 4: Belief as Shield

**The trap:** "I believe in God, in reincarnation, in karma, in [any ideology]. This belief gives me comfort and meaning."

**Why it fails:** Belief is a form of self-protection. It prevents direct perception of what is. "Belief is corruption because behind belief lurk the self, growing big, powerful, and strong."

> **Daily reading: February 14** — "A belief, religious or political, obviously hinders the understanding of ourselves. It acts as a screen through which we look at ourselves."

### Anti-Pattern 5: The Cultivation of Virtue

**The trap:** "I will practice humility, compassion, non-attachment. By disciplining myself, I will become virtuous."

**Why it fails:** Virtue that is practiced, cultivated, or pursued is the self strengthening itself. "A virtuous person who is conscious of pursuing virtue can never find reality."

> **Daily reading: February 7** — "When he says 'I must be without greed,' the state in which he is non-greedy and which he experiences, strengthens the self. That is why it is so important to be poor, not only in the things of the world, but also in belief and in knowledge."

### Anti-Pattern 6: The Authority Trap

**The trap:** "I need a teacher, a guru, a scripture to guide me. I cannot do this alone."

**Why it fails:** Authority — whether of another or of your own past experience — prevents learning. "You cannot follow a leader and understand yourself."

> **Daily reading: January 21** — "Authority of any kind is blinding, it breeds thoughtlessness. Self-awareness is arduous, and since most of us prefer an easy, illusory way, we bring into being the authority that gives shape and pattern to our life."
