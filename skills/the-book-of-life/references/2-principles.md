# 2 — Key Principles

> Source: *The Book of Life* by Jiddu Krishnamurti — synthesized from all 12 months.

## The 8 Principles of Self-Inquiry

### 1. Don't Name the Feeling

The moment you call jealousy "jealousy," you bring it into the framework of the old. The word strengthens the feeling and gives it continuity. Stay with the feeling without the word — just the raw sensation, unnamed.

> **Daily reading: May 13** — "If you do not name a feeling, then what happens? The mind is quiet. When the mind is really quiet, then the feelings which arise can be dealt with immediately."

### 2. Live With the Fact, Not the Idea

The "what is" is always in the present. The "what should be" is an idea, a projection of the mind. When you escape from what is into an ideal, you delay action and deepen conflict.

> **Daily reading: February 8** — "To understand what is requires an enormous capacity, a swift and unprejudiced mind. It is because we don't want to face and understand what is that we invent the many ways of escape."

### 3. Never Compare

Comparison creates the "I am this, I want to be that" structure — the very engine of psychological conflict. When you see yourself without comparing, learning begins.

> **Daily reading: January 11** — "Comparison brings about frustration and merely encourages envy, which is called competition. Like other forms of persuasion, comparison prevents learning and breeds fear."

### 4. Authority Prevents Learning

When you follow a guru, a system, or even your own past experience as authority, you stop learning. Learning requires the freedom to see anew each moment.

> **Daily reading: January 14** — "Authority prevents learning — learning that is not the accumulation of knowledge as memory. A man who is burdened with knowledge is never free, and therefore he is incapable of learning."

### 5. The Means and the End Are One

There is no path to truth. If you use wrong means (force, discipline, suppression) to reach a "good" end, the end is corrupted. The way you act is the result.

### 6. Die to the Past Each Day

Renewal is not possible if the present is burdened with the experience of yesterday. Death to the known is the only way to be new.

> **Daily reading: November 10** — "The mind that dies every day to the memories of yesterday, to all the joys and sorrows of the past — such a mind is fresh, innocent, it has no age."

### 7. Love Is Not Pleasure

Love has no cause, no motive, no attachment. When you love someone because they give you pleasure, you love the pleasure, not the person. Love is a state of being.

> **Daily reading: April 11** — "Love is the unknowable. It can be realized only when the known is understood and transcended."

### 8. The Self Is the Problem

Not "my problems," but the "me" itself — the center around which all problems form. Every effort to improve the self only strengthens it. Understanding the self — not improving it — is the only way out.
