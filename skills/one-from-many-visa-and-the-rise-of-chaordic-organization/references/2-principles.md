# Principles: Purpose, Principles, People, Concepts

> Source: One from Many, Chapters 4-6

## The Four Pillars

Hock's framework: start with purpose, define principles, trust people, build concepts that embody all three.

> **Case: The Purpose Exercise** (Chapter 4): Hock describes the painful process of defining VISA's purpose. Dozens of drafts, months of argument among competing banks. The final statement: "to create the world's most valuable payment system." Simple, but it aligned everyone.

> **Key takeaway**: Getting purpose right takes time and conflict. A vague purpose produces a vague organization. The struggle to define it is essential.

## Principles Over Rules

Principles guide judgment. Rules replace it. Chaordic organizations run on principles.

> **Case: The Hundred-Page Manual** (Chapter 5): Before VISA, credit card operations were governed by hundreds of pages of rules. Hock replaced them with a few clear principles. Member banks used judgment to apply them — no central approval needed. The principles worked better than the rules ever did.

> **Case: The Cooperative Governance** (Chapter 5): VISA's governance was radically different: one bank, one vote — regardless of size. Small banks had the same voice as giants. This principle ensured the system served all members, not just the largest.

## People Before Structure

Hire principled people and trust them. Structure follows.

> **Key takeaway**: Rules are for those who can't be trusted with principles. Hire good people, define clear principles, and get out of their way.
