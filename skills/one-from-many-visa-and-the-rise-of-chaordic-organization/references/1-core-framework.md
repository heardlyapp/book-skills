# Core Framework: VISA and the Chaordic Idea

> Source: One from Many, Introduction and Chapters 1-3

## The Problem VISA Solved

In the 1960s, credit cards were local and incompatible. Banks couldn't agree on standards. Travelers carried dozens of cards. The industry was paralyzed by competitive gridlock.

> **Case: The Impossible Task** (Introduction): Dee Hock was a mid-level banker asked to create a national credit card system. His radical solution: not a bank, not a corporation — a cooperative owned by thousands of competing banks. Everyone said it was impossible. He built it anyway, and it became VISA.

> **Key takeaway**: Hock's breakthrough was not technological but organizational. He realized the problem was the structure of competition itself.

## What Is a Chaordic Organization?

Chaordic = chaos + order. A self-organizing system structured by purpose and principles, not by hierarchy and rules.

> **Case: VISA's Near-Zero Central Staff** (Chapter 3): In its early years, VISA had almost no central employees. It was owned and operated by its member banks. Decisions were made collectively. The "headquarters" was a set of shared agreements. This proved that an organization can exist without a traditional corporate structure.

> **Case: The "Old Monkey Mind"** (Chapter 1): Hock describes his skeptical inner voice — "Old Monkey Mind" — as the rational, conservative part that said his ideas were crazy. Learning to trust his deeper intuition, he argues, is essential for innovation.

> **Key takeaway**: VISA proved that organizations can function without traditional hierarchy. Purpose and principles replace command and control.
