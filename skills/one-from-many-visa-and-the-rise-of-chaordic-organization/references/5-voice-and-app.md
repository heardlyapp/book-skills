# Voice and Application: Hock's Approach

> Source: One from Many, throughout

## Hock's Voice

Dee Hock writes as a philosopher-organizer. His voice combines practical business wisdom with deep reflection on human nature and systems thinking. He quotes poets and physicists alongside banking cases.

> **Case: The "Old Monkey Mind"** (Chapter 1): Hock introduces his skeptical inner voice — "Old Monkey Mind" — the part that says "this is crazy." Learning to listen past it to deeper intuition was essential. This personal framing makes the book intimate and honest.

> **Case: The Lucretius Epigraph** (Chapter 1): Hock opens with Lucretius on how all things flow and change. A banker quoting an ancient Roman poet about atoms and transformation — that's the voice: unexpected, deep, and never merely technical.

> **Key takeaway**: Hock's voice is personal, philosophical, and practical. He's not just describing VISA — he's teaching a way of thinking about organization and life.

## Application: Organizational Design

Start with purpose. Define principles. Trust people. Let structure follow. Works for teams, companies, and communities.

## Application: Leadership

Lead through purpose and influence, not authority.

## Application: Innovation

The greatest leverage point is how we organize ourselves, not any specific technology.
