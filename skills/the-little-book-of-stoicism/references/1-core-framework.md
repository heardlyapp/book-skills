# Stoic Foundations

> Source: The Little Book of Stoicism, Chapters 1-3

## Core Framework

### The Three Great Stoics

| Stoic | When | Key Idea |
|-------|------|----------|
| Epictetus | 55-135 CE | Dichotomy of control: some things are up to us, others are not |
| Seneca | 4 BCE-65 CE | Life is long if you know how to use it; death gives life meaning |
| Marcus Aurelius | 121-180 CE | The inner citadel: you have power over your mind, not outside events |

> **Case: Epictetus and the Dichotomy of Control** (Chapter 2)
> Epictetus was born a slave in Hierapolis. He was crippled (his leg was deliberately broken by his master). He studied Stoicism, was freed, and became the most influential Stoic teacher of his age. His Enchiridion (Handbook) opens: "Some things are up to us, and others are not." What is up to us: opinion, impulse, desire, aversion — everything that is our own doing. What is not up to us: body, property, reputation, wealth — everything that is not our own doing. This single distinction is the entirety of Stoic practice.
> **Key takeaway:** The dichotomy of control is the most practical idea in all of philosophy. Whenever you are anxious, ask: is this within my control? If yes, act. If no, let go. That is the entire system.

> **Case: Marcus Aurelius and the Inner Citadel** (Chapter 3)
> Marcus Aurelius was the emperor of Rome — the most powerful man in the world. His Meditations was his private journal, never intended for publication. In it, he constantly reminded himself: "You have power over your mind — not outside events. Realize this, and you will find strength." Marcus faced war, plague, betrayal, and the death of his children. His journal was his way of maintaining his composure. The "inner citadel" is the fortress of the mind that no external force can breach.
> **Key takeaway:** If the emperor of Rome could find peace through Stoicism, so can you. Marcus's Meditations is proof that Stoicism is not just for philosophers — it is for people who carry immense responsibility and need a way to bear it.
