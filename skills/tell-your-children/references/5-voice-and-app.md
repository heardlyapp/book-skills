# 5 — Berenson's Voice + Application

> Source: "Tell Your Children" by Alex Berenson

## Berenson's Voice

Alex Berenson is a former New York Times reporter and author. His voice: investigative, urgent, controversial. He positions himself as someone willing to tell an unpopular truth. The book is unapologetically polemical.

> **Case: Why He Wrote the Book** (Introduction): Berenson explains that he started researching marijuana expecting to find it mostly harmless. "I was wrong. The evidence forced me to change my mind." His journey from agnostic to alarmist shapes the book's narrative arc.
> **Key takeaway**: Berenson's credibility rests on his claim that he followed the evidence, not ideology. Readers can judge for themselves.

## Key Quote

"Everything you're about to read is true. And it's been hidden from you."

## 5 Scenarios

1. **Teenager using cannabis** → The adolescent brain is uniquely vulnerable. The evidence on psychosis and IQ decline is strongest for teen users.
2. **Daily adult user** → Daily use carries high risk of dependence. Consider tolerance breaks. Be honest with yourself about why you're using.
3. **Parent concerned** → Talk to your kids. Learn about potency. Don't assume it's harmless.
4. **Debating legalization** → Consider potency regulation, age limits, marketing restrictions, and public health consequences.
5. **Using for medical reasons** → Medical use is different. Consult a doctor. Use standardized products with known doses.
