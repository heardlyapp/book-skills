# 3 — Addiction and Withdrawal

> Source: "Tell Your Children" by Alex Berenson

## Cannabis Use Disorder

- ~9% of all users become dependent
- ~17% of those who start as teens
- ~25-50% of daily users
- Withdrawal syndrome: irritability, anxiety, insomnia, depression, loss of appetite

## Why It's Underdiagnosed

- Many users and doctors don't recognize cannabis addiction
- Withdrawal is less dramatic than opioid or alcohol withdrawal
- The "it's just weed" narrative minimizes the problem

> **Case: The Treatment Gap** (Chapter 7): Berenson reports that cannabis use disorder treatment admissions have risen sharply in states that legalized. Yet most users don't seek treatment because they don't believe cannabis is addictive. "The first step to treating a problem is acknowledging it exists."
> **Key takeaway**: The narrative that cannabis is non-addictive prevents people from seeking help. Addiction is real. Treatment is available.

> **Case: The Withdrawal Reality** (Chapter 8): Berenson describes the cannabis withdrawal syndrome — not life-threatening but deeply unpleasant. "Users report irritability, anxiety, insomnia, strange dreams, loss of appetite, and depression. These symptoms can last 1-2 weeks and are a major reason heavy users can't quit."
> **Key takeaway**: Cannabis withdrawal is real and can be severe enough to prevent users from quitting despite wanting to. Knowledge is the first step to treatment.
