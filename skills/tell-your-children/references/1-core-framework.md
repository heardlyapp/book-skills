# 1 — The Evidence Framework

> Source: "Tell Your Children" by Alex Berenson

## The Core Argument

Berenson argues that the widespread acceptance of marijuana as harmless is wrong. The evidence shows that high-THC cannabis carries serious risks, especially for young people.

## THC Potency

- 1970s: 2-4% THC
- 1990s: 8-12% THC
- 2020s: 15-25% THC (flower), 60-90% THC (concentrates)
- This is a fundamentally different drug from what previous generations used.

> **Case: The Colorado Experiment** (Chapter 3): Berenson examines Colorado after legalization. Emergency room visits for cannabis-related psychosis increased sharply. Hospitalizations for cyclic vomiting syndrome (caused by heavy use) rose dramatically. Calls to poison control centers for cannabis exposure in children spiked.
> **Key takeaway**: Legalization created a natural experiment, and the early results show increased public health burdens that were not predicted by legalization advocates.

> **Case: The Schizophrenia Data** (Chapter 5): Berenson cites multiple large-scale studies showing that cannabis use — especially high-THC cannabis — increases the risk of schizophrenia. A Swedish study of 50,000 conscripts found that heavy cannabis users at 18 were 6 times more likely to develop schizophrenia in the next 15 years.
> **Key takeaway**: The relationship between cannabis use and psychosis is not just correlation — large longitudinal studies show a dose-response relationship. The heavier the use, the higher the risk.
