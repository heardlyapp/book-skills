# 4 — Anti-Patterns

> Source: *Go Like Hell* by A. J. Baime.

## Common Errors

### 1. Money Over Talent

**The trap:** "We have more money, so we'll win."

**Why it fails:** Ferrari had less money and more passion. Money is necessary but insufficient. You need obsession, talent, and a culture that can handle speed.

> **Case: The $10M Lesson** (Part I): Ford spent $10M+ on the GT40 program. Ferrari's annual racing budget was a fraction of that. Ford had more engineers, more computers, more wind tunnel time. Ferrari had Enzo's obsession and decades of racing DNA. The money didn't win Le Mans in 1964 or 1965. Only when Ford combined money with a racing culture did they prevail.

### 2. Corporate Politics Over Performance

**The trap:** "We need to manage the optics first."

**Why it fails:** The 1966 photo finish was designed for PR — and it cost the fastest driver his victory.

### 3. Micromanaging Genius

Ford tried to control Shelby. They told him what engines to use. They bureaucratized his team. The 1964 and 1965 failures were the result of this approach.

> **Case: Shelby's Secret 289 Engine** (Part III): When Ford's leadership insisted on the 427 engine, Shelby didn't argue publicly. He simply built the smaller 289 version in parallel. When the 427 cars failed, the 289 was ready. Smart insubordination saved the program.

### 4. The "Throw Money at It" Fallacy

Money was necessary. But the culture shift and talent acquisition were equally important.

### 5. Underestimating Your Rival

Enzo Ferrari believed Ford would never beat him. They did — four years in a row.
