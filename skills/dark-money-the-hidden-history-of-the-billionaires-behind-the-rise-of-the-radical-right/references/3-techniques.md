# Techniques: Dark Money's Infrastructure

> Source: Dark Money, Chapters 6, 10-12

## The Ground Game

The Koch network built real grassroots operations — not just TV ads. Paid canvassers, phone banks, door-knocking, and sophisticated voter data analytics.

> **Case: The Ohio Operation** (Chapter 6): Americans for Prosperity sent teams to swing neighborhoods, organizing under the guise of "neighborhood improvement." Their actual goal: identify conservative voters and turn them out on election day.

> **Case: The Voter Database** (Chapter 6): The network invested heavily in independent voter data — an identification system that the official Republican Party lacked. This gave them independent targeting power.

> **Key takeaway**: Dark money is not just TV ads. It's a full-spectrum political infrastructure.

## The Fossil Fuel Connection

Koch Industries is a giant oil and gas company. Much of the political spending aimed to block climate regulation.

> **Case: Climate Denial Funding** (Chapter 8): Koch-funded think tanks produced studies questioning climate science. Politicians cited these studies. The funding source was invisible to the public.

## The 2010 Midterms

The first major test of post-Citizens United dark money. Republicans won the House decisively.

> **Case: The Shellacking** (Chapter 10): Mayer documents the 2010 election as a turning point. The dark money machine proved it could shift national elections.

> **Key takeaway**: Climate denial was not accidental — a manufactured political strategy funded by fossil fuel money.
