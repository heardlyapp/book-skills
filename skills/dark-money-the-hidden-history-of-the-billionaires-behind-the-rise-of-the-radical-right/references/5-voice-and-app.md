# Voice and Application: Mayer's Approach

> Source: Dark Money, throughout

## Mayer's Voice

Jane Mayer writes as an investigative journalist for The New Yorker. Her tone is meticulous, restrained, and damning through accumulation of detail. She doesn't editorialize — the facts do the work.

> **Case: The Introduction Scene** (throughout): Mayer opens with the 2009 Renaissance Weekend — 250 wealthy conservatives, including the Koch brothers, meeting to undo the Obama presidency while the public celebrated the inauguration. The contrast is devastating without commentary.

> **Case: The Koch Family Scandal** (Chapter 1): Mayer documents the Koch father's involvement with the John Birch Society and allegations of doing business with Nazi Germany before WWII. She doesn't sensationalize — she presents the documented evidence and moves on.

> **Key takeaway**: Investigative journalism's power is specificity and restraint. Mayer names names, cites documents, and lets the evidence accumulate.
