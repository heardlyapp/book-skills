# Principles: The Koch Machine and Citizens United

> Source: Dark Money, Chapters 4-9

## The Kochtopus

The Koch network's infrastructure: Americans for Prosperity, the Cato Institute, Heritage Foundation, FreedomWorks, and dozens of smaller groups. They coordinate strategy, share donors, and amplify each other.

> **Case: The Donor Network** (Chapter 5): By 2010, over 200 donors were contributing at least $100,000 each to the Koch network. Their annual retreats at luxury resorts functioned as a private political convention — vetting candidates, setting strategy, pooling resources.

> **Case: The Cato Institute** (Chapter 5): The Kochs were instrumental in founding the libertarian think tank. It provided intellectual cover for their political agenda, producing papers that made radical ideas seem mainstream.

> **Key takeaway**: The Koch network operates as a private political party with its own platform, candidates, and media.

## Citizens United

The 2010 Supreme Court ruling enabling unlimited corporate political spending was the result of a decades-long legal strategy.

> **Case: The 501(c)(4) Loophole** (Chapter 9): After Citizens United, donors could give unlimited money to nonprofits that don't disclose their funders. Crossroads GPS and Americans for Prosperity became the primary dark money vehicles.

## Tea Time

The Tea Party was not spontaneous. Koch groups provided the infrastructure.

> **Key takeaway**: The Tea Party was grassroots in energy but billionaire-funded in infrastructure and organization.
