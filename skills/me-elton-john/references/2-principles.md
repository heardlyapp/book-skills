# 2 — Key Principles

> Source: *Me* by Elton John.

## The 7 Principles

### 1. Protect Your Creative Partnership

Elton and Bernie's 50+ year partnership is unique in music history. The secret: a complete separation of duties. Bernie writes lyrics. Elton writes music. They never interfere. They trust each other. Protect your partnership by respecting each other's domain.

> **Case: The "Song For Guy" Dispute (Chapter 10):** Elton wrote "Song For Guy" without Bernie — an instrumental piece about a motorcycle messenger who died. The label refused to release it. Bernie backed Elton. The loyalty went both ways. When one partner steps outside the usual territory, the other supports — without controlling.

### 2. Addiction Will Destroy Everything

Cocaine nearly killed Elton. It cost him relationships, dignity, and nearly his life. He could not remember making some of his most famous albums. He was a danger to himself and everyone around him. Addiction is not a lifestyle choice. It is a monster.

### 3. You Cannot Beat Addiction Alone

Elton tried to control his addiction for years. He failed. Rehab, AA, and David Furnish's love saved him. The myth of "going it alone" is fatal.

### 4. Authenticity Is a Lifelong Process

Elton came out as bisexual in 1976. He later clarified he was gay. He lived through eras where being gay was illegal, then dangerous (AIDS crisis), then accepted. He did not "arrive" at authenticity — he lived through it.

### 5. Use Your Platform for Purpose

> **Case: The AIDS Foundation (Chapters 19-20):** Elton founded the Elton John AIDS Foundation in 1992. He had lost friends — Freddie Mercury, Ryan White, many others. He turned his grief into action. The foundation has raised over $600 million. He personally visits projects, speaks out against stigma, and funds prevention and treatment. His fame became a tool for saving lives.

### 6. Love Is Possible After Chaos

Elton had catastrophic relationships before David. He was a nightmare partner. But David saw who he could become — not just who he was. Love is not about finding the perfect person. It is about finding someone who grows with you.

### 7. The Work Is the Constant

Through everything — addiction, recovery, loss, love — Elton never stopped making music. The albums kept coming. The tours kept going. The work was the one thing that never failed him.
