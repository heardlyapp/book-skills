# 4 — Anti-Patterns

> Source: *Me* by Elton John.

## Common Errors

### 1. Believing Success Will Fix Your Internal Problems

**The trap:** "If I get famous enough/rich enough/successful enough, I will be happy."

**Why it fails:** Elton was the biggest pop star in the world — and he was a drug-addicted, bulimic, lonely mess. Fame amplified his problems. It did not solve them. External success does not cure internal chaos.

> **Case: The Cocaine-Fueled Rages (Chapter 11):** Elton would have violent tantrums. He threw things at people. He fired his entire staff mid-tour, then sheepishly rehired them. He would wake up in a hotel room with strangers and no memory of how they got there. "I was a monster. I was miserable. And I had everything anyone could possibly want."
> **Key takeaway**: If you cannot remember your own life, success is meaningless. Fame without health is a prison.

> **Case: The Princess Alexandra Encounter (Chapter 14):** After a show, Princess Alexandra (the Queen's cousin) came backstage and asked Elton: "How do you have so much energy onstage? Do you take a lot of cocaine?" Elton was mortified. Her question made him realize how obvious his addiction had become — even to the royal family. "Jesus, did she know? Had news of my gargantuan appetite for coke actually reached Buckingham Palace?"
> **Key takeaway**: Everyone knows. The secret you think you are keeping is not a secret. Your addiction is visible to everyone but you.

**The trap:** "I can cut back. I can manage it. I can stop anytime I want."

**Why it fails:** Elton tried to control his addiction for years. He could not. He needed rehab, AA, and David Furnish's refusal to enable him. Addiction is not a willpower problem. It is a disease.

### 3. Confusing Sex with Love

**The trap:** "If someone wants to sleep with me, they must care about me."

**Why it fails:** Elton had countless one-night stands. He was lonely. Sex without connection is a temporary fix. David Furnish's refusal to sleep with him on the first date was the signal that this was different.

### 4. Hiding Who You Are

**The trap:** "If people knew the real me, they would reject me."

**Why it fails:** Elton lived in fear of being outed. He came out as bisexual (then gay) and discovered that most people did not care. The fear of rejection was worse than the rejection itself.

### 5. Letting Grief Fester Without Action

**The trap:** "My friends are dying and there is nothing I can do."

**Why it fails:** Elton lost Freddie Mercury, Ryan White, and many others to AIDS. He could have stayed in grief. Instead, he founded the AIDS Foundation. Grief turned into action became purpose.
