# Key Principles — Outgrowing God

## I. You're Already an Atheist About Most Gods

"You don't believe in Zeus, Thor, Poseidon, or any of the thousands of gods humans have worshipped." You're already an atheist about all except possibly one. Dawkins: "I just go one god further." Case: "So many gods to not believe in" — the list of disbelieved gods is longer than believed ones.

## II. Evidence Trumps Faith

"Evidence is what matters." Faith is "belief without evidence — or despite evidence." Case: In every other area of life, we demand evidence before believing. Why is religion the exception?

## III. The Bible Is Not a Reliable Source

"If you were on a jury, would you convict someone based on the Bible as evidence?" Contradictions, historical errors, moral atrocities endorsed. Case: The story of Noah — a global flood is physically impossible. The Bible is not history.

## IV. Darwin Solved the Design Problem

"Living things look designed. That's the most powerful argument for God. Darwin refuted it." Evolution by natural selection explains apparent design without a designer. Case: The eye evolved independently 40+ times. Natural selection works in tiny steps.

## V. Morality Does Not Require God

"Do we need God in order to be good?" No. "We can decide what is good based on empathy, reason, and the desire to reduce suffering." Case: Secular countries are among the most peaceful and generous. Religious belief does not correlate with moral behavior.

## VI. Religion Is a Byproduct

"Did we evolve to be religious?" Religion is not itself an adaptation — it's a byproduct of other useful traits: agency detection, obedience to authority, pattern seeking. Case: "A twig that looks like a snake — better to mistake a twig for a snake than a snake for a twig."

## VII. Science Gives Us Courage

"Science gives us a different kind of courage — the courage to face reality as it is." We don't need comforting myths. We can live with uncertainty and wonder. Case: "The magic of reality is more wonderful than any fairy tale."
