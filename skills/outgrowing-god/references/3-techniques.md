# Techniques — Outgrowing God

## How to Think Critically About Religion (Based on Richard Dawkins)

### Technique 1: The "So Many Gods" Question
When someone says they believe in God, ask: "Which god? Zeus? Thor? Yahweh?" Then: "What's the difference between the reasons you don't believe in Zeus and the reasons I don't believe in Yahweh?" Case: "I don't believe in Jupiter or Poseidon or Thor... And I don't believe in Yahweh."

### Technique 2: The Jury Test
Apply the standard of evidence you'd use in court. "If you were on a jury, would you convict someone based on the Bible as evidence?" Case: The Bible contains contradictions, historical errors, and describes impossible events. Why accept it as truth?

### Technique 3: The Mount Improbable Climb
"Evolution works in tiny steps." When something looks too complex to have evolved (the eye, the bombardier beetle), imagine it as a mountain: you can't jump to the top, but you can walk up a gentle slope. Case: The eye evolved 40+ times independently. Each intermediate stage was an improvement.

### Technique 4: The Designer Question
If life needs a designer, who designed the designer? "If God designed the universe, who designed God?" Case: "The argument from design doesn't prove God — it pushes the problem back one step."

### Technique 5: The Moral Without God Test
"Do we need God to be good?" Case: Ask yourself: if you didn't believe in God, would you suddenly want to murder and steal? If not, you already have an internal moral compass.

### Technique 6: The Agency Detection Check
"When you hear a noise in the dark, your brain assumes it's an agent — a person, an animal." This is a survival instinct. Religion hijacks it. Case: "Better to mistake a twig for a snake than a snake for a twig."

### Technique 7: The Courage to Face Reality
"Science gives us the courage to face reality as it is, not as we wish it were." Case: Dawkins contrasts the "comfort" of religion with the "wonder" of science. Which is more valuable — a comforting lie or an uncomfortable truth?
