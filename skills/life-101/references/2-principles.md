# Key Principles — Life 101

## I. Life Is for Doing, Learning, and Enjoying

"The purpose of life is doing, learning, and enjoying." McWilliams' three-part framework: we do things, we learn from them, and we should enjoy both processes. "If you're not enjoying it, you're doing it wrong." Case: the two brothers shoveling manure — one saw only work, the other saw the possibility of a pony.

## II. You Always Have a Choice

"You always have a choice. No matter what the circumstances, you can choose how you respond." This is the most powerful principle in the book. Between stimulus and response is a space — that space is freedom. Case: two people in the same situation can have completely different experiences based on their chosen response.

## III. Forgiveness Is for You, Not Them

"Forgiveness is not forgetting. Forgiveness is not condoning. Forgiveness is simply releasing the past — for your own sake." Holding onto resentment is "drinking poison and expecting the other person to die." Case: McWilliams argues that the person who can't forgive is the one who suffers most — the resentful party carries the weight long after the original offense.

## IV. Life Is a Metaphor

"Life is like a game of whist. From unseen sources the cards are shuffled, and the hands are dealt" (Eugene Hare). "Life consists not in holding good cards but in playing those you hold well" (Josh Billings). The metaphors we choose shape how we experience life. "Life is either a daring adventure, or nothing" (Helen Keller).

## V. The Smorgasbord Approach

"Take what you can use and leave the rest." McWilliams presents hundreds of techniques and suggestions. "If it works for you, fine — use it. If it doesn't work, let it go and try other things." Case: "The carrot-raisin salad you pass up may be the very thing another person craves."

## VI. The Present Is All You Have

"The past is a canceled check. The future is a promissory note. The present is the only cash you have — spend it wisely." Worrying about yesterday or tomorrow steals from today. "Life is available only in the present moment."

## VII. Love Multiplies When Given

"The more love you give, the more you have." Love is not a finite resource. It grows through expression. Giving is not loss — it's flow. "For it is in giving that we receive" (St. Francis, paraphrased).
