# Core Framework — Life 101

## Introduction: The Smorgasbord

McWilliams calls this book "a smorgasbord" — a buffet of ideas. "If you take from this book ten percent — any ten percent — and use it as your own, I'll consider my job well done." The book contains everything he wished he'd learned in school but didn't. "We know how to figure the square root of an isosceles triangle, but we might not know how to forgive ourselves and others."

## Life Is for Doing, Learning, and Enjoying

McWilliams' core framework: life has three purposes — doing, learning, and enjoying. "Doing allows for more learning. Learning allows for more doing. And in all this doing and learning, let's not forget enjoyment."

**Doing:** "Things won are done; joy's soul lies in the doing" (Shakespeare). Human beings are designed for action. "The shortest answer is doing" (Lord Herbert).

**Learning:** "Wear your learning, like your watch, in a private pocket" (Earl of Chesterfield). Learning doesn't end with formal education. "Commencement does not just mean graduation; it means a new beginning."

**Enjoying:** "Life is either a daring adventure, or nothing" (Helen Keller). The manure-and-pony story: two brothers shoveling manure. One complained; the other laughed. "With all this manure, there must be a pony in here somewhere!"

## Life Is a Metaphor

Many metaphors for life — game, machine, play, music, cabaret, food. "Life is like a game of whist. From unseen sources the cards are shuffled, and the hands are dealt" (Eugene Hare). "Life consists not in holding good cards but in playing those you hold well" (Josh Billings).

## Forgiveness

McWilliams devotes significant space to forgiveness. "Forgiveness is not forgetting. Forgiveness is not condoning. Forgiveness is simply releasing the past — for your own sake." Holding onto resentment is like drinking poison and expecting the other person to die. "Forgiveness is not something you do for the other person. It's something you do for yourself."

## The Power of Choice

"You always have a choice. No matter what the circumstances, you can choose how you respond." Between stimulus and response is a space — and in that space is your freedom. Case: the manure-shoveling story — same situation, two different responses.

## Follow Your Heart

"Your heart knows what your mind can't yet comprehend." McWilliams encourages trusting intuition over pure logic. The highest wisdom is not in books — it's in your own heart. "The real teacher of Life 101 is you."

## Living in the Present

"The past is a canceled check. The future is a promissory note. The present is the only cash you have — spend it wisely." Worrying about tomorrow is a waste of today.

## Giving

The book ends with the importance of giving. "The more love you give, the more you have." Giving is not about loss — it's about flow. Love, kindness, and joy multiply when shared.
