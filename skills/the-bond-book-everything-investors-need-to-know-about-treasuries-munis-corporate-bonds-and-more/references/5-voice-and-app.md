# Voice and Application: Thau's Approach

> Source: The Bond Book, Chapters 14-16

## Thau's Voice

Annette Thau writes as an educator for individual investors. Her tone is patient, thorough, and cautious. She emphasizes understanding before investing and warns repeatedly against complexity.

## Application: Building a Bond Ladder

Buy bonds with staggered maturities. Each rung of the ladder matures in sequence, providing liquidity and capturing current interest rates.

> **Case: The Five-Rung Ladder** (Chapter 16): An investor buys bonds maturing in 1, 2, 3, 4, and 5 years. When the 1-year bond matures, proceeds are reinvested in a new 5-year bond. The portfolio always has bonds maturing soon, providing liquidity while maintaining an average maturity of 3 years.

> **Key takeaway**: Ladders are the simplest and most effective strategy for individual investors. They manage reinvestment risk automatically.

## Application: Individual Bonds vs. Funds

Individual bonds: predictable income, known maturity, no management fees. Funds: diversification, liquidity, professional management, but no maturity guarantee.

> **Case: The Bond Fund Investor** (Chapter 12): An investor buys a bond fund thinking it's "just like bonds." When interest rates rise, the fund NAV drops — and unlike individual bonds, a fund never matures at par. The investor has no guarantee of principal return.

## Application: International and Emerging Market Bonds

Higher yields but currency risk, political risk, and less regulation.

> **Key takeaway**: Know your purpose. Individual bonds for planned income. Funds for flexibility.
