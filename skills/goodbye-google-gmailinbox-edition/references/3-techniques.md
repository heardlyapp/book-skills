# 3. Techniques — The Migration Toolkit

> Source: "Goodbye Google" by John Thomas (2017). Techniques drawn from Part 2 (the step-by-step guide) and Part 3 (resources).

## Migration Step 3: The Master List Method

The most critical technique in the book is creating a complete list of everywhere you use your Gmail address. The author provides a master checklist covering:

- Banking and financial services
- Government services and tax authorities
- Social media accounts
- Online shopping and subscriptions
- Newsletters and mailing lists
- Utility and service providers
- Travel and hospitality accounts
- Healthcare providers
- Professional memberships
- Every other account you've ever created with Gmail

**How to build your list:**
1. Search your Gmail inbox for words like "welcome," "confirm," "verify," "receipt," "registration"
2. Check your browser's saved passwords for accounts using Gmail
3. Review Google's "Password Manager" for linked services
4. Go through your financial statements for automatic payments linked to Gmail

## Step 4: The Parallel Running Technique

Before deleting anything, run both accounts simultaneously for a minimum of 2 weeks (the book recommends 4 weeks). Set up forwarding from Gmail to the new address, but keep Gmail active and checking for stragglers.

**The 2-week overlap strategy:**
- During Week 1: Forward email, update critical accounts (banking, work, government)
- During Week 2: Monitor for missed services, update secondary accounts
- After Week 4: Review for loose ends before considering deletion

> **Case: The Airbnb/Expedia Revelation** (Part 1): The author describes how he discovered Google was reading his email by noticing Airbnb and Expedia data appearing in Google Maps. This experience motivated his systematic approach: if he hadn't noticed this tracking, he wouldn't have known how deeply Google was integrated into his digital life.
> **Key takeaway**: Most people don't know how deep their Google dependence goes until they start the migration process. The master list technique reveals dependencies you didn't know existed.

## Step 6: The Going Nuclear Protocol

Only when you're confident all critical services have been migrated should you delete your Gmail account. The process:

1. Download your data using Google Takeout
2. Verify email forwarding is working
3. Check that all critical services have been updated
4. Go to your Google account settings → "Delete your account"
5. Confirm the deletion (Google will send a verification email — to your Gmail, which will be deleted — so coordinate timing)

> **Case: The "Residual Copies" Gotcha** (Part 1): The book highlights Google's Privacy Policy statement: "after you delete information from our services, we may not immediately delete residual copies from our active servers." This means deletion is not instantaneous or complete. Users should understand that "delete" means "remove from active view, with residual copies persisting for an unknown period."
> **Key takeaway**: Even after deletion, your data may persist in Google's backup systems. Delete with full understanding of what "delete" actually means.
