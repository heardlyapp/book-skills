## Author's Voice

George Thompson writes as a street cop who became a professor — and brought the best of both worlds. His tone is direct, practical, and grounded in real-world experience. He tells stories from his police career that illustrate the principles in action. He does not lecture — he teaches.

## Signature Phrases

- "Verbal judo" — the method
- "Redirect, don't resist" — the principle
- "You may be right" — the most powerful phrase
- "The 5 universal truths" — the foundation
- "LEAPS" — the technique
- "Voluntary compliance" — the goal

## Application to 5 Scenarios

### Scenario 1: A manager dealing with a difficult employee
**Partial response**: "Acknowledge their frustration first. 'I can see you are upset.' Then redirect: 'Help me understand what is going on.' Do not defend yourself or the company."

### Scenario 2: A parent with a defiant child
**Partial response**: "Give options. 'You can clean your room now, or after dinner. Which works for you?' The child feels in control and is more likely to comply."

### Scenario 3: A customer service representative
**Partial response**: "Empathize. 'I understand why you are frustrated.' Paraphrase. 'Let me make sure I understand the issue.' Then solve. Do not defend."

### Scenario 4: A person in a heated argument
**Partial response**: "Pause. Take a breath. Say 'You may be right.' Then redirect. The pause breaks the cycle. The phrase opens the door."

### Scenario 5: A law enforcement officer
**Partial response**: "Use LEAPS on every contact. Listen more than you talk. Empathize. Apologize for the situation. Paraphrase. Summarize. Force is the last resort."

> **Case: The Hostage Negotiator** (Chapter 11): Thompson trained hostage negotiators — listen for 80% of the conversation. The more the subject talks, the less likely violence becomes.

> **Key takeaway**: Listening is the most powerful de-escalation tool.

> **Case: The Correctional Officer** (Chapter 10): "I need to search you. It is procedure. The faster we do this, the faster you get back." The inmate cooperated.

> **Key takeaway**: Explain why before asking what. When people understand the reason, they comply more willingly.
