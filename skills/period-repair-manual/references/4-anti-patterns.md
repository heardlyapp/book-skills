# 4 — Anti-Patterns

> Source: "Period Repair Manual" by Lara Briden

## Anti-Pattern 1: The Pill as Treatment

**The mistake:** Using birth control pills to "treat" period problems. The pill suppresses ovulation and replaces the natural cycle with a pharmaceutical withdrawal bleed. It doesn't heal anything.

## Anti-Pattern 2: Period Suppression

**The mistake:** Thinking it's normal or desirable to have few or no periods. Continuous birth control suppresses menstruation entirely. This is sometimes medically necessary but should not be the default.

## Anti-Pattern 3: Ignoring Root Causes

**The mistake:** Taking a medication for every symptom without asking WHY the symptom is happening.

> **Case: The Pill Cover-Up** (Chapter 3): Briden explains that many women are put on the pill as teenagers for "irregular periods." The pill gives them a "regular" withdrawal bleed — but the underlying condition (PCOS, thyroid, stress) is not addressed. When they come off the pill years later, the original problem returns, often worse.
> **Key takeaway**: The pill does not train your cycle to be regular. It replaces it. If you're on the pill for period problems, the underlying issue is still there.

> **Case: The "Normal" Heavy Period** (Chapter 2): Briden describes how heavy bleeding is often dismissed as "just how it is" for some women. "I've had patients who thought it was normal to go through a super-plus tampon every hour for three days. That's not normal. That's a sign of something wrong." Heavy periods can often be treated naturally.
> **Key takeaway**: Painful or heavy periods are not normal. They are a sign that something needs attention. Don't accept "that's just how periods are."
