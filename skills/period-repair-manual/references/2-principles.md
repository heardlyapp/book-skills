# 2 — Period Problems

> Source: "Period Repair Manual" by Lara Briden

## Common Conditions

- **PCOS**: The most common hormonal disorder. Driven by insulin resistance. Symptoms: irregular periods, acne, hair growth, weight gain.
- **Endometriosis**: Uterine-like tissue growing outside the uterus. Driven by estrogen and inflammation.
- **PMS/PMDD**: Mood and physical symptoms in the luteal phase. Linked to progesterone sensitivity.
- **Heavy Periods**: Can be caused by estrogen dominance, fibroids, or thyroid issues.
- **Amenorrhea**: Missing periods. Often due to low body weight, stress, or PCOS.

> **Case: PCOS Is Not a Mystery** (Chapter 4): Briden argues that PCOS is fundamentally a metabolic disorder — insulin resistance drives the ovaries to produce excess testosterone. The solution is not more medication but treating the insulin resistance through diet, exercise, and supplements like inositol.
> **Key takeaway**: PCOS is not a random condition. It has a clear mechanism. Address the mechanism (insulin resistance) and most symptoms improve.

> **Case: The Endometriosis Puzzle** (Chapter 5): Briden describes endometriosis as an inflammatory condition driven by estrogen dominance. Conventional treatment (the pill, surgery) treats symptoms. Her approach: reduce inflammatory load, support liver detoxification of estrogen, and use targeted supplements.
> **Key takeaway**: Endometriosis requires a multi-pronged approach: anti-inflammatory diet, estrogen metabolism support, and immune system regulation.
