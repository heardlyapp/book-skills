# 3 — Natural Treatments

> Source: "Period Repair Manual" by Lara Briden

## The Foundation

1. **Blood sugar balance**: Protein at breakfast. No sugar spikes. Every meal should have protein + fat + fiber.
2. **Anti-inflammatory diet**: Remove sugar, seed oils, processed food. Add omega-3s, vegetables, whole foods.
3. **Sleep**: 7-9 hours. Cortisol disrupts ovulation. Sleep is when hormones repair.
4. **Movement**: Not excessive cardio. Strength training and walking are best for hormone balance.

## Key Supplements

- **Magnesium**: For cramps, sleep, anxiety, PMS. Magnesium glycinate is best.
- **Vitex (Chasteberry)**: Increases progesterone. Helps PMS, irregular cycles, luteal phase defect.
- **Inositol**: For PCOS. Improves insulin sensitivity, ovulation, and egg quality.
- **Zinc**: For acne, hair health, ovulation support.
- **Vitamin D**: Essential for all hormone function. Most women are deficient.
- **B6**: Helps progesterone production. Reduces PMS.

> **Case: Magnesium for Cramps** (Chapter 7): Briden recommends magnesium for period pain. "Magnesium relaxes smooth muscle. It reduces prostaglandins. It helps you sleep. It's the single most useful supplement for menstrual health."
> **Key takeaway**: Start with magnesium. It's safe, cheap, and effective for most period issues.

> **Case: Inositol for PCOS** (Chapter 8): Briden describes inositol as one of the most researched supplements for PCOS. "Inositol improves insulin sensitivity, reduces testosterone, and restores ovulation. It works as well as metformin for many women, with fewer side effects."
> **Key takeaway**: For PCOS, inositol is often a better first-line treatment than metformin. It addresses the root cause (insulin resistance) without the side effects.
