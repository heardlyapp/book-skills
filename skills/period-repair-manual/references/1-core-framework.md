# 1 — The Menstrual Cycle Framework

> Source: "Period Repair Manual" by Lara Briden

## The Four Phases

### Menstrual Phase (Days 1-5)
The uterine lining sheds. Estrogen and progesterone are low. This is the reset.

### Follicular Phase (Days 6-14)
Estrogen rises, building the uterine lining. An egg matures in the ovary. Energy increases.

### Ovulation (Day 14-ish)
The egg is released. Estrogen peaks. A brief window of fertility. Cervical mucus becomes clear and stretchy.

### Luteal Phase (Days 15-28)
Progesterone rises, thickening the lining. If no pregnancy, progesterone falls and menstruation begins.

> **Case: The Standard Cycle** (Chapter 2): Briden describes the "textbook" 28-day cycle and explains why many women don't have it. "Most women are not textbook. Cycles between 21 and 35 days are normal. What matters more than length is whether you are ovulating."
> **Key takeaway**: There is a wide range of normal. Focus on whether you're ovulating rather than whether you're exactly 28 days.

## Key Hormones

- **Estrogen**: Builds the uterine lining. Too much = estrogen dominance. Too little = thin lining, dryness.
- **Progesterone**: Stabilizes the lining. Needed for implantation. Too little = PMS, heavy bleeding.
- **Testosterone**: Supports libido and energy. Also important for women.
- **FSH/LH**: Pituitary hormones that control the cycle. FSH rises in perimenopause.
