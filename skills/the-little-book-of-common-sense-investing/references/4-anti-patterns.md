# Anti-Patterns — The Little Book of Common Sense Investing

## The Central Error: Believing You Can Beat the Market

"Mutual fund investors are confident that they can consistently select superior fund managers. They are wrong." The evidence: only 2.5% of funds survived and outperformed over 46 years. Not 25%. 2.5%.

### Mistake 1: "Past performance predicts future returns"
Chasing last year's hot fund is the single biggest destroyer of wealth. Bogle: "Investors who choose funds based on recent performance have been consistently wrong." Reversion to the mean ensures that what goes up must come down. The Fidelity Magellan example: 16% fund return → 7% investor return because investors bought after the run and sold after the drop.

### Mistake 2: "Active management is worth the fees"
Active funds charge 1-2% for the privilege of underperforming. Over 15 years, only 16% beat the index. Wall Street takes $565 billion per year from investors. "When trillions of dollars are managed by Wall Streeters charging high fees, it will usually be the managers who reap outsize profits, not the clients." — Warren Buffett.

### Mistake 3: "I can pick the next Peter Lynch"
Lynch himself advised: "Most investors are better off buying an index fund." The few winners are impossible to identify in advance. "If you can't pick winning stocks consistently, and you can't pick winning fund managers consistently, what do you do? You buy an index fund."

### Mistake 4: "I'll time the market"
The timing penalty: investors who try to exit before drops and enter before rallies consistently underperform buy-and-hold. "Time in the market beats timing the market."

### Mistake 5: "I'll invest in ETFs for flexibility"
ETFs charge slightly lower fees but encourage overtrading. The average ETF investor earns less than the average index fund investor because they trade in and out. Bogle: "ETFs are an open invitation to market timing."

### Mistake 6: "I need a financial adviser"
"Investment consultants and advisers don't add value — they add costs." The median adviser-chosen portfolio underperforms the market by about 3% per year (fees + behavioral mistakes).

### Mistake 7: "My situation is special"
Bogle: "Simple arithmetic controls the game. There is no special situation that exempts you from the rules of humble arithmetic." The same laws of compounding and costs that apply to everyone apply to you.
