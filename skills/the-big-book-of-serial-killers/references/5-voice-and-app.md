# 5 — Voice & App

> Source: *The Big Book of Serial Killers* by Jack Rosewood & Rebecca Lo.

## Voice

The book is clinical and encyclopedic. Each entry follows the same structure: Background → Murders → Arrest and Trial → Outcome → Trivia. The tone is factual, not sensationalized.

> **Example: David Berkowitz Profile** — Background (adoption, birth circumstances, army service) → Murders (list of 6 shootings with dates) → Arrest (parking ticket witness) → Outcome (multiple life sentences) → Trivia (quotes, the "Sam" dog story).

> **Example: Jeffrey Dahmer Profile** — Background (unusually quiet child, parents' divorce) → Murders (17 young men, methods) → Arrest (victim escaped and led police to Dahmer) → Outcome (15 life sentences, killed in prison).

## 5 Scenarios

1. **Understanding a serial killer case** — Use the organized/disorganized typology
2. **Learning investigative history** — Study the capture stories
3. **Academic research** — Use the 150 profiles as a reference
4. **Situational awareness** — The victimology patterns are informative, not alarming
5. **True crime interest** — The trivia sections provide unique details
