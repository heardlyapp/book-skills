# 1 — Core Framework

> Source: *The Big Book of Serial Killers* by Jack Rosewood & Rebecca Lo.

## The Two Frameworks

### Framework A: The Organized vs. Disorganized Typology

The FBI's foundational division. Organized killers plan meticulously, select specific victim types, bring weapons, exert control, and conceal bodies. Disorganized killers are impulsive, use available weapons, kill in familiar areas, and leave bodies in view.

> **Example: Ted Bundy (Organized)** — Bundy lured victims, drove them to remote locations, and often returned to the bodies. He was charming, articulate, and planned every detail.

> **Example: Richard Chase (Disorganized)** — "The Vampire of Sacramento" drank victims' blood and left bodies in plain view. His crimes were chaotic and driven by delusion.

### Framework B: The Four Motivations

Visionary (hearing voices/visions), Mission-oriented ("cleansing" society), Hedonistic (pleasure: lust/thrill/comfort), Power/Control (domination).
