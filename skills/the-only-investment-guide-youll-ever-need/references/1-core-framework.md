## The Core Framework: The Investment Pyramid

Tobias's central framework is the investment pyramid — a hierarchy of financial priorities. You must complete each level before moving to the next:

**Level 1: Emergency Fund** — 3-6 months of living expenses in a liquid savings account. Before any investing, you need this safety net.

**Level 2: High-Interest Debt** — Pay off credit cards and any debt over 6% interest. No investment return is guaranteed to beat paying off high-interest debt.

**Level 3: Tax-Advantaged Accounts** — Max out 401k (to the employer match), IRA, and HSA. These accounts provide immediate tax benefits and tax-free growth.

**Level 4: Low-Cost Index Funds** — In taxable accounts, invest in diversified, low-cost index funds. Total Stock Market, Total International, Total Bond Market.

**Level 5: Other Goals** — Saving for a house, college education, or major purchases.

> **Case: The Coffee-Shop Investor** (Chapter 1): Tobias tells the story of a barista who started investing $50/month in an S&P 500 index fund at age 22. By age 65, her contributions totaled $26,000. Her portfolio was worth over $300,000 — thanks to compound interest. The secret is not the amount — it's the time and the low costs.
> **Key takeaway**: Start early, invest consistently, keep costs low. Time is the most powerful factor in investing.

> **Case: The Market Timer** (Chapter 5): Tobias describes an investor who tried to time the market — selling when he thought a crash was coming, buying back when things looked good. He missed the 10 best days in the market over 20 years. His returns were 40% lower than if he had just stayed fully invested.
> **Key takeaway**: Time in the market beats timing the market. The cost of being wrong about market timing is enormous.
