## 6 Common Financial Mistakes

### 1. Trying to Time the Market

"If you miss the 10 best days in the market over 30 years, your returns are cut in half. No one can consistently predict which days those will be."

### 2. Paying High Fees

"A 1% fee sounds small. Over 30 years, it consumes 25% of your returns. That's not small — that's devastating."

> **Case: The 2% Fee** (Chapter 3): A $10,000 investment earning 8% annually with no fees grows to $100,000 over 30 years. With 2% annual fees (earning 6% net), it grows to only $57,000. The fees cost nearly half the portfolio.

### 3. Owning Too Many Funds

"Owning 20 different mutual funds does not provide diversification — it provides confusion. You likely own the same stocks multiple times in different funds."

### 4. Buying Whole Life Insurance

"Whole life insurance is a terrible investment disguised as protection. Buy term and invest the difference."

### 5. Ignoring Asset Allocation

"Your allocation between stocks and bonds determines 90% of your investment returns. Which specific funds you own determines the other 10%."

### 6. Checking Your Portfolio Too Often

"The more often you check your portfolio, the more likely you are to make bad decisions. Annual rebalancing is enough."

> **Case: The Frequent Trader** (Chapter 6): Tobias describes an investor who traded frequently — buying and selling stocks based on tips, news, and hunches. After commissions and taxes, his returns were 60% lower than if he had simply bought and held an index fund. The more he traded, the less he made.
> **Key takeaway**: Trading is not investing. The more active you are, the worse you perform on average.
