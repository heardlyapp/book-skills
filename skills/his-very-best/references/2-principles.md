# 2 — Principles

> Source: *His Very Best* by Jonathan Alter.

## 7 Principles

1. **Do your best. Always.** Rickover's question haunted Carter: "Did you do your best at Annapolis?" He confessed he had not. He never let that happen again.

2. **Integrity is a long-term asset.** Carter "did not lie to the American people." That pledge cost him nothing short-term and gave him everything long-term in reputation.

3. **Outsiders can win. Insiders govern.** Carter mastered the first. He struggled with the second. Know which phase you're in.

4. **Failure is not permanent.** The Nobel Peace Prize came 22 years after the electoral humiliation.

5. **Persistence beats charisma.** Camp David was saved 3 times by Carter refusing to let either party leave.

6. **Plant trees you'll never sit under.** Solar panels. Alaska wilderness. The CDC going global. "A leader can lead people where they want to go. A great leader leads people where they ought to go." — Rosalynn Carter.

7. **The small town is never far away.** Carter returned to Plains. He taught Sunday school. He lived in the same house. The ground you came from keeps you grounded.
