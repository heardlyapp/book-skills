# 4 — Anti-Patterns

> Source: *His Very Best* by Jonathan Alter.

## 5 Mistakes

### 1. Governing Like You Campaign

Carter campaigned as an outsider. He governed as one. He never learned to stroke Congress.

### 2. Micromanagement

Carter personally scheduled the White House tennis court. This was the same obsessive detail that made Camp David succeed — deployed on the wrong things.

### 3. The "Three Smiles" Problem

Carter had 3 smiles: the warm one for crowds, the tight-lipped one when angry, and the relaxed private smile. He too often showed the wrong one.

### 4. Moralizing Without Politics

Carter's "malaise" speech was right about America's crisis of spirit. It was politically suicidal to say it from the Oval Office.

### 5. The Outsider's Loneliness

Carter had no friends in Washington. Brzezinski told him "you need allies." He didn't listen. Outsiders need more allies, not fewer.
