# Techniques · Social Investment · Resilience

> Source: *The Happiness Advantage*, Principles 5-7

---

## Principle 5: Social Investment

**The research:** The number one predictor of happiness and resilience is the quality of your social connections. People with strong social support recover faster from setbacks.

**Active Constructive Responding (ACR):** When someone shares good news, respond actively and constructively:
- Passive destructive: "What?" (ignoring)
- Active destructive: "That's going to be a lot of work" (deflating)
- Passive constructive: "That's nice" (minimal)
- Active constructive: "That's amazing! Tell me everything!" (celebrating)

## Principle 6: Coping → Cultivating

**Traditional approach:** Coping = managing negative emotions, reducing stress, surviving.

**The shift:** Cultivating = building the skills and mindset to thrive regardless of circumstances. Not just surviving — growing.

**The upward spiral:** Small successes breed confidence, which fuels more effort, which creates more success. The spiral goes up, not just "back to baseline."

## Principle 7: The 20-Second Rule (Revisited)

Applied to relationships: reduce the activation energy for positive social interactions. Schedule Friday drinks. Set a recurring lunch with a friend. Make social connection easy.
