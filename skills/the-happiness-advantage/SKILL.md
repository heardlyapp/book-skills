## Philosophy (3 rules to remember)

1. **Happiness fuels success, not the other way around.** The brain in positive mode is smarter, more creative, and more productive. The formula "work hard → succeed → be happy" is backward.
2. **Your brain is wired to scan for threats first.** The "Tetris Effect" trains it to scan for patterns of positivity instead — with 21 days of practice.
3. **Small changes compound.** The 20-Second Rule reduces the activation energy for good habits. The Zorro Circle shrinks your focus to manageable chunks.

## Core Framework Quick Ref
- **The Happiness Advantage**: The brain positive → 31% more productive, 37% better sales, 3x more creative
- **The Tetris Effect**: Train your brain to scan for patterns of opportunity rather than patterns of threat
- **The Zorro Circle**: When overwhelmed, shrink your focus to a small circle of control, master it, expand
- **The 20-Second Rule**: Reduce the activation energy for good habits by 20 seconds; increase it for bad ones
- **Social Investment**: The #1 predictor of happiness and resilience is the quality of your social connections

## Key Principles
1. **Happiness is not the result of success — it's the precursor.** Raise your baseline happiness and your success ceiling rises.
2. **Your brain's default is negative.** Counteract the negativity bias through intentional positive practices.
3. **Small wins create momentum.** The Zorro Circle: master a tiny domain first, then expand.
4. **Relationships are the highest predictor of happiness.** Invest in social connections like any other asset.
5. **Change your mindset, change your outcomes.** The way you perceive a challenge determines your ability to overcome it.

## Anti-Patterns
Waiting for success to create happiness / Scanning for threats instead of opportunities / Trying to change everything at once / Ignoring social connections / Refusing to see the positive in adversity. See `references/4-anti-patterns.md`.
