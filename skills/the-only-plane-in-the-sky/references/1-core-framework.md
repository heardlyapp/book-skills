# 1 — The Timeline

> Source: "The Only Plane in the Sky" by Garrett M. Graff

## The Morning

**8:14 AM**: American Airlines Flight 11, en route to LA, is hijacked.
**8:46 AM**: Flight 11 crashes into the North Tower (1 WTC).
**9:03 AM**: United Flight 175 crashes into the South Tower (2 WTC).
**9:37 AM**: American Flight 77 crashes into the Pentagon.
**9:59 AM**: South Tower collapses (56 minutes after impact).
**10:03 AM**: United Flight 93 crashes in Shanksville, PA.
**10:28 AM**: North Tower collapses (102 minutes after impact).

> **Case: The First Reports** (Opening Chapter): "At 8:46, the news was confused. A plane hit the World Trade Center. Small plane? Accident? Then the second plane hit. That's when everyone knew — this was not an accident. This was an attack." The oral history captures the moment of realization.
> **Key takeaway**: There was a moment when the world changed. The first plane could have been an accident. The second plane was an act of war.

> **Case: The Day of Silence** (Chapter on the Aftermath): "After the towers fell, there was silence. Not normal city silence — a deep, profound silence. No sirens. No traffic. Just the sound of ash falling. And people crying." The oral history captures the sensory experience of a city in shock.
> **Key takeaway**: The silence after the collapses was one of the most haunting aspects of 9/11. A city of 8 million people went quiet. The sound of that day is as important as the images.
