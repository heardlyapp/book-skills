# 4 — Flight 93 and the Pentagon

> Source: "The Only Plane in the Sky" by Garrett M. Graff

## Flight 93

United Flight 93 departed Newark at 8:42 AM, delayed 25 minutes. Hijacked at 9:28 AM. Passengers learned about the WTC attacks via phone calls. They voted to fight back.

## The Pentagon

Flight 77 hit the Pentagon at 9:37 AM. 184 people died. The impact was on the side of the building that had just been renovated — it was more structurally sound, saving lives.

> **Case: The Phone Calls** (Chapter on Flight 93): "Todd Beamer called GTE. He said they were going to do something. He prayed the Lord's Prayer with the operator. Then he said: 'Are you guys ready? Let's roll.' Those were his last words."
> **Key takeaway**: The passengers of Flight 93 knew they were going to die. They had time to think. They made calls to loved ones. They chose action over passivity. "Let's roll" became a national rallying cry.

> **Case: The Pentagon Survivors** (Chapter on Pentagon): "I felt the building shake. Then everything went dark. I thought: this is it. But I survived. I walked through the rubble and saw the sky. The building had a hole in it. I couldn't believe I was alive."
> **Key takeaway**: The Pentagon attack was overshadowed by the WTC, but it was equally devastating. 184 people died. The fact that more didn't die was partly luck and partly the result of building improvements.
