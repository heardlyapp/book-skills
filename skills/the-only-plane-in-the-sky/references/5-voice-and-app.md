# 5 — Graff's Voice + Application

> Source: "The Only Plane in the Sky" by Garrett M. Graff

## Graff's Voice

Garrett Graff is a journalist and historian who compiled this oral history from over 500 interviews. His voice is not present in the book — he lets the witnesses speak. The book's power comes from this restraint.

> **Case: The Title** (Preface): "The only plane in the sky was Air Force One. The rest were grounded. American airspace was completely shut down for the first time in history. President Bush was in the air, protected by fighter jets, not knowing if there would be another attack."
> **Key takeaway**: The image of Air Force One as the only plane in the sky captures the surreal nature of that day. Normal life stopped. The world held its breath.

## Key Quote

"On September 12, 2001, the only people 'over' 9/11 were the terrorists. The rest of us were under it. Under the rubble. Under the investigation. Under the war. Under the grief." — Survivor quoted in the book

## 5 Scenarios

1. **Learning about 9/11** → Read the oral history. Nothing else captures the experience as directly.
2. **Teaching the next generation** → Focus on the human stories, not just the timeline. Make it personal.
3. **Remembering** → Visit the 9/11 Memorial in NYC. The names around the pools are 2,977 individuals, not a statistic.
4. **Processing grief** → The book is a testament to human resilience. Read it when you're ready to feel deeply.
5. **Understanding America today** → 9/11 shaped the world we live in. Understanding the day helps understand the present.
