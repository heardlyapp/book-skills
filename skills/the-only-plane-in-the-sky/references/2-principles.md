# 2 — The World Trade Center

> Source: "The Only Plane in the Sky" by Garrett M. Graff

## The Towers

Each tower had 110 floors. Thousands of people were already at work. The impact zones were floors 93-99 (North) and 77-85 (South).

## The Evacuation

Approximately 15,000-20,000 people evacuated. They walked down dark, smoky stairwells. Many carried injured colleagues. Some never made it out.

> **Case: The Stairwell** (Chapter on Evacuation): "We started walking down. People were calm at first. Then the second plane hit. The building shook. We knew this was not an accident. We kept walking down. 60 floors. 70 floors. We didn't know if we would make it."
> **Key takeaway**: The evacuation of the towers was the largest building evacuation in American history. It worked — most people below the impact zones got out. But those above the impact zones had no escape.

> **Case: The Jumpers** (Chapter on the Towers): "People jumped. Not because they wanted to die — because the fire was worse. I saw a woman holding hands with a man as they jumped together." This is one of the most painful parts of the oral history. "We looked away. We couldn't watch. But we couldn't stop hearing the sounds."
> **Key takeaway**: The jumpers are the most haunting image of 9/11. They faced an impossible choice. The oral history gives voice to those who witnessed this tragedy.
