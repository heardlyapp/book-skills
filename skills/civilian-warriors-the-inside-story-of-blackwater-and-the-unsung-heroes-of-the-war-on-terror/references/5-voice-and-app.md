# Voice and Application: Prince's Perspective

> Source: Civilian Warriors, throughout

## Prince's Voice

Erik Prince writes as a patriot, a businessman, and a defender. His tone is direct, defensive when discussing controversies, and proud of what Blackwater accomplished. He frames Blackwater as a solution to a problem the U.S. government created but couldn't solve internally. The book was reviewed by the CIA before publication, resulting in redactions.

## Application: High-Stakes Entrepreneurship

Building a company in a dangerous, politically charged environment requires a different approach. Invest in capability before demand. Hire for character first. Expect blowback — and plan for it.

## Application: Crisis Management

When the crisis hits, speed matters more than perfection. The Bremer ambush was won before it started — because training made the response automatic. The "Tuna" code initiated the extraction sequence immediately, without deliberation.

## Application: Government Contracting

Government contracts are high-value but high-risk. You become a political target. Prince's advice: diversify your client base, build relationships across the political spectrum, and never rely on a single contract for survival.

## Application: Personal Security

For anyone in a high-risk environment: the principles of protective security are universal — situational awareness, route planning, armored vehicles, trained drivers, communication protocols, and the rule: never be predictable.
