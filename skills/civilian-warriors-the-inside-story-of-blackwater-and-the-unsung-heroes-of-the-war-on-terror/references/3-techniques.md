# Techniques: Security Operations and Crisis Response

> Source: Civilian Warriors, Chapters 6-13

## The WPPS Contract

The Worldwide Personal Protective Services contract from the State Department was Blackwater's core revenue. It required providing armed security details for diplomats in Iraq. The contract structure: fixed price for personnel, reimbursable for equipment and travel. This contract made Blackwater indispensable to U.S. operations in Iraq.

> **Case: The Rapid Scale-Up** (Chapter 6): When the Iraq War started, the State Department needed hundreds of security contractors immediately. Blackwater had to hire, vet, and deploy former special operators faster than any normal HR process could handle. Prince's solution: hire for character first, train for skill second. A former SEAL who could be trusted was worth more than a dozen technically proficient unknowns.

> **Case: The Little Birds** (Chapter 7): Blackwater purchased and operated its own helicopter fleet — including MD 530F Little Birds, the same aircraft used by the 160th SOAR. Having organic air support was critical to the Bremer ambush response. The helicopters provided top cover, scanning the roadway for threats before the convoy passed.

## Crisis Response Protocol

When an incident occurs: secure the principal, move to a safe location, call in support, document everything. The Bremer ambush followed this protocol perfectly — the code "Tuna" initiated the emergency extraction sequence immediately.

> **Case: The Fallujah Families** (Chapter 12): After four Blackwater contractors were killed and mutilated in Fallujah, Prince had to manage the aftermath: recovering remains, supporting families, and dealing with the media firestorm. His approach: personal calls to every family, full transparency, no deflection of responsibility.
