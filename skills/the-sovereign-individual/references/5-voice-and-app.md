> Source: The Sovereign Individual, Introduction through Part IV

## Author's Voice

Davidson and Rees-Mogg write as historians and futurists with a clear ideological stance: they are pessimistic about the state and optimistic about the individual. Their tone is analytical but urgent — they believe they have identified the most important trend of our time. The book is dense with historical analogies (the printing press, the fall of Rome, the rise of the Church) and technical predictions (cryptography, microprocessors, the Internet).

## Signature Phrases

- "The sovereign individual" — the new human archetype
- "Megapolitical change" — large-scale historical transformation
- "The collapse of the welfare state" — the predicted outcome
- "The microprocessor does to the state what the printing press did to the Church" — the central analogy
- "Geographic arbitrage" — strategic location choice
- "The exit option" — the power to leave

## Application to 5 Scenarios

### Scenario 1: A professional concerned about the future
**Partial response**: "Develop portable skills. Reduce dependence on any single employer or jurisdiction. Think globally. The sovereign individual is not a prediction — it is a necessity."

### Scenario 2: An entrepreneur building in the digital economy
**Partial response**: "The information revolution is creating opportunities that the industrial age could not imagine. The sovereign individual builds businesses that operate outside the state's grip — remote teams, crypto payments, global customers."

### Scenario 3: A citizen frustrated with government
**Partial response**: "Your frustration is structural — the state is designed for an era that is ending. Instead of fighting to reform it, build your own sovereignty. Exit is more powerful than voice."

### Scenario 4: A young person planning their career
**Partial response**: "Do not plan for lifetime employment. Plan for lifetime employability. Your skills are your assets. Your network is your safety net. Your location is a strategic choice."

### Scenario 5: A crypto enthusiast
**Partial response**: "The Sovereign Individual predicted crypto before crypto existed. The book explains WHY crypto is important — not just as money but as a tool of sovereignty. You are building what the authors described."

> **Case: The Hong Kong Model** (Chapter 11): The authors point to Hong Kong as a model — a low-tax jurisdiction with independent judiciary and free trade. The sovereign individual chooses their jurisdiction strategically.

> **Key takeaway**: The right location can multiply your freedom and wealth. Choose where you live as carefully as you choose your career.

> **Case: The Internet Prediction** (Chapter 3): In 1997, the authors predicted the Internet would become the dominant medium for commerce and the state would not be able to control it. They were right.

> **Key takeaway**: The Internet is structurally anti-authoritarian. Every attempt to control it has failed.
