## The Davidson/Rees-Mogg Framework: The Megapolitical Lens

The book's framework: history is driven by "megapolitical" shifts — changes in the fundamental technology of violence and production. These shifts occur every 500-1000 years and transform every aspect of society.

**The Key Insight**: The microprocessor is the most important invention since the printing press. It will trigger a megapolitical shift as profound as the agricultural and industrial revolutions.

> **Case: The Printing Press and the Church** (Chapter 1): Before the printing press, the Catholic Church held a monopoly on information. The press destroyed that monopoly, enabling the Reformation and the rise of the nation-state. The microprocessor will do the same to the nation-state: it breaks the state's monopoly on violence, taxation, and identity verification.
> **Key takeaway**: The printing press example is the template for understanding the microprocessor's impact. Both are information technologies that undermine centralized authority.

> **Case: The Mafia's Business Model** (Chapter 4): The authors compare the nation-state to the Mafia. Both offer protection in exchange for payment (taxes). Both claim a monopoly on force within their territory. The information age makes it possible to exit this arrangement — to find protection and services outside the state's reach.
> **Key takeaway**: The state's power rests on its ability to compel payment. When individuals can exit (crypto, offshore, digital identity), the state's power to compel diminishes.
