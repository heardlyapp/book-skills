## Practical Techniques

### 1. The Sovereignty Audit

Assess your dependence on the state: how much of your income goes to taxes? How portable are your skills? Where would you go if your country became unstable? The sovereign individual minimizes dependence.

### 2. The Geographic Arbitrage

Live where the value is high and costs are low. The information age makes it possible to earn in strong currencies and live in weak ones. The sovereign individual thinks globally.

> **Case: The Digital Nomad Lifestyle** (Chapter 9, prophetic): The authors predicted that the information age would allow people to work remotely and live in low-cost jurisdictions. They described this as "geographic arbitrage" — earning in a strong economy while living in a weak one.
> **Key takeaway**: Geography is no longer destiny. The sovereign individual chooses their location strategically — not by birth but by design.

### 3. The Crypto Strategy

Hold assets outside the traditional financial system. Cryptocurrency, precious metals, and assets that cannot be taxed or seized are the foundation of economic sovereignty.

### 4. The Security Portfolio

The state's monopoly on violence is eroding. The sovereign individual invests in multiple layers of protection: legal, digital, physical, and community-based.

> **Case: The Private Security City** (Chapter 10): The authors predicted the rise of private residential communities with their own security, infrastructure, and governance — essentially, private cities. This trend is visible in gated communities, charter cities, and special economic zones.
> **Key takeaway**: Security is becoming a private good, not a public one. The sovereign individual must take responsibility for their own protection.
