# The Progress Data — How the World Got Better

> Source: Enlightenment Now, Chapters 4-16

## Core Framework

Pinker presents data across 15 dimensions of human well-being. Every one shows dramatic improvement.

### The Progress Dashboard (Selected Key Metrics)

| Metric | Then | Now |
|--------|------|-----|
| Global extreme poverty | 90% (1800) | 10% (2018) |
| Life expectancy | 30 years (1800) | 72 years (2018) |
| Child mortality | 43% (1820) | 3.9% (2018) |
| Literacy | 12% (1820) | 86% (2018) |
| Democracy | 0 (1800) | Over 100 countries |
| War deaths | 20th century: 100M+ | 21st century: declining |
| Hours worked per week | 60+ (1900) | 40 (2000) |
| Homicide rate (Europe) | 40/100k (1500) | 1/100k (2018) |
| Deaths from famine | Common until 20th c. | Almost zero |
| Access to clean water | Rare before 1900 | 88% (2018) |

### The Mechanism of Progress

Pinker argues progress is not automatic. It results from specific institutions and values: markets (innovation), science (knowledge), democracy (accountability), human rights (moral progress), and international cooperation (peace).

> **Case: The Great Escape from Poverty** (Chapter 6)
> In 1800, 90% of the world's population lived in extreme poverty (subsistence level). Today, less than 10% do — despite a 7x increase in population. The "Great Escape" is the single most important event in human history. It was caused by the Industrial Revolution, global trade, scientific medicine, and the spread of Enlightenment values. The consequence: billions of people who would have died in childhood or lived in desperate poverty are alive and thriving.
> **Key takeaway**: The decline of extreme poverty is one of the least appreciated and most important facts about the modern world. It is not a natural trend — it is the direct result of Enlightenment institutions (markets, science, democracy). Abandoning those institutions would reverse the progress.

> **Case: The Decline of War** (Chapter 9, building on Better Angels)
> Pinker documents the long-term decline of all forms of organized violence. War deaths have declined from a baseline of 500/100,000 per year in prehistoric times to less than 1/100,000 today. The post-WWII "long peace" is unprecedented in human history. The causes: the spread of democracy (democracies rarely fight each other), international trade (war is bad for business), the nuclear deterrent (the "long peace" is partially a product of nuclear weapons), and the decline of honor cultures and imperial ambitions.
> **Key takeaway**: The decline of war is perhaps the most important political fact of the last 70 years. It receives almost no attention because peace is not news. The data show that the world has become dramatically more peaceful — and that this is the result of specific decisions and institutions, not an accident.
