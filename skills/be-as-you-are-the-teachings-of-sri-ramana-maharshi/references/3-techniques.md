# Practical Instructions — Techniques

## Technique 1: "Who Am I?"

The core practice. When a thought arises, ask "To whom does this thought arise?" The answer is "To me." Then ask "Who am I?"

**How to do it:** Do not try to answer intellectually. Turn attention inward. Follow the "I" thought back to its source. When the mind reaches the source, it dissolves. What remains is the Self.

**Case: The Persistent Seeker.** A seeker asks: "I have been doing this for years. Nothing happens." Ramana: "Who asks this question? Ask 'To whom does this frustration arise?'"

**Case: The Three States.** Ramana uses the analogy of the waking, dream, and deep sleep states. In deep sleep, there is no "I" — but you exist. The Self is that existence without the sense of being a separate person.

## Technique 2: Attention to the Heart

Ramana locates the Self in the spiritual heart, on the right side of the chest.

**How to do it:** Focus attention on the right side of the chest, the spiritual heart center. When thoughts arise, do not follow them. Rest your attention in the heart.

**Case: The Two Hearts.** Ramana distinguishes between the physical heart (left side, a pump) and the spiritual heart (right side, the seat of consciousness).

## Technique 3: Watching the Breath

For those who cannot practice Self-enquiry directly, watching the breath can calm the mind.

**How to do it:** Simply observe the breath without controlling it. As the breath becomes quiet, the mind becomes quiet.

**Case: The Restless Seeker.** A seeker who could not sit still was advised to watch the breath. After some time, the mind settled, and he was able to practice Self-enquiry.
