# The Path of Self-Enquiry — Core Framework

## The Central Teaching

Ramana's entire teaching can be summarized: you are not the body, not the mind, not the personality. You are the Self — pure consciousness. The practice is to realize this directly.

Ramana's teaching is organized around a single question: "Who am I?" Everything else — meditation, surrender, devotion — is preparation for or supplement to this practice.

## The Four Steps of Self-Enquiry

**Step 1: Recognize the "I" Thought.** The sense of being a separate person — the "I" thought — is the root of all other thoughts. It is the first thought that arises in the mind.

**Step 2: Trace It to Its Source.** When the "I" thought arises, ask: "Where does this come from?" Follow it back to its origin in the heart.

**Step 3: Dissolve the "I" Thought.** When the "I" thought is traced to its source, it dissolves. The mind subsides.

**Step 4: Abide as the Self.** What remains when the "I" thought has dissolved is the Self — pure consciousness, awareness without an object. Abide in that.

**Case: The Questioner's Experience.** A seeker asks: "I have been practicing Self-enquiry for years. Why have I not realized the Self?" Ramana: "The Self is always realized. The obstacle is the thought that you have not realized it."

**Case: The Persistent "I."** Another seeker: "The 'I' thought keeps returning." Ramana: "When it returns, ask 'To whom does it return?' It returns to 'me.' Then ask 'Who am I?'"
