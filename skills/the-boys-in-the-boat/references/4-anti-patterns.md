## 6 Anti-Patterns of Team Dysfunction

### 1. "The Star Makes the Team"

In rowing, one star cannot carry the boat. A fast rower who is out of sync slows everyone down. Stars are useless if they cannot synchronize.

### 2. "Winning Is Everything"

The boys remembered their bond more than the gold. If winning comes at the cost of team relationships, it is not worth it.

> **Case: The Broken Rower** (Chapter 6): One member of the team developed a serious infection during training. The team did not replace him — they waited for him to recover. They chose loyalty over speed. He recovered and rowed in the Olympics.
> **Key takeaway**: A team that sacrifices its members for performance is not a team — it is a machine. Real teams support each other through difficulty.

### 3. "The Coach Knows Everything"

Ulbrickson was a great coach. But he also listened to Pocock, trusted his rowers, and adjusted. The best leaders know what they do not know.

### 4. "Talent Is the Only Thing That Matters"

The UW crew was not the most talented. They were the most synchronized. Talent without synchronization is noise.

### 5. "The Best Equipment Makes the Best Team"

Pocock's shells were beautiful. But the shell did not row itself. The team made the shell fast — not the other way around.

### 6. "You Should Never Be Scared"

The boys were terrified before the Olympic final. Fear is real. Courage is not the absence of fear — it is rowing through it.


> **Case: The Sinking Boat** (Chapter 13): During a training race, the boat took on water and began to sink. The crew did not panic. They rowed harder. The boat stayed afloat long enough to finish the race. Afterward, they bailed the water and laughed.
> **Key takeaway**: A team that can face disaster with calm and humor is a team that can win anything.
