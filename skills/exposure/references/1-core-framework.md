# 1 — The Exposure Framework: Contamination → Cover-Up → Discovery → Battle → Justice

> Source: "Exposure" by Robert Bilott

## The Core Framework

### Phase 1: The Contamination

DuPont's Washington Works plant in Parkersburg, West Virginia used PFOA (also called C8) since the 1950s to manufacture Teflon. The chemical was dumped into the Ohio River and unlined landfills. It seeped into groundwater, drinking water wells, and Dry Run — a creek flowing through Earl Tennant's farm.

> **Case: Earl Tennant's Camcorder** (Chapter 1): On July 7, 1996, Earl Tennant filmed Dry Run. The creek that once ran "gin clear" now looked "like dirty dishwater." Foam "trembled like egg whites whipped into stiff peaks." His cows were dying — over 100 calves and 50 cows in less than two years. He performed his own autopsies on camera: blackened teeth, enlarged gallbladders, lungs with "dark purple splotches" that "looked a little bit like cancer." No government agency would help him.
> **Key takeaway**: When no one believes you, document everything. Earl's camcorder footage became the first evidence in a 20-year legal battle.

### Phase 2: The Cover-Up

DuPont had known since the 1960s that C8 was toxic and accumulating in human blood. Internal documents showed they paid the EPA to "go away," funded "independent" studies designed to find the chemical safe, and quietly dumped millions of pounds into the environment.

> **Case: The Call** (Chapter 2): On October 9, 1998, corporate defense lawyer Robert Bilott received a call from Earl Tennant. Earl had gotten Bilott's private number from Bilott's grandmother Alma White — a Parkersburg resident. Earl was "highly agitated, talking so fast that I struggled to understand him." Bilott almost hung up. But the words "Alma White" stopped him. He reached for a legal pad and wrote down "Earl Tennant." That call changed his life.
> **Key takeaway**: The most important cases often start with a call you almost don't take. Bilott nearly hung up on the farmer who would define his career.

### Phase 3: The Discovery

Over years of litigation, Bilott forced DuPont to produce over 110,000 internal documents. These showed the company knew C8 was in the water supply, knew it was building up in human blood, knew it caused birth defects in animal tests — and hid it all.

### Phase 4: The Battle

The legal battle included: a class action covering 70,000+ community members, a court-ordered C8 Science Panel with independent epidemiologists, individual personal injury trials, and an EPA administrative complaint.

### Phase 5: The Outcome

A $670M class action settlement. EPA regulation of PFOA. Global recognition of PFAS as an environmental crisis. But the chemicals are still produced, still contaminating, and still largely unregulated worldwide.
