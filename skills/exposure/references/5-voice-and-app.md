# 5 — Bilott's Voice + 5 Application Scenarios

> Source: "Exposure" by Robert Bilott

## Bilott's Voice

Robert Bilott is a mild-mannered corporate defense attorney who became an environmental crusader by accident. His voice: meticulous, understated, quietly furious. He lets the facts speak. The book is written in first person, but Bilott never makes himself the hero — the documents, the farmers, the science are the protagonists.

> **Case: The Grandmother Connection** (Chapter 2): Bilott almost hung up on Earl Tennant. He was a busy corporate defense lawyer. Why would he take a case against DuPont — a company his own firm represented? But Earl mentioned Bilott's grandmother, Alma White, who lived in Parkersburg. That personal connection made him listen. "I reached for a legal pad and wrote down his name: Earl Tennant." That scribble on a legal pad was the beginning of a 20-year war.
> **Key takeaway**: Justice often hangs on the slimmest of threads — a grandmother's name, a moment of attention, a decision to listen instead of hang up.

> **Case: The Cost of the Fight** (Epilogue): Bilott doesn't hide what the battle cost him. He spent 20 years fighting DuPont. He nearly lost his marriage. His health suffered. He made almost no money from the case while DuPont spent millions on defense. "I had lost nearly everything." But he also says: "I would do it again." This is the cost of taking on a corporation. It's not a movie — it's a life.
> **Key takeaway**: Fighting corporate power is not glamorous. It's exhausting, expensive, and lonely. Be prepared for the cost. But also know that the cost of doing nothing is higher.

## Application Scenario 1: Suspecting Contamination in Your Community

*"My town's water tastes funny. People are getting sick. The company says it's fine."*

**Playbook:**
1. Document. Like Earl Tennant with his camcorder, create a record.
2. Connect with neighbors. Are others experiencing the same?
3. Find a lawyer who specializes in environmental law, not a general practitioner.
4. Use FOIA requests to access government records about the facility.
5. Be prepared for a long fight. Don't expect quick results.

## Application Scenario 2: Whistleblowing

*"I work for a company that is hiding something. I have evidence."*

**Playbook:**
1. Document everything — like DuPont's internal scientists did. Keep copies off-site.
2. Find a lawyer before you go public. Whistleblower laws vary. Protect yourself.
3. Understand that once you speak, your career at that company is over. Accept it.
4. Use multiple channels: internal compliance, regulator, media, courts.

## Application Scenario 3: Supporting a Community Fighting Pollution

*"I don't live in the affected area, but I want to help."*

**Playbook:**
1. Amplify their story. Most environmental justice communities are ignored.
2. Donate to legal funds. Lawsuits are expensive and communities bear the cost.
3. Pressure your elected officials. Corporate polluters only respond to political pressure.
4. Reduce your own PFAS footprint — awareness starts at home.

## Application Scenario 4: Taking on a Goliath

*"I'm an individual trying to hold a large organization accountable."*

**Playbook:**
1. The battle is won on paper. Document, document, document.
2. You don't need to win overnight. Bilott spent 20 years. Think in years, not weeks.
3. Build coalitions. Class actions work because they aggregate power.
4. Use multiple pressure points: legal, regulatory, media, public opinion.
5. Take care of yourself. The toll is real. Bilott nearly lost everything.

## Application Scenario 5: Teaching the Next Generation

*"How do I explain the DuPont / PFAS story to someone who doesn't know about it?"*

**Playbook:**
1. Start with Earl Tennant — one farmer with a camcorder. Human story first.
2. Explain PFAS as "forever chemicals" — intuitive: they never break down.
3. The villain is not a single person but a system that values profit over health.
4. The hero is not a lone lawyer but a community that refused to be silent.
5. End with the message: this is not over. PFAS are still everywhere.
