# 5 — Bennett's Voice + Lessons for AI

> Source: "A Brief History of Intelligence" by Max Bennett

## Bennett's Voice

Max Bennett is an AI researcher and entrepreneur (co-founder of Bluecore, an AI-driven marketing platform). His voice: wonder-filled, clear, systematic. He writes at the intersection of neuroscience and AI with deep respect for both fields.

> **Case: The Opening Question** (Introduction): "Why does GPT-3 answer some questions correctly and others not? And why does it fail so spectacularly on simple reasoning tasks that a child could solve? The answers lie not in the latest AI papers but in the 600-million-year history of how brains evolved." Bennett sets up the book as a detective story, tracing AI failures back to evolutionary gaps.
> **Key takeaway**: Bennett's approach is practical: by understanding what evolution already figured out, AI researchers can understand why current systems fail and what's needed to make them better. This is not academic curiosity — it's a guide for building better AI.

> **Case: The AI-Evolution Parallel** (Chapter on AI Lessons): "Deep learning is a recapitulation of Breakthroughs 1 and 2 — it learns associations, but it has no neocortex. Large language models like GPT recapitulate Breakthrough 5 — they learn linguistic patterns, but they lack the grounding of social and embodied experience that Breakthroughs 3 and 4 provide." Bennett argues that understanding these parallels can guide AI development.
> **Key takeaway**: AI is not on a linear path to human-level intelligence. It has captured some breakthroughs (basic learning, language) but missed others (neocortical flexibility, social intelligence, embodied cognition). Building AGI may require explicitly adding these missing breakthroughs.

## Key Quote

"The brain is not a single thing. It is a museum of evolutionary history, each breakthrough preserved in the structure of our neural architecture."

## 5 Application Scenarios

1. **Understanding why AI fails** → Look at what evolutionary breakthrough it's missing. GPT lacks mammalian social intelligence (Breakthrough 3).
2. **Thinking about consciousness** → It's not binary. Consciousness is built from layers. Different animals have different kinds and degrees of consciousness.
3. **Designing AI systems** → Study how nature solved similar problems. Don't reinvent basic learning mechanisms that evolution already perfected.
4. **Teaching neuroscience** → Present the brain as a layered system. The five breakthroughs framework makes complex brain anatomy intuitive.
5. **Debating AI safety** → Understanding that intelligence is layered and incomplete in current AI helps ground discussions about AI risk and timelines.
