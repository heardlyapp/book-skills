# Preparation, Launch, Return — Core Framework

Hadfield's book is organized around a three-part structure that mirrors the experience of spaceflight: preparation (pre-launch), experience (flight), and reflection (return).

## The Three Phases

**Phase 1: Pre-Launch.** More than half the book is about preparation. Hadfield spends 15 years trying to become an astronaut, then years more training for each flight.

**Case: The 15-Year Application Process.** Hadfield applied to the Canadian Space Agency three times. Each rejection was a lesson. He kept preparing — getting more education, learning Russian, becoming a test pilot. When he was finally accepted at 37, he was ready because he had been preparing for 28 years.

**Case: The Soyuz Training.** Hadfield learned Russian, trained in Star City, Russia, and prepared for the possibility of landing in the Kazakh steppe.

**Phase 2: Flight.** The experience of space is overwhelming — beautiful, dangerous, and transformative.

**Case: The Spacewalk.** Hadfield's first spacewalk: the most beautiful moment of his life, and also the most awkward — trying to get through a round hatch in a square spacesuit.

**Case: The David Bowie Video.** Hadfield's cover of "Space Oddity" filmed on the ISS. It was not just a PR stunt — it was a way of sharing the experience, of reminding everyone on Earth that space is for everyone.

**Phase 3: Coming Down.** Returning to Earth is harder than leaving. The body aches. The mind struggles. The perspective changes.

**Case: The Physical Pain.** After months in zero gravity, Hadfield's body was weak. Gravity felt like a heavy blanket. He describes the difficulty of simply standing.

**Case: The Changed Perspective.** Seeing Earth from space changed Hadfield's perspective permanently. The borders, the conflicts, the divisions — from orbit, they are invisible. He carries that perspective with him.
