### Core Framework Quick Reference

- **Who Gabrielle Bernstein Is:** Spiritual teacher, bestselling author, speaker. Named "a new thought leader" by Oprah. Influenced by Abraham-Hicks, Dr. Wayne Dyer, and A Course in Miracles. Author of The Universe Has Your Back, Judgment Detox, and Spirit Junkie.
- **The Book's Purpose:** The next step after The Universe Has Your Back — teaching readers to fine-tune their spiritual connection to effortlessly attract what they want.
- **The Key Concept:** "Being a Super Attractor means that what I believe is what I receive." Not forcing — aligning.
- **The Three Teachers:** Abraham-Hicks (Law of Attraction, good-feeling emotions), Wayne Dyer (faith, inspiration), A Course in Miracles (love vs. fear, forgiveness).
- **The Freedom Goal:** "Freedom is the greatest gift of all. Nothing holds me back. I no longer play small."
- **The Challenge Path:** "The tough stuff offers a chance to shine the crystal that is me." Difficulties are spiritual assignments, not punishments. "Being a Super Attractor doesn't mean everything is perfect — but it does mean I show up for life with faith, no matter what."
- **Bernstein's Personal Story:** She was 24 and working in PR when she hit bottom — addiction, anxiety, feeling lost. She discovered a meditation center, started studying A Course in Miracles, and transformed her life. "I recovered from addiction, healed PTSD, undid fearful beliefs, and lived with clear purpose."
- **The Four Types of Spiritual Disconnection (Introduction):** 1) "Did You Forget Your Power?" — even spiritual people fall into fear. 2) "Are You Manic Manifesting?" — trying too hard blocks results. 3) "Are You in a Coma?" — numb, disconnected, going through the motions. 4) "Are You Miserable Manifesting?" — manifesting from a place of desperation rather than desire. Each type has a specific remedy that the book provides.
- **Chapter 2: It's Good to Feel Good.** The core of the emotional alignment teaching. "The single most important thing you can do to manifest is to feel good." Not positive thinking — feeling. The emotional guidance system: fear/low vibe vs. love/high vibe. "When you feel good, you attract good."
- **The Abraham-Hicks Influence:** Bernstein draws heavily on Abraham-Hicks teachings: the emotional guidance scale, the importance of feeling good, and the idea that "you create your own reality." The phrase "it's good to feel good" is pure Abraham-Hicks.
- **The Daily Practice:** Bernstein recommends a daily practice: "With each meditation, I vibrate at a higher frequency; with each prayer, I surrender to a power greater than myself." Her prescription: 5-10 minutes of meditation, 3 gratitude items, one aligned action.
- **The "Inspired Action" vs "Forced Action" Distinction:** This is the book's most practical distinction. Inspired action: you're excited, it feels easy, doors open. Forced action: you're pushing, it feels heavy, resistance greets you. "If it doesn't feel good, don't do it."
