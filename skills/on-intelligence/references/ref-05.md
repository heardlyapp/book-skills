# Reference: Implications

## Overview
Hawkins's theory has implications for understanding consciousness, human potential, and the future of intelligence.

## Case: Consciousness
For Hawkins, consciousness is not a mystery — it is the experience of the brain's prediction loop. You are conscious of the predictions your brain is making. The content of consciousness is the set of active predictions at any moment. Qualia are the felt quality of those predictions.

## Case: Human Potential
Understanding the brain's algorithm can help us learn more effectively. To learn something, expose yourself to sequences of patterns. Practice movement. Test predictions. The brain learns by making errors and updating its models.

## Case: The Future
If the brain runs a single algorithm that can be implemented in silicon, the implications are staggering. True AI is possible. Machines that think like humans are inevitable. The challenge is building them the right way — by reverse-engineering the brain's algorithm, not by making bigger neural networks.

## Case: The Common Algorithm in Action
The single algorithm of the neocortex means that any cognitive skill — music, mathematics, language, chess — uses the same basic computation. A musician who takes up mathematics brings the same pattern-learning ability. The brain does not have separate "modules" for different skills. It has one learning algorithm applied to different inputs.

## Case: The HTM Anomaly Detection
HTM systems have been deployed for real-world anomaly detection. The system learns the normal patterns of a data stream (server metrics, financial transactions) and flags anomalies — data points that do not fit the predicted pattern. This application demonstrates the power of the brain-based approach in a practical domain.
