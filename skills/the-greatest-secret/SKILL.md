## Core Framework Quick Reference

- **The Greatest Secret** — You are not your mind, your thoughts, your body, or your personality. You are the awareness in which all of these appear.
- **Awareness vs Mind** — Awareness is what you are. Mind is what you have. They are not the same.
- **The Reveal** — You cannot find the truth by searching for it in the world. It is closer to you than anything. It is what is looking through your eyes right now.
- **The Dream** — The world of form is like a dream. You are not the dream character — you are the one dreaming the dream.
- **The End of Suffering** — Suffering ends when you stop believing your thoughts. Not when you stop having thoughts — when you stop believing them.
- **Dissolving Limiting Beliefs** — Every limitation is a thought you have mistaken for truth. Question the thought. The limitation dissolves.

## Key Principles

- Recognize that you are the awareness behind your thoughts, not the thoughts themselves.
- Negative feelings are your inner guidance system signaling that you have identified with a false belief.
- You do not need to achieve happiness. You need to remove the obstacles that prevent you from seeing it is already here.
- Suffering is the gap between what is and what you think should be. Close the gap by seeing what is.
- Every limiting belief is a thought you have repeated so often it feels like truth. Question it.
- The present moment is the only place where peace exists. The mind lives in past and future. You live here.
- There is nothing to attain. There is only something to realize.

## Anti-Pattern Summary

The most dangerous assumption in the spiritual search: believing that the truth is somewhere else — in a book, a teacher, a technique, a future version of yourself. Rhonda Byrne spent ten years searching through ancient traditions before discovering that what she was looking for had been "right here" all along. The greatest secret is not something you acquire. It is something you recognize. The seeker is the sought.
