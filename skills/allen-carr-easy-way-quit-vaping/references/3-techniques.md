# 3 — Preparing to Quit and Handling Withdrawal

> Source: "Allen Carr's Easy Way to Quit Vaping" by John Dicey

## The Final Vape

The method involves a deliberate final vape — not a rushed decision but a conscious choice. You vape one last time, paying attention to how it actually feels, and then you stop.

> **Case: The Concentration Myth** (Chapter 9 — Conquering Concentration): "Many vapers believe they need nicotine to concentrate. The truth: nicotine withdrawal makes it hard to concentrate. The 'focus' you get from vaping is simply the return to normal concentration after relieving withdrawal. Non-vapers concentrate perfectly well without any of this." Carr dismantles the concentration myth.
> **Key takeaway**: The belief that vaping helps concentration is one of the most powerful illusions. When you quit, concentration temporarily dips during withdrawal. This convinces vapers they need nicotine. But once withdrawal passes (a few days), concentration returns to normal — and stays there without needing constant nicotine boosts.

> **Case: The Cravings** (Chapter 16 — The Biggest Moment of Your Life): "When a craving hits, don't fight it. Welcome it. It's the sound of the little monster dying. Cravings last 3-5 minutes. That's all. Whether you vape or not, the craving passes. The only difference is: if you don't vape, it passes and doesn't come back as strong. If you do vape, it passes and returns stronger." Carr recontextualizes cravings as signs of healing.
> **Key takeaway**: Cravings are brief and manageable. They are not a sign that you're failing — they are a sign that you're succeeding. Every craving you get through without vaping weakens the addiction. Cravings are the death throes of the little monster.
