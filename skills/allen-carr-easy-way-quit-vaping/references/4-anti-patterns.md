# 4 — Substitutes, Relapse Traps, and Fears

> Source: "Allen Carr's Easy Way to Quit Vaping" by John Dicey

## Anti-Pattern: Substitutes That Prolong Addiction

The most common mistake vapers make: replacing vaping with another source of nicotine. Patches, gum, lozenges, and other NRT products keep the addiction alive.

> **Case: The Patch Trap** (Chapter 11 — Substitutes Don't Work): "Nicotine patches and gum are not quitting aids — they are addiction prolongers. They keep nicotine in your system, which means the 'little monster' never dies. You may stop vaping, but you're still addicted. The goal is not to switch delivery methods — the goal is to be free of nicotine entirely." Carr is unequivocal about nicotine replacement therapy.
> **Key takeaway**: The medical establishment promotes NRT because it reduces smoking without requiring a philosophical shift. But it keeps people addicted. The Easyway method: cold turkey is easier than weaning. The physical withdrawal is so mild that NRT is overkill. The only thing keeping you vaping is the belief that you need it — not the physical addiction.

## Anti-Pattern: The Weight Fear

One of the most common rationalizations for not quitting: fear of weight gain.

> **Case: The Weight Myth** (Chapter 12 — Are You Worried About Your Weight?): "Some people gain weight after quitting. This is because they replace vaping with eating — hand-to-mouth habit, oral fixation, boredom. The weight gain is usually modest (5-10 pounds) and temporary. More importantly: the fear of weight gain is worse than the reality. Don't let a few pounds keep you addicted to poison." Carr addresses the weight concern directly.
> **Key takeaway**: Weight gain is not inevitable. It's caused by replacing one habit with another. Be aware of it, prepare for it, and address it separately. The health risks of vaping are far greater than the health risks of a few extra pounds. Quit first. Deal with weight second.
