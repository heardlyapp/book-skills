# Key Principles

## 1. Oil Is Political Power

"Oil wealth doesn't just make Saudi Arabia rich — it makes Saudi Arabia autocratic." The causal chain is straightforward: oil revenue goes to the state, the state doesn't need taxes, citizens don't have representation, and the ruler has no accountability. This is the resource curse in action.

## 2. Reform and Repression Are Not Contradictory

The single most misunderstood aspect of MBS's rule. "Western observers saw social reforms — women driving, tourism, entertainment — and assumed Saudi Arabia was liberalizing. They missed that the reforms were happening alongside the most brutal crackdown in decades. Both served the same purpose: consolidating MBS's power."

## 3. The Personal Is Geopolitical

"MBS is not a typical Saudi ruler. He's a millennial who grew up on video games, who admires Elon Musk and Steve Jobs, who wants to be seen as a visionary. But he's also ruthless, impulsive, and surrounded by people who tell him what he wants to hear." Understanding his personality is essential to understanding Saudi policy.

> **Case: The Khashoggi Operation** (Chapters 14-15): Jamal Khashoggi, a Washington Post columnist and Saudi critic, was lured to the Saudi consulate in Istanbul and murdered by a Saudi hit squad. The operation was personally ordered by individuals close to MBS. The murder and its aftermath — the cover-up, the international outcry, the eventual impunity — revealed the brutality at the heart of MBS's rule.
> **Key takeaway**: The Khashoggi murder demonstrated two things: MBS's willingness to use extreme violence to silence critics, and the international community's ultimate unwillingness to hold him accountable.

## 4. Vision 2030 Is Real — and Limited

"Vision 2030 is a genuine attempt to diversify Saudi Arabia's economy. The projects (NEOM, the Red Sea resorts, the entertainment sector) are real. But they exist alongside the fundamental reality that Saudi Arabia's wealth, power, and stability still depend on oil. The vision and the constraint coexist."

## 5. Everything Is Transactional

"Saudi Arabia does not have allies; it has interests." Relationships with the US, China, Russia, and regional powers are based on what each side can deliver. Understanding that transactions drive policy, not shared values or historical relationships, is the key to predicting Saudi behavior.
