# Techniques: Political Risk Analysis

## The Saudi Political Risk Checklist

Before engaging with Saudi entities:

- [ ] Is the counterparty connected to the current regime or the old guard? (Ritz-Carlton risk)
- [ ] What legal protections exist if the relationship becomes politically inconvenient?
- [ ] Is the deal in a sector aligned with Vision 2030? (priority sectors get more protection)
- [ ] What is the reputational risk exposure? (Khashoggi calculus)
- [ ] Can the agreement survive a succession change?
- [ ] Are there exit clauses that don't depend on Saudi court systems?

## Reading Authoritarian Signals

| Signal | What It Means |
|--------|---------------|
| Sudden crackdown on corruption | Power consolidation, not rule of law |
| Lavish foreign investments | Platforming the ruler's image |
| Rapid social reforms | Building popularity, not liberalizing |
| Aggressive media campaigns | Managing international perception |
| Elimination of political rivals | Succession normalization |

## The Due Diligence Protocol

1. Follow the money — who benefits, who might lose
2. Map the power structure — who has actual power vs visible power
3. Understand the historical context — today's events are echoes of past dynamics
4. Evaluate the ruler's psychology — impulsiveness, risk tolerance, vision
5. Build worst-case scenarios — what happens if the relationship goes bad
