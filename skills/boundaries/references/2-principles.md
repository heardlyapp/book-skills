# Key Principles — Boundaries

## I. Boundaries Define Your "Property Line"

"Just as homeowners set physical property lines around their land, we need to set mental, emotional, and spiritual boundaries for our souls." A boundary is not a wall — it's a fence with a gate. It lets the good in and keeps the bad out. Without boundaries, we don't know where we end and others begin.

## II. You Are Responsible To Others, Not For Others

The Law of Responsibility: "We are responsible to each other, but not for each other." Sherrie felt responsible for her mother's loneliness, for Todd's behavior, for Lois's crises. None of these are her responsibility. "Love thy neighbor" does not mean "be thy neighbor's emotional slave."

## III. Sowing and Reaping Is the Most Important Law

"Don't rescue people from the consequences of their own behavior." The father of the prodigal son let him leave. He didn't run after him. He let him hit bottom. When we rescue someone from the consequences of their choices, we prevent them from learning.

## IV. Perfect Love Drives Out Fear — and So Does a Healthy No

The compliant person says yes because of fear: fear of abandonment, fear of anger, fear of being seen as selfish. But "there is no fear in love. Perfect love drives out fear." A no said out of love is more loving than a yes said out of fear.

## V. You Train People How to Treat You

Sherrie trained her mother that guilt trips work. She trained her boss that she can absorb any deadline. She trained Lois that crisis calls are always answered. "You train people how to treat you by what you allow." This is both freeing and terrifying — because it means the power is in your hands.

## VI. Resistance to Boundaries Is Normal

"When you change, the system around you will try to change you back." Expect pushback: "You're so selfish." "What happened to the old you?" "I liked you better before." The resistance is proof that the boundary is working. "A tree that stands strong in the wind gets the strongest resistance."

## VII. Boundaries Are the Most Loving Thing You Can Do

Cloud and Townsend: "Love cannot exist without limits." If you say yes when you mean no, you are not loving — you are lying. The boundary protects love by ensuring that your yes actually means yes. The goal is not isolation — it's intentional connection through honest communication.
