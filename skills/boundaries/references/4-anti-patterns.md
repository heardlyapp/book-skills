# Anti-Patterns — Boundaries

## The Central Error: Believing That Love Means Never Saying No

"Love cannot exist without limits." The myth that a loving person always says yes is the single biggest barrier to healthy boundaries. "You cannot be loving if you are resentful."

### Mistake 1: "Setting boundaries is selfish"
Cloud: "It takes a lot of love to set a boundary. If you set a boundary out of love, you are being generous. If you set a boundary out of hate, you are being selfish." The intent matters more than the act. Sherrie saying yes to her mother out of guilt was not loving — it was resentful compliance.

### Mistake 2: "I have to help everyone who needs me"
The boundaryless Good Samaritan ruined his business by staying three days. "Not everyone who needs help is called to be helped by you." You have limited time, energy, and resources. Choosing who to help is not selfish — it's stewardship.

### Mistake 3: "If they really loved me, they'd know what I need"
"Far too many boundary problems are caused by expecting others to read our minds." Paul says: "For who among men knows the thoughts of a man except the man's spirit within him?" You must communicate your boundaries. No one can read your mind.

### Mistake 4: "Consequences are punishments"
A consequence is not a punishment — it's a reality. "If you drink, you might lose your job" is not punishing — it's describing reality. "If you call after 9 PM, I won't answer" is not a punishment — it's a boundary. The difference is intent.

### Mistake 5: "Once I set a boundary, I'm done"
Boundaries require ongoing maintenance. "The same person who resists your boundary today will test it tomorrow. And the next day. And the next." Consistency is everything. If you enforce a boundary once and not the second time, you've trained the person to wait you out.

### Mistake 6: "If I set boundaries, I'll be alone"
"This is the deepest fear: that if I say no, I will be abandoned." Sherrie feared losing her mother, her friend Lois, her boss's approval. But "people who can't accept your boundaries aren't likely to be good friends anyway." The relationships that survive boundary-setting are the ones worth keeping.

### Mistake 7: "My parents didn't give me boundaries, so I can't learn now"
"Boundaries are not built in a vacuum." You can learn them at any age through support groups, therapy, practice, and grace. "It is never too late to set a boundary."
