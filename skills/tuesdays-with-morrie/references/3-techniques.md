# Death, Emotions, and Detachment

> Source: Tuesdays With Morrie, The Second, Fourth, Sixth Tuesdays

## Morrie's Approach to Dying

### The Technique of Detachment

| Step | What Morrie Said |
|------|-----------------|
| 1. Feel the emotion fully | Do not suppress it. Do not run from it. |
| 2. Name it | "This is fear. This is sadness. This is grief." |
| 3. Understand it | "What is this emotion teaching me?" |
| 4. Let it go | "Now I will detach from this emotion." |

> **Case: Feeling Sorry for Yourself** (The Third Tuesday)
> Morrie had a routine: every morning, he gave himself a few minutes to feel sorry for himself. He would cry. He would mourn his lost abilities — the dancing, the walking, the swimming. He would feel the full weight of his diagnosis. Then he would say: "Okay. That's enough." And he would get on with his day. He did not suppress his grief. He scheduled it. He gave it space — controlled space — and then moved forward.
> **Key takeaway:** Morrie's approach to self-pity is a masterclass in emotional regulation. He did not pretend to be happy all the time. He acknowledged his pain, gave it room, and then chose to move past it. This is not suppression — it is management.

> **Case: Detachment from Fear** (The Sixth Tuesday)
> Mitch asked Morrie how he could be so peaceful knowing he was dying. Morrie explained: "I give myself a good cry if I need it. But then I think about all the people I will leave behind. I think about all the love I have received. And I detach from the fear." He used a metaphor: imagine a wave in the ocean. The wave thinks it is separate. It fears crashing into the shore. But the wave is not separate — it is part of the ocean. When it crashes, it returns to the ocean. Death is like that.
> **Key takeaway:** The wave-ocean metaphor is Morrie's most beautiful teaching. We are not separate beings who die — we are part of something larger that we return to. Detachment from the fear of death comes from understanding this connectedness.
