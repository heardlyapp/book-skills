# 1 — Core Framework

> Source: *Zenith Man* by McCracken Poston Jr. — Prologue, Chapters 1-23.

## The Three Frameworks

### Framework A: The Narrative Collapse (Prologue, Chapters 1-5)

The media narrative: Alvin Ridley held his wife Virginia a prisoner for 30 years, then killed her. The truth: Virginia had multiple sclerosis and epilepsy. She was not a prisoner. She was cared for by her husband. The entire community "knew" something that was completely false.

> **Case: The Prisoner Narrative** (Prologue, Chapter 1): The media called Alvin Ridley "Zenith Man" (because he was found under a house repairing a Zenith TV). The story spread: he had locked his wife in a back bedroom for 30 years. She had never seen a doctor. He had starved her. The National Examiner ran: "SICKO HOLDS HIS WIFE HOSTAGE FOR 30 YEARS THEN KILLS HER." Every single claim was wrong or distorted.

> **Case: The Medical Records** (Chapter 14): Poston discovered that Virginia Ridley had been treated for multiple sclerosis by a neurologist in Chattanooga for years. She had undergone a spinal tap, had regular MRI scans, and had been prescribed medication. The sheriff had not reviewed her medical records before concluding she had been killed. The entire prosecution case collapsed when the neurologist testified that Virginia's death was consistent with the natural progression of MS.

### Framework B: The Defense of the Unpopular (Chapters 6-15)

Poston took the case knowing it would cost him. It did. He lost clients. His wife was harassed. His reputation suffered. He kept going because someone had to.

> **Case: The Congressman's Call** (Chapter 7): Congressman Nathan Deal called Poston to demand that Alvin stay away from his office. "He scares the ladies there." Poston had to tell his client — a paranoid, frightened man — that even his elected representative wanted nothing to do with him. He turned it into a bonding moment, telling Alvin: "After we win, I'll tell you where he lives."

### Framework C: The Hidden Truth (Chapters 14-23)

The truth about Virginia Ridley: she had multiple sclerosis, diagnosed years before her death. She had not been a prisoner. She had been a woman with a degenerative disease who chose to stay inside. Her husband was not her captor — he was her caretaker.

> **Case: The Neurologist's Testimony** (Chapter 19): Dr. Robert Williams, Virginia's neurologist, testified that she had "definite multiple sclerosis" and that her death could have been caused by an MS complication. The prosecution had never interviewed him. The story that "everyone knew" had been built on no evidence at all.
