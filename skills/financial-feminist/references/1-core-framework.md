# Core Framework: The Financial Game Plan

## The Central Insight

"Personal finance is not just about numbers — it's about power." Tori Dunlap's framework combines practical money management with awareness of the emotional and systemic forces that shape women's relationship with money.

> **Case: The Author's Own Journey** (Introduction): Tori Dunlap started her financial journey as a young woman who was scared of money. She didn't come from wealth. She saved $100K by age 25 through intentional spending, aggressive saving, and increasing her income. Her "Her First 100K" community grew from her willingness to talk openly about money.
> **Key takeaway**: Financial confidence is built through action, not knowledge. You learn by doing, not by reading.

## The Financial Game Plan (5 Steps)

1. **Know your numbers** — Track every dollar for one month. Awareness is the first step.
2. **Build your F-you fund** — Save $1,000-2,000 in emergency savings. This is your freedom fund.
3. **Pay off high-interest debt** — Attack credit card debt (avalanche method). It's the highest-return investment you can make.
4. **Invest for the future** — Start with index funds in a tax-advantaged account. Automate it.
5. **Increase your income** — Negotiate, upskill, side hustle. Your earning power is your biggest wealth tool.

> **Case: The Debt-Free Graduate** (Chapter 4): A woman with $40K in student loans felt hopeless. Using the Financial Game Plan, she put every extra dollar toward debt while building a small emergency fund. She paid off the loans in 3 years — not by deprivation but by earning more and staying intentional.
> **Key takeaway**: The Game Plan works because it addresses the whole picture — savings, debt, investing, AND earning. You don't have to sacrifice everything to get out of debt.
