# Key Principles

## 1. Budget Is a Bad Word — Use "Spending Plan"

"A budget feels like restriction. A spending plan is permission to spend on what matters." The framework: allocate your money consciously — X% for fixed costs, Y% for guilt-free spending, Z% for savings and investing.

## 2. The F-You Fund Is Non-Negotiable

"Before you invest, before you pay down low-interest debt — save $1,000-2,000. This money is your freedom." The F-you fund lets you leave a bad job, take a risk, or handle an emergency without going into debt.

## 3. Invest Early, Invest Often

"Time in the market beats timing the market." The book encourages starting with low-cost index funds in a retirement account. Even $20/month matters because of compound interest. The most important investing decision is when you start, not how much.

> **Case: The Late Starter** (Chapter 5): A 40-year-old woman thought it was too late to start investing. She learned that even starting at 40 with consistent contributions, she could build significant wealth by retirement age. The regret wasn't starting late — it was not starting now.
> **Key takeaway**: The best time to start investing is 10 years ago. The second best time is today. Don't let "it's too late" stop you from starting now.

## 4. Your Earning Potential Is Your Greatest Asset

"The stock market can grow your money at 7-10% annually. A raise can increase your income by 10-20% immediately. Negotiation is the highest-return activity you can do."

## 5. Guilt-Free Spending Is the Goal

"You should not feel guilty spending money on things you love. The secret is to plan for that spending in advance." When your spending plan includes categories for joy, you spend without shame.

> **Case: The Guilt Cycle** (Chapter 2): A woman felt guilty every time she bought something for herself — a coffee, a dress, a dinner out. This guilt led to restriction, which led to rebellion spending, which led to more guilt. The fix was a spending plan that INCLUDED guilt-free spending, ending the cycle.
> **Key takeaway**: Guilt-free spending is not a luxury — it's a system. When you plan for it, you stop the cycle of restriction and rebellion.
