# Author's Voice + Key Scenarios

> **Case: Her First $100K** (Introduction): Tori Dunlap started a blog documenting her journey to save $100K by age 25. She wasn't born wealthy — she worked, saved, and earned intentionally. Her transparency about money — the mistakes, the wins, the shame — built a community. "Her First 100K" grew because she talked about what everyone was thinking but no one was saying.
> **Key takeaway**: The most powerful financial tool is community and conversation. When women talk about money openly, the shame disappears and the learning accelerates.

> **Case: The Latte Factor Done Right** (Chapter 7): The author reframes the classic "skip your latte" advice. Instead of cutting joy, she advocates: keep the latte if it brings you joy, but cut the subscription you never use. The principle is intentionality, not deprivation.
> **Key takeaway**: Financial advice that requires you to give up everything you love is unsustainable. Keep what matters, cut what doesn't.

## Key Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Living Paycheck to Paycheck
Track everything for one month. Create a spending plan. Build the F-you fund first ($1,000). Then tackle one area at a time.

### Scenario 2: Debt Overwhelm
Build the F-you fund first (keeps you from going further into debt). Then attack high-interest debt. Use the avalanche method.

### Scenario 3: Fear of Investing
Start with $20 in an index fund. Automate it. The amount doesn't matter — the habit does.

### Scenario 4: Salary Negotiation
Research your market value. Prepare your case. Practice the script. Ask.

### Scenario 5: Money Shame
Talk to someone you trust. Share your number. The shame shrinks when you speak it.
