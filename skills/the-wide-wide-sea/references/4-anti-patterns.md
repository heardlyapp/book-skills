# 4 — Anti-Patterns

> Source: *The Wide Wide Sea* by Hampton Sides.

## Common Errors

### 1. The "Permanent Welcome" Assumption

**The trap:** "They were happy to see me once. They'll be happy to see me again."

**Why it fails:** Cook returned to Hawaii after departing. The second time was very different. A welcome is not a permanent invitation.

### 2. Interpreting Generosity as Weakness

**The trap:** "If they give freely, they have plenty to give."

**Why it fails:** Pacific island hospitality was based on reciprocity. Cook drained the resources of Kealakekua Bay by staying months with 200+ men. The Hawaiians had been generous. They were not infinitely resourced.

> **Case: The Resource Drain** (Book 5, Chapter 36): Cook's ships carried 200+ men. They consumed enormous amounts of food and water. The Hawaiians had to feed them. By the second month, chiefs were reluctant to supply the ships. Cook saw logistics. The chiefs saw exploitation.
> **Key takeaway**: Your presence is a resource drain whether you intend it or not. Acknowledge the cost to your hosts.

> **Case: The Cutter Theft** (Book 5, Chapter 36): When a Hawaiian stole a ship's cutter, Cook decided to take the chief hostage to recover it. The act was culturally catastrophic. In Hawaiian custom, taking a chief hostage was an act of war. Cook understood Royal Navy protocol. He did not understand Hawaiian law.
> **Key takeaway**: The proportionality of your response is judged through the other culture's lens, not yours.

**The trap:** "If I just stay a bit longer, I can finish what I started."

**Why it fails:** Cook could have sailed home. He chose to overwinter in Hawaii, then return for repairs. The extra time did not fix anything — it destroyed everything.

### 4. Kidnapping as Diplomacy

**The trap:** "If I take the chief hostage, the people will cooperate."

**Why it fails:** Taking Kalani'ōpu'u hostage to recover the stolen cutter was the single decision that got Cook killed. Hostage-taking is not negotiation. It's war.

### 5. Isolating Yourself from Honest Feedback

**The trap:** "I'm the captain. I know best."

**Why it fails:** Cook's officers, especially James King and Charles Clerke, had concerns about the deteriorating situation in Hawaii. They hesitated to speak. Cook did not solicit their views.
