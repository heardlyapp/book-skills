# 5 — Voice & App

> Source: *The Wide Wide Sea* by Hampton Sides.

## Sides' Voice

Sides is a narrative historian in the tradition of David McCullough and Nathaniel Philbrick. His prose is vivid, cinematic, and suspenseful. He builds tension across the narrative even though the reader knows how Cook's story ends. The book is both an adventure story and a tragedy.

> **Case: The Prologue's Opening** (Prologue): "And louder grew the shouting." The book opens at the climax — the beach at Kealakekua Bay, the crowd, the confusion, Cook's death. Then it loops back to the beginning. The structure tells the reader: this is a tragedy. Watch how it unfolds.

> **Case: The Resolution's Voyage Home** (Epilogue): After Cook's death, the Resolution and Discovery returned to England under Captain James King. The news of Cook's death took months to arrive. The nation mourned a hero. The Hawaiians mourned a visitor who had become a burden.

## Five Scenarios

### 1. The Expatriate Worker in a Foreign Country

Sides' book is a case study in overstaying a welcome. The rule: when you feel the welcome cooling, it has already been cold for weeks. Leave before the signals become unambiguous.

### 2. The Long-Term Project Leader

Cook's expedition leadership teaches: increasing penalties signal systemic problems, not individual failures. When discipline becomes harsher, something is wrong with the mission, not the crew.

### 3. The Executive Treated Like a Celebrity

The apotheosis trap: being treated like a god is dangerous even if you know you're not. The more dangerous case: what if you start to believe it?

### 4. The Cross-Cultural Negotiator

First contact dynamics repeat across cultures: awe, exchange, tension, breakdown. Understanding the arc allows you to recognize which phase you're in.

### 5. The Historian of Imperial Encounters

The tension between Cook's scientific mission and his imperial orders was not unique to him. It is the central tension of European expansion.
