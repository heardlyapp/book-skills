# 4 — Anti-Patterns

> Source: *Love Yourself First* by Marc Reklau.

## 5 Errors

### 1. Conditional Self-Worth

"If I achieve X, then I'll be worthy." This guarantees you'll never feel worthy. The bar always moves.

### 2. The People-Pleasing Trap

Making yourself happy only after everyone else is satisfied. The result: no one is happy, least of all you.

### 3. The Inner Critic as Truth

Believing the negative voice in your head. It's not an objective observer — it's a conditioned pattern.

> **Example: The Perfectionist's Fallacy** (Part II): "If I'm perfect, no one can criticize me." But perfection doesn't exist. The pursuit of perfection is the pursuit of failure.

> **Example: The "I'll Be Happy When" Loop** (Part IV): "I'll be happy when I get a promotion / lose weight / find a partner." You get there. You're still unhappy. The problem was never the circumstances.

### 4. Dependent Self-Esteem

Your self-worth depends on others' approval. "You are giving others the remote control to your emotions."

### 5. The Victim Identity

Blaming parents, partners, circumstances. While your past shaped you, it does not define your future.
