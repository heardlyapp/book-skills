# Core Framework — Thermoinfocomplexity Theory

> Source: Thermoinfocomplexity, Chapter 1–4

## The Central Synthesis

Thermoinfocomplexity is a unified theory that integrates thermodynamics, information theory, and complexity science to explain the origin of life and the evolution of complex adaptive systems (CAS). The theory posits that complex systems arise through stochastic encounters of energy and matter, guided by statistical probability rather than deterministic design. As Behzad Mohit writes: "Dice play God with the universe."

The theory replaces the neo-Darwinian synthesis, which Mohit characterizes as describing *what* happened over evolutionary history but failing to explain *how* — the physicochemical mechanisms underlying natural selection. Natural selection, he argues, "is more an observed fact than an explanation of the physicochemical mechanisms operative in the process of natural selection."

## Natural Information vs. Shannon Entropy

A foundational contribution of Thermoinfocomplexity is the Natural Quantum Theory of Information, which overturns Claude Shannon's classical definition. Shannon defined information as the uncertainty (surprise) of a message — making "information" equivalent to entropy. Mohit argues this was a historical error rooted in Shannon's famous conversation with John von Neumann, who suggested naming Shannon's uncertainty function "entropy" because "nobody knows what Entropy really is, so in any discussion you will be in a position of advantage."

Under the Natural Theory:

- **Shannon information = entropy = lack of information**
- **Natural information (Γ) = the true opposite of entropy = a measure of structure and order**
- Natural information measures how much more information the whole system has than the sum of its parts
- Higher natural information = lower entropy = greater internal structure

> "Our definition of natural information, then, is the difference of the pre-information of the parts of the system X, and the pre-information of the whole. Our formulation measures the following ideas: the difference between the whole and the sum of the parts; the degree of interdependence of the parts; how much the parts predict the whole; the internal information, or structure, of an object."

## The Three Fundamental Theorems

**Theorem 1 (First Fundamental Theorem):** In a thermodynamically-closed system with a continuous energy gradient whose magnitude is bounded below by a suitable constant, complexity must increase over time. The Earth together with the energy gradient induced by solar radiation satisfies these conditions.

**Theorem 2 (Second Fundamental Theorem):** In a complexifying system, emergence attracts — emergent complex adaptive systems reside in basins of attraction at local energy maxima. This explains robustness: small perturbations return the system to equilibrium.

**Theorem 3 (Third Fundamental Theorem):** In a complexifying system, emergent complexity increases monotonically (∆Γe ≥ 0). This theorem supplies the proof of punctuated equilibrium — long periods of stability punctuated by rapid change.

> "We have now demonstrated the fundamental principles of Thermoinfocomplexity: Systems in the Universe are complexifying. Emergent complex adaptive systems are robust and persist over time. Complex adaptive systems will emerge to higher states of complexity. Emergent complex adaptive systems operate at a local maximum of energy efficiency, hence such systems will be selected to persist by the principle of least action."

## The Infon

Mohit introduces the **infon** — a conjectured quantum particle carrying both mass/kinetic energy and information. Key properties:

1. Particle-wave duality with mass and kinetic energy
2. Internal structure that carries information
3. Weakest attractive force in the universe between infons
4. Weak interaction with all other elementary particles
5. The building block of all other elementary particles

> "Energy and information carried by the infon drive the evolution of the Universe."

> **Case:** Apply the infon concept to understand how information and energy are unified at the quantum level. For example, when a photon from the sun reaches a water droplet, ∆S = 112.31 bits of thermodynamic information are available. If that photon instead reaches a plant leaf, part of that information-energy is stabilized as high-energy chemical bonds rather than degraded to heat. This demonstrates the energy-information equivalence at the heart of Thermoinfocomplexity.

> **Case:** Use the Natural Quantum Theory of Information to analyze a biological system. A human body contains vastly more natural information than a gas at equilibrium because of the mutual dependence of its components. While a gas at equilibrium "has no statistical structure beyond its lowest-energy distribution, a human has a great deal of statistical structure." The complexity of a system equals its expected natural information content.
