## The Neo-Darwinian Circular Argument

The most pervasive anti-pattern in evolutionary biology is the **circular fitness argument**: evolutionary fitness is the reason for survival, and survival is the measure of fitness. This describes what happened but provides no mechanistic explanation of *how*.

> "The extraordinary diversity of organisms arose from repeated iterations of this process. Biological units, from microbes to mammals, seek to replicate themselves into the next generation, struggling against the odds of survival in a hostile world of competitors. This circular argument—that evolutionary fitness is the reason for survival, and survival is the measure of fitness—is the core mechanism of evolution by natural selection."

Mohit argues this is "more an observed fact than an explanation of the physicochemical mechanisms operative in the process of natural selection." The proper approach is to provide the mathematical, physicochemical basis — which is precisely what Thermoinfocomplexity does.

## The Shannon-Von Neumann Conflation

The historical misidentification of information with entropy — rooted in Shannon's famous conversation with von Neumann — has confused scientists for decades. Shannon's formula measures **lack of information (entropy)**, not information itself. Yet generations of physicists and information theorists have treated them as equivalent.

> "Shannon initially debated whether he should call the sending and receiving of the message between the sender and receiver 'information' or 'uncertainty.' That already showed a confusing conflation of two terms that were opposite in meaning."

The Natural Quantum Theory of Information solves this by defining natural information as the **opposite of Shannon entropy** — a measure of structure, order, and interdependence rather than uncertainty.

> **Case:** When a biologist claims that "genetic information" is Shannon information, they are making the Shannon-von Neumann error. The actual information content of DNA is natural information — it measures the structure, interdependence, and order encoded in the genome. A mutation that increases uncertainty (entropy) in the genetic code actually *reduces* natural information. To correctly analyze a genome's information content, use the Natural Theory's measure Γ (expected natural information) rather than Shannon entropy H.

> **Case:** A common mistake in complexity science is to equate algorithmic complexity (Kolmogorov complexity) with biological complexity. Kolmogorov complexity measures the length of the shortest program that produces a given output — this is a syntactic measure, not a semantic one. Mohit's natural information captures what Kolmogorov complexity misses: the *interdependence* of parts and the degree to which the whole exceeds the sum of its parts, which is the biologically relevant measure of complexity.

## Descriptive-Only Biology

Evolutionary biology has historically been **descriptive** — cataloging what happened without explaining the underlying mechanisms. Mohit contrasts this with the approach of physics and chemistry, which seek predictive, mechanistic explanations.

> "Despite the thoroughness with which Darwin's ideas have been integrated into biology, evolutionary theory continues to only describe the existence of life, in terms of the persistence of life."

The solution: integrate thermodynamics, information theory, and complexity science into biology to provide the "how" — the physicochemical mechanisms that drive evolutionary change.

## Life vs. Non-Life Dichotomy

The traditional distinction between "living" and "non-living" systems creates an artificial boundary that obscures the continuous nature of complexity. Mohit replaces this with the continuum concept of **complex adaptive systems** — from eddies in a river to global economies, all follow the same principles.

> "To avoid the confusion generated by attempting to distinguish between living and nonliving systems in the traditional manner, the phrase 'complex adaptive systems' will be used throughout this book to describe the coherent structures that emerge as the adaptive response to the environment at various levels of complexity."

## Determinism and Goal-Directedness in Evolution

Treating evolution as deterministic or goal-directed is a fundamental error. Evolution proceeds through **stochastic selection** — random encounters of energy and matter guided by statistical probability, not directed toward any end state.

> "Cosmological quanta and the most probable complexity that also constitutes living matter... the process of evolution by stochastic selection."

## Closed-System Thinking

Applying thermodynamic reasoning without accounting for the **open nature** of the biosphere leads to the mistaken conclusion that life violates the Second Law. In reality, the biosphere is constantly receiving thermodynamic information in the form of photons from the sun.

> "The Second Law of Thermodynamics, which states that the entropy (disorder) of the universe always increases, seemingly contradicts the high degree of order and complexity produced by living organisms. This apparent contradiction has its resolution in the information content of the Gibbs free energy which is constantly entering the biosphere from outside sources."
