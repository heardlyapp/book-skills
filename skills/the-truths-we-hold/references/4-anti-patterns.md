# Anti-Patterns — The Truths We Hold

## The Central Error: "You Can't Be Both a Prosecutor and a Reformer"

Harris spent her career insisting these are not contradictory. "I wanted to be on the front lines of criminal justice reform" — meaning inside the system, not outside it. She acknowledges this is controversial but refuses the binary.

### Mistake 1: "Prosecutors only care about convictions"
Back on Track proved otherwise. Less than 10% recidivism. Harris: "I wanted to stop the revolving door — to prevent people from entering it in the first place." A prosecutor who focuses on rehabilitation as well as accountability.

### Mistake 2: "Mass incarceration was inevitable"
California's prison population exploded under the three-strikes law and the war on drugs. Harris doesn't pretend to have solved it, but she created programs that worked at the margins and pushed for reform from within. "You can't reform the system by standing outside it."

### Mistake 3: "Government is always the problem"
The bank settlement proved government can work. $18.4 billion when private negotiations failed. "I refused to accept the first offer because I knew California homeowners deserved better." Government as counterweight to corporate power.

### Mistake 4: "Immigrants are a threat"
Harris' mother was an immigrant from India. Her father from Jamaica. "Immigrants have helped to lift and fuel the economy." Fear of immigrants is "woven into the fabric of our American culture" — and it's wrong. "Our country was built by many hands."

### Mistake 5: "You can't change a system you're part of"
Harris was attacked from both the left (too tough on crime) and the right (too soft on crime). Her answer: "The system is not perfect. But people who care can make it better from within." The alternative — standing outside and criticizing — doesn't change anything.

### Mistake 6: "Bipartisanship means compromising your values"
Harris worked across the aisle on criminal justice reform and privacy rights. But she also walked away from bad deals (bank settlement). "Compromise is necessary. But not when core values are at stake."

### Mistake 7: "One person can't make a difference"
The innocent woman released from jail because a summer intern cared enough to act. Back on Track. The bank settlement. "I never did get to meet her, but I'll never forget her." One person, caring enough to intervene, changed that woman's life forever.
