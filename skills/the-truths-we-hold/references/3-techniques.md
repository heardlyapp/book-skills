# Techniques — The Truths We Hold

## How to Fight for Justice (Based on Kamala Harris)

### Technique 1: The Prosecutor's Question
Harris' signature Senate technique: ask pointed, layered questions that corner witnesses. Not to be theatrical — to get answers. Her questioning of John Kelly on DACA: "Do you agree that we would not use this information against them?" Kelly wouldn't answer. "Are you willing to maintain that policy?" Deflected. "Do you intend to use limited law enforcement resources to remove them?" Wouldn't answer. The technique: ask the same question three ways. The silence becomes the answer.

### Technique 2: Walk Away from a Bad Deal
When the banks offered $2-4 billion for California's foreclosure crisis, Harris walked away. "That's not enough." The banks called her bluff. She held. Final settlement: $18.4 billion. The technique: know your leverage, know the facts, and be willing to say no.

### Technique 3: Build a Coalition Before You Fight
CHIRLA visit two days after election — Harris went straight to the community, not just the Capitol. "We need a coalition." She brought prosecutors, community organizers, immigrant advocates, and law enforcement together. The technique: don't fight alone. Organize before you need to.

### Technique 4: Use Data to Make the Case
The Home Foreclosure Hotspot Map. The maternal mortality statistics for Black women. DACA application requirements. Harris is a data-driven prosecutor. "The data didn't lie." The technique: let the numbers tell the story.

### Technique 5: Create Second Chances That Work
Back on Track program: first-time nonviolent drug offenders earn a high school diploma and get a job. Recidivism: less than 10%. Not therapy, not punishment — opportunity. The technique: measure success by outcomes, not ideology.

### Technique 6: Carry Your History with You
Harris' mother marched with her in a stroller through civil rights protests. She brought Kamala to Rainbow Sign. She cooked smorgasbord (leftovers with cookie-cutter bread). "Fweedom!" — a toddler's demand. The technique: the stories you tell from your childhood are the values you carry. Keep telling them.

### Technique 7: Laugh, Even When It's Hard
Harris' most disarming quality: she laughs. She found humor in her election night Doritos story. In Doug's too-long voicemail. In the "Kamoji" bus. In her mother's "unbirthday parties." The technique: joy is not the opposite of struggle — it's the fuel for it.
