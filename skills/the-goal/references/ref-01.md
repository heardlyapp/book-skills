# Reference: The Theory of Constraints

## Overview
The Theory of Constraints (TOC) is Goldratt's central contribution to management thinking. It provides a systematic method for identifying the single factor that limits a system's performance and improving it.

## Case: The Boy Scouts Hike
The most famous analogy in the book: Alex Rogo takes a scout troop on a hike through the woods. The troop is spread out. The slowest boy (Herbie) determines the troop's speed. If the faster boys walk ahead, the troop becomes stretched out and the group's progress slows. The solution: move Herbie to the front of the line and redistribute his load among the faster boys. The troop can now walk faster as a group. Herbie is the constraint. The hike demonstrates that the system's output is determined by its slowest component, not its fastest.

## Case: The Match Factory
To understand a simple manufacturing system, Goldratt describes a match factory: workers at different stations process matches. The stations have different capacities. The slowest station determines the factory's total output. Improving any station other than the slowest does not increase output. The match factory demonstrates the core TOC insight: local optima do not produce global optima.

## Case: The Dependent Events and Statistical Fluctuations
Every manufacturing system has two features: dependent events (the output of one step becomes the input of the next) and statistical fluctuations (no step runs at exactly the same speed every time). The combination of these two features means that delays accumulate and inventory builds up. The system's actual throughput is always less than its theoretical capacity. Goldratt shows that the only way to manage this is to identify and protect the constraint.
