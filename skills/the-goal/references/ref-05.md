# Reference: Applying TOC Beyond Manufacturing

## Overview
While The Goal is a novel about a manufacturing plant, TOC has been applied to supply chains, project management, healthcare, retail, and software development.

## Case: Critical Chain Project Management
The TOC approach to project management is called Critical Chain. Traditional project management adds safety time to each task (padding). The padding is always consumed. Critical Chain centralizes the safety time as a project buffer. Tasks are scheduled aggressively. The buffer protects the project completion date. Critical Chain has been adopted by dozens of companies and government agencies.

## Case: TOC in Healthcare
Hospitals have applied TOC to emergency room operations, surgical scheduling, and lab processing. The constraint is often the ED physician (throughput limited by physician availability) or the operating room. By identifying and exploiting the constraint, hospitals have reduced waiting times, increased patient throughput, and improved staff satisfaction without adding resources.

## Case: TOC in Services
The drum-buffer-rope method has been applied to call centers (the constraint is agent availability), software development (the constraint is feature completion rate), and retail supply chains (the constraint is shelf space or warehouse capacity). The principles are universal because every system has dependent events, statistical fluctuations, and a constraint.

## Case: Thinking Process Tools
Goldratt developed a set of "thinking process" tools for analyzing complex problems: the Current Reality Tree (identifies root causes), the Evaporating Cloud (resolves conflicts), and the Future Reality Tree (tests solutions). These tools extend TOC from manufacturing to any situation where a system is not performing as desired.
