# Techniques: Education and Microfinance

> Source: Half the Sky, Chapters 7-9

## Girls Education

The single most effective investment in development. Kristof and WuDunn present overwhelming evidence: educating girls reduces poverty, child mortality, and population growth.

> **Case: The Kenyan School Program** (Chapter 7): A program that paid school fees for girls in Kenya. Results: girls stayed in school, married later, had fewer children, and earned more. The intervention paid for itself many times over through reduced poverty and improved health.

> **Case: The Child Bride** (Chapter 7): A 13-year-old Ethiopian girl, married to a much older man, permanently injured by childbirth. She would have been healthy and in school if she had continued her education. Early marriage is the single biggest barrier to girls' education.

> **Key takeaway**: Education is not just a human right — it's the most effective development intervention. The ripple effects last generations.

## Microfinance

Small loans to women. Muhammad Yunus's Grameen Bank proved that women are better credit risks than men.

> **Case: The $100 Sewing Machine** (Chapter 8): A Bangladeshi woman received $100 to buy a sewing machine. She started a tailoring business, employed other women, sent her children to school. Repaid within a year. This story repeats millions of times across the developing world.

> **Key takeaway**: Poor women are excellent investments. They repay at higher rates and invest profits in their children's education and nutrition.
