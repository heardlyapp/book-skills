# Principles: Gender Violence and Maternal Mortality

> Source: Half the Sky, Chapters 3-6

## Mukhtar Mai — Rape as Social Control

A Pakistani woman gang-raped on the orders of her village council as punishment. Her response transformed her tragedy into a global movement for women's education.

> **Case: Mukhtar's School** (Chapter 4): After the rape, Mukhtar wanted to die. Instead, she used compensation money to build a school for girls in her village. She became a global symbol of resistance. Her attacker was acquitted — but the school still stands. Education was her revenge.

> **Key takeaway**: Rape is used as a tool of social control. Mukhtar's response — building a school — is the most powerful counter. Turning trauma into opportunity is the heart of the book.

## Maternal Mortality

Half a million women die in childbirth annually. The solutions are simple, cheap, and proven.

> **Case: The Fistula Patient** (Chapter 6): A young Ethiopian woman with obstetric fistula. She gave birth at home, the baby died, and she was left incontinent. Her husband abandoned her. A $300 surgery repaired the damage and restored her life completely.

> **Case: The Midwife Gap** (Chapter 5): In many poor countries, most births occur without a trained attendant. Training midwives and equipping clinics with basic emergency supplies could prevent most maternal deaths.

> **Key takeaway**: Maternal death is not inevitable. It's a result of neglect. Simple, cheap interventions save hundreds of thousands of lives.
