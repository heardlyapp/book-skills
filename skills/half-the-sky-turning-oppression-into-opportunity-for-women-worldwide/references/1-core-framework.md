# Core Framework: The Oppression of Women

> Source: Half the Sky, Introduction and Chapters 1-2

## The Scale of the Problem

Kristof and WuDunn begin by shocking the reader with the numbers: 60-100 million "missing women," 500,000 maternal deaths, 3 million girls at risk of FGM annually. These numbers dwarf most humanitarian crises but receive minimal attention and funding.

> **Case: Srey Rath** (Introduction): A 15-year-old Cambodian girl who went to Thailand for work, was trafficked to Malaysia, forced into prostitution, escaped by crawling across a plank 12 feet above the ground, and was arrested for illegal immigration. A $400 micro-loan rebuilt her life. Her story is both devastating and hopeful.

> **Key takeaway**: Individual stories make statistics real. Rath's story shows both the brutality of trafficking and the effectiveness of simple economic interventions.

## The Missing Women

Amartya Sen's finding that millions of women are "missing" due to gender discrimination — not just sex-selective abortion but systematic neglect in healthcare and nutrition.

> **Case: The Chinese Baby Girls** (Introduction): After covering Tiananmen Square, Kristof discovered a study showing 39,000 baby girls die annually in China from medical neglect because parents prioritize sons. The death toll from gender discrimination dwarfs political violence — yet it was invisible.

> **Key takeaway**: Gender discrimination is lethal. The "missing women" represent systematic violence through neglect.
