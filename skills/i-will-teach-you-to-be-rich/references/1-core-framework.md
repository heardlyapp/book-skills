# Core Framework: The 6-Week Rich Life Program

## The Central Insight

"The way to get rich is not to cut out lattes and clip coupons. It's to focus on the Big Wins — the handful of things that make the most difference." Ramit Sethi's framework is built around the idea that personal finance should be about living a Rich Life, not depriving yourself.

> **Case: The Latte Myth Debunking** (Introduction): Ramit directly challenges the famous "latte factor" advice. He argues that skipping a $4 latte won't make you rich. Instead, focus on Big Wins: negotiating your salary ($5,000+), optimizing credit card rewards ($1,000+/year), and automating investments (compound growth). Small savings are a distraction from what actually matters.
> **Key takeaway**: Stop obsessing over small savings. Focus energy on the actions that produce real financial results.

## The 6-Week Program

| Week | Chapter | Action |
|------|---------|--------|
| 1 | Optimize Your Credit Cards | Pick the right cards, negotiate fees, set up automatic payments |
| 2 | Beat the Banks | Open high-interest savings, stop paying bank fees |
| 3 | Get Ready to Invest | Open retirement accounts, choose target-date funds |
| 4 | Conscious Spending Plan | Divide income into fixed, guilt-free, and savings |
| 5 | Automatic Investing | Set up automated investments — pay yourself first |
| 6 | The Rich Life | Design your Rich Life vision, optimize earning |

> **Case: The $55K Saver** (Reader Results): Multiple readers describe how implementing the 6-week program transformed their finances. One went from $8,500 in debt to $50,000 in savings by age 28. Another built $100K in retirement by automating contributions. The program works because it's a system, not motivation.
> **Key takeaway**: The 6-week program is designed to be executed, not studied. Each week builds on the previous one.
