# Anti-Patterns

## 1. The Perfect Plan Fallacy

**The mistake**: Waiting until you have the perfect financial plan before you start.

**Fix**: Aim for 85%. A good plan executed is better than a perfect plan never started.

## 2. The Latte Trap

**The mistake**: Obsessing over small savings ($4 coffee, avocado toast) while ignoring Big Wins.

**Why it fails**: The latte mentality makes personal finance feel like deprivation. It's unsustainable and misses the real opportunities.

**Fix**: Spend on what you love. Focus energy on salary negotiation, credit card rewards, and investing.

> **Case: The Avocado Toast Myth** (Introduction): Ramit debunks the media narrative that millennials can't afford homes because of avocado toast. The real issue: large fixed costs like rent and student loans, coupled with not negotiating salary. The small stuff is a distraction.
> **Key takeaway**: Don't let people shame you for small indulgences. The money isn't in the latte — it's in the Big Wins.

## 3. Not Negotiating

**The mistake**: Accepting the first offer and never asking for more.

**Why it fails**: Negotiating your starting salary is worth $500K+ over a career. Not negotiating is the single biggest financial mistake most people make.

**Fix**: Always negotiate. Use a script. Practice. The worst they can say is no.

> **Case: The $5,000 Script** (Chapter 3): A job candidate received an offer. She used Ramit's script: "I'm very excited about this opportunity. Based on my research and experience, I was hoping for something closer to $X. Can you do that?" She got the higher number.
> **Key takeaway**: The simplest negotiation script works. Most people don't ask. By asking, you already separate yourself from the majority.

## 4. Fear-Based Investing

**The mistake**: Avoiding investing because you're scared of losing money.

**Fix**: The stock market goes up over time. Index funds are the low-risk way to participate. Time in the market beats timing the market.

## 5. Guilt-Driven Budgeting

**The mistake**: Creating a budget that cuts everything you enjoy — leading to burnout and giving up.

**Fix**: The Conscious Spending Plan includes guilt-free spending. If your plan doesn't allow for joy, you won't stick with it.
