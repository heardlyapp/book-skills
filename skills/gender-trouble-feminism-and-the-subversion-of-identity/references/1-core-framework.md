# Gender Performativity — Core Framework

Butler's central argument: gender is not an identity but a performance. The performance creates the appearance of a stable identity.

**Case: The Drag Performance.** Drag is not an imitation of an original gender. It shows that all gender is imitation. The "original" (natural gender) is as much a performance as the parody. Drag exposes the constructedness of all gender.

**Case: The Heterosexual Matrix.** Society organizes sex, gender, and desire into a coherent system: female bodies should have feminine genders and desire men. This system produces the categories it claims to describe.

**Case: The Melancholy of Gender.** Gender identity is formed through the unacknowledged loss of same-sex desire. The prohibition on homosexuality is not external to gender — it is constitutive of gender.

**Case: The Parody. Parody can be subversive, but not automatically. Context matters. What is subversive in one context may be conservative in another.**

**Case: The Abject. The abject is what is excluded from the realm of intelligible gender. Abjected bodies — those that do not fit the norm — mark the boundary of the human.**

**Case: The Body. Butler argues that the body is not a pre-discursive biological fact. The body becomes intelligible only within discourse. There is no body before the social.**
