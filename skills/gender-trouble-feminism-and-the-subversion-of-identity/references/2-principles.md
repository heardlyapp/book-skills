# Principles of Gender Theory

**Principle 1: Gender Is Something You Do, Not Something You Are.** Performativity: gender is constituted through repeated acts. The repetition is not voluntary or theatrical — it is compelled by social norms.

**Principle 2: Critique Is Not Destruction.** Questioning the category "women" is not destroying feminism. It is making feminism more rigorous.

**Case: The Category Women.** Butler asks: who does "women" include and exclude? The category is necessary for politics but is also regulatory.

**Case: The Subversive Repetition.** Parodic performances (drag, butch/femme) can expose the constructedness of gender. But parody is not automatically subversive.

**Case: The Lesbian Phallus. Butler deconstructs the idea that the phallus is naturally male. She argues that the phallus can be detached from any particular body part.**

**Case: The Symbolic. Butler challenges Lacan's claim that the symbolic order requires sexual difference. She argues that the symbolic can be reimagined.**

**Case: The Norm. Gender norms are not laws but regulatory ideals. They cannot be fully realized. The failure to fully realize gender norms is what makes change possible.**

**Case: The Universal. Butler questions the universal category of women. She argues that feminism must attend to differences of race, class, and sexuality.**
