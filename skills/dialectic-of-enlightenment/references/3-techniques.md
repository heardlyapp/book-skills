# Critical Techniques

**Technique 1: Negative Dialectics.** Do not affirm what is. Criticize it. Show what it excludes. The truth is found not in the existing order but in what the existing order suppresses.

**Case: The Culture Industry Critique.** Rather than accepting mass entertainment as harmless pleasure, analyze how it shapes consciousness. Ask: what desires does it create? What possibilities does it foreclose?

**Technique 2: Immanent Critique.** Judge a society by its own standards. Show that it fails to live up to its promises. Do not import external standards. Use the society's own ideals against it.

**Case: The Promise of Freedom.** Modern society promises freedom, equality, and self-determination. It delivers conformity, inequality, and heteronomy. The critique is immanent — it judges the society by its own declared values.

**Technique 3: Defamiliarization.** Make the familiar strange. Question what is taken for granted. The culture industry makes everything familiar. Critical theory makes everything unfamiliar again.

**Case: The Everyday.** The most important objects of critique are the ones we take for granted: entertainment, work, consumption, family. Critical theory asks: why is this the way it is? Could it be otherwise?

**Case: The Car.** A seemingly neutral object — the automobile — structures our cities, our environment, our relationships. It is not neutral. It embodies a particular form of social organization.
