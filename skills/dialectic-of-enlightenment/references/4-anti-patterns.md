# Errors — Anti-Patterns

**Anti-Pattern 1: Positivism.** Accepting only measurable facts as real. Positivism reinforces the status quo by treating what is as the only reality. It cannot conceive of alternatives.

**Case: The Fact-Worship.** Modern society treats facts as sacred. But facts are not self-interpreting. They exist within a framework of values and assumptions.

**Anti-Pattern 2: Affirmation.** Celebrating the existing order instead of criticizing it. Affirmative culture reconciles people to their conditions rather than helping them change them.

**Case: The Culture Industry.** It teaches people to accept their place in the existing order. Entertainment is a form of resignation.

**Anti-Pattern 3: Identity Thinking.** Reducing the unique to the general. Treating individuals as instances of categories. The concept of "the consumer" erases the real differences between people.

**Case: The Generic Consumer.** The culture industry treats all audiences as identical. Individuality becomes a marketing category.

**Case: The Administered World.** Modern society administers human beings as if they were things. People are managed, measured, and optimized.

**Case: The Calculation of Everything. When everything is reduced to numbers — GDP, test scores, productivity — what cannot be measured becomes invisible. Love, beauty, justice, meaning — these cannot be calculated.**

**Case: The End of Critique. Affirmative culture encourages people to adapt rather than resist. Critique is replaced by positivity. The result is a society that cannot recognize its own failures.**
