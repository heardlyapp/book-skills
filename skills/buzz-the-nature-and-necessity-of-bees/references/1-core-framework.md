# The Bee Framework

The book is organized around the evolutionary and ecological story of bees. From their origin as vegetarian wasps to their indispensable role in modern agriculture, the narrative spans 120 million years of co-evolution.

**Case: The Vegetarian Wasp.** About 120 million years ago, a wasp species made the switch from hunting prey to collecting pollen. This single dietary shift led to all 20,000+ bee species we have today. The evolutionary story is at the heart of the book, showing how a simple change in diet led to an explosion of biodiversity.

**Case: Co-Evolution with Flowers.** Bees and flowers evolved together for millions of years. Flowers developed colors, shapes, scents, and nectar to attract bees. Bees developed specialized body parts — pollen baskets, branched hairs, long tongues — to collect pollen more efficiently. This mutualism is one of the most important relationships in nature. Neither would exist as they do today without the other.

**Case: The Honeyguide Bird.** In Africa, the honeyguide bird leads humans to wild bee nests. The bird gets access to wax and bee larvae; humans get honey. This rare example of cooperation between humans and wild animals shows how deeply bees are woven into the fabric of life on Earth.
