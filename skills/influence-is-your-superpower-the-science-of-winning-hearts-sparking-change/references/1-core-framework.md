## The Chance Framework: Ethical Influence as Service

Zoe Chance's framework: influence is not about manipulating people — it is about understanding what people genuinely want and helping them get it. The most influential people are not the most forceful — they are the most understanding.

**The Key Insight**: Influence is a superpower because it allows you to create outcomes that benefit everyone — without force, without manipulation, without pressure.

> **Case: The Yale Study on "No"** (Chapter 3): Chance ran a study where students were asked to donate to charity. One group was asked directly. The other group was first asked: "Will you consider donating?" Then asked. The second group donated at a significantly higher rate. The small shift — asking for consideration before action — dramatically increased compliance.
> **Key takeaway**: The word "consider" is magic. It lowers the barrier to engagement. Asking for consideration is not manipulation — it is giving someone room to say yes.

> **Case: The Pre-Suasion Technique** (Chapter 7): Chance describes how a real estate agent presells a house by framing the neighborhood before showing the house. "Is a quiet street important to you?" Then she shows a house on a quiet street. The buyer has already been "presuaded" to value it.
> **Key takeaway**: What you say before your request is as important as the request itself. Frame the context before making the ask.
