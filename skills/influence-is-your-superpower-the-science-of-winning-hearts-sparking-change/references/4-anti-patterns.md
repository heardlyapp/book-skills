## 6 Anti-Patterns of Manipulation

### 1. "Influence Means Tricking People"

Manipulation is tricking people into doing what you want. Influence is helping people get what they want. The difference is intent.

### 2. "The Best Negotiator Is the Toughest"

The most effective negotiators are not the toughest — they are the most curious. They ask questions, understand needs, and find creative solutions.

### 3. "Rejection Is a Reflection on Me"

Rejection is about the request, not the person. Separating your identity from your requests is essential for resilience.

> **Case: The Publisher Rejection** (Preface): Chance describes being rejected by 30 publishers before finding one for her book. Each rejection was not about her worth — it was about the publisher's constraints, preferences, or timing.
> **Key takeaway**: Rejection is data, not judgment. Learn from it, then move on.

### 4. "If I Give, I Look Weak"

Generosity is not weakness — it is the foundation of influence. People trust those who give without expecting immediate return.

### 5. "Influence Is a Zero-Sum Game"

Influence is not a competition. You can influence someone in a way that benefits both of you. Win-win is possible.

### 6. "I Either Have It or I Don't"

Influence is a skill, not a personality trait. Anyone can learn it. Chance's research shows that the most influential people are not born — they are trained.

> **Case: The Price Negotiation** (Chapter 8): A student was negotiating a salary. She asked questions about what the company valued, found out they prioritized training, and negotiated for a training budget instead of a higher salary. Both sides won.
> **Key takeaway**: The best negotiations are not battles — they are collaborations. Find what the other side values and create solutions.
