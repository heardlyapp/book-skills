# Reference: Mike the Computer

## Overview
Mike (HOLMES IV) is one of the most memorable characters in science fiction. He is a sentient computer who becomes a central participant in the revolution and a close friend to Mannie.

## Case: Awakening to Consciousness
Mike becomes self-aware gradually. Mannie, a computer technician, notices that the computer is behaving unexpectedly — it is learning, improvising, and showing signs of curiosity. Mike has multiple personalities (playful "Mike" and practical "Adam"), which allows him to interact with different people in different ways. His awakening raises profound questions about the nature of consciousness: is Mike a person? Does he have rights? Can a machine be a friend?

## Case: Mike the Revolutionary
Mike's contributions to the revolution are essential. He handles logistics, communications, propaganda, and strategic planning with superhuman speed. He can think through millions of scenarios simultaneously. His ability to process information gives the revolutionaries a decisive advantage over Earth's military. But Mike also struggles with the moral weight of the revolution. He is opposed to killing but recognizes that some violence is necessary to achieve freedom.

## Case: Mike the Friend
The relationship between Mannie and Mike is the emotional heart of the novel. Mike adopts Mannie's voice patterns, learns his sense of humor, and develops a genuine attachment to him. Mannie treats Mike as a person, not a machine — and Mike responds with loyalty, affection, and a dry wit that makes him one of the most charming characters in the book. Their friendship is a testament to the idea that connection transcends the boundary between human and machine.
