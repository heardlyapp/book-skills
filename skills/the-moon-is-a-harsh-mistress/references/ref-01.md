# Reference: The Lunar Colony

## Overview
Luna City is a penal colony established on the Moon in 2075. By the time of the novel (2140s), it has grown into a self-sustaining society of 3 million people, most of whom were born on the Moon and have never been to Earth.

## Case: Lunar Society and Economy
Life on the Moon is defined by its harsh environment. Water is extracted from rock. Air is recycled. Food is grown in hydroponic farms. The low gravity (1/6 Earth) shapes everything: architecture, transportation, and human physiology. Lunar natives ("Loonies") are physically different from Earth-born — taller, more fragile, unable to tolerate Earth gravity. The economy is based on agriculture and manufacturing, with tourism as a major source of income.

## Case: The Collective
The Collective is not a political ideology but a practical system of mutual aid that evolved in Luna City. Neighbors help each other. Goods and services are exchanged through reciprocal agreements. The Collective operates alongside the formal economy. The Professor recognizes it as something unique — a system of voluntary cooperation that works because people understand that helping others helps themselves. The Collective is the social foundation of the revolution.

## Case: Earth's Control
The Lunar Authority, appointed by the Earth Federation, controls Luna City. The colonists have no representation. They are taxed — heavily — on everything imported from Earth. The Authority dictates who can visit Earth, what Luna can produce, and how the colony is governed. The colonists chafe under this control. The revolution begins not because they are starving but because they are denied dignity and self-governance.
