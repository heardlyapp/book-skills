# Reference: The Revolution

## Overview
The Lunar revolution is not a spontaneous uprising but a carefully planned campaign conducted over years. It succeeds because of superior strategy, superior intelligence, and an enemy that cannot sustain the cost of occupation.

## Case: The Revolution's Strategy
The revolution's strategy is asymmetric. Luna cannot defeat Earth in a conventional military confrontation. Instead, the revolutionaries target Earth's will to fight. They launch projectiles (rocks) from the Moon at Earth — the kinetic energy of a lunar rock in Earth's gravity well is devastating. They threaten to bombard Earth cities unless their demands are met. The strategy is simple: make the war too expensive for Earth to continue.

## Case: The Propaganda Campaign
The revolution is won as much in the hearts and minds of Earth's population as on the battlefield. The revolutionaries establish the "Loony Party" on Earth — a political organization that advocates for lunar rights. They frame the conflict as a struggle for freedom against tyranny. They make Earth's population question why their taxes are being spent to suppress a colony seeking self-government. The propaganda campaign is masterful.

## Case: The Cost of Victory
The revolution is not clean or painless. Many people die. Luna City is bombarded. The Lunar Authority responds with violence. The revolutionaries live under constant threat of arrest and execution. Mike, the computer, is driven to the brink of destruction. The novel does not romanticize war. It shows that freedom has a price, and that price is paid in blood.
