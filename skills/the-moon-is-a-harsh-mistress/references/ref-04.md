# Reference: Libertarian Philosophy

## Overview
The Moon Is a Harsh Mistress is considered a foundational text of libertarian science fiction. Its political philosophy is woven into the story rather than lectured.

## Case: TANSTAAFL
"There Ain't No Such Thing As A Free Lunch" is the novel's governing principle. Every benefit has a cost. The Lunar colonists understand this instinctively: in a harsh environment, waste is fatal. The Earth government's claim that it "provides" for the colony is exposed as a lie — the colony provides for itself, and Earth takes a cut. The motto applies to the revolution: freedom requires sacrifice. There is no liberation that comes without cost.

## Case: Taxation Without Representation
The novel's political conflict mirrors the American Revolution: the Lunar colonists are taxed by a government in which they have no representation. The Professor argues that taxation without representation is theft. The revolutionaries do not demand lower taxes — they demand control over their own governance. The principle is self-determination: a people have the right to govern themselves, regardless of whether their government is "benevolent."

## Case: Rational Self-Interest
Heinlein argues that rational self-interest, properly understood, leads to cooperation and social harmony. The Collective is the proof: people help each other not because they are altruistic but because they recognize that mutual aid benefits everyone. The revolution succeeds because individuals choose to cooperate for their shared benefit. Self-interest is not greed — it is the recognition that your well-being is connected to the well-being of others.
