# Techniques — Shadow Divers

## How Chatterton and Kohler Solved the Mystery

### Technique 1: The Artifact Trail
Every object on the wreck is a clue. Chatterton cataloged every artifact: dishes, silverware, the periscope, the propeller. "One knife with the name Horenburg became the key." Case: Horenburg's knife was traced to a German machinist's mate assigned to U-869.

### Technique 2: Historical Cross-Referencing
Pore over original WWII documents. German U-boat logs, Allied attack reports, declassified intelligence. Cross-reference every data point. "The official record said U-869 was sunk off Africa." Case: By cross-referencing U-boat patrol logs with the wreck's location, they proved the records were wrong.

### Technique 3: The Multidisciplinary Approach
Diving skill alone wasn't enough. They became historians, linguists, and detectives. "They learned bits of German, pored over original documents, hunted clues abroad." Case: Chatterton taught himself enough German to read original U-boat crew letters.

### Technique 4: The Dive Team Risk Assessment
Deep wreck diving requires meticulous planning. "A minor equipment failure at 230 feet could kill you." Check everything twice. Have redundant systems. Brief and debrief every dive. Case: "You had to have steel balls to do what Nagle did."

### Technique 5: The Expert Challenge
"Challenge professional historians." When the official record doesn't match the evidence, trust the evidence. Case: "They built original theories that contradicted accepted history."

### Technique 6: The Knife Technique
Horenburg's knife had a serial number. They traced it through German naval records. "Personal artifacts tell personal stories." Case: This single knife — found in the wreck — started the chain of evidence that identified U-869.

### Technique 7: The Long Game
The search took six years. Most people would have given up after the first dead end. "They did it because they needed to know." Case: "I could no more turn my back on a chance to write the divers' story than they could have turned their backs on the chance to identify the mystery U-boat."
