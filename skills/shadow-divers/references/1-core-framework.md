# Core Framework — Shadow Divers

## The Discovery (Chapter 1)

Bill Nagle, a legendary deep-wreck diver, receives a scrap of paper from a fisherman with hand-scrawled coordinates. "Nagle's life changed the day a fisherman sat beside him in a ramshackle bar and told him about a mystery." The Seeker, Nagle's 65-foot boat, was built for one purpose: "to take scuba divers to the most dangerous shipwrecks in the Atlantic." Case: In 1991, Nagle and his crew dive the coordinates and find an unidentified German U-boat at 230 feet. No one knows which U-boat it is or why it's there.

## The Divers (Chapters 4 & 6)

**John Chatterton.** A commercial diver who does underwater construction in Manhattan. Methodical, intellectual, driven. "Chatterton wanted to know the name of the U-boat because he needed to know." Case: He parked his vintage Royal Enfield motorcycle next to Kohler's Harley — two different men united by a shared obsession.

**Richie Kohler.** Owns a glass-repair business. From a broken family, diving became his escape. "Kohler dove for shipwrecks on weekends." Case: In his thick Brooklyn accent: "Sounds like a novel, huh?"

## The Hunt (Chapters 7-15)

Six years of diving, research, and obsession. "For six years, Chatterton and Kohler were shadow divers." They pored over original documents, learned German, hunted clues abroad, built original theories, and challenged professional historians. Case: The discovery of Horenburg's knife — a personal artifact that helped identify the U-boat as U-869.

## The Danger

Deep-wreck diving at 230+ feet. "A minor equipment failure could kill the most skilled practitioner." Nitrogen narcosis, decompression sickness, equipment failure. Multiple divers died. Case: "Men died — often — diving the shipwrecks that called to Nagle."

## The Resolution (Epilogue)

The U-boat was U-869, a Type IXC/40 submarine. All 56 crew members died. The boat was not where German records said it was — it was off New Jersey, not Africa. The divers rewrote history. Case: "They had made themselves into expert researchers, built original theories, challenged professional historians, and ultimately rewritten a page of history."
