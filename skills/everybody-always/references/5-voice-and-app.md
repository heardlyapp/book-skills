# 5 — Goff's Voice + 5 Application Scenarios

> Source: "Everybody, Always" by Bob Goff

## Goff's Voice

Bob Goff is an attorney, author, and founder of Love Does, a nonprofit that operates schools and safe houses in conflict zones. His voice: warm, self-deprecating, story-driven, deeply Christian without being preachy. He teaches through narrative — every chapter is a story that illustrates a principle. His humor is gentle, his vulnerability disarming.

> **Case: The Balloon Book Cover** (Chapter 1): When Goff needed to prove his identity to the TSA after losing his ID, he remembered he'd written a book. They Googled it. The cover had balloons on it. "I made a mental note to put a huge photo of myself on the cover of this book just in case it happens again, but I bailed on the idea when I saw what my face looked like on a book cover."
> **Key takeaway**: Goff's humor is a tool for lowering defenses. He doesn't take himself seriously, which makes his message easier to receive.

> **Case: Love Does and the Real Work** (Prologue): Goff's first book, Love Does, funded schools and safe houses in Somalia, Uganda, Iraq, Nepal. "We took all the money from the book and opened schools and safe houses, homes for abandoned infants, and orphanages." The book wasn't just words on a page — it was a funding mechanism for love in action. This is Goff's signature: he doesn't just write about love; he builds things with the proceeds.
> **Key takeaway**: True teachers don't just tell you what to do — they show you by doing it themselves. Goff's credibility comes from his actions, not his words.

## Key Quotes

> "Love isn't something we fall into; love is someone we become."

> "If it doesn't cost you something, it's not love."

> "We can be correct and not right."

> "Burning down others' opinions doesn't make us right. It makes us arsonists."

> "We need to love everybody, always. Jesus never said doing these things would be easy. He just said it would work."

## Application Scenario 1: Dealing with a Difficult Family Member

*"My brother and I disagree on everything — politics, religion, lifestyle. Family dinners are a minefield."*

**Playbook:**
1. Stop trying to be right. "We can be correct and not right."
2. Learn something new about him. Not to debate — to understand.
3. Find one small act of love you can do this week. A text. A coffee. A gift.
4. Three minutes. You don't have to fix the relationship in one conversation.

## Application Scenario 2: Someone Hurt You

*"A close friend betrayed my trust. I'm struggling to forgive them."*

**Playbook:**
1. Love is a verb, not a feeling. Forgiveness is an action, not an emotion.
2. Remember the van thieves. Goff lost months of work and chose love.
3. You don't have to restore the relationship to the same level. But you can start with grace.
4. "Love everybody, always" — not because they deserve it, but because it's who you're becoming.

## Application Scenario 3: Fear of Reaching Out

*"I want to help a friend who's going through a hard time, but I'm afraid of saying the wrong thing."*

**Playbook:**
1. "Be. Not. Afraid." Your fear is a liar.
2. Show up. You don't need the right words — just your presence.
3. Ask one question: "How can I help?" Or: "Tell me your story."
4. Three minutes. You don't have to solve their problems. Just be there for three minutes.

## Application Scenario 4: Loving Someone You Disagree With

*"I have a colleague whose political views I find offensive. How do I work with them?"**

**Playbook:**
1. "Arguments won't change people." Stop trying to convert them.
2. Find common ground. What do you both care about? The work? The team? A hobby?
3. "I've concluded we can be correct and not right." Choose kindness over being right.
4. Love is not about agreement. You can love someone and disagree with them completely.

## Application Scenario 5: Feeling Burned Out on Helping

*"I've been giving and giving and I feel empty. No one seems to appreciate it."**

**Playbook:**
1. Check your motivation: are you loving or score-keeping?
2. Love doesn't keep a record. If you're tracking appreciation, you've left love.
3. Take time to refill. Goff doesn't say run yourself empty. But don't stop loving — just pace yourself.
4. Remember: "You become love by doing love." The burnout is a sign to adjust your approach, not to stop.
