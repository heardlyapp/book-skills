# 2 — Principles of Fearless Love

> Source: "Everybody, Always" by Bob Goff

## Principle 1: Be. Not. Afraid.

Fear is the primary obstacle to love. Goff says fear makes us build barriers — with our opinions, our judgments, our distances. "Every time I try to protect myself by telling somebody about one of my opinions, God whispers to me and asks about my heart. Why are you so afraid?"

> **Case: The TSA Checkpoint** (Chapter 1): After the van theft, Goff had no ID to get through airport security. He emptied his pockets — nothing. "I felt like Jason Bourne." The TSA agent wasn't sympathetic. Then Goff remembered: he had written a book. They Googled it. The book cover had balloons on it. Not exactly convincing. But the real question the situation raised for Goff: "How do we prove who we are?" His answer: not by what we carry in our wallets, but by how we love people.
> **Key takeaway**: When you lose everything that proves your identity, what's left? Goff says: love. Your ID says who you are. Your love shows who you've become.

## Principle 2: Three Minutes at a Time

You don't have to solve everyone's problems. You don't have to have a grand plan. Just love for three minutes. One conversation. One meal. One act of kindness. Then another three.

## Principle 3: The Last One Is the Best One

When you're tired and ready to quit, do one more thing. The last act of love — the one you almost didn't do — is often the most important.

> **Case: Meeting Carol** (Chapter 2): Goff and his wife bought a foreclosure house in terrible shape. "It was so nasty, the mice reported us." They lived in a motor home in the driveway for a year while fixing it up. Then he traded the house (without telling his wife) for a house at Garbage Beach. She cried and told him "every marriage gets one of these mistakes. You just used yours up." But Goff learned something: his wife transformed every "garbage" situation into something beautiful. The principle: show up again and again, love through the mistakes, and transform garbage into grace.
> **Key takeaway**: Love isn't about getting it right every time. It's about keeping showing up, even — especially — when you've messed up.

## Principle 4: Love Is Not About Agreement

You don't have to agree with someone to love them. Goff writes: "I've concluded we can be correct and not right." Being right is not the same as being loving. When we make our opinions more important than people, we "turn the wine back into water."

## Principle 5: You Become Love by Doing Love

"Those who are becoming love don't throw people off roofs; they lower people through them instead." (A reference to the story of the paralyzed man whose friends lowered him through the roof to reach Jesus.) You don't practice love by thinking about it. You practice by doing it, badly at first, then better.
