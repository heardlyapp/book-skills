## Philosophy — 5 Rules to Remember

1. **Love everybody, always.** No exceptions. No fine print. Not just the easy people — especially the hard ones.
2. **Love is a verb, not a noun.** Thoughts and feelings don't count. Love is what you do.
3. **Extravagant grace costs something.** Real love is expensive. If it doesn't cost you, it's not love.
4. **Don't be afraid of creepy people.** The people who scare you the most are often the ones who need love the most.
5. **You are tied for first in God's mind.** So is everyone else — including the person you struggle to love.

## Core Framework Quick Reference

- **Love Everybody, Always**: The title is the thesis. No qualifiers, no exceptions, no circumstances where love takes a break.
- **Love Is a Verb**: Not a feeling you wait for, but an action you take. Goff says: "If you want to love somebody, do something."
- **Extravagant Grace**: Love costs. It cost Jesus everything. It will cost you too. That's how you know it's real.
- **Creepy People**: The ones who scare you, annoy you, or make you uncomfortable — they need love the most.
- **Three Minutes at a Time**: You don't have to solve everything. Just love for three minutes — one conversation, one act, one moment.
- **Be. Not. Afraid.**: Fear is the enemy of love. Every time you're afraid to love, that's exactly when you should.

## Key Principles

1. **Love has no off days.** You don't get to stop loving because someone is difficult. That's when love matters most.
2. **The person you want to avoid is the person you most need to love.** Your resistance is a compass pointing to your growth.
3. **If it doesn't cost you something, it's not love.** Real love requires sacrifice — time, comfort, pride, money.
4. **Don't wait for reciprocity.** Love doesn't keep score. Love because it's who you are, not because of how people respond.
5. **Start with three minutes.** You don't need a grand plan. Just three minutes of intentional love. Then another three.
6. **Fear is a liar.** It tells you not to reach out, not to get involved, not to be vulnerable. Ignore it and love anyway.
7. **You become love by doing love.** You don't practice love by thinking about it. You practice by doing it, badly at first, then better.

## Anti-Pattern Summary

The core mistake this book corrects: **the belief that love is a feeling to be managed or a transaction to be balanced — when real love is a costly, fearless, daily action extended to everyone, especially the people who are hardest to love.**
