# Reference: The Sustainable Future

## Overview
Despite the industry's problems, innovators are proving that sustainable fashion is possible. From organic cotton to recycled polyester to rental models, the future of fashion is being built now.

## Case: Patagonia
Patagonia is the most famous sustainable fashion brand. It donates 1% of sales to environmental causes, is a certified B Corporation, produces high-quality clothing designed to last, and even ran a "Don't Buy This Jacket" ad on Black Friday. Patagonia's model demonstrates that sustainability can be profitable: the company has grown steadily while maintaining its values. The key insight: Patagonia's customers buy less but buy better and stay loyal to the brand.

## Case: Circular Fashion Innovators
Companies like The Renewal Workshop, For Days, and Mud Jeans are building circular business models. They take back used clothing, repair it, and resell it. For Days offers a membership model where customers pay a monthly fee for a rotating wardrobe. Mud Jeans leases jeans — you return them when you want a new pair, and they recycle the old ones. These models keep clothing in use rather than sending it to landfill.

## Case: Fabric Innovation
New fabrics offer alternatives to cotton and polyester. Piñatex is made from pineapple leaves — a waste product of the pineapple industry. Mylo is a leather alternative made from mycelium (mushroom roots). Tencel (Lyocell) is made from sustainably harvested wood pulp and uses far less water than cotton. These innovations prove that we do not have to choose between style and sustainability — we can have both.
