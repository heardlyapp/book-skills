# Key Principles — Preventing Dementia and Protecting the Aging Brain

## Core Principles

> **Case: The Alzheimer's Prevention Protocol** (Chapter 8): Perlmutter argues that Alzheimer's is not inevitable, even with a strong family history. He presents a protocol based on: eliminating gluten, reducing carbs to 60-80g per day, increasing fat to 70% of calories, supplementing with DHA, vitamin D, and probiotics, exercising 30 minutes daily, and managing stress. He cites research showing that lifestyle interventions can reverse early cognitive decline and dramatically reduce Alzheimer's risk. "The genetic code for Alzheimer's may be present in your DNA, but whether it expresses itself depends entirely on your lifestyle."
> **Key takeaway**: Genetics are not destiny when it comes to brain health. Your lifestyle choices determine whether your genetic risk expresses itself.

> **Case: BDNF — The Brain Fertilizer** (Chapter 5): Perlmutter introduces BDNF (brain-derived neurotrophic factor), a protein that supports the survival of existing brain cells and encourages the growth of new ones. BDNF is like fertilizer for the brain. Low BDNF is linked to Alzheimer's, depression, and cognitive decline. The best ways to increase BDNF: aerobic exercise, calorie restriction, intermittent fasting, and eating a ketogenic diet. A high-carb diet, by contrast, reduces BDNF.
> **Key takeaway**: You can grow new brain cells. The key is increasing BDNF through exercise, fasting, and a low-carb diet.

> **Case: The Statin Warning** (Chapter 4): Perlmutter warns against the widespread use of statin drugs to lower cholesterol. He argues that low cholesterol is associated with higher risk of dementia and depression. The brain needs cholesterol to function — it is the raw material for cell membranes and myelin sheaths. Perlmutter writes: "Your brain is the fattest organ in your body. It needs fat. It needs cholesterol. When you lower cholesterol with drugs, you are starving your brain of the materials it needs to maintain itself."
> **Key takeaway**: Not all cholesterol is bad. Your brain needs cholesterol to function. Do not lower your cholesterol without understanding the consequences for your brain.
