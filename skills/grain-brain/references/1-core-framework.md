# Core Framework — The Inflammation Connection and Gluten Sensitivity

## The Model

Perlmutter's framework: diet drives inflammation → inflammation damages the brain → brain damage manifests as dementia, depression, ADHD, anxiety, migraines. The solution: eliminate inflammatory foods (gluten, grains, sugar) and replace them with brain-healing foods (healthy fats, vegetables, clean protein).

> **Case: Gluten and the Brain** (Chapter 1): Perlmutter presents evidence that gluten sensitivity can cause neurological symptoms without any digestive symptoms. He writes: "You can have a completely 'normal' digestive system and still be suffering brain damage from gluten." He describes patients who came to him with headaches, brain fog, depression, and "mysterious" neurological symptoms. When they eliminated gluten, the symptoms disappeared. When they reintroduced it, the symptoms returned. The connection between gluten and brain dysfunction is not rare. It is vastly underdiagnosed.
> **Key takeaway**: You do not need digestive symptoms to have gluten sensitivity damaging your brain. If you have brain fog, mood issues, or cognitive decline, try eliminating gluten for 30 days.

> **Case: The Blood-Brain Barrier** (Chapter 2): Perlmutter explains how gluten can breach the blood-brain barrier. In sensitive individuals, gluten triggers the production of antibodies that cross-react with brain tissue. The immune system, confused by the similarity between gluten proteins and brain proteins, begins to attack the brain. This phenomenon, called molecular mimicry, means that eating gluten can trigger an autoimmune attack on your brain. The result: inflammation, cognitive decline, and increased risk of dementia.
> **Key takeaway**: What you eat can trigger your immune system to attack your brain. The link is not distant. It is direct. The blood-brain barrier is not as impenetrable as we once believed.

> **Case: The Carbohydrate-Insulin Connection** (Chapter 3): Perlmutter shows how high carbohydrate intake leads to insulin resistance in the brain. The brain becomes unable to use glucose efficiently. Without an alternative fuel source, brain cells starve. Alzheimer's is increasingly being called "Type 3 Diabetes" because of this connection. Perlmutter's solution: reduce carbs to lower blood sugar and insulin, and provide the brain with an alternative fuel — ketones from healthy fats.
> **Key takeaway**: Your brain can run on fat. The ketogenic diet was not invented for weight loss. It was invented for epilepsy. It protects the brain.
