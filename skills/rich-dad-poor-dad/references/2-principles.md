# Key Principles · Financial Mindset · Cash Flow

> Source: *Rich Dad Poor Dad*, Lessons 1-3, Chapter 7

---

## The Two Mindset Shift

### "I can't afford it" → "How can I afford it?"
The poor dad says "I can't afford it" — a statement that shuts down thinking.
The rich dad says "How can I afford it?" — a question that forces the brain to work.

> **Principle:** *"My brain gets stronger every day because I exercise it. The stronger it gets, the more money I can make."*

### "Study hard so you can work for a good company" → "Study hard so you can find a good company to buy"
The poor dad's advice leads to job security mindset. The rich dad's advice leads to ownership mindset.

## The Direction of Cash Flow

> Source: Lesson 2

The single most important financial distinction:

| Item | Cash flows | Classification |
|---|---|---|
| Rental property (generates income) | Money comes in ✅ | Asset |
| Your primary residence | Money goes out (mortgage, tax, maintenance) ❌ | Liability |
| Stocks paying dividends | Money comes in ✅ | Asset |
| Car with loan | Money goes out (payment, insurance, gas) ❌ | Liability |
| A business you own but don't operate | Money comes in ✅ | Asset |
| A business you must work in every day | You're trading time for money | Job, not asset |

> **Rule of thumb:** *"An asset puts money in my pocket. A liability takes money out of my pocket."*

## The Rat Race

Most people live in the Rat Race:

```
Get a job → Earn paycheck → Pay bills → Buy more stuff → More bills → Need more money → Work harder → (repeat)
```

Breaking out requires buying assets that generate enough passive income to cover expenses.

## Mind Your Own Business

> Source: Lesson 3

While working your day job, start building:
- **Real assets**: real estate, stocks, bonds, notes, intellectual property
- **Business systems**: something that generates income without your direct labor
- **Your financial education**: the most valuable investment

## Give and You Shall Receive

> Source: Conclusion

Kiyosaki emphasizes: financial success starts with generosity. The more you give (money, time, knowledge), the more comes back. This isn't spiritual — it's practical. Giving opens doors, builds relationships, and creates opportunities.
