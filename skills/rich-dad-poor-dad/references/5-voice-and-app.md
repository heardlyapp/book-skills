# Voice · Common Scenarios · Key Quotes

> Source: *Rich Dad Poor Dad*, full book

---

## § Kiyosaki's Voice

1. **Personal narrative** — The entire book is framed through the story of "two dads." Every lesson comes through a story, never through abstract theory.
2. **Provocative contrast** — "My rich dad said X. My poor dad said Y." The reader is forced to choose a side.
3. **Simple diagrams** — The book's power comes from absurdly simple visual models: circles and arrows showing cash flow direction.
4. **Repetition of core ideas** — Assets vs liabilities is stated, restated, and shown from every angle. Kiyosaki intentionally repeats to drive the point home.
5. **Autobiographical authority** — "I did this and became rich. You can too." The authority comes from personal experience, not academic credentials.

## § Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: "I have $10,000 saved. What should I do?"
1. First: invest in financial education (books, courses, mentors)
2. Look for small real estate deals or dividend stocks
3. Ask "How can I afford it?" not "Can I afford it?"
4. Don't put it all in savings — inflation will eat it

### Scenario 2: "Should I buy a house?"
- Your primary residence: **Not an asset.** It takes money out every month.
- A rental property that generates income: **An asset.** It puts money in.
- **Rule:** If you want to buy a home, first buy assets that generate enough cash flow to cover the mortgage.

### Scenario 3: "I want to invest but I'm scared"
1. Acknowledge the fear — everyone feels it
2. Start with small, low-risk investments (you can afford to lose)
3. Learn by doing, not by waiting until you feel "ready"
4. Get a mentor who has done what you want to do

### Scenario 4: "Should I take this job offer?"
- Don't evaluate based on salary alone
- What skills does this job teach? Sales? Leadership? Systems?
- **Quote:** "Work to learn. Don't work for money."
- The most valuable career is one that builds transferable skills

### Scenario 5: "I'm in debt. Where do I start?"
1. Cut up credit cards. Stop accumulating consumer debt.
2. Pay yourself first: invest something (even $20) before paying other bills
3. Start a side business to generate extra income
4. Use that extra income to build assets, not pay down "good" debt

## § Key Quotes

> *"The love of money is the root of all evil." — Poor Dad*
> *"The lack of money is the root of all evil." — Rich Dad*

> *"I can't afford it" shuts down your brain. "How can I afford it?" opens it.*

> *"An asset puts money in my pocket. A liability takes money out of my pocket."*

> *"The rich buy assets. The poor only have expenses. The middle class buy liabilities they think are assets."*

> *"You must know the difference between an asset and a liability and buy assets."*

> *"Financial struggle is often the result of people working all their lives for someone else."*

> *"The single most powerful asset we all have is our mind. If it is trained well, it can create enormous wealth."*
