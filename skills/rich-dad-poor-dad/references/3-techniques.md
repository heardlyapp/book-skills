## 10 Steps to Financial Freedom

> Chapter 8

### Step 1: The Power of Spirit
Find a reason — a combination of "I want" and "I don't want." A powerful emotional why drives financial discipline more than logic ever can.

### Step 2: Make Your Own Choices Daily
Investment opportunities come every day. Choose to spend time learning about money. Choose to invest in your financial education before investing in anything else.

### Step 3: Choose Friends Carefully
Don't listen to poor or fearful friends. Find friends who are rich or financially smart and learn from them. The power of association shapes your financial reality.

### Step 4: Master a Formula and Then Learn a New One
Learn one investment strategy, master it, then learn another. Speed of learning is the key asset.

### Step 5: Pay Yourself First
The golden rule: when you get paid, pay yourself first (invest), even if you have bills. This forces discipline. The discipline to say "I will pay my bills after I invest" is what separates the wealthy from everyone else.

### Step 6: Pay Your Brokers Well
Good advisors pay for themselves many times over. Find the best (not cheapest) real estate agent, accountant, or attorney.

### Step 7: Be an Indian Giver
When you invest, ask: "How fast do I get my initial investment back?" Once you've recouped your principal, the ongoing returns are essentially free.

### Step 8: Buy Luxuries on Asset Income
Don't buy luxuries with your paycheck. Use the income generated by your assets. The rich use asset income to fund luxuries; the middle class use debt.

### Step 9: The Power of Heroes
Find financial role models and study them. They make the impossible seem possible. Kiyosaki's hero: Donald Trump (at the time of writing).

### Step 10: Teach and You Shall Receive
Teaching others reinforces your own learning. As you teach, you learn more deeply and attract more opportunities.

## Practical Tactics for Asset-Building

### Start Small
You don't need lots of money to start investing. Small steps taken consistently compound.

### Find Opportunities Others Miss
Look for undervalued assets: foreclosures, motivated sellers, distressed properties. Financial intelligence lets you see value where others see problems.

### Use OPM (Other People's Money)
The wealthy use banks and investors to finance deals. If the deal is good, the money will find you.

### Keep Your Day Job
Build assets on the side. Don't quit your job until your passive income exceeds your expenses.
