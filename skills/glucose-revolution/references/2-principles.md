# Key Principles — Glucose Revolution

## I. Glucose Affects Everyone, Not Just Diabetics

"88 percent of Americans are likely to have dysregulated glucose levels (even if they are not overweight), and most don't know it." Glucose spikes affect mood, sleep, weight, skin, immune function, fertility, and aging. "You will rarely hear glucose discussed unless you have diabetes, but glucose actually affects each and every one of us."

## II. The Curve Matters More Than the Calories

"If you were to plot your glucose level every minute of every day on a graph, the line would have peaks and valleys. The flatter our glucose curves, the better." A calorie from sugar is not the same as a calorie from broccoli. Two meals with identical calorie counts can produce wildly different glucose curves — and different health outcomes.

## III. Food Order Changes Everything

Eating the same meal in a different order produces a 73% lower glucose spike. Vegetables first (fiber mesh slows carb absorption), then protein and fat, then starches and sugars last. Case: Jessie's personal CGM experiment — same meal, different order, dramatically different spike.

## IV. Vinegar Is a Glucose Spikeshield

"A tablespoon of vinegar in a glass of water before a meal can reduce a glucose spike by up to 30%." The acetic acid in vinegar slows starch digestion. "You can eat spaghetti carbonara and reduce the glucose spike by a third just by having some vinegar before."

## V. Breakfast Sets the Tone for the Day

"Skipping breakfast or having a sweet breakfast triggers cravings and energy crashes all day." A savory breakfast (eggs, vegetables, protein) flattens the morning curve and reduces the urge to snack on sugar all day. Case: Savory breakfast converts stopped craving afternoon sugar.

## VI. Liquid Sugar Is Worse Than Solid Sugar

"Liquid sugar hits the bloodstream the fastest." Soda, sweetened coffee, and fruit juice produce a spike twice as high as the same number of calories in solid form. "Pick dessert over a sweet drink because dessert comes with fiber, protein, or fat that will buffer the glucose."

## VII. Movement After Meals Is Medicine

"Ten minutes of walking after a meal reduces the glucose spike." Post-meal movement uses glucose in your muscles, preventing the spike. The hack: "Don't sit on the couch after a meal — walk around for 10 minutes."
