# Core Framework — Glucose Revolution

## Part 1: What Is Glucose?

Jessie opens with her personal story: at 19, she jumped off a waterfall in Hawaii and landed butt-first, exploding her second thoracic vertebra into fourteen pieces. After spinal fusion surgery (six metal rods), she suffered from chronic fatigue, brain fog, and cravings. A friend gave her a continuous glucose monitor and she discovered: "My body was not broken. My glucose was."

Glucose is the body's main energy source, derived from food. Sharp increases — glucose spikes — affect everything from mood and sleep to weight and immune function. "If you were to plot your glucose level every minute of every day on a graph, the line between the points would have peaks and valleys. The flatter our glucose curves, the better."

**The Four Horsemen of Metabolic Dysfunction:** Too much glucose (hyperglycemia), too little glucose (hypoglycemia), insulin resistance, and non-alcoholic fatty liver disease. All linked to glucose spikes.

## Part 2: Why Glucose Spikes Are Harmful

Glucose spikes cause short-term problems: hunger, cravings, fatigue, worse menopause symptoms, migraines, poor sleep, weakened immune function. Long-term: aging, acne, eczema, arthritis, Alzheimer's, cancer, depression, heart disease, infertility, PCOS, insulin resistance, type 2 diabetes, fatty liver disease.

**The "Glucose and Fructose" tandem:** Both found together in sugary foods. Flattening glucose also flattens fructose. "Too much fructose increases the likelihood of obesity, heart disease, and nonalcoholic fatty liver disease."

## Part 3: The 10 Hacks

**Hack 1: Eat Foods in the Right Order.** Vegetables first, then protein and fat, then starches and sugars. Case: Jessie's own experiment — eating the exact same meal but in different order produced a 73% lower glucose spike when vegetables preceded carbs. "Fiber creates a mesh in your gut that slows down the absorption of glucose."

**Hack 2: Start Every Meal with a Veggie Starter.** "Imagine you can't have your main course without finishing your veggies first." The fiber barrier slows carb absorption. An apple before a meal is better than after.

**Hack 3: Stop Counting Calories.** "The science is clear: a calorie from sugar is not the same as a calorie from broccoli." Calories matter, but glucose response matters more. Two meals with the same calorie count can produce wildly different glucose curves.

**Hack 4: Flatten Your Breakfast Curve.** Breakfast sets the glucose tone for the day. A sweet breakfast triggers cravings and energy crashes all day. Case: Jessie's community member switched from a sweet oatmeal breakfast to a savory one (eggs + veggies) and lost afternoon cravings within days.

**Hack 5: Have Any Sugar You Like.** "As long as you eat it after something that flattens your curve." Don't eat sugar on an empty stomach. If you want dessert, eat it right after a balanced meal, not hours later as a standalone snack.

**Hack 6: Pick Dessert Over a Sweet Drink.** Liquid sugar (soda, sweetened coffee, fruit juice) hits the bloodstream fastest. Solid sugar with fiber/protein/fat produces a flatter curve. "Coca-Cola produces a spike twice as high as the same number of calories of chocolate."

**Hack 7: Reach for Vinegar Before a Meal.** A tablespoon of vinegar in a glass of water before a meal can reduce a glucose spike by up to 30%. Acetic acid slows starch digestion. Case: Jessie tested this on herself — spaghetti carbonara with vinegar beforehand produced a dramatically flatter curve.

**Hack 8: After You Eat, Move.** Ten minutes of walking after a meal reduces the glucose spike by using the glucose in your muscles. "Don't sit on the couch after a meal — walk around for 10 minutes."

**Hack 9: If You Need a Snack, Make It Savory.** Avoid sweet snacks that cause spikes and crashes. A handful of nuts, cheese, or vegetables is better than granola bars or fruit.

**Hack 10: Put Clothes on Your Carbs.** Always pair carbs with fiber, protein, or fat. Never eat carbs alone. "Naked carbs spike your glucose. Clothed carbs are gentler."
