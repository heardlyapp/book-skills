# Techniques — Glucose Revolution

## How to Flatten Your Glucose Curve (Based on Jessie Inchauspé)

### Technique 1: The Proper Meal Order (Hack 1)
Eat your food in this exact sequence: (1) Fiber (vegetables/salad), (2) Protein and fat, (3) Starches and sugars. This creates a fiber mesh in your gut that slows carb absorption. Case: Jessie's experiment — same meal of bread, butter, jam, and orange juice eaten in different order. Standard order (bread → jam → OJ): steep spike. Veggies-first order (salad → eggs → bread): 73% lower spike.

### Technique 2: The Vinegar Preamble (Hack 7)
Mix 1 tablespoon of vinegar (any kind — apple cider, balsamic, white wine) in a tall glass of water. Drink slowly throughout the meal. Do NOT drink it neat — the acetic acid can damage tooth enamel. Case: Jessie tested spaghetti carbonara — with vinegar beforehand, the spike dropped 30%. The effect lasts through the next meal.

### Technique 3: The Savory Breakfast (Hack 4)
Replace sweet breakfasts (oatmeal, cereal, toast with jam, smoothies, pastries) with savory options (eggs, vegetables, avocado, tofu, cheese). Case: One community member had been eating oatmeal with fruit every morning — tired by 10 AM, craving chocolate by 3 PM. After switching to eggs with spinach and mushrooms, her energy lasted all day and the 3 PM chocolate cravings disappeared.

### Technique 4: The Dessert Rule (Hack 5 + 6)
Never eat sweet things alone. If you want dessert, eat it right after a balanced meal (not hours later). Pick solid desserts over sweet drinks. Case: "Coca-Cola produces a spike twice as high as the same number of calories of chocolate" because the chocolate contains fat that buffers the sugar absorption.

### Technique 5: The 10-Minute Walk (Hack 8)
Within 60 minutes after eating, do 10 minutes of gentle movement — a walk, household chores. Your muscles pull glucose from the bloodstream. Case: Jessie compared sitting on the couch vs walking after identical meals. Walking reduced the post-meal spike by over 25%.

### Technique 6: The Carb Insurance (Hack 10)
Never eat carbs alone. "Put clothes on your carbs" — always pair with fiber, protein, or fat. If you're going to eat bread, add avocado or cheese. If you want fruit, pair it with nuts. Case: An apple eaten alone versus an apple with peanut butter produces a significantly flatter curve.

### Technique 7: The Glucose Goddess Checklist
Before each meal, ask: (1) Did I eat veggies first? (2) Did I add protein and fat to my carbs? (3) Did I have vinegar today? (4) Will I walk after? (5) If having sugar, is it after a meal? Answering yes to even 2-3 of these can dramatically flatten your curve.
