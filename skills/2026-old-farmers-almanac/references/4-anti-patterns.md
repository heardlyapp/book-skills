# 4 — Recipes and Cooking

> Source: "The 2026 Old Farmer's Almanac"

## Seasonal Recipes

Each edition features recipes organized by season — spring lamb, summer tomatoes, autumn squash, winter stews.

## Preserving and Canning

Detailed guides for canning, freezing, drying, and fermenting the harvest.

> **Case: The 2026 Featured Recipes** (Food Section): The 2026 edition includes recipes for rhubarb pie, summer squash casserole, and a winter vegetable stew. "The best recipes come from what's growing in your garden right now."
> **Key takeaway**: Seasonal eating is not just trendy — it's practical. The food tastes better, costs less, and supports local agriculture.

> **Case: The Canning Safety Guide** (Preserving Section): The Almanac includes detailed instructions for safe water bath and pressure canning. "Canning is safe if done correctly. Not following the procedures can be dangerous."
> **Key takeaway**: Preserving food is a valuable skill, but safety comes first. Always follow tested recipes and procedures.
