# 1 — Weather Forecasts

> Source: "The 2026 Old Farmer's Almanac"

## The Secret Formula

The Almanac's weather forecasts are derived from a secret formula developed in 1792 by founder Robert B. Thomas. The formula is locked in a black box at the Almanac's offices.

## Regional Forecasts

The US is divided into 18 regions, each with its own forecast. Forecasts cover temperature and precipitation for each season.

> **Case: The Weather Accuracy Claim** (Introduction): The Almanac claims 80% accuracy for its weather forecasts. This is an unverified claim but has been the Almanac's standard for centuries. "We don't guarantee it — we predict it."
> **Key takeaway**: Use the Almanac's forecasts as guidance, not guarantees. Plan for the predicted weather, but prepare for alternatives.

> **Case: The Weather Proverbs** (Folklore Section): "Red sky at night, sailor's delight. Red sky in morning, sailor's warning." The Almanac collects weather proverbs from around the world. Some have scientific basis (red sky at night often indicates high pressure moving in). Others are just poetry.
> **Key takeaway**: Weather proverbs are a form of traditional knowledge. Some have real predictive value. Test them against modern forecasts and see for yourself.
