# Techniques: The Influence Practice

## The Third Way Process

1. State your position and the other person's position
2. Ask: "What does each of us truly need?"
3. Look for solutions that serve both sets of needs
4. Test the solution: does it truly serve both?
5. Implement and adjust

## The Empathetic Listening Technique

1. Listen without planning your response
2. Reflect back what you hear: "So what I'm hearing is..."
3. Ask clarifying questions: "What matters most to you about this?"
4. Acknowledge their perspective before sharing yours
5. Find common ground before addressing differences

> **Case: The Listening Turnaround** (Chapter 7): A manager whose team was disengaged started every one-on-one with ten minutes of listening — no agenda, no judgments, just listening. His team opened up. Trust grew. Performance improved. The listening alone was the intervention.
> **Key takeaway**: Listening without agenda is a powerful form of giving. In a world where everyone is trying to be heard, the person who truly listens stands out.

> **Case: The Generous Competitor** (Chapter 8): A business owner shared valuable contacts and insights with a competitor. Other business owners thought he was naive. But his reputation for generosity grew. People wanted to do business with him. Opportunities came to him. His "naive" generosity became his greatest competitive advantage.
>
> **Key takeaway**: Generosity toward competitors is not weakness — it's strategy. When you give freely, you build a reputation that attracts opportunities. The giver gains more than the taker.

## The Value-Add Question

Before every interaction, ask yourself:
- "What can I give to this person in this conversation?"
- It might be: attention, a compliment, an insight, a connection, encouragement
- Give it freely, without expecting anything back

## Building Influence Without Authority

1. Be the person who always adds value
2. Listen more than you speak
3. Acknowledge others' contributions publicly
4. Be reliable and consistent
5. Put others' success ahead of your own
