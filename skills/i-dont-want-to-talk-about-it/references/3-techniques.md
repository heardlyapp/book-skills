# Healing

> Source: I Don't Want to Talk About It, Chapters 9-12

## The Path to Recovery

### Steps

| Step | What It Means |
|------|--------------|
| 1. Break the silence | Say it out loud: "I am not okay." |
| 2. Accept help | Therapy, support groups, trusted friends |
| 3. Feel the feelings | Let yourself be sad, scared, vulnerable |
| 4. Connect | Reach out. Do not isolate. |
| 5. Give back | Help other men break their silence |

> **Case: The First Session (Chapter 10)**
> Real describes the first therapy session with a depressed man. The man sits in silence. He does not know what to say. He has never talked about his feelings. Real's approach: start where the man is. Do not force emotion. Build trust. The first step is not "tell me how you feel" — it is "tell me about your life." The feelings come later.
> **Key takeaway:** Therapy with men is different. You cannot expect a man who has never talked about his feelings to open up in session one. The lesson: start where the person is. Build trust first. Feelings come later.

> **Case: The Group (Chapter 12)**
> Real found that men's therapy groups were powerful. Men who could not talk one-on-one would open up in a room with other men. They saw themselves in each other. They realized they were not alone. The group normalized the experience of struggle. The group became a place where men could say: "I don't want to talk about it" — and then talk about it.
> **Key takeaway:** Men need other men. The group is powerful because it breaks the isolation. The lesson: find other men who are doing the work. You cannot heal alone.
