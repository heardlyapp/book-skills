# Author's Voice + Key Scenarios

> **Case: Bittle's Journalism** (Author's Note): Jake Bittle is a journalist who traveled across America to document the human stories behind climate displacement. His reporting is grounded in the experiences of real people — not statistics.
> **Key takeaway**: The book's power comes from its human stories. Bittle lets the people experiencing displacement speak for themselves.

> **Case: The Future of Migration** (Conclusion): Bittle argues that climate migration will reshape America as profoundly as the Great Migration of African Americans from the South or the Dust Bowl migration of the 1930s. This is not a footnote in history — it's one of the defining stories of the 21st century.
> **Key takeaway**: We are living through one of the largest migrations in American history. Understanding it is essential to understanding our future.

## Key Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Rising Insurance Costs
This is a warning sign. Assess your risk. Consider relocating before you're forced.

### Scenario 2: After a Disaster
Document everything. Apply for aid. Don't rush the decision to rebuild. Consider relocating.

### Scenario 3: Buying a Home
Check climate projections. Check insurance costs. Check flood/fire risk. Don't buy in high-risk areas.

### Scenario 4: Community Planning
Advocate for managed retreat and climate adaptation plans. Collective action is more effective than individual.

### Scenario 5: Personal Preparation
Build an emergency fund. Have a go-bag. Know your evacuation routes. Stay informed.
