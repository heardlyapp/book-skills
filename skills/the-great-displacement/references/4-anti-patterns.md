# Anti-Patterns

## 1. Climate Denial

**The mistake**: Believing climate change won't affect you personally.

**Fix**: Climate impacts are already affecting insurance rates, property values, and disaster risk nationwide. Even if you haven't been directly affected yet, you will be.

## 2. Waiting for Government to Act

**The mistake**: Assuming the government will protect you.

**Fix**: Government responses are slow, underfunded, and often counterproductive. Take personal responsibility for assessing your risk and preparing.

> **Case: The Uninsured Family** (Chapter 5): A family in a flood-prone area didn't buy flood insurance because they didn't think it would happen to them. When the flood came, they lost everything. FEMA assistance covered a fraction of their losses. They were displaced permanently.
> **Key takeaway**: You cannot rely on government assistance or insurance alone to protect you. Personal preparation is essential.

## 3. Rebuilding in the Same Place

**The mistake**: Repeatedly rebuilding in flood-prone or fire-prone areas.

**Fix**: After a disaster, consider whether rebuilding is the right choice. Sometimes the wisest decision is to relocate.

> **Case: The Paradise Rebuilders** (Chapter 3): After the Camp Fire destroyed Paradise, California, some residents chose to rebuild. But insurance payouts were insufficient, building costs had risen, and fire risk remained. Those who rebuilt faced the same risks, while those who relocated had to start over somewhere else entirely.
>
> **Key takeaway**: Rebuilding in the same place means accepting the same risks. Sometimes the smartest choice is to move on.

## 4. Ignoring Insurance Signals

**The mistake**: Dismissing rising insurance costs as a market anomaly.

**Fix**: Insurance prices are the market's way of communicating risk. When insurance becomes unaffordable, that's a signal you should pay attention to.

## 5. Thinking It Won't Happen to Your Community

**The mistake**: Believing your community is somehow immune.

**Fix**: Climate change affects every region differently, but no region is untouched. Know your local risks and plan accordingly.
