# Core Framework: The Next American Migration

## The Central Thesis

"Climate change is already creating the largest migration in American history. It's not coming — it's here." Bittle traces how wildfires, floods, sea-level rise, and extreme weather are already forcing Americans to leave their homes.

> **Case: The Louisiana Coastline** (Chapter 1): Louisiana loses a football field of land to the sea every 100 minutes. Entire communities — like the Isle de Jean Charles — are disappearing. The Biloxi-Chitimacha-Choctaw tribe is the first official "climate refugee" community in the US, receiving federal funding for relocation.
> **Key takeaway**: Climate displacement is not theoretical. Entire communities are already being forced to relocate.

## Key Types of Displacement

| Disaster | Affected Region | Type of Displacement |
|----------|----------------|---------------------|
| Wildfire | California, West | Sudden, destructive, often permanent |
| Flood | Gulf Coast, Midwest | Recurring, increasingly uninsurable |
| Sea-Level Rise | Coastal communities | Slow but inevitable, managed retreat |
| Hurricane | Atlantic/Gulf Coast | Temporary often becomes permanent |
| Heat/Drought | Southwest | Increasingly unlivable conditions |

> **Case: The Paradise Fire** (Chapter 3): The 2018 Camp Fire destroyed the town of Paradise, California. 85% of the town's homes were destroyed. Most residents were displaced permanently. Many could not afford to rebuild. The town that was home to 26,000 lost more than half its population.
> **Key takeaway**: A single wildfire can permanently destroy a community. The survivors scatter, and the town may never recover.
