# Techniques: Preparing for Climate Displacement

## Assessing Your Climate Risk

1. Check flood maps (FEMA / NOAA)
2. Check wildfire risk maps
3. Research insurance availability and cost trends in your area
4. Look at historical disaster patterns for your region
5. Project 10-20 years: how will conditions change?

## The Climate-Proof Home Checklist

- [ ] Is the home outside high-risk flood or fire zones?
- [ ] Can it withstand extreme weather?
- [ ] Is there adequate insurance coverage?
- [ ] Is the water supply secure?
- [ ] Is the power grid reliable in extreme weather?
- [ ] Does the community have a climate adaptation plan?

> **Case: The Resilient Community** (Chapter 9): A small coastal town in Maine developed a comprehensive climate adaptation plan: elevating roads, moving critical infrastructure inland, creating a buyout program for waterfront properties, and establishing a community relocation fund. By planning ahead, they avoided the chaos that follows sudden disasters.
> **Key takeaway**: Proactive planning is more effective and less costly than reactive disaster response. Communities that plan ahead preserve more of what matters.

> **Case: The Buyout Dilemma** (Chapter 10): A Louisiana community was offered federal buyouts after repeated flooding. Individual homeowners who accepted buyouts moved away, but those who stayed lost their community — the stores closed, neighbors left, social networks collapsed. The buyout helped individuals but destroyed the community.
>
> **Key takeaway**: Individual solutions to climate displacement can destroy community bonds. Collective relocation — moving entire communities together — preserves the social fabric that makes communities worth saving.

## Individual Steps

- Build an emergency fund (3-6 months expenses)
- Keep important documents in a fireproof/waterproof safe
- Have a go-bag ready
- Know your evacuation routes
- Consider the climate resilience of any property before buying
- Stay informed about local climate risks and adaptation plans
