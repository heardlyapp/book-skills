# Daily Practice and Exercises

> Source: The Master Key System, all Parts

## The Method

### The Daily Practice

| Time | Activity | Purpose |
|------|----------|---------|
| Morning (15-30 min) | Silence, visualization, affirmation | Set the mental tone for the day |
| Throughout day | Hold the ideal in mind | Reinforce the mental pattern |
| Evening (5 min) | Review, gratitude | Close the mental loop |

> **Case: The Silence Practice (Part 2)**
> Haanel's daily practice: find a quiet place where you will not be disturbed. Sit comfortably. Relax your body. Turn your attention inward. Still the mind. Then hold a single thought: "I am one with the Universal Mind." Do not try to do anything. Just be still. In the silence, you connect with the source of all power. This is not meditation as escape — it is meditation as alignment.
> **Key takeaway:** The silence practice is the most important exercise in the entire system. Without it, the intellectual understanding of the principles remains theoretical. The silence is where theory becomes experience.

> **Case: The Visualization Exercise (Part 5)**
> Haanel instructs: "Form a mental image of what you want to accomplish. See it clearly. See the details. See yourself in the picture. Then hold this image in mind with the absolute conviction that it is already accomplished." The key is not to visualize and hope — it is to visualize and know. The feeling of already having what you desire is the creative impulse that brings it into being.
> **Key takeaway:** Visualization is not daydreaming. It is the deliberate construction of a mental reality. When you can see it as already done, the subconscious accepts it as fact and begins to manifest it. The feeling is the secret — feel it as already true.
