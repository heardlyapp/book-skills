# Anti-Patterns

## 1. Doubting While Affirming

**The mistake**: Repeating affirmations while internally doubting they'll work.

**Fix**: Doubt cancels the affirmation. You must silence the skeptical inner voice. With time and repetition, doubt fades.

> **Case: The Skeptical Businessman** (Chapter 2): A businessman said affirmations for prosperity but kept checking his bank account and worrying. His doubt neutralized the affirmation. Murphy taught him to affirm, then release — stop checking, stop worrying. Once the results came, the doubt disappeared.
> **Key takeaway**: Faith and doubt cannot coexist. You must choose one. Checking and worrying is a form of doubt that cancels your affirmations.

## 2. Affirming in the Future Tense

**The mistake**: Saying "I will be successful" instead of "I am successful."

**Fix**: The subconscious takes you at your word. "I will be" means you're not yet. The subconscious will keep you not-yet. Say "I am" and feel it.

## 3. Fighting Negative Thoughts

**The mistake**: Trying to eliminate negative thoughts by fighting them.

**Fix**: You cannot fight darkness — you can only turn on the light. Replace negative thoughts with positive ones rather than fighting them directly.

## 4. Impatience

**The mistake**: Expecting instant results and giving up when they don't come.

**Fix**: Reprogramming takes time. Be consistent. Trust the process. Results compound.

> **Case: The Garden Analogy** (Chapter 10): Murphy compares the mind to a garden. Weeds grow naturally. To have a flower garden, you must plant seeds daily and pull weeds consistently. Same with the mind — negative thoughts grow naturally. Positive thoughts must be planted and cultivated daily.
> **Key takeaway**: Mental cultivation is a daily practice, not a one-time event. Just as a garden needs consistent care, so does your mind.
