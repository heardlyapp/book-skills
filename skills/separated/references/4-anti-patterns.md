# Anti-Patterns — Separated

## The Central Error: "The policy was an accident or unintended consequence"

"It was an avoidable catastrophe made worse by people who could have made it better at multiple inflection points." Zero tolerance was announced. Prosecution requires separation. This was not a mistake — it was policy.

### Mistake 1: "The policy was only for a few months"
The El Paso pilot began in July 2017 — nearly a year before the public noticed. The total separated count is at least 5,556. "Though the Trump administration had been carrying out widespread family separations at the border for more than a year, the horror set in for me personally on the thirteenth day of June 2018."

### Mistake 2: "Parents were given a way to stay together"
Prosecution = separation. There was no "choose your child" option. "The government's data was incomplete." Parents were deported without their children. "The Trump administration was potentially creating thousands of immigrant orphans."

### Mistake 3: "The facilities were adequate shelters"
Casa Padre was a former Walmart. Ursula was a Border Patrol station with "pods" that Soboroff described as "animal kennels." A government lawyer argued that children didn't need toothbrushes. "This place is called a shelter, but effectively these kids are incarcerated."

### Mistake 4: "The government knew what it was doing"
"We don't have those numbers" was a consistent refrain. The government had no centralized tracking system. "Child could get moved even if parent back in a day." The court ordered reunification, and the government couldn't find the parents.

### Mistake 5: "The children were quickly reunited"
The court ordered 30 days. The government asked for extensions. Juan and José were separated for five months. The official tally (2,737 originally, then 5,556) grew over time as more separated families were discovered. Hundreds may never have been reunited.

### Mistake 6: "This was a one-administration problem"
Soboroff notes that President Obama deported more people than any president in history. The system that enabled family separation — mass detention, expedited removal, family detention — was built over decades. Trump expanded and weaponized it.

### Mistake 7: "The media exaggerated"
Trump himself said: "I didn't like the sight or the feeling of families being separated." He signed the executive order ending the policy after seeing the images on television. The media didn't exaggerate — the reality was worse than what could be shown on TV.
