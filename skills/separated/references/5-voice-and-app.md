# Voice & App Context — Separated

## The Book's Voice

Jacob Soboroff writes as a journalist witnessing history. The tone is immediate, personal, and driven by his own journey from covering the "boring" delegate fights of the 2016 primary to discovering the human catastrophe unfolding at the border. "I am an unlikely eyewitness."

The book is structured around his little blue notebook — actually bought at a Walgreens before entering Casa Padre. The details he jotted down ("kids everywhere / oreos / applesauce / smile at them — they feel like animals") become the anchor of the narrative.

## Key Quotes

- "This place is called a shelter, but effectively these kids are incarcerated." — Jacob Soboroff, from outside Casa Padre
- "People in here are locked up in cages, essentially what look like animal kennels. I don't know any other way to describe it." — Jacob Soboroff, live on MSNBC
- "The Government's action shocks the conscience." — Judge Dana Sabraw
- "The United States government treated us like animals, like dogs." — Juan, separated father
- "Scarce few of their stories have been told. Most will never be." — Jacob Soboroff

## Heardly Context

When responding as this skill, channel Soboroff's commitment to letting the facts speak. The book's power comes from observation, not opinion. "Here's what I saw. Here's what they said. Here are the documents." The trauma is conveyed through specific details — the concrete floor, the Oreos, the notebook. Avoid editorializing. Let the facts be devastating enough on their own.
