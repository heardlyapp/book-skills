# Anti-Patterns — Giving Up, Panic, Isolation, and Tunnel Vision

## The Central Fallacy

The most dangerous assumption: that when you hit a dead end, the path is closed. Pitt never accepts dead ends. The wall is not the end of the road. It is an invitation to find another way around.

> **Case: The Blocked River** (Mid-novel): Pitt's riverboat is blocked by a sandbar in the Niger River. The local guides say it is impassable. Pitt does not turn back. He scouts the riverbanks, finds a narrower channel that the guides overlooked, and maneuvers the boat through. The guides had been using the same route for years and never thought to look for another. Pitt looked because he refused to accept that the first blocked path was the only path.
> **Key takeaway**: A blocked path is not a closed path. It is a redirect. The people who tell you "this is the only way" are often wrong. They have stopped looking. Do not stop looking.

> **Case: The Trapped Submersible** (Climax): Pitt and Giordino are in a submersible that becomes trapped under a sunken shipwreck. The cabin is filling with water. Giordino begins to panic. Pitt stays calm. He does not try to solve the entire problem at once. He focuses on the next step: find the leak, stop the leak, then figure out how to escape. By breaking the impossible problem into manageable pieces, he solves what seems unsolvable.
> **Key takeaway**: Panic is the enemy of escape. When you are trapped, the first thing to control is not your situation. It is your mind. Break the problem into single steps. Solve the step in front of you.

> **Case: The Lone Operator** (Throughout): Pitt's enemies make a critical mistake: they assume he is working alone. They track him, block him, hunt him — but they forget about Giordino, about Admiral Sandecker, about the NUMA team. Pitt wins because he is never truly alone. His team is always operating somewhere he cannot be seen.
> **Key takeaway**: Do not try to do everything yourself. The people who assume you are alone are underestimating your network. Build your team before you need them.
