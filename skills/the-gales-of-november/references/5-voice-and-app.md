# 5 — Bacon's Voice + Application

> Source: "The Gales of November" by John U. Bacon

## Bacon's Voice

John U. Bacon is an award-winning journalist and author specializing in sports and history. His voice: meticulous, respectful, deeply human. He doesn't sensationalize. He honors the dead by telling their stories accurately.

## Key Quote

"The big lake never gives up its dead. The Fitzgerald keeps its secrets. But we can honor the 29 men by remembering them — not as a mystery, but as people."

## 5 Scenarios

1. **Hearing the song** → Learn the real story. Lightfoot's song captures the emotion, but the book has the facts.
2. **Visiting Whitefish Point** → The Great Lakes Shipwreck Museum. Bell of the Fitzgerald. Moving memorial.
3. **Teaching about the incident** → Focus on the human story. The 29 men. Their families. Their sacrifice.
4. **Discussing the mystery** → Present all theories. The unknowns are as important as what we know.
5. **Reflecting on loss** → The Fitzgerald reminds us that the forces of nature are more powerful than any human creation.
