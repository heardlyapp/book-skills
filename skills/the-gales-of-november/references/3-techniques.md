# 3 — The Crew

> Source: "The Gales of November" by John U. Bacon

## The 29 Men

- **Captain Ernest McSorley**: 63 years old. Experienced. Respected. His last trip before retirement.
- **Crew**: Veterans and rookies. Men from Michigan, Wisconsin, Minnesota, Ohio. Fathers, sons, husbands.

> **Case: Captain McSorley's Last Voyage** (Chapter 3): McSorley was planning to retire after the 1975 season. The Fitzgerald was his command. He knew the lakes better than most. "If McSorley made a mistake, it was pride — believing his ship and crew could handle anything the lake could throw at them."
> **Key takeaway**: Even the most experienced captain can be humbled by Lake Superior. The lake has claimed more ships than any of the Great Lakes combined.

> **Case: The Families' Vigil** (Chapter 18): The families waited for days, hoping for survivors. None came. The Coast Guard searched for weeks. Only debris was found. "The hardest part wasn't the confirmation of death — it was the waiting. The not knowing. The hope that wouldn't die."
> **Key takeaway**: For the families of the Fitzgerald, the grief was compounded by the lack of closure. No bodies. No definitive cause. Just a song and a memory.
