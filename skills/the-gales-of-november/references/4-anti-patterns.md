# 4 — Great Lakes Shipping

> Source: "The Gales of November" by John U. Bacon

## The Great Lakes Maritime Industry

The Great Lakes were the industrial backbone of the Midwest. Iron ore from Minnesota and Michigan fueled American industry. The freighters — "lakers" — carried the ore to mills in Ohio and Pennsylvania.

## The November Gales

November is the most dangerous month on the Great Lakes. Cold air over warm water creates unstable weather. Storms develop rapidly. Waves can exceed 30 feet.

> **Case: The 1975 Storm** (Chapter 4): The storm that sank the Fitzgerald was not exceptional by November standards. "It was a bad storm, but not the worst the Fitzgerald had survived. The question is: what made this storm different? Why did the Fitz go down when it had weathered worse?"
> **Key takeaway**: The storm was severe but survivable. The Fitzgerald had been through worse. The tragedy was not the storm itself, but the combination of factors that came together that night.

> **Case: The Anderson's Survival** (Chapter 6): The Arthur M. Anderson, following behind the Fitzgerald, survived the same storm. "If the Anderson made it, why didn't the Fitzgerald? The Anderson was smaller. It should have been more vulnerable. Unless the Fitzgerald hit something the Anderson didn't."
> **Key takeaway**: The fact that a smaller ship survived while the "unsinkable" Fitzgerald was lost deepens the mystery. It suggests something specific to the Fitzgerald — not the storm alone.
