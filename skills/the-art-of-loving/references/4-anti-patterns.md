> Source: The Art of Loving, Part III

# Anti-Patterns

## 1. Confusing Infatuation with Love

**The mistake**: Believing the intense feeling at the beginning of a relationship is love.

**Fix**: Fromm calls this "falling in love" — a temporary experience of union. Real love is "standing in love" — the ongoing practice of care, responsibility, respect, and knowledge.

> **Case: The Romantic Rush** (Part III): Fromm observes that modern culture romanticizes the initial rush of falling in love. Movies, songs, and media all celebrate this experience. But the rush is not love — it's the excitement of overcoming separateness for the first time. It cannot last. When it fades, people think love is over and move on.
> **Key takeaway**: The initial rush is not love. It's the spark — love is what you build after the spark.

## 2. Treating Love as a Problem of Being Loved

**The mistake**: Focusing on becoming lovable rather than developing the capacity to love.

**Fix**: Shift from "How can I be loved?" to "How can I practice love?" The first question leads to anxiety and performance. The second leads to growth and connection.

## 3. Believing Self-Love Is Selfish

**The mistake**: Sacrificing yourself for others and calling it love.

**Fix**: Self-love is not selfish. It's the foundation. You cannot give what you don't have. To love others well, you must first love yourself.

> **Case: The Martyr Complex** (Part III): Fromm describes people who sacrifice everything for others — their time, energy, identity — and then resent those they "love." Their self-sacrifice is not love but a form of control. It creates obligation in the other person.
> **Key takeaway**: If your "love" makes the other person feel guilty, it's not love. True love frees the other person, does not burden them.

## 4. Expecting Perfection

**The mistake**: Expecting the person you love to meet all your needs perfectly.

**Fix**: Love involves seeing the other person as they are — with their flaws, limitations, and imperfections. Respect means accepting these.

## 5. Avoiding the Work

**The mistake**: Expecting love to be effortless.

**Fix**: Love is an art. It requires practice, discipline, concentration, and patience — like any art worth mastering.
