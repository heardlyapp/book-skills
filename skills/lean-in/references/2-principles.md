# Principles · Negotiation · Mentorship

> Source: *Lean In*, Chapters 5, 7

---

## Chapter 5: Negotiation

"Women often negotiate less than men — and when they do negotiate, they're viewed less favorably."

**The key:** Frame negotiation as advocating for a fair outcome, not for yourself personally. Use "we" instead of "me." Research shows this reduces the "likeability penalty."

> **Case:** Sandberg describes negotiating her compensation package at Facebook. She prepared extensively, knew her market value, and framed the conversation as "making sure this is fair for both sides." She succeeded—and encourages others to do the same.

## Chapter 7: Finding Mentors

"You can't just ask someone to be your mentor. You have to earn it by being impressive, engaged, and prepared."

**The approach:** Do the work first. Then ask for specific advice on a specific problem. If you're smart about using their advice, they'll want to help you again.

> **Case:** Sandberg's own mentor relationships grew organically—she asked for specific feedback on specific situations, not for general mentorship. The specificity made the advice actionable and the relationship valuable.
