# Techniques · Partnership · Planning

> Source: *Lean In*, Chapters 8-9

---

> **Case:** Sandberg shares the story of her own marriage—she and her husband Dave discussed division of household labor explicitly before having children. When he traveled for work, she felt the imbalance acutely. The lesson: these conversations must happen early and be revisited regularly.

## Chapter 8: Make Your Partner a Real Partner

"The most important career choice you'll ever make is who you marry."

Domestic equality: both partners share household responsibilities and childcare. This is not just fair — it's essential for women's career success.

**The practical approach:**
1. Discuss expectations before marriage or children
2. Choose a partner who genuinely believes in equality
3. Let go of perfectionism at home
4. Accept that "good enough" parenting is good enough

## Chapter 9: The Myth of Doing It All
