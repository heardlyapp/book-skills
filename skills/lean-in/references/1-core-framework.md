# Core Framework · The Confidence Gap · Sit at the Table

> Source: *Lean In*, Chapters 1-4

---

## Chapter 2: The Confidence Gap

"Women systematically underestimate their own abilities. If you ask a man and a woman to describe their performance on a test, the man will overestimate his and the woman will underestimate hers."

**The pattern:** Women attribute success to external factors ("I got lucky," "My team helped me") and failure to internal factors ("I'm not good enough"). Men do the opposite.

**The fix:** When you achieve something, say "I earned this." This is not arrogance — it's accuracy.

> **Case:** Sandberg's own story — when Facebook's COO job was offered, she didn't think she was qualified. Mark Zuckerberg had to convince her. She took the job and succeeded.

## Chapter 1: Sit at the Table

"Women need to sit at the table. Not just physically — mentally. Take your place. Own your voice. Don't apologize for being there."

**The physical metaphor:** At meetings, women often sit in chairs against the wall rather than at the conference table. They literally recede. The first step is to physically take your place.

## Chapter 4: Don't Leave Before You Leave

"The time to step back from your career is when you have a real reason — not when someone tells you you should, and not because of a future that may never arrive."

> **Case:** The senior executive who turned down a promotion because she was planning to have kids in 5 years. She "left before she left." When she was ready to have children 5 years later, she had lost the opportunity and the momentum.
