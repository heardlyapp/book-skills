# Anti-Patterns · Internal Barriers

> Source: *Lean In*, Chapters 2-3, 6

---

> **Case:** In a famous Harvard study, students evaluated a case study about a successful entrepreneur. When the entrepreneur was described as male, students rated him as more likeable. When described as female, they rated her as less likeable—even though the story was identical. This is the likeability penalty in action.

## The Likeability Penalty

"Success and likeability are positively correlated for men and negatively correlated for women." A successful man is liked more. A successful woman is liked less.

**Fix:** Be yourself. Authenticity is more powerful than trying to be liked.

## Stereotype Threat

"Fear of confirming a negative stereotype about your group actually impairs performance." When women are reminded of gender stereotypes before a test, they perform worse.

**Fix:** Remind yourself of your competence before entering a stereotype-threatening situation.

## Impostor Syndrome

"Despite all evidence, I felt like a fraud." This is extremely common among high-achieving women. Recognizing it is the first step to overcoming it.

**Fix:** When you feel like an impostor, ask: "What evidence is there that I'm not qualified?" The answer is usually: none.
