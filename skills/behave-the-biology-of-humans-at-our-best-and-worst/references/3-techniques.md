# Techniques for Understanding Behavior

**Technique 1: Expand the Time Scale.** When trying to understand a behavior, do not just ask what caused it in the moment. Ask: what shaped this person seconds, hours, days, years, and generations before?

**Case: The Impulsive Act.** An act of violence may have causes at every level: a frontal cortex that is not fully developed (developmental), high testosterone (hormonal), low glucose from missed lunch (metabolic), past trauma (experiential), and cultural norms that glorify violence (evolutionary/cultural).

**Technique 2: Question Automatic Judgments.** We judge others based on their actions and ourselves based on our intentions. This is a cognitive bias. Extend to others the same understanding you extend to yourself.

**Case: The Road Rage Driver.** When someone cuts you off, you assume they are a bad person. When you cut someone off, you assume you had a good reason. The asymmetry is automatic — and incorrect.

**Technique 3: Look for the Context.** When you see behavior you do not understand, do not ask "what is wrong with that person." Ask "what is the context?"

**Case: The Harsh Judge.** A judge who gives harsh sentences is not necessarily a bad person. He may be hungry, tired, or stressed.
