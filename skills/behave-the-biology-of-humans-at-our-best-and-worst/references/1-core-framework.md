# The Time Scale Framework

Sapolsky's central insight: to understand a behavior, you need to understand its causes at every time scale — from one second before to millennia before.

**Case: The Hitler Fantasy.** Sapolsky opens with his own fantasy of torturing Hitler. He wants to punish him. But then he asks: what caused Hitler to be Hitler? Brain chemistry? Childhood trauma? Culture? Evolution? The more we understand the causes of his behavior, the harder it becomes to simply hate him. This sets up the book's central question: what does it mean to understand behavior?

**Case: The Judicial Ruling Study.** Judges give harsher sentences just before lunch. When they are hungry, their brains are depleted of glucose. They make different decisions. The same judge, the same crime, different outcomes. That is not justice in any meaningful sense — it is biology. Context shapes behavior in ways we do not recognize.

**Case: The Testosterone Paradox.** Testosterone does not cause aggression. It amplifies whatever behavioral tendencies already exist. In a competitive context, testosterone increases aggression. In a prosocial context, it increases prosocial behavior. The hormone is not the cause — the context is.

**Case: The Frontal Cortex and Impulse Control.** The frontal cortex matures last in human development. Adolescents have fully developed emotional centers but underdeveloped impulse control. This is not a moral failing — it is biology.
