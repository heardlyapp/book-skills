# Principles of Behavior

**Principle 1: Context Is Everything.** The same person behaves differently in different contexts. The difference is often situation, not personality. To understand behavior, understand the environment in which it occurs.

**Case: The Stanford Prison Experiment.** Ordinary students became abusive when placed in the role of guards. The context overwhelmed their individual personalities. Good people can do bad things in bad contexts.

**Principle 2: Biology Is Not Destiny.** Understanding the biological basis of behavior does not mean behavior is predetermined. It means we understand the constraints within which choice operates. Knowledge is the first step to change.

**Case: The Frontal Cortex.** The frontal cortex can override automatic responses — but it takes effort, training, and energy. The capacity for control is biological, but so is the capacity for change.

**Principle 3: The Causes Are Multi-Layered.** No single cause explains a behavior. Every behavior has causes at every time scale. Any explanation that focuses on only one level is incomplete.

**Case: The Violent Act.** An act of violence may be caused by: an underdeveloped frontal cortex (adolescence), high testosterone (hormonal), low glucose (metabolic), past trauma (experiential), and cultural norms (evolutionary). All are true simultaneously.
