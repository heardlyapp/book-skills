# Techniques: Understanding QE

## How Quantitative Easing Works

1. Fed creates digital reserves out of nothing
2. Uses reserves to buy government bonds (and later mortgage-backed securities)
3. Lowers long-term interest rates below what markets would set naturally
4. Encourages borrowing, investing, and spending
5. Raises asset prices (stocks, bonds, real estate)

> **Case: The Repo Market Meltdown** (Chapter 10): In September 2019, the overnight lending market (repo market) seized up. Interest rates spiked to 10%. The Fed was forced to intervene with emergency lending. This revealed that years of QE had damaged the plumbing of the financial system.
> **Key takeaway**: QE doesn't just affect visible markets — it changes how the financial system operates at a fundamental level. When the Fed pulls back, hidden problems emerge.

## Key Tools

| Fed Tool | What It Does |
|----------|--------------|
| Federal Funds Rate | Short-term rate target |
| Quantitative Easing | Buying bonds to lower long-term rates |
| Forward Guidance | Signaling future policy intentions |
| Discount Window | Emergency lending to banks |
| Interest on Reserves | Paying banks to hold reserves |

> **Case: Operation Twist** (Chapter 4): The Fed sold short-term bonds and bought long-term bonds to flatten the yield curve. This was a creative but limited tool. It showed the Fed was running out of conventional options.
> **Key takeaway**: The Fed tried increasingly creative strategies as traditional tools proved insufficient. Each new tool brought new risks.
