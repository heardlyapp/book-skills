# Key Principles

## 1. The Fed's Decisions Create Winners and Losers

When the Fed lowers rates and buys bonds, asset prices go up. Stockholders and homeowners benefit. Renters, savers, and workers without assets lose.

## 2. Easy Money Encourages Risky Behavior

"Low rates force investors to take more risk to get any return." This drives money into junk bonds, leveraged buyouts, cryptocurrencies, and meme stocks.

> **Case: The Corporate Debt Bubble** (Chapter 5): With interest rates at zero, companies borrowed trillions through cheap debt. Much of this went to stock buybacks rather than investment. When rates eventually rose, highly leveraged companies faced a debt crisis.
> **Key takeaway**: Cheap money doesn't necessarily fund productive investment. It often funds financial engineering — buybacks, dividends, and M&A — that benefits executives and shareholders but doesn't grow the economy.

## 3. QE Cannot Be Easily Unwound

"Quantitative easing is easy to start and nearly impossible to stop." The Fed's balance sheet grew from $900 billion in 2007 to nearly $9 trillion in 2022. Shrinking it has proven politically and economically difficult.

## 4. Inflation Is the Price of Easy Money

"After years of near-zero rates and trillions in money creation, inflation was inevitable." The question was not if inflation would come, but when.

## 5. Central Bank Independence Is Under Threat

"When the Fed makes politically unpopular decisions — like raising rates to fight inflation — it faces intense pressure from politicians." Maintaining independence is essential for credibility, but increasingly difficult.

> **Case: Trump's Pressure Campaign** (Chapter 8): President Trump publicly attacked Fed Chair Jerome Powell for raising rates in 2018, calling the Fed the "biggest threat" to the economy. This was unprecedented — no modern president had so openly pressured the Fed.
> **Key takeaway**: Central bank independence is not guaranteed. Political attacks on the Fed erode its credibility and make it harder to make tough decisions.
