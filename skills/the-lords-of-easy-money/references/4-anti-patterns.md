# Anti-Patterns

## 1. Believing the Fed Is Neutral

**The mistake**: Thinking the Fed's decisions are purely technical and apolitical.

**Fix**: Every Fed decision creates winners and losers. Choosing to keep rates low benefits borrowers and asset owners at the expense of savers.

## 2. Assuming Inflation Is Dead

**The mistake**: Believing that after 30 years of low inflation, it would never return.

**Fix**: Inflation is not dead — it was dormant. Massive money creation eventually shows up in prices.

> **Case: The MMT Experiment** (Chapter 6): Some economists argued that Modern Monetary Theory proved the government could spend without limit. The Fed's QE seemed to confirm this — until inflation proved there is no free lunch.
> **Key takeaway**: Money creation cannot continue indefinitely without consequences. Inflation is the ultimate constraint.

## 3. Thinking Easy Money Is Free

**The mistake**: Believing low interest rates and QE have no cost.

**Fix**: Easy money has enormous costs — inequality, asset bubbles, financial instability, and eventual inflation.

> **Case: The Zombie Companies** (Chapter 9): Years of near-zero interest rates kept thousands of unprofitable companies alive. "Zombie companies" — firms that couldn't cover their debt payments — survived only because borrowing was free. When rates rose, many collapsed.
>
> **Key takeaway**: Easy money keeps weak companies alive, delaying necessary economic adjustment. The longer they survive, the more painful the eventual correction.

## 4. Trusting the Fed's Models

**The mistake**: Believing the Fed's economic models can predict the future.

**Fix**: The Fed's models failed to predict the 2008 crisis, the 2013 taper tantrum, and the 2021 inflation surge. Models are only as good as their assumptions.

## 5. Ignoring the Fed

**The mistake**: Not paying attention to central bank policy because it seems irrelevant to daily life.

**Fix**: The Fed's decisions affect your mortgage rate, your savings interest, your job prospects, and your rent. Ignoring the Fed is a luxury you can't afford.
