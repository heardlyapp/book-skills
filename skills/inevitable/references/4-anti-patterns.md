# 4 — Anti-Patterns

> Source: *Inevitable* by Mike Colias.

## Common Errors

### 1. The Compliance Car Trap

**The trap:** "Let's make just enough EVs to satisfy regulators, while keeping our real business the same."

**Why it fails:** GM's EV1, Volt, and Bolt were compliance cars — designed to meet regulations, not to win customers. They were dumpy, uninspiring, and didn't learn anything useful. The Hummer EV was the pivot: make something genuinely exciting, even if it's not profitable yet.

> **Case: GM's History of Half-Hearted EVs** (Chapter 1): From the EV1 in the 1990s to the Volt plug-in hybrid to the Bolt, GM's EVs were defined by what they were not: not a real car, not cool, not desirable. "GM was putting forth the automotive equivalent of nurse's shoes." The Hummer EV overcorrected — but that was the point.

### 2. Betting on the Wrong Horse

**The trap:** "We'll skip the EV transition entirely because we have a better technology."

**Why it fails:** Toyota bet on hydrogen fuel cells — a technology with real advantages — but the industry standard was shifting to battery EVs. Being right about the technology doesn't matter if you're wrong about the market.

### 3. Denial About the Speed of Change

**The trap:** "This will take 30 years. We have plenty of time."

**Why it fails:** The transition might take 20 years instead of 10. But the decisions that determine who wins are being made right now. Delay is a decision.

> **Case: The EV Sales Slowdown** (Conclusion): In early 2024, EV sales growth slowed. Industry skeptics declared the EV revolution dead. "EV euphoria is dead," CNBC said. But the long-term trajectory had not changed. The slowdown was a normal market correction, not a reversal. Farley told investors: "The journey on EVs is inevitable."

### 4. Trying to Go It Alone

**The trap:** "We don't need to partner with anyone. We can do this ourselves."

**Why it fails:** Ford and GM both ended up begging Tesla for Supercharger access — the rival they had spent years dismissing. No single company can solve the chicken-and-egg problem alone.

### 5. Ignoring China

**The trap:** "Chinese cars are low quality. Western consumers won't buy them."

**Why it fails:** BYD was roughly the size of Tesla by the mid-2020s. Its cars were competitive on quality, design, and technology. Chinese EVs were already landing in Europe. The threat is not theoretical.
