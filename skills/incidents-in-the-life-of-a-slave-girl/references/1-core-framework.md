# The Slave Girl's Narrative — Core Framework

Harriet Jacobs wrote the first slave narrative by an African American woman, focusing on the unique horrors of slavery for women.

**Case: The Garret. Jacobs hid for seven years in a tiny attic space, 9 feet by 7 feet by 3 feet tall. She could barely move.**

**Case: Dr. Flint. Jacobs's master sexually harassed her for years. He would not let her marry. He treated her body as his property.**

**Case: The Children. Jacobs had two children with Mr. Sands. Dr. Flint threatened to sell them if she did not submit.**

**Case: The Grandmother. Aunt Martha, Jacobs's grandmother, was a free woman who protected Jacobs and her children.**

**Case: The Escape. Jacobs escaped by boat to Philadelphia in 1842. Her freedom was never secure under the Fugitive Slave Act.**
