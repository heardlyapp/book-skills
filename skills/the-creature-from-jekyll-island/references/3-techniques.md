# 3 — Money and Inflation

> Source: "The Creature from Jekyll Island" by G. Edward Griffin

## Fiat vs Gold

Under the gold standard, the dollar was redeemable for a fixed amount of gold. This limited the government's ability to print money. Since 1971 (Nixon ended convertibility), the dollar is fiat — its value comes from government decree.

## Inflation Mechanism

When the Fed creates money faster than the economy grows, prices rise. This is not an accident — it's a transfer of purchasing power from savers to debtors and the government.

> **Case: The 1913 Decision** (Chapter 4): Griffin argues that the creation of the Fed in 1913 was directly linked to the need for a central bank to manage a fiat currency system. "The Fed was created to manage the inevitable inflation that would come from abandoning sound money."
> **Key takeaway**: The Fed and fiat money are two sides of the same coin. You can't have one without the other.

> **Case: The Lost Decade** (1970s): Griffin points to the 1970s as the predictable result of fiat money. "Inflation destroyed the savings of a generation. People who saved their whole lives found their retirement funds worth a fraction of what they expected."
> **Key takeaway**: Inflation is not an act of God. It's a policy choice. The Fed chooses to inflate the money supply, and savers pay the price.
