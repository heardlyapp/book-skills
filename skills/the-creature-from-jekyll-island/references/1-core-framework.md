# 1 — The Secret Meeting

> Source: "The Creature from Jekyll Island" by G. Edward Griffin

## The Jekyll Island Meeting

November 1910. Seven men traveled separately to Jekyll Island, Georgia, a private resort owned by J.P. Morgan. Their purpose: to draft the Federal Reserve Act.

## The Participants

- Senator Nelson Aldrich (RI) — the public front
- Paul Warburg — Kuhn, Loeb & Co. (representing the Rothschild interests)
- Frank Vanderlip — National City Bank (representing Rockefeller)
- Henry Davison — J.P. Morgan & Co.
- Charles Norton — J.P. Morgan-controlled First National Bank
- Benjamin Strong — J.P. Morgan's Bankers Trust
- A. Piatt Andrew — Assistant Secretary of the Treasury

> **Case: The Cover Story** (Chapter 2): The meeting was deliberately kept secret. The participants told their families they were going duck hunting. They used first names only. The resort staff was told they were a private fishing club. The secret held for years.
> **Key takeaway**: The most important financial legislation in American history was drafted in secret by bankers. No public input. No congressional debate. This is Griffin's central charge.

> **Case: The Aldrich Plan** (Chapter 3): Senator Aldrich had previously proposed a central bank that would be publicly controlled. It was defeated. The Jekyll Island plan was different — a privately controlled central bank with a public name. "The Fed was designed to look like a government agency while being controlled by private banks."
> **Key takeaway**: The Fed's hybrid structure is intentional. It gives the appearance of public accountability while serving private interests.
