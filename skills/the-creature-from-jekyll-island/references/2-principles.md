# 2 — How the Fed Works

> Source: "The Creature from Jekyll Island" by G. Edward Griffin

## Fed Operations

**Open Market Operations**: Buying and selling government bonds to expand or contract the money supply.

**Discount Rate**: The interest rate the Fed charges banks for loans.

**Reserve Requirements**: The fraction of deposits banks must hold in reserve.

## The Money Creation Process

When the Fed buys bonds, it creates money out of thin air to pay for them. This new money enters the banking system and gets multiplied through fractional reserve lending.

> **Case: The Alchemy of Banking** (Chapter 5): Griffin explains how banks create money. "When a bank makes a loan, it doesn't lend existing money — it creates new money by crediting the borrower's account. 90% of the money in circulation was created by banks, not by the government."
> **Key takeaway**: Most money is created by private banks when they make loans. This is not widely understood. Money is debt.

> **Case: The Bailout** (Chapter on 2008): Griffin predicted before 2008 that the Fed would bail out major banks. "The Fed exists to protect the banking system, not the public. When banks make bad bets, the Fed creates money to cover their losses."
> **Key takeaway**: The Fed's role as lender of last resort means banks can take risks knowing they'll be bailed out. This is moral hazard built into the system.
