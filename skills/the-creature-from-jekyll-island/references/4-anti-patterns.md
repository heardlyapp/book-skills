# 4 — Anti-Patterns

> Source: "The Creature from Jekyll Island" by G. Edward Griffin

## Anti-Pattern 1: The Fed is Federal

**The mistake:** Believing the Federal Reserve is a government agency. It's a hybrid — private banks own the regional reserves. The Board in DC is appointed, but the shareholders are private banks.

## Anti-Pattern 2: The Fed Prevents Crises

**The mistake:** Believing the Fed was created to prevent bank runs. Griffin argues it was created to manage the banking system for bankers, not to protect the public.

## Anti-Pattern 3: Inflation is Complex

**The mistake:** Believing inflation has many causes. Griffin: "Inflation is always and everywhere a monetary phenomenon."

> **Case: The Panic of 1907** (Chapter 1): The Panic of 1907 was used as the justification for creating the Fed. Griffin argues that the panic was caused by existing monetary manipulation and that the Fed's creation would make such panics worse, not better. "The cure was worse than the disease."
> **Key takeaway**: Crises are often used to justify expansions of government power. The Panic of 1907 was real — but the solution (a central bank) served the bankers who caused it.

> **Case: The Great Depression** (Chapter on 1929): Griffin argues that the Fed caused the Great Depression by expanding credit in the 1920s and then contracting it in 1929. "The boom was Fed-made. The bust was inevitable. And the Fed made the depression worse by letting banks fail."
> **Key takeaway**: If the Fed prevents depressions, why was the Great Depression the worst in history — and why did it happen after the Fed was created?
