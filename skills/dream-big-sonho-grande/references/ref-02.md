# Reference: The Acquisition Strategy

## Overview
3G Capital's acquisition strategy was based on three pillars: identifying undervalued assets, using leverage to finance acquisitions, and applying a disciplined operating system to improve performance.

## Case: The Leveraged Buyout Model
3G's typical LBO involved acquiring a public company using significant debt (60-80% of the purchase price). The target company's cash flow was used to service the debt. 3G would then apply ZBB and other operational improvements to increase cash flow, pay down debt, and generate returns. The model required target companies with strong brands, stable cash flows, and identifiable inefficiencies.

## Case: Zero-Based Budgeting
ZBB was the key operational tool. Every year, every department had to justify every expense from zero. There was no baseline budget. Managers had to prove that each dollar of spending would generate a return. The process was painful for managers used to incremental budgeting. But it drove dramatic cost reductions: InBev cut $1.5 billion in costs in the first year after acquiring Anheuser-Busch.

## Case: The Management Replacement
3G believed that the old management was the main obstacle to value creation. After an acquisition, they quickly replaced senior management with their own people — usually Brazilian executives trained at Garantia or InBev. The new management implemented the 3G operating system: ZBB, performance metrics, flat hierarchies, and a culture of accountability. The transition was rapid and sometimes brutal.
