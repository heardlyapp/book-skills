# Reference: The Management Philosophy

## Overview
The 3G management philosophy was forged at Garantia and refined over decades. It is distinctive, controversial, and extremely effective.

## Case: Garantia's Culture
Banco Garantia was known for being the most competitive and aggressive investment bank in Brazil. It hired only the best and fired the worst. There was no tenure, no seniority, no safety net. Annual performance reviews were brutal. The top performers were paid extraordinarily well; the bottom performers were let go. Garantia was not a pleasant place to work — but it trained some of the best business executives in Brazil.

## Case: Frugality as a Virtue
The founders' personal frugality was legendary. They flew coach, drove ordinary cars, and lived modestly. This was not false modesty — it was a deeply held belief that waste was a moral failing. The same frugality was imposed on the companies they acquired. Corporate jets were sold. Executive perks were eliminated. Hotel allowances were slashed. The message: every dollar belongs to the company, and every dollar must be justified.

## Case: Succession and Talent
3G placed extraordinary emphasis on talent development. The founders believed that the most important asset was people. They invested heavily in recruiting, training, and retaining the best talent. Succession was planned years in advance. When Lemann stepped back, Telles and Sicupira took over. The next generation of leaders was groomed from within. The result was a management pipeline that could operate the 3G system at scale.
