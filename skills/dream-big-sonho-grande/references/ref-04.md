# Reference: The Global Expansion

## Overview
After conquering beer, 3G Capital applied the same playbook to fast food (Burger King) and packaged goods (Heinz, Kraft). The empire expanded beyond beverages.

## Case: Burger King Turnaround
3G acquired Burger King in 2010 for $4 billion, taking the company private. They applied the ZBB operating system, streamlined operations, revitalized the menu, and improved franchisee relations. Within two years, Burger King went public again. The turnaround was one of the fastest in the fast food industry.

## Case: The Heinz Acquisition
In 2013, 3G partnered with Warren Buffett's Berkshire Hathaway to acquire Heinz for $28 billion. It was the largest deal in food industry history. 3G applied its operating system to Heinz, cutting costs and improving margins. The partnership with Buffett was significant: he provided capital and credibility; 3G provided operational expertise.

## Case: Kraft Heinz
In 2015, Heinz reversed into Kraft to form Kraft Heinz, creating the fifth-largest food and beverage company in the world. The $60 billion merger was a financial engineering triumph. But performance has been mixed: cost-cutting improved short-term margins but may have starved the company of investment in brands and innovation.

## Case: The 3G Operating System
Across all acquisitions, 3G applied the same operating system: zero-based budgeting, flat management structures, aggressive performance metrics, and a culture of cost discipline. The system was scalable — it could be applied to any company in any industry. The challenge was adapting it to different cultural and regulatory environments. In Europe and the US, the cost-cutting approach generated labor relations challenges that 3G had not faced in Brazil.
