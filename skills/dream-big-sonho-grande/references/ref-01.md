# Reference: The Founders

## Overview
Jorge Paulo Lemann, Marcel Telles, and Beto Sicupira are the three Brazilian investors behind 3G Capital. Their partnership is one of the most successful in business history.

## Case: Lemann's Background
Lemann was born into a wealthy Brazilian family of Swiss descent. He attended Harvard, where he was a tennis champion, and returned to Brazil to work as an investment banker. He founded Banco Garantia in 1971. Garantia became the most aggressive investment bank in Brazil, known for its ruthless meritocracy and performance culture. Lemann did not inherit his fortune — he built it.

## Case: The Partnership Formation
Lemann met Telles and Sicupira through Garantia. Telles was a brilliant trader; Sicupira was a disciplined operator. The three men discovered they shared the same values: long-term thinking, frugality, and a belief that performance was everything. They formalized their partnership in the 1980s, agreeing to share ownership and decision-making equally. The partnership was the foundation of everything they built.

## Case: The Shared Philosophy
The three founders lived modestly despite their wealth. Lemann flew coach. Telles drove an old car. They believed that frugality was a virtue and that waste was a sin. Their management philosophy was based on meritocracy: the best people should be promoted, regardless of age, background, or tenure. They paid well but demanded exceptional performance. Underperformers were fired quickly.
