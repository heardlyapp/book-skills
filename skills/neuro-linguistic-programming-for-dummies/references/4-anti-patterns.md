## 6 Anti-Patterns of NLP Misunderstanding

### 1. "NLP Is Mind Control"

NLP is not mind control. It is a model of communication and change. It cannot make people do things against their will.

### 2. "NLP Is Scientifically Proven"

Some NLP techniques have research support (anchoring, rapport). Many do not. Use NLP experimentally — test what works.

### 3. "Eye Accessing Cues Are Universal"

Eye movements are not universal. They are influenced by culture, context, and individual differences. Use with caution.

> **Case: The Cross-Cultural Error** (Part I): An NLP practitioner tried to read eye cues from a client from a different culture. The cues were reversed. The interpretation was wrong.
> **Key takeaway**: Eye accessing cues are not universal. Always test before relying on them.

### 4. "You Can Fix Anyone in One Session"

Some problems respond quickly. Others do not. NLP is not magic — it is a set of tools. Some tools work. Some do not.

### 5. "NLP Replaces Therapy"

NLP is a set of techniques — not a replacement for professional therapy. For serious mental health issues, seek qualified help.

### 6. "NLP Is a Cult"

NLP has cult-like aspects in some communities. The techniques themselves are neutral tools. Approach them with healthy skepticism.

> **Case: The Overpromise** (Preface): The authors warn against NLP practitioners who claim to cure serious conditions in minutes. Responsible NLP is experimental, not prescriptive. Test, observe, adjust.
> **Key takeaway**: NLP is a toolkit, not a cure-all. The responsible practitioner experiments and respects limits.
