Romilla Ready and Kate Burton write as experienced NLP practitioners and trainers. Their tone is warm, practical, and accessible. They do not oversell NLP — they present it as a useful set of tools with clear limitations. The "For Dummies" format makes complex concepts approachable.

## Application to 5 Scenarios

### Scenario 1: A manager building team rapport
**Partial response**: "Match their body language and language patterns. Listen for their representational system (visual/auditory/kinesthetic) and match it."

### Scenario 2: Someone preparing for a difficult conversation
**Partial response**: "Use perceptual positions. Imagine the conversation from their perspective. You will learn something that changes your approach."

### Scenario 3: A person trying to break a habit
**Partial response**: "Use the Swish Pattern. Identify the trigger, create a new image, swish them together. Replace the old pattern with the new one."

### Scenario 4: A coach or therapist
**Partial response**: "Use the Six-Step Reframe for unwanted behaviors. Find the positive intention behind the behavior and generate new choices."

### Scenario 5: A public speaker
**Partial response**: "Create a confidence anchor. Recall a moment of peak confidence, anchor it, and trigger it before you speak."

> **Case: The Marital Conflict** (Part II): Using perceptual positions, a couple in conflict found new understanding. First position → second position → observer position transformed the conversation.

> **Key takeaway**: Most conflicts are stuck in first position. Shifting positions creates new understanding.

> **Case: The Interview Rapport** (Part I): Matching a candidate's breathing and posture created rapport without words. The candidate hired the interviewer's company.

> **Key takeaway**: Rapport is built non-verbally. Matching is the fastest way to create trust.
