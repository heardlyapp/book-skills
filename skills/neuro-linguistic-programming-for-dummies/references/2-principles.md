## 7 Principles of Neuro-Linguistic Programming

### 1. The Map Is Not the Territory

Your perception of reality is not reality. It is a map. Change the map, change the experience.

### 2. People Have All the Resources They Need

NLP presupposes that every person has the resources they need to change. The therapist's job is to help them access those resources.

> **Case: The Resource Anchor** (Part III): A client who lacked confidence was helped to recall a past confidence experience and anchor it. The anchor allowed them to access confidence on demand.
> **Key takeaway**: The resource is already there — you just need to learn how to access it. Anchoring makes resources available when needed.

### 3. There Is No Failure, Only Feedback

Mistakes are not failures. They are data. NLP encourages experimentation: try something, get feedback, adjust.

### 4. The Meaning of Communication Is the Response You Get

If your message is not received as intended, the responsibility is yours to adjust. Blaming the listener is not effective.

### 5. Behind Every Behavior Is a Positive Intention

Even unwanted behaviors serve a positive purpose (protecting, connecting, surviving). The Six-Step Reframe accesses this intention and finds better ways to meet it.

### 6. People Work Perfectly

No one is broken. People are making the best choices available to them based on their current map of reality. Expand the map, expand choice.

### 7. Mind and Body Are One System

Mental states affect physical states. Physical states affect mental states. Change one, change the other.

> **Case: The Phobia Cure Case** (Part IV): A woman with a 20-year phobia of spiders was treated using the NLP phobia cure. In one session, the phobia was eliminated. The technique changed the structure of the memory — from close/colorful to distant/black-and-white.
> **Key takeaway**: The phobia was not "cured" by understanding its cause — it was cured by changing its structure.
