# 2 — Key Principles

> Source: *The Demon-Haunted World* by Carl Sagan — Chapters 1-2, 10-12.

## The 7 Principles

### 1. The Burden of Proof is on the Claimant

You do not need to disprove a claim to have good reason to doubt it. The person making the claim bears the responsibility of providing evidence. "What's the difference between an invisible, incorporeal, floating dragon and no dragon at all?"

### 2. Science is Self-Correcting

The built-in machinery of science — peer review, replication, public debate, error bars — is its greatest strength. "Every time a scientific paper presents data, it is accompanied by an error bar — a quiet but insistent reminder that no knowledge is complete or perfect."

> **Case: The Failure of Newtonian Physics** (Chapter 2): Newton's laws were enormously successful for 200+ years. Scientists didn't stop testing them. They found they broke down at high speeds and strong gravities. Einstein's Relativity replaced them. "Newtonian physics turns out to be an approximation to the truth." The error-correction machinery worked.
> **Key takeaway**: The willingness to overturn even the most successful theory is science's greatest strength.

> **Case: The Binary Pulsar Test** (Chapter 2): Joseph Taylor and Russell Hulse tested General Relativity by observing two pulsars in orbit. For all they knew, the results would have overturned one of the chief pillars of modern physics.
> **Key takeaway**: Every scientific theory is held provisionally and tested continuously — even the most successful ones. Science does not permit sacred truths.

### 3. Skepticism is Not Cynicism

Skepticism is "the careful, provisional suspension of judgment." It is a tool for finding truth, not a refusal to believe. Sagan was not a cynic — he was a wonderer who demanded evidence.

### 4. Authority is Not Evidence

"One of the great commandments of science is: Mistrust arguments from authority." Authorities have been wrong about everything from the shape of the Earth to the cause of disease.

### 5. The Simpler Explanation is Usually Right

Ockham's Razor: "If there are two explanations that explain the data equally well, choose the simpler one." The simpler explanation for the Face on Mars is geology, not an ancient Martian civilization.

### 6. Science and Democracy Are Allied

"Both science and democracy encourage unconventional opinions and vigorous debate. Both demand adequate reason, coherent argument, rigorous standards of evidence and honesty." Scientific literacy is essential for democratic citizenship.

### 7. Wonder and Skepticism Must Coexist

"Skepticism without wonder is cynicism. Wonder without skepticism is gullibility." Sagan's unique gift was to embody both.
