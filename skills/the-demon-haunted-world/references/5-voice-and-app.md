# 5 — Voice & App

> Source: *The Demon-Haunted World* by Carl Sagan — Preface, voice, and scenarios.

## Sagan's Voice

1. **Poet of science.** Sagan made science beautiful. His prose soars. "The nitrogen in our DNA, the calcium in our teeth, the iron in our blood, the carbon in our apple pies were made in the interiors of collapsing stars. We are made of starstuff."
2. **Skeptic and wonderer simultaneously.** He debunked pseudoscience without ever losing his sense of awe. He is the perfect counterexample to the idea that skeptics are joyless.
3. **Worried but not despairing.** The book is a warning, not a surrender. "The candle flickers. Darkness gathers. The demons begin to stir." But he believed in the power of reason and science to prevail.

> **Case: The 1939 World's Fair** (Preface): Sagan's parents took him to the New York World's Fair at age 5. There he saw "the World of Tomorrow" — the oscilloscope, the photocell, the time capsule. "Plainly the world held wonders of a kind I had never guessed." His parents knew nothing about science. They gave him skepticism and wonder. The fair gave him a vision of the future. All three shaped his life.
> **Key takeaway**: You don't need to be a scientist to give a child curiosity and skepticism.

> **Case: The Four-Year-Old's "God"** (Chapter 1): Sagan's four-year-old son saw the Maharishi Mahesh Yogi on television. "You know who that is? God." Sagan didn't argue with his son. He respected the moment — but the anecdote illustrates how easily children absorb supernatural claims from culture. The antidote is not to shield children from the world but to teach them the tools to evaluate claims.
> **Key takeaway**: Children are born with a natural inclination toward wonder. The demons — credulity, superstition — are learned from adults. We must teach wonder AND skepticism.

## Five Scenarios

### 1. The Person Evaluating a New Health Claim

Run the Baloney Detection Kit. Ask: who paid for the study? Was it peer-reviewed? Has it been replicated? Is there an independent confirmation?

### 2. The Person Whose Loved One Believes in Conspiracies

Don't argue facts. Stay connected. Use the Dragon Test: what would change their mind? Meet emotional needs, don't fight them.

### 3. The Student Learning Science

Science is not memorization. It is a way of thinking. The Baloney Detection Kit is more valuable than any fact in a textbook.

### 4. The Person Who Thinks Science Is Soulless

Read Sagan on starstuff. Read Sagan on the marriage of skepticism and wonder. Science is not cold — it is the deepest source of awe.

### 5. The Citizen in a Democracy

Scientific literacy is not optional. You make decisions about climate, health, technology. You must know how to evaluate evidence. The candle is democracy's light.
