# Techniques — Midnight in Chernobyl

## How to Prevent Nuclear Disasters (Based on Adam Higginbotham)

### Technique 1: Design for Failure, Not Ideology
The RBMK was designed to produce plutonium — not to be safe. "The reactor was a bomb, and nobody had realized it." The technique: every safety system must be independent of the operating system. Case: The RBMK's control rods were designed to *increase* reactivity before decreasing it — a catastrophic failure. Modern reactors design for "negative void coefficient" — reactivity naturally decreases if anything goes wrong.

### Technique 2: The 700 MWt Minimum
The test required the reactor to run at low power. But the RBMK was dangerously unstable below 700 MWt. The operators let it drop to 200 MWt. The technique: know your system's safe operating range and never operate outside it. Case: "At low power, the reactor became uncontrollable."

### Technique 3: Question the Test
"The experiment was poorly designed from the start." The turbine generator test could have been simulated without the reactor. The technique: before running any high-risk test, ask: "Can we test this without risking the entire system?" Case: If the test had been run on a simulator, Chernobyl would never have happened.

### Technique 4: Tell the Truth Fast
Gorbachev wasn't told for three days. Pripyat wasn't evacuated for 36 hours. The delay cost lives. Case: The population of Pripyat received significant radiation doses during those 36 hours. "The cloud did not respect borders." The technique: in any emergency, tell the truth immediately — secrecy always makes things worse.

### Technique 5: Protect the First Responders
Pravik and his firefighters arrived in short-sleeved shirts. No dosimeters. No respirators. The technique: first responders to any industrial accident must assume the worst until proven otherwise. Case: Pravik's entire crew died because they didn't know they needed protection.

### Technique 6: Independent Safety Review
The RBMK was approved by the same organization that designed it. No independent body reviewed its safety. The technique: every major engineering project requires independent safety review by a body with no stake in the outcome. Case: Western nuclear regulators consistently flagged the RBMK's design flaws — but Soviet reviewers did not.

### Technique 7: Listen to Whistleblowers
After the disaster, Valery Legasov testified truthfully about what went wrong. The system tried to silence him. He eventually committed suicide, leaving behind tapes that told the full story. The technique: protect people who tell the truth about failures. "Those who most clearly saw the system's flaws were punished for their honesty."
