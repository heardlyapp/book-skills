# Voice and Application: Hitchens' Approach

> Source: The Portable Atheist, throughout

## Hitchens' Voice as Editor

Hitchens is the curator and guide. His introductions place each writer in context. His own voice is sharp, erudite, and passionately committed to reason.

> **Case: The Introduction's Argument** (Introduction): Hitchens sets the tone: atheism is as old as civilization. He celebrates the courage of freethinkers who risked persecution for honesty.

> **Case: The Primo Levi Opening** (Introduction): Hitchens opens with Levi in Auschwitz — a man who refused prayer at the moment of death. This frame establishes atheism as a moral position, not just an intellectual one.

> **Key takeaway**: Hitchens is not neutral. He advocates passionately for reason against religion.

## Application: Critical Thinking

Apply the burden of proof test. Who's making the claim? What's the evidence?

## Application: Historical Perspective

Freethought has a 2000-year tradition. Atheism is not modern.

## Application: Meaningful Life

Meaning comes from relationships, creativity, and contribution — not divine purpose.
