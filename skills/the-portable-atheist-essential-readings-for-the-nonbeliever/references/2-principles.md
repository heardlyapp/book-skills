# Principles: Key Arguments Against Religion

> Source: The Portable Atheist, Chapters 11-25

## The Problem of Evil

The strongest argument against an omnipotent, benevolent God. If God is all-powerful and all-good, why does unnecessary suffering exist?

> **Case: Darwin's Torment** (Chapter 11): Darwin was horrified by the cruelty of nature — ichneumon wasps that lay eggs in living caterpillars. This suffering was incompatible with a benevolent creator. He couldn't reconcile it.

> **Key takeaway**: The problem of evil is not just philosophical. It's empirical — we can observe unnecessary suffering daily.

## Faith as Intellectual Dishonesty

Many contributors argue that faith — belief without evidence — is not a virtue but intellectual dishonesty.

> **Case: Hitchens' Argument** (Introduction): Hitchens argues that claiming "it's a matter of faith" is an admission that the claim can't be supported by evidence. In no other area of life would this be acceptable.

> **Key takeaway**: Faith is not a valid method for determining truth. Evidence and reason are the only reliable guides.

## Scripture as Human Document

The Bible, Quran, and other scriptures contain errors, contradictions, and moral atrocities that reflect human origins.

> **Case: Mark Twain's Critique** (Chapter 14): Twain satirizes the Biblical God — capricious, vengeful, and absurd. The God of the Old Testament is a human projection.

> **Key takeaway**: Religious texts are human documents, not divine revelation. They reflect the time and place of their creation.
