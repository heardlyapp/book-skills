# Core Framework: The Atheist Tradition

> Source: The Portable Atheist, Introduction and Chapters 1-10

## Hitchens' Framing

The anthology opens with Hitchens arguing that atheism is as old as religion. He collects the best skeptical arguments from 2000 years of freethought.

> **Case: Primo Levi's Testimony** (Introduction): Hitchens opens with Primo Levi in Auschwitz — facing imminent death, he refused to pray. Not from doubt but from certainty: "One does not change the rules of the game at the end of the match."

> **Key takeaway**: Atheism is not comfortable. It's an honest intellectual stance in response to the world as it is.

## Lucretius and the Atomic World

Earliest text: Lucretius' De Rerum Natura (~50 BCE). A universe of atoms, no gods.

> **Case: Lucretius' Nature** (Chapter 1): Lucretius described atoms moving in void, everything governed by natural laws. No divine intervention. The scientific worldview, 2000 years before modern science confirmed it.

> **Case: Hume on Miracles** (Chapter 5): Hume argued it's always more likely someone is mistaken than that a miracle occurred. Evidence for miracles is weaker than evidence for natural law.

> **Case: Darwin's Doubt** (Chapter 11): Darwin described how his faith slowly eroded as he studied nature. The cruelty of the natural world was incompatible with divine benevolence.

> **Key takeaway**: The atheist tradition is ancient and consistent. The arguments — evidence, reason, and the problem of evil — have remained the same for millennia.
