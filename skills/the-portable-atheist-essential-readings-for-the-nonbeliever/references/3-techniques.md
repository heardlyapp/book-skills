# Techniques: Science, Morality, and Meaning

> Source: The Portable Atheist, Chapters 26-40

## Science Explains What Religion Claimed

Lightning, disease, the origin of species and the cosmos — all once attributed to God now have natural explanations.

> **Case: Sagan's Pale Blue Dot** (Chapter 35): Sagan shows that the universe is awe-inspiring without a creator. Our planet is a mote of dust in a vast cosmos. This perspective is more wonderful than any creation myth.

> **Key takeaway**: Science deepens wonder. The universe is amazing because of how it works, not despite scientific understanding.

## Morality Without Religion

Ethical behavior does not require divine command. Humans have evolved moral instincts and social reasoning.

> **Case: Humanist Ethics** (Chapter 38): Morality based on empathy, reason, and human flourishing produces better outcomes than divine command. We don't need threats of hell or promises of heaven to be good to each other.

> **Case: The Golden Rule** (Chapter 38): The principle of treating others as you'd like to be treated exists in every culture — it's universal. Religion codifies it but didn't invent it.

> **Key takeaway**: Morality is innate and social. Religion co-opts pre-existing moral instincts but doesn't create them.

## Meaning Without God

Life's meaning is created, not found in divine purpose.

> **Case**: The anthology closes with arguments that meaning comes from relationships, creativity, and contributing to human flourishing.
