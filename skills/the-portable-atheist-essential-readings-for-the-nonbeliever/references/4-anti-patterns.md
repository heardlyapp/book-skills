# Anti-Patterns: Misunderstanding Atheism

> Source: The Portable Atheist, throughout

## Atheism Is a Faith

The most persistent misconception: atheism requires as much faith as religion. This is logically false.

> **Case: The Burden of Proof** (Introduction): Hitchens argues the default is atheism. The theist makes a positive claim. The atheist says "your evidence is insufficient." That's not faith — that's skepticism. Faith is believing without evidence; atheism is withholding belief.

> **Key takeaway**: Atheism is not a faith. It's the absence of belief in the absence of sufficient evidence.

## Atheists Can't Be Moral

The unfounded assumption that without God, there can be no basis for morality.

> **Case: Secular Ethics** (Chapter 38): Multiple contributors argue that ethics based on reason, empathy, and consequences is more consistent and humane than divine command ethics. We don't need threats of hell to be good.

> **Key takeaway**: Atheists are not less moral. Morality is grounded differently — in reason and empathy rather than obedience to authority.

## Atheism Leads to Nihilism

The claim that without belief in God, life has no meaning or purpose.

> **Case: The Full Life** (Chapter 40): The anthology's closing selections show atheists finding deep meaning in relationships, creativity, nature, and contributing to human flourishing. Meaning is created, not handed down.

> **Key takeaway**: Atheism doesn't lead to despair. Life's meaning is found in life itself — in love, work, creativity, and connection.
