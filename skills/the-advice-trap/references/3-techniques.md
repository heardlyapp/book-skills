# Techniques: Coaching Conversations

## Taming the Advice Monster

1. Notice when you feel the urge to advise — that's the Advice Monster stirring
2. Pause — take a breath before speaking
3. Ask a question instead — start with "What's on your mind?"
4. Listen — really listen, without planning your response
5. Follow with "And what else?" — keep curiosity alive

## The 7 Coaching Questions in Practice

1. **What's on your mind?** — The opening question. Any topic, any issue.
2. **And what else?** — The follow-up. Ask it until they run out of answers.
3. **What's the real challenge here?** — Dig deeper beneath the surface issue.
4. **What do you want?** — Get clear on the desired outcome.
5. **What are you assuming?** — Challenge limiting beliefs.
6. **What needs to be said?** — Address the elephant in the room.
7. **What could you do to move forward?** — Create accountability.

> **Case: The One-Question Meeting** (Chapter 8): A manager tried using just one question per meeting: "What's on your mind?" followed by "And what else?" until the employee was done. Meetings went from 30 minutes of the manager talking to 20 minutes of the employee talking. The manager learned more in less time.
> **Key takeaway**: You don't need a system of questions. Start with just the first two: "What's on your mind?" and "And what else?" Master those before adding more.

> **Case: The Silence That Changed Everything** (Chapter 9): A leader asked her employee "What do you want?" and then stayed silent. The 15-second pause felt like an eternity. But the employee used it to think and came up with an answer that surprised them both. The silence was where the insight happened.
>
> **Key takeaway**: Silence is uncomfortable but productive. After asking a question, wait at least 10 seconds before speaking. The answer is worth the wait.

## Building the Coaching Habit

- Start your day by asking one coaching question
- Before giving advice, pause and ask one question
- End conversations by asking "What's your next step?"
- Practice with low-stakes situations first
- Reflect: "When did my Advice Monster show up today?"
