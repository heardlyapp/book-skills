# Reference: Society

## Overview
Open marriage carries a stigma. Coming out to friends, family, and colleagues requires courage.

## Case: Coming Out to Friends
Molly told a few close friends. Some were supportive. Others were confused or judgmental. The fear of judgment was constant.

## Case: The Stigma
Open marriage is still widely misunderstood and judged. People assume it signals a failing marriage, promiscuity, or emotional immaturity. Molly had to develop resilience to others' opinions.

## Case: The Loneliness
There is no community for people in open marriages the way there is for polyamorous or queer communities. Molly felt isolated. She found support in therapy and in a few close friends.

## Case: Living Openly
Over time, Molly and Stewart became more open about their arrangement. The honesty was liberating. Some relationships deepened. Others ended. Living openly was a choice that brought freedom and consequences.

## Case: The Therapy
Therapy was essential. Molly's therapist helped her navigate the emotional complexity of open marriage. Therapy provided a space to explore feelings without judgment.

## Case: The Cost of Honesty
Some relationships ended when Molly and Stewart came out. Friends who disapproved pulled away. The honesty was necessary but costly. They accepted this as part of the journey.
Molly concludes that honesty was worth the cost. Living authentically required it.
She writes: 'The people who mattered did not mind, and the people who minded did not matter.'
