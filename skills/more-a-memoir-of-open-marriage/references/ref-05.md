# Reference: The Aftermath

## Overview
The open marriage experiment did not last forever. Molly reflects on what she learned.

## Case: The End
Eventually, Molly and Stewart closed their marriage. The experiment had run its course. They had learned what they needed to learn. The marriage was stronger for the journey.

## Case: The Lessons
Molly learned that love is not a limited resource. She learned that she could survive her deepest fears. She learned that honesty is the only path to intimacy. She learned that growth is painful but worth it.

## Case: The Marriage After
The marriage that emerged from the open experiment was different. More honest. More intentional. More aware of what it meant to choose each other every day. The open period had permanently changed them.

## Case: The Recommendation
Molly does not recommend open marriage for everyone. She says it is the hardest thing she ever did. But for those who need to explore, who are willing to do the work, it can be transformative. The journey is the destination.

## Case: The Book
Writing the book was part of Molly's process. Telling her story publicly was an act of vulnerability. She hoped it would help others who were struggling with the same questions.

## Case: The Legacy
The experiment changed how Molly understands love, marriage, and herself. She no longer believes in one path for everyone. She believes in honesty, growth, and the courage to live authentically.
This is the gift of the journey: knowing yourself more deeply than you thought possible.
