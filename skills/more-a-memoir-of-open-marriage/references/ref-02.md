# Reference: Jealousy and Compersion

## Overview
Jealousy is the central emotional challenge of open marriage. Molly's account of learning to manage jealousy is the heart of the book.

## Case: The Jealousy Monster
Molly describes the physical sensation of jealousy: a knot in her stomach, a racing heart, a feeling of rage and fear. When Stewart began dating, she discovered depths of jealousy she did not know she had. She learned that jealousy must be felt, not avoided.

## Case: Compersion
Compersion — feeling joy at your partner's joy with someone else — is the opposite of jealousy. Molly experienced it rarely at first, then more often as she did the work of understanding her emotions.

## Case: The Work of Self-Knowledge
Open marriage forced Molly to confront her insecurities. Why was she jealous? What did she fear? The answers were painful: fear of abandonment, fear of inadequacy, fear that she was not enough. Facing these fears was the hardest work she ever did.

## Case: The Fear of Abandonment
Molly discovered that her jealousy was rooted in a deep fear that Stewart would leave her for someone else. Open marriage forced her to face this fear directly. She learned that love is not ownership and that Stewart's desire for others did not diminish his love for her.

## Case: The Growth
Over time, the jealousy softened. Molly learned to trust Stewart and herself. She learned that she could survive the painful feelings. The confidence she gained extended beyond the marriage.
