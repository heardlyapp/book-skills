# Reference: Logistics

## Overview
Beyond emotions, open marriage requires managing practical challenges. Scheduling, communication, and boundaries are the mechanics.

## Case: The Calendar
Open marriage requires a calendar. Date nights with Stewart, date nights with other partners, family time, alone time. The logistics were exhausting. Molly describes the feeling of running from one commitment to another, never having enough time for anyone, including herself.

## Case: Boundaries
Boundaries are the rules that make open marriage safe. Some boundaries were simple (use protection). Others were complex (not in our bed, not with friends, not on holidays). Boundaries evolved. What worked at the beginning failed later.

## Case: The Marriage Priority
The most important rule: the marriage comes first. If a date conflicted with a marriage need, the date was canceled. If a relationship threatened the marriage, it ended. This was not always easy. Molly had to end relationships she cared about because they were destabilizing her marriage.

## Case: Safe Sex
Safe sex was non-negotiable. Testing, condoms, and honest conversations about sexual health. The practicalities of safe sex in non-monogamy require constant attention.

## Case: The Children
Molly and Stewart have children. They were careful to protect their children from the details of their arrangement. The marriage appeared conventional from the outside. This added a layer of complexity: the need for privacy and discretion.
Discretion was essential.
