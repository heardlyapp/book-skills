# Reference: The Decision

## Overview
Molly and her husband Stewart faced a choice: stay in a conventional marriage that felt increasingly confining, or explore something more.

## Case: Why Open Marriage?
Molly felt a restless desire for something beyond the domestic routine. She loved Stewart but wanted to experience parts of herself that marriage could not contain. The decision was not about dissatisfaction with Stewart but about a desire for growth and exploration.

## Case: Stewart's Hesitation
Stewart was reluctant. He did not need or want other partners. But he loved Molly and wanted her to be happy. They negotiated rules, boundaries, and a framework for honesty. Stewart's willingness to try was an act of trust and love.

## Case: The First Date
Molly describes her first date outside the marriage: the excitement, the guilt, the strange feeling of coming home to Stewart afterward. The experience was both liberating and destabilizing. The marriage would never be the same.

## Case: The Rules
They agreed on rules: always use protection, tell each other about new partners, no secrets, put the marriage first. The rules evolved as they learned what worked and what did not.

## Case: The First Crush
Molly developed a crush on someone she met. The intensity of new relationship energy (NRE) was intoxicating. She had to learn to manage her feelings and communicate honestly without damaging her marriage. The NRE was both the most exciting and most dangerous aspect of open marriage.
