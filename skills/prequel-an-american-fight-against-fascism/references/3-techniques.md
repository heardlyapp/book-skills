# Techniques — Prequel: An American Fight Against Fascism

## How the Antifascist Resistance Actually Worked

### Technique 1: The Spy Network (Leon Lewis Model)
Leon Lewis, an attorney in Los Angeles, built a volunteer spy network that infiltrated every major fascist group in Southern California from 1933 onwards. His spies attended meetings, took notes, and reported to law enforcement. Case: "Lewis's network operated for over a decade, providing crucial intelligence that disrupted multiple plots."

### Technique 2: Congressional Exposure (Dillard Stokes)
Washington Post reporter Dillard Stokes had a knack for being "in the right place at the right time." He exposed Congressional collaboration with Nazi agents by following paper trails and cultivating sources inside the Capitol. Case: "Stokes broke the story of Viereck's Congressional propaganda network."

### Technique 3: The Undercover Reporter (John Metcalfe)
John C. Metcalfe, a German American reporter, went undercover to investigate Nazis all across America. He attended their meetings, earned their trust, and published detailed exposés. Case: "Metcalfe provided the first detailed map of Nazi activity in the United States."

### Technique 4: The Prosecutorial Bulldog (Maloney & Rogge)
William Power Maloney and O. John Rogge used the legal system to fight fascism. They pursued charges of sedition, conspiracy, and foreign agent registration. Case: "Maloney was 'hard-charging and cantankerous' — exactly what the situation required."

### Technique 5: Hollywood's Counter-Programming
The film industry produced antifascist films to counter Nazi propaganda. Warners' "Confessions of a Nazi Spy" (1939) was the first major Hollywood film to take on Nazism directly. Case: "Hollywood used its greatest power — storytelling — to expose the threat."

### Technique 6: The Nazi Agent Capture (Deatheridge)
George Deatherage sought advice from the German embassy and got it. His capture by the FBI demonstrated how German intelligence was actively supporting American fascist groups. Case: "The Deatherage case proved direct German government involvement in American fascist organizing."

### Technique 7: The Sedition Trial
In 1944, the Justice Department brought 30 fascist leaders to trial on charges of sedition. The largest sedition trial in American history. Though it ended in mistrial, it exposed the full extent of the conspiracy. Case: "The trial was chaotic and ultimately failed — but it seriously weakened the movement."
