# Anti-Patterns — Prequel: An American Fight Against Fascism

## The Central Error: "Fascism Could Never Happen in America"

The entire book is a refutation of this assumption. "It did happen. It was homegrown. It almost succeeded." Case: "There were millions of American fascist sympathizers in the 1930s. They had friends in high places."

### Mistake 1: "It Was Just a German Plot"
"American fascism was entirely manufactured by German agents." The truth: German agents exploited existing American movements. The Silver Shirts, Father Coughlin, Huey Long — these were homegrown. Case: "Lawrence Dennis wrote The Coming American Fascism — an original American work."

### Mistake 2: "The Press Would Have Warned Us"
"Journalists would expose any fascist threat." Some did (Dillard Stokes, John Metcalfe). But many newspapers were owned by isolationists or fascist sympathizers. Case: "Henry Ford's newspaper published the Protocols of the Elders of Zion."

### Mistake 3: "Congress Would Never Help Fascists"
"Members of Congress wouldn't collaborate with Nazi agents." They did. Senators Lundeen, Reynolds, Wheeler, and Representative Hamilton Fish all had direct dealings with Nazi agents. Case: "Viereck's propaganda was distributed via Congressional franking privileges."

### Mistake 4: "The System Would Justice Work"
"The justice system stopped the fascists." The sedition trial ended in a mistrial. Most defendants walked free. Viereck was convicted but served minimal time. Case: "The largest sedition trial in American history ended in failure."

### Mistake 5: "The Threat Ended in 1945"
"After WWII, American fascism was defeated." The ideas didn't disappear. Many participants returned to public life. The same conspiratorial antisemitism and anti-democratic ideas resurfaced in later decades. Case: "Every tactic the 1930s fascists used — direct mail, radio, conspiracy theories — has a modern equivalent."

### Mistake 6: "Modern Parallels Are a Stretch"
"Comparing today's politics to 1930s fascism is unfair." Maddow argues the exact opposite: the parallels are specific and undeniable. Direct mail evolved into social media algorithms. Coughlin's radio broadcasts evolved into podcast demagogues. Case: "The same isolationist arguments used against WWII are used today against NATO."
