## Philosophy — 5 rules to remember

1. **Grit is the one quality that separates those who succeed from those who don't.** Talent is overrated. Persistence is everything.
2. **Discipline is doing what needs to be done, even when you don't feel like it.** Motivation comes and goes. Discipline stays.
3. **Adversity is not a sign to stop — it's a test.** Every obstacle is an opportunity to prove your grit.
4. **Your attitude determines your altitude.** A positive mindset is not denial — it's a choice to focus on what you can control.
5. **There are no shortcuts.** The old school rules — hard work, persistence, discipline, integrity — have never changed, and they never will.

## Core Framework Quick Reference

- **Grit** = The combination of passion and perseverance for long-term goals. More important than talent, IQ, or luck.
- **The 9 Timeless Rules** = The book's core framework: 1) Work Hard 2) Never Quit 3) Be Disciplined 4) Stay Positive 5) Take Responsibility 6) Keep Learning 7) Help Others 8) Be Honest 9) Have Faith.
- **The Grind** = The daily, unglamorous work that produces results over time. There is no substitute.
- **Mental Toughness** = The ability to perform under pressure, handle adversity, and stay focused on what matters.
- **The Attitude Choice** = You cannot control what happens to you, but you can control how you respond. That choice determines everything.

## Key Principles

1. **Grit beats talent every time.** Consistent effort over time produces results that talent alone cannot.
2. **Discipline is a habit, not a trait.** Build it like a muscle — start small and increase the load.
3. **Adversity reveals character.** How you respond to difficulty defines who you are.
4. **The only failure is quitting.** Setbacks are not failures. Stopping is.
5. **Attitude is a choice.** You can't control the situation, but you control your response.
6. **There are no shortcuts.** The old school rules have worked for generations because they are true.
7. **Success is a byproduct of the person you become.** Focus on becoming the right person, and success follows.
