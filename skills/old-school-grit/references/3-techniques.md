# Techniques: Building Grit Daily

## The Grit Habit Loop

1. Set a meaningful long-term goal — something that matters deeply to you
2. Break it into daily non-negotiable actions — the small things you WILL do every day
3. When you don't feel like it, do it anyway — this is where grit is built
4. When setbacks come, ask: "What can I learn from this?" — not "Why me?"
5. Celebrate small wins — recognize progress, not just results

## The Daily Discipline Practice

- Morning: Write down ONE thing you will complete today, no matter what
- Throughout the day: When you want to quit, do one more rep, read one more page, work 10 more minutes
- Evening: Review — what did I do today that required discipline? What did I avoid?

## The Attitude Shift Technique

When negativity hits:
1. Pause and notice: "I'm choosing a negative interpretation"
2. Ask: "What's one positive thing about this situation?"
3. Ask: "What CAN I control right now?"
4. Take action on what you can control

> **Case: The Four-Minute Mile** (Chapter 8): For years, runners believed the four-minute mile was impossible. Then Roger Bannister broke it in 1954. Within months, multiple runners broke it. The barrier was not physical — it was mental. The same principle applies to nearly every limitation we accept.
> **Key takeaway**: Many of our limits are self-imposed. The first step to breaking through is believing it's possible.

> **Case: The Recovered Addict** (Chapter 9): A man who had lost everything to addiction used the Grit Habit Loop to rebuild his life. One day at a time, one small discipline at a time, he created a new identity. Years later, he was helping others do the same.
>
> **Key takeaway**: Grit can be built even in the most difficult circumstances. It's never too late to start.

## The Never-Quit Protocol

1. When you want to quit, wait 24 hours before making a final decision
2. During those 24 hours, ask: "Why did I start this in the first place?"
3. Ask: "Will I regret quitting more than I'll regret continuing?"
4. Make the decision — not in the heat of the moment, but after reflection
