# Anti-Patterns

## 1. Looking for Shortcuts

**The mistake**: Believing there's a faster, easier path to success.

**Fix**: The old school rules have never changed because they are the only path that works. Shortcuts are mirages.

> **Case: The Get-Rich-Quick Trap** (Chapter 1): A young professional jumped from scheme to scheme — network marketing, crypto, real estate flips. Each promised easy money. Each failed. He was chasing shortcuts while his friends were building real careers. The shortcuts cost him years.
> **Key takeaway**: If it promises something for nothing, it's a trap. Real success requires real work.

## 2. Quitting on a Bad Day

**The mistake**: Making permanent decisions based on temporary emotions.

**Fix**: The Never-Quit Protocol: wait 24 hours before deciding to quit. The heat of the moment is the worst time to make a life decision.

## 3. Excuses Over Responsibility

**The mistake**: Blaming circumstances, other people, or bad luck for lack of progress.

**Fix**: Take full responsibility for your results. Even if only 10% is your fault, focus on that 10%. That's where your power lies.

> **Case: The Entrepreneur's Excuse** (Chapter 4): An entrepreneur blamed the economy, his employees, and his competitors for his failing business. When a mentor forced him to take responsibility, he realized he had been avoiding hard decisions — pricing, hiring, strategy. Once he owned it, he could fix it.
> **Key takeaway**: Blame gives away your power. Responsibility gives you control. Own everything, and you can change everything.

## 4. Waiting for Motivation

**The mistake**: Waiting until you "feel like it" to take action.

**Fix**: Action comes before motivation. Start moving, and the motivation follows. Discipline is doing it anyway.

## 5. Comparing Yourself to Others

**The mistake**: Measuring your progress against other people's highlight reels.

**Fix**: Compare yourself only to who you were yesterday. Progress is personal.
