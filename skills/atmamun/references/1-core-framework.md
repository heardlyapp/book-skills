# 1 — The Teaching of Atmamun

> Source: "Atmamun" by Kapil Gupta

## The Direct Pointing

Kapil Gupta's teaching is uncompromising. He does not offer practices, methods, or gradual paths. He points directly to the truth, repeatedly, in different ways.

> **Case: The Nature of the Search** (Chapter on the Search): "The search for enlightenment is itself the barrier to enlightenment. The one who seeks is the one who must be seen through. When the seeker dissolves, what remains is what was always there. You are not on a journey to somewhere else. You are recognizing what has always been true." Gupta dismantles the very idea of spiritual progress.
> **Key takeaway**: The most radical teaching in the book: stop searching. The search is the problem. Not the solution. Not a step on the path. The problem. This is not a practice to adopt — it is a seeing to recognize.

> **Case: The Mind's Sleight of Hand** (Chapter on the Mind): "The mind is a master magician. It tells you that you need to do something to become free. It sells you practices, teachers, and techniques. All of these keep you trapped. The mind cannot produce freedom because the mind is the prison. Seeing through the mind is the only way." Gupta exposes the mind's deepest trick.
> **Key takeaway**: The mind's primary function in spiritual seeking is to keep you seeking. As long as you are seeking, the mind remains in control. The gate is to see the mind's game — not to play better but to stop playing entirely.
