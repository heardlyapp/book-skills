# 3 — The Direct Path

> Source: "Atmamun" by Kapil Gupta

## Beyond Practice

The direct path is not a method among methods. It is the recognition that no method works. Liberation is not caused by anything you do.

> **Case: The No-Practice** (Chapter on Practice): "Meditation, yoga, breathwork, chanting, visualization — these are all activities of the mind. They can produce pleasant experiences, even profound states. But they cannot produce liberation. Liberation is not a state. It is the end of the need for states." Gupta dismisses spiritual practices categorically.
> **Key takeaway**: This is the hardest teaching for spiritual seekers to hear. If you have spent years meditating, the mind will resist this message. It will say "but meditation helps me." Gupta would say: "It helps you remain a seeker. It gives you a comfortable prison. True freedom requires no practice."

> **Case: The Direct Seeing** (Chapter on Seeing): "If you want to know the truth, you must be willing to see directly. Not through the words of others. Not through the filter of your beliefs and expectations. Just see. What is here, right now, without interpretation? This is the only path — and it is not a path." Gupta points to immediate, direct experience.
> **Key takeaway**: Direct seeing is available now, not after more practice. The truth is not hidden. It is hidden only by the belief that it is hidden. The seeker's job is not to find something new but to see through the illusions that obscure what is already present.
