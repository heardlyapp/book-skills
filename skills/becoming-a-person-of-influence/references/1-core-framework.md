# 1 — Integrity: The Foundation of Influence

> Source: "Becoming a Person of Influence" by John C. Maxwell and Jim Dornan

## Character Before Action

Integrity is the non-negotiable foundation of influence. Without trust, no one will follow you. Trust is built through consistent alignment between words and actions.

> **Case: The Trust Test** (Chapter 1 — Integrity): "People will forgive mistakes. They will not forgive a lack of integrity. If you make a mistake, admit it, fix it, and move on. But if you lie, cheat, or break your word, the damage to your influence may be permanent. Trust is built in drops and lost in buckets." Maxwell establishes integrity as the irreplaceable foundation.
> **Key takeaway**: Integrity is not about being perfect. It's about being authentic, honest, and accountable. When you make a mistake, own it. This paradoxically builds more trust than pretending to be perfect.

> **Case: The Consistency Principle** (Chapter 1): "Your actions speak so loudly that people cannot hear what you say. If your words say one thing and your actions say another, people will believe your actions. Consistent alignment between values and behavior is the mark of a person of integrity." Maxwell emphasizes the power of modeling.
> **Key takeaway**: You cannot lead people where you have not been yourself. The most powerful influence is the example you set. If you want your team to be honest, be honest. If you want them to work hard, work hard. Your example is your most influential message.
