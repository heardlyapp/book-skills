# 3 — Faith in People

> Source: "Becoming a Person of Influence" by John C. Maxwell and Jim Dornan

## Seeing Potential

The most influential people see something in others that others don't see in themselves. They have faith in people's potential and communicate that belief.

> **Case: The Goldmine Principle** (Chapter 3 — Faith): "Every person is a goldmine of potential. Most people have barely scratched the surface of what they can achieve. The person of influence sees the gold and helps others mine it. When you believe in someone, you give them permission to believe in themselves." Maxwell argues that faith in others is a self-fulfilling prophecy.
> **Key takeaway**: People rise to the level of expectation you set. If you expect little, you'll get little. If you communicate high expectations and genuine belief, people will stretch to meet them. The most powerful gift you can give someone is the gift of your belief in them.

> **Case: The Potential Unlocker** (Chapter 3): "Maxwell tells a story of a young man who was struggling in school and labeled as a problem student. A teacher took an interest in him, spent extra time with him, and told him he had potential. That young man went on to become a successful executive. One person's belief changed his life." The story illustrates the power of faith in people.
> **Key takeaway**: The people who changed your life were the ones who believed in you before you had earned that belief. Pay it forward. Look for someone who needs to hear "I believe in you" and say it. You may be the only person who has ever told them that.
