# Reference: Inflammation

## Overview
Inflammation is the body's response to injury or infection. Acute inflammation is protective. Chronic inflammation is destructive. Understanding the difference is essential to understanding modern disease.

## Case: Acute vs Chronic Inflammation
Acute inflammation is the redness, swelling, and pain that follows an injury or infection. It is caused by immune cells releasing signaling molecules (cytokines) that increase blood flow and recruit more immune cells. When the threat is eliminated, inflammation resolves. Chronic inflammation persists when the threat does not go away — or when the immune system mistakenly identifies a normal tissue as a threat.

## Case: Lifestyle and Inflammation
Modern lifestyle promotes chronic inflammation. Poor diet (ultra-processed foods, refined sugar, excess omega-6 fatty acids), lack of sleep, chronic stress, and environmental toxins all drive inflammation. These factors activate the immune system's stress response, which evolved for acute threats, not chronic ones. The result is persistent low-grade inflammation that contributes to heart disease, diabetes, cancer, and autoimmune disease.

## Case: The Anti-Inflammatory Diet
Richtel recommends an anti-inflammatory diet: whole foods, plenty of vegetables and fruits, healthy fats (olive oil, fish oil), adequate protein, and limited sugar and processed foods. The Mediterranean diet is the best-studied anti-inflammatory diet. No single food or supplement can replace the overall pattern of healthy eating.
