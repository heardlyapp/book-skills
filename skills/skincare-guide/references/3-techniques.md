# 3 — Key Ingredients

> Source: "Skincare" by Caroline Hirons

## The Heavy Hitters

- **Retinol**: Gold standard for anti-aging. Speeds cell turnover, boosts collagen. Start low (0.25-0.5%), use 2-3x/week, build up. Only at night. SPF mandatory.
- **Vitamin C (L-Ascorbic Acid)**: Antioxidant. Brightens, protects from environmental damage. Best in AM. Start with lower concentrations (10-15%).
- **Niacinamide (Vitamin B3)**: Multi-tasker. Reduces redness, minimizes pores, regulates oil, supports barrier function. Works with most other ingredients.
- **AHAs (Glycolic, Lactic, Mandelic)**: Exfoliate the skin's surface. Improve texture, tone, brightness. Use at night, 2-3x/week.
- **BHAs (Salicylic Acid)**: Exfoliate inside the pore. Best for acne, blackheads, oily skin. Can be used in AM or PM.

> **Case: Starting Retinol** (Chapter on Retinol): Hirons is clear: "Start low, go slow." Begin with 0.25% or 0.5% retinol, 2x/week for two weeks. If no irritation, increase to 3x/week. "Retinol is a marathon, not a sprint. The people who overdo it and burn their skin are the ones who give up."
> **Key takeaway**: Retinol is powerful but patient. Start with the lowest concentration, use sparingly, and build up over months.

> **Case: The SPF Sermon** (Chapter on Sunscreen): Hirons doesn't mince words: "If you don't wear SPF every day, nothing else matters. Sun damage is the #1 cause of premature aging. It's not negotiable." She recommends SPF 30 minimum, SPF 50 ideally. "Find one you like and wear it every single day."
> **Key takeaway**: Sunscreen is not optional. It is the single most important product in your routine. No retinol, vitamin C, or fancy serum will undo UV damage.
