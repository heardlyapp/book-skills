## Practical Techniques

### 1. The Pilot Method

Before implementing a major change, test it on a small scale. Deng's SEZs were pilots. A successful pilot builds confidence and provides evidence for scaling.

### 2. The Sequencing Principle

Reform in the right order. Start with what is easiest, most controlled, and most likely to succeed. Build momentum from early wins.

> **Case: Agriculture Before Industry** (Chapter 11): Deng reformed agriculture first because it was simpler and had the most immediate impact on people's lives. With farmers producing more food, urban reform became possible. The sequence was strategic.
> **Key takeaway**: The right sequence multiplies success. The wrong sequence creates chaos.

### 3. The "Seek Truth" Check

When making a decision, ask: "What does the evidence say?" Not "What does my ideology say?" Not "What is comfortable?" What actually works?

### 4. The Comeback Mindset

Deng was purged three times. Each time, he continued to work, to learn, and to prepare for his return. Resilience is not about never falling — it is about getting up.

> **Case: The Jiangxi Factory** (Chapter 8): During his second purge, Deng was sent to work in a tractor factory in Jiangxi. He did not complain. He studied the factory's operations, talked to workers, and understood how industrialization worked at the ground level.
> **Key takeaway**: Even when you are down, you can learn. Deng used his exile to gain knowledge he would later use.
