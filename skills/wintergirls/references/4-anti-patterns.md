# Anti-Patterns — The Numbers, the Lies, and the Voice

## The Central Fallacy

The most dangerous assumption: that the voice telling you to starve is your own. It is not. It is the disease. And it lies.

> **Case: The Numbers** (Throughout): Lia counts everything. Calories. Pounds. Miles run. Minutes exercised. She believes the numbers give her control. In reality, the numbers control her. She is a slave to the scale. The lower the number, the more the disease approves. The approval is never enough.
> **Key takeaway**: The numbers are not your friends. They are the disease's language. The only way to win is to stop counting.

> **Case: The Lies** (Throughout): Lia lies about eating. She lies about exercising. She lies about how she feels. She lies to her parents, her therapist, her friends. The lies protect the disease. They also isolate her. The deeper she goes into the lies, the further she is from help.
> **Key takeaway**: Secrecy is the disease's best friend. The lies grow in the dark. When you tell the truth, you starve the disease.

> **Case: The Perfectionism Trap** (Throughout): Lia believes that if she cannot be perfect, she is worthless. The disease demands perfection. One cookie is a failure. One missed workout is a catastrophe. The perfectionism is impossible to satisfy. It is designed to be impossible so that you keep trying and keep failing.
> **Key takeaway**: The disease sets an impossible standard. You will never be thin enough, good enough, perfect enough. That is the point. The disease needs you to keep trying. The only way to win is to stop playing the game.
