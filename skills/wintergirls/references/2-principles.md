> Source: Wintergirls, Laurie Halse Anderson (2009).

# Key Principles — Grief, Friendship, and Survival

## Core Principles

> **Case: The Friendship** (Throughout): Lia and Cassie were inseparable. They shared everything: clothes, secrets, and the disease. Their friendship was both a lifeline and a death pact. They enabled each other's anorexia. They also loved each other. The complexity of their relationship — love and destruction intertwined — is the emotional heart of the novel.
> **Key takeaway**: Relationships in the midst of disease are complicated. You can love someone and be toxic for each other. Both can be true.

> **Case: The Funeral** (Mid-novel): Lia attends Cassie's funeral. She is surrounded by people who do not understand. They say things like "She was so beautiful" and "She had so much to live for." No one says the truth: she died of anorexia. The silence around the disease is part of what killed her.
> **Key takeaway**: Silence about mental illness kills. If someone dies of anorexia, say it. Name it. Break the silence.

> **Case: The Last Phone Call** (Repeated): Lia plays Cassie's voicemail over and over. Cassie's voice, slurred and desperate: "Lia, pick up. Please pick up." Lia cannot forgive herself for not answering. She cannot move forward until she does. Therapy helps her understand: she could not have saved Cassie. Cassie needed more than one phone call.
> **Key takeaway**: You cannot save someone who does not want to be saved. It is not your fault. Lia did not cause Cassie's death by not answering. Cassie's disease killed Cassie.
