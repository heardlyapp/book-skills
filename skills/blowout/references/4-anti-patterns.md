# Anti-Patterns

## 1. The "It's Just Business" Rationalization

**The mistake**: Believing that oil deals with authoritarian regimes are purely commercial and have no political consequences.

**Why it fails**: Every oil deal with a kleptocracy strengthens that regime. There is no apolitical oil transaction.

**Fix**: Recognize that business with authoritarian regimes is inherently political. If you're putting money into an oil deal with Russia, you're supporting the Putin system.

> **Case: The Exxon Defense** (Chapter 4): When criticized for partnering with Russia, Exxon argued they were "just doing business." But the deal gave Rosneft credibility, provided Western technology, and enriched the Putin circle. There was nothing apolitical about it.
> **Key takeaway**: "Just business" ignores the political consequences of business. There is no neutral ground when dealing with kleptocracies.

## 2. The "Cheap Energy Now" Fallacy

**The mistake**: Prioritizing low energy prices over the long-term costs of oil dependence — environmental damage, geopolitical risk, and democratic erosion.

**Fix**: Cheap oil is expensive when you account for all the costs.

## 3. Believing Transparency Alone Is Enough

**The mistake**: Assuming that publishing data about oil revenues will solve corruption.

**Fix**: Transparency is necessary but not sufficient. It must be paired with enforcement, civil society engagement, and political will.

> **Case: The EITI Limits** (Epilogue): While the Extractive Industries Transparency Initiative helped shed light on oil payments, it didn't prevent corruption in countries that lacked the will to act on the information. Transparency without accountability is just data.
> **Key takeaway**: Sunlight is a good disinfectant, but you still need someone willing to clean.

## 4. Ignoring Environmental Costs

**The mistake**: Treating environmental damage as a separate issue from political corruption.

**Fix**: The oil industry's environmental and political impacts are produced by the same business model. Addressing one requires addressing both.

## 5. Cynicism ("Nothing Will Change")

**The mistake**: Seeing the scale of the problem and concluding that change is impossible.

**Fix**: The oil industry is powerful but not invincible. The renewable energy transition, citizen movements, and investigative journalism have all made progress.
