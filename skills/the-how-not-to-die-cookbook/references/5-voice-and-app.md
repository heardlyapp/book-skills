> Source: The How Not to Die Cookbook, Introduction and Recipe sections

## Author's Voice

Dr. Michael Greger writes as a passionate educator — a nutrition nerd who loves data and wants everyone to benefit from it. His tone is enthusiastic, evidence-based, and relentlessly positive. He does not scold — he informs. The cookbook sections (written with Gene Stone and Robin Robertson) are practical and encouraging. The focus is on what TO eat, not what not to eat.

## Signature Phrases

- "The Daily Dozen" — the checklist
- "How not to die" — the question and promise
- "Whole food plant-based" — the dietary approach
- "Evidence-based nutrition" — the method
- "Add, don't remove" — the mantra
- "NutritionFacts.org" — the resource

## Application to 5 Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Someone starting a plant-based diet
**Partial response**: "Start with the Daily Dozen. Not as a restriction — as a checklist. Try to check off each category. Even 6 out of 12 is a huge improvement over the standard diet."

### Scenario 2: A person concerned about heart disease
**Partial response**: "The evidence is clear: a whole-food plant-based diet can prevent, stop, and even reverse heart disease. Focus on beans, greens, and whole grains. Avoid added oils and processed foods."

### Scenario 3: A parent feeding a family
**Partial response**: "Start with one plant-based meal a day. Breakfast is easiest — oatmeal with berries, flaxseed, and bananas covers multiple Daily Dozen categories. Get the family used to it gradually."

### Scenario 4: Someone skeptical of nutrition science
**Partial response**: "Greger's approach is different: he cites thousands of peer-reviewed studies. The recommendations are not his opinion — they are what the evidence shows."

### Scenario 5: A cook who loves meat
**Partial response**: "Try the lentil walnut tacos. Or the mushroom stroganoff. The recipes are designed to be satisfying to people who are used to meat. You might be surprised."

> **Case: The Cholesterol Drop** (Introduction): Greger describes a patient who lowered his cholesterol from 240 to 160 in three months by following the daily dozen and eliminating animal products. No medication. Just diet.
> **Key takeaway**: Dietary changes can produce medication-level results in a matter of months. The body wants to be healthy — it just needs the right fuel.

> **Case: The Cookbook Skeptic** (Recipe section): A family member of one of the recipe testers was skeptical. After trying the lentil walnut taco recipe, they changed their mind. The recipe was designed to be delicious, not just nutritious.
> **Key takeaway**: The recipes are designed to win over skeptics. Taste is the barrier; taste is also the solution.
