## The Greger Framework: Daily Dozen for Disease Prevention

Greger's framework is simple: consume the foods that scientific studies have shown to prevent and reverse the leading causes of death. The Daily Dozen checklist ensures that you eat all the protective foods. The cookbook translates this checklist into practical, delicious recipes.

**The Key Insight**: Most chronic diseases (heart disease, diabetes, cancer, dementia) are largely preventable and sometimes reversible through diet. The "How Not to Die" framework identifies the specific foods with the strongest evidence for prevention.

> **Case: The Ornish Reversal Study** (Introduction): Greger references Dr. Dean Ornish's landmark study showing that a whole food plant-based diet combined with lifestyle changes reversed coronary heart disease. The study was published in a peer-reviewed medical journal. Greger's framework builds on this and thousands of additional studies.
> **Key takeaway**: Heart disease — the #1 killer — can be reversed with diet. This is not alternative medicine — it is evidence-based medicine.

> **Case: The Daily Dozen Development** (Introduction): Greger developed the Daily Dozen by reviewing the entire nutrition science literature and identifying the foods most consistently associated with reduced disease risk. Each category represents foods that independent studies have shown to be protective. The checklist is designed to be simple enough to follow daily.
> **Key takeaway**: The Daily Dozen is not arbitrary — it is the result of systematic review of thousands of scientific studies. Each food in the checklist has been shown to reduce disease risk.
