## Practical Techniques

### 1. The Daily Dozen Checklist

Use a simple checklist to track your Daily Dozen servings each day. A notebook app or even a physical notepad works. The goal is not perfection — it is awareness.

### 2. The Batch Cooking Strategy

Cook beans, grains, and vegetables in bulk on weekends. A pot of lentils, a batch of quinoa, and roasted vegetables give you building blocks for the week.

> **Case: The Weeknight Meal** (Recipe section): Greger recommends spending Sunday prepping: cook 2 types of beans, roast a tray of vegetables, wash greens, and cook a batch of grains. During the week, you can assemble meals in 10 minutes from these prepped components.
> **Key takeaway**: The barrier to healthy eating is not motivation — it is convenience. Batch cooking removes the convenience barrier.

### 3. The Water Sauté

Instead of oil, use water, vegetable broth, or lemon juice to sauté vegetables. This dramatically reduces calories and avoids the inflammatory compounds created by heating oil to high temperatures.

### 4. The Flaxseed Hack

Keep ground flaxseed in the refrigerator. Add 1 tablespoon to oatmeal, smoothies, salads, or soups daily. It is the easiest way to ensure you get your daily omega-3s and lignans.

> **Case: The Simple Smoothie** (Breakfast section): Greger recommends a morning smoothie that can cover multiple Daily Dozen categories: berries, greens (spinach), flaxseed, and a banana. This single meal can knock out 4-5 Daily Dozen servings.
> **Key takeaway**: Smoothies are an efficient way to pack multiple servings of protective foods into one meal. They are the lazy person's path to health.
