# Principles of Tragedy

**Principle 1: Good Intentions Are Not Enough.** Dr. Bliss genuinely wanted to save Garfield. His treatments were well-intentioned. They were also deadly. Intention does not guarantee outcome.

**Principle 2: Question Authority.** The doctors who treated Garfield were the most respected in America. No one questioned them. The result was a preventable death.

**Principle 3: Science Must Be Applied, Not Just Known.** Germ theory had been developed by Pasteur and Lister years before. American doctors knew about it. They chose not to apply it.

**Case: The Unwashed Hands.** Dr. Bliss and his colleagues probed Garfield's wound repeatedly without washing their hands or sterilizing their instruments. They knew about germ theory. They did not believe it applied to them.

**Case: The Bedsprings.** Alexander Graham Bell's metal detector could have found the bullet — if someone had told him Garfield's bed had metal springs. A simple missing piece of information doomed the effort.
