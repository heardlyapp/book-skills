# Reference: Post-Work Society

## Overview
Rifkin argues that society must prepare for a future where traditional employment cannot provide everyone with income and purpose.

## Case: Universal Basic Income
UBI is the most discussed policy response. Every citizen receives a regular, unconditional cash payment. Experiments in Finland, Canada, and Kenya suggest UBI reduces stress without reducing work. Rifkin supports UBI as a transitional mechanism.

## Case: Shortened Work Week
The work week has declined from 60+ hours (1900) to 40 hours (mid-20th century). Rifkin argues it should continue to decline to 30, 20, or even fewer hours. The goal: share the remaining work among more people.

## Case: The Social Economy
Beyond employment and consumption is the social economy: care work, volunteering, community service, artistic creation, and civil society. Rifkin argues this should be valued and supported. It is where meaningful human activity will increasingly take place.

## Case: The Meaning of Work
Work provides income, purpose, community, and identity. Rifkin acknowledges that the end of employment does not have to mean the end of meaning. The social economy can provide purpose and community.

## Case: Resistance to Change
The post-work future faces immense political and cultural resistance. The Protestant work ethic defines millions of identities. The transition will require a fundamental rethinking of what it means to contribute to society.
The debate is just beginning.
Rifkin argues it will be one of the defining debates of the 21st century.
