# Reference: The Third Industrial Revolution

## Overview
Rifkin outlines a framework of three industrial revolutions, each driven by new communication and energy technologies.

## Case: The First Revolution
The 18th century combined the printing press (communication) with steam power (energy). This created the factory system, mass literacy, and the modern nation-state. Work moved from fields to factories.

## Case: The Second Revolution
The 20th century combined electricity (communication through telegraph/radio/TV) with oil and the internal combustion engine (energy). This created mass production, the assembly line, the suburbs, and the consumer society.

## Case: The Third Revolution
The 21st century combines the internet (communication) with renewable energy (solar, wind) and autonomous transportation. This creates the smart grid, distributed manufacturing, the sharing economy, and the collaborative commons. Work moves from employment to peer production.

## Case: The Convergence
The key insight: communication and energy technologies converge during each revolution. The internet enables the management of distributed renewable energy sources. Smart grids balance supply and demand. 3D printing enables distributed manufacturing.

## Case: The Infrastructure
Each revolution requires massive infrastructure investment: canals and railroads (first), highways and power grids (second), smart grids and broadband (third). This infrastructure is a public good that requires government leadership.
