# Anti-Patterns — The New Menopause

## The Central Error: "It's just a natural part of aging. Get over it."

Haver: "Yes, the process is natural, but that doesn't mean that it is not harmful." Dismissing symptoms as "just getting older" denies women access to treatments that could extend both health span and life span.

### Mistake 1: "Hot flashes are the only real symptom"
Haver lists 50+ symptoms. "Frozen shoulder, tinnitus, vertigo, brain fog, crawling skin — these are real symptoms of estrogen decline, not signs of mental illness." Medical training teaches only 5 symptoms. The real list is 10x longer.

### Mistake 2: "Hormone therapy causes breast cancer"
The 2002 WHI finding: combined estrogen+progestin MHT increased breast cancer risk by 26% (relative). Absolute risk: from 30 per 10,000 to 38 per 10,000. Estrogen-only therapy (for women without a uterus) does not increase breast cancer risk. "The increased risk from MHT is similar to the increased risk from being overweight or drinking two glasses of wine per day."

### Mistake 3: "If you're in perimenopause, you have to wait until it's over"
"You have to go a full year without a period before we can intervene" — this is outdated. Perimenopause is when symptoms are often worst, and treatment should not be withheld. "Why would we make women suffer for years before helping them?"

### Mistake 4: "You can fix menopause with diet and exercise alone"
Lifestyle changes are powerful but insufficient for many women. "I tried melatonin, meditation, and proper sleep hygiene, but nothing was working." The combination of MHT + lifestyle is more effective than either alone.

### Mistake 5: "Compounded bioidentical hormones are safer"
Compounded "custom" bioidentical hormones are not FDA-approved. Their potency varies. Their safety has not been studied in large trials. "Patients paying $100+ per month for unregulated creams may be getting unreliable doses."

### Mistake 6: "I'm too young for perimenopause"
Perimenopause can begin in the mid-30s. "My doctor said I was too young for menopause" is one of the most common complaints Haver hears. "If you have symptoms, you're not too young — and you deserve answers."

### Mistake 7: "This is just my new normal"
"Welcome to your new normal" is the most exhausted phrase in menopause care. "Your new normal should not include suffering that can be treated." Women deserve better than dismissal.
