# Techniques — The New Menopause

## How to Navigate Menopause with Knowledge and Power (Based on Dr. Haver)

### Technique 1: Track Symptoms with the Greene Scale
Haver recommends the validated Greene Scale — a symptom-scoring sheet that tracks severity of psychological, somatic, vasomotor, and sexual symptoms over time. "What gets measured gets managed." Bring this to your doctor's appointment. It converts subjective suffering into objective data.

### Technique 2: The "Window of Opportunity" Check
If you're under 60 or within 10 years of your last period, MHT is likely safe and beneficial. If you're over 60 or more than 10 years past menopause, the risk-benefit analysis shifts. The technique: know where you are in the timeline before making decisions.

### Technique 3: The 80%-95% Rule
MHT reduces hot flashes and night sweats by 80-95%. "If your MHT isn't working that well, the dose or delivery method may need adjustment." Don't accept "this is as good as it gets."

### Technique 4: Protein First
Haver's nutrition strategy: 1.2-1.7g of protein per kg of body weight per day. Spread across meals. This preserves muscle mass, supports bone density, and stabilizes blood sugar. "Protein is the single most important nutrient for menopausal women."

### Technique 5: The Systemic vs. Local Distinction
Systemic MHT (patches, pills, gels) treats whole-body symptoms (hot flashes, sleep, brain fog, bone loss). Local MHT (creams, rings, tablets) treats only vaginal symptoms. The technique: know the difference. Many women who are told "no MHT" are good candidates for local therapy.

### Technique 6: The WHI Re-Analysis
When a doctor says "hormones cause breast cancer," ask: "Are you citing the 2002 WHI findings or the updated analysis?" The original found a relative risk of 1.26 for breast cancer (combined estrogen+progestin after 5 years). The re-analysis: estrogen-only therapy doesn't increase breast cancer risk and may decrease it.

### Technique 7: The Galveston Diet Triangle
Three pillars: (1) Anti-inflammatory eating (Mediterranean pattern, plenty of vegetables, healthy fats). (2) Intermittent energy restriction (time-restricted eating 16:8). (3) Macronutrient focus (high protein, moderate fat, controlled carbs). The combination targets menopausal belly fat specifically.
