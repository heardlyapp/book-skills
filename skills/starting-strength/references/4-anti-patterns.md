# 4 — Programming the Novice

> Source: "Starting Strength" by Mark Rippetoe

## The Novice Program

Workout A: Squat 3x5, Bench 3x5, Deadlift 1x5
Workout B: Squat 3x5, Press 3x5, Power Clean 5x3

Alternate A/B. Train Mon/Wed/Fri or Tue/Thu/Sat. Squat every session.

## Linear Progression

Add 5-10 lbs to squat and deadlift every workout. Add 2.5-5 lbs to press and bench. This works until it doesn't.

> **Case: The "Chasing the Pump" Error** (Chapter 7): Rippetoe warns against adding bodybuilding-style accessory work to the novice program. "You don't need curls. You don't need leg extensions. You don't need anything except squat, bench, press, deadlift, and power clean. Everything else is a distraction."
> **Key takeaway**: The novice program works because it's simple. Adding extra exercises will interfere with recovery and slow progress. Trust the program.

> **Case: The Deload Protocol** (Chapter 8): When linear progression stalls, Rippetoe recommends a 10% deload. "Drop the weight 10%. Work back up. Most of the time, you'll blow past the sticking point. If you stall again at the same weight, it might be time for intermediate programming."
> **Key takeaway**: Stalling is normal. Don't panic. Deload 10%, work back up. One more run of linear progression almost always works.
