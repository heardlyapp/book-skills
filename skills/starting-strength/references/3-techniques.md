# 3 — Deadlift and Power Clean

> Source: "Starting Strength" by Mark Rippetoe

## Deadlift

- **Setup**: Bar over mid-foot. Shins touch bar. Grip the bar. Drop hips. Flatten back. Pull.
- **The pull**: Drag the bar up your shins. Keep the bar over mid-foot. Chest up. Hips and shoulders rise together.
- **Lockout**: Stand up straight. Shoulders back. Don't hyperextend.
- **Reset**: Lower under control. Reset. Pull again.

## Power Clean

The power clean is a pulling movement that teaches explosive hip extension. Starts like a deadlift. At mid-thigh, extend the hips violently. Rack the bar on your shoulders.

> **Case: The Deadlift as a Pull** (Chapter 4): Rippetoe is emphatic: the deadlift is not a squat. "If your hips rise before the bar leaves the floor, your start position is wrong." The deadlift is a pull — the bar moves because you push the floor away with your legs, not because you lift it with your back.
> **Key takeaway**: The deadlift starts with leg drive. If you're "lifting" with your lower back, you're doing it wrong. Drive through the floor with your feet.

> **Case: The Power Clean as Explosive Training** (Chapter 5): Rippetoe argues that the power clean is essential for novices because it's the only way to train explosive hip extension. "Without the power clean, you are missing the most important component of athletic performance — the ability to produce force rapidly."
> **Key takeaway**: The power clean is not optional. It teaches your body to produce force quickly, which carries over to every sport and every lift.
