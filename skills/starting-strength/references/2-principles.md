# 2 — Pressing Movements

> Source: "Starting Strength" by Mark Rippetoe

## Bench Press

- **Grip**: Thumbs around the bar. Forearms vertical at the bottom.
- **Setup**: Arch the back. Pull the bar apart. Touch below the sternum.
- **Bar path**: From touch point to over the shoulders. Not vertical — diagonal.

## Overhead Press (Strict)

- **Grip**: Just outside shoulder width. Forearms vertical.
- **Bar position**: Resting on the anterior deltoids. Elbows slightly in front.
- **Bar path**: Press up and back. The bar should end over the back of your head.
- **Breath**: Hold through each rep. Don't bounce. No leg drive allowed.

> **Case: The Bar Path in the Press** (Chapter 3): Rippetoe explains that the bar must travel in a straight line — but the lifter must move out of its way. "The bar starts in front of your face. Press it up and back so it ends over your head. If it goes straight up, it won't clear your face." The cue: pull your head back as the bar passes.
> **Key takeaway**: The press bar path is not vertical — it's a slight arc that keeps the bar over the mid-foot. If you can't press your max, check your bar path first.

> **Case: The Leg Drive in the Bench** (Chapter 3): Many lifters ignore leg drive in the bench press. Rippetoe: "Your feet should be flat on the floor, pushing your upper back into the bench. The leg drive stabilizes the shoulders and allows heavier weights." The cue: push your shoulder blades into the bench with your feet.
> **Key takeaway**: Bench press is not just upper body. Your legs provide stability that allows your upper body to press more weight.
