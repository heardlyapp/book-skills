## 7 Principles of the Quranic Worldview

### 1. God Is One and Unique (Tawhid)

There is no god but God. He has no partners, no children, no equals. This is the central claim of the Quran and the foundation of Islam.

### 2. Prophets Are Messengers, Not Divinities

All prophets — from Adam to Muhammad — were human. They received revelation and conveyed God's message. None should be worshiped.

> **Case: Jesus in the Quran** (Surah 19 — Maryam, Surah 3 — Al Imran): The Quran honors Jesus as a great prophet born of the Virgin Mary. It mentions his miracles: healing the blind, raising the dead, creating birds from clay by God's permission. But it explicitly rejects his divinity: "It is not befitting for God to take a son" (19:35). Jesus is a prophet, not God.
> **Key takeaway**: The Quran's Jesus is a figure of immense respect — a prophet, messiah, and sign of God — but not divine. This is the central theological difference between Islam and Christianity.

### 3. Life Is a Test

This world is temporary. It is a testing ground for the soul. Every action, word, and intention will be accounted for on the Day of Judgment.

### 4. Mercy Prevails Over Wrath

God describes Himself as "The Most Gracious, The Most Merciful" — repeated at the start of every surah except one. His mercy encompasses all things.

> **Case: The Verse of Mercy** (Surah 39, Verse 53): "Say: O My servants who have transgressed against themselves, do not despair of the mercy of God. Indeed, God forgives all sins. He is the Most Forgiving, the Most Merciful."
> **Key takeaway**: Even for those who have sinned greatly, God's mercy is available. Despair of God's mercy is itself a sin.

### 5. Justice Requires Accountability

If there were no afterlife, the injustices of this world would never be fully addressed. The Day of Judgment is the ultimate guarantee of justice.

### 6. Community Matters

The Quran speaks to individuals but envisions a community (umma) of believers bound by shared faith, prayer, charity, and mutual support.

### 7. The Quran Is a "Reminder"

The Quran does not claim to bring entirely new teachings. It reminds humanity of what was already known: that God exists, that He is One, that accountability follows action.
