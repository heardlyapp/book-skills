# 4 — Anti-Patterns: What the Book Corrects

> Source: "Heaven: Think On These Things" by Carl Martin

## Anti-Pattern 1: Heaven as Boring

**The mistake**: Imagining heaven as an endless church service, floating on clouds, playing harps. This is not a biblical picture. Scripture describes heaven as active, creative, relational, and joyful.

## Anti-Pattern 2: Escapism

**The mistake**: Using the hope of heaven to avoid responsibility on earth. Heaven is not an escape from reality — it's the fulfillment of it. We are called to build God's kingdom here and now.

## Anti-Pattern 3: Vague Spirituality

**The mistake**: Thinking heaven is a "state of consciousness" or a "cosmic energy." The Bible describes heaven as a real place with real people, real bodies, and real relationships.

## Anti-Pattern 4: Fear of Death

**The mistake**: Living in fear of death instead of hope of resurrection. For the believer, death is not the end — it's the beginning.

> **Case: The "Old Women" Objection** (Introduction): Someone told Martin: "If heaven is full of people like the elderly women in my church, I don't want to go there." Martin's patient response: heaven transforms people. The difficult people on earth will not be difficult in heaven. And neither will you. We will all be made whole.
> **Key takeaway**: Your experience of difficult people on earth is not a valid objection to heaven. Heaven changes everyone — including you. Give it (and them) a chance.
