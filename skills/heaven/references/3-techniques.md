# 3 — Theological Framework

> Source: "Heaven: Think On These Things" by Carl Martin

## 5 Key Theological Topics

1. **The Bible on Heaven**: Martin builds his case primarily from Scripture — Revelation 21-22, 1 Corinthians 15, John 14, Isaiah 65. Heaven is described as a city, a garden, a kingdom, a wedding feast.
2. **Resurrection Bodies**: Like Jesus after His resurrection — physical but no longer subject to death, decay, or limitation. We will eat, talk, move, and interact.
3. **The New Earth**: Heaven comes down to earth. The physical creation is redeemed, not destroyed. We will live in a renewed world.
4. **Theodicy (Why Suffering?)**: Martin devotes a full chapter to this. His answer: free will requires the possibility of evil, and God redeems suffering without always explaining it.
5. **The War in Heaven**: A chapter on the cosmic conflict between good and evil, and how it relates to our daily struggles.

> **Case: The CWP Bible Marking System** (Appendices): Martin developed a simple Bible marking system (CWP — Comfort, Warning, Promise) to help readers identify key passages about heaven. "You'll see why!" It's a practical tool for studying what the Bible says about the afterlife.
> **Key takeaway**: You don't need a theology degree to understand what the Bible says about heaven. A simple marking system helps you find and remember key passages.
