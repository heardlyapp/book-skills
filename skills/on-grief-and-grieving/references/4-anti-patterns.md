# Anti-Patterns — On Grief and Grieving

## The Central Error: "I Should Be Over This By Now"

"People want you to get back to normal as soon as possible." But grief has no timeline. "You don't 'get over' a loss. You learn to live with it." Case: "Ten years after a loss, you may still cry. That's not regression — it's love."

### Mistake 1: "The five stages happen in order"
"The stages have been very misunderstood. They are not linear." People cycle through them, skip some, repeat others. Case: "You can be in acceptance on Monday and back in anger on Tuesday."

### Mistake 2: "If you're not crying, you're not grieving"
"Grief is not measured by tears." Some people grieve quietly, privately, without tears. "Silent grief is still grief."

### Mistake 3: "You need to be strong for others"
"The strongest thing you can do is be vulnerable." Case: "People thought I was so strong after my mother died. The truth is, I was falling apart."

### Mistake 4: "Grief ends"
"Grief doesn't close — it changes shape." The idea of "closure" is a myth. Case: "You don't get over it. You learn to carry it differently."

### Mistake 5: "Everyone grieves the same way"
"There is no typical response to loss, as there is no typical loss." Case: "Your grief is as unique as your relationship with the person you lost."

### Mistake 6: "Grief is only about death"
"We grieve many losses: divorce, job loss, moving, the loss of health, the loss of a dream." All grief deserves acknowledgment. Case: "Grief is the feeling of reaching out for someone who has always been there, only to find when you need them, they're no longer there."
