# 2 — Key Principles

> Source: *Uncommon Favor* by Dawn Staley.

## The 7 Principles

### 1. Discipline Is the Foundation of Freedom

The more disciplined you are, the more you can do. Discipline is not restriction — it is capacity. Staley's mother taught her that discipline in all areas of life creates the foundation for excellence in the one area that matters most.

> **Case: Discipline on the Court (Preface):** Staley describes her relationship with basketball: "I never cheated on it. I never took it for granted. I didn't care what anybody else was doing. Not then. Not now. Come hell or high water, I was going to play." This single-minded discipline — showing up every day, working when no one watches — is what separated her from other talented players.

### 2. Home Travels with You

Staley never left North Philly behind. When she walks onto a court anywhere in the world, she carries her childhood neighborhood with her. The projects gave her toughness, community, and pride. There is no shame in where you come from — only in pretending you came from somewhere else.

### 3. Service Completes Success

Staley's most important lesson is that achievement is incomplete until it has been used to lift others. The championship net is the symbol: receiving it, holding it, then cutting it into pieces and sending it to seventy-plus other coaches.

### 4. Discomfort Is the Price of Growth

Staley was miserable at UVA. She wanted to leave. She stayed — and became a Hall of Famer. The discomfort did not kill her. It made her. Growth does not happen in the comfortable place.

### 5. You Do Not Need to Be Famous to Be a Role Model

> **Case: The Nike Mural (Lesson 2):** Staley was not the biggest star on her Olympic teams — Lisa Leslie and Sheryl Swoopes were more famous. But Nike chose her for a 100-foot mural in Philadelphia. "I viewed myself as an up-and-coming point guard who happened to be playing with iconic household names. I occupied more of what I determined was a service role." Yet her impact was recognized. Leadership is not about being the star — it is about being the point guard, the server, the one who makes everyone else better.

### 6. Community Raises Champions

No one rises alone. Miss Jones, the block captain. The neighbors who fed each other. The adults who parented every child on the block. Staley's success is North Philly's success.

### 7. Championship Cultures Are Built on Character

As a coach, Staley recruits people before she recruits players. She builds trust before skills. Winning follows.
