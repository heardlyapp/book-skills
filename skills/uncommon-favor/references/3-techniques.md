# 3 — Techniques

> Source: *Uncommon Favor* by Dawn Staley.

## Practical Tools

### 1. The Discipline Cascade

Start with one small, daily act of discipline. Staley's mother started with flowers — maintaining a garden in the projects. From that came discipline in church, school, home. From that came discipline in basketball. The principle: discipline transfers. It does not matter where you start — what matters is that you start. Choose one small thing and do it every day. The discipline will spread.

> **Case: Estelle's Flower Garden (Lesson 1):** Staley's mother planted flowers for every season — annuals, perennials, all bright colors. In a housing project, she cultivated beauty. "We may have been rough around the edges, but we took care of each other, we looked out for each other. We planted flowers. We cultivated joy." The discipline of tending a garden was the same discipline that raised a champion.
> **Key takeaway**: Discipline in one area of life creates the pattern for discipline everywhere. Start small. Start anywhere. The pattern will spread.

> **Case: The Germaphobe Dorm Decision (Lesson 3):** Staley chose UVA over Penn State partly because of the dorms — UVA had private suites while Penn State had shared bathrooms. "I'd already lived in a household of seven people sharing one bathroom. I was due for a come up." This decision — honoring her nonnegotiables — meant she would be comfortable enough in her living space to focus on basketball and academics.
> **Key takeaway**: Know your nonnegotiables. The small things that affect your well-being are not small. Honor them.

Carry your origins with you. Staley never pretended she was not from North Philly. "When all is said and done, I'm most proud of being an odds beater. Growing up where I did, people looking in believed I wasn't supposed to amount to anything. The odds were stacked against me. And I demolished them." Your history is not a weakness — it is your story. Use it.

### 3. The Championship Net Protocol

When you achieve something significant, immediately ask: "Who deserves a piece of this? Who is coming behind me? How can I make their path easier?" Staley sent pieces of her net to seventy-plus coaches. This is the practical application of service: identify the next generation and actively support them.

### 4. The Anchor Strategy

When you are in an environment where you do not belong, find your anchor. Staley's anchor was basketball — the gym, the court, the game tape. What is your anchor? The one activity that reminds you who you are and why you are there. Hold onto it. Use it as a lifeline until you find your footing.

### 5. The Presence Over Extroversion Principle

Staley is naturally shy and socially uncomfortable. She does not try to be the life of the party. She leads by being present, by excellence, by commitment. You do not have to be the loudest person in the room to be the most impactful.
