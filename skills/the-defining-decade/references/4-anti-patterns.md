# Anti-Patterns — The Defining Decade

## The Central Error: "My Twenties Don’t Matter"

"A thirty-is-the-new-twenty culture has told us that the twenties don’t matter." This is the most dangerous myth. The twenties are the most defining decade. Case: "80% of life’s defining moments happen by 35."

### Mistake 1: "I Have Time to Waste"
"There is time to kill today." The Pink Floyd lyrics are seductive but wrong. Your twenties are the time to invest, not waste. Case: Kate at 26 with nothing to show.

### Mistake 2: "My Friends Are My Family"
"The urban tribe" myth. Close friendships are important but they can’t replace romantic commitment. Case: "Your friends cannot replace a partner."

### Mistake 3: "Living Together Is a Trial Run"
"Let’s live together to see if we’re compatible." Cohabitation without commitment creates inertia. Case: Higher divorce rates for those who cohabit before marriage.

### Mistake 4: "I’ll Figure Out Fertility Later"
"I can have kids whenever I want." Fertility declines sharply after 35 for women, and after 40 for men. Case: "Many couples are shocked to find they can’t conceive."

### Mistake 5: "I Just Need to Find Myself"
"Wandering is the point of your twenties." Exploration is fine, but it should build toward something. Not all wandering is productive. Case: "The unlived life is not worth examining."

### Mistake 6: "My Network Is My Closest Friends"
"Strong ties are all I need." Weak ties (acquaintances, former colleagues) are actually more valuable for career opportunities. Case: Most jobs come from weak ties.
