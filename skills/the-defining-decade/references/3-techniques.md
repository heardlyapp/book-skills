# Techniques — The Defining Decade

## How to Make the Most of Your Twenties

### Technique 1: The Identity Capital Audit
List what you’ve invested in yourself so far: education, skills, experiences, relationships. Then ask: "What can I add this year that will increase my identity capital?" Case: Kate had nothing on her list — no job, no license, no relationship. She started with a driver’s license.

### Technique 2: The Weak Tie Connection
Reach out to former colleagues, acquaintances, and people in fields you’re interested in. "Most job offers come from weak ties." Case: A twentysomething got their dream job through a former professor they hadn’t spoken to in years.

### Technique 3: The Urban Tribe Check
Ask yourself: "Are my friendships replacing romantic relationships?" If you’re spending all your time with friends and avoiding dating, you may be using the urban tribe as a crutch. Case: "Your friends cannot replace a partner."

### Technique 4: The Cohabitation Decision
Before moving in with a partner, have a clear conversation: "Are we moving toward marriage or not?" Sliding vs. deciding. Case: Couples who cohabit without engagement are less likely to marry.

### Technique 5: The Fertility Awareness
Know the facts about fertility. Women’s fertility drops after 35. Men’s declines later but also decreases. Plan accordingly. Case: "Many women who wait until their late thirties find they cannot conceive."

### Technique 6: The Backward Planning Method
Think about where you want to be at 30, then work backward. What do you need to do at 25, 26, 27 to get there? Case: "The unlived life is not worth examining" — plan your life, don’t just analyze it.

### Technique 7: The Critical Period Investment
"Your brain is still developing." Invest in challenging learning experiences now. The plasticity of the twenties is a limited window. Case: "Almost invariably, growth has a critical period."
