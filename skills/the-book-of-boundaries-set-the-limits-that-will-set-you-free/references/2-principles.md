# Principles: Family and Work Boundaries

> Source: The Book of Boundaries, Chapters 3-6

## Family Boundaries

The hardest boundaries are often with family. History, guilt, and expectations make them uniquely challenging.

> **Case: The Holiday Visit** (Chapter 3): A mother expects the whole family to stay for a full week. Urban's script: "We can come for three days. [Mirror] I know you want more time, but this is what works for us. [Shine] We'll make those three days really special together."

> **Case: The Unsolicited Advice** (Chapter 4): A parent who constantly gives unwanted parenting advice. Script: "I appreciate your concern. We've got this handled. Let's change the subject."

> **Key takeaway**: Family scripts need to be clear, kind, and firm. Mirror the boundary calmly if pushed. Shine to maintain connection.

## Work Boundaries

Workplace power dynamics complicate boundaries. Scripts are essential.

> **Case: The Late-Night Email Boss** (Chapter 5): A boss who expects after-hours responses. Script: "I'll handle this first thing tomorrow. I'm unavailable after 7 PM." Delivered consistently, this creates a new expectation.

> **Case: The Meeting Overload** (Chapter 5): A colleague scheduling too many meetings. Script: "I can't make that time. Could we do a 15-minute stand-up instead?"

> **Key takeaway**: Work boundaries require 100% consistency. One after-hours reply undoes weeks of boundary-setting.

## Self-Boundaries

Before setting boundaries with others, set them with yourself. Don't check your phone. Don't say yes when you mean no. Self-boundaries are the foundation.
