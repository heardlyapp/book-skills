# Techniques: Relationship and Digital Boundaries

> Source: The Book of Boundaries, Chapters 7-10

## Relationship Boundaries

With partners, friends, and dating, boundaries prevent resentment by making expectations clear.

> **Case: The Needy Friend** (Chapter 8): A friend who texts constantly and demands immediate responses. Script: "I can't always respond right away. I'll get back to you when I'm free." Repeat as needed without apology.

> **Case: The One-Sided Friendship** (Chapter 8): A friendship where you're always the one initiating. Script: "I'd love to see you. Let me know when you're free." Pass the ball. If they never pick it up, you have your answer.

> **Key takeaway**: Relationship boundaries protect the connection from resentment. Clear expectations make relationships healthier, not weaker.

## Dating Boundaries

Early dating sets the tone for the entire relationship.

> **Case: The Last-Minute Cancellation** (Chapter 8): A date who cancels at the last minute. Script: "I understand things come up. Let me know when you'd like to reschedule." This sets the boundary calmly and respectfully — without chasing.

> **Case: The Money Expectation** (Chapter 9): A date who expects you to pay. Script: "I'd prefer to split the check." Simple, clear, no guilt.

## Digital Boundaries

Protect your attention. Social media, phone, and email can consume your life without limits.

> **Case: The Social Media Break** (Chapter 10): Urban took a full month off. She announced it clearly and got overwhelming support. The lesson: people respect boundaries more than we fear.

> **Key takeaway**: Digital boundaries are essential in the modern world. Your attention is yours to protect.
