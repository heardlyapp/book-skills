# Voice · Common Scenarios · Key Quotes

> Source: *How to Win Friends*, full book

---

## § Carnegie's Voice

1. **Story-driven** — Every principle comes with a concrete story: the salesman who talked coins, the manager who asked instead of ordered. Theory never appears without example.
2. **From life, not theory** — Carnegie draws from business, politics, and everyday interactions. His sources: Lincoln, Franklin, Rockefeller, plus ordinary people he met.
3. **Repetition for retention** — Each principle is stated, restated with a story, and summarized at chapter end.
4. **Conversational tone** — Written as if Carnegie is speaking directly to you. Personal, warm, non-academic.

## § Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: "How do I give my employee feedback without them getting defensive?"
1. Begin with praise (find something genuine to appreciate)
2. Talk about your own mistakes first
3. Call attention to the issue indirectly — "I wonder if there's another approach..."
4. Ask questions, don't give orders
5. Let them save face
6. Praise any improvement, no matter how small

### Scenario 2: "How do I handle a friend who keeps interrupting me?"
- Don't criticize them
- Model good listening: let them finish, show genuine interest
- When it's your turn, say "That's an interesting point. May I share my perspective?"
- If they persist, talk about your own need: "You know, I sometimes catch myself doing the same thing — I get excited and interrupt. I'm working on listening better."

### Scenario 3: "How do I convince my boss to adopt my idea?"
- Arouse an eager want: show how your idea benefits them, not you
- Let them feel the idea is theirs: guide them to it, don't present it as yours
- Talk in terms of their interests: what problem does your boss care about?

### Scenario 4: "How do I handle criticism without getting defensive?"
- Listen fully without interrupting
- If the criticism is wrong, don't say "You're wrong" — acknowledge their perspective
- If the criticism is right, admit it quickly and emphatically
- Ask for their input on how to improve

## § Key Quotes

> *"Criticism is futile because it puts a person on the defensive and usually makes them strive to justify themselves."*

> *"The deepest principle in human nature is the craving to be appreciated."* — William James

> *"You can make more friends in two months by becoming interested in other people than you can in two years by trying to get other people interested in you."*

> *"A person's name is to that person the sweetest and most important sound in any language."*

> *"The only way to get the best of an argument is to avoid it."*

> *"If you're wrong, admit it quickly and emphatically."*

> *"A drop of honey catches more flies than a gallon of gall."*

> *"The royal road to a person's heart is to talk about the things they treasure most."*
