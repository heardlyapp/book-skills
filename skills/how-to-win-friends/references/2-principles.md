# Principles · 12 Ways to Win People to Your Way of Thinking

> Source: *How to Win Friends*, Part 3

---

## 1. Avoid Arguments
> *"The only way to get the best of an argument is to avoid it."*

A man convinced against his will is of the same opinion still. You can't win an argument — if you lose, you lose; if you win, you've made an enemy.

## 2. Never Say "You're Wrong"
> *"Show respect for the other person's opinions. Never say 'You're wrong.'"*

If someone is wrong, don't tell them. Use a diplomatic approach: "Well, now, look, I thought otherwise, but I may be wrong. I frequently am. Let's check the facts."

## 3. If You're Wrong, Admit It Quickly
> *"Any fool can try to defend his or her mistakes — and most fools do — but it raises one above the herd to admit one's mistakes."*

A swift, emphatic admission disarms the other person and turns potential conflict into cooperation.

## 4. Begin in a Friendly Way
> *"A drop of honey catches more flies than a gallon of gall."*

A friendly opening lowers defenses. Even if you're delivering bad news, start with warmth.

## 5. Get Them Saying "Yes, Yes" Immediately
Start with things you agree on. Keep asking questions that lead to "yes." The momentum of agreement makes it harder to disagree later.

## 6. Let Them Do the Talking
> *"Let the other person do a great deal of the talking."*

People trust their own ideas more than yours. Let them talk themselves into your point of view.

## 7. Let Them Feel the Idea Is Theirs
> *"The person who talks about what they want is usually seen as a schemer. The person who helps others discover their own solutions is seen as a leader."*

## 8. See Things from Their Point of View
> *"Try honestly to see things from the other person's point of view."*

Ask yourself: "Why would they want to do this?" Understanding their motivation is the key to influence.

## 9. Be Sympathetic to Their Ideas
> *"Be sympathetic with the other person's ideas and desires. The only thing that most people want is sympathy."*

## 10. Appeal to Their Nobler Motives
People want to feel they're acting on high principles. Show them how their action aligns with their best self.

## 11. Dramatize Your Ideas
> *"Merely stating a truth isn't enough. The truth must be made vivid, interesting, dramatic."*

Show, don't just tell. Use stories, demonstrations, vivid imagery.

## 12. Throw Down a Challenge
> *"The way to get things done is to stimulate competition. Not in a sordid, money-getting way, but the desire to excel."*
