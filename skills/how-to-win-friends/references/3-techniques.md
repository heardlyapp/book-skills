# Techniques · 9 Ways to Change People Without Offense · Be a Leader

> Source: *How to Win Friends*, Part 4

---

## 9 Ways to Change People Without Giving Offense

### 1. Begin with Praise and Honest Appreciation
Start with genuine appreciation before criticizing. People are more receptive to hearing criticism after they've been praised.

### 2. Call Attention to Mistakes Indirectly
Instead of "You're wrong," say "I have an idea that might work better." Criticism doesn't need to be direct to be effective.

### 3. Talk About Your Own Mistakes First
Before criticizing someone, admit your own shortcomings. It removes the sting and makes you seem human.

### 4. Ask Questions Instead of Giving Orders
"Don't you think this might work?" works better than "Do it this way." People prefer being asked over being told.

### 5. Let Them Save Face
> *"I have no right to say or do anything that diminishes a man in his own eyes. What matters is not what I think of him, but what he thinks of himself."*

### 6. Praise Every Improvement
> *"Praise the slightest improvement and praise every improvement. Be 'hearty in your approbation and lavish in your praise.'"*

### 7. Give Them a Fine Reputation to Live Up To
If you want someone to improve, give them a reputation as someone who already possesses that quality.

### 8. Use Encouragement — Make the Fault Seem Easy to Correct
> *"Tell them they have the ability to do it and they'll work until they do."*

### 9. Make Them Happy to Do What You Suggest
Show how the change benefits them. People do things for their reasons, not yours.

## Key Principles from the Book

- Criticism is futile — it puts people on the defensive
- The deepest human need is the feeling of importance
- Talk about what the other person wants
- A person's name is the sweetest sound to them
- To be interesting, be interested
- The only way to win an argument is to avoid it
- If you're wrong, admit it — fast
- Begin in a friendly way
- Let them feel the idea is theirs
- Throw down a challenge — people love to excel
