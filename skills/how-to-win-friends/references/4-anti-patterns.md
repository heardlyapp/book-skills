# Anti-Patterns · What NOT to Do

> Source: *How to Win Friends*, full book

---

## The Master Anti-Pattern: Criticism

> *"Criticism is futile because it puts a person on the defensive and usually makes them strive to justify themselves. Criticism is dangerous because it wounds a person's precious pride, hurts their sense of importance, and arouses resentment."*

Carnegie's entire book can be summarized as: **don't criticize; instead, understand and appreciate.**

---

## Specific Anti-Patterns

### 1. Arguing
Trying to "win" an argument creates an enemy, not a convert. The moment you raise your voice or insist you're right, you've lost.

### 2. Telling Someone They're Wrong
Directly telling someone "you're wrong" attacks their intelligence, judgment, and pride. They'll defend themselves rather than listen.

### 3. Giving Direct Orders
Orders breed resentment. People want to feel they're acting from their own choice, not under compulsion.

### 4. Interrupting
Not listening signals that you value your own thoughts more than theirs. People crave being heard.

### 5. Public Embarrassment
Never humiliate someone in front of others. The damage to their ego will make them your enemy for life.

### 6. Insincere Flattery
People can tell the difference between genuine appreciation and flattery. Flattery is selfish and counterfeit.

### 7. Focusing on Your Own Interests
Talking only about what you want makes you boring and self-centered. The path to influence is understanding what they want.

### 8. Monopolizing the Conversation
People who talk too much lose listeners. Let others express themselves — their ideas, their achievements, their pride.

### 9. Not Remembering Names
Forgetting someone's name signals they weren't important enough to remember. It's a subtle insult.

### 10. The "I'll Fix You" Attitude
Approaching relationships as problems to solve destroys connection. Relationships are about understanding, not fixing.
