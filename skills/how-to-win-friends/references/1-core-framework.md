# Core Framework · 3 Fundamental Techniques · 6 Ways to Make People Like You

> Source: *How to Win Friends and Influence People*, Parts 1-2

---

## Part 1: Fundamental Techniques in Handling People

### Technique 1: Don't Criticize, Condemn, or Complain

**Source:** Chapter 1

> *"Criticism is futile because it puts a person on the defensive and usually makes them strive to justify themselves."*

Carnegie's core insight: criticism doesn't change behavior — it breeds resentment. Even when justified, criticism wounds the other person's pride and damages relationships.

**Story:** The infamous criminal "Two Gun" Crowley and Al Capone — neither saw themselves as bad. They justified everything. If hardened criminals don't feel guilty about their actions, how much more will ordinary people defend themselves against criticism?

### Technique 2: Give Honest, Sincere Appreciation

**Source:** Chapter 2

> *"The deepest principle in human nature is the craving to be appreciated."* — William James

Carnegie distinguishes between **appreciation** (genuine recognition of someone's worth) and **flattery** (insincere, selfish, counterfeit). Appreciation comes from the heart; flattery from the teeth.

### Technique 3: Arouse in the Other Person an Eager Want

**Source:** Chapter 3

> *"The only way to influence people is to talk about what they want and show them how to get it."*

Instead of asking "What do I need?", ask "What does the other person need, and how can they get it by doing what I suggest?"

## Part 2: Six Ways to Make People Like You

### 1. Become Genuinely Interested in Other People
> *"You can make more friends in two months by becoming interested in other people than you can in two years by trying to get other people interested in you."*

### 2. Smile
> *"Actions speak louder than words, and a smile says, 'I like you. You make me happy. I am glad to see you.'"*

### 3. Remember Their Name
> *"A person's name is to that person the sweetest and most important sound in any language."*

### 4. Be a Good Listener
> *"If you aspire to be a good conversationalist, be an attentive listener. To be interesting, be interested."*

### 5. Talk in Terms of Their Interests
> Story: The salesman who won a client by talking about the client's hobby (collecting coins) for 30 minutes before mentioning business.

### 6. Make the Other Person Feel Important — Sincerely
> *"The desire to feel important is one of the most craving human appetites."*
