> **Case: Helliwell's Plan** (Chapter 2)
> Colonel Paul Helliwell, an OSS officer in China, observed that Chiang Kai-shek funded his army through opium sales. He proposed the same model for US intelligence: sell heroin to black communities in American ghettos to generate untraceable funding for Gladio. The OSS brass — Donovan, Dulles, Angleton — approved. Helliwell co-founded Civil Air Transport (CAT), the precursor to Air America, to transport drugs from the Golden Triangle.
> **Key takeaway:** If Williams is correct, the CIA's involvement in drug trafficking was not a side effect of covert operations — it was by design.

> **Case: Lucky Luciano's Release** (Chapter 2)
> In 1942, the US Navy recruited Lucky Luciano through his Mafia associates to secure the New York waterfront against Nazi saboteurs. After the war, per Williams, the CIA kept its promise: Luciano was released from prison and deported to Italy. In return, he rebuilt the international heroin trade with CIA support. The heroin flowed from the Golden Triangle through Corsican Mafia labs in Marseilles to Harlem. The profits funded Gladio.
> **Key takeaway:** The alliance between US intelligence and organized crime began during WWII and was never dissolved. It was institutionalized as a covert funding mechanism.
