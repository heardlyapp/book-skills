# Gladio — The Secret Army

> Source: Operation Gladio, Prologue, Chapters 1, 2, 23

## Core Framework

Gladio is the code name for NATO's secret "stay-behind" networks — underground armies established after WWII to resist a potential Soviet invasion.

### The Founding

| Event | Year | Significance |
|-------|------|-------------|
| Fort Hunt Conference | Feb 1945 | Dulles and Gehlen agree to create stay-behind units |
| Operation Sunrise | 1945 | Separate peace with Nazis, excluding Soviets |
| X MAS recruitment | 1945 | Borghese's fascist commandos become Italian Gladio |
| Gehlen Organization | 1946 | Nazi intelligence network reborn under US control |
| CIA founded | 1947 | Gladio formalized under new agency |

> **Case: The Fort Hunt Conference** (Chapter 1)
> In February 1945, OSS director Allen Dulles transported Nazi General Reinhard Gehlen to Fort Hunt, Virginia. Gehlen had commanded the "werewolves" — Nazi stay-behind units intended to fight the Red Army after Germany's defeat. Dulles and Gehlen agreed: the werewolves would be reborn as "gladiators" under US command. Gehlen returned to Germany under US protection to rebuild his network. The Gehlen Organization became the foundation of Gladio.
> **Key takeaway:** Gladio was born from a Nazi-OSS alliance, not a democratic mandate. Its founders included individuals who had been complicit in Nazi atrocities.

> **Case: The Black Prince** (Chapter 2)
> Prince Junio Valerio Borghese, the fascist commander of X MAS (Decima Mas), led a naval commando unit that killed thousands of Italian partisans. After the war, OSS officer James Jesus Angleton secured Borghese's release from prison and made him the commander of Italian Gladio. Borghese's forces were divided into 40 groups: sabotage, espionage, propaganda, guerrilla warfare. A training camp was set up in Sardinia. Borghese was later involved in a 1970 coup attempt (the "Golpe Borghese").
> **Key takeaway:** The same fascist units that had fought against the Allies were re-recruited into the anti-communist secret army. The enemy of my enemy became my ally.
