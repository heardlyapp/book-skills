# Principles: Social Currency and Triggers

> Source: Contagious, Chapters 1-2

## Social Currency

People share things that make them look good. Status is the currency of word of mouth.

> **Case: The $100 Cheesesteak** (Chapter 1): Berger describes Barclay Prime in Philadelphia — they sell a $100 cheesesteak with wagyu beef and truffle butter. The ridiculous price is itself the marketing. People share the experience because it makes them seem interesting and sophisticated.

> **Key takeaway:** Give people insider knowledge or remarkable experiences and they'll share them to boost their own status.

## The Status of Not Knowing

Scarcity and exclusivity increase perceived value.

> **Case: The "Hotmail" Trick** (Chapter 1): Hotmail included "Get your free email at Hotmail" in every email sent. The user gained status by being an early adopter. Each email was a Social Currency ad.

> **Key takeaway:** Make people feel like insiders. Exclusivity drives word of mouth.

## Triggers

The environment cues word of mouth. Things we think about frequently are things we talk about.

> **Case: Mars Candy and NASA** (Chapter 2): Mars candy sales spiked when NASA's Mars rover landed. Why? The word "Mars" was a trigger — people heard about the planet and thought of the candy bar. No advertising needed.

> **Key takeaway:** Connect your product to common environmental triggers. The trigger frequency determines sharing frequency.

## Growing the Habitat

Berger describes "growing the habitat" — creating more triggers.

> **Case: Kit Kat and Coffee** (Chapter 2): Kit Kat linked itself to coffee as a "break" companion. The trigger: every coffee break. The result: increased sales. The link was deliberate and manufactured.

> **Key takeaway:** You can create new triggers by linking your product to common daily behaviors.
