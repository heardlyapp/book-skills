# Anti-Patterns: What Prevents Sharing

> Source: Contagious, Chapters 5-6

## Ignoring Practical Value

People share useful information. If your content isn't useful, it won't spread.

> **Case: The Coupon Experiment** (Chapter 5): Berger found that deals and discounts (practical value) are among the most shared content online. People share coupons not to help the company — to help their friends save money.

> **Key takeaway:** Useful content spreads because sharing it makes the sharer look helpful. Offer actionable value.

## Forgetting the "News You Can Use" Factor

Berger coins "news you can use" as the most shareable category.

> **Case: The Consumer Reports Study** (Chapter 5): Product reviews and comparison guides are shared more than brand advertising. Why? Because they're useful. People share practical information to help others make decisions.

> **Key takeaway:** Create content that solves a specific problem or saves someone something (time, money, effort).

## Boring Trojan Horses

A story that isn't interesting won't carry your message.

> **Case: The Subway Jared Story** (Chapter 6): Jared Fogle lost weight eating Subway sandwiches. The story was compelling on its own — a human transformation narrative. Subway didn't push the message. The story carried it naturally.

> **Key takeaway:** Build your message into a story worth telling. If the story isn't interesting, the message goes nowhere.

## Forget the "Sleeper Effect" Danger

A story so good that people remember the story but not the brand.

> **Case: The Parable Problem** (Chapter 6): Some viral videos get millions of views but no one remembers who made them. Berger warns: your story must be tightly connected to your brand. If the link is too loose, the message is lost.

> **Key takeaway:** The Trojan horse story must carry your message. Don't make the story so entertaining that the message is forgotten.
