# 5 — Voice & App

> Source: *Concise Guide to APA Style (7th ed.)* — Introduction.

## Voice

APA Style's voice is authoritative, precise, and instructional. It does not persuade or inform in the usual sense — it prescribes. Every guideline is presented as "do this" with an explanation of why.

> **Example: The 7th Edition Revisions** (Introduction): APA 7 simplified many rules — 3+ authors are always "et al." (no first citation exception), "DOI:" label is dropped, running heads are no longer required for student papers.

> **Example: Bias-Free Language Evolution** (Chapter 3): APA's guidelines have evolved from "man" for humanity (1st ed.) to gender-neutral language (3rd ed.) to the current nuanced guidance on intersectionality and identity-first vs. person-first language.

## 5 Scenarios

1. **Formatting a term paper** — Title page → headings → citations → references
2. **Citing an unusual source** — Use the reference categories in Chapter 10
3. **Writing about a person or group** — Apply the bias-free guidelines from Chapter 3
4. **Presenting data in a table** — Chapter 7 provides templates
5. **Revising for clarity** — Chapter 2: active voice, concise language, correct tense
