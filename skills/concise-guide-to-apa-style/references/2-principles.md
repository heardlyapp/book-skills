# 2 — Key Principles

> Source: *Concise Guide to APA Style (7th ed.)* — Chapters 2-3.

## 6 Principles

### 1. Active Voice Over Passive

"Good writers use active voice most of the time." E.g., "The participants completed the survey" not "The survey was completed by the participants."

### 2. Past Tense for Literature Review, Present for Results

Past tense for the lit review (the work already happened), present tense for explaining results (the findings speak to the present).

### 3. Specificity Over Labels

Don't call someone a "schizophrenic" — say "a person diagnosed with schizophrenia." Don't say "the disabled" — say "people with disabilities."

> **Example: Person-First Language** (Chapter 3): Use "people with depression" not "the depressed." Exception: identity-first language may be preferred by some communities (e.g., "autistic person").

> **Example: Avoid False Binaries** (Chapter 3): Use "all genders" not "both sexes." Use "partner" not "boyfriend/girlfriend" unless the individual's identity is known.

### 4. One Space After a Period

APA 7th edition: use one space after period at end of sentence.

### 5. Paraphrase More Than Quote

Paraphrasing is preferred. Quote only when the exact words are necessary. "Quoting should be done sparingly."

### 6. Cite Carefully

Every claim from a source needs a citation. Overcite rather than undercite.
