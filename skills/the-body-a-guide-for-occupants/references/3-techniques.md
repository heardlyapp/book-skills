# Techniques: Body Hacks, Medical Insights, and What Science Can Teach Us

## The Body as Instruction Manual

### How to Read Your Own Signals
1. **Blinking frequency (14,000/day)** — Eyes shut 23 min per waking day. Don't force it.
2. **Facial microexpressions** — Paul Ekman identified 6 universal expressions: fear, anger, surprise, pleasure, disgust, sorrow. A genuine smile involves orbicularis oculi muscles around the eyes—you can't fake it.
3. **Goose bumps** — Horripilation (piloerection). In furry mammals it adds insulation. In humans: zero benefit, just a reminder we used to be furrier.
4. **Taste vs flavor** — 70-90% of what we call "taste" is actually smell via retronasal route. Pinch your nose while eating a jelly bean—you can taste sweet but not identify the flavor.

### Medical Tests You Should Know

| Test | What It Measures | Why It Matters |
|------|-----------------|----------------|
| Blood pressure (120/80) | Systolic/diastolic pressure | 130/80 is now considered hypertensive (2017 AHA revision) |
| Vitamin D levels | Sunlight + dietary intake | 50% of people globally are deficient; 90% in northern climes in winter |
| Microbiome diversity | Gut bacteria variety | Thin people have more gut microbes than fat people; diversity = health |

## Key Discoveries in the History of Medicine

### The Penicillin Story
1. **1928**: Alexander Fleming discovers mold on a petri dish after returning from holiday
2. **1941**: Oxford team (Florey + Chain) finally produces enough to test on Albert Alexander (rose thorn scratch → near death)
3. **1943**: Mary Hunt finds "pretty golden mold" on a cantaloupe in Peoria, IL — 200x more potent. Every penicillin since descends from that single cantaloupe
4. **1945**: Fleming warns in Nobel speech about antibiotic resistance

### The Heart
- **1929**: Werner Forssmann catheterizes his own heart, then walks to radiology for X-ray proof
- **1953**: John Gibbon's heart-lung machine successfully repairs a hole in a woman's heart
- **1958**: Rune Elmqvist builds first pacemaker on kitchen table; Arne Larsson lives 43 years on 26 pacemakers
- **1967**: René Favaloro invents bypass surgery at Cleveland Clinic; first patient lives 30 more years
- **1967**: Christiaan Barnard performs first heart transplant (recipient lives 18 days)

### The Brain
- **1848**: Phineas Gage — tamping rod through brain; first proof brain damage alters personality
- **1861**: Paul Broca discovers speech center in frontal lobe (Broca's area)
- **1953**: Henry Molaison's lobotomy reveals hippocampus's role in memory
- **2014**: Adrian Owen shows ~15-20% of "vegetative" patients are fully aware

## What Doesn't Kill You (But Might)

### Choking
- 5,000/year die in US; 200 in UK (probably undercounted due to misdiagnosis as heart attacks)
- Epiglottis is the only thing between you and catastrophe
- The Heimlich maneuver = "abdominal thrusts" (rebranded by Red Cross in 2006)

### Antibiotic Resistance
- MRSA kills 700,000/year globally; projected 10 million by 2050
- 80% of US antibiotics go to farm animals (fattening, not treatment)
- 73% of human antibiotic prescriptions are for conditions antibiotics can't treat
- Only 2 of 18 largest pharma companies still develop new antibiotics

### The Smell of Death
- Morning breath contains methyl mercaptan (cabbage), hydrogen sulfide (rotten eggs), cadaverine
- Sweat is odorless until bacteria break it down
- Foot odor comes from isovaleric acid + methanediol — also found in cheese

## The Body's Built-in Safeguards

### Sweating
- Humans have many more sweat glands than chimpanzees
- "Unglamorous sweat has made humans what they are today" (Jablonski)
- Loss of body hair + efficient sweating = enabled brain growth (brain is temperature-sensitive)

### The Immune System
- Kills 1-5 cancerous cells every day without you noticing
- Tonsils and adenoids can be removed without apparent effect
- Vaccination programs have eliminated smallpox; polio is nearly gone

### Pain
- The body releases endorphins when you eat capsaicin (chilies) — no one knows why
- The brain interprets chili heat as actual burning — same neurons as touching a 335°F stove
- Chilies evolved capsaicin to deter small mammals (who destroy seeds) while birds (who spread seeds) can't taste it
