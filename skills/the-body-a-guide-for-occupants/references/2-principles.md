# Key Principles: 10 Lessons About Your Body

## 1. You Are Worth More Than You Think

The idea that a human body could be assembled for pocket change is a myth. The Royal Society of Chemistry calculated it would cost $151,578.46 to buy the 59 elements needed to build a Benedict Cumberbatch-sized human. Carbon alone costs $69,550. But the real miracle isn't the materials—it's that those inert substances, when combined correctly, become a living, thinking being. "The only thing special about the elements that make you is that they make you."

## 2. Your Microbes Are Your Partners, Not Your Enemies

You are home to roughly 40,000 species of microbes weighing about 3 pounds—equal to your brain. Your 20,000 human genes are vastly outnumbered by 20 million bacterial genes. You're "99 percent bacterial and not quite 1 percent you." Bacteria provide 10% of your calories, produce vital vitamins B2 and B12, and generate 10,000 digestive enzymes (vs your 20). The antibiotics crisis—700,000 deaths/year from resistant infections, projected 10 million by 2050—stems from overuse: 80% of US antibiotics go to farm animals, and 73% of human prescriptions are for conditions antibiotics can't treat.

## 3. Your Brain Creates Your Reality

The brain exists in "silence and darkness, like a dungeoned prisoner," and has never seen the world directly. It constructs everything you perceive from electrical pulses. You never see the present—your brain continuously forecasts what the world will look like 200 milliseconds ahead. Memory is not a fixed record but "a little more like a Wikipedia page. You can go in there and change it, and so can other people." (Elizabeth Loftus) Two-thirds of your cerebral cortex is devoted to vision alone.

## 4. Medical Progress Is Recent and Fragile

Before penicillin (discovered 1928, mass-produced 1943), the closest thing to a wonder drug was Salvarsan—made from arsenic, requiring 50 weekly injections, and risking amputation if injected wrong. Today we take antibiotics for granted, but resistance is growing faster than new drug development. Only 2 of the 18 largest pharmaceutical companies still develop new antibiotics. "The day when people die once again from the scratch of a rose thorn may not be far away."

## 5. Your Skin Is a Frontier of Mystery

Skin is your largest organ (~2 square meters, 10-15 pounds). "All that race is—a sliver of epidermis." Skin color is just a reaction to sunlight involving 120+ pigmentation genes. Humans have evolved light skin at least 3 times independently. You shed 25,000 skin flakes/minute, ~1 pound of dust/year. There are 2-5 million hair follicles and twice as many sweat glands. Your unique fingerprint patterns—dermatoglyphics—exist for reasons nobody knows.

## 6. Your Heart Doesn't Care About Your Feelings

The heart is a pump doing 100,000 beats/day, 3.5 billion/lifetime, pushing 1,680 gallons of blood daily. It has just one job. "The heart has no time for distractions." Heart disease was barely recognized as a problem until FDR died of it in 1945 at 63 with BP 300/190. The Framingham Heart Study (1948-present) identified the major risk factors. But for 25% of victims, the first symptom of heart disease is a fatal heart attack.

## 7. Your Genes Came From Fish

"Your genes come from ancestors who most of the time weren't even human. Some of them were fish. Lots more were tiny and furry and lived in burrows." The three tiny bones in your middle ear (ossicles) were jawbones in ancient ancestors. Your body is "the product of three billion years of evolutionary tweaks" that happened to result in you.

## 8. Sleep Is Non-Negotiable

Your brain uses 20% of your energy (65% in newborns—that's why babies sleep constantly). When you sleep, your glymphatic system clears metabolic waste from your brain. Sleep deprivation is linked to Alzheimer's, heart disease, obesity, depression. Most people need 7-9 hours, yet "suicide by lifestyle takes ages."

## 9. Your Body Is More Robust Than You Deserve

Despite smoking, overeating, under-exercising, and general abuse, your body maintains you. 1-5 of your cells turn cancerous every day; your immune system catches and kills them. Most heart attack victims who survive live decades more. "Even when you do nearly everything wrong, your body maintains and preserves you."

## 10. We Know Less Than We Think We Know

Throughout the book, Bryson repeatedly confronts the limits of scientific knowledge:
- We don't know why we have fingerprints
- We don't know why we have chins (unique to humans)
- We don't know why we cry from emotion (unique to humans)
- We don't know how consciousness works
- We don't know what 98% of DNA does (the "dark DNA")
- We don't know why the brain has been shrinking (from 1,500 cc to 1,350 cc in 12,000 years)
- We don't know why the appendix exists
- We don't know why we dream
- We don't know what the uvula is for
- We can't agree on basic facts like how many neurons or microbial cells we have
