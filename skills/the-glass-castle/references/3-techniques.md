# Rose Mary Walls — The Mother

> Source: The Glass Castle, Parts I, II, III, IV

## The Neglectful Artist

Rose Mary Walls was as complex as Rex. She was educated, talented, and willfully neglectful.

### The Mother

| What She Had | What She Chose |
|-------------|---------------|
| Teaching degree | Painting instead of teaching |
| Talent as an artist | Art over her children's welfare |
| Seven children | Her freedom over their security |

> **Case: The Christmas Candy (Part II)**
> In Welch, the family was starving. There was no food in the house. Rose Mary had a stash of candy bars — her Christmas gift — hidden in her room. The children begged for food. She refused to share the candy bars, insisting they were hers. Jeannette writes this scene without judgment — but the reader is left horrified: a mother who lets her children go hungry while hiding candy for herself is not just eccentric. She is neglectful. Rose Mary's logic: "I deserve this. I've had a hard life."
> **Key takeaway:** Rose Mary's selfishness is harder to understand than Rex's addiction. Rex was chemically driven. Rose Mary made conscious choices to prioritize her own comfort over her children's basic needs. This is the harder truth of the memoir: some parental failures are not caused by addiction — they are caused by choice.

> **Case: The Mother's Philosophy (Part IV)**
> When Jeannette, as an adult, confronts her mother about the homelessness, Rose Mary insists she is living exactly as she wants. She says: "People in this country are too wasteful. It's my way of recycling." She tells Jeannette her values are "confused." She refuses help. She refuses to change. The memoir does not resolve this — Rose Mary remains herself, stubborn and unapologetic, to the end.
> **Key takeaway:** Rose Mary's refusal to be saved is the most frustrating and honest part of the memoir. Some people do not want to be saved. They are comfortable in their dysfunction. The lesson for Jeannette — and the reader — is: you cannot save someone who does not want to be saved. You can only save yourself.
