# The Walls Family Story

> Source: The Glass Castle, Parts I-IV

## Core Framework

### The Arc

| Part | Setting | Key Events |
|------|---------|-----------|
| The Desert | Arizona, Nevada | Jeannette burned at 3. Family roams. Rex teaches astronomy. |
| Welch | West Virginia | Move to coal town. Extreme poverty. Rex's drinking worsens. Jeannette works at 13. |
| New York City | NYC | The children escape. Jeannette builds a career. Parents follow and become homeless. |
| Thanksgiving | NYC (reunion) | Adult children and homeless parents share a meal. Jeannette has made peace. |

> **Case: The Fire (Part II)**
> At age 3, Jeannette was cooking hot dogs alone — unsupervised — when her dress caught fire. She suffered severe burns. At the hospital, nurses asked if her parents hurt her. Jeannette said no. She explained: she was "mature for her age." But the implication is clear: a 3-year-old should not be cooking alone. The fire was the family's pattern in miniature: the children fending for themselves, the parents absent or negligent, the system (hospital, doctors) seeing something wrong but being unable to intervene.
> **Key takeaway:** The fire story is the perfect opening for the memoir. It shows everything: the danger, the neglect, the children's resilience, and the system's inability to save them. Jeannette learned early: she had to take care of herself.

> **Case: The Prologue (Part I)**
> The book opens with adult Jeannette, a successful journalist on Park Avenue, seeing her mother rooting through a Dumpster. She hides. She is ashamed. She is torn: she loves her mother, she has resources, but she cannot save her. The opening sets up the central tension: how do you reconcile success and gratitude with guilt and shame? How do you enjoy your life when your parents are homeless?
> **Key takeaway:** The opening scene is Walls's thesis statement: I got out. They did not. I carry both the guilt of surviving and the shame of being embarrassed by them. This is not a simple success story — it is a complicated survival story.
