# Key Principles — The Diabetes Code

## I. Type 2 Diabetes Is Reversible

"This is a great big lie: that type 2 diabetes is chronic and progressive." Everyone intuitively knows that weight loss reverses diabetes — yet doctors and official guidelines treat it as a one-way street. "If you lost 50 pounds and your blood glucose normalized, what would you say? 'Great job.' Not 'You must be lying, my doctor says it's chronic.'"

## II. The Problem Is Not Calories — It's Insulin

The "calories in, calories out" model is wrong. "Diabesity" (obesity + diabetes) is a hormonal disease driven by hyperinsulinemia — too much insulin. Insulin drives fat storage. Lower insulin = you burn fat. The question is not "how many calories?" but "how much insulin?"

## III. Fructose Is a Liver Toxin

"Glucose is the energy of life; fructose is a toxin." Table sugar is half glucose, half fructose. Fructose is metabolized almost entirely by the liver, driving fatty liver disease and insulin resistance. This is why sugary drinks are uniquely damaging — liquid fructose bypasses normal satiety signals.

## IV. Drugs Can't Cure a Dietary Disease

"No less than seven multinational trials proved standard glucose-lowering medications do not reduce heart disease — the major killer of diabetics." Insulin, metformin, sulfonylureas — they hide the sugar in the body instead of eliminating it. "Like hiding garbage under the bed and pretending the house is clean."

## V. Insulin Resistance Is an Overflow Phenomenon, Not a Defect

Cells are not "broken" — they're full. The Japanese subway analogy: commuters packed in can't accept more passengers. "Insulin resistance" is the body's protective mechanism against further glucose overload. Forcing more glucose in (with insulin or drugs) worsens the problem.

## VI. Low-Carb and Fasting Are the Cure

Two interventions: (1) Reduce sugar and refined carbohydrates (put less in), (2) Intermittent fasting (burn off the rest). A 2016 trial: 97% of patients reduced or stopped insulin use, 58% reversed diabetes entirely at one year on a very low-carb diet.

## VII. The Epidemic Started in the 1980s

Diabetes has quadrupled since 1980 — one generation. The cause is not genetic (genes don't change that fast). The cause is dietary: the low-fat, high-carbohydrate dietary guidelines that took hold in the late 1970s. "The last several decades of low-fat, high-carb nutrition advice has almost certainly fueled the very obesity and diabetes epidemics it was intended to prevent."
