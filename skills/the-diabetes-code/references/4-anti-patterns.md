# Anti-Patterns — The Diabetes Code

## The Central Error: Believing Type 2 Diabetes Is a Progressive, Incurable Disease

"Most health professionals consider type 2 diabetes to be a chronic and progressive disease. This promotes the idea that type 2 diabetes is a one-way street, a life sentence with no possibility of parole." The evidence proves otherwise: weight loss reverses it.

### Mistake 1: "You can manage diabetes with medication alone"
"You can't use drugs to cure a dietary disease." Seven major trials proved glucose-lowering medications do not prevent heart disease. "We have pretended these medications make people healthier, but it's been a lie."

### Mistake 2: "Insulin is the best treatment for type 2 diabetes"
Insulin causes weight gain, which worsens insulin resistance, requiring more insulin. "Once patients start on insulin injections for type 2 diabetes, they often sense they are heading down the wrong path." They're right.

### Mistake 3: "Eating fat causes diabetes"
"The last several decades of low-fat, high-carbohydrate nutrition advice has almost certainly fueled the very obesity and diabetes epidemics it was intended to prevent." Dietary fat has minimal effect on blood glucose or insulin. Avocado, olive oil, nuts, eggs, butter are beneficial.

### Mistake 4: "Fasting is dangerous"
"Fasting is literally the oldest dietary therapy known and has been practiced throughout human history without problems." For those on diabetes medications, it should be medically supervised — but for most people, skipping breakfast is safe.

### Mistake 5: "If my blood glucose is normal, my diabetes is under control"
"Medications only hid the blood glucose by cramming it into the already engorged cells." Normal blood glucose on medication does not mean reversing the disease — it means hiding the problem. The underlying hyperinsulinemia continues.

### Mistake 6: "You just need to eat less and exercise more"
Caloric restriction without carbohydrate restriction keeps insulin high. The body adapts by lowering metabolism. "Exercise is far less powerful at reversing the disease than dietary interventions."

### Mistake 7: "Diabetes runs in my family, so there's nothing I can do"
"Type 2 diabetes has quadrupled since 1980 — genes don't change that fast." The epidemic is driven by lifestyle, not genetics. A single generation ago, this disease was rare. "We only need to go back a single generation to find a way of life that can prevent most incidents."
