# Core Framework — The Diabetes Code

## Quick Start: Type 2 Diabetes Is Reversible

Dr. Jason Fung's central claim: "Most health professionals consider type 2 diabetes to be a chronic and progressive disease. But this is actually a great big lie, which is excellent news for anyone who has been diagnosed." The disease is caused by too much sugar in the body — put less in, burn the rest off, and it reverses.

**The sugar bowl analogy:** Your body is a sugar bowl. Over decades, you eat sugar and refined carbs and the bowl fills up. When it's full, sugar spills into the blood (high blood glucose). Standard medications hide the sugar back into the body instead of eliminating it. "You can't use drugs to cure a dietary disease."

**The suitcase analogy:** Overstuffed cells resist more glucose — just like an overpacked suitcase resists more clothes. "Insulin resistance" is not a defect; it's a protective mechanism. Forcing more glucose in makes things worse.

## Part 1: The Epidemic

**Diabetes has quadrupled since 1980 — one generation.** Ancient disease recognized since 1550 BC (Ebers Papyrus). Type 1 (autoimmune, not reversible) vs Type 2 (dietary, reversible). "The simplest proof is that weight loss reverses type 2 diabetes — everyone has seen it happen."

**The whole body effect:** Type 2 diabetes destroys every organ — eyes (blindness), kidneys (dialysis), heart (failure), brain (Alzheimer's), nerves (neuropathy), legs (amputations). "No part of your body is spared."

## Part 2: Hyperinsulinemia and Insulin Resistance

**The calorie deception:** "Diabesity" — not about calories, but about hormones. Insulin is the master hormone controlling energy storage. High insulin → fat storage → insulin resistance → more insulin → vicious cycle.

**The overflow phenomenon:** Cells are not "resistant" because they're defective — they're resistant because they're full. The Japanese subway analogy: commuters are packed in. More glucose can't fit. "The body has reached its capacity to store sugar."

**The fructose connection:** "Glucose is the energy of life; fructose is a toxin." Fructose (half of table sugar, high in HFCS) is metabolized almost entirely in the liver, causing fatty liver, driving insulin resistance far more potently than glucose. Liver becomes insulin resistant → pancreas pumps more insulin → hyperinsulinemia.

## Part 3: Sugar and the Rise of Type 2 Diabetes

**Dual defects:** Insulin resistance (cells full) + hyperinsulinemia (too much insulin). The problem is not just high blood sugar — it's the excess sugar stored everywhere.

**Metabolic syndrome:** Abdominal obesity, hypertension, high triglycerides, low HDL, high fasting glucose — all driven by hyperinsulinemia.

## Part 4: How Not to Treat Type 2 Diabetes

**Insulin makes it worse:** "Once patients start on insulin injections for type 2 diabetes, they often sense they are heading down the wrong path." Insulin causes weight gain, which worsens insulin resistance, requiring more insulin — a vicious cycle.

**Oral hypoglycemics:** "We have pretended these medications make people healthier, but it's been a lie." Seven major trials proved blood glucose lowering does not reduce heart disease. "You can't use drugs to cure a dietary disease."

**Low-calorie diets fail:** Caloric restriction without carbohydrate restriction keeps insulin high. The body compensates by lowering metabolism (adaptive thermogenesis). Exercise alone is not powerful enough at reversal.

## Part 5: How to Effectively Treat Type 2 Diabetes

**Lessons from bariatric surgery:** Gastric bypass works not because of food restriction but because it reduces insulin resistance almost immediately — often before any weight loss. "The drop in blood glucose happens within days, not weeks."

**Carbohydrate-reduced diets:** Low-carb (20-50g net carbs/day) and moderate protein are the foundation. "Eliminate all sugar and refined carbohydrates." The 1923 Osler framework: diabetes is a disease where "the normal utilization of carbohydrate is impaired."

**Intermittent fasting:** "Fasting is the simplest and surest method to force your body to burn sugar." Two main strategies: (1) 24-hour fasts 2-3 times per week, (2) 16-hour daily fasts (time-restricted eating). Fasting burns stored glucose and lowers insulin.

**The reversal trial:** A 2016 trial of 330 patients on very low-carb diet: 97% reduced or stopped insulin use; 58% no longer had a formal diabetes diagnosis at one year.
