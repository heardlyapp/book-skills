# Anti-Patterns — Security Analysis: Sixth Edition

## The Central Error: "The Market Is Always Right"

"The Great Illusion" is believing market prices reflect true value. Markets are driven by emotion, not rationality. "Price and value diverge — sometimes wildly." Case: The 1999-2000 Internet bubble. Stocks trading at hundreds of times earnings with no profits.

### Mistake 1: Chasing Fads
"Money sloshes around in giant waves from one fad to the next." Tech bubble, Japanese stocks, mortgage-backed securities — each fad felt "different this time." Case: "If anything, people controlling these mega-sums have become less intelligent over time."

### Mistake 2: Believing It's Easy
"Investing looks easy, particularly with inexpensive software and online trading." The reality: "All the people I know who've built wealth have worked very hard at it." Case: Online trading made people believe day trading was a shortcut to wealth.

### Mistake 3: Ignoring Management Compensation
"Management options are a subtle form of theft from shareholders." When management can issue themselves options without performance requirements, they're incentivized to pump the stock price rather than build long-term value. Case: "Excessive option grants diluted shareholder value at countless companies."

### Mistake 4: Using Relative Value in a Bubble
"If everyone is expensive, this stock is cheap compared to peers." Relative value can be a trap during bubbles. An entire sector can be overvalued. Case: "A stock can be cheap relative to peers but still overvalued absolutely."

### Mistake 5: Speculating When You Think You're Investing
"Buying a stock without a margin of safety is speculation, not investment." Graham was clear: if you don't have a margin of safety, you're gambling. Case: Buying tech stocks at 100x earnings in 1999 was speculation dressed as "growth investing."

### Mistake 6: Failing to Do the Work
"I have a 'gut feeling' about this stock." Graham demanded systematic, hard work. "Many securities must be examined before one is found that has real possibilities." Case: "Gut feelings" are not analysis. "Hard and systematic work" is the only path.
