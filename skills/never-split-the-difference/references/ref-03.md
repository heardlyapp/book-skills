# Reference: Bargaining

## Overview
The Ackerman model is a systematic bargaining method that maximizes the outcome without leaving money on the table.

## Case: The Ackerman Model
The Ackerman model: set your target price, offer 65% of it, use calibrated questions to encourage the other side to counter, increase in decreasing increments (85%, 95%), and finally offer exactly 100% of your target. The decreasing increments signal that you are reaching your limit. The exact number at the end shows that you have done your homework.

## Case: Bending Reality
Deadlines are reality-bending events. Voss teaches that the side that uses deadlines better wins. Never be the first to give in to a deadline. When the other side has a deadline, ask: "What happens if we don't meet your deadline?" Often, the consequence is less dire than they want you to believe.

## Case: The Fair Card
When someone says "I just want what's fair," they are often about to propose something unfair. Voss recommends responding: "I'm sorry, but I don't think you mean that." This calls out the manipulation. Alternatively, ask: "What would you like me to do?" This shifts the burden to them to propose something reasonable.

## Case: The Range-Offer Trick
Instead of giving a single number, Voss suggests giving a range. If your target is $50, say "We're looking at offers in the $45-55 range." The range signals flexibility. The other side's reaction tells you where in the range they are willing to go. If they push back on the top of the range, that is valuable information.
