# 3 — Techniques

> Source: *Born to Run* by Christopher McDougall.

## Practical Tools

### 1. The Barefoot Form Check

Take off your shoes. Stand on a hard surface. Jog in place for 10 seconds. Notice how your foot lands. You'll naturally land on your midfoot or forefoot with a shorter stride. Now put your running shoes on. Jog again. Most people will immediately start heel-striking with a longer stride. The difference tells you everything: your shoes are changing your form.

> **Case: Caballo's Ankle (Chapter 7):** Caballo Blanco, who lived and ran in tire-tread sandals, wore thick-soled Nike trail shoes for one run and severely sprained his ankle. The shoes altered his form. "That's what I get for bragging, and wearing shoes while trying to save my light sandals for running faster."
> **Key takeaway**: Changing your footwear changes your form. If your form works, don't fix what isn't broken.

> **Case: Ann Trason vs Victoriano (Chapter 10):** Ann Trason, the greatest female ultrarunner of her time, was locked in an epic duel with Victoriano, a 23-year-old Tarahumara, at the Leadville 100. They ran together for 80 miles. Ann had a pacer, coaching, and modern gear. Victoriano had sandals, a corn drink, and his two younger brothers who ran alongside him singing. He beat her by 18 minutes.
> **Key takeaway**: Community and joy are performance enhancers. Victoriano brought his brothers. They sang. It wasn't a distraction — it was fuel.

Aim for 180 steps per minute — no matter how fast or slow you're going. Most runners over-stride at a lower cadence (150-170). Increasing cadence automatically shortens your stride, reduces impact, and reduces injury. Count your steps for 30 seconds and multiply by 4. If under 180, work on shorter, quicker steps.

### 3. The Iskiate (Chia) Fuel

The Tarahumara's secret fuel: chia seeds soaked in water with a little lime and sugar. Iskiate is a natural energy drink that out-performs modern sports drinks. Chia seeds are packed with omega-3, protein, and slow-release carbohydrates. Make your own: 1 tbsp chia seeds in 8 oz water, a squeeze of lime, and a touch of honey. Let sit 10 minutes.

### 4. The "Run to the Party" Mindset

Stop "training" and start "running to a party." Pick a destination that excites you — a friend's house, a viewpoint, a coffee shop. Run there, enjoy the reward, then run back. The destination is the motivation. The running is just the transportation.

### 5. The Run-Together Principle

The Tarahumara never run alone. Running together creates accountability, reduces perceived effort, and turns pain into shared experience. Find a partner. Not a competitor — a companion.
