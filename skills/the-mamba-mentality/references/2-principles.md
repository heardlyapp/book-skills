# 2 — Training and Preparation

> Source: "The Mamba Mentality" by Kobe Bryant

## Kobe's Training Regime

4 AM wakeup. 5 AM gym session. Weights. Skill work. Film. Practice with the team. Another session after practice. Repeat.

## The Philosophy

"Rest is part of the work." Kobe was disciplined about sleep, nutrition, and recovery. He didn't burn out because he treated his body like a machine that needed maintenance.

> **Case: The Offseason** (Chapter on Training): Kobe didn't take off-seasons off. "While others were on vacation, I was in the gym. That's when champions are made — not during the season, but in the months when nobody is watching."
> **Key takeaway**: The off-season is when you separate yourself. Anyone can work during the season. Real dedication is what you do when no one is watching.

> **Case: The 800-Move Workout** (Chapter on Skills): Kobe describes a workout where he practiced 800 moves — game speed, with intensity. "Most players practice 10 moves. The game is too fast to think. Your body has to know what to do automatically."
> **Key takeaway**: Practice beyond what you need. Drill until the moves are automatic. Then drill more. When the game speeds up, you need instincts, not thoughts.
