# 4 — Leadership and Teamwork

> Source: "The Mamba Mentality" by Kobe Bryant

## The Standard

Kobe held his teammates to the same standard he held himself. This sometimes created friction. But the best players of his era — Gasol, Odom, Fisher — credit him with making them better.

> **Case: The Trust Building** (Chapter on Pau Gasol): When Pau Gasol joined the Lakers, Kobe challenged him immediately. "I pushed him hard in practice. I wanted to see if he would push back. He did. That's when I knew we could win together."
> **Key takeaway**: Leadership is not about being liked — it's about getting the best out of people. Sometimes that means pushing them in ways that are uncomfortable.

> **Case: The Accountability** (Chapter on Leadership): Kobe believed in holding everyone accountable — teammates, coaches, even himself. "If you see something that needs to be addressed, address it. Don't wait. The team is more important than anyone's feelings."
> **Key takeaway**: Real teams are built on honest accountability, not false harmony. Address problems directly. The team's success depends on it.
