# 3 — Studying the Game

> Source: "The Mamba Mentality" by Kobe Bryant

## Film Study

Kobe watched game film constantly. He studied opponents' tendencies — which hand they favored, how they moved off screens, where they liked to shoot from.

## Footwork

"The game is played with your feet." Kobe spent hours on footwork. Pivots, jab steps, drop steps. Every move starts with the feet.

> **Case: The "Study Your Opponent" Method** (Chapter on Opponents): Kobe describes studying LeBron James for hours before facing him. "I knew his patterns. In one game, I knew he would look off the pass before driving left. I stepped in front of him before he moved. He was shocked."
> **Key takeaway**: Knowledge is a weapon. Study your opponent so thoroughly that you can predict their moves. In competition, anticipation beats reaction.

> **Case: Learning from the Greats** (Chapter on Mentors): Kobe reached out to great players — Hakeem Olajuwon, Michael Jordan, Jerry West. He studied their moves and incorporated them into his game. "You learn from everyone. Even your opponents. If they do something well, steal it."
> **Key takeaway**: Don't reinvent the wheel. Study the best, learn their techniques, and make them your own. Great artists steal.
