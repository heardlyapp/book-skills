# 1 — The Mamba Mentality Framework

> Source: "The Mamba Mentality" by Kobe Bryant

## What It Is

The Mamba Mentality is a philosophy of constant improvement. It's not about winning — it's about the process of preparing to win. "It's a way of life. You don't turn it on and off."

## The Core

1. Obsession with the craft
2. Outworking everyone
3. Study everything
4. Relentless preparation
5. No excuses

> **Case: The 4 AM Workouts** (Foreword by Pau Gasol): Gasol writes about arriving at the gym for the first time with the Lakers. "I got there two hours early. Kobe was already there, drenched in sweat. He had been working out since 4 AM. That set the tone." Gasol says that moment changed how he approached the game.
> **Key takeaway**: Showing up early is not enough. Show up earlier than early. Kobe's work ethic wasn't a secret — it was an example that elevated everyone around him.

> **Case: The Mamba Name** (Introduction): Kobe explains how he took on the nickname "Black Mamba" from the movie Kill Bill. "I needed an alter ego. A way to separate Kobe the person from Kobe the competitor. The Black Mamba was the killer on the court. The person at home was different."
> **Key takeaway**: Separating your competitive self from your personal self is a technique. The Mamba was not who Kobe was — it was how he competed.
