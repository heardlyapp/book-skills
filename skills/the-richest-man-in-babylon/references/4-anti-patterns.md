# Anti-Patterns · Financial Pitfalls

> Source: *The Richest Man in Babylon*, Parables 5-6

---

## 1. Spending Everything You Earn

The most common pattern. Income grows, expenses grow faster. The result: a full salary and an empty purse, regardless of earnings.

**Fix:** The 1st Cure. Before any spending, set aside 10%. This is non-negotiable.

## 2. Investing Without Understanding

Gold slips away from those who invest in what they don't understand. The safest investment is the one you can explain to someone else.

**Fix:** The 4th Law. Before investing in anything, seek three independent opinions from people who actually do that thing.

## 3. Spending Principal

Cashing out investments to buy luxuries. A cow that gives milk is valuable; a cow that is sold for a feast is worthless.

**Fix:** The 3rd Cure. Make your gold multiply. Don't eat your seed corn.

## 4. Chasing Get-Rich-Quick Schemes

The promise of "impossible earnings" is always a trap. The 5th Law of Gold: gold flees those who chase impossible returns.

**Fix:** Slow and steady. There are no shortcuts to wealth — only discipline and time.

## 5. Living Beyond Your Means

Buying things you don't need with money you don't have, to impress people you don't like. Debt for consumption is the enemy of wealth.

**Fix:** The 2nd Cure. Budget for necessities. Delay satisfaction on desires until you have saved enough to afford them.
