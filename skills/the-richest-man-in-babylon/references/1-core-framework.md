# Core Framework · The 7 Cures · The 5 Laws of Gold

> Source: *The Richest Man in Babylon*, Parables 1-3

---

## The 7 Cures for a Lean Purse

The first five clay tablets — the foundational principles of wealth:

**Cure 1: Start thy purse to fattening** — Save at least 10% of everything you earn. Not 5%, not 15% — 10%. This is non-negotiable.

**Cure 2: Control thy expenditures** — Budget for necessities. Most people's wants expand to consume their income. A budget distinguishes need from want.

**Cure 3: Make thy gold multiply** — Put your savings to work. A hoarded coin is useless. An invested coin works for you.

**Cure 4: Guard thy treasures from loss** — Invest only in what you understand. The first rule of investing: don't lose your principal.

**Cure 5: Make of thy dwelling a profitable investment** — Own, don't rent. Make your home a source of stability and eventual profit.

**Cure 6: Insure a future income** — Prepare for retirement and for hardship. Save for old age while you are young and strong.

**Cure 7: Increase thy ability to earn** — Invest in yourself. Learn more, become more skilled. Your earning capacity is your most valuable asset.

---

## The 5 Laws of Gold

1. **Gold comes gladly to those who save at least one-tenth of their earnings** and keep it for future investment.
2. **Gold works diligently for those who invest it wisely.** It multiplies like herds in a fertile pasture.
3. **Gold clings to those who entrust it to wise counsel.** Follow the advice of those skilled in its keeping.
4. **Gold slips away from those who invest it in schemes they do not understand** or in purposes not approved by those skilled in its use.
5. **Gold flees from those who force it to impossible earnings** or who follow the alluring advice of schemers and dreamers.
