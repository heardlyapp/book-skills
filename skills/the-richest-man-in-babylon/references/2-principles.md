# Principles · Budgeting · Wise Counsel · Preparing for the Future

> Source: *The Richest Man in Babylon*, Parables 4-6

---

## Budgeting for Necessities

The second cure teaches: expenses expand to meet income. If you earn more, you'll find new ways to spend. The only way to save is to decide in advance what you will spend.

**The practice:** Write down every expense for 30 days. You'll discover that many expenses are "wants" masquerading as "needs." Cut them ruthlessly. Allocate no more than 70% of your income to necessities and 20% to savings/investing — but start with 10%.

## Taking Counsel of Wise Men

Gold clings to those who seek advice from those skilled in its keeping. Before any investment, ask someone who has done it before.

**The key question:** "If you had a sum like mine to invest, where would you put it?"

## Insuring a Future Income

Life is unpredictable. Prepare for old age, for sickness, for the unexpected. The wealthiest Babylonians set aside gold for their families, for retirement, and for unforeseen hardship. This is not optional — it is the 6th cure.

## Increasing Your Ability to Earn

The 7th cure is often overlooked but may be the most important: the more you learn, the more you can earn. A skilled worker earns more than an unskilled one. An educated mind finds opportunities an ignorant one misses.
