## § Clason's Voice

Written in the style of ancient Babylonian parables. Each chapter tells a story — of a chariot builder, a gold lender, a farmer — and draws a financial lesson from it. The tone is wise, paternal, and timeless. These are the same truths that every culture's wisdom literature teaches, dressed in the robes of Babylon.

## § Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: "I have no savings and I'm living paycheck to paycheck"
1. Start the 1st Cure immediately: save 10% of your next paycheck, no matter how small
2. Apply the 2nd Cure: track every dollar for 30 days, find the waste
3. Use the 7th Cure: invest one hour each day in learning a skill that increases your income

### Scenario 2: "I inherited $10,000 — what should I do?"
1. Apply the 4th Law: don't invest in anything you don't understand
2. Consult someone wise: ask a financial advisor or a friend who has built wealth
3. Apply the 3rd Cure: put it to work — don't spend it
4. Follow the 1st Law: save a portion of the returns, reinvest the rest

### Scenario 3: "I'm in debt and can't save"
1. Budget strictly: find the 10% that must be saved first (pay yourself first)
2. Use the remaining 90% to pay necessities and debt
3. Apply the 7th Cure: increase your income through skill development
