# Techniques · Practical Steps to Apply the Parables

> Source: *The Richest Man in Babylon*, full book

---

## Daily Application

**Step 1: Pay yourself today.** Take 10% of whatever you have now (even if it's just $5) and put it somewhere separate from your spending money. This starts the habit.

**Step 2: Create a simple budget.** List your income, your fixed expenses, and your variable expenses. Where is the money going? Find the 10% that can be redirected to savings.

**Step 3: Find one investment to study.** Pick one asset you understand — real estate, a small business, dividend stocks — and learn about it from someone who's done it.

**Step 4: Increase your earning power.** Invest time and money in learning a skill that increases your income. A $50 book that leads to a $5,000 raise is the best investment you'll ever make.

## The Parable of the Gold Lender (Case Study)

> **Case:** A man borrowed gold from Arkad to invest in a business selling jewels to travelers. He did not ask the jewelers whether the business was sound. The travelers did not come, the business failed, and he lost not only the gold but his friendship with Arkad.
> **Key takeaway:** He failed the 4th Law of Gold — he invested in a scheme he didn't understand, without consulting anyone who knew the business.

## Practical Budgeting Method

From the parable of the lean purse: divide your income into 7 portions:
- 70%: Living expenses (shelter, food, clothing, necessities)
- 10%: Savings (the first portion — pay yourself first)
- 10%: Debt repayment
- 10%: Investment fund
