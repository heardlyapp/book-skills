# 2 — Pregnancy and Prenatal Care

> Source: "Spiritual Midwifery" by Ina May Gaskin

## The Farm Approach

Whole foods, plenty of rest, exercise, emotional preparation, and a supportive community. Pregnancy is a time to prepare body, mind, and spirit.

## Key Prenatal Principles

- **Nutrition**: High-protein diet, plenty of vegetables, healthy fats. Avoid processed foods.
- **Exercise**: Walking, squats, pelvic rocking. Stay active without exhausting yourself.
- **Emotional readiness**: Talk about your fears. Read positive birth stories. Surround yourself with supportive people.
- **Partner involvement**: Birth partners should attend classes, practice relaxation techniques, and be fully present.

> **Case: The Farm Diet** (Prenatal Chapter): Gaskin recommends a diet rich in protein for pregnant women. "We saw better birth outcomes when mothers ate plenty of protein — meat, eggs, beans, nuts. Women who ate mostly carbs had longer, harder labors."
> **Key takeaway**: What you eat during pregnancy affects your labor. Protein is especially important for building the strength needed for birth.

> **Case: The Squatting Practice** (Prenatal Preparation): Gaskin advises pregnant women to practice squatting daily. "Squatting is the natural position for birth. If you can't squat, you're limiting your options." She recommends squatting for 5-10 minutes a day as labor approaches.
> **Key takeaway**: Physical preparation matters. Squatting opens the pelvis and strengthens the legs. It's one of the best things you can do to prepare for labor.
