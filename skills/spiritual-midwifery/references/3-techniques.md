# 3 — Labor and Delivery Techniques

> Source: "Spiritual Midwifery" by Ina May Gaskin

## Positions

- **Standing**: Uses gravity. Allows pelvic movement. Partner can support from behind.
- **Squatting**: Opens the pelvis. Most effective pushing position.
- **Hands and Knees**: Good for back labor. Reduces pressure on the perineum.
- **Side-Lying**: Restful but can slow labor. Good for resting between contractions.

## Pain Management

- **Relaxation**: The most important technique. Practice relaxing every muscle during contractions.
- **Vocalization**: Low moaning, not high screaming. Low sounds open the cervix. High sounds tighten it.
- **Water**: Warm water reduces pain, relaxes muscles, and speeds labor.
- **Massage**: Partner can massage lower back, shoulders, and legs.

> **Case: The Vocalization Technique** (Pain and Birth): Gaskin advises women to make low, deep sounds during contractions. "The sounds a woman makes in labor tell you a lot about how she's coping. Low moans mean she's relaxed and opening. High-pitched screams mean she's scared and tensing."
> **Key takeaway**: Your voice is a tool in labor. Keep sounds low and deep. High pitched sounds indicate fear — breathe down, not up.

> **Case: The Water Birth** (Water Birth Stories): The Farm was one of the first birth centers to use water for labor and birth. "Women in water often report less pain, faster dilation, and a more peaceful birth experience. The water supports the body and relaxes the mind."
> **Key takeaway**: Water is one of the most effective natural pain management tools for labor. Consider a tub or shower for pain relief.
