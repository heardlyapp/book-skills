# 2 — The FBI and DOJ

> Source: "The Russia Hoax" by Gregg Jarrett

## Key Figures

**James Comey**: FBI Director. Made key decisions about the investigation. Controversial for both reopening the Clinton email investigation and the Trump-Russia probe.

**Andrew McCabe**: Deputy FBI Director. Involved in key decisions. Fired just before retirement.

**Peter Strzok**: FBI agent. Sent anti-Trump texts. Removed from Mueller team.

> **Case: The Strzok Text Messages** (Chapter 5): FBI agent Peter Strzok, who worked on both the Clinton email investigation and the Russia investigation, sent text messages calling Trump an "idiot" and suggesting there was a "secret society" within the FBI. "These texts suggest bias — but they don't prove the investigation was illegitimate."
> **Key takeaway**: The Strzok texts are a flashpoint. To critics, they prove bias. To defenders, they show individual opinions that didn't affect the investigation's outcome.

> **Case: The FISA Warrant** (Chapter 6): The FISA warrant to surveil Carter Page was approved by four different federal judges. But the Horowitz IG Report found 17 significant errors or omissions. "The court was not given the full picture. The warrant was renewed three times — with the same problems."
> **Key takeaway**: FISA warrants require full disclosure. The IG Report found that the FBI failed to meet this standard. This is a procedural problem regardless of the underlying investigation's validity.
