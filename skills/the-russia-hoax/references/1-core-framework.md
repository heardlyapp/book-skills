# 1 — The Origins

> Source: "The Russia Hoax" by Gregg Jarrett

## The Dossier

Christopher Steele, a former British intelligence officer, was hired by Fusion GPS to research Trump's ties to Russia. Fusion GPS was hired by the Clinton campaign's law firm. The dossier was used to obtain FISA warrants.

## Crossfire Hurricane

The investigation was opened July 31, 2016. It was triggered by a meeting between George Papadopoulos and Australian diplomat Alexander Downer.

> **Case: The Papadopoulos Lead** (Chapter 1): George Papadopoulos, a low-level Trump campaign foreign policy advisor, told an Australian diplomat that Russia had damaging information on Hillary Clinton. The diplomat reported this to US officials. This was the basis for opening the investigation. Jarrett argues the information was vague and unreliable.
> **Key takeaway**: The entire investigation was launched based on a conversation in a London bar. The reliability of the source and the interpretation of the conversation are disputed.

> **Case: The Dossier's Funding** (Chapter 3): Jarrett details how the Steele Dossier was funded: Hillary Clinton's campaign → DNC → Perkins Coie law firm → Fusion GPS → Christopher Steele. "The dossier was political opposition research, not intelligence. But it was treated as intelligence by the FBI."
> **Key takeaway**: The chain of funding matters. If the FBI had known the political origins of the dossier from the start, the FISA process might have been different.
