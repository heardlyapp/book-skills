# 2 — Skin Types and Conditions

> Source: "Skincare" by Caroline Hirons

## Skin Types

- **Oily**: Shiny, enlarged pores, prone to breakouts. Needs: balancing, non-comedogenic products.
- **Dry**: Flaky, tight, dull. Needs: rich moisturizers, oil-based products, gentle cleansers.
- **Combination**: Oily T-zone, dry cheeks. Needs: targeted approach.
- **Sensitive**: Red, reactive, easily irritated. Needs: fragrance-free, minimal ingredients.

> **Case: The Oily Skin Myth** (Chapter on Oily Skin): Hirons debunks the myth that oily skin doesn't need moisturizer. "Oily skin can be dehydrated. When you strip it with harsh products, it produces more oil to compensate. The solution is hydration, not more stripping."
> **Key takeaway**: Oily skin needs moisturizer. If you strip it, it gets oilier. Hydration is the friend of oily skin, not the enemy.

> **Case: Adult Acne** (Chapter on Acne): Hirons explains that adult acne is different from teenage acne. "Adult acne is often hormonal. It's about the jawline and chin. It's about stress, sleep, and hormones." She recommends BHA, niacinamide, and a gentle, supportive routine — not harsh scrubs.
> **Key takeaway**: Adult acne requires a different approach than teenage acne. Harsh treatments make it worse. Gentle, targeted treatments work better.
