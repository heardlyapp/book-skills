# 1 — The Skincare Routine

> Source: "Skincare" by Caroline Hirons

## The Core Four

1. **Cleanse**: Remove dirt, oil, makeup. Double cleanse at night (oil-based then water-based). Morning: water or gentle cleanser.
2. **Treat**: Active ingredients targeting your concerns (retinol, vitamin C, acids, niacinamide).
3. **Moisturize**: Hydrate and seal. All skin types need moisturizer, even oily.
4. **Protect**: SPF 30-50 every single day. Non-negotiable.

## AM Routine

Cleanse (or water) → Vitamin C → Moisturize → Sunscreen

## PM Routine

Double Cleanse → Treat (retinol/acid) → Moisturize

> **Case: The "Core Four" Philosophy** (Introduction): Hirons argues that most skincare routines are too complicated. "You don't need 12 products. You need four: Cleanse, Treat, Moisturize, Protect. Everything else is nice-to-have." This is the foundation of her approach.
> **Key takeaway**: Before adding anything, master the basics. A simple, consistent routine beats a complex, sporadic one.

> **Case: The Double Cleanse** (Chapter on Cleansing): Hirons is adamant about double cleansing at night. "If you wear makeup or SPF, you need to double cleanse. Period." Oil cleanser first (breaks down oils and waterproof products), then water-based cleanser (cleanses the skin). Morning: just splash with water or use a gentle cleanser.
> **Key takeaway**: Cleansing is the most important step. A good routine starts with clean skin. Don't skip the double cleanse.
