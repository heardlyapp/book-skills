# 4 — Anti-Patterns

> Source: "Skincare" by Caroline Hirons

## Anti-Pattern 1: Ignoring Sunscreen

**The mistake:** Thinking sunscreen is only for sunny days or beach holidays. UV damage is cumulative and happens every day.

## Anti-Pattern 2: Over-Exfoliation

**The mistake:** Using too many acids, too often. Damaging the skin barrier leads to irritation, redness, breakouts.

## Anti-Pattern 3: Too Many Products

**The mistake:** Using 15 products because brands told you to. More is not better. Most people need 5-6 products max.

## Anti-Pattern 4: Believing the Price Myth

**The mistake:** Thinking expensive = effective. Many drugstore products outperform luxury brands. Marketing budgets are not ingredient quality.

> **Case: The "Brand" Trap** (Chapter on Products): Hirons calls out the beauty industry for convincing people they need expensive products. "A $5 drugstore cleanser can be better than a $50 one. A high price tag doesn't mean better ingredients. It often just means a bigger marketing budget."
> **Key takeaway**: Don't let price guide your skincare choices. Read ingredients, not price tags. Some of the best products are affordable.

> **Case: The Over-Exfoliation Epidemic** (Chapter on Acids): Hirons describes patients who "exfoliate their face off" with multiple acids every day, then wonder why their skin is red and broken out. "Your skin barrier is not indestructible. Once you damage it, everything stings, everything breaks you out, and it takes months to repair."
> **Key takeaway**: More exfoliation is not better. Stick to 2-3x per week max. If your skin stings or feels tight, you've overdone it. Back off and repair.
