# Voice and Application: Dawkins' Approach

> Source: The Extended Phenotype, throughout

## Dawkins' Voice

The Extended Phenotype is Dawkins' most rigorous and academic work. The tone is precise, argumentative, and occasionally combative. He builds a case against the organism-centered view of evolution.

> **Case: The Scientific Argument Structure** (throughout): Dawkins defines terms, anticipates objections, and presents evidence systematically. Less accessible than The Selfish Gene but more thorough — his most important theoretical contribution.

> **Case: The Preface's Positioning** (Preface): Dawkins positions the book as a "sequel to The Selfish Gene" that develops the gene-centered view to its logical conclusion. This framing explains why the book is more technical.

> **Key takeaway**: The Extended Phenotype completes the gene-centered view. It's Dawkins' most important contribution to theory.

## Application: Biology

Use the extended phenotype concept for any adaptation. Ask: what does this trait do for the gene?

## Application: Human Technology

Cities, roads, computers, phones — these are our extended phenotype. They are products of human genes.

## Application: Behavior

Animal behavior is phenotype. It can be analyzed from a gene-centered perspective.

## Key Quotes

- "The phenotypic effect of a gene is the sum of all its effects on the world."
- "Organisms are vehicles for replicators."
- "The extended phenotype is the long reach of the gene."
- "There is no fundamental distinction between an organism and its environment."
