# 4 — Anti-Patterns

> Source: *LeBron* by Jeff Benedict — Chapters 1, 18-19, 31.

## Common Errors

### 1. The Televised Exit

**The trap:** "I owe my fans an explanation. A big, public, prime-time explanation."

**Why it fails:** 13 million people watched LeBron tell Cleveland he was leaving. The medium made the rejection feel personal and theatrical. The problem wasn't the decision — it was the show. "I'm taking my talents to South Beach" became a punchline.

> **Case: The Comic Sans Letter** (Chapter 18, aftermath): Dan Gilbert, the Cavaliers' owner, responded to LeBron's Decision with a public letter published in Comic Sans font. Gilbert called LeBron's move "cowardly" and promised the Cavs would win a championship before LeBron. The letter became almost as famous as the Decision itself — for all the wrong reasons. Cleveland didn't win until LeBron returned. The lesson: when you're angry, don't write. Especially not in Comic Sans.
> **Key takeaway**: The Decision taught LeBron and Gilbert the same lesson from opposite sides: the medium matters as much as the message. Never announce a breakup on prime time. Never respond to heartbreak in anger.

### 2. Protecting Your Brand at All Costs

The worst advice LeBron received was from brand managers who told him not to speak out on social issues. The Trayvon Martin hoodie photo was "risky." The George Floyd statement was "polarizing." But silence is also a statement — and it's often the wrong one.

### 3. Assuming Past Success Prevents Future Failure

After seven successful seasons in Cleveland, LeBron assumed he could do no wrong. He was wrong. Past performance does not predict future reputation.

### 4. Taking Advice from People Who Don't Share Your Values

The Decision was Maverick Carter's idea. Carter was loyal and brilliant — but also young and inexperienced. The advisor who loves you may not have the wisdom to guide you.

### 5. Forgetting That Money Changes Everything

The Horsemen survived billions because they had explicit agreements. "No one gets cut out." Without those agreements, money destroys friendships.
