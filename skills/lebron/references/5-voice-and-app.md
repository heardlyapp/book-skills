# 5 — Voice & App

> Source: *LeBron* by Jeff Benedict — voice and scenarios.

## The Book's Voice

Benedict writes with journalist objectivity — admiring but not hagiographic. He includes the ugly moments (The Decision, the LeBron vs. Spoelstra tension, the Laker injuries, the controversy over China) alongside the triumphs. The book's structure follows LeBron's life chronologically, but each chapter is built around a specific tension or turning point.

## Five Scenarios

### 1. The Person Building Their First Team

**Situation:** You're hiring your founding team. You have no money for experienced talent.

**Playbook:** Hire for loyalty, train for skill. The Horsemen had zero experience. They had LeBron's trust. In a startup, the founding team's belief in the mission matters more than their CVs.

### 2. The Person Recovering from a Public Failure

**Situation:** You made a very public mistake. The internet is coming for you.

**Playbook:** The 10-year recovery timeline. You don't control the narrative in week one. You control it over a decade. Win where it matters. Return to the scene of the crime and make it right.

### 3. The Person With a Platform

**Situation:** You have influence. People want you to stay quiet on an issue you care about.

**Playbook:** The Trayvon Martin test: does this cost you something to say? If it costs nothing, reconsider. If it costs real risk, that's usually the sign you should speak.

### 4. The Person Caught Between Hometown and Growth

**Situation:** Your hometown made you. You feel guilty for leaving.

**Playbook:** LeBron left Cleveland twice. The first time, he announced it on national TV and felt guilty. The second time, he went to LA after winning Cleveland a title and felt free. The difference: he had paid his debt. You can leave your hometown. Just make sure you've honored it before you go.

### 5. The Wealthy Person Who Started Poor

**Situation:** You made money. Your old friends treat you differently. Your new friends don't understand you.

**Playbook:** The Horsemen survived because they agreed on the rules before the money came. "No one gets left behind." If you haven't had that conversation with your inner circle, have it now.
