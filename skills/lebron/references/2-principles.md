# 2 — Key Principles

> Source: *LeBron* by Jeff Benedict — across all chapters.

## The 7 Principles

### 1. Hire for Loyalty, Train for Skill

The Four Horsemen had zero experience in sports management, marketing, or talent representation. LeBron hired them because he trusted them. He taught them the business. They taught him the world.

> **Case: Rich Paul's Rise** (Chapter 25): Rich Paul met LeBron at the Akron-Canton Airport in 2002 — LeBron was boarding a plane, Paul was selling vintage jerseys out of his car. They became friends. When LeBron needed an agent in 2012, he didn't hire CAA. He hired Paul. Paul had no license, no clients, no office. He slept on floors and learned the business by doing it. By 2020, he was the most powerful agent in the NBA, representing Anthony Davis, Draymond Green, and dozens of other stars.
> **Key takeaway**: Experience can be acquired. Trust cannot. Hire people whose values you know, then invest in their skills.

### 2. Your Origin Story Is Your Power

LeBron never ran from Akron. He returned. He built the I Promise School in the neighborhood where he grew up. He built SpringHill telling stories about Akron first. The place that made you is not a wound to hide — it's the foundation of your narrative.

### 3. The Recovery Defines You More Than the Failure

"The Decision" was a disaster. LeBron could have hidden, blamed others, or bought PR. He did none of that. He won two championships in Miami. He returned to Cleveland and delivered the city's first title in 52 years. He built a school in Akron. The 10-year recovery was more impressive than any single victory.

### 4. Use Your Voice When It Costs You Something

After Trayvon Martin was killed in 2012, LeBron posted a photo of the Miami Heat in hoodies. His brand managers at Nike were uncomfortable. He did it anyway. "If something costs you something to say, it means something when you say it."

### 5. Build Institutions, Not Just Philanthropy

The I Promise School is a public school in Akron. It has regular teachers, a principal, and district oversight. It's not a charity — it's an institution. That means it outlasts LeBron.

### 6. Protect Your Inner Circle From Money

The Horsemen survived billions because they had an agreement: no one gets left behind, and no one gets cut out. Money that enters a friendship must be governed by rules that the friendship set, not the money set.

### 7. Never Forget Who Made You

Gloria James is at every game. LeBron still calls Akron home. He still talks to the Walker family. The people who made you are the only people who can keep you real.
