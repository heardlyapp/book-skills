# Core Framework: The Missing Middle + MELDS

## The Three Waves of Business Transformation

### First Wave: Standardized Processes (1910s-1970s)
Henry Ford's assembly line. Deconstructed manufacturing into repeatable, measurable steps. Optimization through time-and-motion analysis. The era of mass production.

### Second Wave: Automated Processes (1970s-2000s)
IT-driven business process reengineering (BPR). Desktop computers, databases, and software automated back-office tasks. Walmart rode this wave. UPS transformed from package delivery to global logistics. **Key trap:** machines replaced humans.

### Third Wave: Adaptive Processes (now)
AI systems enable fluid, self-adapting processes. Human-machine teams replace rigid assembly lines. Processes respond to real-time data rather than fixed sequences. **Key insight:** this wave is about augmentation, not replacement.

> **💡 Distinction:** Waze vs early GPS. Early GPS digitized paper maps (second wave). Waze combines real-time user data + AI to create living, dynamic routes (third wave). Apply this test to any process: are you digitizing old maps or creating Waze?

## The Missing Middle

**Core concept:** The dynamic space where humans and machines collaborate to achieve orders-of-magnitude performance increases. "Missing" because almost no one talks about it.

### Left Side: Humans Help Machines (3 Roles)

| Role | Category | What they do | Example |
|------|----------|-------------|---------|
| **Trainers** | Training | Teach AI systems to perform tasks; develop empathy, personality, cultural awareness; clean training data | Koko teaching Alexa empathy; Microsoft poets training Cortana's personality |
| **Explainers** | Explanation | Bridge the gap between black-box AI and business leaders; conduct algorithm forensics; classify system transparency | ZestFinance explaining loan decisions; LIME for model-agnostic explanations |
| **Sustainers** | Sustaining | Ensure AI is ethical, safe, and functioning correctly; ethics compliance; machine relations management | AI safety engineers; machine relations managers conducting performance reviews |

### Right Side: Machines Augment Humans (3 Categories)

| Category | What it does | Example |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| **Amplification** | Gives people extraordinary data-driven insights | Autodesk's Dreamcatcher generating millions of chair designs for one designer to curate |
| **Interaction** | Employs AI agents with natural-language interfaces at scale | SEB's Aida handling 1M customer conversations; Amazon's Alexa becoming the brand |
| **Embodiment** | Physical collaboration in shared spaces | Mercedes-Benz cobots on assembly lines; Amazon warehouse robots bringing shelves to workers |

> **Case: Stitch Fix** (Part Two Introduction)
> A born-digital company built around the missing middle. ML algorithms narrow options by style/size/brand; human stylists use expertise and judgment to finalize selections. Both learn from each keep/return decision. Over 2,800 stylists work with AI augmentation daily.

> **Case: Rio Tinto** (Part Two Introduction)
> A mining company running its fleet of autonomous drills, excavators, and driverless trucks from a central operations center. Data analysts, engineers, and remote operators work together — more like NASA mission control than a traditional mine.

## MELDS Framework — The 5 Pillars

### M — Mindset: Reimagine, Don't Automate
- Move from "How can AI make our current process faster?" to "What if we completely rethought this process?"
- **3-step method:** Discover & Describe → Co-create → Scale & Sustain
- Use design thinking to identify customer pain points that were previously impossible to solve

### E — Experimentation: Build-Measure-Learn
- Amazon Go: open a real store, test with employees first, iterate on technology, treat failures as data
- SEB: test Aida on 15,000 internal employees → then deploy to 1M customers
- Walmart's Store No. 8: incubator isolated from corporate bureaucracy

### L — Leadership: Responsible AI from Day One
- **Guardrails:** Set boundaries on what AI can/can't do. Keyword filters, sentiment monitors.
- **Human checkpoints:** Visual dashboards, explainability tools. Don't let AI be a black box.
- **Moral crumple zones:** When AI systems fail, ensure people don't become "liability sponges." Build accountability into algorithms, not just humans.

### D — Data: Build a Data Supply Chain
- **Dynamic, not static:** Real-time data from sensors, social media, IoT — continuously flowing
- **Widen access:** Syndicated data (Nielsen), open data, data-as-a-service
- **Increase velocity:** Hot/cold data tiering (Facebook stores popular photos on fast servers)
- **Enable discovery:** Democratize analytics so non-technical users can interact with data

## Key Case Studies by Chapter

### Chapter 1: The Self-Aware Factory Floor
- **Fanuc + Preferred Networks:** Robotic arms that use deep reinforcement learning to master tasks in 8 hours — no programming needed. Once one learns, it shares knowledge with all others ("distributed learning").
- **GE Predix / Digital Twins:** Virtual models of jet engines, turbines, wind farms. Engineers run virtual flights, test bird strikes, optimize wind farm output (20% gain = $100M value over lifetime). Maintenance shifts from fixed schedules to predictive, as-needed.
- **Symbotic warehouse robots:** Machine vision + AI to handle oddly shaped packages. No pallet storage needed — robots move goods directly to shelves. Warehouse stores 2x goods or operates in half the space.
- **AeroFarms:** Vertical farm in Newark using 95% less water, 50% less fertilizer, zero pesticides. 2M lbs of produce/year, 15 miles from Manhattan.

### Chapter 2: Accounting for Robots
- **Global bank AML:** ML algorithms reduced false positives by 30%, investigation time by 40%. Human experts focus on truly suspicious cases.
- **Virgin Trains / inSTREAM:** ML platform with NLP reads/sorts/routes complaints. Manual work decreased 85%, correspondence increased 20%. Now handles email, fax, snail mail, social media.
- **Unilever hiring:** 12 neuroscience games + AI video interview analysis (HireVue). Applications doubled to 30K, time-to-hire 4 months → 4 weeks, recruiter review time down 75%. Universities represented: 840 → 2,600.
- **Gigster:** AI assembles ad hoc software development teams. Quotes generated by AI (500+ feature "software genome" mapped). Team builder matches developers to project needs. Productivity monitored by AI.

### Chapter 3: The Ultimate Innovation Machine
- **Tesla:** Every new car has full self-driving hardware. Background AI software simulates driving decisions. Only when simulations are safer than human driver → released via software update. Tesla drivers, in aggregate, train the fleet.
- **GNS Healthcare / REFS:** AI generated 45 quadrillion hypotheses on drug interactions in 3 months — replicating a 2-year human study. Also uncovered a new drug interaction only patients had noticed. Recreated PCSK9 biology (70 years of research, $10B+) in 10 months for <$1M.
- **Nike sprint spikes:** AI design software optimized for stiffness AND lightness. 3D-printed prototypes. Result: shoe shaves 0.1 second off sprinter's time (difference between 1st and 4th place).

### Chapter 4: Say Hello to Your New Front-Office Bots
- **Coca-Cola + Einstein:** Smart cooler cabinets. Employee takes photo → AI counts bottles, predicts restocking (weather, promotions, historical data, seasonal trends). Saves paperwork, improves sales.
- **Ralph Lauren + Oak Labs:** Smart mirror in fitting room recognizes items via RFID. Displays details, changes lighting, checks other sizes/colors. Tracks conversion ratios.
- **Percolata:** AI scheduling for retail stores. 53% overstaffed, 33% understaffed. Sales boosted 10-30%. Freed 3 hours/day of manager time.
- **Almax mannequins:** Facial recognition identifies gender, age, race. Chinese shoppers = 1/3 of one store's 4pm+ customers → Chinese-speaking staff deployed during those hours.
