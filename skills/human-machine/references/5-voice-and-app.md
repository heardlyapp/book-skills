# Author Voice & Application Scenarios

## Author Voice

### Tone Characteristics
- **Evidence-driven:** Every claim backed by real company case studies, Accenture's survey of 1,500+ practitioners, or peer-reviewed research
- **Optimistic but grounded:** Acknowledges AI risks (job displacement, bias, ethical concerns) while focusing on actionable solutions
- **Executive-facing:** Written for C-suite decision makers — practical, framework-oriented, not academic
- **Balanced:** Explicitly addresses both opportunities AND dangers of AI. Conclusion donates royalties to retraining programs — not just talk

### Signature Sentence Structure
- Short, declarative claims followed by concrete examples
- Framework-first: introduce a model (MELDS, Missing Middle), then populate with cases
- "The paradox is that..." — frequently surfaces counterintuitive insights
- "In the past... Now..." — binary contrast to highlight transformation

### Key Quotable Lines

> "The simple truth is that machines are not taking over the world, nor are they obviating the need for humans in the workplace."

> "The third wave of business transformation is about using machines to complement and augment human capabilities, not replace them."

> "In the missing middle, humans and machines aren't adversaries, fighting for each other's jobs. Instead, they are symbiotic partners, each pushing the other to higher levels of performance."

> "Think like Waze." — the ultimate metaphor for third-wave reimagination

> "AI lets people work more like humans and less like robots."

> "The real opportunity is to make work more human."

> "When humans and machines are allowed to do what each does best, the result is a virtuous cycle of enhanced work."

> "The great irony is that some of the most-automated environments — the factory and other industrial settings — are experiencing a renaissance of human labor."

## 5 Application Scenarios

### Scenario 1: AI Strategy for a Traditional Manufacturer

**Context:** A 50-year-old manufacturing company wants to "adopt AI" but leadership is split between those who see it as a cost-cutting tool (replace workers with robots) and those who are afraid of it.

**How Human + Machine helps:**
- Use the Three Waves framework to diagnose where the company is stuck (second-wave thinking)
- Introduce the Missing Middle to show that the goal is human+machine, not machine vs human
- Walk through MELDS: Mindset (reimagine, don't automate) → Experimentation (pilot a cobot alongside workers) → Leadership (responsible deployment, retrain displaced workers) → Data (sensor infrastructure) → Skills (trainers for cobots, fusion skills for the workforce)
- Reference GE's Predix and BMW's collaborative factories as proof points

### Scenario 2: Building a Responsible AI Team

**Context:** A fintech company just deployed an ML-based credit scoring system. A customer complaint revealed potential bias, and the CEO wants to know who should be on the AI governance team.

**How Human + Machine helps:**
- Map the 6 Missing Middle roles to the company's needs:
  - **Trainer:** Data hygienist to clean training data + interaction modeler for the customer-facing chatbot
  - **Explainer:** Algorithm forensics analyst to investigate the bias complaint + explainability strategist to choose transparent models
  - **Sustainer:** Ethics compliance manager + machine relations manager
- Reference ZestFinance for how explainable AI lending works in practice
- Reference ZestFinance's Veracity/Stability/Prudence scoring framework
- Reference Capital One's Center of Excellence model

### Scenario 3: Redesigning Customer Service with AI

**Context:** A telecom company's customer service center is overwhelmed. They want to deploy a chatbot to handle basic inquiries. Previous attempts failed — customers hated the bot.

**How Human + Machine helps:**
- Diagnose the failure: likely Anti-Pattern #1 (automated existing processes without reimagining) or #3 (underestimated algorithm aversion)
- Walk through the 3-step reimagination process:
  1. **Discover:** What are the real pain points? Not "calls are too long" but "customers repeat their problem to 3 different people"
  2. **Co-create:** Design the missing middle — AI handles Tier 1 (account balance, bill pay), human agents handle escalated issues, both learn from each interaction
  3. **Scale:** Test internally first (SEB's approach)
- Reference Virgin Trains' inSTREAM system (85% reduction in manual work)
- Reference SEB's Aida (1M customers handled, 4,000 conversations in first 3 weeks)
- Reference Koko empathy training to make the bot not sound robotic

### Scenario 4: Overcoming Algorithm Aversion in a Hospital

**Context:** A hospital deployed AI to optimize bed allocation and operating room scheduling. Doctors and nurses ignore the recommendations. The CIO is frustrated.

**How Human + Machine helps:**
- Explain Algorithm Aversion (Anti-Pattern #3) — this is a known psychological phenomenon, not a technology failure
- Implement the two fixes from the book:
  1. **Add explanations:** Every AI recommendation should say WHY — "Patient X to Bed Y because they'll need cardiac monitoring"
  2. **Give control:** Nurses keep final authority, have ability to modify within parameters
- Reference the petroleum engineer case: engineers who could modify AI parameters trusted the system more
- Reference the hospital bed study: zero improvement → add explanations → system works

### Scenario 5: Training for the Future Workplace

**Context:** A company's HR department wants to know what skills to look for when hiring for their new AI division.

**How Human + Machine helps:**
- Start with the 8 Fusion Skills diagnostic:
  1. **Intelligent Interrogation:** Can they ask layered questions of an AI system?
  2. **Holistic Melding:** Do they develop mental models of how AI works?
  3. **Relentless Reimagining:** Can they redesign processes from scratch?
  4. **Reciprocal Apprenticing:** Are they willing to learn from AND teach machines?
  5. **Judgment Integration:** Can they make calls when AI is uncertain?
  6. **Rehumanizing Time:** Will they use freed-up time for higher-value work?
  7. **Responsible Normalizing:** Can they shape how others perceive AI?
  8. **Bot-based Empowerment:** Do they use AI tools to amplify their own output?

- Then map to the 6 Missing Middle roles — which specific roles does the company need most?
- Reference: 61% of activities in the missing middle require employees to "do different things and to do things differently" — expect to retrain, not just hire
