# Key Principles: 8 Fusion Skills for an AI Workplace

The eight fusion skills define what humans need to thrive in the Missing Middle. Each combines human talent with machine capability — not technical skills, but relational, judgmental, and adaptive capabilities.

## 1. Rehumanizing Time

**Definition:** The ability to increase the time available for distinctly human tasks — interpersonal interactions, creativity, decision making — in a reimagined business process.

**How it works:**
- AI offloads routine, repetitive tasks (data entry, sorting, basic analysis)
- Freed time should be reinvested in higher-value human activities, not just more throughput
- **Key trap:** If managers don't rethink productivity, AI just means more work, not better work

> **Case: AT&T sales team** (Chapter 8)
> AI pulls sales leads across multiple systems, freeing sales associates to spend more time developing relationships with customers instead of hunting through databases.

> **Case: UPMC + Microsoft** (Chapter 8)
> 54% of doctors show burnout symptoms, linked to digital medical records. AI with NLP listens to doctor-patient conversations and auto-fills records. Doctors reclaim time for actual patient care.

## 2. Responsible Normalizing

**Definition:** The act of responsibly shaping the purpose and perception of human-machine interaction as it relates to individuals, businesses, and society.

**How it works:**
- Bridge the gap between AI capability and public understanding
- Anticipate resistance from workers, customers, and communities
- CEOs must develop a clear thesis on the future of work that addresses concerns proactively

> **Case: Audi piloted driving campaign** (Chapter 8)
> Audi presents self-driving as human-machine collaboration, not full autonomy. Ads show a T-Rex at the wheel — emotional, not technical. Frames the technology as assistance, not replacement.

> **Case: Telecom executive** (Chapter 8)
> When AI was introduced, the company's clear message was: "AI makes current workers more successful, not laid off." A growing business means displaced workers are reskilled into new roles.

## 3. Judgment Integration

**Definition:** The judgment-based ability to decide a course of action when a machine is uncertain about what to do — or lacks business/ethical context.

**How it works:**
- Humans need to sense where, how, and when to step in
- Combine analytics (model deviation tracking) with intuition (ethical concerns)
- Most valuable when AI has a "framing problem" — it can get things right but can't read situations

> **Case: Capital One** (Chapter 8)
> Adam Wenchel's team tracks how ML models deviate from rule-based systems or previous versions. Employees are encouraged to voice concerns: "We may be getting great accuracy, but I have concerns about how we're getting there."

> **Case: Shell + Sensabot** (Chapter 8)
> In Kazakhstan, Sensabot (first inspection robot approved for oil/gas) is operated remotely by a worker who watches video feed and makes judgment calls about risks. Human expertise remains crucial.

## 4. Intelligent Interrogation

**Definition:** Knowing how best to ask questions of AI, across levels of abstraction, to get the insights you need.

**How it works:**
- Probe AI systems with layered questions: "How certain are you?" "What do you recommend?" "What happens if we change X?"
- The machine trains the human in how to use it, just as the human trains the machine
- Includes knowing when outputs don't make sense — the machine is not all-powerful

> **Case: GE Digital Twin maintenance** (Chapter 8)
> Worker asks the digital twin: "What's wrong?" → "How bad is it?" → "What are my options?" → "What's the cost?" Each question moves from technical to operational to financial. A 10-minute conversation saves $12M.

> **Case: Revionics retail pricing** (Chapter 8)
> Steve Schnur asks: "If you raise the price of Advil, what happens to Tylenol?" The system reveals relationships between 7,000 SKUs. "Show me the most beneficial price changes" — the smarter the question, the better the insight.

## 5. Bot-based Empowerment

**Definition:** Working well with AI agents to extend your capabilities — creating superpowers in business processes and careers.

**How it works:**
- Assemble a portfolio of bots (scheduling, research, social media, writing, coordination)
- Scale yourself as if you had a staff of digital assistants
- The effect: a freelancer can operate like a CEO

> **Case: Marc Benioff + Einstein Forecasting** (Chapter 8)
> Salesforce's CEO relies on Einstein in staff meetings. AI produces unbiased forecasts. Benioff: "Without bias… it has transformed me as a CEO." Minimizes internal politics.

> **Case: Shivon Zilis, Bloomberg Beta** (Chapter 8)
> "Most knowledge workers spend less than half their time doing things they're really good at. The rest is research, meetings, coordination — tasks easily done by a machine."

**Available bots mentioned:** Clara/x.ai (scheduling), Howdy/Standup Bot/Tatsu/Geekbot (Slack bot assistants), Gridspace Sift/Pogo (meeting notes), Textio/IBM Watson Tone Analyzer (writing), Doli.io (social media)

## 6. Holistic Melding

**Definition:** The ability to develop robust mental models of AI agents to improve collaborative outcomes — the AI becomes an extension of your body or mind.

**How it works:**
- Physical melding: surgical robots, cobots on factory floors
- Cognitive melding: Google autocomplete that feels like it's reading your thoughts
- Requires trial and error — teams "decide how to decide" with their AI partners

> **Case: Robotic eye surgery** (Chapter 8)
> Oxford University's John Radcliffe Hospital. Surgeon sits at a console with a joystick. Robot eliminates hand tremor. Surgeon's skill is projected through the machine to make incisions a hundredth of a millimeter thick.

> **Case: Kindred AI** (Chapter 8)
> Canadian startup pairing human "pilots" in VR headsets with robots. Movement data is transmitted directly to robots. Melding trains robots to perform dexterous tasks at ultrafast speeds.

> **Case: NASA Mars Rovers** (Chapter 8)
> Programmers and robots form ad hoc teams that "decide how to decide" — protocols, codes of conduct, rules of governing emerge organically.

## 7. Reciprocal Apprenticing

**Definition:** (1) Performing tasks alongside AI agents so they can learn new skills. (2) On-the-job training for people to work well within AI-enhanced processes.

**How it works:**
- AI learns by watching human operators (e.g., Amelia observing customer service agents)
- Humans learn from AI (e.g., getting recommendations and improving their own performance)
- Traditional one-way technology education → two-way learning relationship
- **Best practice:** Make apprenticing transparent — don't let employees feel they're secretly training their replacements

> **Case: IPsoft's Amelia** (Chapter 8)
> Amelia observes human behavior in the background, escalates when uncertain, and watches how the human solves the problem. Unlike traditional automation that degrades over time, intelligent automation continuously improves.

> **Case: Financial services CIO** (Chapter 8)
> When AI was deployed in loans, senior team members became trainers for new data scientists and taught the AI algorithm. Role shifted from processing loans to training people AND machines.

## 8. Relentless Reimagining

**Definition:** The rigorous discipline of creating new processes and business models from scratch, rather than simply automating old processes.

**How it works:**
- This is the meta-skill that powers all other fusion skills
- Requires "neural opportunism" — using AI tools to extend your cognition
- Built on a culture of "test and learn" — fail fast to succeed quickly

> **Case: Capital One** (Chapter 8)
> First bank to launch on Amazon Alexa. First to introduce an intelligent chatbot (Eno). Established a machine learning Center of Excellence. Created "the Garage" — an innovation center where staffers are given no specific orders, just "massively improve our consumers' experience."

> **Case: Stitch Fix** (Part Two Introduction)
> Reimagined online clothing retail from scratch around the missing middle. ML handles structured data; stylists handle unstructured data (Pinterest boards, customer notes). Both learn from every keep/return.

**Note for AI practitioners:** The 8 fusion skills require expertise in neither machine learning nor programming. They require thoughtful humans eager to adapt these skills to their specific business context.
