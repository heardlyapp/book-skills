# Key Principles — Discipline Equals Freedom

## I. Discipline Is the Root of All Good Qualities

"Discipline: The root of all good qualities. The driver of daily execution." Without discipline, nothing else works. Not talent. Not intelligence. Not opportunity. Jocko's fundamental claim: "Discipline equals freedom." The more disciplined you are, the more freedom you have to do what matters. The undisciplined are slaves to their own impulses.

## II. You Control Your Mind — or It Controls You

"Mind control" is not about controlling others. It's about declaring martial law on your own mind. "Weakness doesn't get a vote. Laziness doesn't get a vote. Sadness doesn't get a vote." When people ask "How do I get tougher?" — the answer is "Be tougher." It's a decision. You can make it right now.

## III. Go. Now. Not Tomorrow. Not "When You Feel Ready."

Jocko's cure for procrastination: "Start right HERE. Start right NOW." The idea won't execute itself. The book won't write itself. The weights won't move themselves. Taking a break is "the one thing I put off until tomorrow." When tomorrow comes, if you still need rest, take it. But chances are the desire to rest was just weakness.

## IV. Good. Say It Out Loud.

Jocko's single-word response to every setback. "When things are going bad: Don't get all bummed out, don't get startled, don't get frustrated. No. Just look at the issue and say: 'Good.'" Mission canceled? Good. Didn't get promoted? Good. Got injured? Good. Got beat? Good. This is not toxic positivity — it's focusing on the solution instead of dwelling on the problem. "If you're still breathing, that means you've still got some fight left in you."

## V. Internal Compromise Is Never Allowed

External compromise is necessary — with people, teams, situations. But "with myself, I have to hold the line." No compromise on: working hard, training hard, improving yourself, owning mistakes, facing demons, maintaining self-discipline. "On those points there will be No Compromise. NOT NOW. NOT EVER."

## VI. Draw Fire — Absorb the Negativity

Jocko's combat leadership lesson: when things go bad, step up and "draw fire." Be the one who people look to. "Absorb the impact — and the negativity." Be the one good thing standing tall that can be relied upon. "The positive attitude will spread." And even if you lose the battle, "our spirit will never surrender."

## VII. Honor the Fallen by Living Fully

Jocko's why is the men who didn't come home: Marc Lee, Mike Monsoor, Ryan Job. "I will make every day — every minute — every second — I will make it all count. I will live to honor their sacrifice." Death is the ultimate teacher of life's value. "Let us LIVE — for those WHO live no more."
