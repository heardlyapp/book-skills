# Techniques — Discipline Equals Freedom

## How to Build Unshakeable Self-Discipline

### Technique 1: Declare Martial Law on Your Mind

When you feel weak, lazy, sad, frustrated, angry — tell those feelings "they don't get a vote." Jocko: "You are declaring martial law on your mind." This is the technique behind "just go anyway." The feeling doesn't need to disappear. It just doesn't get to decide.

### Technique 2: The "Good" Reframe

Make it a reflex. Something goes wrong → say the word "Good." Out loud. Jocko's team learned to anticipate it: "I already know what you're going to say." The technique works because it forces your brain to look for the opportunity in every setback. The key: you're not denying reality — you're accepting it and immediately moving to the solution.

### Technique 3: HERE and NOW

Stop the planning paralysis. The technique is simple: "Where do you start? HERE. When? NOW." Don't wait for the perfect moment. Don't research one more article. Don't debate pros and cons. Take the first step physically. The idea won't execute itself.

### Technique 4: The No-Compromise Line

Define your internal lines. Write them down. Jocko's: "I am going to work hard. I am going to train hard. I am going to improve myself. I am not going to rest on my laurels. I am going to own my mistakes and confront them. I am going to face my demons." On those lines: "No Compromise."

### Technique 5: The Swap (Emotion ↔ Logic)

When one fails, use the other. "When it doesn't make any logical sense to go on, that's when you use your emotion, your anger, your frustration, your fear, to push further." When your emotions say "I've had enough," override with cold logic and willpower. The balance of the two is "Destroyer Mode."

### Technique 6: Go Through the Motions

On days when you're not feeling it — don't skip. Go through the motions anyway. "Even if I am just going through the motions — I GO THROUGH THE MOTIONS." The key insight: often the feeling of "I need a break" is just weakness. By procrastinating on the break — "taking a break is the one thing I put off until tomorrow" — you discover you didn't need it.

### Technique 7: Regret as Fuel, Not Weight

The only value in regret is the lesson. Extract the lesson, then move on. But use the FEAR of future regret as fuel. "Let the fear of regret fuel you — to take action — today — now. Take action now to become a person not filled with regret, but a person filled with knowledge and strength."
