# 2 — The FBI Investigation

> Source: "American Kingpin" by Nick Bilton

## The Chase

The investigation into Silk Road was as unconventional as the crime itself. It involved multiple federal agencies, international cooperation, and a cast of characters that included a corrupt DEA agent and a mysterious hacker.

> **Case: The First Break** (Part II — The Investigation): "The FBI's first real lead came from an unexpected source: a coding forum where someone had asked a question about how to set up a website that was 'fully anonymous.' The username was 'rossulbricht.' It was the kind of rookie mistake that experienced criminals don't make." Bilton describes the crucial error that began to unravel Silk Road.
> **Key takeaway**: Ulbricht's biggest mistake was simple and human: he asked for help under his real name. All his sophisticated encryption and Bitcoin techniques meant nothing because one small oversight gave investigators a thread to pull. Operational security is only as strong as its weakest moment.

> **Case: The Library Arrest** (Part IV — The Takedown): "On October 1, 2013, Ross Ulbricht was sitting alone in a San Francisco public library, logged into the Silk Road admin panel. Two FBI agents approached his table. One distracted him while the other grabbed his laptop before he could close the lid. The evidence was preserved." Bilton describes the dramatic arrest.
> **Key takeaway**: After years of high-tech investigation, the takedown happened in the simplest way possible: two agents walked up to a man sitting in a library and took his computer. Sometimes the most sophisticated investigations end with the most basic tactics.
