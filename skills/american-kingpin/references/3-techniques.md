# 3 — Technology, Tor, and Bitcoin

> Source: "American Kingpin" by Nick Bilton

## The Anonymity Puzzle

Silk Road relied on two key technologies: Tor (for anonymous browsing) and Bitcoin (for anonymous payments). Together, they created what seemed like an impenetrable shield.

> **Case: The Bitcoin Trail** (Part II — The Economy): "Bitcoin was the perfect currency for Silk Road. Transactions were recorded on the public blockchain, but they weren't linked to real-world identities. Law enforcement could see the money moving but couldn't tell who was moving it. At the time, Bitcoin was still obscure enough that most investigators didn't understand it." Bilton explains why Bitcoin was essential to Silk Road's operation.
> **Key takeaway**: Bitcoin enabled the Silk Road — but it also created a permanent, public record of every transaction. Years later, when investigators had the keys to link blockchain addresses to people, the trail was still there. Anonymity in Bitcoin was (and is) pseudonymity, not true anonymity.

> **Case: The Tor Connection** (Part II — The Technology): "Tor — The Onion Router — routed internet traffic through multiple layers of encryption, making it nearly impossible to trace where a user was connecting from. For law enforcement, Tor was a nightmare. If Ulbricht had been more careful with Tor, he might never have been caught." Bilton describes the technology that made the investigation so difficult.
> **Key takeaway**: Tor worked as designed. The breakdown in anonymity came from human error, not technological failure. Ulbricht's use of Tor was fine — it was his use of his real email address, his real connections to his real identity, that gave him away. Technology can't protect you from your own mistakes.
