# Techniques: Cultivating the Evolving Self

> Source: The Evolving Self, Chapters 6-8

## Investing Attention Wisely

The fundamental skill of conscious evolution: directing psychic energy toward meaningful goals.

> **Case: The Attention Audit** (Chapter 6): Csikszentmihalyi suggests tracking how you spend attention for a week. Most people discover vast amounts wasted on passive consumption, worry, and distraction. The first step to evolving the self is knowing where your attention actually goes.

> **Key takeaway**: Attention is the most valuable resource. Audit where it goes, then invest it deliberately in activities that build complexity.

## Choosing Memes Consciously

We are surrounded by cultural memes — values, beliefs, practices. Most absorb them unconsciously.

> **Case: The Consumer Meme** (Chapter 7): Advertising promotes "more possessions = happiness." Csikszentmihalyi argues this meme is destructive. Choosing to reject it and adopt alternatives (simplicity, relationships, growth) is an act of conscious evolution.

> **Case: The Success Meme** (Chapter 7): The cultural meme that success = money + status leads to psychic entropy for many who achieve it. Choosing a different definition of success — complexity, meaning, contribution — produces a more evolved self.

> **Key takeaway**: We can choose our memes. The memes we adopt shape our entire lives.

## Building Complexity Through Skills

Cultivate diverse skills (differentiation) and connect them through purpose (integration).

> **Case: The Lifelong Learner** (Chapter 8): Someone who takes up new challenges throughout life — instruments, languages, crafts — builds differentiation. Connecting these through a growth narrative builds integration.
