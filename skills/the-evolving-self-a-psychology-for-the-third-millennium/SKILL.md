## Core Framework Quick Reference

- **Psychic Entropy** — Disorder in consciousness: when information conflicts with goals or attention fragments. The opposite of flow. The default state of untrained consciousness.
- **Flow** — Optimal experience: complete absorption in activity, loss of self-consciousness, clear goals, immediate feedback, distorted time. Ordered consciousness.
- **Complexity** — The integration of differentiation and integration. A complex person integrates diverse experiences and perspectives.
- **Psychic Energy** — Attention. The most valuable resource. Where you invest attention determines who you become.
- **Autotelic Self** — A self whose goals come from within. Self-directed, purpose-driven, continually evolving.
- **Meme** — A unit of cultural information. The cultural equivalent of a gene. Csikszentmihalyi explores which memes serve evolution.

## Key Principles

1. **Consciousness can be directed** — We shape our lives through how we invest attention. This is our greatest freedom.
2. **Flow is the engine of growth** — Optimal experience pushes us to higher levels of complexity.
3. **Psychic entropy is the enemy** — Addiction, apathy, anxiety, and distraction fragment attention and prevent growth.
4. **Complexity is evolution's goal** — The evolved self integrates diverse experiences without losing coherence.
5. **The self can evolve throughout life** — Through intentional cultivation of attention, skills, and meaningful relationships.
6. **Culture shapes consciousness** — The memes and values we absorb determine how we invest psychic energy.
7. **Meaning emerges from connection** — A meaningful life connects individual goals to larger systems — family, community, humanity, the cosmos.

## Anti-Pattern Summary

Biggest mistake: **believing consciousness is passive.** We can direct our attention and shape our experience. Second mistake: equating pleasure with flow. Pleasure is passive consumption; flow requires active engagement and challenge. Third: thinking the self is fixed. The self evolves — but only through active cultivation. Fourth: ignoring cultural memes. The values we absorb unconsciously shape our lives. Choose your memes deliberately.
