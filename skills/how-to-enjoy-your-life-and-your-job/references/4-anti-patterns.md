# Anti-Patterns

## 1. Criticism as a Management Tool

**The mistake**: Believing that criticism motivates people to improve.

**Fix**: Praise builds. Criticism destroys. If you must correct someone, do it with appreciation first and privately.

> **Case: The Humiliated Employee** (Part 1): A manager criticized a report in front of the whole team. The employee felt humiliated. Instead of improving the report, he resented his boss and stopped caring. Carnegie's principle: never criticize in public. If you must address a problem, do it privately and with respect.
> **Key takeaway**: Public criticism humiliates. Private, respectful feedback improves. The difference is everything.

## 2. Trying to Make People Like You

**The mistake**: Focusing on being liked instead of being interested.

**Fix**: The paradox is that when you stop trying to be liked and start being genuinely interested, people naturally like you.

## 3. Borrowing Trouble from Tomorrow

**The mistake**: Worrying about things that haven't happened yet.

**Fix**: "Don't cross the bridge until you come to it." Most of what we worry about never happens. Living in day-tight compartments frees you from imagined catastrophes.

> **Case: The Worried Executive** (Part 2): A CEO spent sleepless nights worrying about a potential lawsuit that might bankrupt his company. He finally asked: "What's the worst that can happen?" The answer: bankruptcy. He accepted that. Then he realized he could start over. The worry lost its power. (The lawsuit never materialized.)
> **Key takeaway**: Accepting the worst-case scenario frees you to deal with the present. Most fears dissolve when you face them directly.

## 4. Complaining as Conversation

**The mistake**: Using complaints as a way to bond with others.

**Fix**: People eventually avoid those who complain. Find something positive in every situation and lead with that.

## 5. Forgetting Names

**The mistake**: Not bothering to remember names because you're "bad with names."

**Fix**: "Bad with names" is a choice, not a trait. Use the Name Memory System consistently.
