# Core Framework: Carnegie's Six Ways to Make People Like You

## The Core Insight

The book is built on Dale Carnegie's foundational insight: "You can make more friends in two months by becoming genuinely interested in other people than you can in two years by trying to get other people interested in you."

## The Six Ways

1. **Become genuinely interested in other people** — Not pretend interest. Real curiosity.
2. **Smile** — A sincere smile says "I like you. I'm glad to see you."
3. **Remember names** — "A person's name is the sweetest sound in any language."
4. **Be a good listener** — Encourage others to talk about themselves.
5. **Talk in terms of the other person's interests** — Connect your message to their world.
6. **Make the other person feel important — and do it sincerely.**

> **Case: The Listening Salesman** (Part 1): A struggling salesman was failing at cold calls because he talked too much about his product. When he started asking prospects about their needs and truly listening, his conversion rate tripled. People bought from him because he understood them.
> **Key takeaway**: The best conversationalists are the best listeners. When you listen, people feel valued — and they value you in return.

> **Case: The Name Trick** (Part 1): Carnegie describes a business owner who made a point of remembering every employee's name — not just their first name but details about their families, hobbies, and interests. Employees felt seen and valued. Turnover dropped dramatically.
> **Key takeaway**: Remembering names and personal details is not a trick — it's a signal that the person matters to you.
