# Principles: Achieving Fit

> Source: Value Proposition Design, Chapters 4-6

## The Three Fits in Sequence

Fit must be achieved in order. Problem-Solution Fit: is the problem real and does your solution address it? Product-Market Fit: are customers willing to pay, use, and recommend? Business Model Fit: does the economics work? Each is a prerequisite for the next.

> **Case: The Premature Scale** (Chapter 4): Companies that skip to Product-Market Fit without confirming Problem-Solution Fit build a product nobody needs. The canvas prevents this: if the Customer Profile is incomplete, the Value Map has no foundation.

> **Key takeaway**: Don't raise money or scale before achieving fit. Prove each stage before moving to the next.

## Prototyping Value Propositions

Test with the cheapest possible prototype first. Sketches, storyboards, landing pages, fake-it-til-you-make-it. Graduate to MVPs. Never build the final product before testing core assumptions.

> **Case: The Landing Page Test** (Chapter 5): Create a landing page describing your product with a "buy now" or "sign up" button. Measure click-through rate. If nobody clicks, you have evidence before building. This is faster and cheaper than any alternative.

## The Business Model Connection

A great value proposition fails without a viable business model. Design value proposition and business model together.

> **Key takeaway**: Value is only valuable if you can capture it profitably. Great product + bad business model = failure.
