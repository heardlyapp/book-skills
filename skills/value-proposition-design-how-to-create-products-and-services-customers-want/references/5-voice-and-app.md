# Voice and Application: Osterwalder's Approach

> Source: Value Proposition Design, throughout

## Osterwalder's Voice

Alexander Osterwalder writes as a designer and entrepreneur. His books are highly visual — the Value Proposition Canvas is a practical tool, not just a theoretical framework. He emphasizes rapid iteration and evidence-based decision-making over lengthy analysis.

## Application: Startup Founders

The Value Proposition Canvas is your most important tool. Never build a product before completing it with real customer evidence. The canvas makes your hidden assumptions visible and testable. Many successful startups trace their breakthrough to completing this canvas.

## Application: Product Managers

Use the canvas to evaluate existing products. Does your Value Map still match your Customer Profile? Markets and customers change. The canvas reveals when you've lost alignment and need to pivot.

## Application: Corporate Innovators

Large companies often operate with a Customer Profile based on outdated assumptions. The canvas forces revalidation with current market realities.

## Application: Design Teams

The canvas aligns the whole team around a shared understanding of the customer. It prevents the "features-first" bias that kills innovation projects.

## Key Quotes

- "Start with the Customer Profile. Understand before you build."
- "Fit is a process, not a destination."
- "Test assumptions from cheapest to most expensive."
- "If you can't connect it to a job, pain, or gain, it doesn't belong."
- "The market votes with its wallet. Listen carefully."
