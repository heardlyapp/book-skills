# Anti-Patterns — Caste: The Origins of Our Discontents

## The Central Error: "America Is a Post-Racial Society"

"The election of Barack Obama was cited as proof that America had overcome its racial past." Wilkerson argues the opposite: Obama's election triggered a massive caste backlash. "The permafrost had not thawed — it had merely frozen over."

### Mistake 1: "Racism is just about individual prejudice"
"Race is the skin. Caste is the bones." Prejudice is individual. Caste is structural. Case: "You can have a caste system without overt racists — just people following the rules of the hierarchy."

### Mistake 2: "Nazi Germany was unique — you can't compare it to America"
Wilkerson does compare them. The Nazis studied American race laws. "The Nazis realized that the Americans had developed effective methods of racial subordination." Case: Hitler praised American immigration restrictions.

### Mistake 3: "Caste systems are natural — it's just how societies organize"
"Caste is a human invention — not natural, not divinely ordained." The Eight Pillars are constructed, maintained, and enforced. Case: "The measure of humanity is not inherent — it is assigned."

### Mistake 4: "The oppressed will inevitably rise up"
"There is a powerful counterforce: the Stockholm syndrome of caste." Some members of the subordinate caste internalize the hierarchy. Case: "Subordinate caste members can become invested in the system because it provides certainty."

### Mistake 5: "The dominant caste doesn't suffer"
"The dominant caste suffers too." Wilkerson cites research on moral injury, narcissism, and the health effects of enforcing the hierarchy. Case: "The burden of maintaining the hierarchy distorts the humanity of the dominant caste."

### Mistake 6: "Caste is about the past — not the present"
"Caste had lain dormant for decades but was not dead." Case: The resurgence of white nationalism after Obama's election. "A change in the script triggers a reaction."
