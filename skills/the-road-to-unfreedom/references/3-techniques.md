# 3 — Techniques

> Source: *The Road to Unfreedom* by Timothy Snyder — Chapters 4-6.

## Practical Tools

### 1. The "Who Benefits?" Test

Behind every disinformation campaign, ask: who benefits? The Lisa F. affair benefited Putin by weakening Merkel and strengthening the AfD. The "cruci-fiction" benefited the Russian government by justifying the invasion of Ukraine.

### 2. The Falsifiability Check

Can this claim be disproven? True claims can be tested. Disinformation is often designed to be unfalsifiable — conspiracies can always be expanded.

### 3. The "Creative Ignorance" Detection

When a claim is presented with the explicit message that facts don't matter — that's "creative ignorance." The goal is not to argue but to destroy the possibility of argument.

> **Case: Lavrov's Computer Game Claim** (Chapter 5): When presented with photographs of Russian soldiers in Ukraine, Foreign Minister Lavrov said the images were "from computer games." He did not claim the photos were faked. He claimed factuality itself was the enemy.
> **Key takeaway**: Don't waste time proving a photo is real when the opponent has already declared war on factuality itself. The battle is over the status of truth, not the truth of a specific claim.

> **Case: The Bike Show Gesamtkunstwerk** (Chapter 5): In August 2014, the Night Wolves motorcycle gang staged a six-hour show in Sevastopol with nationalist ska music, burning armored vehicles, and the "crucified boy" imagery. Snyder identifies this as a Nazi Gesamtkunstwerk — a "total work of art" meant to replace the world with a worldview, and history with eternity. The spectacle was the message.
> **Key takeaway**: Modern disinformation is not just a news story — it's a fully immersive spectacle designed to replace reality.

Read primary sources, not commentary about them. Documents, official statements, independent journalists on the ground. Skip the hot takes.

### 5. The Private Sphere Protection

Guard your private conversations. The destruction of privacy is how totalitarianism begins. Use encrypted messaging. Be skeptical of anyone who tries to shame you for having private conversations.
