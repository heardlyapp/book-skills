# 5 — Voice & App

> Source: *The Road to Unfreedom* by Timothy Snyder — voice and scenarios.

## Snyder's Voice

Snyder writes as a historian who saw the future coming. The book was published in 2018 and predicted much of what unfolded after. His voice is urgent, erudite, and angry — but controlled. He does not sensationalize. The facts are enough.

> **Example: The Prologue's Birth Story** (Prologue): Snyder's son was born in Vienna in 2010. As he sent the birth announcement, a friend died in the Smolensk plane crash that killed Poland's president. Private joy and public catastrophe in the same email. The prologue establishes the core theme: the fragility of life and the vulnerability of democratic institutions.

> **Example: The MH17 Cacophony** (Chapter 5): On July 17, 2014, Russia shot down Malaysia Airlines Flight 17 over Ukraine, killing 298 people. Russian television immediately produced seven different explanations — a Ukrainian fighter jet, a ground-to-air missile, a false flag operation, a computer simulation. None were true. The cacophony was the point: if all explanations are equally false, all are equally possible, and truth dissolves.

## Five Scenarios

### 1. The News Consumer Facing Disinformation

Use the "who benefits?" test. Cross-reference with primary sources. Don't share before verifying.

### 2. The Democracy Activist

Understand the politics of eternity and inevitability. The first defends against change by denying history. The second assumes change is automatic and becomes passive.

### 3. The Journalist Covering Authoritarian Regimes

The information war is not about winning arguments — it's about destroying the ground of argument. Don't play that game.

### 4. The Citizen Watching Private Life Erode

The Polish tapes scandal showed how private conversations can be weaponized. Guard your privacy. Support legal protections for encryption.

### 5. The Historian or Teacher

The politics of eternity replaces history with myth. Your role is to teach that history is not inevitable — it is a series of choices.
