# 1 — Core Framework

> Source: *The Road to Unfreedom* by Timothy Snyder — Prologue, Chapters 1, 4, 5.

## The Two Frameworks

### Framework A: The Politics of Eternity (Chapter 4)

Snyder's central concept: authoritarian regimes replace history with myth. They freeze time and present the nation as perpetually innocent and perpetually victimized. The hero-leader defends against eternal enemies. This is not ideology in the traditional sense — it's a strategy for maintaining power by denying the possibility of change.

> **Case: Ivan Ilyin's Revival** (Chapter 1): Putin adopted the philosophy of Ivan Ilyin, a fascist philosopher who died in 1954. Ilyin wrote that the state is a "personality" that must inspire fear and respect. He rejected democracy as "democratic putrefaction." His philosophy had three features: (1) will and violence over intellect and law, (2) the state as a mystical personality, (3) the nation as a spiritual community beyond individual rights. Putin organized Ilyin's reburial in Moscow in 2005 and made his works required reading in Russian military academies. This is not ancient history — this is the operating philosophy of the current Russian state.

> **Case: The Cruci-Fiction** (Chapter 5): In July 2014, Russian television broadcast the story of a three-year-old boy crucified by Ukrainian soldiers. The story was fabricated — "cruci-fiction" — but it served a purpose: to transform Russians into innocent victims who could then justify aggression. A mother of a boy who had not been crucified said the child was unharmed and "alive and well." Nevertheless, the image was seared into the consciousness of Russian viewers, who then supported the war against Ukraine. The purpose was not to make people believe the specific lie, but to create a world in which emotional response replaces factual verification.

### Framework B: Strategic Relativism (Chapters 3, 5)

Russia's key geopolitical strategy: since it cannot increase its own power (due to kleptocracy and commodity dependence), it weakens others. This is a negative-sum game where the goal is to lose less than everyone else.

> **Case: The Lisa F. Affair** (Chapter 5): In January 2016, a 13-year-old German girl of Russian origin did not come home from school. She was found. She was unharmed. Russian state television broadcast the story as a gang rape by Muslim refugees, and the Russian foreign ministry intervened on behalf of "our Lisa." The story was completely false — but it served to weaken Angela Merkel, exploit European anti-immigrant sentiment, and strengthen the far-right AfD party. A private family drama was turned into a global weapon.
