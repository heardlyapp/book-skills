# Core Framework: Creativity Is a Habit

## The Central Insight

"Creativity is not a gift from the gods. It's a habit. And like any habit, it can be learned, practiced, and mastered." Twyla Tharp — one of the greatest choreographers of our time — argues that the key to creativity is not inspiration but ritual.

> **Case: Tharp's Morning Ritual** (Chapter 1): Twyla Tharp's creative day starts at 5:30 AM. She hails a cab, goes to the gym, works out. By the time she arrives at her studio, her body has been active for hours. She doesn't need to "get in the mood" to create — the ritual has already put her there.
> **Key takeaway**: The ritual is not superstition — it's technology. A consistent starting routine removes the need for motivation. Your body knows what's coming, and it prepares.

## Key Concepts

| Concept | Meaning | Practice |
|---------|---------|----------|
| The Ritual | A consistent starting routine | Same time, same place, same actions |
| Scaffolding | Preparation that supports creation | Research, notes, outlines |
| The Spine | Your central creative project | One big thing that organizes everything |
| Memory | Your accumulated knowledge | Read widely, experience deeply |
| Skill | Your technical ability | Practice relentlessly |

> **Case: The Box** (Chapter 2): Tharp describes using a box for each project. She puts everything related to the project in the box — notes, photos, articles, objects. The box contains the scaffolding. When she opens the box, she's surrounded by the project's world. The box is the physical manifestation of preparation.
> **Key takeaway**: Creating a physical container for your project does two things: it organizes your research (scaffolding) and it creates a powerful ritual trigger (opening the box signals "it's time to work on THIS project").
