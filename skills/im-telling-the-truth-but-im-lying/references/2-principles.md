# 2 — The Nigerian-American Experience

> Source: "I'm Telling the Truth, but I'm Lying" by Bassey Ikpi

## Growing Up Between Worlds

Ikpi's parents were Nigerian immigrants who expected excellence. She was an American kid who wanted to fit in. The tension between these two identities runs through the book.

## Key Themes

- **Pressure to succeed**: Nigerian parents do not accept failure. The pressure was immense.
- **Cultural expectations**: The expectations of a Nigerian daughter vs. the reality of growing up in America.
- **Belonging nowhere**: Too American for Nigeria, too Nigerian for America. This is the immigrant child's burden.

> **Case: The Childhood Essay** (Chapter 2): Ikpi describes her childhood as a series of contradictions. Nigerian church on Sunday, American school on Monday. Pounded yam at home, pizza with friends. She never fully belonged in either world.
> **Key takeaway**: The immigrant experience is not about choosing one identity over another. It's about learning to hold both, even when they contradict each other.
