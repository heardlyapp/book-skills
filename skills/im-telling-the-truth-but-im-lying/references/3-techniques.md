# 3 — Writing as Survival

> Source: "I'm Telling the Truth, but I'm Lying" by Bassey Ikpi

## The Essay as Form

Ikpi chose the essay form because life doesn't happen in chapters. It happens in fragments, memories, moments. The essay is honest about fragmentation.

## Writing Through Pain

Ikpi wrote to survive. The book exists because writing was how she processed the unprocessable.

> **Case: The Title Essay** (Chapter 10): "I'm telling the truth, but I'm lying." Ikpi describes the experience of telling your story and feeling like a fraud, because the truth is too strange, too painful, too different from what people expect. The solution: tell it anyway. The feeling of lying passes. The truth remains.
> **Key takeaway**: If your truth feels like a lie, you're probably telling it right. Keep going.

> **Case: The Notebook** (Chapter on Writing): Ikpi kept notebooks everywhere — in her purse, by her bed, in the car. She wrote fragments, not full essays. Later, she pieced them together. The book was built from these fragments.
> **Key takeaway**: You don't need a plan to write. You need a notebook and the willingness to capture moments. The structure comes later.
