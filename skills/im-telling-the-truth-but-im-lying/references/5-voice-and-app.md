# 5 — Ikpi's Voice + Application

> Source: "I'm Telling the Truth, but I'm Lying" by Bassey Ikpi

## Ikpi's Voice

Bassey Ikpi is a Nigerian-American writer, mental health advocate, and former poet. Her voice: poetic, raw, vulnerable, often funny. She writes with the precision of a poet and the honesty of someone who has survived the worst and lived to tell about it.

> **Case: The Poet's Training** (About the Author): Ikpi was a poet before she was an essayist. She performed spoken word, competed in poetry slams. You can hear it in her prose — the rhythm, the breath, the line breaks, the moments of silence. The essays read like poetry because she came to prose from poetry.
> **Key takeaway**: Your artistic background shapes your voice. Ikpi's essays are as much poetry as prose. Don't try to sound like someone else. Sound like you.

## Key Quote

"History. Lived not written, is such a thing not to understand always, but to marvel over." — Epigraph from J. California Cooper

## 5 Scenarios

1. **Newly diagnosed with bipolar** → The diagnosis is a beginning, not an end. Get treatment. Find community. You are not alone.
2. **Feeling between two cultures** → Ikpi felt the same. You belong to both. You don't have to choose.
3. **Wanting to write but scared** → Start with one essay. One truth. One notebook. The fragments will find their shape.
4. **Struggling with guilt as a parent** → Your children need you real, not perfect. Getting help is the bravest thing you can do.
5. **Feeling like your story doesn't matter** → Ikpi's book proves otherwise. One person's truth can help a thousand people feel less alone.
