# 4 — Motherhood and Mental Health

> Source: "I'm Telling the Truth, but I'm Lying" by Bassey Ikpi

## Anti-Pattern: The Perfect Mother Myth

**The mistake:** Believing that a good mother is always calm, always present, always in control. This is a lie that hurts both mothers and children.

## The Reality

Ikpi writes about the fear that her bipolar would make her a bad mother. The guilt of being too depressed to play. The terror of having an episode in front of her children. But also the love — fierce, constant, and ultimately more powerful than the illness.

> **Case: The Motherhood Essay** (Chapter 7): Ikpi describes a depressive episode when she couldn't get out of bed. Her young children came to her room and climbed into bed with her. She felt like a failure. But they didn't see a failure — they saw their mother who needed rest.
> **Key takeaway**: Your children don't need you to be perfect. They need you to be real. The love is visible even through the illness. Don't let guilt convince you otherwise.

> **Case: The Hospitalization** (Chapter 8): Ikpi was hospitalized during a manic episode. Her children visited. She was terrified they would be traumatized. But what they remembered was not the hospital — it was that their mother was getting help.
> **Key takeaway**: Getting help is not a sign of weakness — it's a gift to your children. They need a healthy mother more than they need a present-but-broken one.
