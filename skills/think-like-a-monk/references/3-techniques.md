# 3 — Daily Practices

> Source: "Think Like a Monk" by Jay Shetty

## The Monk's Routine

Wake up early. Meditate. Move. Set an intention. These are not optional — they are the foundation of the monk's training.

## Meditation

Shetty recommends starting with 5 minutes. Sit. Breathe. Notice thoughts. Return to breath. That's it.

## Gratitude Practice

Write down three things you're grateful for every day. Shetty calls this "the single most impactful practice for happiness."

> **Case: The Morning Routine** (Chapter on Routine): Shetty describes his morning routine: "Wake up at 5 AM. Drink warm water. Meditate for 20 minutes. Journal. Work out. This routine is not a burden — it's a liberation." The key is consistency, not perfection.
> **Key takeaway**: Your morning routine sets the tone for your entire day. The monk's structure is not rigid — it's intentional. Design your morning to support the person you want to be.

> **Case: The Digital Detox** (Chapter on Focus): Shetty advocates for periods of digital silence. "Monks take vows of silence. You can take a vow of phone-free mornings." The constant stimulation of social media is the enemy of the focused, peaceful mind.
> **Key takeaway**: Your attention is your most precious resource. Guard it. The monk knows that distraction is not a small problem — it's the enemy of the life you want to live.
