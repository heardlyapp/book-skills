# 5 — Shetty's Voice + Application

> Source: "Think Like a Monk" by Jay Shetty

## Shetty's Voice

Jay Shetty is a former monk, storyteller, and podcaster. His voice: warm, approachable, practical. He spent three years as a monk in India and now translates ancient wisdom for modern audiences.

> **Case: The Monastery** (Introduction): Shetty describes his decision to become a monk: "I was 22 years old, living in London, and I felt empty. I had everything I thought I wanted — but I wasn't happy." He left it all to spend three years as a monk in India. "The monk's training is the most practical education I ever received."
> **Key takeaway**: Shetty's credibility comes from lived experience. He didn't just study monks — he became one for three years. His advice is not theoretical.

## Key Quote

"You are not your thoughts. You are the observer of your thoughts."

## 5 Application Scenarios

1. **Feeling lost and aimless** → Practice silence. Your purpose will emerge from the stillness.
2. **Overwhelmed by negativity** → Observe your thoughts without engaging them. They are clouds passing through the sky.
3. **Lacking discipline** → Start with one small habit. Do it every day for 21 days.
4. **Comparing yourself to others** → Your path is yours. Comparison is a distraction from your growth.
5. **Wanting more meaning** → Serve others. Purpose is found in contribution.
