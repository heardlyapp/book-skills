# 1 — Dharma and Identity

> Source: "Think Like a Monk" by Jay Shetty

## The Framework: Let Go → Grow → Give

The book is structured in three parts: releasing what holds you back, building strength, and serving others.

## Dharma

Shetty defines dharma as your unique purpose — the intersection of what you love, what you're good at, and what the world needs. It's not a career choice — it's a calling.

> **Case: Daniel Day-Lewis and Method Acting** (Chapter 1, Identity): Shetty uses Daniel Day-Lewis's method acting as a metaphor for how we all adopt personas. "Unconsciously, we're all method acting. We have personas we play online, at work, with friends, and at home." The monk's path is to strip away these personas and discover who you actually are.
> **Key takeaway**: Your identity is not fixed. It's a construction. The good news: if you built it, you can rebuild it. You are not your job, your relationship, or your past.

> **Case: Charles Cooley's Mirror** (Chapter 1): Shetty quotes sociologist Charles Horton Cooley: "I am not what I think I am, and I am not what you think I am. I am what I think you think I am." This insight — that our identity is shaped by our perception of others' perceptions — is the starting point for the monk's work of self-discovery.
> **Key takeaway**: Most of us live not as ourselves, but as our perception of what others expect us to be. The monk's first work is to see through this illusion.
