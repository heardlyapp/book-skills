# 4 — Growth Mindset and Traps

> Source: "Think Like a Monk" by Jay Shetty

## Fixed vs Growth Mindset

Shetty draws on Carol Dweck's research: those with a growth mindset believe they can develop their abilities through effort. Those with a fixed mindset believe their abilities are innate and unchangeable.

## Anti-Pattern: Identity Attachment

**The mistake:** Attaching your identity to a fixed label. "I'm not a morning person." "I'm bad at math." These labels become self-fulfilling prophecies.

> **Case: The Monk and the Challenger** (Chapter on Growth): Shetty shares a story of a monk who was always challenging himself, even in small ways — taking cold showers, fasting, learning new skills. "The monk is never done growing. Every day is an opportunity to stretch beyond yesterday's limits."
> **Key takeaway**: Growth is a choice you make every day — not a destination you arrive at. The monk's path is one of continuous, intentional growth.

> **Case: The Gratitude Experiment** (Chapter on Gratitude): Shetty describes a study where participants wrote down three things they were grateful for every day for 21 days. "At the end of the study, they reported significantly higher levels of happiness and lower levels of depression." This simple practice rewires the brain.
> **Key takeaway**: Happiness is not a consequence of circumstances — it's a practice. Gratitude is the most direct path to a happier mind. Start today.
