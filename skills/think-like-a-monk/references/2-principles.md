# 2 — Letting Go

> Source: "Think Like a Monk" by Jay Shetty

## The Monk's Mindset

A monk trains the mind like a muscle. The goal is not to stop thoughts — it's to observe them without attachment.

## Letting Go of Negativity

Your mind has a negativity bias — it remembers negative experiences more strongly than positive ones. The practice: notice negative thoughts without engaging them.

> **Case: The Negativity Bias** (Chapter on Negativity): Shetty explains that our brains have a "Velcro for negative, Teflon for positive" bias. "It takes five positive interactions to outweigh one negative interaction. This is not a character flaw — it's evolution." The practice is to consciously savor positive moments.
> **Key takeaway**: Your brain is wired to notice threats, not joys. This kept your ancestors alive, but it makes you unhappy. Counteract it by deliberately focusing on what's good.

> **Case: Fear as Excitement** (Chapter on Fear): Shetty reframes fear. "Fear is excitement without breath. When you feel fear, your body is preparing for action. If you breathe, you access that energy as excitement. If you hold your breath, it stays as fear."
> **Key takeaway**: The physical sensation of fear and excitement are almost identical. The difference is your interpretation. Breathe into fear, and it transforms.
