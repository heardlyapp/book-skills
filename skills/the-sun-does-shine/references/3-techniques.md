# 3 — Forgiveness

> Source: "The Sun Does Shine" by Anthony Ray Hinton

## The Choice to Forgive

Hinton made a conscious decision not to hate. He describes forgiveness not as a feeling but as a choice.

## Why

"Forgiveness is not for them. It's for you. If I had stayed angry, I would have been in prison even after they let me out."

> **Case: The Prosecutor** (Chapter on Forgiveness): Hinton chose to forgive the prosecutor who sought the death penalty against an innocent man. "I saw him years later. He couldn't look me in the eye. I had nothing but pity for him. He had to live with what he did. I didn't."
> **Key takeaway**: Forgiveness gave Hinton freedom that the prison could not take away. His anger would have been justified. But he chose peace.

> **Case: The Call for Clemency** (Chapter 14): "A man whose execution date had been set asked me to speak at his clemency hearing. I spoke from my heart. He was executed anyway. But I was grateful I could be there for him." Hinton's capacity for compassion extended even to those the system had given up on.
> **Key takeaway**: Hinton never stopped fighting for others, even when he could not fight for himself. His compassion was not diminished by his circumstances.
