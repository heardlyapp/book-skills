# 3 — Forgiveness

> Source: "The Sun Does Shine" by Anthony Ray Hinton

## Hinton's Practice of Forgiveness

Hinton made a conscious decision not to hate. He describes forgiveness not as a feeling but as a choice he made every day.

## Why He Chose Forgiveness

"Forgiveness is not for them. It's for you. If I had stayed angry, I would have been in prison even after they let me out."

> **Case: The Prosecutor** (Chapter on Forgiveness): Years after his release, Hinton encountered the prosecutor who had sought the death penalty against an innocent man. "He couldn't look me in the eye. I had nothing but pity for him. He had to live with what he did. I didn't." Hinton's capacity for forgiveness did not require an apology.
> **Key takeaway**: Forgiveness gave Hinton freedom that the prison could not take away. His anger would have been justified. But he chose peace — and that choice made him truly free.

> **Case: The Clemency Hearing** (Chapter 14): A man whose execution date had been set asked Hinton to speak at his clemency hearing. "I spoke from my heart. I didn't care if it helped. I needed to be there for him. He was executed anyway. But I was grateful I could show him love in his final days."
> **Key takeaway**: Even when he could not save his friend, Hinton chose to show up. His compassion was not diminished by his own suffering. This is the highest form of love — giving even when you have nothing to gain.
