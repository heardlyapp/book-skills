# 2 — Life on Death Row

> Source: "The Sun Does Shine" by Anthony Ray Hinton

## Daily Life

Hinton spent 23 hours a day in a 5x7 foot cell. He had one hour of outdoor time. The lights were never off.

## The Brotherhood

The men on death row became his family. They shared meals, jokes, and the constant fear of execution.

> **Case: The Book Club** (Chapter on Friendship): Hinton and his fellow inmates started a book club. "We read everything. We discussed everything. Those conversations kept me alive. We weren't just inmates — we were scholars, philosophers, friends."
> **Key takeaway**: Hinton found humanity in the least human place. The book club was not an escape from prison — it was a way of being free inside prison.

> **Case: The Executions** (Chapter on Loss): Hinton lost friends to the death penalty. "The night before an execution, the row would go silent. We could hear the generator hum. We would pray. The next morning, there was an empty cell." He describes the psychological toll of watching others die.
> **Key takeaway**: The death penalty doesn't just kill the condemned — it traumatizes everyone on the row. This is a cost that never gets counted.
