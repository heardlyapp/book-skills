# 4 — Exoneration

> Source: "The Sun Does Shine" by Anthony Ray Hinton

## The Fight

Bryan Stevenson and the Equal Justice Initiative took Hinton's case. They proved the bullet evidence was junk science. After 30 years, Hinton was exonerated.

## Walking Free

April 3, 2015. Hinton walked out of prison. He had entered at 26. He left at 56.

> **Case: The Walk Out** (Final Chapter): "I walked out the gates. The sun was shining. I had waited 30 years to feel the sun without a fence blocking it. I fell to my knees and thanked God." The title of the book comes from this moment.
> **Key takeaway**: The moment of freedom was not just joy — it was also grief. Hinton had lost 30 years. His mother had died while he was in prison. Freedom was bittersweet.

> **Case: The Phone Call** (EJI Chapter): Bryan Stevenson called Hinton to tell him the court had ruled in his favor. "I didn't believe him at first. I had been disappointed too many times. But he said: 'Ray, you're going home.' And then I believed."
> **Key takeaway**: After 30 years of fighting, Hinton had learned not to hope too much. But the call came. Justice arrived. It was late, but it arrived.
