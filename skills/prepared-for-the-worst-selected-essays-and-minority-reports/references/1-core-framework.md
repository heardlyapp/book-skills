# Core Framework: Hitchens' Method

> Source: Prepared for the Worst, Introduction

## The Contrarian Vocation

Hitchens opens by explaining his approach: the writer's role is to keep confrontation alive, refuse consensus, and write with absolute clarity and conviction.

> **Case: The Nadine Gordimer Frame** (Introduction): Hitchens references Gordimer's idea of writing "posthumously" — beyond caring about reputation. He admits he can't achieve that detachment. Instead, he presents himself as an anxious parent over his collected essays. Classic Hitchens: serious purpose, self-deprecating delivery.

> **Case: The "Idiotis" Concept** (Introduction): Hitchens uses the Greek term for one indifferent to public affairs. He argues there are two idiocies: apathy (ignoring politics) and hyper-politicization (professional politicking). The writer's job is to navigate between them with independence.

> **Key takeaway**: Hitchens combines serious moral purpose with wit and self-awareness. The humor makes the arguments land rather than preach.

## The Minority Report

The title signals Hitchens' pride in dissenting. He was often in the minority.

> **Case: The Reagan/Thatcher 1980s** (Introduction): Hitchens describes the 1980s as "calamitous cynicism." He opposed Reagan and Thatcher from the left, but also opposed the Soviet Union from the left. He refused to join any consensus — right, left, or center.

> **Key takeaway**: Hitchens followed principles, not tribes. That's what made him a true contrarian.
