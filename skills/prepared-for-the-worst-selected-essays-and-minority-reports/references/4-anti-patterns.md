# Anti-Patterns: Misreading Hitchens

> Source: Prepared for the Worst, throughout

## Confusing Contrarianism with Crankiness

Hitchens was not a crank who disagreed for the sake of it. He was a principled dissenter whose positions followed from core beliefs.

> **Case: The Iraq War Position** (throughout): Hitchens supported the Iraq War, losing many left-wing friends. This wasn't a flip. His consistent principle was anti-totalitarianism. Saddam was a tyrant. You can disagree with the conclusion without questioning the integrity.

> **Case: The Anti-Soviet Position** (throughout): Hitchens opposed the Soviet Union from the left, not the right. He never became a conservative. His principles remained socialist and anti-totalitarian throughout.

> **Key takeaway**: Hitchens was consistent in principles even when positions shifted. The principles: anti-totalitarian, pro-secular, pro-human rights. Everything else followed.

## Mistaking Style for Substance

Hitchens' style was brilliant — but it served his arguments. Imitation without substance is empty.

> **Case: The Hollow Mimic** (throughout): Many tried to imitate Hitchens' combative style. Without his reading, his experience, his intellectual seriousness, it's just rudeness. The style worked because the substance was earned.

## Thinking Hitchens Was Cynical

Hitchens was a passionate believer in Enlightenment values — reason, science, human rights, free expression. He was the opposite of cynical.

> **Key takeaway**: Imitating Hitchens' style without his substance is pointless. The wit works because the knowledge behind it is real and hard-won.
