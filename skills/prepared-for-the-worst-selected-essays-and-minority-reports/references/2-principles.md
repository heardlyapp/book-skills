# Principles: Politics and the Intellectual

> Source: Prepared for the Worst, "GOOD AND BAD" and "PEOPLE" sections

## Thomas Paine — The Actuarial Radical

Hitchens' essay on Paine reclaims a radical from both hagiography and neglect. Paine wrote clearly and acted effectively.

> **Case: Paine's Common Sense** (GOOD AND BAD): Hitchens celebrates Paine's clarity and timing. "Common Sense" was written in language anyone could understand, at the moment it was most needed. Paine combined radical principle with the ability to actually move history. This is Hitchens' ideal of the writer-intellectual.

> **Case: Paine's Neglect** (GOOD AND BAD): Hitchens notes Paine has no proper memorial in either America or England. The radical who inspired two revolutions is forgotten by both countries. This neglect is emblematic of how establishment history treats inconvenient figures.

> **Key takeaway**: Hitchens champions writers who combine principle with real-world impact. Paine is the archetype.
