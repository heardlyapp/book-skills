# 3 — Techniques for Living in Crazy Faith

> Source: "Crazy Faith" by Michael Todd

## Technique 1: State It Before You See It

Stating Faith (Chapter 9) is about speaking God's promises into existence before they materialize. Not "positive thinking" — biblical declaration.

## Technique 2: Take One Step

You don't need to see the whole staircase. Just the first step. Faith is action before full understanding.

## Technique 3: Wait Actively

Waiting Faith (Chapter 4) is not passive. It's trusting, praying, preparing, and positioning yourself while you wait. The waiting room is a classroom.

## Technique 4: Stop Trading

Trading Faith (Chapter 7) is "If You do this, I'll do that." God doesn't bargain. Obey without conditions.

## Technique 5: Keep Going When It Fades

Fading Faith (Chapter 10) happens when initial excitement fades. Push through. The feeling returns when you keep moving.

> **Case: The Step-by-Step Journey** (Chapter 2, Baby Faith): Todd explains that you don't go from baby faith to crazy faith in one jump. In the natural, a baby first crawls, then walks, then runs. Faith is the same. The mistake people make is trying to run in faith when they haven't learned to walk yet. Start with small acts of obedience — tithing when you're scared, forgiving a small offense — and build up from there.
> **Key takeaway**: Don't try to have "crazy faith" if you're still in baby faith. Take the next logical step. Faith grows by practice, not by effort.

> **Case: The Waiting Room Is a Classroom** (Chapter 4, Waiting Faith): Todd describes waiting faith as the hardest but most important stage. Abraham waited 25 years for Isaac. Joseph waited 13 years in slavery and prison before his dream came true. The waiting is not empty time — it's preparation time. In the waiting, God works on your character, not just your circumstances.
> **Key takeaway**: Don't waste the waiting. If you're in a season of waiting, ask: what is God developing in me right now? The answer is usually character, patience, and deeper trust.
