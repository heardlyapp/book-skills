# 2 — Principles of Crazy Faith

> Source: "Crazy Faith" by Michael Todd

## Principle 1: Faith Is Progression, Not Perfection

"You're going to want to highlight this: I define Crazy Faith as 'having the audacity to trust God to do what He said He would do, even when it doesn't make sense to anyone else.'" You don't jump from baby faith to crazy faith overnight. You grow.

> **Case: The Vacant Lot Foundation** (Introduction): "Whether your foundation is at vacant-lot status or the Taj Mahal, remember, Crazy Faith is about progression, not perfection." Todd emphasizes that where you start doesn't matter. What matters is that you take the next step.
> **Key takeaway**: Don't compare your faith journey to others. A foundation doesn't have to be perfect — it just has to be real. Grow from where you are.

## Principle 2: Obedience Is the Engine of Faith

You can't say you believe and then not do what God says. Obedience proves faith is real. Without obedience, faith is just opinion.

> **Case: The Heartbreak That Became a Ministry** (Introduction): Todd shares that God wants to use even your heartbreak for His purpose. "Your Crazy Faith is what He wants to use to make a miracle." The most painful experiences in his life became the foundation of his most powerful ministry.
> **Key takeaway**: Your pain is not wasted. God doesn't waste anything. When you trust Him with your brokenness, He turns it into someone else's breakthrough.
