# 5 — Michael Todd's Voice + Application

> Source: "Crazy Faith" by Michael Todd

## Voice

Michael Todd is pastor of Transformation Church in Tulsa, Oklahoma. His voice: high-energy, humorous, street-smart, deeply biblical. He teaches Scripture with modern language, cultural references, and personal stories. He's known for his catchphrases, his transparency about his own struggles, and his ability to make complex spiritual concepts accessible.

> **Case: The Book's Origin** (Introduction): Todd writes that Crazy Faith is "my life message because this is the life I'm living: one of Crazy Faith." He doesn't teach from theory. He teaches from lived experience — including his failures. This authenticity is what makes the book hit home.
> **Key takeaway**: The most powerful teaching comes from lived experience. People don't need perfect teachers — they need honest ones.

## Key Quotes

> "Crazy Faith is having the audacity to trust God to do what He said He would do, even when it doesn't make sense to anyone else."

> "Faith is progression, not perfection."

> "Your Crazy Faith is what He wants to use to make a miracle."

## 5 Application Scenarios

1. **Called to make a big change** → Identify which faith stage you're in → take one obedient step → state God's promise aloud
2. **Waiting feels endless** → Waiting Faith → don't waste the waiting: pray, prepare, learn, serve
3. **Faith feels dead/routine** → Check for Fugazi/Lazy Faith → return to obedience → real faith always moves
4. **Afraid of what others think** → Crazy Faith looks crazy to the world → embrace the awkward → their opinion isn't your calling
5. **Doubting if God is real** → Maybe Faith is a valid stage → don't pretend you're not doubting → bring your doubts to God honestly

> **Case: Todd's Personal Crazy Faith Story** (Introduction): Michael Todd shares that he wrote this book from his own life. "God has shown me Crazy Faith over and over again — in my marriage, in my ministry, in my finances." He doesn't teach from a place of having arrived. He teaches from the trenches. His church started small, grew through struggles, and now reaches thousands. Every step required a decision that looked crazy to someone.
> **Key takeaway**: You don't need to have it all together to have crazy faith. You just need to have more faith in God than fear of looking foolish.
