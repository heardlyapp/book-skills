# 1 — The 10 Types of Faith Framework

> Source: "Crazy Faith" by Michael Todd

## The Core Framework

Michael Todd defines Crazy Faith as "having the audacity to trust God to do what He said He would do, even when it doesn't make sense to anyone else." The book maps 10 types of faith along a journey from immaturity to maturity.

## The 10 Types

| Type | Chapter | Description |
|------|---------|-------------|
| Baby Faith | 2 | New, fragile, needs nurturing. Learning to trust God for small things |
| Maybe Faith | 3 | "I think God can, but I'm not sure He will." Doubt mixed with belief |
| Waiting Faith | 4 | Trusting during the gap between promise and fulfillment |
| Wavy Faith | 5 | Up and down — driven by emotions and circumstances |
| Lazy Faith | 6 | Believing but not acting. Head knowledge without feet |
| Trading Faith | 7 | Bargaining with God. "If You do X, I'll do Y" |
| Fugazi Faith | 8 | Fake faith. Looks real, isn't. Going through motions |
| Stating Faith | 9 | Speaking promises into existence before seeing them |
| Fading Faith | 10 | Losing steam when initial excitement fades |
| Saving Faith | 11 | Complete trust in Jesus — the faith that saves |

> **Case: The Blackest Part of the City** (Introduction): Michael Todd's spiritual parents, Bishop Gary and Pastor Debbie McIntosh, moved their family into the blackest part of the city to plant a church. Everyone thought they were crazy. "They acted on their Crazy Faith. They moved into a neighborhood others were fleeing." Years later, that church transformed the community.
> **Key takeaway**: Crazy faith looks irresponsible to the world. God calls you to move in when everyone else is moving out. The "crazy" is the sign that you're following God, not common sense.

> **Case: Michael Todd's Parents** (Introduction): Brenda and Tommy Todd planted a church that they led for over thirty years. "They trusted God in Crazy Faith." They weren't wealthy or well-connected. They just believed God would provide — and He did, year after year, for three decades.
> **Key takeaway**: Crazy faith is not a one-time leap. It's a lifestyle of daily trust, sustained over decades.
