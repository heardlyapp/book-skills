# 4 — Faith Killers: Anti-Patterns

> Source: "Crazy Faith" by Michael Todd

## Anti-Pattern 1: Maybe Faith

Staying in "maybe" forever. "I think God can, but I'm not sure He will." Maybe faith never commits. It's belief without decision.

## Anti-Pattern 2: Lazy Faith

Believing without acting. Head knowledge without obedience. James says faith without works is dead. Lazy faith is comfortable but powerless.

## Anti-Pattern 3: Wavy Faith

Emotional faith. Up when circumstances are good, down when they're bad. Walking by sight, not by faith.

## Anti-Pattern 4: Fugazi Faith

Fake faith. Named after the military slang "Fugazi" (F***ed Up, Got Ambushed, Zipped In). Looks real on the outside. Empty on the inside. Going through the motions at church but not actually trusting God.

> **Case: The Fugazi Trap** (Chapter 8): Todd warns that Fugazi Faith is the most dangerous because it's hard to detect. You look spiritual — you go to church, you say the right things, you post Bible verses — but your heart is far from God. The easiest person to fool is yourself.
> **Key takeaway**: The opposite of faith is not doubt — it's pretense. It's better to have struggling but real faith than polished but fake faith.

## Anti-Pattern 5: Trading Faith

Treating God like a vending machine. "If You give me this, I'll serve You." Trading Faith turns relationship into transaction.

> **Case: The Wavy Faith Trap** (Chapter 5): Todd describes people whose faith depends on their Sunday morning experience. If worship was good, they believe God is real. If the sermon was bad, they doubt everything. This is wavy faith — driven by feelings, not by decision. The fix: make a decision about God's faithfulness on a good day, and stick to it on a bad day.
> **Key takeaway**: Feelings are not faith. Faith is what you decide when feelings are absent. Anchor your trust in God's character, not your emotional state.
