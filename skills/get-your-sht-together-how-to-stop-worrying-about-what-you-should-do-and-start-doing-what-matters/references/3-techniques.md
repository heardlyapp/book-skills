# Techniques: Career, Confidence, and Relationships

> Source: Get Your Sh*t Together, Part III

## Building Confidence

Knight argues confidence follows action, not the other way around. Do the thing first.

> **Case: The Salary Negotiation** (Part III): Prepare evidence of your value. Practice the conversation. Ask directly. The worst response is no — which is the same as the outcome of not asking.

> **Case: The Public Speaking Fear** (Part III): Knight advises readers to speak up in meetings even when nervous. After a few attempts, the fear diminishes. Action precedes confidence.

> **Key takeaway**: Don't wait to feel confident. Act first. Confidence is the reward.

## Asking for What You Want

Whether a raise, help, or a date — ask. The answer is always no if you don't ask.

> **Case: The Delegation Request** (Part III): An overwhelmed employee asked her boss to redistribute tasks. Her boss said yes immediately. She'd been suffering in silence.

## Relationships and Boundaries

Say no to draining obligations. Protect your energy.

> **Case: The Social Calendar** (Part III): "Thanks, I can't make it" is a complete sentence. You don't need to explain. Knight gives permission to skip events you attend out of guilt.

> **Case: The Friendship Purge** (Part III): Knight suggests evaluating friendships: which ones energize you, which drain you. Invest in the former; let the latter fade.

> **Key takeaway**: Setting boundaries isn't rude. It protects your energy for what matters.
