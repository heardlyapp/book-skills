# Core Framework: The Mental Bootcamp

> Source: Get Your Sh*t Together, Introduction and Part I

## The Oh Shit Moment

Everyone has the moment of realizing something is wrong. The question is what you do next.

> **Case: The Career Crisis** (Introduction): Knight's own Oh Shit moment: she hated not just her job but her entire career. She quit publishing, moved to the Dominican Republic, and became a writer and self-help author. Her story proves big changes are possible with the right mindset.

> **Key takeaway**: The first step is admitting something needs to change. Then follow through with a system.

## Stop Should-ing All Over Yourself

"Should" is guilt disguised as motivation. It keeps you stuck.

> **Case: The Exercise Trap** (Introduction): "I should exercise more" produces guilt, not action. Either commit to a plan or decide not to. Should lets you off the hook.

> **Case: The Finance Guilt** (Part I): "I should save more" feels virtuous without producing change. "I will save $200/month" is a real decision. The difference is commitment.

## No Zero Days

Every day, do at least one thing. A tiny action beats no action.

## The Fucket List

Stop caring about things that don't matter. Free up energy for what does.

> **Case: The Social Obligation** (Part I): Knight advises cutting social obligations that drain you. Say no to parties you don't want to attend. Put them on your Fucket List.

> **Key takeaway**: Replace "should" with "will" or "won't." Vagueness is the enemy of progress.
