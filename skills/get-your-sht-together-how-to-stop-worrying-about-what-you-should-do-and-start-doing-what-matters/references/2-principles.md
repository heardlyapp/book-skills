# Principles: Time and Money

> Source: Get Your Sh*t Together, Part II

## The Time Audit

Track every hour for a week. The gap between where you think your time goes and where it actually goes will shock you.

> **Case: The Social Media Black Hole** (Part II): Most readers discover 2-3 hours daily on social media — 15-20 hours per week that could go toward actual goals like exercise, learning, or a side project.

> **Case: The Email Addiction** (Part II): Knight checked email 20+ times daily, wasting 2+ hours. She switched to three scheduled checks. Same responsiveness, dramatically less wasted time.

> **Key takeaway**: You can't manage time you don't track. A time audit reveals the truth and gives you control over your schedule.

## The Money Audit

Track every dollar for a month. Small expenses compound into significant sums.

> **Case: The Coffee Trap** (Part II): $5 coffee every weekday: $100/month, $1,200/year. That's a vacation or emergency fund. These small daily leaks add up to enormous annual totals.

> **Case: The Subscription Stack** (Part II): Multiple small subscriptions — streaming, apps, gym — can total $200+/month. Most people can cut 30% without noticing any change in quality of life.

## Delegation

Don't try to do everything yourself. Delegate, outsource, or say no. Perfectionism prevents progress.

> **Key takeaway**: Time and money audits remove the mystery. Once you see where everything actually goes, you can make intentional, empowered choices about both.
