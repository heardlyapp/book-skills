# Anti-Patterns: Common Mistakes

> Source: Get Your Sh*t Together, Parts I-III

## Trying to Fix Everything at Once

The biggest mistake: overhauling your entire life at the same time. It never works.

> **Case: The New Year's Resolution Trap** (Part I): People resolve to fix health, finances, career, and relationships — all in January. By February, nothing has changed. Knight's approach: pick ONE area and focus until it's working well before moving to the next.

> **Key takeaway**: Small, sustained changes beat grand overhauls. Pick one thing and finish it.

## Waiting for Motivation

Motivation follows action, not the other way around. Waiting to feel motivated means waiting forever.

> **Case: The Gym Membership** (Part I): People spend money on memberships but never go because they don't "feel like it." Knight's advice: go when you don't want to. Action creates momentum.

> **Case: The Clean Garage** (Part II): Knight advises setting a 10-minute timer. Anyone can clean for 10 minutes. Often, once you start, you keep going.

## Perfectionism as Procrastination

"Not doing it perfectly" is just an excuse for not doing it at all.

> **Case: The Unwritten Novel** (Part III): A writer spent years planning the perfect first chapter. Knight: "Write a terrible first chapter. You can't edit a blank page."

> **Case: The Never-Sent Email** (Part III): People spend hours crafting the perfect email. Knight's advice: write it, check for errors once, send. Done beats perfect.

## Confusing the Fucket List with Being Mean

Not caring about some things doesn't mean neglecting people. It means having honest priorities.

> **Key takeaway**: Done is better than perfect. Action beats waiting. The Fucket List is about focus, not cruelty.
