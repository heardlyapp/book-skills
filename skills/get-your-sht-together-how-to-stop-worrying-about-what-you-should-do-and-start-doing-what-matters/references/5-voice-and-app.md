# Voice and Application: Knight's Voice

> Source: Get Your Sh*t Together, throughout

## Knight's Voice

Sarah Knight writes like a profane best friend who has your back. She swears, she's funny, and she's brutally honest. The profanity is intentional — it cuts through self-deception and forces you to stop bullshitting yourself.

> **Case: The "Fuck Yes or No" Rule** (throughout): Knight's litmus test for decisions. If you can't say an enthusiastic "fuck yes," say no. This eliminates the ambivalent middle ground that wastes your time and energy. It's simple, direct, and effective.

> **Case: The Permission Slip** (throughout): Knight gives readers explicit permission to stop trying to be perfect, to say no to draining obligations, and to prioritize their own happiness. This simple permission is the most powerful gift of the book.

> **Key takeaway**: Knight's style is a kick in the ass, not a gentle nudge. For people who need blunt honesty, this approach works brilliantly.

## Application: Getting Started

Read, do the exercise, move on. The book is designed for action.

## Application: Decision Making

Use "fuck yes or no." No middle ground allowed.

## Application: Boundaries

"Thanks, I can't make it" is a complete sentence. No explanation needed.

## Application: Career

Ask for the raise. Delegate tasks. Speak up in meetings. The worst outcome is no.
