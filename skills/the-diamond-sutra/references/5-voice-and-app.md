# 5 — Voice + Application

> Source: "The Diamond Sutra and the Sutra of Hui-neng"

## The Voice of the Sutras

The Diamond Sutra: precise, paradoxical, cutting. The Buddha speaks in negations: "X is not X, therefore it is X." This is the logic of emptiness.

The Platform Sutra: direct, warm, accessible. Hui-neng speaks to ordinary people. "Look directly into your own mind."

## 5 Scenarios

1. **Overwhelmed by thoughts in meditation** → Don't fight them. See them. They are not the problem — attachment is.
2. **Struggling with non-attachment** → You can't "achieve" non-attachment. You can only see that there's nothing to attach to.
3. **Feeling separate from enlightenment** → Hui-neng: your true nature is already enlightened. You're just not seeing it.
4. **Reading spiritual texts** → Don't collect intellectual understanding. Read to see — not to know.
5. **Dealing with loss or change** → The Diamond Sutra's teaching on impermanence: form is emptiness. Things pass. That's not a tragedy — it's their nature.
