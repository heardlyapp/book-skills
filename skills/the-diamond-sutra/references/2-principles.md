# 2 — Hui-neng and the Platform Sutra

> Source: "The Sutra of Hui-neng" (trans. Wong Mou-Lam)

## Hui-neng's Story

Born poor in 638 CE. Illiterate. Heard the Diamond Sutra being recited and awakened immediately. Became the Sixth Patriarch of Zen. His teachings form the Platform Sutra.

## Key Teachings

- **Sudden enlightenment**: Realization happens in an instant, not through gradual cultivation.
- **Direct pointing**: See your true nature directly. Don't rely on words or concepts.
- **No-thought**: Not the absence of thought — but not being carried away by thoughts.

> **Case: The Verse Contest** (Chapter on Hui-neng): The Fifth Patriarch asked his monks to write verses showing their understanding. The head monk Shenxiu wrote: "The body is the Bodhi tree. The mind is like a clear mirror. At all times wipe it diligently. Do not let it gather dust." Hui-neng (illiterate, had someone write for him) replied: "Bodhi originally has no tree. The clear mirror has no stand. Originally there is not a single thing. Where can dust alight?" The Patriarch recognized Hui-neng's deeper realization and secretly transmitted the robe and Dharma.
> **Key takeaway**: Shenxiu's verse represents gradual cultivation — wiping the mirror clean. Hui-neng's represents sudden enlightenment — seeing that the mirror never existed. Two approaches to practice.

> **Case: The Wind and the Flag** (Platform Sutra): Two monks see a flag waving. One says: "The flag moves." The other: "The wind moves." Hui-neng: "Neither the flag nor the wind moves. Your minds move." This is the direct teaching of Zen — all phenomena are projections of the mind.
> **Key takeaway**: Problems appear to be external — flag, wind, other people. But Hui-neng points to the mind as the source. Look there first.
