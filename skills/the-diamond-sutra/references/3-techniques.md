# 3 — Zen Practice

> Source: "The Diamond Sutra and the Sutra of Hui-neng"

## No-Thought (Wu-nien)

Not the suppression of thought — but seeing thoughts without being attached to them. Thoughts arise naturally. Don't follow them. Don't push them away.

## Non-Abiding (Wu-zhu)

Don't dwell anywhere. Not in thoughts, not in feelings, not in concepts, not in emptiness.

## Direct Seeing

Hui-neng: "The wisdom of enlightenment is inherent in all people. It is only because of deluded attachments that it is not realized."

> **Case: The Illiterate Master** (Platform Sutra): Hui-neng could not read. Yet his understanding was the deepest. This shows that intellectual knowledge is not necessary for enlightenment. "The truth is not in words. It is directly pointed to." His illiteracy was not a limitation — it was an advantage. He wasn't trapped by concepts.
> **Key takeaway**: You don't need to study. You need to see. Intellectual understanding can become an obstacle. Direct looking is the path.

> **Case: The Sudden School** (Platform Sutra): Hui-neng's teaching was called the "Sudden School" because it held that enlightenment is instantaneous — not gradual. "Those who awaken suddenly see their true nature in an instant. There is no gradation."
> **Key takeaway**: The debate between sudden and gradual enlightenment is ancient. Hui-neng's position: realization is sudden. Practice is gradual. They are not in conflict.
