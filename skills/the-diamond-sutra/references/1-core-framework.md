# 1 — The Diamond Sutra

> Source: "The Diamond Sutra" (trans. A.F. Price)

## The Setting

The Buddha is in Jetavana Grove with 1,250 monks. Subhuti asks: "How should a Bodhisattva abide? How should he practice?"

## The Answer

The Buddha's answer: don't dwell anywhere. Abide nowhere. Practice non-abiding.

## Key Passages

"Form is emptiness, emptiness is form."
"The past mind cannot be grasped. The present mind cannot be grasped. The future mind cannot be grasped."
"The Dharma spoken by the Buddha is not the Dharma. We only call it the Dharma."

> **Case: The Oldest Printed Book** (Publishers' Note): The Diamond Sutra is the world's oldest dated printed book — printed in 868 CE. It was discovered in the Dunhuang caves in 1900. "The text that follows is the most precious of the printed treasures of humanity."
> **Key takeaway**: The Diamond Sutra is not just a philosophical text — it's a historical artifact, the first printed book in human history. Its teachings have been studied for 1,500+ years.

> **Case: The Raft Parable** (Sutra Text): The Buddha teaches that the Dharma is like a raft used to cross a river. Once you reach the other shore, you don't carry the raft on your back. "The Dharma must be let go of. How much more so what is not the Dharma?"
> **Key takeaway**: Don't cling even to the teaching. The teaching is a tool, not the truth itself. Once you've understood, let it go. The Diamond Sutra is famous for this radical non-attachment.
