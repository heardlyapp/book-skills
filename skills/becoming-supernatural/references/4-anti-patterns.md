# 4 — Anti-Patterns: What Blocks Transformation

> Source: "Becoming Supernatural: How Common People Are Doing the Uncommon" by Dr. Joe Dispenza

## Anti-Pattern 1: Living in the Past or Future

**The mistake:** Spending your mental energy rehearsing past traumas or worrying about future outcomes. This keeps your brain locked in beta wave patterns and your body locked in stress responses.

> **Case: The Autonomic Trap** (Chapter 2): When you think about a past trauma, your brain activates the same neural networks as the original event. Your body releases cortisol, your heart rate increases, you enter fight/flight. You are literally reliving the trauma biologically — even though the event is over. The same applies to future anxiety: your body responds to the imagined future as if it's happening now.
> **Key takeaway**: The past and future are not harmless thoughts — they are biological stressors that prevent healing. The body cannot distinguish between a real threat and a vividly imagined one.

**The fix:** When you catch yourself reliving the past or rehearsing the future, stop. Breathe into your heart. Come into the present moment. The present moment is the only place where the body can heal.

## Anti-Pattern 2: Trying to Change Without Changing State

**The mistake:** Attempting to create a new reality while staying in your ordinary state of consciousness. This is the most common reason people say "I tried and it didn't work."

> **Case: The Positive Thinking Trap** (Chapter 2): Dispenza distinguishes his work from affirmations and positive thinking. "When you say an affirmation but your body feels the opposite — your body is vibrating with the energy of the old self — the affirmation is powerless." Affirmations from a state of lack are just words. The body is the unconscious mind, and it hasn't changed.
> **Key takeaway**: Speaking new words while vibrating old energy is futile. You must change the energy first (through meditation, heart coherence, elevated emotions), and then the words become powerful.

**The fix:** Before you try to manifest, heal, or change — enter a meditative state first. Shift your brain waves. Open your heart. Become coherent. Then, from that state, intend the change. The state is not optional — it's the mechanism.

## Anti-Pattern 3: The Victim Identity

**The mistake:** Identifying as the person who was hurt, who is sick, who is limited. This identity becomes a self-fulfilling prophecy.

> **Case: The Diagnosis Identity** (Chapters 6, 10, 14): Dispenza's case studies consistently show that healing required a shift in identity. As long as participants saw themselves as "cancer patients" or "chronic pain sufferers," they remained in the energy of the disease. The ones who healed were the ones who stopped identifying with the diagnosis and started identifying with wholeness.
> **Key takeaway**: Your diagnosis is not your identity. As long as you think of yourself as "someone who has X," you will keep having X. To heal, you must become someone who no longer has it — in your mind, in your energy, in your feeling.

**The fix:** Stop using your diagnosis, your story, your past as your primary identity. Experiment with introducing yourself (to yourself) as someone who is whole, free, and healthy. Not as denial — but as a new identity to step into.

## Anti-Pattern 4: Forcing Outcomes (Unbalanced Yang)

**The mistake:** Approaching transformation with the same driven, striving energy that you use for work, exercise, or projects. Spiritual transformation requires surrender, not more effort.

> **Case: The Doer's Dilemma** (Chapter 4): Dispenza instructs participants to "move from doing to being." After the initial intention and focus, you must surrender. If you continue to try, you block the very thing you're trying to create. "The creative state is a surrendered state. You cannot force the quantum field to give you what you want."
> **Key takeaway**: The same drive that makes you successful in the material world can block you in the spiritual world. Learning to shift from doing to being is a skill.

**The fix:** After doing the practice (meditation, intention, heart coherence), practice letting go. Don't check. Don't evaluate. Don't analyze. Simply trust that the work is done and return to ordinary life. The outcome is not your job after the practice — it's the universe's job.

## Anti-Pattern 5: Separation Consciousness

**The mistake:** Believing that you are separate from the quantum field, from Source, from others. This belief creates a sense of powerlessness — you are a small self trying to get something from a big universe.

> **Case: The Rock Geshe** (Foreword): The Tibetan geshe who said "I am part of the rock, not separate from it" offers the antidote. When you know you are not separate, you don't need to get anything from the field — you are the field. Your intention is not a request; it's a statement of reality. "Be it, then see it" replaces "ask for it, hope for it, wait for it."
> **Key takeaway**: Separation is the root of all suffering and limitation. Unity consciousness is the root of all power. When you know you are one with the field, your intention becomes a declaration, not a petition.

**The fix:** In meditation, practice dissolving the boundary between yourself and your environment. Feel yourself expand. Feel your energy field merge with the room, the building, the sky. There is no "you" and "not-you." There is only one field.
