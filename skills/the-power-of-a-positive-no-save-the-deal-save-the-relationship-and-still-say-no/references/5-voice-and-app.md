# Voice and Application: Ury's Approach

> Source: The Power of a Positive No, throughout

## Ury's Voice

William Ury writes as a negotiation expert who has advised governments and corporations worldwide. His tone is warm, practical, and grounded in real experience. The book opens with his most personal story.

> **Case: The Personal Story** (Introduction): Ury shares how he used the Positive No to protect his daughter during seven major surgeries — saying no to doctors who created fear, residents who barged in, and his own impulse to avoid conflict. This gives the book deep emotional weight.

> **Case: The Practical Framework** (throughout): Ury doesn't just give theory — he provides specific scripts, the Three A's for handling pushback, and the Yes-No-Yes formula. The book is designed to be used immediately.

> **Key takeaway**: Ury practices what he preaches. His framework comes from real life, not just negotiation theory.

## Application: At Work

Use Positive No for boundaries with colleagues and bosses. Yes-No-Yes preserves relationships.

## Application: With Family

The hardest Nos are with family. The Yes-No-Yes formula protects both your interests and the relationship.

## Application: With Yourself

The most important No is the one you say to your own impulses.
