# Techniques: Handling Pushback

> Source: The Power of a Positive No, Chapters 7-9

## The Three A's

When the other person pushes back: Acknowledge their feelings, Assert your No calmly, Advance with alternatives.

> **Case: The Persistent Client** (Chapter 7): Client demands a Friday delivery you can't meet. Acknowledge: "I understand this deadline matters to you." Assert: "I can't deliver by Friday with the quality you expect." Advance: "Let's look at what's possible by Monday."

> **Key takeaway**: The Three A's keep you firm without escalating conflict. Acknowledge their feelings, then restate your No calmly.

## Stay on Your Side of the Net

You cannot control the other person's reaction. You can only control your own response.

> **Case: The Angry Colleague** (Chapter 8): A colleague becomes angry when you say no to their request. Ury's advice: don't mirror their anger. Stay calm. Repeat your No. Not taking their reaction personally is key.

> **Key takeaway**: You are responsible for your side of the conversation. Let the other person have their feelings.

## The Most Important No Is to Yourself

Saying no to your own impulses — the urge to please, the fear of discomfort, the desire to avoid conflict.

> **Case: The People-Pleaser's No** (Chapter 9): The hardest No isn't to others — it's to yourself. No to saying yes when you mean no. No to avoiding the difficult conversation. Ury argues that self-discipline is the foundation of the Positive No.

> **Case: The Impulse No** (Chapter 9): Saying no to checking email, no to procrastination, no to the easy path. Each self-No strengthens your ability to say No to others.

> **Key takeaway**: Master the No to yourself. All other Nos become easier.
