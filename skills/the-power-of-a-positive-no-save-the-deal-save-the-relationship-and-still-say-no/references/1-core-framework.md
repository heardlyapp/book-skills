# Core Framework: The Positive No

> Source: The Power of a Positive No, Introduction and Chapters 1-3

## The Three Steps

Prepare, Propose, Follow Through. Each step builds on the one before. Most people skip Prepare — that's why their Nos fail or damage relationships.

> **Case: Ury's Daughter's Medical Journey** (Introduction): Ury's infant daughter faced seven major surgeries. He had to say no to doctors who created fear, residents who barged in, and endless demands. His Yes: protecting his daughter. His No: structured visits and calm communication. His Yes to relationship: appreciation for the medical team's care. The Yes made the No possible and reasonable.

> **Key takeaway**: The Positive No starts with clarifying your Yes. A principled No is rooted in a clear Yes.

## Yes-No-Yes Formula

The heart of the framework: Yes to your interests, No to the request, Yes to the relationship.

> **Case: The Boss's Extra Project** (Chapter 2): "I want to deliver excellent work for you (Yes). I can't take this on right now without compromising quality (No). Let's discuss how to prioritize (Yes)."

> **Case: The Family Obligation** (Chapter 2): "I love you and want to help (Yes). I can't do what you're asking today (No). Let's find a time that works for both of us (Yes)."

> **Key takeaway**: Yes-No-Yes turns a refusal into collaboration. It protects your interests while honoring the relationship.

## Know Your Yes

Before you can say no powerfully, you must know what you stand for. Your Yes is the foundation.
