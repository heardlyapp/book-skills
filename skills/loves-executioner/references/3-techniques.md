# Death Anxiety and the Two Defenses

> Source: Love's Executioner, Prologue, Afterword

## How We Cope with Mortality

### The Two Major Defense Systems

| Defense | Description | Collapse |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| Personal Specialness | "I am beyond harm. Bad things happen to others, not me." | Shattered by the first major crisis — illness, accident, loss. |
| Ultimate Rescuer | "Someone will save me. I am not alone in the face of death." | The rescuer fails, dies, or reveals their own helplessness. |

> **Case: Elva's Purse Snatching** (Chapter 5)
> Elva, an elderly woman, had lived a charmed life. She was beautiful, wealthy, loved. She believed — at some level — that she was special, that tragedy happened to other people. Then her purse was snatched. It was a minor event, but it shattered her world. She could not stop thinking about it. She became anxious, depressed, unable to function. Yalom's interpretation: the purse snatching collapsed her specialness defense. She could no longer pretend she was beyond harm. The therapy was about helping her rebuild — not the illusion of invulnerability, but a realistic acceptance of vulnerability.
> **Key takeaway:** Elva's story is about the fragility of the specialness defense. It can be destroyed by a single event — even something as small as a purse snatching. The goal of existential therapy is not to rebuild the defense — it is to face the reality that the defense was hiding.

> **Case: The Dreamer** (Chapter 10)
> Marvin had a recurring nightmare: a dark figure with a cane pursuing him. Yalom explored the dream as a "failed dream" — a dream in which death anxiety broke through the defenses. The dark figure was death. The cane was a phallic symbol — the dreamer's attempt to defend against death through sexuality. Yalom showed that the nightmare was not a punishment or a message from the unconscious. It was raw death anxiety that had escaped its keepers.
> **Key takeaway:** Nightmares are not just random — they are the breakdown of death denial. The mind's usual defenses fail, and the terror of non-existence erupts into consciousness. Yalom's approach: do not try to suppress the nightmare — listen to it. It is telling you something true.
