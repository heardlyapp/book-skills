# 5 — Morin's Voice + 5 Application Scenarios

> Source: "13 Things Mentally Strong Women Don't Do" by Amy Morin

## Morin's Voice

Amy Morin is a licensed clinical social worker, psychotherapist, and author. Her voice: warm, empathetic, practical. She writes from the dual perspective of a therapist who has helped hundreds of women and a woman who has navigated her own grief and challenges.

> **Case: The Personal Story** (Introduction): "I grew up driving an ATV and catching night crawlers to use as fishing bait. I never liked dolls. I wasn't interested in makeup. And I hated to shop. But I was also deeply affected by sexism from a young age — a teacher who accused me of cheating for no reason, a father who wouldn't raise the issue." Morin opens with her own story to establish that she's not writing from an ivory tower.
> **Key takeaway**: Morin's credibility comes from both her professional expertise and her personal experience. She's not telling women what to do from above — she's sharing what she's learned as a therapist and as a woman navigating the same pressures.

> **Case: The Book's Origin** (Introduction): "After I wrote '13 Things Mentally Strong People Don't Do,' women reached out asking for a version that specifically addressed their unique challenges. The research showed that women face distinct pressures — perfectionism, comparison, self-doubt — that affect them differently." Morin explains why a women-focused version was necessary.
> **Key takeaway**: The book acknowledges that while mental strength principles apply to everyone, women face unique societal pressures that require specific attention. This is not about separation — it's about recognition of real differences in experience.

## Key Quote

"You don't have to be mentally strong every moment of every day. You just have to be a little stronger today than you were yesterday."

## 5 Application Scenarios

1. **Struggling with social media envy** → Audit your feed. Unfollow accounts that trigger comparison. Follow accounts that inspire you.
2. **Perfectionism causing procrastination** → Set a timer. Give yourself 30 minutes to complete a task as well as you can — then stop. Ship it.
3. **Feeling like an imposter** → Write down three facts about your achievements. Compare evidence, not feelings. Then take one small action.
4. **Overthinking a decision** → Set a deadline: "I will decide by Friday at 5pm." Gather enough info, then decide. No more research after that point.
5. **Avoiding a tough challenge** → Break it into the smallest possible first step. Do that step today. Momentum is more powerful than motivation.
