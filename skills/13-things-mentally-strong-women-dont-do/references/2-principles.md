# 2 — Let Go of Perfectionism

> Source: "13 Things Mentally Strong Women Don't Do" by Amy Morin

## Perfectionism as Fear in Disguise

Perfectionism is often mistaken for high standards. Morin argues it's actually a fear-based coping mechanism. It delays action, increases stress, and prevents genuine progress.

> **Case: The Procrastination-Perfection Loop** (Chapter 2 — Don't Insist on Perfection): "Shelby was a star athlete and stellar student who secretly struggled with perfectionism. She would spend hours rewriting the same paragraph because it wasn't good enough. The result: she submitted assignments late and felt constantly exhausted." Morin uses Shelby's story to illustrate how perfectionism doesn't produce better results — it produces more anxiety and less output.
> **Key takeaway**: Perfectionism is the enemy of done. The cost of perfect is often the complete loss of the project. "Done is better than perfect" is not a lowering of standards — it's a recognition that finished work has value that unfinished "perfect" work doesn't.

> **Case: The Fear of Judgment** (Chapter 2): "Many women report that their perfectionism is rooted in a fear of being judged. If the work is perfect, no one can criticize it. But this logic is flawed: perfect work doesn't exist, and the pursuit of it guarantees you'll never finish anything." Morin connects perfectionism to the social pressure women face to be exceptional in every domain.
> **Key takeaway**: Perfectionism is a protection mechanism against criticism. The irony is that it guarantees more criticism (for being late, not delivering, burning out) than simply doing good work and putting it out there.
