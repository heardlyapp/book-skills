# 1 — Stop Comparing and Embrace Your Own Path

> Source: "13 Things Mentally Strong Women Don't Do" by Amy Morin

## Comparison as a Mental Strength Killer

Comparison is the first and most fundamental habit mentally strong women give up. Social comparison is amplified by social media and deeply ingrained by cultural pressure on women.

> **Case: The Social Media Trap** (Chapter 1 — Don't Compare): "A 2018 study published in Computers in Human Behavior found that women who spend more time on social media report higher levels of social comparison and lower self-esteem. The curated nature of social media — everyone's best moments, filtered and edited — creates an impossible standard." Morin presents this research to show how comparison is not just a personal failing but a structural problem.
> **Key takeaway**: Social media is designed to trigger comparison. Recognizing this is the first step. The solution isn't to eliminate social media entirely but to become aware of how it affects you and to curate your feed intentionally.

> **Case: The Envy-Apology Cycle** (Chapter 1): "Brandi was a successful executive who felt jealous of her colleague's promotion. Instead of ignoring the feeling, she scheduled a coffee meeting with that colleague and learned about her journey. The envy turned into inspiration and a mentorship." Morin shows how comparison can be transformed from a negative force into a positive one.
> **Key takeaway**: Envy is information — it tells you what you value. The question is what you do with it. Use envy as a compass to understand your aspirations, not as a weapon against yourself.
