# 3 — Mindfulness Practices

> Source: "Old Path White Clouds" by Thich Nhat Hanh

## Walking Meditation

The Buddha taught walking meditation as a core practice. Walk slowly, consciously, feeling each step. "Walk as if you are kissing the earth with your feet."

## Mindful Breathing

Follow your breath. In: "I am breathing in." Out: "I am breathing out." The simplest and most powerful practice.

## The Tangerine Meditation

Eat one segment of a tangerine as if it's the only thing in the universe. Taste it fully. Be completely present.

> **Case: Svasti the Buffalo Boy** (Chapter 2): The book opens with Svasti, a poor buffalo boy who meets the Buddha. The Buddha teaches him how to walk mindfully, how to eat mindfully, how to be present. Svasti becomes a monk. The narrative follows his journey alongside the Buddha's.
> **Key takeaway**: The Buddha's teachings are accessible to everyone — a buffalo boy, a prince, a wealthy merchant. Mindfulness is not for the elite. It's for anyone who wants to suffer less.

> **Case: Walking Just to Walk** (Chapter 1): The first chapter describes the Buddha walking — not to get anywhere, just to walk. "When I walk, I arrive with every step." This is the essence of walking meditation: not to reach a destination, but to be fully present with each step.
> **Key takeaway**: Life is not about reaching a destination. It's about being present with each step. When you walk just to walk, every step is an arrival.
