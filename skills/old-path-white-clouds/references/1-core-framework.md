# 1 — The Buddha's Life Story

> Source: "Old Path White Clouds" by Thich Nhat Hanh

## The Journey: Birth to Nirvana

### Part 1: The Prince (Chapters 1-12)
Siddhartha Gautama was born a prince in Lumbini (now Nepal) in the 6th century BCE. Raised in luxury, shielded from suffering. Married to Yasodhara, father of Rahula.

> **Case: The Four Sights** (Chapters 3-5): Siddhartha ventured outside the palace four times. He saw an old man, a sick man, a corpse, and a wandering ascetic. These four sights shattered his sheltered world. He realized suffering was universal — and that there must be a path beyond it.
> **Key takeaway**: Comfort can be a cage. Siddhartha could have stayed in the palace, ignoring suffering. He chose to see the truth, even though it was painful. That choice changed the world.

### Part 2: The Seeker (Chapters 13-18)
He left the palace at 29, spent six years practicing extreme asceticism with various teachers, nearly died from self-mortification.

### Part 3: The Awakened One (Chapters 19-20)
Under the bodhi tree, he meditated through the night. By morning, he saw the true nature of reality. He became the Buddha — the Awakened One.

> **Case: Enlightenment Under the Bodhi Tree** (Chapter 19): The Buddha sat under the bodhi tree and vowed not to rise until he understood the nature of suffering. Through the night, he was visited by Mara (temptation). He did not fight. He simply touched the earth and asked her to witness his merit. By dawn, he was enlightened.
> **Key takeaway**: Enlightenment is not achieved through fighting or struggling. It comes from stillness, from touching the earth, from trusting the ground beneath you.

### Part 4: The Teacher (Chapters 21-80)
He taught for 45 years, establishing a community of monks and nuns, wandering through India, teaching all who would listen.

### Part 5: Final Nirvana (Chapter 80)
At 80, he passed into final nirvana, his last words: "All conditioned things are impermanent. Strive on with diligence."
