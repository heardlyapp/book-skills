# Reference: Neural Signatures

## Overview
Consciousness leaves measurable traces in brain activity. Dehaene identifies several reliable signatures that distinguish conscious from unconscious processing.

## Case: The P3 Wave
The P3 wave is a large positive deflection in the EEG, occurring about 300ms after a conscious stimulus. It is the most reliable neural signature of consciousness. Unconscious stimuli (briefly presented and masked) do not produce a P3. The P3 reflects the moment of ignition — widespread broadcasting of information across the global workspace.

## Case: Sustained Activity
Conscious stimuli produce sustained, long-lasting brain activity. Unconscious stimuli produce only a brief, transient burst. Conscious activity can persist for seconds; unconscious activity dies out within a few hundred milliseconds. The duration of activity is a distinguishing feature.

## Case: fMRI Signatures
Using fMRI, conscious processing is associated with widespread activation in prefrontal and parietal regions. Unconscious processing is limited to specialized sensory areas. The "global ignition" is visible in fMRI as a burst of activity across these higher-order regions. The fMRI pattern can predict whether a stimulus was perceived consciously, even when the subject does not report it.

## Case: The Conscious Threshold
Dehaene's experiments precisely measure the conscious threshold. A stimulus can be presented for increasingly long durations. The subject's ability to report it increases suddenly at a specific duration — the conscious threshold. Below this threshold, performance is at chance. Above it, performance jumps. The transition is not gradual but abrupt, supporting the ignition model.
