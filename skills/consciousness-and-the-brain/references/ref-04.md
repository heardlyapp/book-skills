# Reference: Subliminal Perception

## Overview
The brain can process a remarkable amount of information without conscious awareness. Subliminal perception reveals the extent of unconscious processing and its limits.

## Case: Unconscious Arithmetic
Dehaene showed that subjects can perform simple arithmetic unconsciously. A masked number is presented, followed by a conscious number. The subject's reaction time shows that the unconscious number influenced their processing. Abstract operations like addition can occur without awareness — demonstrating that high-level cognitive functions do not require consciousness.

## Case: The Limits of Unconscious Processing
While the unconscious brain can process words, numbers, and simple relations, it cannot handle novel combinations or logical reasoning. Unconscious processing is local and automatic. It cannot integrate information across different domains. The inability to flexibly combine information is the fundamental limitation of unconscious cognition.

## Case: Subliminal Advertising
Dehaene reviews the evidence for subliminal advertising. Subliminal messages can influence preferences for a few seconds, but the effect is weak and temporary. There is no evidence that subliminal messages can produce lasting behavioral change. The fear of "subliminal brainwashing" is not supported by the science.

## Case: The Masking Technique
Masking is the key experimental technique. A stimulus is presented briefly (30ms) and followed by a meaningless pattern (the mask). The mask interrupts conscious processing. The stimulus was perceived (the retina and early visual cortex processed it) but not consciously. The contrast between masked and unmasked conditions reveals the neural signature of consciousness.
