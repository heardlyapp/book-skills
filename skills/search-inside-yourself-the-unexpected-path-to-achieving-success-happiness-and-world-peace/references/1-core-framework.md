# Core Framework: Mindfulness and EQ Fundamentals

> Source: Search Inside Yourself, Introduction and Chapters 1-2

## Meng's Story and the SIY Program

Chade-Meng Tan ("Meng") was Google engineer #107. He convinced the company to let him create a mindfulness and emotional intelligence course — then turned it into this book with forewords by Daniel Goleman and Jon Kabat-Zinn.

> **Case: The Unlikely Mindfulness Teacher** (Introduction): Meng was known around Google as the "jolly good fellow" — his business card included the title "which nobody can deny." He was famous for his photo wall with celebrities. Yet he was also a serious meditator who believed mindfulness could create world peace. This combination of joy and depth is the SIY method.

> **Key takeaway**: Meng proves you don't have to be a somber meditation master. Mindfulness is compatible with joy, humor, and even silliness.

## Attention Training — The Foundation

The SIY program begins with attention. Before you can regulate emotions or understand others, you must be able to direct your own focus.

> **Case: The Breath Counting Practice** (Chapter 2): Meng describes the basic practice: sit upright, close your eyes, count breaths (1 on exhale, 2 on next, up to 10, restart). When the mind wanders — and it will — just notice and return. Each return is one "rep" at the mental gym.

> **Case: The Google Engineer's Objection** (Chapter 1): A typical engineer says "I can't meditate — my mind is too busy." Meng's response: a busy mind is exactly why you need to practice. The mind doesn't need to be empty, it needs to be trained.

> **Key takeaway**: Mindfulness is not about having a blank mind. It's about noticing when you've wandered and returning. The return is the practice. Like any skill, it improves with repetition.
