# Principles: Self-Awareness and Emotional Regulation

> Source: Search Inside Yourself, Chapters 3-5

## Mindful Self-Awareness

The first EQ competency. Knowing what you're feeling as it arises. Meng calls this "the foundation of all emotional intelligence."

> **Case: Emotional Granularity** (Chapter 3): Meng introduces the concept of naming emotions precisely. Not "I feel bad" but "I feel disappointed, frustrated, and slightly anxious." The more precise the label, the more control you have. This skill can be practiced.

> **Key takeaway**: You cannot regulate what you cannot name. Emotional granularity is the gateway to emotional regulation.

## Riding Emotions Like a Horse

Meng's metaphor: don't fight the emotion, don't ignore it, don't let it carry you away. Learn to ride it skillfully.

> **Case: The Second Arrow** (Chapter 5): Meng shares the Buddhist teaching: the first arrow is the event itself (pain, frustration). The second arrow is our reaction to it (anger at being frustrated, anxiety about the reaction). You can't avoid the first arrow, but you can avoid shooting the second.

> **Key takeaway**: Between stimulus and response is a space. Mindfulness creates that space. In that space is your freedom to choose.

## Self-Confidence Through Self-Compassion

True confidence comes not from believing you're special but from accepting yourself as you are.

> **Key takeaway**: Emotions are data, not directives. They inform you without controlling you. This is the skill Meng's method develops.
