# Voice and Application: Meng's Approach

> Source: Search Inside Yourself, throughout

## Meng's Voice

Chade-Meng Tan writes with joy, humor, and genuine humility. He's an engineer teaching mindfulness — and he never forgets his audience. The book is full of jokes, pop culture references, and warmth. He's deeply serious about the practice but completely unserious about himself.

> **Case: The "Jolly Good Fellow"** (Introduction): Meng's business card read "Jolly Good Fellow (Which Nobody Can Deny)." His photo wall with celebrities and world leaders was legendary. Yet underneath the jokes was a sincere meditation practitioner who believed he could help create world peace.

> **Case: The Dalai Lama Story** (Introduction): Meng hosted the Dalai Lama at Google. The Dalai Lama, after hearing about SIY, told Meng he was "very happy" with the program. This blend of highest spiritual authority and corporate cultural context is uniquely Meng.

> **Key takeaway**: Meng proves that joy and wisdom, humor and depth, can coexist. Mindfulness doesn't require solemnity.

## Application: Daily Practice

Begin with one minute. Build the habit. Expand slowly. Consistency over intensity.

## Application: Workplace

Use SIY for difficult conversations, leadership, team building, and stress management.

## Application: Relationships

Practice loving-kindness meditation. It directly increases empathy.
