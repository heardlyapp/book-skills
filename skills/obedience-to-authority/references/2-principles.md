# Key Principles — Obedience to Authority

## I. Obedience Is the Cement That Binds Society

"Obedience is as basic an element in the structure of social life as one can point to." Without authority systems, society cannot function. But the same mechanism that enables cooperation also enables atrocities. "Obedience is the psychological mechanism that links individual action to political purpose."

## II. Ordinary People Will Commit Extraordinary Acts

"65% of subjects went to the maximum 450 volts." These were not psychopaths — they were "ordinary people, simply doing their jobs." The psychiatrists' prediction of 1% was spectacularly wrong. Case: "The most fundamental lesson of our study: ordinary people, simply doing their jobs, and without any particular hostility on their part, can become agents in a terrible destructive process."

## III. The Agentic State Explains the Shift

"A person comes to view himself as the instrument for carrying out another person's wishes." In this state, he no longer feels responsible. "I'm just doing my job" is the voice of the agentic state. Case: The subject who protested even as he flipped the switches — his conscience was active, but he couldn't translate it into action.

## IV. Situation Over Personality

"The key lies not in the personalities of the perpetrators but in the structure of the situation." Milgram's variations proved that changing the situation changed obedience more than any individual difference. Case: When peers disobeyed, only 10% continued. When the experimenter was remote, obedience halved.

## V. Gradual Escalation Is Crucial

"The subject could not see quite how to extricate himself." The shocks increased by 15 volts at a time. At no point was there a single "jump" that demanded a clear moral decision. Case: The incremental nature of the escalation made it possible — each step was "just a little more."

## VI. Proximity Reduces Obedience

When the victim was in the same room, obedience dropped from 65% to 40%. When touch was required (forcing the hand onto the plate), it dropped to 30%. Case: "It is psychologically easy to harm someone when they are not present."

## VII. The Capacity to Resist Must Be Cultivated

"Obedience is learned through socialization. The capacity to resist authority must also be learned." It is not natural to defy authority — it takes practice, moral clarity, and social support. Case: When subjects had a model of defiance (a confederate who refused), their own defiance skyrocketed.
