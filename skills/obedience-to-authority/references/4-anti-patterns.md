# Anti-Patterns — Obedience to Authority

## The Central Error: "I Would Never Do That"

"Before the experiment, everyone says they would defy authority." Milgram surveyed 40 psychiatrists — they predicted fewer than 1% would go to 450V. The actual result: 65%. "We vastly overestimate our capacity for moral resistance." Case: "The most fundamental lesson: ordinary people can become agents in a terrible destructive process."

### Mistake 1: "Only bad people do bad things"
"The key lies not in the personalities of the perpetrators but in the structure of the situation." Milgram's subjects were a cross-section of society. "It is not that evil people do evil things — it is that ordinary people do evil things in certain situations."

### Mistake 2: "I'd know when to stop"
"The experiment's power came from gradual escalation." The shocks increased by 15 volts. At no point was there a clear, dramatic line. Case: "If the subject had been asked to deliver 450V on the first trial, almost no one would have done it. But 450V was the 30th step."

### Mistake 3: "Saying 'I can't' is enough"
"Many subjects protested verbally while continuing to obey." They said "I can't do this" — and then did it. Case: "Verbal protest without behavioral defiance is not resistance."

### Mistake 4: "Orders from above absolve me of responsibility"
"The essence of the agentic state: the person no longer regards himself as responsible." Case: Adolf Eichmann's defense: "I was just following orders." The Milgram experiment showed that this psychological shift happens even in a trivial laboratory setting.

### Mistake 5: "Auschwitz couldn't happen here"
"The capacity for obedience exists in all human societies." Milgram's experiment was replicated in multiple countries with similar results. Case: The My Lai Massacre (Vietnam) happened in 1968 — American soldiers killing unarmed civilians on orders.

### Mistake 6: "The Milgram experiment was unethical, so we can ignore its findings"
"The ethical controversy is real. The findings are still true." Later replications with tighter ethics have found similar results. Case: Jerry Burger's 2009 partial replication found obedience levels comparable to Milgram's.
