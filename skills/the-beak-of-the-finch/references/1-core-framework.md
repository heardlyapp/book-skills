## The Weiner Framework: Evolution as Contemporary History

Weiner's framework: evolution is not ancient history — it is current events. The Grants' finches show natural selection operating year by year, generation by generation, measurable and visible.

**The Key Insight**: The Grants proved that Darwin was right — and that he underestimated the speed of evolution. Under strong selection pressure, measurable change can occur in a single generation.

> **Case: The 1977 Drought** (Chapter 4): In 1977, Daphne Major received almost no rain. Small seeds were exhausted. Only large, hard seeds remained. The medium ground finches with larger beaks could crack these seeds. Smaller-beaked birds died. By the next generation, average beak size had increased by 5%. Evolution had occurred.
> **Key takeaway**: Natural selection is not slow. When the environment changes, the population can change visibly in a single year.

> **Case: The Immigrant Finch** (Chapter 10): In 1981, a male hybrid finch arrived on Daphne Major. It was distinct from the resident finches — different song, different beak shape. It bred with native females and produced offspring. Over the next decades, the Grants tracked this lineage. The hybrid population diverged from the native finches — potentially a new species in formation.
> **Key takeaway**: Speciation begins with a single individual. The arrival of one bird on one island can start the process of creating a new species.
