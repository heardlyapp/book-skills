# Techniques: Detox and Lifestyle

> Source: The Menopause Reset, Chapters 9-13

## Detoxification

Environmental toxins disrupt hormones. Supporting the body's natural detox pathways is essential for hormone balance.

> **Case: The Liver Support Protocol** (Chapter 9): Dr. Pelz recommends specific foods and practices to support liver detox — cruciferous vegetables (broccoli, kale, cabbage), adequate protein for Phase 2 detox, hydration, and avoiding processed foods and alcohol.

> **Key takeaway**: You can't avoid all environmental toxins, but you can strongly support your body's ability to process and eliminate them.

## Rushing Woman's Syndrome

Chronic stress from constant hurry destroys hormone balance. Dr. Pelz dedicates a full chapter to this.

> **Case: The Overwhelmed Executive** (Chapter 10): A patient with severe hot flashes and insomnia was rushing from task to task, never pausing. When she built even 10 minutes of deliberate rest into her day — no phone, no agenda — her symptoms began to improve within weeks.

> **Key takeaway**: Stress is one of the primary drivers of menopause symptoms. Rest is not optional — it's essential medicine.

## The Power of Sleep

Hormone production depends on deep, restorative sleep. It's the foundation everything else builds on.

> **Case: The Insomnia Cycle** (Chapter 11): Poor sleep worsens hormone imbalance, which worsens sleep further. Dr. Pelz breaks this cycle with sleep hygiene, fasting (going to bed on an empty stomach), and evening routines.

> **Case: The Sleep-Fasting Connection** (Chapter 11): Fasting before bed (stopping eating 3-4 hours before sleep) dramatically improves sleep quality and hormone production.

> **Key takeaway**: Sleep is when your body heals and produces hormones. Prioritize it above almost everything else.
