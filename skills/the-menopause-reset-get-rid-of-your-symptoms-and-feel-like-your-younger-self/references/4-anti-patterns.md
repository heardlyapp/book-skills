# Anti-Patterns: Menopause Mistakes

> Source: The Menopause Reset, Chapters 7-12

## Treating Symptoms, Not Causes

The most common medical approach: prescribe medication for hot flashes, sleeping pills for insomnia, antidepressants for mood. These address symptoms, not root causes.

> **Case: The Multimedicated Patient** (Chapter 7): A patient on multiple medications for menopause symptoms still felt terrible. Each drug addressed one symptom, but none addressed the cellular dysfunction. Only when she addressed root causes did she truly heal.

> **Key takeaway**: Symptom suppression has its place for acute relief, but it's not a long-term solution. Address the root cause at the cellular level.

## Starving Yourself

Many women reduce calories drastically during menopause. This stresses the body and worsens hormone imbalance.

> **Case: The 1200-Calorie Trap** (Chapter 7): A woman eating 1200 calories/day couldn't lose weight. Her body was in starvation mode, flooding her system with stress hormones. When she ate MORE nutrient-dense food, her weight normalized.

> **Key takeaway**: Don't confuse calorie restriction with fasting. Fasting is about cellular repair through eating windows. Calorie restriction is chronic stress. They produce opposite effects on hormones.

## Ignoring Stress

Stress is the elephant in the room. Many focus entirely on diet and exercise while ignoring the primary driver of symptoms.

> **Case: The Stressed Executive** (Chapter 10): A woman tried keto, fasting, and supplements — but her symptoms persisted. She was chronically stressed at work. Only when she addressed stress did everything else start working.

> **Key takeaway**: Diet and exercise are important, but stress management may be more important. Start with rest, then add the other tools.
