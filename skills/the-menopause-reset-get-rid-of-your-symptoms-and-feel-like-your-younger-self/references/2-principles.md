# Principles: Fasting and Keto for Menopause

> Source: The Menopause Reset, Chapters 4-7

## Intermittent Fasting

Fasting is the most powerful tool for cellular repair. It promotes autophagy — the body's cellular cleaning process.

> **Case: The 16:8 Start** (Chapter 6): Dr. Pelz recommends starting with 16:8 — eat within an 8-hour window, fast for 16 hours. Many women experience reduced hot flashes, better energy, and weight loss within the first week.

> **Key takeaway**: Fasting is not starvation. It's giving your body time to repair. Start with 16:8 and adjust.

## Ketogenic Diet

Low-carb, high-fat eating supports hormone production and stabilizes blood sugar.

> **Case: The Breakfast Shift** (Chapter 6): Dr. Pelz challenges the conventional advice to eat breakfast. She argues that starting the day with a high-carb breakfast spikes blood sugar and worsens hormone imbalance. Delaying the first meal benefits hormone health.

> **Key takeaway**: Keto and fasting work together. Fasting creates the time window; keto provides the fuel source.

## The Estrobolome

The gut microbiome plays a key role in estrogen metabolism. A healthy estrobolome supports hormone balance.

> **Case: The Gut-Hormone Connection** (Chapter 8): Gut bacteria process estrogen. When the gut microbiome is unhealthy (dysbiosis), estrogen metabolism is disrupted, worsening menopause symptoms.

> **Key takeaway**: Keto and intermittent fasting are powerful tools because they address root causes — cellular repair and metabolic flexibility.
