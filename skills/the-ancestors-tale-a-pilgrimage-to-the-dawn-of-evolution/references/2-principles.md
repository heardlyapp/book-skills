# Principles: The Human Lineage

> Source: The Ancestor's Tale, Rendezvous 0-3

## Our Closest Cousins

The journey begins with our closest relatives. Dawkins presents each group's "tale" from their own perspective — a unique feature of the book.

> **Case: The Chimpanzee's Tale** (Rendezvous 1): Chimps share ~99% of our DNA. Dawkins argues we should see them as evolutionary kin, not exotic animals. The difference between us and chimps is less than the genetic difference between two species of gibbon.

> **Key takeaway**: 99% DNA shared. Our closeness to chimpanzees is not opinion — it's a testable, verified fact of biology.

## The Hominid Journey

From humans back through hominid ancestors: archaic Homo sapiens, ergaster, habilis, australopithecines.

> **Case: Little Foot's Tale** (Rendezvous 0): "Little Foot" is a 3.7-million-year-old Australopithecus from South Africa. She walked upright with a small brain — a mosaic. Dawkins uses her to show that each species was adapted to its time, not "striving" toward humans.

> **Case: The Neanderthal Tale** (Rendezvous 0): Neanderthals were not brutish cavemen. They had larger brains than modern humans, made art, buried their dead. They were a separate branch, not a stage. Dawkins corrects the popular image.

> **Key takeaway**: Each hominid was adapted to its own time. They weren't trying to become us. The ladder image is wrong.

## The Phylogenetic Order

Rendezvous order reveals relatedness: chimps first, gorillas, orangutans, gibbons — each deeper in time.
