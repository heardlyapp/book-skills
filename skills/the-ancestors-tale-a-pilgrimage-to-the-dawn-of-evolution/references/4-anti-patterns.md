# Anti-Patterns: Misunderstanding Evolution

> Source: The Ancestor's Tale, throughout

## The Ladder Fallacy

The most persistent error: seeing evolution as a ladder from "primitive" to "advanced," with humans at the top.

> **Case: The Living Fossil Myth** (Rendezvous 30): The velvet worm is called a "living fossil" — but it hasn't stopped evolving. It has been evolving for 500 million years just like everything else. "Primitive" is an ignorant judgment, not a biological description.

> **Key takeaway**: Every species is perfectly adapted to its environment. There are no "primitive" species — only differently adapted ones.

## The Missing Link Fallacy

The idea that there should be a single "missing link" between species. Evolution is a bush, not a chain with humans at the end.

> **Case: Neanderthal Misrepresentation** (Rendezvous 0): Neanderthals were depicted as brutish "missing links." Actually, they had larger brains, made art, and were a separate branch. They weren't "on the way" to becoming us — they were their own evolutionary path.

> **Key takeaway**: There is no single "missing link." The tree of life has millions of links, and every fossil fills a gap.

## "Just a Theory"

Evolution is dismissed as "just a theory." This reflects a profound misunderstanding of science.

> **Case: The Theory of Gravity** (throughout): Dawkins draws the comparison: gravity is "just a theory" — that doesn't mean it's uncertain. In science, "theory" is the highest level of explanatory power. Evolution by natural selection is supported by more independent lines of evidence than any other theory in biology.

> **Key takeaway**: "Just a theory" reveals ignorance of what scientific theory means. Evolution is a fact, supported by overwhelming evidence. The theory explains the fact.
