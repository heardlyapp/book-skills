# Techniques: Deep Time and the Origin of Life

> Source: The Ancestor's Tale, Rendezvous 10-40

## The Deep Rendezvous

As the pilgrimage goes deeper, we meet more distant cousins: rodents, marsupials, reptiles, amphibians, fish, invertebrates, bacteria.

> **Case: The Hippo's Tale** (Rendezvous 11): Whales evolved from land mammals, and their closest living relative is the hippopotamus. This is one of the most surprising facts in the book — whales and hippos share a recent concestor.

> **Case: The Velvet Worm's Tale** (Rendezvous 30): The velvet worm (onychophora) is a living fossil that has barely changed for 500 million years. It represents our concestor with arthropods — insects, spiders, crustaceans.

> **Key takeaway**: DNA reveals relationships that anatomy alone would never suggest. Whales' closest relative is not a fish — it's a hippo.

## Convergent Evolution

Different lineages independently solve similar problems.

> **Case: The Marsupial Mole** (Rendezvous 12): Australia's marsupial mole looks identical to the placental mole of other continents — but they evolved independently. Their similarity is convergence.

> **Case: The Cactus and the Euphorb** (throughout): African euphorb plants look identical to American cacti. Both evolved succulent stems and spines in response to desert conditions — but from completely different ancestors.

> **Key takeaway**: Convergent evolution shows that natural selection is constrained by physics and ecology.

## LUCA — The Last Universal Common Ancestor

Rendezvous 40: the origin of all life on Earth.

> **Case: The Bacterial World** (Rendezvous 40): Bacteria have dominated Earth for most of its history. Humans are a recent twig on a bacterial tree. Our concestor with E. coli is the origin of life itself.
