# Core Framework: The Pilgrimage and Rendezvous

> Source: The Ancestor's Tale, Prologue and Rendezvous 0-1

## The Pilgrimage Concept

Dawkins frames evolution as a pilgrimage backward in time. At each "rendezvous" point, our lineage meets another living group, sharing a "concestor" (common ancestor). There are 40 rendezvous, from chimpanzees to bacteria.

> **Case: The Chimpanzee Rendezvous** (Rendezvous 1): Our first rendezvous is with chimpanzees and bonobos. Our concestor lived 6-7 million years ago. After diverging, we became humans; they became chimps. Neither line is "more evolved" — both have been evolving for exactly the same amount of time.

> **Key takeaway**: The pilgrimage structure makes common ancestry vivid and concrete. We travel back to meet our ancestors in imagination.

## The Tree of Life

Every species is a twig on a vast branching tree. The tree is not a ladder.

> **Case: The Bacterial Cousins at Rendezvous 40**: Our deepest rendezvous is with bacteria. Our concestor with E. coli is LUCA — the Last Universal Common Ancestor of all life. Bacteria are not "less evolved" — they've been evolving for 4 billion years, just like us.

> **Case: The Neanderthal's Tale** (Rendezvous 0): Neanderthals were not our ancestors but cousins, sharing Concestor 0 — the most recent common ancestor of all living humans. They were a separate branch, not a rung on a ladder.

> **Key takeaway**: The tree of life connects everything. "Higher" and "lower" are human projections, not biological reality. Every living thing is equally ancient.
