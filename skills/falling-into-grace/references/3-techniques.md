# Techniques — Falling into Grace

## How to End Suffering (Based on Adyashanti)

### Technique 1: The Childhood Observer
Adya's practice: notice when you believe your thoughts. The moment you catch yourself believing a thought, you are already free of it. Case: As a child, Adya watched adults arguing. "They actually believe the thoughts in their head." That single observation was the beginning of understanding the root of suffering. Practice: watch your mind the way a child watches adults — with curiosity, not belief.

### Technique 2: The Space Between Thoughts
"All thoughts arise and disappear into a vast space." Instead of following the next thought, notice the gap. The gap is not between thoughts — it's the space in which all thoughts occur. Case: "If we can, just for a moment, relinquish this anxious tendency to grab our thoughts, we begin to notice something very profound: that thoughts arise and play out, spontaneously and on their own, within a vast space."

### Technique 3: The Raw Emotion Bypass
When a strong emotion arises, feel it in the body before adding the story. "When we feel an emotion without the thought 'this shouldn't be happening,' it's just energy moving through." Case: Anger arises. Normally you think "he shouldn't have done that" (story). Instead, feel the vibration in your chest (raw energy). The story creates suffering. The energy passes. "This is a direct gateway to freedom."

### Technique 4: The Dog Witness
Adya noticed dogs could put down suffering within seconds. "A dog could get upset — but within minutes, it would just slough it off." The technique: watch how animals process emotions — they feel, release, and move on without adding narrative. Be like a dog.

### Technique 5: Not-Knowing
"Be willing to entertain the possibility that you may not know what you think you know." In the gap of not-knowing, grace can enter. Case: When you catch yourself in a strong opinion or certainty, ask: "Do I actually know this is true?" The suspension of conclusion opens the door to fresh perception.

### Technique 6: Stop Seeking
"The most profound thing we can do is to stop searching for something outside of where we already are." Seeking reinforces the sense of lack. Grace comes when you stop climbing and allow yourself to fall.

### Technique 7: Inner Stability
When chaos arises externally, don't look for stability outside. "The ground of being is not something you attain — it's what you already are when you stop trying to be something else." Sit still. Be the space. Feel the stability that is always present beneath the surface of thought.
