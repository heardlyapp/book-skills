# Reference: Put First Things First

## Overview
Habit 3 is the physical creation — the actual execution of Habit 1 (choice) and Habit 2 (vision). It is about prioritizing what matters most.

## Case: The Time Management Matrix
Covey's Time Management Matrix has four quadrants. Quadrant I: urgent and important (crises, deadlines). Quadrant II: not urgent but important (relationships, planning, exercise). Quadrant III: urgent but not important (interruptions, some calls/email). Quadrant IV: not urgent and not important (trivia, busywork). Effective people minimize Quadrant I, say no to Quadrants III and IV, and spend as much time as possible in Quadrant II.

## Case: The Big Rocks
A powerful metaphor: a jar filled with rocks (big priorities) and sand (smaller activities). If you put the sand in first, the rocks will not fit. If you put the rocks in first, the sand fills the spaces around them. The lesson: do the most important things first. Schedule your big priorities (Quadrant II) before you fill your day with the small stuff.

## Case: Weekly Planning
Covey recommends planning by the week, not by the day. At the beginning of each week, review your mission statement and roles, and schedule the most important things you can do in each role that week. The week is long enough to be strategic but short enough to be actionable.

## Case: Effective Delegation
There are two types of delegation: gofer delegation (tell someone exactly what to do) and stewardship delegation (agree on results and let the person choose the method). Covey recommends stewardship delegation for Quadrant II activities. It takes more time upfront but develops people and frees you to focus on your highest value activities.
