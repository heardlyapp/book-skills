# Reference: Sharpen the Saw

## Overview
Habit 7 is renewal — preserving and enhancing your greatest asset: yourself. It is the habit that makes all the other habits possible.

## Case: The Four Dimensions
Sharpen the saw has four dimensions: physical (exercise, nutrition, rest), mental (reading, writing, learning), social/emotional (service, empathy, synergy), and spiritual (meditation, prayer, values clarification). Neglecting any dimension weakens the others. Physical neglect leads to low energy. Mental neglect leads to stagnation. Social neglect leads to isolation. Spiritual neglect leads to loss of purpose.

## Case: The P/PC Balance
The fable of the goose and the golden egg: the goose lays a golden egg every day. The farmer, impatient, kills the goose to get all the eggs at once — and ends up with nothing. P (production) is the golden egg. PC (production capability) is the goose. Effectiveness is P + PC in balance. Most people focus on P and neglect PC, destroying long-term effectiveness.

## Case: Continuous Improvement
Sharpening the saw is not a one-time event — it is a continuous process of renewal and growth. Covey compares it to sharpening a saw before cutting a tree. If you are too busy cutting to sharpen the saw, you will become less effective over time. The most effective people invest time in self-renewal regularly.

## Case: The Upward Spiral
The 7 Habits create an upward spiral. Habit 7 renews the capacity to practice the other six. The physical dimension (exercise) renews energy for proactivity. The mental dimension (learning) deepens understanding for Habit 5. The spiritual dimension (values) strengthens commitment to Habit 2. The upward spiral is the process of continuous growth.
