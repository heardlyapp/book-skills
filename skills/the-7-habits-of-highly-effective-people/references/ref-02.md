# Reference: Begin with the End in Mind

## Overview
Habit 2 is about mental creation. All things are created twice: first in the mind, then in reality. Begin with a clear vision of your desired outcome.

## Case: The Personal Mission Statement
Covey recommends writing a personal mission statement — a written document that captures what you want to be (character), what you want to do (contributions), and the principles on which your life is based. The mission statement becomes your constitution, the foundation for all decisions. Many people spend more time planning a vacation than planning their lives.

## Case: Visualization
Our ability to visualize — to see in our mind's eye what is not yet physical reality — is a uniquely human gift. Olympic athletes use visualization to improve performance. Leaders use it to create a compelling vision. Covey recommends practicing visualization daily: see yourself living your mission statement, making choices aligned with your values.

## Case: Roles and Goals
The mission statement is the vision; roles and goals bring it into daily reality. Each person has multiple roles (parent, spouse, professional, community member). Covey recommends writing goals for each role that align with your mission statement. This ensures that you are not just busy but effective — working toward what matters most in every dimension of your life.

## Case: The Two Creations
Everything begins with mental creation — a vision in the mind. Physical creation follows. Building a house requires a blueprint. Writing a book requires an outline. Living an effective life requires a vision. Those who skip the first creation often find themselves building a reality they did not intend.
