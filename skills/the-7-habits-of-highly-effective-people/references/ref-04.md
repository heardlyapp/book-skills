# Reference: Public Victory Habits

## Overview
Public Victory — Habits 4-6 — is the journey from independence to interdependence. You cannot succeed alone. The public victory habits build trust, communication, and collaboration.

## Case: Habit 4 — Think Win-Win
Win-win is a frame of mind that seeks mutual benefit. It is based on six paradigms: win-win, win-lose, lose-win, lose-lose, win (independent), and win-win or no deal. Covey recommends "win-win or no deal" for the most important relationships: if we cannot find a solution that benefits both, we agree not to proceed. This preserves the relationship for future opportunities.

## Case: Habit 5 — Seek First to Understand
Most people listen with the intent to reply, not to understand. Empathic listening — listening with the intent to truly understand the other person — is the deepest need of the human soul. When people feel truly understood, they become more open to being influenced. Covey says: "Diagnose before you prescribe."

## Case: Habit 6 — Synergize
Synergy is not compromise or agreement — it is creation. When two people truly communicate with mutual understanding and respect, they create something neither could have created alone. Synergy requires valuing differences. People who think alike do not produce synergies; people who think differently can. The ability to synergize is the highest form of cooperation.

## Case: The Emotional Bank Account
Trust in relationships is like a bank account. You make deposits: understanding, small kindnesses, keeping commitments, clarifying expectations, showing integrity. Withdrawals: unkindness, broken promises, secrets, gossip, arrogance. A high-balance relationship can tolerate mistakes. A low-balance relationship cannot. Proactively making deposits is a Quadrant II activity.
