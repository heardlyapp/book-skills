# Techniques — Inner Excellence

## How to Train Your Mind for Peak Performance (Based on Jim Murphy)

### Technique 1: The State Shift
Murphy's core technique: "You cannot always control what happens to you, but you can always control your state." When feeling anxious, shift your physiology: take three slow deep breaths, stand up straight, soften your gaze. Then shift your focus: ask yourself "What am I grateful for right now?" Case: PGA Tour golfer Vaughn Taylor used the state shift before crucial putts — and went from struggling to winning.

### Technique 2: The Opponent Watch
Identify which of the three internal opponents is active. The Critic ("You're not good enough") → respond with truth. Monkey Mind (scattered thoughts) → bring focus with breath. Trickster ("Just this once won't hurt") → name it. Case: When Murphy played in the Cubs organization, he noticed his Critic would emerge after a strikeout. Learning to recognize it was the first step to quieting it.

### Technique 3: The Identity Notecard
Write down a one-sentence statement of who you are regardless of outcomes. Read it before every performance. "A hero is not someone who never fails. A hero is someone who knows who they are regardless of outcomes." Case: Michael Jordan's identity was not "basketball player who never misses" — it was "someone who works harder than anyone and learns from failure."

### Technique 4: The Pre-Mortem
Before a high-pressure event, imagine the worst has happened. Accept it fully. Then proceed. "If you are not afraid to die, you are free to truly live." The Samurai practice of "imagining oneself already dead" eliminates the fear that paralyzes.

### Technique 5: Single-Tasking
"Multitasking is a myth. The brain can only focus on one thing at a time." Murphy's technique: set a timer for 25 minutes. Do one thing. No phone, no email, no switching. "The most productive people do not do more things — they do fewer things better." Case: Navy SEALs train to focus entirely on the immediate task, not the overall mission.

### Technique 6: The Gratitude Pivot
When faced with a stressful situation, immediately find three things to be grateful for. This shifts focus from what's wrong to what's right. Case: After a devastating loss, Murphy would write down three things he was grateful for — this prevented the spiral into self-pity and opened space for learning.

### Technique 7: The Heart Check
Murphy's daily practice: examine what your heart is attached to. "Is my identity tied to this outcome? Am I seeking approval or meaning?" If your heart is attached to something temporary, redirect it. Case: Bunpachiro's heart was attached to being a Samurai. When that ended, he broke. Yoshitaka's heart was attached to purpose and service — which could never be taken away.
