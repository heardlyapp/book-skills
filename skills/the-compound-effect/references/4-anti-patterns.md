# Anti-Patterns — The Compound Effect

## The Central Error: Believing in Overnight Success

"Most people get tripped up by the simplicity of the Compound Effect. They quit after the eighth day of running because they're still overweight." The magic penny does nothing for 20 days. Then it explodes. Most people quit at Day 20.

### Mistake 1: "Massive effort produces massive results"
The all-or-nothing approach: crash diet, gym for 3 hours/day, 90-day startup sprint. These fizzle because they're unsustainable. Hardy: "Microwave mentality." The hare doesn't win. The tortoise does.

### Mistake 2: "It's the big things that derail us"
"Elephants don't bite. It's the mosquitoes." Not the one huge mistake. It's the accumulated effect of a hundred tiny bad choices — the soda here, the skipped workout there, the extra 15 minutes of TV. The elephants look scary, but the mosquitoes are what bring you down.

### Mistake 3: "This one won't matter" (the rounding error)
"Stray off course by just two millimeters, and your trajectory changes." A single extra muffin seems harmless. A single skipped workout. A single late night. Each one individually is meaningless. Compounded over months — devastating.

### Mistake 4: "I'll start tomorrow"
The failure is not in the gap between intention and action — it's in letting that gap repeat. "The difference between Scott and Brad was 125 calories per day. That's nothing. But EVERY day? That's everything."

### Mistake 5: "Success means I can relax"
The restaurant story: impeccable → successful → complacent → bankrupt in 18 months. Hardy: "Nothing fails like success." The moment you take your foot off the gas is the moment you start coasting downhill.

### Mistake 6: "Information is the solution"
"You already know all that you need to succeed. You don't need to learn anything more." Information is cheap. Execution is expensive. Reading this book changes nothing. Applying one principle changes everything.

### Mistake 7: "I'm not disciplined enough"
Hardy: "Discipline comes from within. You make a decision to be disciplined." His father didn't give him a choice — but that was external discipline. Real, lasting discipline comes from an internal decision. You can make it right now.
