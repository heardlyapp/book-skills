## 6 Anti-Patterns of Disease Denial

### 1. "It Won't Happen Here"

The Reston Ebola was 10 miles from Washington D.C. Hot zones are everywhere. The belief that "it won't happen here" is the most dangerous assumption.

### 2. "The Scientists Will Save Us"

Science is fragile. Level 4 teams are small. Funding is limited. The scientists who face hot agents are overworked and under-resourced.

> **Case: USAMRIID's Budget** (Part III): Preston notes that the Army's infectious disease research facility was underfunded and understaffed. The scientists were working on a shoestring budget. If Ebola had been airborne, the team would not have had the resources to contain it.
> **Key takeaway**: The belief that "someone is handling it" is a fantasy. The people handling it are doing their best with what they have — and it is not enough.

### 3. "Ebola Is Too Rare to Worry About"

Ebola has caused outbreaks every few years since its discovery. Each outbreak is a reminder that the virus is still out there, still evolving, and still capable of producing a strain that could spread more easily.

### 4. "Modern Medicine Can Handle It"

There is no cure for Ebola. Treatment is supportive — fluids, electrolytes, comfort. The survival rate for Ebola Zaire is 10%. Modern medicine is almost useless against it.

### 5. "The Virus Will Burn Itself Out"

Ebola does burn itself out — but not before it has killed. The question is how many people die before it does. In 2014, the West Africa outbreak killed 11,000 before it was contained.

### 6. "Preston Exaggerated for Drama"

Critics say Preston sensationalized. But the events he described were real: Monet's death, the Reston facility, the monkey shipments, the suit puncture, Kitum Cave. The drama was not invented — it was reported.

> **Case: The Space Needle Comparison** (Part II): Preston asks readers to imagine Ebola entering a densely populated area by comparing it to the Space Needle in Seattle being filled with the virus and opened at street level. This image — deliberately provocative — is meant to convey: we are unprepared for what could emerge.
> **Key takeaway**: The most frightening anti-pattern is not denial of the past but refusal to imagine the future. The Space Needle image is deliberately shocking because the reality requires shock.
