## Practical Techniques

### 1. The Level 4 Mindset

When dealing with any high-risk situation, adopt a Level 4 mindset: assume the worst, plan every step, have a decontamination protocol, and never work alone.

### 2. The Contact Tracing Principle

When a pathogen appears, you must trace every contact back to the source. The Reston team traced every monkey, every shipment, every human contact. This is epidemiology at its most basic and most powerful.

> **Case: The Travis Air Force Base** (Part III): When USAMRIID discovered that infected monkeys had been shipped to other facilities, they traced every location, every shipment, every person who had handled the animals. Containment required knowing the full network of exposure.
> **Key takeaway**: In an outbreak, the map is everything. Every connection must be traced. The missing link is where the outbreak escapes.

### 3. The Fear Management Protocol

Preston describes how the Reston team managed fear: acknowledge it, train for the worst, follow the protocol, and do not panic. Panic is the enemy of survival.

### 4. The Hot Zone Awareness

Living in an interconnected world means being aware that hot zones are not distant. They are one flight away. A virus in a cave in Africa can be in New York in 12 hours.

> **Case: The Suit Protocol** (Part III): Every Level 4 entry followed a strict protocol: 20 minutes to suit up with a partner checking every seal. Each person had a "buddy" who watched them. The buddy system was not optional — it was survival.
> **Key takeaway**: The buddy system — having someone who watches your back — is the most important safety protocol. You cannot see your own mistakes.
