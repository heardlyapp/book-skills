# Core Framework — The Hundred-Year Marathon

## Introduction: Wishful Thinking

Michael Pillsbury — a China expert who worked in the U.S. government for every administration from Nixon to Obama — argues that America has systematically misread China. "Every one of the assumptions behind the policy of 'constructive engagement' was wrong — dangerously so."

**The Cai Guo Qiang incident (November 2012):** A Chinese artist blew up a Christmas tree on the National Mall, funded by the State Department. The message was lost on the American audience. The Chinese saw it as a "subversive gesture" — a symbol of Western decline.

**The thirty-six stratagems:** Pillsbury argues Chinese strategy prizes deception. "Hide in plain sight." "Cross the sea in full view." The Chinese have used these stratagems to lull the West while advancing their long-term plan.

**Pillsbury's conversion:** He was a "panda hugger" for decades — advocating engagement. Then in the late 1990s he began translating classified Chinese documents and realized he had been deceived. "I started connecting the pieces of an alternative narrative."

## Chapter 1: The China Dream

Xi Jinping's "China Dream" is not a vague aspiration — it is a concrete plan to restore China to its "rightful place" as the world's dominant power.

**The Monument to the People's Heroes:** A ten-story obelisk in Tiananmen Square. Most Westerners walk past it oblivious. It depicts the "century of humiliation" (1839-1949) and the triumph of Mao. The message: China was once great, was humiliated by the West, and must rise again.

**The 2049 goal:** The 100th anniversary of the Communist Revolution. China aims to surpass the United States economically, militarily, and politically. This is the Hundred-Year Marathon.

## Chapter 2: Warring States

China's strategic thinking is rooted in the Warring States period (475-221 BC) and Sun Tzu's The Art of War. "The Chinese do not view strategy the same way Americans do." Americans favor direct action. Chinese favor indirection, ambiguity, and deception.

**Nathan Leites' study:** A 1940s U.S. government study concluded that Chinese strategy prizes deception "more than many military doctrines." The study was never published — considered too politically incorrect.

## Chapter 3: Only China Could Go to Nixon

The 1971 rapprochement was not simply a response to the Sino-Soviet split — it was a calculated move by Mao to gain technology and intelligence from the West while playing the "Soviet card."

## Chapter 5: America, the Great Satan

The Chinese regime considers America its primary adversary. The _ying pai_ (hawks) have advocated for the U.S. as the main obstacle to China's rise. "America is the great Satan" is a common refrain in internal Chinese debates.

## Chapter 7: The Assassin's Mace

_Sha shou jian_ — "assassin's mace" — is a Chinese military concept referring to a secret weapon or tactic that can defeat a more powerful opponent. "The assassin's mace is a surprise that can turn the tables." The U.S. has focused on China's conventional military buildup while missing the asymmetric capabilities.

## Chapter 8: The Capitalist Charade

China has not become a free market democracy. It has created "authoritarian capitalism" — using capitalist tools while maintaining absolute Party control. "China has certainly changed in the past thirty years, but its political system has not evolved in the ways that we advocates of engagement had hoped."

## Chapter 9: A China World Order in 2049

The ultimate goal: replace the U.S.-dominated Bretton Woods system with a China-led order. This includes:
- The Belt and Road Initiative (expanding Chinese influence globally)
- Replacing the U.S. dollar as the world's reserve currency
- Establishing Chinese military bases worldwide
- Taiwan: "reunification with the motherland"

## Chapter 11: America as a Warring State

Pillsbury's prescription: The U.S. must recognize that it is in a long-term strategic competition with China. "America cannot afford to continue the wishful thinking that has governed its China policy for forty years." He advocates for:
- Ending technology transfer to China
- Strengthening alliances in Asia
- Countering Chinese influence operations
- Avoiding the trap of China's "deception strategy"
