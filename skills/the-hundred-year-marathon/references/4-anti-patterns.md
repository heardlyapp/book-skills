# Anti-Patterns — The Hundred-Year Marathon

## The Central Error: Believing China Wants to Be Like America

"In our hubris, Americans love to believe that the aspiration of every other country is to be just like the United States." This assumption — that China will inevitably become a democracy and free market — has governed U.S. policy for four decades. It has been wrong every time.

### Mistake 1: "Engagement will make China a democracy"
"China has certainly changed in the past thirty years, but its political system has not evolved in the ways that we advocates of engagement had hoped." Village-level "elections" are tightly controlled. Candidates cannot criticize the Party. "Authoritarian capitalism" is the reality.

### Mistake 2: "China is fragile and about to collapse"
A RAND study listed ten factors that would cause China's collapse. That was in the 1990s. "While we worried about China's woes, its economy more than doubled." The "coming collapse" narrative has been used by Beijing itself to extract concessions — "we're fragile, give us more aid."

### Mistake 3: "Chinese hawks are fringe"
Dismissed as "nationalist crazies" by Western experts, the ying pai have been winning. "They represent the real voice of China." The Global Times (China's hawkish state paper) has become the second or third most popular news source.

### Mistake 4: "Chinese leaders mean what they say in English"
Xi's "China Dream" sounds inclusive in translation. In Mandarin, it carries explicit anti-Western, nationalist connotations. "The Chinese government had long portrayed itself as a backward nation in need of assistance for its 'peaceful rise.'"

### Mistake 5: "China's technology was developed independently"
"Much of what China has, it has stolen." Pillsbury documents systematic theft of U.S. military and commercial technology, facilitated by academic exchanges and joint ventures.

### Mistake 6: "The Two-State Solution is the only option" (China-Taiwan)
The Chinese government is committed to "reunification." Pillsbury: "Taiwan is the great unresolved issue. The hawks believe time is on their side."

### Mistake 7: "There is no plan"
"The first rule of the Marathon is that you do not talk about the Marathon." There is no single document labeled "The Plan." But the strategy is embedded in Chinese culture, policy, and military doctrine. "The Marathon is so well known to China's leaders that there is no need to risk exposure by writing it down."
