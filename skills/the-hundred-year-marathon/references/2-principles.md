# Key Principles — The Hundred-Year Marathon

## I. China Has a Long-Term Strategy — and It Is Not Peaceful

Pillsbury's central thesis: "The Chinese Communist Party has had a long-term plan to replace the United States as the world's dominant power, a plan it has been executing for decades while systematically deceiving the West about its intentions." The Hundred-Year Marathon (1949-2049) is not a metaphor — it is an operational plan.

## II. The West Has Been Deceived Through Strategic Ambiguity

The Chinese language is inherently ambiguous. Chinese leaders use vague phrasing that can be interpreted differently by Western and Chinese audiences. "Many of us who study China have been taught to view the country as a helpless victim of Western imperialists — a notion that China's leaders not only believe, but also actively encourage."

## III. The Ying Pai Are the Real Voice of China

Pillsbury argues the Chinese hawks, dismissed as fringe by Western experts, represent the mainstream. "The hard-line views are not fringe, but are very much in the mainstream of Chinese geostrategic thought." They have been winning the internal debate since Xi Jinping took power.

## IV. China Uses Deception as a Strategic Tool

The Thirty-Six Stratagems are not ancient history — they are operational doctrine. "'Hide in plain sight' is one of the stratagems. It is designed to defeat a more powerful opponent by using the opponent's own strength against him, without his knowing he is even in a contest."

## V. The "Assassin's Mace" Is Real

_Sha shou jian_ — a secret weapon that can defeat a more powerful opponent. "When I asked Chinese generals about the assassin's mace, they smiled. They knew what I was talking about, but they weren't telling me." This could be cyber, space, hypersonic weapons, or something else entirely.

## VI. America Created Its Own Rival

Through technology transfer, economic investment, and diplomatic support, the U.S. has helped China rise. "The United States, and the West more broadly, have helped the Chinese accomplish their goals from the beginning." Pillsbury includes himself in this indictment.

## VII. The Marathon Can Still Be Stopped — But Not Through Wishful Thinking

Pillsbury's prescription: the U.S. must recognize the nature of the competition and respond accordingly. "America cannot afford to continue the wishful thinking that has governed its China policy for forty years." But the window is closing.
