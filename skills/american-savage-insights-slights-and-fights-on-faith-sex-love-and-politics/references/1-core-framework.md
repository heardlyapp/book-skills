# Core Framework: Dan Savage and the Savage Love Ethos

> Source: American Savage, Chapters 1-4

## Who Is Dan Savage?

America's most famous sex advice columnist. He writes "Savage Love" for The Stranger, syndicated to millions worldwide. Gay rights activist, author, father, and co-founder of the It Gets Better Project — an online video campaign to prevent LGBTQ youth suicide that has garnered thousands of videos from around the world.

> **Case: The Preacher's Kid** (Chapter 1): Savage grew up Catholic with a father who was a permanent deacon. He attended Catholic school, was an altar boy, and enrolled in a high school for boys thinking about the priesthood. Then he realized he was gay. His response: "The Church must be wrong about me — and about a lot of other things." This conviction led him to question everything and build his own ethical framework.

> **Key takeaway**: Savage's journey from altar boy to sex columnist shows the power of trusting your own experience over received authority.

## The GGG Standard

Good, Giving, and Game — be good in bed, give equal time and pleasure, and be game for new experiences within reason.

> **Case: The GGG Couple** (Chapter 4): A couple where one partner wants to explore kink. Savage applies GGG as a two-way standard: the reluctant partner should be game (try it), but the enthusiastic partner should be giving (make it safe, make it mutual).

> **Key takeaway**: GGG is a mutual standard, not a license for one person to get their way.

## The Savage Love Philosophy

Honesty over structure. Consent over rules. Enthusiasm over obligation. The relationship structure (monogamy, open, poly) matters less than the honesty within it.
