# Techniques: Activism, Family, and the It Gets Better Project

> Source: American Savage, Chapters 6, 9-11, 16

## My Son Comes Out (as Straight)

After growing up with two gay dads, D.J. announced he's straight. Savage's reaction: unconditional love and humor.

> **Case: The Straight Coming Out** (Chapter 6): D.J. nervously sat his dads down — they worried he might have bad news. Instead, he confessed he's "different" — he's straight. The moment was hilarious and profoundly touching. Savage realized: whether your child is gay or straight, the job is the same: love them.

> **Key takeaway**: The coming out experience is universal. Everyone deserves to be who they are.

## The Straight Pride Parade

Savage creates a satirical Straight Pride Parade to illustrate privilege.

> **Case: The Parody Parade** (Chapter 9): Savage organizes an "Annual Straight Pride Parade" in Seattle. Straight attendees arrive confused. Gay attendees laugh knowingly. The point: when you've never been marginalized, you don't understand why pride matters.

## The Closet Cases

Savage has zero tolerance for closeted anti-gay politicians.

> **Case: The Hypocritical Senator** (Chapter 10): Savage profiles politicians who vote against gay rights while secretly being gay themselves. His fury is unmatched: "You don't get to come out on your deathbed and expect forgiveness."

> **Case: It Gets Better Launch** (Chapter 16): After youth suicides, Savage and Terry make a YouTube video in their kitchen. It went viral, sparking thousands of videos from around the world.
