## The Navarro Framework: Reading from the Ground Up

Navarro's system is built on one foundation: the limbic system is honest. Unlike the neocortex (thinking brain) which can lie, the limbic system (survival brain) always tells the truth. It controls freeze, flight, and fight responses, and all pacifying behaviors.

**The Bottom-Up Reading Protocol:**
1. Start with the feet — they are farthest from the conscious brain and hardest to control
2. Move up to legs, torso, arms, hands
3. End with the face — it is the most controlled and therefore the least reliable

> **Case: The Ice Pick Killer** (Foreword): An FBI agent (Navarro's colleague) interviewed a suspect in a murder. The suspect was not the primary target. When the agent listed possible murder weapons — "gun, knife, ice pick, hammer" — the suspect's eyelids came down hard and stayed down on "ice pick" before returning to normal. Only the killer knew which weapon was used. The behavior was unconscious, limbic, and undeniable. The suspect confessed.
> **Key takeaway**: The limbic response (eye blocking) revealed knowledge the suspect was consciously trying to hide. The body knew the truth before the mind could censor it.

> **Case: The Happy Feet Interview** (Chapter 3): Navarro interviewed a suspect who was later cleared. During the interview, the suspect's feet were bouncing — a "happy feet" behavior. Navarro knew the suspect was not the perpetrator because happy feet indicate comfort and positive emotion, not stress. If the suspect had been lying, his feet would have been still (freeze) or oriented toward the exit (flight).
> **Key takeaway**: Happy feet are a reliable indicator of comfort. When a person is at ease, their feet move freely. When they are stressed, their feet become still or orient toward escape.
