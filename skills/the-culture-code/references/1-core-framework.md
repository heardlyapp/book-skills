# Core Framework — The Culture Code

## Introduction: When Two Plus Two Equals Ten

The spaghetti-marshmallow challenge: kindergartners outperformed business school students by 2.5x. Why? Kindergartners didn't waste time on status management. They stood close, grabbed materials, built together. "Their interactions were not smooth or organized... they were tapping into a powerful method in which a group of ordinary people can create a performance far beyond the sum of their parts." Case: The business students got into "status management" — figuring out hierarchies — while the kindergartners just built.

## Skill 1: Build Safety (Chapters 1-6)

Signals of connection create psychological safety. Belonging cues: proximity, eye contact, energy, mimicry, turn-taking, attention. "Belonging is a feeling that you are part of a group." Case: The "good apples" experiment — a single supportive person empowered a group to resist the conformity effect.

## Skill 2: Share Vulnerability (Chapters 7-12)

"Cooperation doesn't happen by magic. It happens by exchanges of vulnerability." Leaders who signal vulnerability build trust. "Tell me what you want, and I'll help you." Case: The vulnerability loop — one person takes a risk, others reciprocate, trust builds.

## Skill 3: Establish Purpose (Chapters 13-17)

High-purpose cultures have clear narratives that connect daily work to a larger mission. "Three hundred and eleven words" — the length of the US Navy SEALs creed. Case: The hooligans and the surgeons — comparing two high-stakes groups: one chaotic, one precise. The difference was purpose.
