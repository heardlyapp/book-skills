# Anti-Patterns — The Culture Code

## The Central Error: "Culture Happens Naturally"

"Culture is not something you are. It's something you do." Many leaders assume culture emerges on its own. It doesn't — it must be built intentionally. Case: The business school students had all the individual talent but no intentional culture-building.

### Mistake 1: Overvaluing Individual Talent
"Let's hire the smartest people." Individual smarts don't guarantee group performance. Case: Kindergartners beat business school students and lawyers and CEOs.

### Mistake 2: Confusing Nice with Safe
"Everyone is polite, so culture is fine." Politeness can mask status management and fear. Psychological safety requires honest conflict. Case: Pixar's Braintrust is famously harsh — but safe because everyone shares the mission.

### Mistake 3: Leading with Invulnerability
"Leaders must project confidence at all times." Invulnerable leaders prevent vulnerability loops. Case: Leaders who signal "I don't know" and "I need help" build more trust, not less.

### Mistake 4: One-Time Purpose Declarations
"We launched our mission statement at the all-hands." Purpose must be reinforced constantly. Case: The SEALs recite 311 words regularly — not once.

### Mistake 5: Ignoring Micro-Interactions
"Culture is about the big things." Culture is built in micro-interactions: a shared laugh, a nod, a reaching hand. Case: The kindergartners' tiny signals of connection.

### Mistake 6: Outsourcing Culture to HR
"We'll hire a culture consultant." Culture can't be delegated. It's built by leaders in daily interactions. Case: "Culture is a set of living relationships working toward a shared goal."
