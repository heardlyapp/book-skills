# Key Principles — The Culture Code

## I. Individual Skill Is Overrated

"Individual skills are not what matters. What matters is the interaction." Smart people can produce poor group performance. Case: Business school students (smart) lost to kindergartners (unsophisticated) because of poor interaction.

## II. Status Management Kills Performance

"The business school students appear to be collaborating, but they are engaged in status management." Time spent on hierarchy is time not spent on the task. Case: "Instead of focusing on the task, they spent their time navigating uncertainty about one another."

## III. Safety Must Come First

"Belonging cues build psychological safety." Without safety, vulnerability and purpose can't take root. Case: The "good apples" experiment where one supportive person transformed group dynamics.

## IV. Vulnerability Is Contagious

"Cooperation happens by exchanges of vulnerability." When leaders show vulnerability, others follow. Case: The vulnerability loop — risk, reciprocity, trust.

## V. Purpose Needs Narrative

"High-purpose cultures use clear narratives to connect daily work to a larger mission." Not mission statements — stories. Case: The Navy SEALs creed (311 words).

## VI. Small Signals Matter

"A reaching hand, a nod, a shared laugh — these small signals build culture." Culture is built in micro-interactions. Case: The kindergartners standing close and grabbing materials together.

## VII. Culture Is Something You Do, Not Something You Have

"Culture is not something you are. It's something you do." It's built through daily habits, not declared in mission statements. Case: "Strong culture increases net income 756 percent over eleven years" — but only if practiced daily.
