# Techniques — The Culture Code

## How to Build Strong Group Culture

### Technique 1: The Belonging Cue
Signal safety through: eye contact, physical proximity, turn-taking, attentive listening, energy. "Belonging cues create psychological safety." Case: The kindergartners stood shoulder to shoulder — physical closeness created belonging.

### Technique 2: The Vulnerability Loop
One person takes a risk by showing vulnerability. Another reciprocates. Trust builds. Repeat. "Vulnerability is not about weakness — it's about sending a signal of trust." Case: The "Tell me what you want, and I'll help you" exercise.

### Technique 3: The Over-Communication of Purpose
Repeat the group's purpose in multiple ways: stories, rituals, symbols, catchphrases. "High-purpose cultures don't assume people know the mission." Case: The SEALs creed (311 words recited regularly) — purpose embedded through repetition.

### Technique 4: The Candor Breakthrough
Create moments where honest feedback is expected. "Create high-candor, low-safety moments." Case: Pixar's Braintrust — honest critique of films without personal offense.

### Technique 5: The Handoff Signal
In high-functioning teams, transitions are signaled clearly. "A simple phrase or gesture that says 'I've got your back.'" Case: Navy SEALs' "paddle signals" — constant communication even in silence.

### Technique 6: The Flash Habit
Short, frequent bursts of connection. "Flash habits are small, repeatable interactions that build culture fast." Case: Daily stand-up meetings, team huddles, pre-game rituals.

### Technique 7: The Purpose Check
Regularly ask: "How does this connect to our larger mission?" Case: The hooligans vs. the surgeons — purpose transforms chaotic energy into coordinated action.
