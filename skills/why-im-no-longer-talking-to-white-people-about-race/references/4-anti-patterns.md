# Anti-Patterns — Why I'm No Longer Talking to White People About Race

## The Central Error: "I Don't See Color"

Colorblindness is not a solution — it's a form of denial. "When white people claim they don't see race, they are asserting that they don't see the life experiences of people of color." To refuse to see race is to refuse to see racism.

### Mistake 1: "Racism ended with slavery"
"The Abolition of Slavery Act was introduced in 1833, less than two hundred years ago. Less than two hundred years later, that damage is still to be undone." Racism didn't end — it evolved.

### Mistake 2: "All lives matter"
"Black Lives Matter is a specific response to a specific problem. Saying 'All Lives Matter' in response is like saying 'all roofs matter' when someone's house is on fire."

### Mistake 3: "Class, not race"
"The left has often been guilty of subsuming race under class." Eddo-Lodge argues this is a way to dismiss the specific experiences of people of color. She illustrates with the 2015 Labour leadership election: Jeremy Corbyn's campaign focused on class while ignoring the race dimension, leaving many black voters feeling the party had abandoned them. "When the left says 'class not race,' they're asking non-white people to abandon their identities."

### Mistake 4: "I'm not racist, so racism isn't my problem"
Structural racism doesn't require individual racists. Eddo-Lodge uses the Stephen Lawrence case — where the Macpherson Report found the Metropolitan Police "institutionally racist" — to show that a system can be racist without every individual officer being a conscious racist. "I don't need to call anyone a racist to talk about racism." The system operates regardless of individual good intentions.

### Mistake 5: "White people can experience racism too"
Prejudice can happen to anyone. Racism requires systemic power. "A white person can experience prejudice. They cannot experience racism in a system designed to benefit them at the expense of others."

### Mistake 6: "Why can't we just focus on the economy?"
Ignoring racism doesn't make it go away. "If you're not actively fighting racism, you're not fighting for equality."

### Mistake 7: "I feel attacked when you talk about race"
"The emotional disconnect" — feeling attacked when race is discussed is itself a symptom of privilege. "They interpret it as an affront because they've never had to think about what it means, in power terms, to be white."
