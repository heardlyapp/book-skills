# Key Principles — Why I'm No Longer Talking to White People About Race

## I. Racism Is Discrimination Plus Power

Eddo-Lodge: "Thinking about power made me realise that racism was about so much more than personal prejudice. It was about being in the position to negatively affect other people's life chances." Prejudice can exist in anyone. Racism requires the power to institutionalize that prejudice.

## II. History Has Been Erased — and That's Intentional

Black British history is not taught in schools. The history of slavery, colonialism, the Windrush generation, Dr. Harold Moody, the 1919 race riots — all erased. "I had been denied a context, an ability to understand myself." The erasure is not accidental.

## III. White Privilege Is Invisibility

"To be white is to be human; to be white is universal. I only know this because I am not." White privilege is not about having an easy life — it's about not being disadvantaged by your race. "When white people pick up a magazine, it is never rare or odd to see people who look like them in positions of power."

## IV. The Emotional Disconnect Is Real

Eddo-Lodge's central observation: white people cannot hear conversations about race without becoming defensive. "You can see their eyes shut down and harden. It's like treacle is poured into their ears." This is not malice — it's the result of never having to think about race.

## V. Intersectionality Is Essential

A black woman is not a white woman plus blackness. She experiences the world differently. Kimberlé Crenshaw's concept of intersectionality: race, gender, class, sexuality all interact. "A black woman is not just a woman plus blackness — she experiences the world differently from both white women and black men."

## VI. The Left Has Failed on Race

"Class not race" is not a progressive position — it's a way to dismiss racism. "When the left says 'class not race,' they're asking non-white people to abandon their identities in favor of a common class identity that has already excluded them."

## VII. Self-Preservation Is Not Giving Up

Eddo-Lodge's blog post was about setting a boundary, not surrender. "I don't think giving up is a sign of weakness. Sometimes it's about self-preservation." But she turned the post into a book — paradoxically — to continue the conversation. Silence is a tactic, not defeat.
