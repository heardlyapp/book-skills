# Anti-Patterns

## 1. Confusing Position with Power

**The mistake**: Assuming the person with the title has the real power.

**Fix**: Yeroen was alpha in name but lost it. Nikkie became alpha in name but Yeroen had real power. Title and influence are different.

> **Case: Yeroen Behind the Throne** (Chapters 2-3): After losing the alpha position, Yeroen became more influential by being the coalition-maker. The new alpha Nikkie had the title, but Yeroen could make or break his reign.
> **Key takeaway**: The most powerful person in an organization is often not the one with the title. Look for the person others must keep happy.

## 2. Ignoring Female Power Dynamics

**The mistake**: Overlooking women's influence because their power operates differently.

**Fix**: Female chimps build long-term coalitions that are more stable than male alliances.

## 3. Avoiding Conflict Entirely

**The mistake**: Believing healthy groups are conflict-free.

**Fix**: Conflict is natural. The key is repair after conflict, not avoiding it.

> **Case: The Unstable Colony** (Chapter 5): When the Arnhem colony lost its mediating alpha, conflict increased and reconciliation decreased. The group became unstable. Individual chimps showed stress. Without repair, conflict destroys cohesion.
> **Key takeaway**: Don't eliminate conflict — build a culture of repair. The ability to reconcile determines long-term stability.

## 4. Overvaluing Individual Strength

**The mistake**: Hiring the "strongest" individual without considering social skills.

**Fix**: The strongest chimp doesn't become alpha. The best coalition-builder does.

## 5. Ignoring the Social System

**The mistake**: Analyzing individuals in isolation from their context.

**Fix**: Behavior only makes sense in the context of relationships and group dynamics.
