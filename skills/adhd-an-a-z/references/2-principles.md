# 2 — Diagnosis, Medication, and Treatment

> Source: "ADHD an A-Z" by Leanne Maskell

## The Path to Diagnosis

For many adults, getting an ADHD diagnosis is life-changing. It explains years of struggle and opens the door to effective treatment.

> **Case: D is for Diagnosis** (Chapter D): "I was diagnosed with ADHD in October 2021 at the age of 24. Before that, I thought I was just lazy, disorganized, and failing at life. The diagnosis changed everything — not because I was suddenly fixed, but because I finally understood why I was struggling." Maskell shares her own diagnosis story to validate the reader's experience.
> **Key takeaway**: Diagnosis is not about getting a label. It's about getting an explanation. For many adults, especially those diagnosed late, it is a moment of profound relief — all those years of thinking you were broken finally make sense. It is the beginning, not the end, of the journey.

> **Case: M is for Medication** (Chapter M): "Medication is not a silver bullet, but it is a powerful tool. Stimulants like methylphenidate and dexamfetamine increase dopamine levels and can dramatically improve focus and impulse control. Non-stimulants are another option. Finding the right medication and dose is a process — it takes time and trial." Maskell presents medication as one tool among many, not the whole solution.
> **Key takeaway**: Medication helps many but not all people with ADHD. The right medication and dose is highly individual. And even with medication, strategies, therapy, and lifestyle changes are essential. Medication enables change — it doesn't create it.
