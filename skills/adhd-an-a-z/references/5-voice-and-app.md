# 5 — Maskell's Voice + 5 Application Scenarios

> Source: "ADHD an A-Z" by Leanne Maskell

## Maskell's Voice

Leanne Maskell is an ADHD coach and founder of the ADHD Adults community. Her voice: warm, validating, practical. She writes from lived experience — diagnosed at 24 after years of struggling — combined with professional coaching expertise.

> **Case: The Author's Journey** (Introduction): "I wrote this to provide an overview of my personal experiences in navigating the wonderful world of being an ADHD adult. I used to think that being smart meant being complicated. I thought I had to struggle to be taken seriously. I was wrong." Maskell establishes herself as a fellow traveler, not an authority figure.
> **Key takeaway**: The book's power comes from Maskell's dual perspective: she is both an ADHD coach who has studied the science and a person with ADHD who has lived the struggle. This combination creates a book that is both informative and deeply validating.

> **Case: The Community** (Chapter U — Unite): "I started an ADHD community because I needed to connect with people who understood. The response was overwhelming. Thousands of people, all feeling the same things, all thinking they were alone. Finding your people — other ADHDers who get it — is one of the most powerful interventions there is." Maskell emphasizes the importance of community.
> **Key takeaway**: ADHD is isolating. The shame and secrecy make it worse. Finding community — online or in person — is not optional. It is essential. Other ADHDers will give you strategies, validation, and hope in a way that no professional can replicate.

## Key Quote

"You are not lazy. You are not broken. Your brain works differently — and that's okay."

## 5 Application Scenarios

1. **Just diagnosed and overwhelmed** → Read A (Acceptance) and D (Diagnosis). Give yourself time to process. Find a community.
2. **Struggling with executive function** → Read E (Executive Functioning) and O (Organisation). Pick one micro-strategy to try.
3. **Burning out at work** → Read B (Burnout) and J (Jobs). Rest first, then rethink your work environment and accommodations.
4. **Emotionally dysregulated** → Read R (Rejection) and V (Vices). Name the RSD, learn to pause before reacting. Be kind to yourself.
5. **Feeling alone in your ADHD journey** → Read U (Unite). Find a community. You are not the only one. You are not too much.
