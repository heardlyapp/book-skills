# 1 — January 6th and Its Aftermath

> Source: "Oath and Honor" by Liz Cheney

## The Day

January 6, 2021. Cheney was in the House chamber when the attack began. She describes the fear, the chaos, and the moment she realized this was not a protest — it was an insurrection.

## The Aftermath

Cheney was one of 10 Republicans who voted to impeach Trump. She knew it would cost her. It did. She lost her leadership position, was censured by her party, and eventually lost her primary.

## The Committee

The select committee was created in July 2021. Cheney was appointed by Nancy Pelosi — a Democrat. She served alongside Adam Schiff, Adam Kinzinger (the only other Republican), and others.

> **Case: The Basement Bunker** (Chapter 3): During the attack, Cheney was evacuated to an undisclosed location with other members. She describes the surreal experience of being in a secure bunker while the Capitol was under siege. "I never imagined I would experience something like this in America."
> **Key takeaway**: The attack was not a protest that got out of hand. It was a planned assault on the seat of American democracy. Being there changed everything.

> **Case: The Impeachment Vote** (Chapter 5): Cheney knew voting to impeach would end her political career. She did it anyway. "I voted my conscience. I voted my oath. I knew it was the right thing to do." The cost was immediate and severe.
> **Key takeaway**: Doing the right thing is not always rewarded. Sometimes it costs everything. But the alternative — silence — costs more.
