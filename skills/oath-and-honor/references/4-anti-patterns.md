# 4 — Threats to Democracy

> Source: "Oath and Honor" by Liz Cheney

## Anti-Pattern 1: The Big Lie

**The mistake:** Repeating and amplifying false claims about the 2020 election, even (especially) when you know they're false.

## Anti-Pattern 2: Institutional Erosion

**The mistake:** Attacking the institutions that protect democracy — courts, elections, the press — when they produce results you don't like.

## Anti-Pattern 3: Normalization of Violence

**The mistake:** Minimizing the January 6th attack as "legitimate political discourse" or "a protest that got out of hand."

> **Case: The "Tour" Lie** (Chapter on Denial): Some Republican members initially claimed the January 6th attackers were just "tourists" who wandered into the Capitol. Cheney: "They were not tourists. They were insurrectionists. Words matter. Calling them tourists is a lie."
> **Key takeaway**: The first step in authoritarianism is language. When you rename violence as tourism, you enable the next attack.

> **Case: The Pressure Campaign** (Chapter on Threats): Cheney describes the pressure campaign from party leadership to downplay the attack. Members were told to stay quiet, change the subject, blame Antifa. "They knew it wasn't true. They said it anyway."
> **Key takeaway**: Disinformation is not a mistake — it's a strategy. Recognizing it as a strategy is the first step to countering it.
