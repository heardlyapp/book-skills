# 3 — The Investigation

> Source: "Oath and Honor" by Liz Cheney

## The Bipartisan Approach

The January 6th Committee was notable for its bipartisanship. Cheney and Democrat Adam Schiff worked side by side. They didn't agree on policy. They agreed on the Constitution.

## The Evidence

The committee collected thousands of documents, interviewed hundreds of witnesses, and held multiple public hearings. The evidence was overwhelming.

> **Case: Cassie Hutchinson's Testimony** (Chapter on Hearings): The former White House aide testified that Trump knew the crowd was armed and wanted security checkpoints removed. Her testimony was a turning point. Cheney describes the moment in the hearing room — silence, shock, the weight of the evidence.
> **Key takeaway**: The truth comes out when people have the courage to tell it. Hutchinson was 26 years old. She had more courage than most of her former bosses.

> **Case: The 8-Hour Gap** (Chapter on Evidence): The committee discovered an 8-hour gap in White House phone logs from January 6th. Cheney: "We still don't know what happened in those 8 hours. I suspect we never will." The gap was more damning than any single piece of evidence.
> **Key takeaway**: Sometimes what's missing is more important than what's present. The absence of evidence is itself a form of evidence.
