# Key Principles — It Starts with the Egg

## I. Egg Quality Is the Single Most Important Factor

"Egg quality is the single most important factor in determining whether an egg will fertilize and survive to the blastocyst stage." Chromosomal abnormalities in eggs are "the single most important cause of early miscarriages and failed IVF cycles." Case: Fett's own IVF result — 22 eggs, 19 fertilized, all 19 became good-quality blastocysts after she implemented her protocol.

## II. You Can Improve Egg Quality

"Many women are told there is little they can do to improve egg quality, but the latest research defies that old assumption." The 3-4 months before ovulation is a critical window. "There is a brief window of opportunity in which you can make a difference." Case: Fett was diagnosed with diminished ovarian reserve and produced a record number of good-quality embryos after three months of supplementation and toxin avoidance.

## III. BPA and Phthalates Are Major Fertility Threats

Harvard School of Public Health (2012): women with higher BPA levels produced fewer eggs and embryos in IVF. BPA is in plastics, receipts, and canned food linings. Phthalates are in fragrances and vinyl. "You are unlikely to find out about BPA from your doctor." The fix: glass over plastic, unscented products, fresh over canned.

## IV. CoQ10 Is the Most Important Supplement

"CoQ10 levels decline with age, and this decline is directly linked to the decline in egg quality." CoQ10 powers the mitochondria that drive the energy-intensive process of egg maturation. Fett recommends ubiquinol (active form) at 200-600 mg daily. Studies show it improves embryo quality and reduces chromosomal abnormalities.

## V. Different Problems Need Different Solutions

Melatonin helps egg quality but can disrupt ovulation — use only in IVF context. Myo-inositol is for PCOS. DHEA is for diminished ovarian reserve. There is no one-size-fits-all fertility protocol. "The advice in this book is based on a comprehensive analysis of a large body of medical research."

## VI. Diet Matters — Cut Sugar, Reduce Caffeine

"Refined carbohydrates raise insulin, and elevated insulin is shown to impact egg quality." Fett's egg quality diet: Mediterranean diet with extra antioxidants, minimal sugar, healthy fats. Avoid trans fats. "The egg quality diet is fundamentally about stabilizing blood sugar and providing antioxidant protection."

## VII. Sperm Quality Is Equally Important

"Many couples focus entirely on the woman's egg quality, but sperm quality is equally important for creating a healthy embryo." Male factor contributes to about 40% of infertility. Sperm quality can be improved in 2-3 months with CoQ10, zinc, selenium, and avoiding heat. "A healthy embryo requires healthy eggs and healthy sperm."
