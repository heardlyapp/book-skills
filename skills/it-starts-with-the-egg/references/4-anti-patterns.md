# Anti-Patterns — It Starts with the Egg

## The Central Error: "There's Nothing You Can Do About Egg Quality"

"Many women are told there is little they can do to improve egg quality, but the latest research defies that old assumption." This is the single most harmful myth — it takes away agency from women who are desperate to improve their fertility.

### Mistake 1: "IVF can fix all fertility problems"
"IVF cannot overcome poor egg quality." Many couples believe that IVF bypasses all fertility issues. It doesn't. "Chromosomal abnormalities are the single most important cause of failed IVF cycles." The only way to improve IVF outcomes is to improve egg quality.

### Mistake 2: "Internet supplements are safe and helpful"
"Trawling the Internet for ideas on what to take for fertility is likely to miss nuances and cause trouble." Royal jelly and L-arginine are commonly recommended on forums but lack scientific support. Melatonin is good for IVF but can disrupt natural ovulation. "This book not only discusses measures that may help but debunks myths about supplements that may do more harm than good."

### Mistake 3: "Plastic is fine if I don't microwave it"
"BPA leaches from plastic containers even at room temperature." Especially when the container is scratched, worn, or heated by the dishwasher. "Replace plastic with glass" is the only safe approach. Even BPA-free plastics may contain BPS, which is similar.

### Mistake 4: "Natural products always work better"
"Supplements aren't regulated for safety or efficacy." The supplement market is poorly regulated. Fett recommends only supplements backed by multiple clinical studies. "Royal jelly, bee pollen, and other 'natural' products have no scientific support for improving fertility."

### Mistake 5: "Only the woman needs to prepare"
"Male factor contributes to about 40% of infertility cases." Many couples focus entirely on the woman. "Sperm quality can be improved in 2-3 months with CoQ10, zinc, selenium, and avoiding heat." A healthy embryo requires both healthy eggs and healthy sperm.

### Mistake 6: "Fertility is just about age"
"Age is a major factor, but it is not the only factor." Chromosomal abnormalities increase with age, but toxins, diet, and supplement status also significantly impact egg quality. Case: Fett was under 30 with diminished ovarian reserve — her protocol normalized her egg production. "The research revealed a quiet revolution in the way we think about egg quality."
