# Core Framework — It Starts with the Egg

## Introduction: Rebecca's Story

Fett was diagnosed with diminished ovarian reserve and told she would need aggressive IVF. "I asked our fertility specialist if there were any particular supplements I should take that could improve our chances, but there were no clear answers." Using her background in molecular biology and biochemistry, she researched the science. She improved her diet, took specific supplements (CoQ10, DHEA), eliminated plastics and toxins. Her first IVF cycle produced 22 eggs, 19 fertilized, and every single one became a good-quality blastocyst — a clinic record. "This result was simply unheard of."

## Chapter 1: Understanding Egg Quality

Eggs are in suspended animation for decades, but in the 3-4 months before ovulation, they undergo a major transformation — growing dramatically in size, producing more energy, and executing a precise chromosome separation process. "Chromosomal abnormalities are the single most important cause of early miscarriages and failed IVF cycles." The good news: egg quality can be improved. "There is a brief window of opportunity in which you can make a difference to your egg quality."

## Chapter 2: The Dangers of BPA

BPA (bisphenol A) is found in plastics, receipts, and canned food linings. Harvard School of Public Health (2012): women with higher BPA levels produced fewer eggs and embryos in IVF, and their embryos were less likely to implant. "You are unlikely to find out about BPA from your doctor." Fett's advice: replace plastic containers with glass, avoid canned foods, refuse receipts.

## Chapter 3: Phthalates and Other Toxins

Phthalates ("everywhere chemicals") are found in fragrances, vinyl flooring, food packaging. They disrupt hormone signaling. Parabens (preservatives in cosmetics) also negatively impact fertility. PCBs (industrial chemicals found in fatty fish) accumulate in the body and reduce IVF success. Fett provides a "toxin avoidance" system: use unscented products, avoid vinyl, choose fresh over packaged foods.

## Chapter 6: The Power of Coenzyme Q10

CoQ10 is the most important supplement for egg quality. It powers the mitochondria (energy producers) in egg cells. "CoQ10 levels decline with age, and this decline is directly linked to the decline in egg quality." Studies show CoQ10 supplementation improves embryo quality and reduces aneuploidy (chromosomal abnormalities). Fett recommends ubiquinol (the active form) at 200-600 mg daily.

## Chapter 7: Melatonin and Other Antioxidants

Melatonin is a powerful antioxidant that protects eggs from oxidative damage. But it can disrupt ovulation if taken long-term. "Melatonin is only helpful in the IVF context, where natural regulation of ovulation is less important." If trying to conceive naturally, use other antioxidants (vitamin C, vitamin E, NAC).

## Chapter 8: Restoring Ovulation with Myo-Inositol

For women with PCOS, myo-inositol is "one of the most well-supported supplement choices." It improves insulin sensitivity, restores ovulation, and improves egg quality. "Myo-inositol has been shown to be as effective as metformin (the standard drug treatment) for improving ovulation rates in women with PCOS."

## Chapter 9: DHEA for Diminished Ovarian Reserve

DHEA (dehydroepiandrosterone) is a controversial supplement. Studies show it increases the number of eggs retrieved and improves pregnancy rates in women with diminished ovarian reserve. "Whether you will be advised to take DHEA depends more on which IVF clinic you happen to be attending rather than any logical basis."

## Chapter 11: The Egg Quality Diet

Cut refined carbohydrates to lower insulin (which impacts egg quality). Eat antioxidants (berries, dark leafy greens). Healthy fats (avocado, olive oil, nuts). Avoid trans fats. Reduce caffeine. "The egg quality diet is essentially a Mediterranean diet with extra emphasis on antioxidants and minimal sugar."

## Chapter 12: Sperm Quality

Male factor contributes to about 40% of infertility cases. Sperm quality can be improved in 2-3 months with CoQ10, zinc, selenium, vitamin C, and avoiding heat (hot tubs, tight underwear). "Many couples focus entirely on the woman's egg quality, but sperm quality is equally important for creating a healthy embryo."
