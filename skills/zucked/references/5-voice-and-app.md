# 5 — Voice & App

> Source: *Zucked* by Roger McNamee.

## McNamee's Voice

McNamee writes as a Silicon Valley insider turned whistleblower. His tone is urgent, personal, and morally indignant — but grounded in decades of tech industry experience. He is not an outsider attacking tech. He is a true believer who lost his faith and wants to warn others. The book is structured as a conversion narrative: from early investor and advisor to concerned citizen and activist. This insider perspective gives the book its authority.

> **Case: The Rear Window Analogy (Prologue):** "I was more like Jimmy Stewart in Hitchcock's Rear Window. He is minding his own business, checking out the view from his living room, when he sees what looks like a crime in progress, and then he has to ask himself what he should do." This analogy captures McNamee's voice perfectly: an accidental witness who cannot unsee what he has seen.

> **Case: The Tylenol Advice (Prologue):** McNamee urged Facebook to follow Johnson & Johnson's response to the 1982 Tylenol poisonings — recall every bottle, accept responsibility, earn back trust. Facebook's refusal to do so is the turning point of the book. "I thought Facebook could convert a potential disaster into a victory." The contrast between the two responses — one heroic, one defensive — defines the tragedy.

## Five Scenarios

### 1. The Addicted User

You can't stop checking your phone. You feel anxious without it. McNamee's message: the addiction is not your fault. It is by design. Awareness is the first step. Delete the apps from your phone. Use the browser instead. The friction matters.

### 2. The Concerned Parent

Your child is on social media and you can see the effects. McNamee's message: you are not overreacting. The evidence is clear — Facebook's own research confirms the harm. Set boundaries, have conversations, find offline alternatives. You cannot protect your child by banning Instagram alone, but you can help them see the system.

### 3. The Voter/Citizen

You are alarmed by polarization and disinformation. McNamee's message: social media is a primary cause. The algorithmic amplification of outrage is a systemic threat to democracy. Support privacy legislation, algorithmic transparency, and antitrust enforcement. Vote for candidates who take this seriously.

### 4. The Tech Employee

You work at a social media company and feel uncomfortable. McNamee's message: your discomfort is justified. You are building products that harm people. McNamee himself was a tech insider — he understands the cognitive dissonance. Speak up. Document. Find allies. Consider alternatives.

### 5. The Regulator/Policy Maker

You are designing policy for the digital age. McNamee's message: understand the business model. Regulation that does not address the incentive structure — advertising revenue from engagement — will fail. Privacy laws (GDPR), algorithmic transparency, and antitrust are the three pillars.
