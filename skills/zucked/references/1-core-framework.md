# 1 — Core Framework

> Source: *Zucked* by Roger McNamee — Prologue, Chapters 1-14.

## The Three Frameworks

### Framework A: The Attention Economy Engine (Chapters 1, 3, 4, 7)

Facebook's business model is built on capturing and monetizing user attention. Every design decision — infinite scroll, push notifications, like buttons, autoplay video, algorithmic news feed — is optimized to maximize time spent on platform. BJ Fogg's "Captology" (computers as persuasive technologies) at Stanford provided the theoretical foundation. The result: platforms that exploit psychological vulnerabilities for profit.

> **Case: The Tylenol vs Facebook Response (Prologue):** McNamee urged Facebook to follow Johnson & Johnson's 1982 Tylenol playbook: when someone tampered with Tylenol bottles, J&J immediately recalled every bottle nationwide and redesigned the packaging — absorbing short-term costs for long-term trust. McNamee's advice to Facebook: investigate the systemic flaws, fix them, and be transparent. Facebook's response: polite denial. They treated a systemic crisis as a PR problem.

> **Case: BJ Fogg's Stanford Lab (Chapter 4):** McNamee traces Facebook's addiction design to BJ Fogg's Persuasive Technology Lab at Stanford. Fogg's formula: Behavior = Motivation + Ability + Trigger. Facebook applies this perfectly: notifications (trigger) are instantly accessible (ability) during moments of boredom or loneliness (motivation). The result: compulsive checking. Many of Facebook's early designers and product managers came through Fogg's lab.

### Framework B: The Democracy Crisis (Chapters 7, 10, 11)

Facebook's algorithmic amplification of divisive content, combined with its unparalleled targeting capabilities, made it the ideal weapon for foreign interference. The Russian Internet Research Agency (IRA) used Facebook Groups, Pages, and ads to reach tens of millions of Americans with divisive content during 2016. Cambridge Analytica harvested data from 87 million profiles for psychographic targeting.

> **Case: The Bernie Sanders Meme Farms (Prologue):** In February 2016, McNamee noticed a surge of deeply misogynistic images about Hillary Clinton being shared from Facebook Groups ostensibly supporting Bernie Sanders. The images were spreading virally with unnatural speed. McNamee realized: "Groups on Facebook do not emerge full grown overnight." Someone was spending money to grow these pages. The Russian IRA was later confirmed as the source.

### Framework C: The Mental Health Crisis (Chapters 2, 13)

Facebook's own internal research — leaked by whistleblower Frances Haugen as the "Facebook Papers" — showed that Instagram causes body image issues, anxiety, and depression in teenage girls. The company suppressed this research and did not make meaningful changes. McNamee argues this is not negligence — it is a predictable consequence of a business model optimized for engagement.

> **Case: The Facebook Papers (Chapters 13):** Internal Facebook research (2021) found that 32% of teenage girls said Instagram made them feel worse about their bodies. The company knew but did not act. McNamee: "When historians finish with this corner of history, I suspect they will cut Facebook some slack about poor choices... Where I fault Facebook is for the company's response to criticism and evidence."
