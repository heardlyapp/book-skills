# 1 — Core Framework

> Source: *Zucked* by Roger McNamee — Prologue, Chapters 1-14.

## The Three Frameworks

### Framework A: The Attention Economy Machine (Chapters 3, 4, 7)

Facebook's business model is built on capturing user attention and monetizing it through advertising. Every feature — infinite scroll, push notifications, like buttons, autoplay video, algorithmic news feed — is designed to maximize time spent on platform. The theoretical foundation comes from BJ Fogg's Persuasive Technology Lab at Stanford, where a generation of future Facebook product managers learned how to make technology behaviorally addictive.

> **Case: The Stanford Captology Pipeline (Chapter 4):** McNamee traces Facebook's addiction design directly to BJ Fogg's Stanford lab. Fogg's formula — Behavior = Motivation + Ability + Trigger — was applied systematically by Facebook's early product teams. The notification (trigger) appears during a moment of boredom or loneliness (motivation). The app opens instantly (ability). The result: compulsive checking, hundreds of times per day.

> **Case: The Tylenol vs. Facebook Response (Prologue):** In 1982, when someone tampered with Tylenol bottles in Chicago, Johnson & Johnson immediately recalled every bottle nationwide and redesigned the packaging — absorbing huge short-term costs for long-term trust. McNamee urged Facebook to do the same: investigate, fix the systemic flaws, be transparent. Facebook's response: polite denial and deflection. They treated a systemic crisis as a PR problem.

### Framework B: The Democracy Crisis (Chapters 7, 10, 11)

Facebook's algorithmic amplification of divisive content, combined with its unparalleled ad-targeting capabilities, made it the ideal vehicle for foreign interference. The Russian Internet Research Agency (IRA) used Facebook Groups, Pages, and ads to reach tens of millions of Americans with targeted disinformation during 2016. Cambridge Analytica harvested data from 87 million profiles for psychographic targeting.

> **Case: The Bernie Sanders Meme Farms (Prologue):** In February 2016, McNamee noticed a surge of deeply misogynistic images about Hillary Clinton spreading from Facebook Groups ostensibly supporting Bernie Sanders. The images spread with unnatural speed. "Groups on Facebook do not emerge full grown overnight." Someone was paying to grow these pages. The Russian IRA was later confirmed as the source. The algorithm amplified the content because it generated high engagement.

### Framework C: The Mental Health Crisis (Chapters 2, 13)

Facebook's internal research — leaked by whistleblower Frances Haugen as the "Facebook Papers" — showed that Instagram causes body image issues, anxiety, and depression in teenage girls. Internal documents revealed that 32% of teen girls said Instagram made them feel worse about their bodies. The company suppressed this research and refused to make meaningful design changes.

> **Case: The Facebook Papers Leak (Chapters 13):** Frances Haugen, a former Facebook product manager, leaked thousands of pages of internal research to the Wall Street Journal in 2021. The documents proved that Facebook knew Instagram was harming teenagers' mental health and chose not to act. McNamee: "When historians finish with this corner of history, I suspect they will cut Facebook some slack about poor choices... Where I fault Facebook is for the company's response to criticism and evidence."
