## 6 Common Anti-Patterns

### 1. Over-Analyzing — trying to control every movement through conscious thought. This is Self 1 taking over and blocking Self 2.

### 2. Judgment — "That was bad." "I'm terrible." Every judgment activates Self 1. Replace judgment with observation.

### 3. Over-Coaching — telling someone exactly what to do. This creates dependence and blocks natural learning. The best coach creates discovery.

> **Case: The Silent Lesson** (Chapter 7): Gallwey describes watching a coach who never gave technical instruction. He only asked questions: "What did you notice?" "What felt different?" His students improved faster than those who received detailed instruction.

### 4. Choking Under Pressure — trying harder when it matters most. The more you try, the more you interfere with your natural ability.

### 5. Perfectionism — the belief that mistakes are failures. Mistakes are data. Without mistakes, there is no learning.

### 6. Focusing on Outcomes — winning, the score, what others think. Outcome focus activates Self 1's fear. Process focus frees Self 2.

> **Case: The Scoreboard Watcher** (Chapter 8): A player who was winning stopped paying attention to the game and started worrying about losing his lead. His performance collapsed. The focus shifted from the ball to the score — from process to outcome. The solution: bring attention back to the seams of the ball, one point at a time.
> **Key takeaway**: The scoreboard tells you the result. It tells you nothing about how to play the next point. Focus on the process, and the outcome takes care of itself.
