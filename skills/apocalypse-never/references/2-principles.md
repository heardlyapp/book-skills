# 2 — Climate Realities

> Source: "Apocalypse Never" by Michael Shellenberger

## The Facts Without Fear

Shellenberger accepts the scientific consensus on climate change but rejects the apocalyptic framing. The difference matters: acknowledging the problem while maintaining hope and perspective.

> **Case: The IPCC Projections** (Chapter on Climate): "The IPCC's worst-case emission scenario (RCP 8.5) projects 4-5°C warming by 2100. But Shellenberger points out that this scenario is increasingly unlikely — emissions are not following the worst-case path. The more likely outcome is 2-3°C warming, which is serious but manageable." Shellenberger challenges the assumption that the worst case is the most likely.
> **Key takeaway**: Media coverage of climate change consistently uses the worst-case scenario as if it were the most likely. This creates unrealistic fear. The most likely outcomes are bad but survivable — and we have the tools to make them better.

> **Case: Adaptation Works** (Chapter on Adaptation): "The Netherlands has been below sea level for centuries. They didn't stop the sea — they adapted with dikes and pumps. The same principle applies to climate change. We can and should reduce emissions, but we should also invest in adaptation — sea walls, drought-resistant crops, better building codes." Shellenberger argues that adaptation is not surrender.
> **Key takeaway**: The choice between mitigation and adaptation is false. We need both. And adaptation is not defeat — it's what humans have always done. The Netherlands didn't give up and move to higher ground. They built defenses and thrived.
