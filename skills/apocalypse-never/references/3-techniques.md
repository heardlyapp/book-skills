# 3 — Nuclear Energy and Energy Policy

> Source: "Apocalypse Never" by Michael Shellenberger

## The Nuclear Solution

Shellenberger's most provocative argument: the environmental movement's opposition to nuclear energy has been the single greatest mistake in the history of environmentalism.

> **Case: France's Success** (Chapter on Nuclear): "France generates over 70% of its electricity from nuclear power. It has the lowest carbon emissions per capita of any major industrialized country. It did this in 15 years. France is proof that it is possible to decarbonize an entire electricity grid quickly and affordably." Shellenberger uses France as the model that the world should follow.
> **Key takeaway**: France already solved the problem that the world is struggling with. They decarbonized their grid in a decade using nuclear power. The solution exists. The obstacle is not technical but political — the environmental movement has spent 40 years fighting nuclear, and this fight has made climate change worse.

> **Case: Germany's Failure** (Chapter on Renewables): "Germany invested hundreds of billions in renewable energy. After Fukushima, they shut down their nuclear plants. The result: Germany's emissions stopped declining and actually increased. They had to burn more coal to make up for the lost nuclear power. Germany's Energiewende is a cautionary tale." Shellenberger presents Germany as the dark mirror of France.
> **Key takeaway**: The German experience is the single best argument against renewable-only energy policy. Even the world's most aggressive renewable build-out could not compensate for the loss of nuclear. The lesson: if you care about climate change, you should support nuclear energy.
