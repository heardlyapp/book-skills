# 4 — Environmental Misconceptions

> Source: "Apocalypse Never" by Michael Shellenberger

## Anti-Pattern: Doomism

The most pervasive anti-pattern: the belief that everything is getting worse and there is nothing we can do. Shellenberger calls this "doomism" — and argues it is as dangerous as denial.

> **Case: The Doomism Trap** (Chapter on Progress): "A 2019 survey found that most people in developed countries believe the world is getting worse. In fact, on almost every measurable dimension — poverty, life expectancy, education, pollution — the world is improving. The disconnect between perception and reality is driven by media coverage that emphasizes crisis." Shellenberger documents the gap between perception and reality.
> **Key takeaway**: Doomism is not just inaccurate — it's dangerous. If people believe nothing is getting better, they lose the motivation to act. The environmental movement has inadvertently created a narrative of hopelessness that undermines the very action it seeks to inspire.

## Anti-Pattern: Conservation Colonialism

Shellenberger criticizes environmental organizations that prioritize wildlife over human welfare in developing countries.

> **Case: The Anti-Human Environmentalism** (Chapter on Conservation): "Some environmental organizations oppose economic development in Africa and Asia, arguing that it will increase emissions or harm wildlife. But the single best thing for the environment in developing countries is economic growth — which reduces poverty, enables environmental regulation, and slows deforestation." Shellenberger critiques a strain of environmentalism that is anti-human.
> **Key takeaway**: The poorest people cause the least environmental damage but suffer the most from it. Economic development is not the enemy of the environment — it is the precondition for environmental protection. Rich countries telling poor countries not to develop is environmental colonialism.
