# 1 — Yogananda's Life Story

> Source: "Autobiography of a Yogi" by Paramahansa Yogananda

## The Journey from India to America

Yogananda was born in 1893 in Gorakhpur, India. From childhood, he was drawn to the spiritual life. His search for his guru and his mission to bring yoga to the West define the arc of his story.

> **Case: The Search for the Guru** (Chapter 1 — Early Life): "As a young man, Yogananda searched for a guru. He visited countless saints and ashrams, but none felt like the teacher he was looking for. He prayed intensely. Finally, in 1910, he met Sri Yukteswar for the first time. He immediately recognized him as the master he had been seeking across lifetimes." This meeting is the spiritual turning point of the book.
> **Key takeaway**: Yogananda's search for a guru was not casual. It was an intense, focused quest that led him across India. The meeting with Sri Yukteswar was instantaneous recognition — the disciple knew the teacher, not through words but through the soul.

> **Case: The Mission to America** (Chapter on America): "In 1920, Yogananda was invited to speak at the Congress of Religious Liberals in Boston. He took this as a sign from his guru. He traveled to America, knowing he might never return to India. He spent the rest of his life teaching yoga and meditation to the West." Yogananda's move to America was an act of obedience to his guru and faith in his mission.
> **Key takeaway**: Yogananda did not come to America to become famous. He came because his guru instructed him. The mission was a spiritual duty, not a personal ambition. His success in spreading yoga is a testament to his dedication and the power of the teachings he carried.
