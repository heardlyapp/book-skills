# 3 — Meditation and Spiritual Practice

> Source: "Autobiography of a Yogi" by Paramahansa Yogananda

## The Science of Self-Realization

Yogananda presents meditation not as religious ritual but as a precise science. Kriya Yoga is a technique for experiencing the Divine directly through inner stillness and energy control.

> **Case: The Meditation Technique** (Chapter on Kriya Yoga): "Kriya Yoga consists of a series of breathing and concentration techniques that move the energy of life force (prana) up and down the spine. This calms the mind, purifies the nervous system, and eventually leads to the direct experience of God as the Divine Light within." Yogananda describes Kriya as a scientific method for spiritual evolution.
> **Key takeaway**: Kriya Yoga is precise and systematic. It is not about blind faith or emotional devotion — it is about practice. Regular meditation produces measurable results: greater calm, clearer mind, deeper connection to the Divine. Yogananda called it the "airplane route to God" — the fastest path.

> **Case: The Purpose of Meditation** (Chapter on Spiritual Practice): "Meditation is not about having visions or experiencing bliss. These are side effects. The real purpose is to realize your unity with God — to know that you are not the body or the mind but the immortal soul. Kriya Yoga accelerates this realization." Yogananda distinguishes means from ends.
> **Key takeaway**: Spiritual experiences are not the goal. They can even become distractions if you become attached to them. The goal is self-realization — direct, unshakable knowledge of your true nature as one with the Divine. Everything else is just scenery on the journey.
