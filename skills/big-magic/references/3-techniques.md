# Techniques: Practices for Creative Living

## The Fear Letter

Write a letter to your fear. Address it directly:
- "Dear Fear, thank you for trying to protect me. I know you're worried. Here's what I'm going to do anyway..."
- Acknowledge the fear without letting it make decisions
- Read it aloud to show yourself that fear has been heard but not obeyed

## The Curiosity Log

Keep a notebook of everything that sparks your curiosity for one week. Don't judge it. No idea is too small:
- A line from a book
- A question you can't stop thinking about
- A scene you witnessed
- A conversation that stuck with you

After one week, look for patterns. Curiosity leaves a trail.

## The Permission Ritual

When you feel like you need permission to create:
1. Write down: "Who told me I couldn't do this?"
2. Write down: "Who do I think has the authority to give me permission?"
3. Write down: "What would I do if I had full permission right now?"
4. Then do that thing.

## The Done-Is-Better-Than-Good Protocol

1. Set a deadline — even an artificial one
2. Define "done" before you start — not "perfect" but "complete"
3. At the deadline, release the work — even if you're not satisfied
4. Celebrate completion
5. Start the next thing

## The "Stubborn Curiosity" Practice

When you feel stuck or unmotivated:
- Ask: "What about this project still fascinates me?"
- Follow that one thread of fascination, even if it's small
- Let curiosity, not passion, carry you through the hard parts
