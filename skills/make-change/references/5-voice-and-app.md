# 5 — Voice & App

> Source: *Make Change* by Shaun King.

## King's Voice

King writes as an activist in the middle of the fight — urgent, personal, and unflinchingly direct. He does not write from a distance. He writes from the front lines, describing the exhaustion, the failure, the despair, and the stubborn hope that keeps him going. His voice is not academic or detached. It is the voice of someone who has been in the streets, on the phones with grieving families, in the meetings where plans were made and broken.

> **Case: The 48-Hour Snapshot (Introduction):** "In August of 2019, a white supremacist opened fire at a Walmart in El Paso, Texas, killing twenty-two... Before we could process what was happening, late that night, a gunman killed nine people in Dayton, Ohio... Three days later ICE raided chicken-processing plants in Mississippi arresting 680 Latinx workers." King does not let the reader look away. He piles the horrors on because that is what the moment feels like — overwhelming and unrelenting.

> **Case: The Personal Vulnerability (Chapter 2):** King opens up about his own background — the struggles, the mistakes, the failures. "I'm going to be vulnerable and put all of my business out there, showing you my scars and wounds, so that you can understand how these experiences shaped my activism." He offers his own failures as lessons, not credentials.

## Five Scenarios

### 1. The Person Who Wants to Start but Feels Overwhelmed

You see injustice everywhere and do not know where to start. King's message: choose one issue. Identify your gift. Take one action this week. Not everything. Just one.

### 2. The Experienced Activist Who Is Burned Out

You have been fighting for years and are exhausted. King's message: revolutionary self-care is not selfish. It is strategic. Rest is part of the movement. You cannot pour from an empty cup.

### 3. The Person Who Thinks They Have Nothing to Offer

You are not a speaker, not a leader, not an organizer. You think you cannot contribute. King's message: everyone has a gift. Writer, funder, connector, artist, volunteer. Find yours and use it.

### 4. The Person Whose Campaign Failed

You tried to organize something and it did not work. You feel like quitting. King's message: failure is data. His early campaigns failed for years before he found what worked. Analyze, adjust, try again.

### 5. The Ally Who Wants to Support But Not Lead

You want to help the movement without being at the center. King's message: every movement needs all kinds of contributors. Supporting roles are not lesser roles. Use your gift where you are.
