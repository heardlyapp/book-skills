# 2 — Key Principles

> Source: *Make Change* by Shaun King.

## The 7 Principles

### 1. Organized People Defeat Organized Money

This is the central thesis of King's organizing philosophy. Money is powerful — but coordinated human action is more powerful. The civil rights movement, the labor movement, and Black Lives Matter all demonstrated that organized people can shift the balance of power.

> **Case: Bernie Sanders' Foreword:** "Nothing is more powerful than the power of organizing. Our opponents have organized money, but organized money can be defeated by organized people — and this book is about how we can all throw our lives into organizing to make this world a better place together."

### 2. Action Precedes Expertise

You do not need to know everything to start. You learn by doing. King's own journey: he was not an expert when he started. He learned by organizing, failing, and trying again. The perfect activist does not exist. The active activist does.

### 3. Every Movement Needs Three Ingredients Equally

Energized people without organization = chaos. Organization without energy = bureaucracy. Sophisticated plans without people = fantasies. All three must be present and balanced.

### 4. Find and Use Your Gift

> **Case: King's Own Gift (Introduction):** King's gift was writing and storytelling. He used it to amplify the stories of families impacted by police violence. "I saw my primary responsibility as providing color, shape, and nuance to the very real pain being experienced by victims of police violence." He did not need to be the protest leader. He used what he had.

### 5. Failure Is Data, Not a Verdict

King failed for years before finding strategies that worked. Each failure taught something. The only true failure is stopping.

### 6. Stay Human

The struggle can make you bitter, cynical, and exhausted. King emphasizes maintaining compassion — for your allies, your community, and even your opponents. The fight is not just to change systems. It is to remain fully human while doing so.

### 7. Revolutionary Self-Care Is Part of the Movement

Burnout is not a sign of weakness. It is a sign that you are giving more than you have. Rest is not retreat. It is refueling. You cannot sustain the fight if you are empty.
