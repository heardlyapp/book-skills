# 4 — Anti-Patterns

> Source: *Make Change* by Shaun King.

## Common Errors

### 1. Waiting for the Perfect Moment

**The trap:** "I'll start organizing when I have more time, more money, more knowledge, more support."

**Why it fails:** The perfect moment never comes. King's message: start where you are with what you have. Action creates readiness. Waiting creates nothing.

> **Case: King's Own Starting Point (Chapter 2):** King did not start as an expert. He started as a pastor and writer who watched a video of Eric Garner being killed and made a choice to act. He did not have a plan, an organization, or funding. He had a phone, a social media account, and a decision. That was enough to begin.
> **Key takeaway**: You do not need to be ready. You need to start.

> **Case: The Failed Early Campaigns (Chapter 2):** Before finding effective strategies, King spent years organizing campaigns that produced minimal results. He writes: "For years, I thought I knew what making change looked and felt like. I was wrong — several times." His early failures taught him that good intentions without the right strategy lead nowhere.
> **Key takeaway**: Failure is not the end. It is data. Analyze it and adjust.

**The trap:** "People are passionate and angry. That is enough to win."

**Why it fails:** Energy without structure burns out. The Ferguson protests had enormous energy — but without organization and a sophisticated plan, the energy could not be sustained or translated into lasting change. Energy is fuel. Organization is the engine.

### 3. Going It Alone

**The trap:** "I will do this myself. I do not need anyone else."

**Why it fails:** Movements are collective by definition. King emphasizes that no single person can create systemic change. "I need us to be in this struggle together." Isolation leads to burnout and ineffectiveness.

### 4. Ignoring Self-Care

**The trap:** "The work is too urgent for rest. I can sleep when the revolution is won."

**Why it fails:** King describes his own January 2015 "gloom and dread" — months of nonstop work with little to show for it, leaving him depleted. Burnout is not a badge of honor. It is a strategic failure. You cannot sustain the fight if you are empty.

### 5. Comparing Your Contribution to Others'

**The trap:** "I am not Martin Luther King Jr. / not Shaun King / not as talented as X. My contribution does not matter."

**Why it fails:** King's entire framework is built on the idea that everyone has a gift. The writer's contribution is different from the organizer's. The funder's contribution is different from the artist's. Comparison is a distraction. Show up with what you have.
