## Philosophy

History does not repeat — it rhymes. The same pattern plays out every 80-100 years: a new order, a spiritual awakening, a fraying of institutions, and a crisis that forges a new order.

Generations are not just age groups — they are archetypes. Each generation is shaped by the turning in which it came of age, and each plays a specific role in the cycle.

The crisis is coming. It is not the end of the world — it is the end of an era.

## Core Framework Quick Reference

1. **The Saeculum**: An 80-100 year cycle of history with four seasons (turnings): High (spring), Awakening (summer), Unraveling (autumn), Crisis (winter).
2. **The Four Generational Archetypes**: Prophet (born during a High), Nomad (born during an Awakening), Hero (born during an Unraveling), Artist (born during a Crisis).
3. **The Most Recent Cycle**: GI Generation (Hero, born 1901-24), Silent Generation (Artist, 1925-42), Baby Boomers (Prophet, 1943-60), Gen X (Nomad, 1961-81), Millennials (Hero, 1982-04?).
4. **Current Standing (written 1997)**: The Third Turning (Unraveling, 1984-2005) was underway. The Fourth Turning (Crisis) would begin around 2005 and peak in the 2020s.
5. **What Crisis Looks Like**: An existential threat to the nation — war, economic collapse, revolution, or natural catastrophe. Institutions are rebuilt from scratch. The old order dies; a new one is born.

## Key Principles

1. Generations are shaped by the turning in which they come of age (roughly ages 18-25). This determines their collective personality for life.
2. The cycle is predictable but not deterministic. The timing and severity of turnings are influenced by human action.
3. Each turning lasts approximately 20-25 years. The Fourth Turning is the shortest but most intense.
4. The Crisis is not the end — it is a reset. Every Fourth Turning in American history has been followed by a new High.
5. The generational archetypes repeat in sequence: Prophet → Nomad → Hero → Artist → Prophet (next cycle).
6. Institutions weaken during the Unraveling, making society vulnerable during the Crisis.
7. The Crisis requires collective action. Individualism is suspended. Society reorganizes around shared purpose.

> This toolkit is based on William Strauss and Neil Howe's The Fourth Turning: An American Prophecy, published in 1997. The book builds on their earlier work Generations (1991), which traced generational cycles through Anglo-American history since the 15th century. The Fourth Turning specifically predicted that a crisis period would begin around 2005 and peak around the 2020s. Written before 9/11, before the 2008 financial crisis, before COVID-19, before January 6th — it is one of the most eerily prescient books of the late 20th century.
