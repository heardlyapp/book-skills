## The Saeculum Framework: History as Seasons

Strauss and Howe's central insight: Western history follows a predictable cycle of four "turnings" — seasons — that each last roughly 20-25 years, forming a complete "saeculum" of 80-100 years. Each turning is defined by the mood of the era and the generational archetype that comes of age during it.

**The Framework in Action:**
- The last full saeculum began with the American High (1946-64), went through the Consciousness Revolution (1964-84), the Culture Wars (1984-2005), and was due to end with a Fourth Turning Crisis.
- The identity of each generation is determined by which turning they experienced during their "coming of age" (roughly ages 18-25).

> **Case: The GI Generation — The Greatest** (Chapter 5): The GI Generation was born during the Unraveling of the late 19th/early 20th centuries and came of age during the Great Depression and WWII. They were a "Hero" archetype — optimistic, civic-minded, team-oriented. They built the post-war world, created the New Deal, fought WWII, and established the institutions of the American High.
> **Key takeaway**: The Hero generation is forged in crisis. Their defining experience shapes the next High. What the GI Generation built, their children (Boomers) would rebel against.

> **Case: The 1960s — A Second Turning** (Chapter 7): The Consciousness Revolution of 1964-84 was a classic Awakening — a spiritual upheaval against the institutions the GI Generation had built. Boomers (Prophet archetype) led the counterculture, civil rights movement, and anti-war protests. Their moralism and idealism were the defining traits of a Prophet generation challenging the established order.
> **Key takeaway**: The Awakening is necessary — it breaks the rigidity of the High. But it also begins the process of institutional decay that leads to the Unraveling and eventually the Crisis.
