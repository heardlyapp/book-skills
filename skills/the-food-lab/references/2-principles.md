# 2 — Meat and Poultry

> Source: "The Food Lab" by J. Kenji López-Alt

## The Science of Perfect Meat

**Sear**: Pat dry. Hot pan. Don't crowd. Maillard reaction at 285°F+.

**Cook to temperature**: Always use a thermometer. Carryover cooking adds 5-10°F.

**Rest**: 5-10 minutes. Juices redistribute. Rush this and you lose flavor.

## Steak Temperatures

Rare: 120°F | Medium-rare: 130°F | Medium: 140°F | Medium-well: 150°F | Well: 160°F+

> **Case: The 30-Second Steak Flip** (Steak Chapter): Conventional wisdom says flip steak once. Kenji tested flipping every 30 seconds. The result: faster cooking, more even doneness, better crust. "The constant-flip method cooks steak in half the time with superior results."
> **Key takeaway**: Conventional wisdom is often wrong. Flip your steak every 30 seconds for the best results. Test it yourself.

> **Case: The Dry Brine Chicken** (Poultry Chapter): Kenji tested salting chicken at different intervals. "Salting 24 hours ahead produced the juiciest, most flavorful chicken. Even 45 minutes helped. Salting 5 minutes before cooking did almost nothing." The salt dissolves, penetrates the meat, and changes protein structure to retain moisture.
> **Key takeaway**: Plan ahead. Salt your chicken (and steak) at least 45 minutes before cooking. 24 hours is ideal. The difference is dramatic.
