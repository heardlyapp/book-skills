# 1 — Eggs and Breakfast

> Source: "The Food Lab" by J. Kenji López-Alt

## Eggs

Eggs are a science lab in a shell. Kenji tested every method exhaustively.

**Scrambled**: Low heat. Constant stirring. Keep moving. Remove while still wet. Carryover finishes.

**Fried**: High heat. Butter or oil. Baste with fat. Crispy edges, runny yolk.

**Poached**: Fresh eggs. Vinegar in water. Swirl a vortex. Slip egg in. 3 minutes.

> **Case: The Egg Timer Experiment** (Eggs Chapter): Kenji boiled eggs at 30-second intervals from 0-12 minutes and photographed each one. The result: an exact visual guide to how time affects yolk and white. "There is no such thing as a 'hard-boiled' egg — only an egg cooked to a specific temperature for a specific time."
> **Key takeaway**: Precision matters. 30 seconds changes an egg from soft to medium to hard. Use a timer.

> **Case: The Salt-Orange-Yellow Test** (Seasoning Eggs): Kenji debunks the myth that salting scrambled eggs before cooking makes them watery. "I scrambled dozens of eggs with salt at different times. The salted-early eggs were actually creamier. The science: salt breaks down proteins, allowing them to trap more fat."
> **Key takeaway**: Salt your eggs early. The conventional wisdom is wrong. Salt before cooking gives you creamier scrambled eggs.
