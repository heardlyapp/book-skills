# 4 — Sauces, Braises, and Frying

> Source: "The Food Lab" by J. Kenji López-Alt

## Pan Sauce

Remove meat. Pour off excess fat. Add liquid (wine, stock). Scrape up browned bits. Reduce. Whisk in cold butter. Season. Done.

## Braising

Sear first. Add liquid. Low heat (300°F). Long time (2-4 hours). The collagen breaks down into gelatin. Tough cuts become tender.

> **Case: The Cold Butter Trick** (Pan Sauce Chapter): Kenji tested adding butter at different temperatures. "Adding cold butter at the end and whisking vigorously creates a creamy, emulsified sauce. Adding melted butter doesn't work — it breaks." The science: cold butter contains water and fat that emulsify with the liquid.
> **Key takeaway**: For perfect pan sauce, add cold butter at the end. Whisk constantly. Never add melted butter. The temperature drop helps emulsify.

> **Case: The Deep Fry Temperature** (Frying Chapter): Kenji explains why temperature matters: "Too low = greasy. Too high = burnt outside, raw inside. 350°F is the sweet spot. Don't crowd the oil — it drops temperature." He also debunks the myth that food absorbs more oil at lower temperatures.
> **Key takeaway**: Use a thermometer for frying. 350°F is ideal. Don't crowd. Let the oil come back to temperature between batches.
