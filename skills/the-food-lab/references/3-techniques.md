# 3 — Vegetables, Soups, and Salads

> Source: "The Food Lab" by J. Kenji López-Alt

## Roasted Vegetables

High heat (425°F+). Cut uniform pieces. Don't crowd the pan. Season well. Flip once. The goal: caramelized outside, tender inside.

## Stock

Roast bones and vegetables first. Cover with cold water. Simmer, don't boil. Skim. Cook 4-6 hours for chicken, 8-12 for beef.

> **Case: The Single-Layer Test** (Roasted Vegetables): Kenji tested roasting broccoli in crowded vs single-layer pans. Single layer: caramelized, crispy, delicious. Crowded: steamed, grey, sad. "The pan must not be crowded. If the pieces are touching, they steam instead of brown."
> **Key takeaway**: Crowding the pan is the #1 mistake in roasting vegetables. Spread them out. Use two pans if necessary. The browning is worth the extra pan to wash.

> **Case: The Vinaigrette Formula** (Salad Chapter): Kenji's basic vinaigrette: 3 parts oil to 1 part acid. Emulsify with mustard. Season with salt and pepper. "The ratio is not a law. Taste and adjust. Some salads need more acid. Some need less."
> **Key takeaway**: Vinaigrette is simple. Oil + acid + mustard + seasoning. Master this and you have dressing for life. Adjust the ratio to taste.
