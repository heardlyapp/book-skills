# Anti-Patterns: Gender Equality Traps

> Source: We Should All Be Feminists, throughout

## Believing Equality Is Already Achieved

Many believe gender inequality is a thing of the past. The data says otherwise: women globally earn less, hold fewer leadership positions, do more unpaid labor, and face higher rates of violence and harassment.

> **Case: The Accomplished Woman** (throughout): Even highly accomplished women face different standards. They are expected to be grateful for opportunities that men simply take for granted. The playing field is not close to level.

> **Key takeaway**: Equality is not achieved when a few women succeed. It's achieved when all women have the same opportunities as all men.

## The "Man-Hater" Myth

The stereotype that feminists hate men prevents many from identifying as feminist — even when they agree with the principles.

> **Case: The Label Refuser** (throughout): Many agree with gender equality but reject the "feminist" label. Adichie argues this leaves the stigma unchallenged. Claim the word and redefine it.

## Thinking Feminism Is Only for Women

Gender equality benefits everyone — especially men. Men gain freedom to express emotions, share parenting, and escape sole-provider pressure.

> **Case: The Emotional Man** (throughout): Men socialized to suppress emotions suffer higher suicide rates, shorter life expectancy, and isolation. Feminism liberates men too.

> **Key takeaway**: Feminism is not zero-sum. When women gain, everyone gains.
