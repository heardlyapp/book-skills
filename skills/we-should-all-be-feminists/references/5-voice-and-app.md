# Voice and Application: Adichie's Voice

> Source: We Should All Be Feminists, throughout

## Adichie's Voice

Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie writes with clarity, warmth, and personal honesty. This essay is told through her own experience — growing up in Nigeria, navigating gender expectations, and discovering feminism. The tone is conversational but serious.

> **Case: The Personal Essay Form** (throughout): Adichie uses her own life — her friend from primary school, the journalist's comment, her experience being called a feminist — to illustrate broader truths. Personal stories make the case more powerful than abstract arguments would.

> **Case: The Nigerian Context** (throughout): Adichie's stories are rooted in Nigeria but resonate globally. She shows how gender inequality manifests differently across cultures while remaining structurally similar.

## Application: Parents

The most actionable audience. Adichie's message about raising children without gender stereotypes is direct and practical.

## Application: Educators

Teachers can use Adichie's observations — from how we call on students to what we praise them for — to create more gender-equal classrooms.

## Application: Workplace Leaders

Managers can use the essay to understand how gender bias operates in professional settings.

## Application: Men

Adichie directly addresses men as allies and beneficiaries. They gain as much as women from equality.

## Key Quotes

- "A feminist is a man or woman who says there's a problem with gender as it's today and we must fix it, we must do better."
- "We do a great disservice to boys on how we raise them."
- "Culture does not make people. People make culture."
- "If we do something over and over again, it becomes normal."
