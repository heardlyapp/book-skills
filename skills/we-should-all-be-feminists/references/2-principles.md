# Principles: How Gender Roles Damage Both Sexes

> Source: We Should All Be Feminists, throughout

## The Ambition Penalty

Girls are taught to be ambitious in limited ways, then punished for succeeding. When a woman is successful, she's often called "aggressive" or "pushy" — terms not applied to equally ambitious men. This double standard limits women's advancement.

> **Case: The Journalist's Compliment** (throughout): A journalist complimented Adichie on her work but said it was a shame she wasn't married. When a man achieves professionally, his marital status is never mentioned. Women's success is still filtered through their relationships to men.

> **Key takeaway**: Same behavior, different labels. Ambitious men are "driven." Ambitious women are "difficult."

## The Cost to Boys and Men

Adichie argues we "do a great disservice to boys" by teaching them to suppress emotions. Men are taught to be hard, unemotional providers. This creates emotional repression, loneliness, and poor mental health.

> **Case: The Unemotional Man** (throughout): From childhood, boys hear "boys don't cry." They grow into men who can't express vulnerability. This connects to higher suicide rates and shorter life expectancy among men. Gender equality benefits men too.

## The Likability Trap

Girls are taught that being likeable is their primary goal — which conflicts with being ambitious, direct, and powerful.

> **Key takeaway**: We must teach girls that ambition is not a dirty word. And teach boys that vulnerability is not weakness.
