# Techniques: Creating Change

> Source: We Should All Be Feminists, throughout

## Raising Children Differently

Adichie's central practical advice: raise girls and boys the same way. Teach both ambition and empathy, strength and vulnerability, independence and caring. The goal is not to eliminate differences but to stop imposing them through culture.

> **Case: The Feminist Parents** (throughout): Parents who consciously reject gender stereotypes — giving dolls to boys and trucks to girls, encouraging both to express emotions freely. These small daily choices accumulate into dramatically different life outcomes for their children.

> **Key takeaway**: Culture changes one child at a time. How we raise the next generation is how we change the world.

## Speaking Up Against Bias

When you witness gender bias — a sexist comment, an interruption, a double standard — name it. Silence enables the status quo to continue unchanged.

> **Case: The Meeting Room Dynamic** (throughout): Women are interrupted more often than men in professional settings. Men who notice this and call it out — "she wasn't finished" — create space for equal participation. This simple act changes the room's norms.

## Education and Awareness

Gender equality should be taught in schools. Both boys and girls need to understand how gender socialization works and why feminism matters.

> **Case: The Classroom** (throughout): Adichie argues that introducing feminist ideas in education — not as blame but as liberation — would create a generation free from limiting gender roles.

> **Key takeaway**: You don't have to be confrontational. But you do have to be willing to speak when it matters.
