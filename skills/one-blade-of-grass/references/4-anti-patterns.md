# Anti-Patterns — One Blade of Grass

## The Central Error: "Enlightenment Is a Permanent State"

"Either you're cooked or you're not." But even after awakening, Shukman struggled. He still had difficult thoughts, doubts, challenges. Case: "After the profound experience, I still had to go set up the interview room."

### Mistake 1: "If I can't have a dramatic awakening, there's no point"
"Awakening is not the goal — practice is." Case: Shukman's first "gap" experience as a teenager didn't change his life for years. It was a seed.

### Mistake 2: "Reading about Zen is enough"
Shukman had to abandon "book burial." "I had to stop reading about Zen and start doing it." Case: He was a scholar of Buddhism before he met his first teacher. The scholarship didn't help.

### Mistake 3: "One teacher fits all"
Shukman had multiple teachers before finding the right one. Case: "Teacher, teacher" — the search itself was part of the practice.

### Mistake 4: "If I were enlightened, my problems would disappear"
"After Zen, mountains are mountains again." Shukman still had eczema, still had life challenges. The difference was in how he experienced them.

### Mistake 5: "Spiritual experiences are the goal"
"People go in ordinary; then something happens in the middle; then they reemerge new." But the "something" cannot be forced. Case: Shukman's awakening came when he wasn't seeking it — on the third or fourth night of a retreat.

### Mistake 6: "You need perfect conditions to practice"
The retreat center had trains. "All day long, a long, sonorous blast on a train whistle would come hooting up the hillside." Case: There are never perfect conditions. Practice anyway.
