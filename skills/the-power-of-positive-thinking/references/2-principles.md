## 7 Principles of Positive Thinking

### 1. Believe in Yourself

Self-confidence is the foundation. Without it, nothing else works. Faith in God gives you faith in yourself.

### 2. A Peaceful Mind Generates Power

Anxiety and worry drain energy. A calm mind is a powerful mind. Peale's prescription: daily quiet time, prayer, and positive mental imagery.

> **Case: The Executive Under Pressure** (Chapter 3): A high-pressure executive was exhausted and irritable. Peale taught him the "quiet minute" — taking one minute before each meeting to breathe, pray, and center himself. The result: he became calmer, more effective, and his team noticed the difference.
> **Key takeaway**: The busiest people need stillness the most. A minute of peace before activity changes the quality of the activity.

### 3. Prayer Is Practical Power

Peale treats prayer not as religious ritual but as a practical problem-solving tool. Prayer shifts the mind from problem to solution, from fear to faith.

### 4. Happiness Is a Decision

Happiness is not found — it is created. You can decide to be happy regardless of circumstances. The decision precedes the feeling.

### 5. Expect the Best

What you expect tends to happen. Expect success, and you will move toward it. Expect failure, and you will find it.

> **Case: The Salesman Who Expected Failure** (Chapter 7): A salesman consistently failed because he expected rejection. Peale taught him to expect acceptance. Before each call, he would say: "I expect a positive response." His sales improved dramatically.
> **Key takeaway**: Your expectations shape your behavior, which shapes outcomes. Expect success, and you act in ways that make success more likely.

### 6. Don't Believe in Defeat

Defeat is never final. It is a lesson. Refuse to accept defeat, and you will eventually win.

### 7. Break the Worry Habit

Worry is a habit — it can be broken. Replace worry with faith. Each time a worry arises, substitute a positive thought.
