## Practical Techniques

### 1. The Morning Affirmation

Before getting out of bed, say aloud: "This is going to be a great day. I am capable, confident, and guided by faith." Repeat it. The first thought sets the tone.

### 2. The Worry Substitution

When worry arises, immediately substitute a positive thought or prayer. Do not fight the worry — replace it. The mind cannot hold two thoughts at once.

> **Case: The Financial Worrier** (Chapter 9): A man was consumed by fear of financial ruin. Peale taught him: every time the worry came, pray immediately. "I release this to God. I trust that I will be provided for." Over weeks, the worry lost its hold.
> **Key takeaway**: Worry is a habit. The habit is broken by substitution, not suppression. Replace the worry thought with a faith thought.

### 3. The Picture Method

Close your eyes. Visualize yourself succeeding. See it vividly. Smell the air, hear the sounds. The subconscious accepts the image as reality and works toward it.

### 4. The Faith Formula in Practice

When facing a problem: 1) Pray about it. 2) Release the outcome. 3) Take one actionable step. 4) Expect a positive result.

> **Case: The Job Interview** (Chapter 10): A nervous job applicant used Peale's formula: prayed for guidance, released the outcome to God, prepared thoroughly, and expected a positive result. He got the job. The formula did not guarantee the outcome — but it allowed him to show up as his best self.
> **Key takeaway**: The Faith Formula works not by magic but by removing the mental blocks (anxiety, fear, doubt) that prevent you from performing at your best.
