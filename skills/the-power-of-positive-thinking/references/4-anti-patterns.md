## 6 Anti-Patterns of Negative Thinking

### 1. "I Can't"

The word "can't" is a self-fulfilling prophecy. Replace it with "I can." The mind believes what the tongue says.

### 2. "My Circumstances Determine My Mood"

Circumstances do NOT determine your mood — your thoughts about them do. You can choose your response regardless of the situation.

> **Case: The Hospital Patient** (Chapter 6): A man with a serious illness was depressed and hopeless. Peale taught him to focus on gratitude — for the nurses, the window, a kind word. His mood improved. His healing accelerated. The illness was real — but his response to it was a choice.
> **Key takeaway**: Even in the worst circumstances, you can choose your attitude. Gratitude is always available.

### 3. "Worrying Prepares Me"

Worry does not prepare you — it paralyzes you. Preparation is action. Worry is inaction disguised as concern.

### 4. "I'm Not a Positive Person"

Positive thinking is a skill, not a personality trait. Anyone can learn it. It requires practice, not innate disposition.

### 5. "If I'm Positive, Bad Things Won't Happen"

Positive thinking does not prevent problems. It gives you the resources to handle problems effectively when they come.

### 6. "This Is Just Denial"

Positive thinking is not denial. It is choosing to focus on solutions and possibilities rather than problems and limitations. Denial ignores reality. Positive thinking transforms it.


> **Case: The Complainer** (Chapter 11): Peale describes a man who complained constantly — about his job, his health, his family. Peale challenged him: for one week, say nothing negative. The man found it nearly impossible. But by the end of the week, his outlook had shifted. He realized his complaining was a habit, not a response to reality.
> **Key takeaway**: Complaining is a mental habit. Breaking it for even one week can transform your perspective.
