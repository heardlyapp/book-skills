# Key Principles — No Future Without Forgiveness

## I. There Is No Future Without Forgiveness

"The title is the thesis." South Africa could not move forward without confronting the past. "Forgetting is not the same as forgiving. The past would not stay past." Case: The Nuremberg model would have destabilized the country. Amnesia would have allowed the poison to fester. Only truth-telling + forgiveness could create a future.

## II. Truth Is the Midwife of Forgiveness

"You cannot forgive what you do not know." The TRC's central mechanism: amnesty in exchange for full disclosure. "We had to know what we were forgiving." Case: Victims' families sat in the same room as torturers who finally told them the truth about what happened to their loved ones.

## III. Ubuntu — I Am Because You Are

"Ubuntu speaks of the very essence of being human." In African philosophy, a person is a person through other persons. "My humanity is caught up, is inextricably bound up, in yours." Case: "We are bound together — black and white — as the convicts in The Defiant Ones were manacled together. We can only survive together."

## IV. Forgiveness Is Not Weakness

"Forgiveness is not being soft or pretending things didn't happen." Tutu: "It requires enormous strength — to acknowledge the wrong, to name it, to feel the pain — and then to let go of the desire for revenge." Case: Mothers of murdered children who looked their killers in the eye and said "I forgive you."

## V. We Are All Capable of Both Good and Evil

"None of us can claim to be entirely good or entirely evil." Tutu rejects the demonization of apartheid's perpetrators. "I have seen more goodness in the worst of us than I ever expected." Case: Apartheid police who confessed to atrocities — and also had families, loved ones, and, in some cases, genuine remorse.

## VI. The Third Way Is Possible

"Nuremberg or amnesia — there was a third way." The TRC was not perfect, but it was better than the alternatives. Case: Countries emerging from conflict around the world have studied the TRC model — from Rwanda to Northern Ireland to Colombia.

## VII. God's World and God Is in Charge

Tutu's Christian faith underpins everything. "We had held on to this article of faith by the skin of our teeth — that this is God's world and God is in charge." Case: After voting, Tutu's prayer: "For goodness' sake, why don't You make it more obvious that You are in charge?"
