# Anti-Patterns — No Future Without Forgiveness

## The Central Error: "Justice Means Punishment"

"Retributive justice — an eye for an eye — leaves everyone blind." Tutu argues that the Western justice system's focus on punishment is insufficient for healing a broken society. "Good retribution is healing, restorative — not punitive." Case: Nuremberg-style trials would have destabilized the fragile transition and satisfied only the desire for vengeance.

### Mistake 1: "Forgetting is the same as forgiving"
"Many wanted to draw a line through the past and pretend nothing had happened." Tutu: "Forgetting is not the same as forgiving. The past would not stay past." Unresolved trauma festers. Case: The TRC was needed precisely because amnesia was not an option.

### Mistake 2: "Victims should forgive and move on"
"Forgiveness cannot be imposed." The TRC never forced victims to forgive. "Forgiveness is a gift — not an obligation." Case: Some victims chose not to forgive. That was their right. "Forgiveness must be freely given or it is meaningless."

### Mistake 3: "Perpetrators are monsters"
Tutu rejects this utterly. "None of us can claim to be entirely good or entirely evil." Case: The Vlakplaas policemen had families, loved ones, and, in some cases, genuine remorse. "I have seen more goodness in the worst of us than I ever expected."

### Mistake 4: "I didn't know about apartheid's crimes"
"We did not know" was a common defense. Tutu challenges it: "You must have known something was terribly wrong." Case: The pass laws, the forced removals, the police brutality — "How could you not know?"

### Mistake 5: "Forgiveness is a one-time event"
"Forgiveness is a process, not a single act." Case: The TRC hearings were just one step. Healing continues for generations. "You don't forgive once and it's done — you forgive, and forgive, and forgive."

### Mistake 6: "The TRC was too soft on perpetrators"
"The amnesty provision was the most controversial." But the alternative was worse. "Without amnesty, there would have been no TRC. Without the TRC, there would have been no truth. Without truth, there would have been no forgiveness."
