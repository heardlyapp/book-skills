## 7 Principles of Moral Psychology

### 1. Intuitions Come First

Moral judgment is not the product of reasoning. It is the product of fast, automatic intuitions. Reasoning follows and tries to justify what the intuition already decided.

### 2. The Rider Serves the Elephant

The rider (reasoning) evolved to help the elephant (intuition) navigate the world — not to find truth. This is why people are so good at rationalizing their positions and so bad at changing them.

### 3. There Are Six Moral Foundations

Not one. Care, Fairness, Liberty, Loyalty, Authority, and Sanctity are the psychological building blocks of human morality. Each evolved to solve a specific adaptive challenge.

> **Case: The Liberty Foundation** (Chapter 8): Haidt initially identified five foundations. Later he added Liberty — the resistance to domination and oppression. Liberty explains why libertarians and leftists can both be passionate about freedom, even though they define it differently (freedom from government vs. freedom from exploitation).
> **Key takeaway**: Moral foundations theory is not a fixed list. Haidt discovered Liberty by listening to people whose morality his original framework could not explain. The framework evolves.

### 4. Conservatives Use More Foundations

Liberals build their morality primarily on Care and Fairness (and Liberty). Conservatives use all six. This gives conservatives a broader moral vocabulary and a deeper intuitive connection to values like loyalty, tradition, and purity.

### 5. WEIRD People Are Moral Outliers

Western, educated, individualistic people are unusual in human history. Most societies throughout history emphasized Loyalty, Authority, and Sanctity as much as Care and Fairness. WEIRD morality is the exception, not the rule.

> **Case: The Cross-Cultural Study** (Chapter 6): Haidt and colleagues studied moral judgments across cultures. Brazilians and Indians used all six foundations. Americans and Europeans used primarily Care and Fairness. The difference was not education — it was culture. Western individualism had pruned the moral palate.
> **Key takeaway**: If you think morality is only about harm and fairness, you have been shaped by your culture. Most of humanity sees a wider moral landscape.

### 6. The Hive Switch Is Real

Humans can transcend self-interest and merge with a group. This capacity evolved through multilevel selection — groups of cooperative individuals outcompeted groups of selfish ones.

### 7. Morality Binds and Blinds

The same mechanisms that bind us to our group blind us to others. This is why politics is so passionate — and why understanding the other side is so hard.
