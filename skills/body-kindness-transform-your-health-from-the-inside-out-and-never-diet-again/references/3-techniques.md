# Techniques: Feelings, Resilience, and Self-Compassion

> Source: Body Kindness, Chapters 5-7

## All Feelings Matter

Don't suppress bad feelings. Sadness, anger, anxiety are valid. Feel them without being controlled by them.

> **Case: The Emotional Eater** (Chapter 5): A client ate when stressed. Scritchfield: don't judge the eating. First notice the feeling. Then ask what the feeling actually needs — comfort food, a walk, or a conversation?

> **Case: The Anger Journal** (Chapter 5): A client who suppressed anger was encouraged to journal it. Naming the feeling reduced its power. Body kindness means making room for all feelings.

> **Key takeaway**: Feelings are information, not problems. Notice, name, then respond intentionally.

## Building Resilience

Self-compassion is the key to bouncing back from setbacks. Treat yourself like a friend.

> **Case: The Diet "Failure"** (Chapter 7): A client felt shame after a weekend of "bad eating." Scritchfield asked: what would you say to a friend? The answer was kindness. She applied the same kindness to herself and bounced back faster.

> **Case: The Perfectionist Mama** (Chapter 7): A mother who felt she had to do everything perfectly for her family. Self-compassion meant accepting "good enough" and asking for help.

## Making Room for Fun

Joy is not a reward — it's a necessary part of health. Schedule fun.

> **Key takeaway**: Self-compassion is not self-indulgence. It's the most effective way to create lasting behavior change.
