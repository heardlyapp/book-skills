# Anti-Patterns: Diet Culture Traps

> Source: Body Kindness, Chapters 8-10

## Weight Loss as a Goal

Setting weight loss as a primary goal backfires. It reinforces that your body is a problem to fix.

> **Case: The Scale Obsession** (Chapter 8): A client weighed herself daily. If the number went up, she restricted. If it went down, she was anxious about maintaining it. Body kindness: focus on behaviors, let weight settle where it will.

> **Case: The "After" Photo Trap** (Chapter 8): A client postponed happiness until she reached her goal weight. Body kindness: you deserve to be happy now, at your current size. Happiness is not a reward for weight loss.

> **Key takeaway**: Your body is not a problem to solve. The belief that you need a different size to be worthy is the problem.

## The All-or-Nothing Mindset

"One cookie = might as well eat the whole box." Perfectionism leads to giving up at the first slip.

> **Case: The Monday Reset** (Chapter 9): A client who ate "badly" on Friday would wait until Monday to restart. Three lost days. Body kindness: every meal is a fresh start. Nothing is ruined.

## Using Exercise as Punishment

"I ate too much, so I have to run it off." Exercise becomes penance, destroying joy.

> **Case: The Guilt Workout** (Chapter 9): A client who overate would force herself to exercise. Body kindness: move because it feels good, not to compensate. Joyful movement is sustainable; punishment is not.

> **Key takeaway**: Exercise is celebration, not penance. Every meal is a fresh start.
