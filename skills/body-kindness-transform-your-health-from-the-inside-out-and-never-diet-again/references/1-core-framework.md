# Core Framework: The Body Kindness Philosophy

> Source: Body Kindness, Introduction and Chapter 1

## The Three Pillars

Love, Connect, and Care. Health grows from these three foundations — not from criticism, restriction, or punishment.

> **Case: The Client Who Hated Her Cookies** (Introduction): A client insisted on making "healthy" baked goods — dry, flavorless substitutes. She couldn't permit herself real cookies. Body kindness means allowing satisfaction and pleasure in eating.

> **Case: The Red-Eye Gym Goer** (Introduction): A client dragged herself to the gym after a red-eye flight, exhausted. Body kindness says: listen to your body. Rest is health. Exercise when rested, not when punishing yourself.

> **Key takeaway**: Body kindness starts with love. The three pillars (Love, Connect, Care) provide the structure for all health decisions.

## The Body Kindness Question

"Is this helping to create a better life for myself?" Use for any health decision.

## Your Body Is Not the Problem

Scritchfield emphasizes that your body is not something to fix. The problem is in your mind — the beliefs that you must look a certain way to be worthy.

> **Case: The Weight Loss Trap** (Chapter 1): A client believed she couldn't be happy until she lost weight. She had a loving family, good friends, and a healthy lifestyle — but none of it was "good enough" because of her weight.

> **Key takeaway**: The Body Kindness Question cuts through "should" and helps you choose real wellbeing.
