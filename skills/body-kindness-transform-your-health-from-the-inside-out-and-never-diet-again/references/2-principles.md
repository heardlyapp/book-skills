# Principles: Eating and Food Freedom

> Source: Body Kindness, Chapters 2-4

## No Good or Bad Foods

Labeling foods "good" or "bad" creates guilt and restriction, which triggers overeating. Body kindness means giving yourself permission to eat all foods.

> **Case: The Cookie Permission** (Chapter 2): A client restricted sweets, then binged. When she gave herself unconditional permission to eat cookies anytime, they lost their power. She ate fewer and enjoyed them more. Permission is peace.

> **Case: The Restaurant Dinner** (Chapter 2): A client "saved up" calories before eating out, then overate. Body kindness: eat normally all day. Trust yourself at the restaurant.

> **Key takeaway**: Forbidden foods become obsessive. Unconditional permission creates peace with food.

## Intuitive Eating

Eat when hungry. Stop when satisfied. Choose food that tastes good and makes you feel good. No tracking.

> **Case: The Calorie Counter** (Chapter 2): Tracking every calorie created stress and disconnection from body signals. When she stopped tracking and started listening, she learned what her body actually needed.

## Joyful Movement and Sleep

Exercise should feel good, not like punishment. Sleep is the foundation of health — it affects hunger, mood, and decision-making.

> **Case: The Exercise Hater** (Chapter 3): A client who hated running discovered she loved dancing. Body kindness says: if you hate an activity, don't do it. Find movement you enjoy.

> **Key takeaway**: Your body knows what it needs better than any external rule. Trust it. Move joyfully. Sleep deeply.
