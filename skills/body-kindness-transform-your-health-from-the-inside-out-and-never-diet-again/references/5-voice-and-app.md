# Voice and Application: Scritchfield's Approach

> Source: Body Kindness, throughout

## Scritchfield's Voice

Rebecca Scritchfield writes as a registered dietitian nutritionist who's been through her own struggles. She's warm, scientifically grounded, and deeply empathetic. Client stories illustrate every principle in action.

> **Case: Her Own Story** (Introduction): Scritchfield shares her history of dieting, body hatred, and food rules. She's not preaching from outside — she's sharing what worked for her and hundreds of clients she's counseled.

> **Case: The Client Who Couldn't Be Happy** (Chapter 10): A client postponed all happiness until reaching her goal weight. She had a loving family, good health, and meaningful work — but none of it counted. Body kindness's breakthrough: you deserve joy now, at your current size. Your body is not holding happiness hostage.

> **Key takeaway**: Scritchfield's credibility comes from both professional expertise as a dietitian and personal experience. She's been where her readers are.

## Application: Daily Practice

The Body Kindness Question is the most practical tool. Ask it before eating, exercising, or talking to yourself. It cuts through habits and reveals what you actually need.

## Application: Self-Talk

Notice body criticism. Replace it with curiosity. "Is this thought helpful? Is it creating a better life?"

## Application: Food

Choose foods that nourish AND satisfy. No guilt. No rules. Just awareness and care.

## Application: Movement

Find what feels good. Walking, dancing, stretching — if it brings joy, do it. If not, find something else.

## Key Quotes

- "Is this helping to create a better life for myself?"
- "Your body is not the problem."
- "Health grows from love."
- "Move because it feels good, not to punish."
