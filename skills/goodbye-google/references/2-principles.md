# Key Principles — Step-by-Step Migration, Forwarding, and the Non-Technical Approach

## Core Principles

> **Case: The Forwarding Strategy** (Step 4): After choosing a new email provider, set up forwarding from Gmail to your new address. This is a safety net. Even if you miss changing some accounts, email will reach your new inbox. Keep forwarding active for 3-6 months. You will discover accounts you forgot about.
> **Key takeaway**: Forwarding is insurance. Do not delete your Gmail until you are certain you have migrated everything.

> **Case: The Dependencies List** (Step 3): Thomas advises spending a week making a list of every single account and service connected to your Gmail. This includes: banking, social media, shopping (Amazon, eBay), subscriptions (Netflix, Spotify), professional accounts (LinkedIn, Slack), cloud services, newsletters, forums, healthcare portals.
> **Key takeaway**: Your Gmail is the hub of your digital life. Changing the hub requires mapping every spoke. One missed spoke can block the entire migration.

> **Case: The Non-Technical Guarantee** (Throughout): Thomas designed the book for people who are not technical. Every step is explained in plain English. Every technical term is defined. The assumption: if you can use Gmail, you can leave it.
> **Key takeaway**: Privacy should not require a computer science degree. If a step seems too technical, there is probably a simpler alternative. Look for it.
