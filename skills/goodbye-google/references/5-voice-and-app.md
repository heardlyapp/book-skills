# Author Voice and Application Scenarios — Practical, Reassuring, Step-by-Step

## John Thomas's Voice

Thomas writes like a friend who has already done what you are trying to do. He is practical, reassuring, and patient. He assumes you are not technical but that you are capable. He never talks down to the reader. The tone is: "I have done this. It is not as hard as you think. Let me walk you through it."

## Five Common Application Scenarios

> **Case 1: The Frustrated Gmail User** — "I've had enough of Google but I don't know where to start." Start with Step 1: decide what you want. Do you want privacy, convenience, or both? Then choose a provider. Then forward your email. One step at a time.

> **Case 2: The Privacy-Conscious Professional** — "I need my email to be secure for work." ProtonMail offers end-to-end encryption and is based in Switzerland, outside US jurisdiction. It is the gold standard for privacy-conscious professionals.

> **Case 3: The Parent Protecting Their Family** — "I want to move my whole family off Google." This is more complex. Each family member needs their own account on the new service. Start with your own migration. Do one person at a time. Do not try to move everyone at once.

> **Case 4: The Person Locked into the Google Ecosystem** — "I use Android, Chrome, Gmail, Drive, Photos, Maps, and YouTube. I'm too deep to get out." You are never too deep. But be realistic: it will take 2-3 months. Do not try to do it in a weekend.

> **Case 5: The Minimalist** — "I want to use as few services as possible. What is the minimum viable alternative?" Fastmail for email and calendar. Nextcloud for file storage. DuckDuckGo for search. Firefox for browser. That is the minimum stack.

- "Privacy is a practice, not a destination."
- "Do not delete your account until you have migrated everything. This is the first rule."

- "Your Google account is connected to more services than you realize. The checklist is essential."
- "You can leave Google without losing access to anything that matters. It just takes planning."
