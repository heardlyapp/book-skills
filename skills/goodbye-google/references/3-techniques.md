# Techniques — Service Alternatives, Google Takeout, and the Post-Google Life

## Practical Methods

> **Case: Email Alternatives** (Step 2): The book compares providers: ProtonMail (Switzerland, end-to-end encrypted, paid option removes limits), Fastmail (Australia, fast and reliable, paid), Tutanota (Germany, encrypted, affordable), Posteo (Germany, green energy, anonymous). Each has pros and cons. None is perfect. Choose based on your priorities.
> **Key takeaway**: There is no perfect email provider. Choose the one whose trade-offs match your priorities. Security? Convenience? Jurisdiction? Pick two.

> **Case: Google Takeout** (Before Step 6): Google Takeout allows you to download all your data: emails, Drive files, Calendar entries, Contacts, Photos, YouTube data, and more. The download can take days. Plan for it.
> **Key takeaway**: Your data belongs to you. Download it before you delete. This is not optional.

> **Case: Service-by-Service Replacement** (Step 4): Thomas provides alternatives for every Google service: Drive → Nextcloud, pCloud, or Sync.com. Calendar → ProtonCalendar, Fastmail Calendar, or Apple Calendar. Docs → Microsoft Office, LibreOffice, or OnlyOffice. Maps → OpenStreetMap, OsmAnd, Maps.me. Photos → Synology Photos, iCloud, or Amazon Photos.
> **Key takeaway**: There is a viable alternative for every Google service. Some are better. Some are worse. All give you your data back.

> **Key takeaway**: The right tool for the right job. Do not use one service for everything. Pick the best tool for each need.
