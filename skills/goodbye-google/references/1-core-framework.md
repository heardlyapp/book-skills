# Core Framework — The Gameplan and the Goodbye Books Method

## The Model

The book's framework: understand why privacy matters → choose your new provider → migrate one service at a time → redirect forward → handle loose ends → delete when ready. Six steps repeated across services.

> **Case: The Privacy Cost of Free** (Part 1): Thomas explains that Google's business model is advertising. Advertising requires data about you. The more data Google has, the more money it makes. The services are not free — they are payment vehicles for data extraction. Google's Terms of Service grant them broad rights to use your data. Reading the privacy policy reveals: they collect your location, your search history, your emails (for ads), your YouTube history, your voice recordings, your face recognition data.
> **Key takeaway**: You are not the customer of Google's services. You are the raw material. The real customers are the advertisers. Understand this fully before you decide whether to stay.

> **Case: The Migration Gameplan** (Part 2): The book's central framework is a six-step process: decide what you want, choose an alternative, list dependencies, redirect email, handle loose ends, delete account. Each step has sub-steps. The process is designed to prevent the most common mistake: deleting the account before everything is migrated.
> **Key takeaway**: Do not skip steps. The order matters. Migrate first. Verify. Then delete.

> **Case: The Master Checklist** (Part 3): A comprehensive list of everything to do before deletion. Including: download all Google Takeout data, update accounts that use Gmail, check for services that require Google login (Stack Overflow, Quora, Medium), save Google Maps saved places, download Google Photos, check Google Play purchases.
> **Key takeaway**: Your Google account is probably connected to more services than you realize. The checklist is essential. Do not rely on memory.
