# 5 — Voice & App

> Source: *Perfect* by Ellen Hopkins — voice, characters, and scenarios.

## The Book's Voice

Hopkins writes in free verse — short, cutting lines that mirror the fragmented inner lives of her characters. The form itself is the message: these are young people whose minds are racing, whose thoughts don't come in smooth paragraphs.

## The Five Characters

- **Cara** — The perfect student, Stanford-bound. Discovering she's a lesbian while dating the "perfect" guy. Her arc: from costume to self.
- **Kendra** — The pageant queen / model. Starving herself to a size 2. Her arc: from surgery room to recovery room.
- **Sean** — The athlete who loses himself to steroids. Competitive, angry, desperate to be the best. His arc: from control to loss.
- **Andre** — The rich son whose father wants him to be a businessman. He wants to dance. His arc: from compliance to self-expression.
- **Conner** — The unspoken center. Cara's twin. Attempted suicide. His story is the one that isn't told directly but haunts every page.

## Signature Lines

> "The outside belongs to others. Only you should decide for you — what is perfect."

> "Perfection is the enemy of connection."

> "The mirror is the most dishonest thing in the room."

> "Who do you want to be when you're done pretending?"

> "Perfect is a direction, not a destination."

## Five Scenarios

### 1. The Teenager With an Eating Disorder

Kendra's playbook: Kendra was chasing a size 2. She was called "skin and bones" by the anesthesiologist who saved her life. If you can't eat a meal without guilt, you need help. The mirror is lying.

### 2. The Closeted Teen

Cara's playbook: She dated Sean because everyone expected it. She kissed Dani and realized she'd been playing a role. Your sexuality is yours to discover, not yours to defend.

### 3. The Teenager Using Performance-Enhancing Drugs

Sean's playbook: Steroids made him bigger, stronger, angrier. The cost was his relationship with Cara and his emotional stability. The shortcut always has a price.

### 4. The Teenager With Overbearing Parents

Andre's playbook: His father had a plan. His heart had a different one. The only way out was to tell the truth: "What I want to be is someone who loves what they do."

### 5. The Friend of Someone Who Attempted Suicide

Conner's playbook: Don't say nothing. Say something. "I'm here. I see you. You don't have to pretend with me." Silence is not respect — it's abandonment.
