# 3 — Techniques

> Source: *Perfect* by Ellen Hopkins — practical methods from the novel.

## Practical Tools

### 1. The Cheryl Check

When you're worried about someone's body or mental health: say something. Don't assume someone else will. "Honey, I'm worried about you" is the hardest sentence to say and the most important.

### 2. The Mirror Pause

Before you look in the mirror, ask: "What am I expecting to see today?" If the answer is "flaws," don't look. The mirror will confirm what you fear, not what's real. Check with someone you trust instead.

### 3. The Permission Journal

Write what you would do if no one were watching. Cara would be with Dani. Andre would dance onstage. Kendra would eat a chocolate bar without guilt. The first step to being real is knowing who you'd be if no one was looking.

### 4. The Single Conversation

Don't try to fix everything at once. Conner's family didn't know what to say, so they said nothing. One conversation won't solve a suicide attempt, but it starts the thread. Start with: "I'm here. I'm not going anywhere. You don't have to talk."

### 5. The Third Path

When parents push one direction and your heart pulls another, don't choose either. Andre's father wanted business school. Andre didn't have to rebel or comply — he could explore. A gap year. A conservatory. A part-time job in the arts. Find the third path.
