# 4 — Anti-Patterns

> Source: *Perfect* by Ellen Hopkins.

## Common Errors

### 1. Chasing Perfect at the Cost of Real

Every character in this book sacrifices something real for something perfect. Kendra sacrifices health for a model's body. Sean sacrifices emotional stability for athletic performance. Cara sacrifices authenticity for the perfect relationship.

### 2. Hiding Behind "I'm Fine"

Kendra said "I'm fine." Sean said "I'm fine." Conner said "I'm fine." The word "fine" was a wall, not an answer. When someone says "I'm fine" after a crisis, don't accept it.

### 3. Assuming You'd See the Signs

Kendra's mother is a plastic surgeon. She didn't see her daughter's eating disorder. Conner's family didn't see he was suicidal. The people closest to you can be the blindest.

### 4. Treating the Symptom Instead of the Root

Kendra's nose job was to fix the bump in her nose. The problem wasn't her nose — it was her relationship with her body. Sean's steroids were to build muscle. The problem wasn't his performance — it was his sense of worth.

### 5. The "It's Just a Phase" Dismissal

Cara's parents would probably have called her lesbianism "a phase." Andre's father dismissed dance. Dismissal is not understanding. It's avoidance.
