# 2 — Key Principles

> Source: *Perfect* by Ellen Hopkins — all four POVs.

## The 7 Principles

### 1. The Mirror is a Liar

Kendra saw "fat" at 109 pounds and 5'10". The mirror was not reflecting her body — it was reflecting her fear. The mirror is the most dishonest thing in the room.

### 2. Someone Has to Tell the Truth

Cheryl the anesthesiologist could have ignored Kendra's emaciated body. She didn't. She canceled the surgery and started a conversation that might have saved Kendra's life. Sometimes being "polite" is dangerous. Be the Cheryl.

> **Case: Cheryl vs. Dr. Kane** (page 550-552): Kendra's mother the plastic surgeon had looked at her daughter daily and seen nothing wrong. The anesthesiologist looked once and saw a girl starving to death. The difference was not training — it was courage.

### 3. The Mask Always Cracks

Cara's perfect life, Andre's compliance, Sean's confidence, Kendra's control — every mask in the book cracks under pressure. Better to take it off yourself than wait for it to be ripped off.

### 4. Parents Have Their Own Masks

Dr. Kane is a plastic surgeon who believes beauty = happiness. But she's also a woman who can't look at her own daughter. Sean's father was a hero — who died. The pressure Sean carries is legacy, not love.

### 5. What You Build Your Body With Builds Your Character

Sean's steroids built rage, not just muscle. Kendra's starvation built control, not beauty. Your body is not a construction project — it's the vehicle of your life.

### 6. You Can't Save Someone Who Doesn't Want to Be Saved

Conner's "next time I'll have to try harder" is devastating. Kendra wanted to save him. You can't. What you can do is be present, listen, and not pretend the problem doesn't exist.

### 7. Perfect is a Direction, Not a Destination

Cara kissed Dani in the snow. Andre danced to Coltrane in front of the kids. Those moments were not perfect — they were real. That's enough.
