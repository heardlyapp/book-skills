# Reference: The Trump-Russia Investigation

## Overview
The investigation into Trump's ties to Russia predated the dossier. The FBI had already opened Crossfire Hurricane based on the Papadopoulos tip. The dossier became one thread in a much larger investigation.

## Case: The Trump Tower Moscow Project
Michael Cohen, Trump's personal lawyer, pursued a Trump Tower development in Moscow during the 2016 campaign. The project was never built, but Cohen lied to Congress about how long it continued. The dossier alleged extensive details about the project, including that Cohen met secretly with Russian officials in Prague. The Prague meeting has never been corroborated. Cohen denied it under oath.

## Case: Carter Page
Carter Page was a Trump campaign foreign policy advisor who had been on the FBI's radar since 2013 as a potential Russian intelligence target. The FBI obtained a FISA warrant to surveil Page in October 2016. The warrant application relied in part on information from the Steele dossier. The OIG later found 17 errors and omissions in the FISA applications, including failures to disclose that the dossier was funded by the Clinton campaign.

## Case: Paul Manafort
Manafort, Trump's campaign chairman, had a long history of political consulting work for pro-Russian politicians in Ukraine. He was convicted of financial crimes and tax fraud. The dossier alleged that Manafort served as a backchannel between the Trump campaign and Russian interests. Manafort's conviction was a significant outcome of the broader Russia investigation, though it was not directly related to collusion with the Russian government.
