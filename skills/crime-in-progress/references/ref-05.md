# Reference: The Aftermath

## Overview
The dossier did not end with the election. Its legacy has been the subject of multiple investigations, endless political debate, and a fundamental challenge to public trust in both journalism and the intelligence community.

## Case: The Mueller Investigation
Robert Mueller's investigation (May 2017 - March 2019) was the official outcome of the Russia probe. The Mueller report did not rely on the Steele dossier. It concluded that Russia interfered in the 2016 election, that the Trump campaign had numerous contacts with Russians, and that Trump could not be exonerated on obstruction of justice — but did not establish a criminal conspiracy between the campaign and Russia. The authors argue that the dossier's core claim — that the Trump campaign cooperated with Russian interference — was not proven.

## Case: The OIG Report
The Justice Department Inspector General's report (December 2019) was a detailed examination of the FBI's FISA applications. It found 17 errors and omissions but concluded the investigation was not politically motivated. The report was used by Trump supporters to argue that the FBI had abused its authority. The authors argue the OIG report showed sloppiness and bias but not corruption.

## Case: The Durham Investigation
Special Counsel John Durham was appointed in 2019 to investigate the investigators. His investigation went on for years and resulted in three prosecutions — all of which ended in acquittal or dismissal. The Durham investigation became a symbol of the ongoing struggle to determine whether the Russia investigation was legitimate or a "witch hunt."
