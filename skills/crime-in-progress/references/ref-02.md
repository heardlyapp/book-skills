# Reference: Fusion GPS

## Overview
Fusion GPS was founded by Glenn Simpson and Peter Fritsch, both former Wall Street Journal investigative reporters. The firm specialized in research for legal clients — often digging into the backgrounds of individuals or companies involved in litigation or political controversies.

## Case: The Trump Project
Fusion GPS was initially hired by a Republican client (The Washington Free Beacon) to research Donald Trump during the Republican primaries. After Trump won the nomination, the Free Beacon stopped funding the research. The DNC, through Perkins Coie, then hired Fusion GPS to continue the Trump research. The project shifted from biography to Russia, as Simpson and Fritsch began digging into Trump's business dealings with Russian oligarchs.

## Case: The Investigative Method
Fusion GPS's method was journalistic: interviews, document requests, public records research, and source development. Simpson and Fritsch met with former intelligence officials, Russian-speaking journalists, and business associates of Trump. They traveled to Europe to interview sources. The result was not a single document but a body of research that informed Steele's memos and was shared with reporters from various news organizations.

## Case: The Legal Defense
The authors argue that their research was legitimate investigation, not political dirty tricks. They point out that they provided information to journalists from multiple outlets (The New York Times, The Washington Post, The Wall Street Journal) — not just to political allies. They also note that many of the dossier's core claims about Trump's business ties to Russia have been corroborated by other reporting, even if the specific allegations about collusion remain unproven.
