# Reference: The Steele Dossier

## Overview
The Steele dossier is the most controversial intelligence document in modern American politics. A series of 17 memos written by a former British spy, it alleged extensive connections between Donald Trump and the Russian government. Its creation, funding, and leak transformed the 2016 election and its aftermath.

## Case: Christopher Steele
Steele was a highly regarded MI6 officer who had spent decades studying Russian intelligence. He was the MI6 station chief in Moscow and later the head of the Russia desk. After retiring, he co-founded Orbis Business Intelligence. Simpson and Fritsch hired Steele because of his unique expertise on Russia and his network of sources. Steele believed he had discovered a genuine national security threat and felt obligated to share his findings with the FBI.

## Case: The Funding Chain
The dossier was funded by the DNC and the Clinton campaign, through a chain that went: Perkins Coie (law firm) → Fusion GPS → Orbis (Steele's firm). The funding source would become the central controversy. When it was revealed in 2017, Trump supporters argued the entire dossier was a political hit job. The authors argue that opposition research is always funded by political opponents — the question is whether the content is true, not who paid for it.

## Case: The 17 Reports
Steele wrote 17 separate memos between June and December 2016. The first memo (June 20) described Trump's alleged "dirty tricks" against Hillary Clinton. Later memos escalated, describing alleged collusion between the Trump campaign and Russian intelligence, compromising information (the "pee tape"), and money laundering. The memos varied in quality: some contained specific, reportable information; others were raw rumor from unidentified sources.
