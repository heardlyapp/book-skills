# Reference: The Media and the Dossier

## Overview
The dossier's leak to the media was as controversial as its contents. The book describes a complex dance between Fusion GPS, journalists, and government sources.

## Case: The Leak
The dossier was leaked to multiple journalists in the fall of 2016. The authors describe meeting with journalists from major news organizations and sharing their findings without revealing their full source. The most controversial meeting was with Ken Dilanian at NBC News. The exact chain of events that led to the dossier's publication has never been fully documented.

## Case: The BuzzFeed Publication
On January 10, 2017, BuzzFeed News published the entire 35-page dossier. Editor Ben Smith argued that the dossier was newsworthy because it was circulating among government officials and being reported on by other outlets. Published alongside a story about a classified briefing that mentioned the dossier, the full document went viral. Critics argued that publishing raw, unverified intelligence was irresponsible.

## Case: The Media Firestorm
After BuzzFeed's publication, the dossier became the dominant story. News organizations rushed to report on its contents while simultaneously questioning its veracity. Trump attacked the press for publishing what he called "fake news." The authors argue that the media covered the dossier badly — focusing on the most salacious allegations (the "pee tape") rather than the more substantive claims about business ties to Russia.
