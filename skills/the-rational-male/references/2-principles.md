# Principles of the Rational Male

## I. The Cardinal Rule of Relationships

"Never abandon your frame for a woman's emotions." This is the foundational principle. A woman may test a man's frame with emotional outbursts, tears, threats, or silent treatment. The correct response is not to mirror her emotion but to maintain composure.

**Case: The Test.** A woman says "I don't think this is working." She is testing his frame. The AFC responds by panicking, pleading, promising to change. The rational male responds by saying "I'm sorry you feel that way" and continuing about his day. The frame is maintained.

**Case: The Silent Treatment.** When a woman gives the silent treatment, she wants the man to chase her. The rational male does not chase. He uses the silence as an opportunity to focus on his own life.

## II. There Is No "One"

The soulmate myth is one of the most damaging beliefs men can hold. It creates ONEitis — a pathological attachment to a single woman who may not deserve or reciprocate that attachment.

**Case: The Fantasy vs. The Reality.** A man obsesses over a woman he barely knows, building a fantasy version of her in his mind. He puts her on a pedestal. When she fails to live up to his fantasy, he is crushed. But she never asked to be on the pedestal.

**Case: The Ex Who Got Away.** Many men remain emotionally invested in an ex-partner for years after the relationship ended. Tomassi argues that this is not love — it is ONEitis. The fixation persists because the man has not cultivated abundance.

## III. Hypergamy Does Not Care

Hypergamy is not fair, not kind, and not negotiable. It does not care that a man is a "good guy" who treats women well. It responds to value, not virtue.

**Case: The Loyal Beta.** A man who is reliable, faithful, hardworking, and emotionally available may still lose his wife to a man who is less "good" but higher in Alpha traits. Tomassi's explanation: hypergamy values leadership, confidence, and social status over kindness and reliability.

**Case: The "Good" Man Who Can't Get a Date.** Tomassi addresses the common complaint: "I'm a nice guy, why can't I find a girlfriend?" The answer: niceness is not a mating strategy. It is baseline decency. What attracts women is not goodness but value — and value is communicated through frame, status, and behavior, not intentions.

## IV. The Contextual Alpha

Alpha is not a fixed personality type. It is a context-dependent expression of leadership, decisiveness, and frame control. The same man may be Alpha in one context and Beta in another.

**Case: The CEO at Home.** A man who runs a company with authority (Alpha at work) may be completely Beta at home — deferring to his wife, seeking her approval, surrendering his frame. Tomassi's point: Alpha is contextual. The same man needs to learn to bring his Alpha frame home.

**Case: The Beta Professional.** A man who is deferential and cooperative at work (Beta) may need to learn how to switch to Alpha in his romantic life. The skills are learnable.

## V. Game Is the Practical Application

Game is not manipulation. It is the practical application of understanding intergender dynamics. It is knowing what to do and when to do it.

**Case: The Approach.** Game is not a pickup line. It is the frame you carry into an interaction. A man with game approaches a woman not from a place of seeking her approval but from a place of abundance. He is screening her as much as she is screening him.

**Case: The Relationship.** Game does not end when the relationship begins. In fact, it becomes more important. The man who stops gaming his woman — stops leading, stops maintaining frame, stops being the man she was attracted to — will eventually lose her.
