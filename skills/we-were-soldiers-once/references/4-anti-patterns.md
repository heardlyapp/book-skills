# Anti-Patterns — We Were Soldiers Once...And Young

## The Central Error: Believing War Has a Simple Lesson

"Ia Drang was to Vietnam what the Spanish Civil War was to WWII: a dress rehearsal." Both sides studied Ia Drang and drew conclusions. Both were wrong. The US believed airmobility and firepower could win ("search and destroy"). The NVA believed they could stand and fight ("fight and talk"). Both conclusions led to a decade of death.

### Mistake 1: "Technology wins wars"
The Huey helicopter was revolutionary — but it could not win against a determined enemy who was willing to take losses. Ia Drang proved that high-tech weaponry could dominate a battlefield — but not win a war.

### Mistake 2: "Body count = victory"
The US claimed victory because they killed more NVA. But the NVA kept coming. "The enemy had been hurt badly, but he was not broken, not even close."

### Mistake 3: "War is a movie"
"Holleywood got it wrong every damned time, whetting twisted political knives on the bones of our dead brothers." The reality: soldiers did not "get up and walk away." War is not cinematic.

### Mistake 4: "The troops were all heroes"
The book makes clear that many soldiers were draftees who didn't want to be there. "They fought because they had no choice, and they died because that's what happens in war."

### Mistake 5: "You can't trust the media"
Galloway was a reporter on the ground. He carried a rifle when necessary. He helped wounded men. He is the co-author. "The media were not the enemy. The enemy was the enemy."

### Mistake 6: "Friendly fire means someone was negligent"
Friendly fire happened at Ia Drang. It happens in every battle. "In the fog of war, bombs fall on the wrong coordinates." The lesson is not blame — it's the nature of combat.

### Mistake 7: "The war was lost because of politics at home"
Moore and Galloway do not make this argument. "We answered the call of one president who was now dead; we followed the orders of another who would be hounded from office." They focus on the soldiers, not the politics.
