# Reference: Lou Salomé

## Overview
Lou Salomé is the catalyst. She is brilliant, beautiful, and utterly independent.

## Case: The Summons
Lou writes Breuer an impertinent note. She summons him to Venice. She commands his attention. She is a force of nature.

## Case: The Love Triangle
Both Nietzsche and Paul Rée proposed to Lou. She refused both. She wants friendship, not marriage. She is ahead of her time.

## Case: The Intervention
Lou knows Nietzsche is suicidal. She cannot save him alone. She brings Breuer into the situation. She orchestrates the healing.

## Case: The Witness
Lou represents the future. She is a woman who will not be contained by society's expectations. She is the kind of person both men are learning to become.

## Case: The Legacy
Lou Salomé is a historical figure. She became a psychoanalyst, a writer, and a friend to Freud. The novel captures her brilliance.

## Case: The Beauty
Breuer is immediately struck by Lou's beauty. But it is her mind that captivates him. She is his equal intellectually. She challenges him.

## Case: The Departure
Lou leaves Vienna after setting the therapy in motion. She has done her work. The men must heal themselves. She moves on.
Lou Salomé is not a love interest or a plot device. She is the architect of the healing. Without her, neither man would have been saved.
She is, in a sense, the first existential therapist.
Lou Salomé: the woman who could not be possessed, who refused to be anyone's muse, who insisted on being herself.
