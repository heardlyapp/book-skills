# Reference: Friedrich Nietzsche

## Overview
Nietzsche in the novel is 38, brilliant, and in despair. He is the philosopher who cannot save himself.

## Case: The Condition
Nietzsche suffers from debilitating migraines. He cannot work. He cannot read. He is in constant pain. He believes his life is over.

## Case: The Isolation
Nietzsche has no friends. No one understands him. Lou Salomé rejected his proposal. He is alone. He believes he will die alone.

## Case: The Philosophy
Nietzsche's philosophy is his lifeline. Eternal recurrence. The will to power. Amor fati. These ideas are not abstract — they are how he survives.

## Case: The Resistance
Nietzsche does not want to be treated. He is proud. He is stubborn. He believes no one can help him. Breuer must earn his trust.

## Case: The Transformation
Nietzsche discovers that he can be understood. Breuer's vulnerability opens him. He weeps. He is not alone. He writes Zarathustra.

## Case: The Tears
Nietzsche weeps. The title. He has never let anyone see him cry. Breuer's honesty breaks through his defenses.

## Case: The Farewell
Nietzsche leaves Breuer. He is healed enough to return to his work. He writes his greatest book. The friendship that healed him becomes the foundation of his philosophy.
Nietzsche is the novel's proof that even the greatest mind needs connection. Philosophy cannot save you alone. You need another person.
When Nietzsche weeps, he becomes human. And that is his salvation.
The philosopher who said God is dead discovers that he cannot live without human love.
