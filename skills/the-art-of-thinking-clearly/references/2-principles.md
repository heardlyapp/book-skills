# Principles · Risk · Probability · Base Rates

> Source: *The Art of Thinking Clearly*, chapters on probability and risk

---

## Availability Bias (Ch. 1)

We overweigh vivid, recent, or emotionally charged events. Plane crashes get more coverage than car crashes, so we overestimate the risk of flying. **Fix:** Replace "what comes to mind" with "what do the statistics say?"

## Probability Neglect

We ignore probabilities when emotions are high. We'll pay anything to reduce a tiny risk if the outcome is terrifying. **Fix:** Always ask: "What are the actual odds?"

## Base Rate Fallacy (Ch. 18)

We ignore general statistics (base rates) and focus on specific information. A doctor may focus on a patient's symptoms and forget how rare the disease actually is. **Fix:** Start with the base rate. How often does this thing happen in general? Then adjust for specifics.

## Survivorship Bias (Ch. 2)

We only see the winners and draw conclusions from them. Failed startups aren't in the news. Failed actors aren't giving speeches. **Fix:** Seek out the silent evidence — the stories of those who tried and failed.
