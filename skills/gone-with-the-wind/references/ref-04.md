# Reference: Survival

## Overview
Survival is the novel's central theme. Scarlett will do anything to survive and protect Tara.

## Case: The Oath
Standing in the devastated fields of Tara, starving and exhausted, Scarlett makes an oath: "As God is my witness, I'll never be hungry again." This moment defines her character for the rest of the novel.

## Case: The Pragmatist
Scarlett marries for money, runs a business, and uses every tool available. She is not sentimental about the past. She adapts. She is the only character who fully embraces the new world.

## Case: The Cost of Survival
Scarlett's survival comes at a cost. She loses her innocence, her friends (many of them), and eventually Rhett. She becomes ruthless. The novel asks: is survival worth the price?

## Case: Ashley's Failure
Ashley cannot adapt. He clings to the past, to the old ways. He survives only through Scarlett's help and Melanie's love. He represents what happens to those who refuse to face reality.

## Case: Melanie's Strength
Melanie is physically fragile but morally strong. She is the quiet hero of the novel. She holds the community together through kindness and courage. Her death is the loss of the South's best self.

## Case: The Conclusion
Scarlett survives. She returns to Tara. The land will sustain her. She will think about Rhett tomorrow. The novel ends with hope and uncertainty.
Tomorrow is another day — the most famous line in American literature.
Scarlett O'Hara will survive. That is the one thing she has always known how to do.
