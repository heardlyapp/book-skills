# Anti-Patterns — The Lies We've Been Sold

> Source: Metabolical, Chapters 4, 6, 22, 23, 24

## The Six Fatal Beliefs

### 1. The Calorie Dogma
**Error**: "A calorie is a calorie. Weight loss is about calories in, calories out."
**Reality**: A fructose calorie is metabolically different from a protein calorie. Biochemistry > physics. The calorie model is an oversimplification that benefits the food industry.

### 2. The "Everything in Moderation" Lie
**Error**: "All foods can fit in a healthy diet."
**Reality**: This message was created by the food industry to sell processed food. Some foods (ultra-processed) are not food at all.

### 3. The Fat-Phobia
**Error**: "Fat makes you fat. Eat low-fat to be healthy."
**Reality**: The low-fat era (1980s-2010s) was a disaster — companies replaced fat with sugar, and obesity and diabetes exploded.

### 4. The Blame the Patient
**Error**: "Chronic disease is the patient's fault — they lack willpower."
**Reality**: Processed food is engineered to be addictive. It hijacks the brain's reward system the same way drugs do.

### 5. The USDA/FDA Complicity
**Error**: "Government agencies protect our health."
**Reality**: The USDA promotes the food industry (its mandate includes promoting agriculture). The FDA is captured by the industries it regulates.

### 6. The "Healthy" on the Label
**Error**: "If it says 'healthy' on the package, it is healthy."
**Reality**: The most "healthy" foods have no labels. The foods with labels and health claims are the ones to avoid.

> **Case: Sugar Addiction** (Chapter 21)
> Lustig argues that sugar meets all the criteria for an addictive substance: it triggers dopamine release in the brain's reward center, it causes cravings, it produces withdrawal, and tolerance develops over time (you need more to get the same effect). Brain imaging studies show that sugar activates the same neural pathways as cocaine. The food industry knows this — and designs products to maximize "bliss point" (the perfect amount of sugar to trigger addiction without causing sensory aversion).
> **Key takeaway:** Sugar is not just unhealthy — it is addictive. The food industry uses this addiction to sell products. The parallel to tobacco is exact: an addictive substance, sold by companies that deny the harm, regulated by captured agencies.

> **Case: The Fraud of "Processed Food"** (Chapter 22)
> Lustig defines food fraud as: "the intentional adulteration of food for economic gain." He argues that most ultra-processed food is fraud: it claims to be food but is not. It has been stripped of beneficial components (fiber, micronutrients, phytonutrients) and adulterated with harmful ones (sugar, industrial fats, chemical additives). This is not "food" — it is an "edible food-like substance." The fraud is legal, but it is still fraud.
> **Key takeaway:** The food industry is not selling food. It is selling shelf-stable, profitable, addictive consumable products that happen to look like food. Recognizing this is the first step toward escaping the trap.
