# Protect the Liver, Feed the Gut

> Source: Metabolical, Chapters 8, 9, 11, 25

## The Two Precepts

### What Each Means

| Precept | What to Avoid | What to Eat |
|---------|---------------|-------------|
| Protect the liver | Sugar (fructose), refined carbs, alcohol, trans fats | Healthy fats (olive oil, avocado, nuts), protein, fiber |
| Feed the gut | Processed foods, artificial sweeteners, emulsifiers | Fiber (vegetables, legumes, whole intact grains), fermented foods |

> **Case: The Liver and Fructose** (Chapter 8)
> The liver is the organ that processes fructose. Unlike glucose, which can be used by any cell in the body, fructose must be processed by the liver. When you eat an apple, the fiber slows absorption, and the liver can handle the small fructose load. When you drink a soda, the fructose hits the liver all at once. The liver has two choices: convert it to fat (lipogenesis) or let it build up. The fat accumulates in the liver, causing Non-Alcoholic Fatty Liver Disease (NAFLD). This is the root of metabolic syndrome.
> **Key takeaway:** Fructose from whole fruit is fine. Fructose from processed food (soda, juice, HFCS) is a liver toxin. The difference is dose, speed, and the presence of fiber. Protect your liver — limit added sugar.

> **Case: The Gut Microbiome** (Chapters 11, 25)
> Your gut contains trillions of bacteria that regulate your immune system, your mood, and your metabolism. These bacteria eat fiber. When you don't eat enough fiber, your gut bacteria starve. They start eating the mucus lining of your intestine. This creates "leaky gut" — a permeable intestinal barrier that allows bacterial fragments to enter the bloodstream, triggering systemic inflammation. This inflammation drives insulin resistance, obesity, and chronic disease.
> **Key takeaway:** Feeding your gut bacteria is not optional — it is essential. The single best thing you can do for your gut health is eat more fiber from whole plant foods. Your gut bacteria will thank you by keeping you healthy.
