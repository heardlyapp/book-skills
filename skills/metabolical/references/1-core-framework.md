# Processed Food as Poison

> Source: Metabolical, Introduction, Chapters 1, 11, 17-22

## Core Framework

### The Biochemistry of Processing

| What they do | Why it matters |
|-------------|----------------|
| Remove fiber | Slows digestion, prevents overeating |
| Add sugar | Overloads liver, drives insulin resistance |
| Add industrial fats | Creates inflammation |
| Alter food matrix | Changes how your body processes it |

> **Case: The Apple vs. Apple Juice** (Chapters 11, 18)
> An apple is real food. It contains fiber (pectin), fructose, vitamins, and water in a natural matrix. Your body digests it slowly, the liver handles the fructose without overload, and the gut bacteria get fed by the fiber. Apple juice — same fruit, same sugar — but the fiber is gone. Now the fructose hits your liver all at once, creating fat, driving insulin resistance, and damaging liver cells. The Nutrition Facts label on both products lists similar sugar content. The difference is processing.
> **Key takeaway:** The Nutrition Facts label is useless for distinguishing real food from processed food. The label tells you what's IN the food. It doesn't tell you what's been DONE to the food. Processing matters more than composition.

> **Case: The Food Matrix** (Chapters 17, 20)
> Lustig introduces the concept of the "food matrix" — the three-dimensional structure of real food that determines how it is digested and metabolized. Processing destroys the matrix. When almonds are ground into almond butter, the fat becomes more accessible. When whole wheat is ground into white flour, the starch becomes rapidly digestible. When fruit is juiced, the sugars become free. The matrix is not listed on any label — but it determines the metabolic effect of the food.
> **Key takeaway:** Real food has an intact matrix. Processed food has a destroyed matrix. Your body processes them differently. The same ingredients, processed differently, produce different metabolic outcomes. This is why "a calorie is a calorie" is wrong.
