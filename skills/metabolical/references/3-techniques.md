# The Failure of Modern Medicine

> Source: Metabolical, Chapters 1-6, 9, 10

## Why Doctors Don't Know Nutrition

### The Problem

| Issue | Explanation |
|-------|-------------|
| No nutrition education | Most medical schools spend <25 hours on nutrition in 4 years |
| Pharma-funded research | Drug trials fund medicine, not diet studies |
| Treatment over cure | Pills are profitable; lifestyle advice is not |
| Symptom focus | Treating cholesterol, not the dietary cause |

> **Case: The "Treatment" of Type 2 Diabetes** (Chapters 1, 10)
> Type 2 diabetes is a dietary disease: it is caused by chronic consumption of sugar and refined carbohydrates that drive insulin resistance. But the medical system does not treat the cause — it treats the symptoms. Patients are given metformin, then insulin, then more drugs — each managing a symptom while the underlying metabolic dysfunction worsens. Lustig argues that most type 2 diabetes can be reversed with dietary change (low sugar, high fiber, real food). But doctors are not trained to prescribe this — and the system does not pay for it.
> **Key takeaway:** Treatment is not cure. Modern medicine is excellent at managing acute illness and trauma. It is terrible at preventing or reversing chronic disease. The reason: it is built on a pharmaceutical model that treats symptoms, not causes.

> **Case: The Dietitian Problem** (Chapter 4)
> Dietitians are supposed to be the nutrition experts. But Lustig argues that the dietetics profession has been captured by the food industry. The Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics receives millions in sponsorship from Coca-Cola, PepsiCo, and processed food companies. Dietitians are taught the calorie dogma — "all foods fit in moderation." This is the food industry's preferred message. The result: dietitians often recommend processed foods and low-fat products that are worse than what they replace.
> **Key takeaway:** Even the "experts" have been compromised. The institutions that are supposed to guide us on nutrition have been corrupted by industry money. You cannot trust the establishment to tell you the truth about food.
