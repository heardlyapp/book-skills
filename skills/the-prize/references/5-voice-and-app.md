# Author's Voice + Key Scenarios

> **Case: Yergin's Epic** (Introduction): Daniel Yergin spent years researching The Prize, interviewing industry leaders, diplomats, and historians. The book won the Pulitzer Prize. It remains the definitive history of oil.
> **Key takeaway**: Yergin's authority is unmatched. The Prize is the gold standard for oil history.

> **Case: The End of an Era** (Conclusion): Yergin concludes that oil will remain central to the global economy, but the dynamics are shifting. The end of the Cold War, new production regions, and environmental concerns will reshape the industry.
> **Key takeaway**: While oil's supremacy continues, the industry is constantly evolving. Understanding its history helps understand its future.

## Key Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Understanding Oil's Role
Oil is at the center of modern history — wars, economies, and geopolitics.

### Scenario 2: Oil in War
From WWII to the Gulf War, oil has driven military strategy and conflict.

### Scenario 3: Oil and the Economy
Oil prices affect inflation, employment, and growth. Shocks cause recessions.

### Scenario 4: OPEC's Role
OPEC controls significant supply but has limits. Internal divisions weaken it.

### Scenario 5: The Future of Oil
Alternatives exist but oil remains dominant. The cycle continues.
