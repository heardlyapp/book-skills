# Core Framework: The Epic History of Oil

## The Central Thesis

"Oil built the modern world. It fueled the wars that shaped it. It created fortunes beyond imagination and brought empires to their knees. Understanding oil is understanding the 20th century."

> **Case: Rockefeller's Standard Oil** (Part 1): John D. Rockefeller didn't invent oil — he invented the modern oil industry. Through ruthless efficiency, vertical integration, and crushing competition, Standard Oil controlled 90% of U.S. refining. The company was broken up in 1911 — but its pieces became Exxon, Mobil, Chevron, and others.
> **Key takeaway**: The structure of the modern oil industry was shaped by one man. Rockefeller's legacy is not just wealth — it's the corporate model of the modern world.

## Key Concepts

| Concept | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| Standard Oil | Rockefeller's monopoly, controlling 90% of refining |
| Seven Sisters | Exxon, Shell, BP, Chevron, Mobil, Gulf, Texaco |
| OPEC | Producer cartel controlling ~40% of global oil |
| Resource Curse | Paradox: oil-rich nations often have worse development outcomes |
| Nationalization | Governments seizing oil assets from foreign companies |

> **Case: The Iran Crisis** (Part 2): In 1953, Iran nationalized its oil industry. Britain and the U.S. responded with a coup, overthrowing the democratically elected prime minister. Iran's oil stayed under Western control — for a while. This event created decades of mistrust and laid the groundwork for the 1979 revolution.
> **Key takeaway**: Oil has driven covert operations, regime changes, and decades-long conflicts. The struggle for oil control has shaped the Middle East's modern political landscape.
