# Techniques — The Complete Guide to Breast Cancer

## How to Navigate Breast Cancer

### Technique 1: The Expert Patient Approach
Learn about your specific cancer type. "You probably don't even know where to start reading." Start with the type, stage, and receptor status of your cancer. Case: "Most breast cancer patients become 'experts' within a few months of diagnosis."

### Technique 2: The Two-Phase Framework
Recognize which phase you're in: active treatment or survivorship. Different challenges apply to each. Case: "Understanding which phase you're in helps you focus on what matters now."

### Technique 3: The Question List
Write down questions before each appointment. "You will have a lot of questions and may forget them." Bring a list. Case: "Ask your surgeon: What type of cancer? What stage? What are my treatment options?"

### Technique 4: The Support Network
"Where to get support" (Chapter 14). Build a team: medical team, family, friends, support groups, online communities. Case: The authors met on Twitter — online support can be life-changing.

### Technique 5: The Body Image Check-In
After surgery and chemo, your body changes. Check in with yourself regularly. "Coping with changes to your body" (Chapter 15) is an ongoing process. Case: "Look at your scars. Touch them. They are part of your story."

### Technique 6: The Relationship Talk
"Relationships and sex" (Chapter 17). Communicate openly with your partner. Treatment affects intimacy. Case: "It's normal for sex to be the last thing on your mind during treatment."

### Technique 7: The Moving Forward Plan
After active treatment ends, make a plan. Surveillance schedule, healthy lifestyle, fear of recurrence management. Case: "Staying healthy during and after treatment" (Chapter 18) is a lifelong commitment.
