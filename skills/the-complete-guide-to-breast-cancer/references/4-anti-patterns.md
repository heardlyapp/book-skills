# Anti-Patterns — The Complete Guide to Breast Cancer

## The Central Error: "I Caused My Cancer"

"Getting breast cancer is not your fault." Many patients blame themselves. The authors are emphatic: the most common causes are being female and getting older. Case: "Liz was slim, exercised, and still got breast cancer. It's not your fault."

### Mistake 1: "I Don't Need to Learn About My Cancer"
"I'll just let the doctors decide." Passive patients feel less in control. Information is empowering. Case: "Knowledge is power — the more you know, the more you can participate in decisions."

### Mistake 2: "I'll Be the Same Person After Treatment"
"You will never return to being a non-cancer patient." Life is different. Expecting to return to exactly how you were leads to frustration. Case: "Moving forward means finding a new normal."

### Mistake 3: "All Information Is Reliable"
"Not all of the information out there is accurate, and some of it may be very scary." Stick to trusted sources. Case: "Use this book as a map, alongside reputable charity websites."

### Mistake 4: "I Should Suffer in Silence"
"Many patients feel they should be strong and not complain." Share your feelings. Ask for help. Case: "Coping emotionally is as important as medical treatment."

### Mistake 5: "Treatment Is the Same for Everyone"
"Your breast cancer is unique." Receptor status, stage, genetics, age, and overall health all affect your treatment plan. Case: "What worked for your friend may not work for you."

### Mistake 6: "After Treatment, It's Over"
"Living after active treatment has its own challenges." Fear of recurrence, follow-up appointments, managing long-term side effects. Case: "Survivorship is a phase, not an ending."
