# Key Principles — The Complete Guide to Breast Cancer

## I. You Are Not Alone

"It can feel like your world has collapsed, leaving you feeling powerless and alone. But you are not alone." The authors have been there. Case: "We met on Twitter and became 'virtual chemo buddies.'"

## II. Getting Cancer Is Not Your Fault

"Getting breast cancer is not your fault." The two biggest risk factors are being a woman and getting older. Case: "Most breast cancers happen in women over 50."

## III. Knowledge Is Power

"You will soon become an expert in how you feel." Information gathering is empowering. Case: "This book is intended to complement your own exploration of the information out there."

## IV. Two Phases of Life

"For the rest of your life, you will be in one of two phases: active treatment or survivorship." Understanding this helps set expectations. Case: "You will never return to being a non-cancer patient."

## V. Patient Experience Complements Medical Knowledge

"Doctors tell you what will happen, but patients show you how to cope." The book uniquely combines both. Case: The authors' personal experience of chemo, surgery, and radiotherapy informs every chapter.

## VI. Treatment Is Highly Individual

"Your breast cancer is unique to you." There is no one-size-fits-all approach. Case: "Even if you are a medical expert in breast cancer yourself (like Liz was), you have never been a breast cancer patient before."

## VII. Life After Treatment Is a New Normal

"Most people who get breast cancer go on to live for many years." But life is different. Finding a new normal is the goal. Case: "You will never return to being a non-cancer patient, but you can live well."
