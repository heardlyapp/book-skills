# 3 — Key Battles and Combat Techniques

> Source: "Beyond Band of Brothers" by Major Dick Winters

## D-Day / Brecourt Manor (June 6, 1944)

Objective: Destroy German artillery firing on Utah Beach. 12 men vs. 50+ with four howitzers. Result: all four guns destroyed. Winters received the Distinguished Service Cross.

## Carentan (June 1944)

The hedgerow country. Every field was a fortress. Easy Company cleared the town street by street. Losses mounted. Winters learned: in urban combat, speed and aggression save lives.

## Operation Market Garden (Holland, September 1944)

The bridge too far. Easy Company held the "Island" between the Waal and Rhine rivers. Constant patrols, constant shelling. Winters: "We were old beyond our years."

## Bastogne (December 1944)

The Battle of the Bulge. Surrounded. Freezing. Out of ammunition. Out of food. Winters kept the perimeter intact. His calm during the artillery barrages kept the men from breaking. When asked if they'd surrender, the acting commander replied: "Nuts."

> **Case: The Perimeter** (Chapter 10): Winters walked the line constantly. He checked positions. He talked to every man. His presence alone held the line. When supplies finally came, Easy Company was still standing.
> **Key takeaway**: In a siege, the leader's visibility is the most critical resource. If the men see their leader is calm, they can be calm.
