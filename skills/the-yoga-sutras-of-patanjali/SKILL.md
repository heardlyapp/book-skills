## Philosophy (4 Rules to Remember)

1. **Yoga is the restraint of the modifications of the mind.** Everything else — poses, breathing, rituals — is preparation for this single goal.

2. **The mind is a mirror. When it is still and clean, you see your true Self. When it is agitated or dirty, you see distortion.**

3. **You are not your thoughts. You are the Seer who watches the thoughts.** Realizing this distinction is the entire path.

4. **The world is neither good nor bad. Your mental attitude makes it so. Change the mind, change everything.**

## Core Framework Quick Reference

- **Yoga Defined: Citta Vritti Nirodhah** — The restraint of the modifications of the mind-stuff is Yoga. This is Patanjali's complete definition. Everything else in the text explains how to achieve this.
- **The Five Vrittis (Modifications)** — Right knowledge (pramana), wrong knowledge (viparyaya), imagination (vikalpa), sleep (nidra), and memory (smriti). All five can be either painful (klishta) or non-painful (aklishta) depending on context.
- **The Five Kleshas (Afflictions)** — Avidya (ignorance of the real), asmita (egoism), raga (attachment), dvesha (aversion), abhinivesha (clinging to life). These are the root causes of suffering.
- **The Eight Limbs (Ashtanga)** — Yama (restraints), niyama (observances), asana (posture), pranayama (breath control), pratyahara (sense withdrawal), dharana (concentration), dhyana (meditation), samadhi (absorption).
- **The Seer and the Seen** — The Seer (Purusha, pure consciousness) is distinct from the seen (Prakriti, nature including mind and body). Suffering arises from confusing the two. Liberation comes from understanding their distinction.
- **Practice and Detachment (Abhyasa and Vairagya)** — Two complementary disciplines: sustained practice and non-attachment. One without the other is incomplete.
- **The Four Books (Padas)** — Samadhi Pada (goal), Sadhana Pada (practice), Vibhuti Pada (powers), Kaivalya Pada (liberation). A complete path from where you are to full freedom.

## Key Principles (7 Rules)

1. **You are not your thoughts.** — The first and most important realization. Thoughts arise and pass. You are the one who watches them. Identify with the watcher, not the thought.

2. **Practice must be sustained over a long time without interruption.** — Patanjali says: practice becomes firmly grounded when it is done for a long time, without break, and with sincere devotion. There are no shortcuts.

3. **Non-attachment is as important as practice.** — You can practice yoga for decades, but if you're attached to results, you won't progress. Practice and detachment are the two wings of the same bird.

4. **The obstacles to yoga are also part of the path.** — Kleshas are not enemies to be destroyed but conditions to be understood. Their presence is not failure — it's an invitation to go deeper.

5. **Ethics come before meditation.** — Yama and niyama (ethical precepts) are the first two limbs for a reason. You cannot still a guilty mind. Purify conduct before attempting higher practices.

6. **The body is a tool, not the goal.** — Asana (posture) is the third limb — not the first. Physical yoga is preparation for sitting in meditation. It's not the destination.

7. **The highest state is not an experience — it's a realization.** — Samadhi is not a peak experience you have. It's the recognition of what you always were, beyond the mind and its modifications.

## Anti-Pattern Summary

The central error the Yoga Sutras correct is the belief that yoga is primarily physical exercise, stress relief, or spiritual entertainment — when the classical tradition defines it as a complete science of mind control aimed at the permanent realization of the true Self beyond the modifications of thought.
