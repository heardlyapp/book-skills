# Core Framework: The Customer Development Model

## The Central Insight

"The Product Development model is the path to disaster. The Customer Development model is the path to epiphany." Blank's framework replaces the traditional "build → launch → hope" approach with a systematic customer-first method.

> **Case: The Dot-Com Cemetery** (Chapter 1): Blank describes the dot-com era where startups raised millions, built products in stealth for 18 months, and launched to crickets. They assumed they knew what customers wanted without ever asking. Most failed. The ones that succeeded had iterated with customers from day one.
> **Key takeaway**: Building a product without customer input is gambling, not entrepreneurship. The Product Development model assumes you know — and assumptions are not facts.

## The Four Steps

| Step | Goal | Key Question |
|------|------|-------------|
| Customer Discovery | Test problem/solution hypotheses | Do people need this? |
| Customer Validation | Test the sales model | Can we sell this repeatedly? |
| Customer Creation | Create demand | How do we scale? |
| Company Building | Build the organization | How do we execute? |

> **Case: The Apple Newton** (Chapter 2): Apple's Newton was a revolutionary product built in secret. It failed because Apple didn't validate the market first. Years later, the Palm Pilot succeeded by iterating with customers — it started as a simple device and improved based on feedback. The Newton was built; the Palm Pilot was developed with customers.
> **Key takeaway**: Even Apple can fail when they skip customer validation. The difference between the Newton and the Palm Pilot was not technology — it was customer development.
