# Author's Voice + Key Scenarios

> **Case: Blank's Entrepreneurial Background** (Introduction): Steve Blank is a serial entrepreneur who founded or worked in eight startups before writing this book. He taught Customer Development at Stanford, Berkeley, and Columbia. His framework emerged from his own failures and successes.
> **Key takeaway**: Blank's framework is not academic theory — it's the distilled wisdom of his entrepreneurial experience. He failed using the old model before creating the new one.

> **Case: The Lean Startup Connection** (Conclusion): Eric Ries's Lean Startup is based on Blank's Customer Development model. Ries built on Blank's framework, adding the build-measure-learn loop and MVP concept.
> **Key takeaway**: The Customer Development model is the foundation of modern startup methodology. It has shaped how thousands of startups are built.

## Key Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Validating an Idea
Use Customer Discovery interviews. Talk to 30+ potential customers. Look for patterns.

### Scenario 2: Building an MVP
Identify the core problem. Build the simplest solution. Test with real customers.

### Scenario 3: Deciding to Pivot
Review customer feedback. Are you solving the right problem? If not, pivot.

### Scenario 4: Scaling
Only scale after you've validated the business model. Premature scaling is the #1 startup killer.

### Scenario 5: Raising Money
Investors want to see traction, not ideas. Use Customer Development to prove traction.
