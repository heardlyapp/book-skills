# Anti-Patterns

## 1. Building Before Validating

**The mistake**: Spending months building a product before talking to customers.

**Fix**: Customer discovery must come before product development. No exceptions.

## 2. Confusing Beta Testers with Customers

**The mistake**: Believing that free beta users validate your business model.

**Fix**: Real validation requires someone paying money. Free users prove nothing.

> **Case: The Free User Pitfall** (Chapter 4): A startup had 100,000 free users but couldn't convert any to paying customers. They had built something people would try but not pay for. The free users gave them false validation.
> **Key takeaway**: Free usage is not validation. The only signal that matters is willingness to pay.

> **Case: The Stealth Mode Mistake** (Chapter 1): A startup spent 18 months in stealth mode building a product. They launched with great press coverage. Nobody bought it. They had never talked to a single customer during development. The stealth mode that was supposed to protect them actually doomed them.
>
> **Key takeaway**: Stealth mode is a vanity trap. There is no competitive advantage in secrecy when you haven't validated the market. Talk to customers openly, learn fast, and adjust.

## 3. Ignoring the Sales Model

**The mistake**: Focusing only on product features, never on how to sell.

**Fix**: Customer validation must prove that you have a repeatable, scalable sales process — not just that people want the product.

## 4. Premature Scaling

**The mistake**: Hiring sales teams and building infrastructure before finding product-market fit.

**Fix**: Stay in learning mode until you've validated the business model. Then scale.

## 5. Falling in Love with Your Solution

**The mistake**: Being so attached to your solution that you ignore evidence that it's wrong.

**Fix**: Be in love with the problem, not your solution. The solution will change many times.
