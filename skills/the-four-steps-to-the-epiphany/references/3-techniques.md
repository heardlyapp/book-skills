# Techniques: The Customer Development Practice

## The Customer Discovery Interview

1. Identify your target customer persona
2. Prepare hypotheses about their problem
3. Talk to 10-15 potential customers — don't pitch, listen
4. Ask open-ended questions about their current behavior
5. Look for patterns across interviews
6. Refine your hypotheses based on what you learn

> **Case: The Interview That Changed Everything** (Chapter 6): A startup founder was convinced her target customer was small business owners. After 10 customer interviews, she discovered that her real customers were not owners but operations managers. The pivot was painful but saved years of wasted effort.
> **Key takeaway**: Your initial assumptions about who the customer is are probably wrong. Talk to people early and adjust.

> **Case: The MVP That Was Too MVP** (Chapter 7): A startup launched an MVP that was so minimal it didn't solve any real problem. Customers tried it once and never returned. The startup learned that MVP doesn't mean "barely works" — it means "solves the core problem in the simplest way."
>
> **Key takeaway**: Your MVP must actually solve a real problem, even if simply. A product that barely works teaches you nothing because customers won't use it.

## The Minimum Viable Product (MVP)

1. Identify the core problem your product solves
2. Build the simplest version that solves that problem
3. Test with real customers
4. Measure: do they use it? Do they pay?
5. Iterate based on feedback, not opinions

## The Pivot Decision

When to pivot:
- Customer interviews consistently reveal a different problem than you're solving
- Early adopters don't use your product as expected
- The sales cycle is not repeatable
- Growth has stalled despite product improvements

## The Customer Validation Test

- Can you find customers without spending heavily on marketing?
- Do customers understand your value proposition without explanation?
- Do customers come back and bring others?
- Is the sales process repeatable?
- Are customers willing to pay?
