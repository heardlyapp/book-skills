# Anti-Patterns — Walking with the Wind

## The Central Error: Believing That Change Happens Without Sacrifice

Change does not come through polite requests or court cases alone. Lewis: "There are times when more immediate, more dramatic means are called for, when the people themselves must be asked to put their own bodies and hearts and spirits on the line."

### Mistake 1: "Violence is the only language the oppressor understands"
The Black Muslim / Malcolm X position. Lewis respected Malcolm but rejected him. "I never accepted his ideas. I never had sympathy with violence of any sort." The entire movement proved that disciplined nonviolence — organizing people to sacrifice without striking back — was more powerful than any weapon.

### Mistake 2: "Older leaders know better — follow them"
Thurgood Marshall told Lewis to stop the Freedom Rides — "You'll get people killed." The NAACP leadership tried to slow the sit-ins. King's father told him not to join. Lewis: "Our revolt was as much against the traditional black leadership structure as it was against segregation." Sometimes the young must lead.

### Mistake 3: "If you wait, change will come through the system"
Kennedy asked for a "cooling-off period." Farmer: "We've been cooling off for a hundred years. If we get any cooler, we'll be in a deep freeze." Lewis: the courts and the law were too slow. Direct action — putting bodies on the line — was necessary to make change.

### Mistake 4: "Anger is a weakness"
Lewis was angry about sharecropping at age 6. "Gambling. This is nothing but gambling." That anger was fuel — but it had to be channeled. "The anger I felt as a child was a good anger, a healthy anger. It was a positive, constructive type of anger." The danger is not anger — it's undisciplined anger.

### Mistake 5: "Comfort is a reasonable expectation"
Lewis earned $10/week at SNCC. He slept in cars. He was jailed 40+ times. "Comfort was simply not a concern." The movement required everything. Those who held back — who wanted titles before they took risks — didn't change anything.

### Mistake 6: "Fame and credit are important"
Forman kept telling Lewis: "Get out front! Don't let King get all the credit." Lewis: "I just never thought that way. Let's get the job done, and there will be plenty of credit to go around." Focusing on credit leads to flash-in-the-pan leadership. "Fame never lasts. The work endures."

### Mistake 7: "The movement ends when you leave it"
SNCC grew militant. Lewis was "de-elected." He didn't give up on the movement — he found a new way to serve. Congress for 33 years. "The Beloved Community is not a destination. It's a direction. You keep walking."
