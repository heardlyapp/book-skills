# 3 — Techniques

> Source: *The Black Girl's Guide to Financial Freedom* by Paris Woods.

## 5 Tools

### 1. The Snowball Method

List debts smallest to largest. Pay minimum on all. Put every extra dollar toward the smallest until it's gone. Repeat. Momentum builds motivation.

### 2. The 50/30/20 Budget With FIRE Twist

50% needs → push lower. 30% wants → reduce to 10-20%. 20+% savings → increase to 40-50%+ for FIRE.

### 3. The Salary Negotiation Script

Research market rate. Ask for 10-20% more. Use "I'd like to discuss my salary. Based on my research, the market range is X to Y." Practice out loud.

> **Case: Paris's Own Raise** (Chapter 6): "I asked for a 20% increase. My boss said yes." You don't get what you don't ask for.

> **Case: The 401(k) Surprise** (Chapter 7): Paris discovered that her employer offered a 401(k) match she hadn't been using — free money left on the table. "I was leaving thousands of dollars on the table each year."
> **Key takeaway**: Check if your employer offers retirement matching. If you don't contribute enough to capture the full match, you are turning down free money.

For FIRE: accumulate 25× your annual expenses. Withdraw 4% annually. Historically, this lasts at least 30 years.

### 5. The House Hack

Buy a multi-unit property. Live in one unit. Rent the others. The rent covers your mortgage. Live for free while building equity.
