# 4 — Anti-Patterns

> Source: *The Black Girl's Guide to Financial Freedom* by Paris Woods.

## 5 Errors

### 1. The "I'll Start Investing When I Have More Money" Trap

You don't need much to start. $50/month in an index fund is enough. The habit matters more than the amount.

### 2. Lifestyle Creep

As your income rises, your spending rises with it. You buy a better car, a bigger apartment, nicer clothes. You stay broke at every income level.

> **Example: Harvard Paycheck to Paycheck** (Chapter 1): Paris was earning a good salary at Harvard — and still going deeper into debt. The problem wasn't income — it was spending.
> **Key takeaway**: Track where your money goes. A raise won't fix a spending problem until you know where the money is going.

> **Example: The $5 Latte Trap** (Paris's concept): "If you spend $5 on coffee every day, that's $150/month — $1,800/year. Invested over 30 years at 8%, that's over $200,000." Not to guilt you about coffee — to show the power of small changes.

Financial literacy must be passed on. The Black wealth gap will not close through individual effort alone. Share what you learn.

### 4. All Debt Is Bad

Mortgage debt on a reasonably-priced house can be an investment. Student loans at low rates may be acceptable. Credit card debt at 25% APR is never acceptable.

### 5. Waiting for the "Right Time" to Invest

Time in the market beats timing the market. Start now.
