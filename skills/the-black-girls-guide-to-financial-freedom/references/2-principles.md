# 2 — Principles

> Source: *The Black Girl's Guide to Financial Freedom* by Paris Woods.

## 7 Principles

### 1. Run from Everything That Smells Like a Payment

Paris's core rule: "If you can't pay cash, you can't afford it." This applies to cars, education, furniture — everything except a house.

> **Case: The $2,000 France Trip** (Chapter 1): Paris put a high school trip to France on a credit card she couldn't pay off. That single decision started her into debt. Years later, she learned to pay cash.

### 2. Your Savings Rate is Everything

The percentage of your income you save determines how soon you reach financial independence. 10% → ~51 years to FI. 50% → ~17 years.

### 3. Your Career is Your Greatest Asset

The #1 way to increase your savings rate is to increase your income. Negotiate. Switch jobs. Build skills.

### 4. Invest Early, Invest Often

Compound interest: $200/month starting at age 25 → ~$500K at 65. Starting at 35 → ~$250K. Time is the multiplier.

### 5. Buy Used Cars in Cash

"One of the biggest money traps I see people fall into is buying a car that's too expensive."

> **Case: The $17,000 Economy Car** (Chapter 2): Paris's first car was a brand-new economy car. She regretted it. Ten years later, she ran the numbers and "I was horrified." She sold it and bought used with cash.

### 6. Don't Go to Grad School on Loans

Student loans are non-bankruptable. Pay cash or get an employer to pay.

### 7. Financial Freedom is About Options, Not Retirement

Paris is financially independent. She still works — because she chooses to. That's the point.
