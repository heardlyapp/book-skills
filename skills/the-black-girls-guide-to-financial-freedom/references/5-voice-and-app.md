# 5 — Voice & App

> Source: *The Black Girl's Guide to Financial Freedom* by Paris Woods.

## Voice

Paris writes like a "motivational big sister" — direct, encouraging, and honest about her own mistakes. She shares her debt journey, her failed investments, and her real estate missteps. The voice is uniquely tuned to the experience of Black women navigating a system that wasn't built for them.

> **Example: The Awkward Dinner** (Chapter 1): At a dinner with Harvard colleagues, Paris was evasive about money talk. "I didn't know how to talk about money." A friend's blunt question forced her to confront her financial illiteracy.

> **Example: The Final Student Loan Payment** (Chapter 3): "That final payment was a tremendous weight lifted, and I vowed never to take out a student loan again." January 2020, nearly 12 years after completing her degree.

## 5 Scenarios

1. **Pay off credit card debt** — Snowball method, stop using cards, increase income
2. **Start investing** — Open a brokerage account, automate $50/month into VTSAX
3. **Negotiate salary** — Research market, ask for 20% more, practice the script
4. **Buy a first home** — House hack a multi-unit property
5. **Design a FIRE plan** — Calculate savings rate, target 50%, use the 4% rule
