# 1 — Core Framework

> Source: *The Black Girl's Guide to Financial Freedom* by Paris Woods — Chapters 1-8.

## The Three Frameworks

### Framework A: The Debt Elimination System (Chapters 1-3)

Paris's approach: debt is an emergency. Credit card debt, car loans, and student loans must be eliminated before any serious wealth building can begin. The system: stop accumulating → snowball (smallest to largest) → increase income to accelerate.

> **Case: Paris's Own Credit Card Debt** (Chapter 1): Paris had accumulated thousands in credit card debt while working at Harvard. She put a $2,000 trip to France on a high-interest card. "I was adding to my debt by the minute." Her awakening came when a friend asked her bluntly: "You're working at Harvard. You're supposed to be smart. Why are you so bad with money?" The embarrassment sparked her journey. She stopped using credit cards, paid cash, and chipped away until she was free.

### Framework B: The FIRE Path (Chapters 7-8)

Financial Independence, Retire Early: save aggressively (40-50%+ of income), invest in low-cost index funds, achieve a portfolio of 25× annual expenses, then either retire or work by choice.

> **Case: Paris's Slow Cooker Budget** (Chapter 8): "Everyone wants to be rich overnight." But FIRE is the "slow cooker approach in a microwave world." Paris cut her spending while working at Harvard, built her savings rate, and reached financial independence. She could have retired early, but chose to continue working by choice. "I have options. That's what financial freedom looks like."

### Framework C: Real Estate as Wealth Accelerator (Chapter 7)

Real estate is one of the most reliable paths to wealth for Black women. Paris covers: house hacking (renting rooms), BRRRR (buy-rehab-rent-refinance-repeat), and cash flow positive properties.
