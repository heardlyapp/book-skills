### Philosophy — 5 Rules to Remember

1. **You are the CEO of your money.** The person most responsible for your financial future is the person in the mirror. No one is coming to save you.
2. **Debt is an emergency — treat it like one.** Credit cards, car loans, and student loans are the biggest barriers to wealth for most Black women.
3. **Your career is your greatest wealth-building tool.** The #1 way to increase your savings rate is to increase your income.
4. **Investing is not optional.** Money sitting in a savings account loses value to inflation every year. The market is the only proven path to long-term wealth.
5. **Financial freedom is about designing your life, not just your portfolio.** FIRE is not about deprivation — it's about aligning your spending with your values.

### Core Framework Quick Reference

- **The Money Mindset** — Reframe your relationship with money: from scarcity to abundance, from fear to empowerment, from "I can't afford it" to "how can I afford it?"
- **Debt Freedom Plan** — Step 1: Stop accumulating. Step 2: Snowball (smallest to largest). Step 3: Increase income to accelerate payoff.
- **The 50/30/20 Budget** — 50% needs, 30% wants, 20% savings/investing. Adjust for FIRE: push savings rate to 40-50%+.
- **Index Fund Investing** — VTSAX (total market), low-cost, diversified, passive. Time in the market beats timing the market.
- **FIRE Formula** — Savings rate determines time to FI. Save 50% → retire in ~17 years. Save 25% → retire in ~32 years.
- **The Wealth Gap Context** — Black women face unique barriers: lower starting wealth, pay gap, discrimination. The tools work the same, but the starting line is different.

### Key Principles

1. **Stop buying things you don't need with money you don't have.** Paris's "run from everything that smells like a payment."
2. **Your savings rate matters more than your income.** You can earn $200K and save nothing, or earn $60K and save 50%.
3. **Pay yourself first.** Automate savings and investing before you have a chance to spend.
4. **Negotiate everything — salary, rent, bills, interest rates.** Ask. The worst they can say is no.
5. **Buy used cars in cash.** Nothing destroys wealth faster than car payments.
6. **Don't go to graduate school unless you can pay cash.** Student loans are non-bankruptable.
7. **Build community.** Financial literacy must be shared. Send the elevator back down.

### Anti-Pattern Summary

The central error: **believing financial freedom is for "other" people — people with more money, better jobs, or different backgrounds.** The anti-pattern is waiting until you're "ready" to start. You start where you are with what you have. See `references/4-anti-patterns.md`.
