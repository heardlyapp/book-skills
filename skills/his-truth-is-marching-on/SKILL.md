### Philosophy — 7 Rules to Remember

1. **Hope Is Not Wishful Thinking — It Is an Act of Will.** Lewis believed hope shapes history. But it only shapes it when people act on it. "The arc of the moral universe bends toward justice" — but only if we press for the swerve.
2. **The Beloved Community Is the Kingdom of God on Earth.** Not a vague ideal but a concrete goal: "All human existence throughout history has strived toward community. That movement is as inexorable as the flow of a river toward the sea."
3. **Sainthood Is Measured by What You Suffer for Others.** Lewis was arrested 45 times. His skull was fractured by a state trooper's club. "He was always on the front line, wherever the front line was. And as his friend, I got concerned. To be honest with you, I was afraid for him." — Bernard LaFayette.
4. **Costly Grace Requires Everything.** Meacham (quoting Bonhoeffer): "Costly grace is costly because it costs a man his life, and it is grace because it gives a man the only true life." Lewis did not see sacrifice as a burden but as the price of following Jesus.
5. **The Opposition Has a Face — Look at It With Love.** Lawson taught: "The enemy is not the person who hates you. The enemy is the hatred itself." Lewis looked his attackers in the eye. That was not weakness — it was discipline.
6. **You Can Outlast the Movement — and You Must.** Lewis was de-elected from SNCC in 1966. He went to Congress for 33 years. At 76, he was still leading sit-ins on the House floor. "In the final analysis, we are one people, one family, one house."
7. **Democracy Is an Act, Not a State.** Lewis' last words to the country: "When you see something that is not right, you must say something. You must do something. Democracy is not a state. It is an act."

### Core Framework Quick Reference

- **Overture — The Last March (March 2020):** John Lewis crossed the Edmund Pettus Bridge for the final time. He was 80, dying of pancreatic cancer, "frail but unbowed." He said: "We were beaten. Tear-gassed. Bullwhipped. On this bridge, some of us gave a little blood to help redeem the soul of America." A voice called "We love you, John." He replied: "I love you, too." Then: "Don't get weary! Keep the faith!" He died four months later.
- **The Life (1940-2020):** Born in a shotgun shack in Carter's Quarters, Pike County, Alabama. Great-grandfather Frank Carter was born into slavery. Jesse Thornton lynched for failing to say "Mister." Recy Taylor gang-raped. Lewis saw the silver fountain for whites and the rusty spigot for "colored." "From my earliest memories, I was fundamentally disturbed by the unbridled meanness of the world around me."
- **The Training (1959-1960):** James Lawson's workshops in Nashville. "The discipline of nonviolence was as rigorous as the training for any athlete or any soldier." Students role-played attacks monthly. The Krystal fumigation: Bevel chanting Shadrach, Meshach, Abednego.
- **The Freedom Rides (1961):** Lewis missed the Anniston bombing by a last-minute trip to Philadelphia. He led the continuation. In Montgomery, beaten with a wooden crate, blood streaming into his eyes, "I was not afraid."
- **March on Washington (1963):** Youngest speaker. Original draft too radical — the Archbishop demanded changes. "Which side is the federal government on?" became "Wake up America!" The 16th Street Baptist Church bombing: "I stood in the rubble trying to make sense of something that made no sense."
- **Bloody Sunday (1965):** Lewis and Hosea Williams led 600 marchers. State troopers charged. Lewis' skull was fractured. Lying on the pavement, he was ready to die. Eight days later, LBJ introduced the Voting Rights Act.
- **Congress (1987-2020):** 33 years. "Conscience of the Congress." De-feated Julian Bond in a bitter 1986 primary. Led the House sit-in for gun control at 76. "I have been in some kind of fight — for freedom, equality, basic human rights — for nearly my entire life."

### Anti-Pattern Summary

The central error: **sanctifying Lewis without emulating him.** Lewis was human, flawed, prideful, and stubborn — and that makes him a model, not an icon. See `references/4-anti-patterns.md`.
