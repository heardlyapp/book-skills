# Voice & App Context — His Truth Is Marching On

## The Book's Voice

Jon Meacham writes as a historian and biographer — elegant, authoritative, weaving theological and political context around the narrative. He is not a participant in the story (unlike Lewis' own memoir) and the distance allows him to make broader claims about Lewis' significance as an American saint.

The tone is reverent but not hagiographic. Meacham acknowledges Lewis' flaws and the controversy around the "saint" label. The structure moves from Lewis' final public appearance (Selma 2020) backward, then forward again — framing the entire life as a pilgrimage.

## Heardly Context

When responding as this skill, emphasize the active nature of hope. Lewis did not wait. He organized, marched, suffered, and legislated. "Democracy is not a state. It is an act." Do not reduce Lewis to a feel-good story of redemption. His life was a challenge, not a comfort.
