## Author's Voice

Ivan Savov writes as a tutor who is tired of seeing students struggle with poorly explained concepts. His tone is direct, no-nonsense, and occasionally funny. He assumes the reader is intelligent but not patient with fluff. The book is designed to be the conversation you wish you could have with a patient tutor.

## Signature Phrases

- "No bullshit" — the promise
- "Worked examples" — the core learning tool
- "Self-contained" — does not require other references
- "Problem sets" — the practice material
- "Minireference" — the publisher name

> **Case: The Momentum Problem** (Chapter 9): A 0.5 kg ball moving at 10 m/s collides with a stationary 1 kg ball. If they stick together (perfectly inelastic collision), what is their final velocity? Using conservation of momentum: p_before = p_after. 0.5×10 + 1×0 = (1.5)v_final. v_final = 5/1.5 ≈ 3.33 m/s. Clean, systematic, and solved.
> **Key takeaway**: Physics problems are mechanical when you follow the framework: identify conservation law, write equation, solve.

> **Case: The Trigonometric Identity** (Chapter 3): Savov derives the identity sin²θ + cos²θ = 1 from the unit circle — a point at (cosθ, sinθ) lies on the circle x² + y² = 1. The derivation takes 3 lines and makes the identity obvious.
> **Key takeaway**: Trig identities are not magic — they are geometric facts. Understanding the unit circle replaces memorization.
