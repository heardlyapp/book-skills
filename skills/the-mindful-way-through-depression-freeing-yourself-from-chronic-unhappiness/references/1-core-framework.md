# Core Framework: The Depression Cycle and MBCT

> Source: The Mindful Way through Depression, Chapters 1-3

## The Depression Cycle

Depression is maintained by a vicious cycle: a low mood triggers negative thinking, which triggers rumination ("why do I feel this way?"), which deepens the low mood further. Automatic pilot — acting without awareness — allows this cycle to run completely unchecked.

> **Case: The Spiral** (Chapter 1): A small letdown — a friend cancels plans — triggers the thought "nobody wants to be with me." This leads to rumination about every past rejection. The mood drops further. Before you know it, you're in a full depressive spiral. The initial thought triggered the feeling, which triggered more thoughts. This is how a small setback becomes a major depressive episode.

> **Case: The Alternative** (Chapter 2): Mindfulness offers a different response. Instead of engaging with the thought, you notice it: "Ah, there's the thought 'nobody wants to be with me.'" You see it as a mental event — a passing cloud — not a fact. The thought may arise, but it doesn't trigger the spiral.

## MBCT: The Synthesis

MBCT combines mindfulness meditation with cognitive therapy. The cognitive therapy component teaches you to recognize automatic negative thoughts. The mindfulness component teaches you to relate to them differently — observing from a distance without arguing or believing.

> **Key takeaway**: You can't stop the first domino from falling. But you can refuse to push the second one.
