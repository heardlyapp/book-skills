# Techniques: Core Meditation Practices

> Source: The Mindful Way through Depression, Chapters 5-8

## The Body Scan

Lying down, systematically moving attention through each part of the body. Not relaxing — just noticing. This practice teaches you to direct attention intentionally and be present with whatever is there.

> **Case: The Foot** (Chapter 5): Many people find it hard to feel their left foot during a body scan. The instruction: don't try harder. Just notice the absence of sensation. Noticing nothing is still noticing. This is a powerful lesson for depression — being with "nothing" without trying to force feeling.

> **Key takeaway**: The body scan teaches you to be with sensation without needing it to change. This skill transfers to being with emotions without needing them to change.

## The 3-Minute Breathing Space

Step 1: Notice what's here (thoughts, feelings, body sensations). Step 2: Gather attention to the breath. Step 3: Expand awareness to the whole body. Can be done anywhere.

> **Case: The First-Response Tool** (Chapter 7): The breathing space is the first response when you notice mood dipping. It interrupts automatic pilot and creates a choice point. You can then respond skillfully rather than react automatically.

## Sitting Meditation

Sitting with awareness of breath. When the mind wanders, gently bring it back. This strengthening of attention is the core of the practice.

> **Key takeaway**: Every time you notice the mind has wandered and bring it back, you've just done a rep of mindfulness. This is the exercise. The wandering is not failure — it's the opportunity to practice.
