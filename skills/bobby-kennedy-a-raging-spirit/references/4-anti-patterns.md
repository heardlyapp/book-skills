## 6 Anti-Patterns of Misunderstanding RFK

### 1. "RFK Was Always a Liberal"

He started as a conservative. He worked for Joe McCarthy. He was JFK's enforcer. His transformation was real — and it makes his story more impressive, not less.

### 2. "He Only Ran Because of JFK"

RFK's 1968 campaign was his own. He had grown beyond his brother's shadow. He was not running to continue JFK's work — he was running to do his own.

> **Case: The Decision to Run** (Chapter 15): RFK agonized over entering the 1968 race. He knew it would split the Democratic Party. He knew it would anger LBJ. He knew the risks. He ran because he believed the country was on the wrong track — not because of his brother.
> **Key takeaway**: RFK's campaign was not about legacy — it was about conviction. He risked everything because he believed.

### 3. "The 'Raging Spirit' Was Anger"

It was not just anger — it was passion, intensity, drive, and love. RFK's spirit was complex. He could be cold and warm, ruthless and kind, ambitious and idealistic.

### 4. "RFK Was the Same as JFK"

JFK was cool, ironic, detached. RFK was hot, passionate, engaged. They were very different men. RFK was not trying to be his brother — he was finding his own way.

### 5. "His Assassination Was Inevitable"

The assassination was not inevitable. It was a specific act by a specific man. The tragedy of 1968 is that America was torn apart — and RFK might have been the one person who could have held it together.

### 6. "RFK Was Perfect"

He was not. He made mistakes. He was ruthless. He was ambitious. His transformation was real but incomplete. He was a man, not a saint.

> **Case: The Wiretap** (Chapter 5): RFK authorized wiretaps on Martin Luther King — a decision that contradicts everything he later stood for. It shows his transformation was real because he started from a place that was genuinely different.
> **Key takeaway**: Judging RFK only by his early actions misses the point. The transformation is the story.
