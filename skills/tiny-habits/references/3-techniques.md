# Techniques · Growing Habits · Breaking Bad Habits

> Source: *Tiny Habits*, Chapters 6-7

---

## Growing Tiny to Big (Chapter 6)

Tiny habits naturally expand on their own. After doing "one pushup" for a week, you'll start doing five. This is organic growth — let it happen naturally.

**Don't force growth.** If a habit feels like a chore, shrink it back to tiny. The key is maintaining the feeling of success.

## Untangling Bad Habits (Chapter 7)

**Step 1: Diagnose with B=MAP**
- What prompt triggers the bad habit?
- What ability makes it easy?
- What motivation drives it?

**Step 2: Redesign**
1. **Remove the prompt** — Make the trigger invisible (leave phone in another room)
2. **Increase difficulty** — Add friction (unsubscribe from delivery apps)
3. **Reduce motivation** — Reframe: this behavior hurts the person I want to become
4. **Substitute** — Replace the bad habit with a good one at the same prompt

## Golden Behaviors

From Chapter 2: A "Golden Behavior" is one that:
- You actually want to do (high motivation)
- You can make tiny (high ability)
- You can anchor to an existing routine (clear prompt)

If it's not golden, redesign until it is.
