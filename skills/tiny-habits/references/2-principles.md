# Principles · Motivation · Ability · Prompts · Celebration

> Source: *Tiny Habits*, Chapters 3-5

---

## Motivation — Don't Rely on It

High motivation is rare and unreliable. Instead of waiting for motivation, design habits that require almost none. The secret: make the behavior so easy that even on your worst day, you can do it.

## Ability — Easy Does It

The **Ability Chain**: When a behavior is hard, check which link is weak:
- **Time** — Do you have enough time?
- **Money** — Does it cost too much?
- **Physical Effort** — Is it physically demanding?
- **Mental Effort** — Does it require thinking?
- **Routine** — Does it conflict with your existing routines?

Make the behavior easier on whichever link is weakest.

## Prompts — The Power of After

Three types of prompts:
1. **Person Prompt** — Your own internal thought (unreliable)
2. **Context Prompt** — Something in your environment (a sticky note, an alarm)
3. **Action Prompt** — An existing routine. The most reliable of all.

**Always use an Action Prompt.** "After I [existing routine]" is the foundation of the Tiny Habits method.

## Celebration — Emotion Creates Habits

This is Fogg's breakthrough insight: **emotions create habits, not repetition.** If you feel successful after a tiny behavior, your brain encodes it as worth repeating.

**Celebration techniques:** Fist pump. Say "Yes!" quietly. Smile. Think "I'm awesome." Whatever makes you feel genuinely good for 2-3 seconds.
