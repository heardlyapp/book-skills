# Core Framework · B=MAP · Tiny Habits Recipe

> Source: *Tiny Habits*, Introduction, Chapters 1-2

## B=MAP: The Fogg Behavior Model

**Behavior = Motivation × Ability × Prompt**

All three must converge at the same moment. If a behavior doesn't happen, it's because at least one element is missing. Never blame willpower — redesign the system.

**The 3 Elements:**
- **Motivation**: Your desire to do the behavior. Unreliable — fluctuates day to day.
- **Ability**: How easy the behavior is. This is the element YOU control. Make it so easy you can do it even with zero motivation.
- **Prompt**: The trigger. Without it, nothing happens. The best prompts are existing routines.

## The Tiny Habits Recipe

> *"After I [ANCHOR MOMENT], I will [TINY BEHAVIOR]. Then I CELEBRATE."*

**Example:** "After I pour my morning coffee, I will take one deep breath. Then I whisper 'Good start!'"

**The 3 parts:**
1. **Anchor** — An existing routine (brushing teeth, pouring coffee, sitting down at desk)
2. **Tiny Behavior** — Takes less than 30 seconds. So easy you can't say no.
3. **Celebration** — A genuine positive feeling immediately after. This wires the habit.
