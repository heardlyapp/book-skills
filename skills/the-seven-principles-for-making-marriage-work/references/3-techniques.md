# Techniques: Problem Solving, the Magic Six Hours, and Sex

## How to Solve Solvable Problems (Principle 5)

Not all problems are solvable. Identify whether you're dealing with a solvable problem (situational, specific) or a perpetual problem (personality-based, recurring). This technique is for solvable problems only.

### Step 1: Soften Your Start-Up

96% of the time, how a conversation begins determines how it ends.

**The formula for a softened start-up:**
1. "I feel..." (state your feeling, not your judgment)
2. "About..." (specific situation, not character attack)
3. "I need / I'd like..." (positive need, not demand)

*Bad:* "You never help around here. You're so lazy."
*Good:* "I'm really frustrated that the dishes haven't been done. Could you please do them before bed?"

### Step 2: Make and Receive Repair Attempts

A repair attempt is any statement or action that prevents negativity from escalating. Common repairs:
- "Let's take a break"
- "I'm sorry"
- "I hear you"
- A joke, a silly face, a gentle touch
- "I need to calm down"

**Key insight:** The success of a repair is determined by the health of the friendship underneath it. A goofy smile works for happily married couples; a sincere apology is ignored by couples in negative override.

### Step 3: Soothe Yourself and Each Other

When heart rate exceeds 100 bpm, you cannot process information or problem-solve. You must take a 20-minute break. During the break:
- Do NOT rehearse your argument (this keeps your physiology elevated)
- Do something calming: walk, read, listen to music, meditate
- Check your pulse before returning to the conversation

### Step 4: Compromise

The two-circle method:
1. Draw two overlapping circles
2. In one circle: your nonnegotiable areas (can't give on these)
3. In the other circle: your partner's nonnegotiable areas
4. In the overlap: where you can both be flexible
5. Find a temporary compromise in the overlap

**Trap:** If your proposed compromise has "no influence" of your partner's perspective, it's not a compromise — it's a demand.

## The Magic Six Hours — Weekly Maintenance Routine

This is the book's proven "minimum effective dose" for marriage health:

| Activity | Daily time | Weekly total |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| **Partings** — 2 min each morning, learn one thing about your spouse's day ahead | 2 min × 5 days | 10 min |
| **Reunions** — 6-second kiss + 20-min stress-reducing conversation | 20 min × 5 days | 1 hr 40 min |
| **Admiration & Appreciation** — one genuine expression daily | 5 min × 7 days | 35 min |
| **Affection** — physical touch, embrace before sleep | 5 min × 7 days | 35 min |
| **Weekly Date** — just the two of you, open-ended questions | 2 hours × 1 | 2 hrs |
| **State of the Union** — talk about relationship: appreciations, issues, what you need | 1 hour × 1 | 1 hr |
| **GRAND TOTAL** | | **6 hours** |

### The Stress-Reducing Conversation (for reunions)

Rules:
1. Take turns being the speaker and listener
2. Don't give unsolicited advice — "You should..." is banned
3. Show genuine interest — ask questions, maintain eye contact
4. Communicate understanding — "That makes sense," "I see why you'd feel that way"
5. Be on your partner's side — don't defend the other person
6. Express a "we against others" attitude — "That was unfair of them"
7. Express affection — hold hands, touch

## The Marital Poop Detector — Early Warning System

Weekly self-check questions (if you check more than 4, talk within 3 days):
- "I have been feeling irritable / distant / lonely / angry"
- "We have been out of touch"
- "Small issues escalate"
- "There hasn't been much joy"

## Talking About Sex

**Key findings:**
- Friendship quality determines sexual satisfaction (70% for men, 70% for women)
- In happy marriages, sex is not the focus of complaints — it's the barometer of friendship health
- Women's desire is heavily influenced by relationship quality; men's by physical factors

**Practical techniques:**
1. Redefine sex — include any positive connecting (not just intercourse)
2. Put the partner with lower interest in charge of the couple's sensual experience
3. Initiate and refuse gently — use a 1-9 scale ("I'm a 5 tonight, let's see where it goes")
4. Use Barbach's initiation ritual — a clear, accepted framework for yes/no

**Counterintuitive finding:** Husbands who reward their wives for saying no end up having more sex. Game theory modeling: if Jim punishes Mary's "no," sex once every 3 weeks; if he accepts it, sex 4 times/week.

### Processing a "Regrettable Incident"

The confessor's role:
1. Report your own feelings, not their flaws
2. Take partial responsibility
3. Avoid "but" — it invalidates everything before it

The listener's role:
1. Listen without defensiveness
2. Validate their perspective ("I can see why you'd feel that way")
3. Thank them for sharing
