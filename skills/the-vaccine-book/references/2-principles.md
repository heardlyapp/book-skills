# 2 — Alternative Vaccine Schedules

> Source: "The Vaccine Book" by Dr. Robert W. Sears

## The Standard CDC Schedule

The AAP/CDC schedule gives multiple vaccines simultaneously at well-child visits: 5 shots at 2 months, 4-5 at 4 months, 4-6 at 6 months, and so on.

## The Problem Sears Identifies

Giving multiple vaccines at once makes it impossible to know which one caused a reaction. It also concentrates exposure to vaccine ingredients.

## Sears' Alternative Schedules

Sears provides several alternatives: spacing out shots (one at a time), delaying certain vaccines until the child is older, and postponing lower-priority vaccines.

> **Case: The 2-Month Visit** (Chapter 18): "At the 2-month visit, the standard schedule gives HIB, PCV (pneumococcal), DTaP, rotavirus, and polio — five vaccines at once. Many parents are uncomfortable with this. My alternative schedule would split these across two visits or delay the lower-priority ones." Sears provides a specific month-by-month alternative schedule.
> **Key takeaway**: The 2-month visit is where most parents first feel overwhelmed. Sears' alternatives help parents who want to vaccinate but not all at once.

> **Case: The Delayed Hepatitis B** (Chapter 4): The standard schedule gives Hep B at birth. Sears notes that Hep B is a sexually transmitted disease and blood-borne illness. "Newborns are not at risk for hepatitis B unless the mother is infected. If the mother tests negative, delaying the Hep B vaccine is a reasonable choice for parents who want to reduce the number of shots at birth."
> **Key takeaway**: The Hep B vaccine at birth is standard, but Sears argues it is not medically necessary for babies of Hep B-negative mothers. This is one of the most common decisions parents face about alternative scheduling.
