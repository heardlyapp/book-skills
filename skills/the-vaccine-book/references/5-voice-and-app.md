# 5 — Sears' Voice + 5 Application Scenarios

> Source: "The Vaccine Book" by Dr. Robert W. Sears

## Sears' Voice

Dr. Robert W. Sears is a board-certified pediatrician and the son of Dr. William Sears ("the attachment parenting doctor"). His voice: measured, informative, respectful of parental autonomy. He is a pro-vaccine doctor who believes in full disclosure and shared decision-making.

> **Case: The Pro-Vaccine Pro-Information Stance** (Preface): "I am a pro-vaccine doctor. I am also a pro-information doctor." Sears makes it clear from the start that he vaccinates his own patients and believes in vaccination. But he also believes that parents deserve complete information to make their own decisions. "The days of doctors simply advising parents to get all vaccines without any explanation are over."
> **Key takeaway**: Sears positions himself between the "pro-vaccine" and "anti-vaccine" camps. His message: vaccination is important, but informed consent is equally important. Parents should be partners in the decision, not passive recipients of medical orders.

> **Case: The Personal Story** (Dedication): Sears dedicates the book to a friend who asked him a question that made him think, and to his wife, who asked him to research vaccines before vaccinating their own children. "My wife asked me to do a little research before we began our children's vaccines, just to make sure we were doing it right." This personal experience shapes the book's approach.
> **Key takeaway**: Even a pediatrician had questions about vaccines for his own children. The book was born from that personal journey. This gives Sears credibility with parents who are asking the same questions.

## Key Quote

"Vaccination isn't an all-or-nothing decision. There are many choices that can be made."

## 5 Application Scenarios

1. **Preparing for a 2-month well visit** → Know which vaccines are scheduled. Decide in advance if you want all at once or an alternative schedule.
2. **Concerned about MMR and autism** → Read Chapter 16. The research does not support a link, but parents should understand both sides of the debate.
3. **Wanting to space out shots** → Work with your pediatrician. Not all doctors will agree. Sears' alternative schedules provide a template.
4. **Considering skipping the flu shot** → Weigh the risks. Flu is serious for some children. The vaccine is recommended annually.
5. **Approaching the HPV decision for a teenager** → HPV is extremely common and can cause cancer. The vaccine is most effective when given before sexual activity begins.
