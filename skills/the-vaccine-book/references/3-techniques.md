# 3 — Vaccine Ingredients

> Source: "The Vaccine Book" by Dr. Robert W. Sears

## What Is in Vaccines

Beyond the active ingredient (killed or weakened virus/bacteria), vaccines contain:
- **Aluminum**: Adjuvant that boosts immune response
- **Formaldehyde**: Used to inactivate viruses
- **Gelatin**: Stabilizer (can cause allergic reactions)
- **Egg protein**: Traces from vaccine production
- **Thimerosal**: Mercury-containing preservative (removed from most childhood vaccines since 2001)
- **Antibiotics**: Trace amounts (neomycin, polymyxin B)

> **Case: The Aluminum Question** (Chapter 17): Sears notes that a baby receiving the standard 2-month visit gets about 1,225 micrograms of aluminum. "The amount of aluminum in vaccines is within safety limits set by the FDA. However, parents should know that aluminum is a neurotoxin and that cumulative exposure over the first year is significant." He presents both sides — the safety data and the concerns.
> **Key takeaway**: Sears believes in transparency about ingredients. He does not say aluminum is dangerous at vaccine levels, but he believes parents should know what they are putting into their child's body and make an informed choice.

> **Case: The Thimerosal Removal** (Chapter 16): Thimerosal (mercury) was removed from most childhood vaccines in 2001 as a precautionary measure. "The removal of thimerosal was not because it was proven dangerous — it was because of public concern. But the removal itself is a natural experiment. If thimerosal caused autism, autism rates should have dropped after its removal. They did not." Sears presents both the studies showing no link and the studies showing a possible link.
> **Key takeaway**: The thimerosal story is complex. It was removed due to public concern, not scientific proof of harm. Yet autism rates continued to rise after removal, which is evidence against the thimerosal-autism link.
