# 4 — Anti-Patterns

> Source: *Technical Analysis of the Financial Markets* by John J. Murphy.

## Common Errors

### 1. Fighting the Primary Trend

**The trap:** "The market has gone up so much. It MUST reverse soon. I'll short it."

**Why it fails:** Markets can remain overbought longer than traders can remain solvent. An uptrend does not end because it has gone "too far." It ends when the trend reverses. Trying to pick tops and bottoms is the fastest way to lose money.

> **Case: Oscillators in Strong Trends** (Chapter 10): Murphy warns that oscillators like RSI can remain in overbought territory for weeks during a strong uptrend. A trader who shorts because RSI >70 will be repeatedly stopped out. The solution: use oscillators as warnings, not triggers. In a strong trend, wait for the oscillator to drop BELOW 70 (or rise above 30 in a downtrend) before acting.
> **Key takeaway**: When the trend is strong, oscillator extremes are unreliable. The trend itself is more important than the oscillator.

### 2. Premature Pattern Diagnosis

**The trap:** "This looks like a double top. I'll sell now."

**Why it fails:** Most potential double tops and bottoms never complete. The normal action of prices is to pull back from a prior peak. The double top exists only when support is broken. Until then, the odds favor continuation. Murphy gives clear guidance: the interval between peaks or troughs should be at least one month for a valid double top or bottom.
> **Key takeaway**: Don't call a reversal pattern until it has completed. Premature diagnosis leads to premature losses.

> **Case: The Bull Trap** (Chapter 5): Murphy describes the "bull trap" — a false breakout above a prior peak that reverses into a double top. The false breakout happens on light volume, luring traders into long positions. Then the market reverses sharply. A price filter (1-3% penetration) and a time filter (two consecutive closes) help reduce these whipsaws.
> **Key takeaway**: Filters don't eliminate false signals, but they reduce them. Use both price and time filters together for best results.

### 3. Ignoring Volume

**The trap:** "The breakout happened. I'm in."

**Why it fails:** A breakout on light volume is unreliable. Heavy volume confirms the move. Low-volume rallies are suspect and often precede a reversal.

### 4. Overcomplicating with Too Many Indicators

**The trap:** "I'll use RSI, MACD, Stochastic, Bollinger Bands, Point and Figure, and Elliott Wave all at once."

**Why it fails:** Murphy recommends combining tools, but not too many. Two or three confirming indicators are better than one. Ten conflicting indicators create paralysis. Keep it simple.

### 5. No Risk Management

**The trap:** "I know this trade will work. I don't need a stop."

**Why it fails:** Even the best pattern, indicator, or system occasionally fails. A trade without a stop loss is not a trade — it's gambling. The failed head and shoulders pattern happens. The key is survival.
