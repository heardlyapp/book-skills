# 2 — Cooking Principles

> Source: "Fifty Shades of Chicken" by F.L. Fowler

## 7 Essential Principles

1. **Brine or dry brine. Always.** Salt + time = juicy chicken. Minimum 1 hour, ideally 12-24.
2. **Dry the skin.** Pat it dry before cooking. Wet skin = steamed, not crispy.
3. **Room temperature.** Let chicken sit out 20-30 mins before cooking. Even cooking.
4. **Thermometer.** 165°F breast, 175°F thigh. Don't guess.
5. **Rest.** After cooking, rest 5-10 mins before cutting. Juices redistribute.
6. **Pan sauce.** After searing, deglaze with wine/broth + butter. Instant sauce from drippings.
7. **Save bones.** Make stock. Free stock is better than store-bought.

> **Case: The Wolf's Kitchen** (Chapter 1): Christian's kitchen is described as a temple of cooking perfection: "All sleek white tile, blonde wood, and black granite." Every tool in its place. A thermometer, a cast iron pan, a roasting rack. The implied principle: your tools matter. You don't need a professional kitchen, but you need the right basics.
> **Key takeaway**: Cooking good chicken doesn't require expensive equipment. A cast iron pan, a thermometer, and salt. That's 80% of the battle.

> **Case: The Brining Revelation** (Chapter 3): Ana experiences the brine for the first time. "I sink into the warm liquid. It's... overwhelming. I've never felt anything like this." The brine transforms the chicken from ordinary to extraordinary. This is the book's core cooking lesson.
> **Key takeaway**: If you only learn one technique from this book, let it be brining. It's the difference between "okay chicken" and "unforgettable chicken."
