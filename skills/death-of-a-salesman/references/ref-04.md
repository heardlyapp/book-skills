# Reference: Linda and the Women

## Overview
Linda Loman is the play's moral center. She loves Willy completely and protects him from the truth.

## Case: The Wife
Linda knows Willy is failing. She knows he is suicidal. She protects him from knowing what she knows. She mends his stockings. She waits.

## Case: The Plea
"Attention, attention must be finally paid to such a person." Linda's speech is the play's most powerful moment. She demands that Willy be seen, not as a failure, but as a human being.

## Case: The Enabler
Linda's love is also a form of enabling. She protects Willy from reality. She does not confront him. She helps maintain the delusion.

## Case: The Woman
The Woman in Boston represents the other side of Willy's life. She laughs at his jokes. She gives him the attention Linda gives him without demand. The affair is not about sex — it is about recognition.

## Case: The Stockings
Linda mends her stockings while Willy buys stockings for the Woman. The stockings are a symbol of Willy's betrayal and Linda's unwitting humiliation.

## Case: The Requiem
Linda speaks at Willy's grave. "I can't cry. I don't know what it is. Forgive me, dear." She cannot mourn properly. She has been mourning for years.
Linda is the play's most neglected character in critical analysis. She is not just the loyal wife — she is the person who has paid attention all along, even when Willy could not.
She knows the truth. She chooses love anyway. That is her tragedy.
Linda Loman: the woman left behind, the woman who paid attention, the woman who is forgotten.
