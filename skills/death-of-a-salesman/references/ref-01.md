# Reference: Willy Loman

## Overview
Willy Loman is one of the great tragic figures of American theater. He is a salesman who has lost his way, haunted by the past and unable to face the present.

## Case: The Exhaustion
Willy cannot drive anymore. He keeps almost crashing. He talks to himself. He forgets where he is. His mind is failing. He is physically and mentally exhausted.

## Case: The Delusions
Willy lives partly in memory. He relives conversations with his dead brother Ben. He imagines Biff as the golden boy he once was. He cannot tell past from present.

## Case: The Affair
Willy had an affair with a woman in Boston. Biff discovered them. This destroyed Biff. Willy carries this guilt throughout the play.

## Case: The Suicide
Willy plans his death. He believes the insurance money will save Biff. He dies for a delusion.

## Case: The Tragic Hero
Willy is not a great man. He is a small man destroyed by a big dream. This is Arthur Miller's definition of tragedy: the common man as tragic hero.

## Case: The Seeds
Willy is obsessed with planting seeds at night. He wants to grow something, to leave something behind. The seeds are a symbol of his desperation to create meaning in a life that feels worthless.

## Case: The Car
Willy's car is his office, his prison, his coffin. He travels through New England selling nothing. The car represents the rootless life of a salesman.
Willy is not a villain, not a fool, not a failure by choice. He is a man who believed what his country told him and was destroyed when it proved false.
