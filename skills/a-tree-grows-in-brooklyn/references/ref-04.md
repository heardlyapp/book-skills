# Reference: Education as Escape

## Overview
Education is Francie's path out of poverty. Reading is the foundation.

## Case: The Library
Francie goes to the library every day. She reads one book a day. She reads alphabetically. The librarian is indifferent. Francie does not care.

## Case: The School
Francie excels in school. She loves learning. She wants to go to college. She works for it.

## Case: The Writing
Francie writes stories. She wins a prize. She is published in a magazine. She knows she is a writer.

## Case: The Mother's Sacrifice
Katie's hands are ruined from scrubbing. She works so Francie can study. She denies herself everything. Francie's education is built on her mother's body.

## Case: The Escape
Francie leaves Brooklyn. She goes to college. She becomes a writer. She escapes.

## Case: The Teacher
A teacher recognizes Francie's talent. She encourages her. One person who sees potential can change a life.

## Case: The Cost
Francie pays for her education with her mother's sacrifice and her own labor. Nothing is given. Everything is earned.
Francie's education is not a gift. It is a battle won against poverty, tradition, and the expectation that girls like her do not become writers.
She wins because she never stops reading. The library is her university.
The novel argues that education is the only true escape from poverty. Not luck, not charity — knowledge.
That is the legacy Katie gave Francie: not money, not property — the ability to read.
 
A library card is more powerful than any inheritance.
