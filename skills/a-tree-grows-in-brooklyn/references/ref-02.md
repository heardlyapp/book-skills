# Reference: The Nolan Family

## Overview
The Nolan family is the heart of the novel. Each member contributes to the tapestry of Francie's life.

## Case: Katie Nolan
Katie is the mother. She cleans houses. Her hands are ruined from scrubbing. She is stern but devoted. She gives Francie the gift of education through her sacrifice.

## Case: Johnny Nolan
Johnny is the father. A singing waiter. Charming and loving. An alcoholic. He loves his children deeply but cannot provide for them. Francie's love for him is unconditional.

## Case: Neeley Nolan
Neeley is Francie's brother. He is the favorite child. Francie does not resent this. Neeley is her companion, her protector, her link to childhood.

## Case: Aunt Sissy
Sissy is warm, generous, and scandalous. She has had many husbands and many miscarriages. She gives love freely. She is the family's emotional center.

## Case: Rommely
Katie's mother. Illiterate but wise. She advises Katie to let Francie read and dream. Her wisdom shapes Francie's future.

## Case: The Sacrifice
Every member of the Nolan family sacrifices for each other. Katie's hands. Johnny's dignity. Francie's youth. Neeley's attention. Sissy's money. They are poor but they share everything.

## Case: The Love
The Nolans love each other fiercely. They fail each other, disappoint each other, and forgive each other. They are a family.
The Nolan family is the novel's argument that wealth is not what makes a family rich. Love, sacrifice, and resilience — that is the real inheritance.
