# Reference: Ruth McBride's Story

## Overview
Ruth McBride Jordan was born Rachel Deborah Shilsky to Orthodox Jewish parents in Poland in 1921. Her family emigrated to America when she was a child and settled in Suffolk, Virginia, where her father became the local rabbi and shopkeeper.

## Case: The Abusive Father
Ruth's father, Fishel Shilsky, was a violent and controlling man. He beat his wife and children, sexually abused Ruth beginning at age four, and treated his Black customers with contempt while depending on their business. Ruth describes the hypocrisy of a man who preached from the Torah on Saturday and cheated his customers on Monday. She left her family at age 17, fleeing to New York and changing her name. She never spoke to her father again.

## Case: The Mother's Silence
Ruth's mother, Hudis, was paralyzed by a childhood illness and walked with crutches. She could not protect her children from her husband. Ruth describes the pain of watching her mother suffer in silence — and the guilt of abandoning her when she left. Hudis died alone in an institution, and Ruth did not attend the funeral. The silence between mother and daughter was never broken.

## Case: The Marriage to Dennis McBride
Ruth met Andrew Dennis McBride in New York. He was Black, a Christian minister, and kind. They married in 1942. Interracial marriage was illegal in Virginia and socially unacceptable everywhere. Ruth's family disowned her. Dennis's family was initially suspicious of a White Jewish woman. The marriage was a radical act of love that cost Ruth her family, her community, and her Jewish identity. When Dennis died suddenly in 1957, Ruth was left with eight children, no money, and a life that had been built entirely on a foundation that had just collapsed.
