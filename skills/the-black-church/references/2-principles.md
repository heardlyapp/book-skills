# Key Principles — The Black Church

## I. The Church Is the Oldest Black Institution in America

"Collectively, these churches make up the oldest institution created and controlled by African Americans." Richard Allen founded the AME Church in 1816 — 47 years before Emancipation. There was no other institution that Black people fully controlled. The church was the foundation of everything: politics, education, music, community life. "It's a mistake to think that enslaved Africans came to North America tabula rasa. They came bearing a rich cultural heritage, and this cultural world got filtered through Black churches."

## II. The Church Was Both Refuge and Weapon

"The role of Black Christianity in motivating our country's largest slave rebellion, Nat Turner's Rebellion, is only the most dramatic example." But the church also provided daily sustenance: a place where "you not only were somebody, you felt like you were somebody" (Vernon Jordan). It was simultaneously a sanctuary from white supremacy and a staging ground for attacking it. Marx got it half right: religion was "the sigh of the oppressed creature" — but also "a protest against real suffering."

## III. Music Is the Church's Most Enduring Gift

The spirituals, gospel, and freedom songs shaped every genre of American popular music — blues, jazz, rock and roll, soul, R&B, and hip-hop. Aretha Franklin, James Brown, Sam Cooke, Marvin Gaye, Whitney Houston, John Legend — all products of the church choir. "We tell our story with music. We forget sermons, but we can remember songs." — Bishop Yvette Flunder.

## IV. The Preaching Tradition Is an Art Form

The King James Bible is the "silent second text" of Black culture. The oratorical tradition of the Black pulpit — from John Jasper's "De Sun Do Move" (1882) through MLK's "I Have a Dream" — is one of America's greatest artistic achievements. "Never confuse position with power. Pharaoh had a position, but Moses had the power." — Rev. Otis Moss III.

## V. The Church Is a Training Ground for Political Leadership

Richard Harvey Cain went from pastor to U.S. House of Representatives during Reconstruction. Adam Clayton Powell Jr. pastored Abyssinian Baptist and served in Congress. MLK, John Lewis, Andrew Young, Raphael Warnock — all church-trained. "The Black Church is one of the parents of the civil rights movement, and today's Black Lives Matter movement is one of its heirs."

## VI. The Church Has Always Been Contested and Contradictory

Sexism, homophobia, anti-Semitism, the prosperity gospel — Gates does not flinch from condemning these failures. "The church is diverse and contested." But acknowledging these faults "does not gainsaid the importance of the role of the Black Church at its best." Like any human institution, it is imperfect. Its value is not diminished by its sins.

## VII. Faith Is About the Future — Both This World and the Next

Gates' epilogue: at 12, he made a deal with Jesus to save his mother. He kept his end. "What empowered our ancestors with 'hope against hope'? If a people cannot imagine a future, its culture will die." The Black Church gave African Americans the ability to imagine a future — for themselves, their children, and their country.
