# 4 — Anti-Patterns: What's Wrong with Washington

> Source: "How to Test Negative for Stupid" by John Kennedy

## Anti-Pattern 1: Careerism

**The mistake:** Treating Congress as a career, not a service. The average senator stays 10+ years. The system rewards longevity over effectiveness.

## Anti-Pattern 2: Partisan Bickering

**The mistake:** Caring more about beating the other party than about governing. The media amplifies conflict because conflict gets ratings.

## Anti-Pattern 3: The Revolving Door

**The mistake:** Former members and staff become lobbyists. They use their connections to influence the same people they used to work with.

## Anti-Pattern 4: Complexity as Strategy

**The mistake:** Making laws so complicated that nobody can understand them. This benefits those who wrote them and their donors.

> **Case: The Swamp is Real** (Chapter 7): Kennedy tells the story of a former senator who retired and immediately became a lobbyist. His first client? The same industry he used to regulate. "People call this experience. I call it something else."
> **Key takeaway**: The revolving door between Congress and K Street is the core of the swamp. Without addressing it, nothing else will change.
