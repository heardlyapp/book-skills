# Anti-Patterns

## 1. Rejecting Evidence Because It's Inconvenient

**The mistake**: Dismissing data that contradicts your beliefs.

**Fix**: Lister's critics had the data — they just refused to accept it. When evidence contradicts your assumptions, question your assumptions.

> **Case: The Surgeon Who Refused to See** (Chapter 10): A prominent surgeon visited Lister's ward, saw patients recovering from surgeries that would have killed them a year earlier, and still refused to adopt antisepsis. He couldn't accept that his lifetime of practice had been wrong.
> **Key takeaway**: Even undeniable evidence can be rejected when it threatens identity. The surgeon couldn't accept that he had been killing patients his entire career.

## 2. Attacking the Messenger

**The mistake**: Discrediting the person instead of engaging the evidence.

**Fix**: When someone attacks the innovator instead of the innovation, it's a sign they can't argue with the evidence.

## 3. Believing "We've Always Done It This Way"

**The mistake**: Treating tradition as evidence.

**Fix**: "Always done it this way" is not an argument — it's an admission that you haven't questioned your methods.

> **Case: The Surgical Tradition** (Chapter 6): Victorian surgeons believed that "laudable pus" (pus in a wound) was a sign of healing. They actually encouraged wounds to suppurate. Lister proved that pus was infection, not healing — but this contradicted centuries of surgical tradition.
> **Key takeaway**: Some of the most deeply held medical beliefs are completely wrong. Tradition is not evidence.

## 4. Ignoring Preventive Measures

**The mistake**: Focusing on treatment while ignoring prevention.

**Fix**: Lister focused on preventing infection before it started. This shift — from treatment to prevention — was revolutionary.

## 5. Suppressing Dissent

**The mistake**: Silencing those who challenge orthodoxy.

**Fix**: Semmelweis was destroyed by the establishment. Lister was marginalized. The establishment's attempt to suppress dissent delayed medical progress by decades.
